"Baliff, are you awake?" Marrox Flamebeard asked pushing the flap away from him. "Get out of the wagon, Blackhood wants to see you immediatley." He walked out after he finished his sentence, he was almost invisible by the black leather he was wearing.
Baliff struggled out off the floor with the green horse blanket over it. The wagon floor was hard wood, and he didn't get to sleep in the guild tents judging by his rank of volunteer. All he did was do towel boy type work.

He was just strolling along the campus when a DBS recruiting poster caught his eye. He thought about joining the Dwarven Musketeer Brigade (More elite than the Rifleman Corps.) but when he heard of the guilds and clans that Magni Bronzbeard signed contract for, he was wildly intrested.
Most guild members were better paid than musketeers, the housing was better, and they were a little easier to get into. He signed up to DBS at Ironforge.

But it was nothing like he expected.

He was a lowley cabin boy, who was given simple food, low pay, cracked weapons, and wasn't treated with good respect by guild members.
Plus he had a bad reputation for court marshalling Durin Blackhood for a guild misunderstanding.

He put on his leather boots and his soft wool jacket, still trying to keep his lazy eyelids open.
Marrox had already walked off to his post, knowing that Baliff was doing what he was supposed to do.
After he stepped out of the wagon, he looked at the other Dwarves and soldiers that didn't look up to see him, or pay any attention to. Most of them wore leather armor with a steel helmet that was the only metal armor on their short and stout bodies.
The Dwarves in the camp were very deticated to their job, and morale was high (thanks to the ale).
They ate their bacon, beef and potato soup that they boiled in their pots. The ones on guard duty kept close attention to the landscapes they were on watch for. Most of the only landscapes there were endless snow and forest. Nothing else more. But Dwarves just can't get enough of watching their gorgeous homeland, no matter what it would look like.


Shaleskin walked into a war tent. A fire was set up, and an opening in the roof was present to let the smoke escape.
The DBS members in the tent were mostly the fighters.
Ullrich of Frost laid in his cot drinking Dwarven ale. He tried not to get it all over his blankets.
Murlin Stormhammer was eating the soup that everyone else at the camp was scooping in their mouths. He had his chain mail armor on, unlike the people who weren't wearing their armor when off duty.
Seth Windwalker, a Human, towered over the Dwarves, standing at least three and a half feet more than them. He was eating a peice of delecate, Human style, buttered toast. Shaleskin couldn't imagne eating somthing that low fat. After all, he was a Spy, he couldn't go on missions having a fourteen inch waist.

"Hey! Shaleskin!" Thran Ironwind yelled from his bunk. Thran was one of few that saw Baliff not as a cabin boy, but as a real Dwarf. Baliff looked his way.
Ironwind stood up from his bunk and pulled somthing out of his foot locker, and threw it to Shaleskin. When he looked in his hand, he saw a waxpaper wrapper with somthing in it.
"It's Stromguarde chocolate!" He yelled, and pulled out one of his own, pulled the waxpaper off of it, and started snacking on it.
Baliff smiled. "Thanks Thran." He said with a nod, then put the sweet substance in his pocket. He stepped out, and walked towards a blue tent with a black rectangular flag on top. This was Blackhood's tent. He could have just walked to it without going through the tent that he passed through, but it was the quickest way to Blackhood's.

As he stepped into the private tent, his eyes were greeted by a portable desk, a rack with two pearl handled flintlock pistols and Durin's well known scoped longrifle, a bunk which was hidden by a folding screen for privacy, a footlocker with potions sticking out of it, and a pile of ammunition and gunpowder. Magnus Gurnisson, and Durin himself were standing over the table studying their battle plan.
"You are excused Magnus." Durin ordered, he voice had no sign of Dwarven accent or dialect.
Magnus walked out, he was always carrying that blunderbuss on his back. He brushed the flap away from him, walking out very fast. Durin stepped around the room with his hands behind his stout back. He was wearing fine and hard leather armor. He faced in Shaleskin's direction. Shaleskin expected the worst after he court marshalled Durin for an arguement that could be won without law and order.
"When you are in my presence you salute Youthman." Blackhood ordered quite firmly. Baliff snapped into attention and saluted as Durin ordered.
"You aren't showing any respect for your officers Baliff. I don't like that." He continued, walking around under the tent.
"I'm sorry sir." Baliff dumbly apologized, but Blackhood knew he didn't mean it.
"Baliff..." Durin sighed. "Look, the only reason why I let you in here was to inform you that you are going to be in a part of our assault.
"Assault?" Baliff was suprised. He knew it had something to do with the Troggs, but he never got any details for the reason of his rank.

"You know that underground complex we've been camping around Baliff?" Durin asked.
"No sir." Baliff replied, standing with Humanlike posture in the presence of his commanding officer.
"It was a DES bunker." Durin stated, expected the suprised look on Shaleskin's face.
"DES?" Baliff was pretictably suprised.
DES standed for 'Dwarven Espionage Society', which was Khaz Modan's secret service. They were since made famous during the late days of the second war, when they sabotaged Orcish oil refineries and ships with their infamous explosives. They also did countless successful recon missions at the battles at Northerend. Now their numbers were few since the order was broken up by undead assasins killing high ranking spymasters. The Goblins also found the intelligence to figure out where the facilities were. After they did that, they either launched a tank blitz, or blew up the facility with sapper raids.
"The Troggs took over that place, they didn't know what it was until it was too late, they just thought it was an ordinary facility, but some of the little devils with enough brains can find out our battle plans. We have been sent to blow that place to kingdom come." Durin was very clear about this.
"Why am I a part of this operation? I'm just a cabin boy." Baliff thought he was correcting his officer, but he was wrong.

"You are going to be a special part of it." Durin wen't to his desk and sat down.
"Special??" Shaleskin wondered out of his mouth.
"Yes, your going to hand out the weapons to people when they leave for the infested place." Durin stated, writing down somthing on his paper with a blue quill.
So this is what he would do. Just hand out the weapons like a town cryer would a leaflet. Dwarves were very stubborn, and Baliff was one of them.
"Um-" Baliff started to say.
"Do you have a problem with that Shaleskin?" Durin asked much too firmly. He stood up from his chair. Baliff couldn't answer yes to this.
"No sir." He responded, almost whispering.
"Good, you'll be cooking breakfast with Ploc Fecit, tommorow." He ordered. "It's very late so get some sleep. Remember to drink an entire canteen of water before you get some shuteye, so your bladder will wake you up." He continued.
"Yes sir." He stepped away from Durin and pulled the flap open to get out of the blue tent.

It was almost pitch black outside, except for the torches lighting the path. He wen't through the tent again where everyone was sleeping. Seth Windwalker's cot took up alot of space in the Dwarf sized tent.
After he stepped into the wagonhe was greeted by Albino-Raven, the guild smith.
"Try not to knock over my iron bars tonight ye-hear?" Albino said scratching his white beard.
"Yeah Mr. Raven." Baliff responed drearily. And Raven wen't back to smacking his hammer against a long iron bar, which he was presumably going to make a sword out of.
He reached for the canteen, it was all the way full. He downed it, and took off his jacket and boots.
Lastly he pressed himself against the wool blanket on the wagon floor, then pulled the other wool blanket over his small, but robust body. Baliff was hoping dearly that Albino would stop clanging his materials so he could get some sleep. He tried to keep his eyes shut.
