Right, gather around all!

Here is the saga of me, Magnus “Boomstick” Gurnisson. I never knew my parents, I was found by a young elven druid, Tyrion Woodshadow, at the battle of Stillwater. I know what you're thinking: "Why would an elf save a wee dwarf", but he did and now I owe him my life! He brought me to a nearby dwarven village, which name I’ve forgotten, where I was given to the town’s engineer, old Gurni Steelbender. He raised me like the father I never had, and treated me as an equal to his own son, Gotrek. I showed more of a knack and interest in engineering, than his own son Gotrek, so I was apprentice by him as soon as I was able to learn and quickly grew fond of experimenting with gunpowder, to much frustration for old Gurni, as I blew the roof of the workshop quite a few times. I still remember the smell of that old workshop. I tell you; nothing smells sweeter in the morning, than freshly made gunpowder. Thusly, when Gotrek took the surname he bears to honour his father, so did I, and we both went our seperate ways out into the world, after giving each other a blood oath that we would help each other in times of need.

Now, old Gurni had only one thing he truly loved; his rifles. He cared for every one of them as they were his children. He seemed to share a bond with all the things he made, a bond I still try to attain with my own creations. It made him a pretty poor engineer though. He would never part with any of the guns he made, and as such didn’t help the war effort much.

My nickname comes from it being the name I gave the first gun he made me. It was lost in battle a long time ago though. To this day I have still to find its equal! Thus, when I left old Gurni behind, and set out for adventure, I took it upon myself to learn all the intricacies of gunsmithing, to make old Gurni as proud of me, as I knew he had to be of his son, who had risen to become a great warrior of much renown. Sometimes I wonder if he still has his old workshop and still continue to make the best rifles in the world.

Anyhow; I traveled to the grand city of Ironforge, since it’s the nexus of dwarven and gnomish civilization, and thus the perfect place to begin my studies in engineering. In Ironforge however, I came upon an idealistic dwarven hunter, Durin Blackhood, who had been charged by the Dwarven King to create a guild of brave souls to help restore the shattered lands of the Alliance to their former glory. I quickly found myself fascinated by this noble quest, and therefore joined with Durin and his guild-in-the-making, The Dwarven Blacksmith. Later Tyrion Woodshadow and my brother Gotrek were also to join the guild, which only strengthened my loyalty towards Durin and his idealsl.

I was later appointed diplomat of the guild, and have since traveled wide and far in the role of ambassador to such glorious guilds as The Crusaders of The Golden Phoenix, The Knights of Valor, Malador and the Empyreal Guard. My journeys, as well as my aforementioned life-debt to an elf, have made me more tolerant than most dwarves when it comes to the different races and their customs. Hell, I can even enjoy a glass of elven wine on occasion, but best not to mention that to my brother. *winks*
