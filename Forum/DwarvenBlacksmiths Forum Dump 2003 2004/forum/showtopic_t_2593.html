Yea, thats right, my critique. 2 nights ago my friend called me up saying this: "Dude, get to my house now. Halo 2. *click*" So off i went, like a lightning bolt to a metal rod. I walked into his house, and what did i see on the TV?

Why, Halo 2 multiplayer action going on, of course!

Word on the street has been a French version of Halo 2 had been leaked, and sure enough, its true. The text is in english, the announcer voice in multiplayer is in english [you know, they guy who says "Running Riot!", but in campaign mode everyone speaks french... which makes understanding the plot a little harder, but whatever, i'm still happy.

NEwhooz, back to my critique:

My younger brother, my friend, and i played multiplayer to get a feel for whats going on. Creating a profile is a little bit more in depth. You can choose your primary color, secondary color, emblem, color of your emblem, background of your emblem, and whether you are an Elite or a Spartan [human]. The controls selection is a little less plentiful [i was a default-jumpy configuration, but you can't use jumpy anymore]. So i just use default all around now. Left-trigger throws grenades, unless you're dual-wielding, then you use your left hand weapon!

Now, they've changed the weapons alot. The pistol is now called the magnum, and it sux nuts. You can't zoom and shoot anymore, it does less damage, but it has 3 more shots per clip... hardly worth it. I've yet to find an assault rifle, but you now start off with the SMG [sub-machine gun]. The Battle Rilfe [the assault rilfe with a scope] fires in small bursts. It fires 4 shots every time you click the trigger, and then stops. It's quite powerful if used correctly. The shotgun is still beefy, and the sniper rilfe seems slightly easier to use, but i'm not sure if its because they changed the physics slightly or the weapon. The rocket launcher fires faster, and can lock-on to vehicles! If you hold the trigger down for a little bit, red dots circle around the target and then stop, singaling that you are locked on.

Covenant weapons have been changed as well. The plasma rifle doesn't slow the target down as much. The Needler is 20x better; it shoots a lot faster, more accurate [homing], but seems to do less damage. I'm not sure how much ammo it holds, but i think its more. The snot rag [plasma pistol] is a great weapon to dual wield with. Nothing is greater than taking out their shields, then hitting them a few times with your gun to kill.

As for the new Covenant weapons... some are awesome, some not so much. They are very situational. The plasma beam [energy sniper rifle] shoots a bolt of purple energy out. Its alright, but i still prefer the human sniper. There's a weapon called the Brute Shot [its the huge weapon in one of the screen shots]. Picture a good grenade launcher and this is basically it. It carries 3 shots in a 'clip' and does considerable amounts of damage, if you can hit. A great thing about it is the way it arcs, allowing you to kill people behind walls. but, the shot doesn't bounce, which i would've prefered. The Energy Sword is awesome. If you lock on to someone, it takes 1 hit to kill, otherwise its about 2 hits [1 to take shields down, 1 to kill]. Its a weapon to be feared by all. If you see someone with this, kill them. Quickly. From far away. another weapon is the Energy Combine [or something like that]. I think this is the covenant version of the Battle Rifle. But it carries 8 shots a clip [i think] and i'm having diffculty killing someone with one clip. But, i'll keep trying for this. I'm going to see what kind of damage it causes to vehicles...

Now, you know the beam the sentinels [the flying bastards with 343 guilty spark] shoot? Well, if you kill one, you can use it! You can also use a fuel rod gun the black grunts drop as well! I'm not sure if you can use them in multiplayer, but i'll give a looksee tonight.

As for vehicles, OMFG! The first thing i did in multiplayers was hop into the back of a warthog, shoot another warthog, and watch it explode! Vehicles take damage specific to wear they are hit. So if you're flying a banshee, and your left wing hits a wall, your left wing will come off. All covenant vehicles have the speed boost function. Even the Wraith [the covenant tank] and the Specter [a 4 person covenant warthog basically]. Banshees can roll left, right, and do a back flip to dodge enemy fire. A draw back now is that you can't press back to hover in space, but it makes it more challenging to fly and do combat, so i enjoy that.

Now, here's what you're probably wonder: What about hijacking a vehicle? Let me tell you, you can hijack ANY vehicle! Thats right, even a banshee, even the wraith! What's awesome is jumping onto a banshee, letting the person fly high into the air, and then ripping them out and taking it over [oh, i've yet to see anyone take falling damage. Which makes me wonder if this version is the actual version, or a leaked earlier version.... but whatever, no falling damage anymore with what i have]. When you hijack the Wraith, you have to punch open the hatch [by pressing B] and then you have two options; punch the person to death, or drop a grenade in! Both options are fun, but i love seeing things explode, so i always choose a grenade!

Alright, this will be a random notes on the game section:

When you play has an Elite, your hud is purple, compared to blue for being Spartan.

There is no longer a health bar. Instead, there's a blue sphere... but i've yet to understand it. I've only played 4 player split screen and you can't really see it. Also, its right above your radar on the bottom left. Which gives the top more room, where they place the two greandes next to each other so you can always see how much of each you have.

They revised two multiplayer levels from the first; Blood Gultch and Battle Creek. They're both bigger, bases are completely remodeled, and are still a load of fun.

The graphics for things have changed. The teleporter looks a lot better, the overshield is now more yellow and slightly smaller, and the terrain looks... smoother i guess. i'm not really sure how to describe it.

There are, what i call, "Gravity Pushes" [actually, elevators, but they push you up, and you can't go back down]. These add a whole to aspect to combat. My brother and i were fighting on the lower level, and i hoped up to recharge my shields. I lobbed a grenade down so he wouldn't come up, and then i hoped off the side back down to that level and shot him in the back.

The "You were killed..." text is now more detailed: "You were slpattered by..." [if you get hit by a car] or "You were assassinated by..." [if you get shot in the back].

Well... thats all for now. I've gotta get to class!
