For many months, Hadir worked peacefully among the dwarves. Endless battles occured between the Alliance and the Horde in Khaz Modan. To the North, the long feared Undead were amassing a huge demontic army to prepare assault upon Azeroth. But within every passing week no invasion set foot into Khaz Modan. It was up to the Dwarven Kingdom to hold off any invasion comming from the North. For the fierce Gnome warriors were also among them. Yet the remaining Human survivors were left alone to govern their own affairs. Dark hours these were for Hadir, though he hoped that he could summon the Night Elves and his kin to fight along the Alliance. But it could not be done. One day, Hadir recieved a letter bearing its way from Teldrassil. It was from the rest of his kin, and thus it read:

"Yet we alone understand that you have left us for many reasons, but you are needed here with haste Hadir. Trouble not yourself with the Undead, for the Alliance can hold them off. You may truimph against the Undead for years, but against the power that now arises here, there can be no victory without you Hadir. The leigions of the Horde have threatened our people in unspeakable ways, controling nearly half of Ashenvale. We cannot hold them off for long without you, for our faith and strenghths are failing, due to the loss of the first world tree. Soon now the Horde will have exterminated our ancient race. We need you here to summon our long-lost forefathers, yet you are the only one that may awaken them from their long slumber.With haste you must return quickly, before we are all overrun. Your very presence give us hope, and without it all is lost. We will await your arrival at Darnassus.

Grieved ths news was to Hadir, but he was greatly needed among the guild against the Undead. He gathered his belongings and prepared to leave. Yet upon his nightsabre he rode out from the gate, and many were puzzled with his unanimous departure.
Later that night, they gathered together to discuss it. "He leaves because to him there is no hope among us against the Undead." one dwarf said. "Say not that, he leaves because he longs to see his homeland again!" another dwarf said. "Nay, he left because we never bought him a beer after he killed that monsterous Abomination!" Then out of the crowd an unseen person said something: "Hadir would never think of such things my lads!" It was Thorin, and he held up a small paper so everyone could see. "He leaves because he must, and he left me a letter with a mug of beer ontop of it." Thus, Thorin read it aloud:

"You may be curious to see why I am gone Thorin, but I am needed more among my kin. My people are dying Thorin, and I am in more need there than here. I know that this guild cannot spare any to help my people, but such matters would hold them to govern their own affairs.Even now I am needed there, so I will say no more. Remember this, when I have rid the Horde out of my ancient homeland, I will return with strenghth and honor to the guild. Farewell Thorin! May the powers of Elune and my Forefathers guide you in the darkest of all your quests!

Thus, I shall return...
