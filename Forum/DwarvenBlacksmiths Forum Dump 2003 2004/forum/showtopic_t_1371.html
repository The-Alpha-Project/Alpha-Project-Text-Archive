Ok, here it goes...


Traveling to Theramore was a bitch and a half.
Going through security, putting up with pushy people. It was so tiresome I needed all the ale in Ironforge to keep me from going crazy.

The way we traveled was taking a Gnomish zepplin.

(All the Gnomes in the tavern smile)

The first one we took was from Elmheart to Stormwind.
It was small so it was very turbulent in the air. It shook and shook, and you know my fear of heights...
Fortunatley the Gnomes were good pilots, and were dedicated to their job.

(The Gnomes in the tavern smile again)

When we arived in Stormwind I knew this was a very populated city. It had good food too. Those sandwiches were worth it.

I met up with three Humans there who were supposed to travel with me.
Thier names were James of Dalaran, Thomas Goldberry, and Sean Dragontail.
James of Dalaran was in the Lorderon army, and the other two were in the Azeroth army.

They towered over me, especially Sean who was 6.8.
All of them were friendly which wasn't a suprise.

We ate lunch, went to the airport and prepared for an eleven hour travel to Theramore.

It wasn't a very comfortable flight. Dwarves and Gnomes were not allowed to ride in first class because they thought they didn't need that much leg room anyway judging by their size.

Who ever thought that up was as dumb as a post, or just a racist. Because I was so fat that the armrests sqeezed the crap out of my lovehandles.

But I wasn't as miserable as the Humans who couldn't get their tree trunk sized legs to fit behind the leather seats. Sean was so miserable, the flight attendents had no choice but to bump him up to first class.

We finally landed after what seemed like a century.

Theramore was nothing like the books described it.
The books said it was a paradise with fantastic beaches, good food and drinks, great inns, and a remarkable economy.

But that was just a myth it seemed like.

The last attack from the Horde changed it to ruin and despair.
The streets were cracked from the seiges, and a memorial was placed in the town square with countless soldiers names on it.

At the mountian they had some of the orchish catapults, axes and other weapons on display.

And at the very top they had a huge golden memorial of Admiral Proudmoore.
This attack must have really put it in ruins.

And I noticed this was the reason I was here.....

For a meeting about what we should do with the horde....

The whole carrage bursted and boomed with peices of feiry wood going everywhere. The leather seats became hot, and the roof of the carrage was gone.

The carrage had exploded with me, my brother, another Dwarf and a Human inside.

I noticed I was on fire. It was so painful to be on fire I don't even want to talk about it.

I jumped out of the burning carriage tripping on a burning peice of wood.
A mage flashed an ice spell on to me and I felt sudden releif.
I wasn't burned completly bad but my leather armor was in crisp.

I saw my brother who had just got cooled down thank the light. But he had bad burns on him and he was wailing in pain.

It was time to use my palidan powers.
I mumbled some words of from the book of the light, and instantly he was healed.
He still had some burns on him but he was healed enough.

He thanked me, but we heard more explosions and we saw five angry people charging at us with swords, bucklers and pitchforks. The resistance was trying to kill us!

My brother pulled his pistol from what was left of his belt, aimed at the mob, and shot a human with a two handed sword in the chest.

But there were still four to go, and it wasn't going to be easy.
I grabbed my little brother by the shirt and pulled him away from the fight. He was reloading even with me pulling him across a bumpy road.

The mob drew closer and my brother shot another one with a shortsword to the ground.

There were still more to kill though, and it wasn't going to be easy.

But then James and Sean came and sliced them like paper. We were saved.

He told me that Thomas was killed in the explosion and James needed a heal because of his back, so I casted holy light on him.

I needed to help others in dire need of healing, so I ran around our convoy searching under wreckages healing whoever I could find while my brother watched my back.

I had healed ten of my comrades, including our captian who was the brains of this operation.

But there was a bad thing, 25 of 39 of us were killed in the explosions, including the other two people inside his carrage.

Time was running out, we were close to the inn that was our destination, and an angry mob with an estimated of about 60 people were charging at us.

We had to move on...

None of the horses were harmed in the explosions, so we restrained the ones who didn't run off, mounted them, and retreated to the inn.

We finally made it there and we told the innkeepers to leave this place, they cooperated without question.

The inn gaurds stayed to protect the place, and two of the people in the inn were hunters, so they took up their bows.

Our forces were bolstered up from 14 people to 19.

We thought this wasn't good enough but the gaurds gave us a box full of crossbows, swords, armor, and even Dwarven hand grenades!

I donned a chain mail shirt that was meant for humans so it was a little to big.
Since I lost my hammer in the explosion I picked up a shortsword.

Lastly I took up a crossbow.

Most of us were on the third floor of the building, aiming down on them, while the rest were outside with chairs and tables for barricades in front of them.

The mob closed in, and we hoped we could last the fight...

We threw a hail of grenades down on them which killed or gravely wounded at least 20 of them. This was a good hit.

The warriors below charged at them armed with swords while we shot at the enemy with crossbows and Dwarven guns.

They broke through the lines and killed only a fair amout of them. But their numbers were few and they could not defeat a mob of this size. It was not long before they had to turn back.

Four out of seven of the warriors down there were killed or being killed down there, including Sean who was hit by two arrows then glutted with a pitchfork.

We threw what was last of the grenades and killed 8 of them and wounded 10 of them, but they kept on charging.

My brother fired a shot and hit one in the stomach. The two hunters fired their arrows with exellent accuracy, hitting both their targets.

James was upstairs by now firing his crossbow and hitting one in the leg.

I fired at one of them with a crossbow of my own. The bolt smacked into him down his shoulder. Another hail of arrows and bolts came out of our windows and another 10 were dead or dying.

The mob started to have second thoughts now. Except they weren't even a mob anymore.

There were only three left...

They dropped their weapons and sprinted madly back where they came from.

We all let out a deafening cheer.
