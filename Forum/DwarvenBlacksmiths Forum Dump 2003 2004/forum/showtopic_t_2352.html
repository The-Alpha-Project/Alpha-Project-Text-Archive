Sit down lads, sit down. Grab a mug of ale. Hmm, where to begin...

I grew up in the outskirts of Ironforge, together with me mom. Never knew me father, but I'm pretty sure he was a great man. I used to have a brother, but he grew up, y'know... He left home at a early age, seeking gold and glory for our family name. Don't know if he ever accomplished anythin'... He never returned. I often se Mom starin' of into the distance, looking for my brother. Borgan, was his name.
Anyways, life in Ironforge was boring. I often wandered into the streets, jus' seeking exitement. Then, one day, I came by the local blacksmith. I don't know what happened that day, but I... Let's say I fell in love with the metal. I jus'... Couldn't help staring on the metal... Feeling the powerfull hammer forming a sharp sword or a piercing arrow.
Some days later I pulled meself together and started a conversation with the blacksmith. Yeah, he even learned me how to use a hammer. I went to see him often, and I learned much from the old fella...

Yeah, I learned much of the old fella... Thanks to him, I am what I am today. Cheers for tha ol' blacksmith!

But then, the old blacksmith retired. I was given the blacksmith, 'cause the blacksmith had no sons. I spent most of my youth there. I also witnessed the hard work of bein' a blacksmith... Working day øn night to fill quotas 'n buying the raw metal. Specially the last caused me problems, as the miners often overpriced the wares, rippin' me of... So I decided to close the shack for a while.

That's when I started my second proffession... Another round of ale for everyone! Let's bring out a cheers for the mines of Ironforge!

That was the fist time I visited the mines of Ironforge! With only a few gold pieces in my hands, I bought a old pick from a retired miner. As I walked into the dark pits of the mines of Ironforge, my eyes expanded, and they began glowing in the flacking light of the torches. My dwarven eyes had finally come to a use.
Full of determination, I spend the rest of the day in the mines. When I came back to the surface, my face was black but my bag was full. I went back to the smith where my dealers were peeking through my windows to spot me. I grabbed their shoulders from behind and pulled them away. This would be the last time they sold me overpriced raw materials.

Yeah, those scandrels would do anythin' for a few gold pieces. Another round of ale for everyone!

After that day, me shop grew and grew, and eventually became the richest in the neighbourhood. Soon I had to expand to follow the succes up. I hired two apprentices. Brothers they were, Aggron and Bogor. They learned quickly, and they were good. Together we forged many sturdy tools and deadly weapons. Yeah, I remember every single thing we made. Though we still kept making tools, our specialty became weapons. Mostly axes 'n hammers, as the dwarven tradition is. Our greatest work has a mighty battleaxe. We called it "Skullcleaver". Not a very original name, but believe me, it certainly could cleave skulls.

That was a mighty weapon, indeed. And it got to taste blood, lads... Let's bring out a cheers for tha Skullcleaver!

When the guards spotted the first wave of orcs, I volunteered to the front line. With me, I brought Skullcleaver and a hateful glimpse in my eyes. I was there as dwarves and orcs first met face to face. It was a bloody battle, indeed. Skullcleaver cut many orc necks that day, and the dwarves certainly taught the orcs a important lesson. No orc is ever gonna' beat the dwarves in their own home. Ironforge has never been taken, and it never will!

Another round of ale for everyone! I'm gettin' thirsty of all this talkin'. Anyways...

When the first war raged the lands, business skyrocketed. The need for weapons and armor had never been bigger, and people would pay anythin' for a stout axe and a sturdy shield. After the first war, my blacksmith was well known in many parts of Ironforge. Even the thane came by me shop once in a while... He showed much interrest in my art, and often he stayed the night to watch me work. Then, one day, I showed him Skullcleaver. He instantly asked me of the price, and I replied that it was not for sale. He continued to ask me, for it was a mighty weapon. At last, I gave up and gave him a tremendous price, hoping he would give up on the matter. But he instantly accepted and told one of his men to get the gold from his treasure chamber. Surprised of his reaction, I tried to convince him that it wasn't worth it, but he just laughed and gave me the gold. A bit confused, I handed over Skullcleaver. He thanked me and rushed back to his fortress.

Well, I don't know I should've sold Skullcleaver to tha Thane. He is a fine man, no offense, no offense. But I'm just not sure if he understands how to wield a weapon like that. Anyways, let's bring out a cheers for the Thane!
Hey, what, you gettin' tired already?! Stop yawning you ogreson!
HEAR ME OUT! I'm endin' me story soon y'know.

With the fortune the thane had payed me, people thought I'd expand the shop, but to their big surprise, I closed down. With tons of customers flockin' outside the locked door, wonderin' why I would shut down the big succes the blacksmith had become. But I... Got tired of it. It became too unpersonal. With only my axe and hammer, and my blacksmithing equipment, I traveled through Ironforge, sharin' my experience with all blacksmiths that crossed me path and tasting the fine ales of the dwarven kingdom. As I sat in a tavern, drinking my sixth mug of ale, I saw a dwarf with a black cape hangin' from his shoulders talking to the bartender. I asked the bartender who the hooded person would be. He told me that his name was Durin Blackhood, leader of a alliance of dwarven and non-dwarven members that called themselves "The Dwarven Blacksmith". I pulled myself together and approached Durin about the blacksmiths. We talked for hours over several glasses of ale, and later that night, I applied for membership of the Dwarven Blacksmiths.

You know the rest lads! Thanks for hearin' me out. Bartender! Another round of ale for everyone. Me throut is dryin' out.
