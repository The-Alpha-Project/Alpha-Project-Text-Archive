Kalena looked into the full length bronze mirror admiring the robe her Aunt had made for her. Aunt Kzana knew the secrets of weaving magic thread into wonderful robes and garments. Many folk came to Kzana for her magic garments, elfs, gnomes, and men. The robe fitted nicely upon her shoulders. The jade neckless around her neck brought out the color of her green eyes. For a dwarf female she is a bit shorter then most and not as stocky. Her fiqure kept slim and agile from the hours of sports and dueling practice with the other girls of Ironforge. Kalena did spend most of her free time sitting by the founatain reading her scrolls. Scrolls about magic, histories of the different peoples, scrolls about wonderful and dreadful places. It has been her favorite place to come and sit since that day her parents departed leaving her in the care of her mothers oldest sister Kzana.
After the war her parents took up the task of exploring the world looking for any traces of other dwarfs or their histories. On this last trip they had joined a large party lead by a great explorer from the noble house of the Thane. In the months that followed Ironforge would receive word of the parties progress and adventures. Then the messages became less and less untill the messages stopped altogether. It has been so long ago...Her Aunt had since then been teaching Kalena the ways of the healer and the secrets of magic tailoring.
Kalena adjusted the silk belt tightly around her waist. Only one more month and I shall come of age, then I can explore and travel the world too...Kalena stepped into the silken boots, a good fit she thought.
Weeks ago Kalena and Kzana discussed her desire to travel and see the world. " You must seek out the company of a strong guild, they will protect you and you them. Your experience with the healing arts will grow as will as your reputation and wealth." "I know a excellent company, I will speak to its Leader for you." Kalena pulled the cloak over her head and shoulders making minor adjustments to insure it fitted properly. Just this morning her Aunt had told her, " Lucky girl the Leader of the party that I spoke of has agreed to see you". "When ?" Kalena asked. "This night" her Aunt replied. Go in your room and put on the robe I made for you ...and wear the cloak!
Kalena had did as her Aunt instructed her to do, gazing into the mirror Kalena thought of her parents, somewere out there, maybe I can find them, maybe they are....Kalena! Come Out here! Now Girl! The excitement in her Aunts voice startled her, turning quickly she ran into the corner post of her oaken bed knocking the wind out of her . She continued at a sprint through the silk curtains into the main chamber slamming into a heavy metal something....the metal did not yield to Kalena knocking her to the floor on her bottom. Dazed and feeling a bit sick, huge hands reached out and pulled her to her feet in one smooth effortless motion. Her eyes still had dark spots dancing in front of them. But she had enough vision to see that the hands belong to the largest Dwarf she had ever seen.The stern look on his face told her he would tolerate no foolishness and fear begin to replace the pain Kalena felt.
He wore armor that had seen many battles. His battle axe which leaned against the nearby table had no doubt cleaved many a head. For it to was worned and notched. On his back was slung a great shield of wood and metal. This was the unyielding metal that Kalena in her rush had slammed into. Fool girl! her Aunt said. "meet the Lord and Master of The Dwarven Blacksmiths" (to be continued)
