The Troll Hunts



Well, after my father had died (to understand what had happened please read Eugene Kuzmin’s bio in the bio part of the forum), I said I spent my days hunting Trolls, well now the saga of The Troll Hunts shall unfold. Most stories shall be mine, but some may come from others who post on this thread.



The Teaching of the Hunts.

After my father died I went home and mourned for a few days, but then after 2 days I made a vow, that I would avenge my father, and hunt trolls for the rest of my days. I learned with the local engineer for a while, and when I created my first rifles, I would practice with them a lot. Then after 4 months, I finally decided to leave my village and go learn the ways of the hunter. I took up training with a Hunter by the name of Marazar Flintlock. He was rumored to be the best hunter in 200 miles of anywhere near his village. There were many who had tried to become his apprentice, but he did not choose anyone because he did not believe they had any potential. But as soon as I had entered his lodge, he saw something promising in me, even though I thought there was no hope of me becoming good at hunting.
“What is your name lad?” asked Marazar.
“Eugene, sir.” I answered.
“Would you like to become a Hunter?” he asked, “I think you would do very good at it, seeing you have a pretty good rifle there.” He said pointing at my favorite gun, “Who did you get it from?”
“I made it myself, I was the apprentice of engineer Beryl.”
“Good. Engineers are very successful at hunting.”
I was accepted as Marazar’s apprentice hunter. On my first day, he woke me up by pouring very cold water on my back. I woke up instantly and started yelling. When I asked him why he did it, he answered that I overslept and it was already 5:24 AM. He told me a good hunter must always wake up with the sun, and it came up 10 minutes ago. From that point I knew my training was going to be very hard, and I had to keep up with the training to avenge my father.
“First things first,” he said, “you must learn how to track animals. To track an animal it requires a lot of skill and quite a bit of will power. First find the tracks of an animal. Then you must analyze it. Then draw a good sketch of it in your notebook. You do have a notebook, don’t you?”
“Um…”
“I thought so. You must now remember this lesson today, because I will not tell you how to do it again. Here is a sheet of paper to draw the track on.” He gave me a sheet of paper, and I drew a good sketch of the print. “That is a rabbit print. Write rabbit on the top of your parchment. Now you must follow it. Tell me when you find the animal, or when the tracks are crossing paths with another animal’s.” He lay down on his hammock, and seemed to fall asleep.
It took me a half-hour to find other tracks similar to the rabbit’s tracks, and they were intertwining, so I lost which one was the rabbit’s. Then I realized I did not know which way I had to go to get back. As soon as I got to the crossroad of the tracks, I stopped. I did not remember which one was the rabbit’s. I spent an hour panicking, but then I realized that Marazar had intended this to happen. I studied the tracks, and found that the new tracks were a bit shorter than the original ones I was following, and it was the same with, I figured out the answer to the problem, and started moving back to the hunter. Then I thought of drawing the other tracks on the other side of the paper. Then I walked back to the lodge.
“What took you so long?” asked the hunter. “I was about to open a new opening for a new apprentice.”
“I found similar tracks intertwining with the ones I was following, but then got lost, and forgot which tracks were the originals. But then I figured it out. Could you tell me whose tracks these are?” I asked and showed him the drawing.
“That is a crossbreed of a rabbit and cat. Those are quite rare, but they are not good for anything really, and being crossbreeds, they can not reproduce.” Answered Marazar. The rest of the week he taught me of animal droppings, age of footprints, and other ways of animal tracking. I had learned many different tracks of animals.
On the second week, I was falling apart, because of only 6-hour sleeps, and an hour-long break from noon. That week I tracked a cat, and Marazar followed me. When I found the cat, it was injured, and it looked like it was about to attack. My trainer finally let on one of the secrets of using spirit force. He taught me how to sooth creatures. As soon as I soothed the cat, I mended its broken paw, and I cleaned its cuts and other injuries.
“Now you must learn the most important ability that hunters use. It is the power to tame wild animals. It requires a lot of skill, willpower, and spirit. You must concentrate on the animal, and then try and eliminate all uncertainties, and doubts about you from the animal’s minds. Then the creature will be tamed by you, and will only listen to your commands. But be cautious, if an animal that is tamed by you sees that you tame another animal, it will become jealous and will run away, never to return. Beware of that last thing that I have told you.”
I concentrated on the cat, and I felt compassion towards me from the cat. My mind felt as one with the cat, and I felt the cautiousness and doubt, and then pushed it all away. Then the cat was tame. For a few more months I learned how to manage my pet, and other hunter abilities. Then Marazar told me that I have learned all, and completed all of my training but 1 thing. I had to live by myself, hunting, living with nature, using my skills together at once, and to kill a troll, and bring back its skull. That one month would be called my first hunt.
The next day I set out, with only my one best/favorite rifle, the cloths on my back, ammunition, a pack, and a wooden hammer since some things might get too close. And I also had my cat. At 5 in the morning I set out on my first hunt.

Here is my second story.

The The First Hunt


As I left the home of Marazar, I went into the forest, and found a nice meadow to sleep in. I slept for 10 hours straight. I had not had such a great sleep in a long while. As soon as I was up, I was feeling hungry. I trapped 3 rabbits, and cooked them on a fire. There was enough firewood around, since the last rain was a month ago. I then needed to find shelter. I found a pretty good cave, and it was very defendable. It had a narrow entrance, so then only one person could go in at a time, or I could just seal it with a boulder. Then it widened out into a huge cavern that had a small lake in it, and it was rich with fish. There was also a hole at the bottom of the lake, through which I went, and discovered that it went to another lake on top of a small mountain. There were many air pockets along the way, so I was able to get there easily. I went back into the cavern, and started to stockpile firewood, just incase so that if it rained I would have a fire in my cave. Then I went to bed.
I woke with the sun, again at 5, and then started to make traps to catch animals. I then went and made a fishing pole, and took some of the string that I had with me, and I also made a hook from some pieces of wood. The fish were stupid, since they have never been caught, and I had caught 30 in an hour, but then I ran out of worms. I then fashioned a few barrels out of hollow stumps. I then also made some lids, and put the fish and some water in to them. The fish were still alive of course, because then they would be fresh when I would take them out and cook them. I also caught 5 rabbits, 2 squirrels, and a wild turkey in my traps. I also shot a male deer. I already had enough meat for weeks, and it was only my second day. I ate some venison, 2 fish, and the turkey. Then I went to bed. I smoked the meat, so it would last longer, and then I went outside. I set up my traps, and got some more worms. I fished for an hour and caught another 40 fish, and decided that it was enough. I then went looking for berries and other edibles.
When I spotted a raspberry bush, I started gathering, but then I noticed troll tracks. A lot of troll tracks. They were 6 days old though. I guessed a patrol came through the woods days before I got there. I then started to gather wood and mine some raw minerals to make weapons. I then brought in a boulder, that I would use as a rock to pound on. I also constructed a crappy, but workable bellows. I started making a knife for cutting wood, and I also made a small dagger, just in case. Then I tried to make a hammer, but it was unbalanced, and lopsided. I then made some modifications to my rifle for troll killing. I then made booby traps in the cave, some that I activate, and some that are self-activating. I then quickly went and removed my traps for animals. I had caught another 8 rabbits, and 3 wild turkeys. I then brought the meat to my cave. After that I went and shot 3 deer, and dragged that in to my cave. Finally I went to gather berries again. I gathered about 10 pounds of blueberries, 5 pounds of raspberries, and 2 pounds of strawberries. I decided that I had enough food to last me a long while. When I got back to the cave and put the food into its barrels, I heard something outside. I looked and saw an orc. I shot it, but missed his head. It hit him in the axe arm fortunately. I then charged him with my dagger, and slit his throat. Then I burned his body, and took his axe as a souvenir. I then checked my inventory to make sure there was enough for a siege. There were 4 barrels of smoked venison, 1 barrel of smoked meat from little creatures that I caught in my traps, a barrel filled with 17 pounds of berries, and 13 barrels with 5 fish in each. I also had about 325 pounds of firewood. I was set.
I then went outside, and then chipped a boulder to fit the size of my cave entrance. I did not want a party of 20 trolls to attack me, because then the chances were against my winning, no matter how good I use the rifle and dagger. I then went and scouted 3 miles in radius from the entrance. There was one set of tracks, troll tracks and they were not that old. The troll passed by here 3 hours ago. I then took my rifle into my hands, checked the ammo, and then secured my wooden hammer and dagger. I followed the troll tracks, and then saw that they met up with human armor tracks. The armor tracks seemed to be 20 minutes passing, so I picked up my pace. I caught up to the humans, and then they told me that an army was passing through the forest sent by the horde. I told them that I saw tracks from about 20 trolls in the forest, which were 7 or 8 days old. The humans thought that it was a squad of trolls and went to find the army and figure out how long it would take it to get to Lordaeron. I went and followed the toll that I was tracking previously.
After about 6 hours of quiet walking and running I finally spotted the troll. It was a big one. It was about 8 feet high. I was only a 4-foot tall dwarf, half his size. I took out my rifle, and shot his right shoulder, and then his left foot. It then charged at me, swung its mighty axe, and then broke my rifle. I could easily fix it, but not during a fight. I took out my hammer and started beating the troll with it. I think I broke its other leg, and it started to fall yelling in pain. It was down, but not yet defenseless. It threw a few small axes at me, but missed. Then I charged at it with my hammer, but then the troll threw its axe at me, and it cut my shoulder. I yelled out in pain, and then took out my dagger, and jumped on the troll’s back. It tried throwing me off, but failed. I then slit its throat, and it died. I then burned the skin from its head, and had the skull.
I fixed my gun, and my wounds healed. I spent the rest of the month resting in my cave, and hunting, and fishing. Once I returned to Marazar’s lodge, I showed him the skull, and the orc’s axe. Marazar said I had finally completed his course, and he now was proud to call me his colleague. I was finally a hunter! With my first hunt over, I spent a while planning expeditions and different ways of killing trolls. The skull of the troll I killed still hangs on the wall of my house.

Here is my third story.


DeadEye


With my first hunt over, I planned an expedition to the southern parts of Khaz Modan. First I would need to travel to the great city of Ironforge. It took Marazar and me 6 days to get to Ironforge. When we got there, I was surprised at the grandness of it. On the outskirts of the city, I was already seeing more towns, and less forest. After passing through the great gates, I saw many dwarves walking around, doing their daily business. I expected them to be much more pampered than us village folk, but I was wrong. I got into a bar brawl in the first 5 minutes entering it. I won of course, but the city dwarves were much tougher than I thought. One of them nearly broke my arm. After getting kicked out of the bar, I headed to the weapon smith. I still had a great judgement of hammers since I used to be a paladin, and the hammers the smith made were better than any I had ever seen. I then went to visit the shooting range.
The champion was a dwarf with gray, but combed hair. His beard was very long, so I expected him to be fairly old, and proud. His nickname was DeadEye. I did not get his real name, but I assumed someone with such a nickname, would be a worthy opponent. I walked up to him and asked:
“So, you’re the best shooter in Ironforge?”
“What if I am,” answered DeadEye, “wild-man?”
“I challenge you to shoot-off. Whoever wins, is then titled to be the greatest marksman in Ironforge, and all of Khaz Modan.”
“I already have that title, seeing that nearly every good hunter has challenged me.”
“But the name cant be official, if you have not out-shot everyone, can it?”
“Alright, villager. I accept your challenge. We will have a contest in 3 fields. Number one: still targets. Number 2: moving targets. Number 3: accuracy on a combination of moving and still targets. Take out your rifle.”
I took out my favorite rifle.
“You call that piece of shit a rifle?” asked DeadEye.
“Shut up, and lets start.”
DeadEye took out his rifle, and it was all shiny, and only made of metal. It also had a scope. DeadEye and I entered a large sealed area, and the gates shut behind us. I looked around. It was a circular area, with some rocks and dwarf-made bushes and trees. There were also some holes in the ground. I guessed that was where the targets would come from.
There was also a counter at the top. There was a gnome on it, to change the score. 3-2-1 GO! Targets started to spring out of the ground. They were fairly good drawings of deer, and rabbits. I shot a lot, but the score was Eugene 37, DeadEye 43.
“Ha-ha-ha. That was easy. I didn’t even use my scope!” gloated DeadEye.
“You know just as I do, that the scope would have worsened your score, because it would have slowed you down.”
“You are not the primitive villager I thought you were.”
“And you are not the pampered city dweller I expected you to be.”
Then we entered a second room, just like the first. When we started, I noticed that the system was primitive. The drawings moved back and forth, side to side, or in circles. I shot, not even trying, and beat DeadEye. The score was Eugene 48, DeadEye 32. His followers were dismayed.
“You must have cheated somehow!” Yelled DeadEye in anger.
“It is just that you have not been hunting real animals as long as I have.” I answered.
“I have been hunting for centuries, but have been confined in the city for the past few decades. How I wish to escape the city.”
I made no comment, and we entered the last room. It was similar, but it was twice as big. The last contest started, and the system was much better. Robots replaced the drawings, and they used basic animal instincts. I believe it was due to the gnomes’ ingenuity. It took a long while, but we finally finished off the last of the moving and still targets. The score was 75 to 75. In the end the scores added up to Eugene 160, DeadEye 150. I won the tournament.
“No. I could not have lost to a villager!” cried out DeadEye. “Now I will have no life in this city.
“I have a proposition,” I told him, “Marazar the hunter, and I are going to do a little exploring, and we need more people for a group expedition. Would you like to join us?”
“I would be able to hunt and see the glorious outdoors again?”
“Of course. So is that a yes?”
“Hell yeah.”
Marazar, DeadEye, and I then left the city with the supplies we bought. We were ready to move on to the southern villages, and recruit some more people. Then, we would go on the expedition. I will avenge my father by killing as many trolls as I can.

The Dwarven Blacksmiths


As Marazar, Deadeye, and I were traveling south, we spotted some troll tracks. I started to follow them without saying anything to my comrades, and they followed. Deadeye asked why we started to track the trolls, and then Marazar explained my hate for trolls. We followed the tracks for quite a while, and finally we reached the camp by nightfall. There were 18 trolls camping near some dwarven fort. The three of us aimed, and then fired. We each shot one through the head, and started to reload. Then we heard shots being fired from all around, and 13 more trolls were dead. I charged at one of the trolls, and with my trusty dagger, stabbed it straight into the heart. I then finished him off with a blow to the head, from my hammer. Another dwarf leaped out of the bushes, and quickly slew the remaining troll.
“Who are you, and what are you doing here?” asked the dwarf. 12 other dwarves stepped out of the woods, holding my friends as captives.
“We are not here to cause trouble. We are just hunting trolls, and did not mean to intrude on to your land. What is this outpost called?” I answered, and asked.
“We are sorry friend dwarf.” Answered the dwarf, releasing my friends. “This is a new guild called the Dwarven Blacksmiths, the smiths of eternity. I am the leader, Durin Blackhood. This is a guild, which was requested to be created by King Magni Bronzebeard himself! The army going through the forests sent these trolls out to try and destroy our outpost. We have slain many troll and orc scouts. It seems that this is the southernmost outpost with contacts to King Magni. Would you like to stay with us for a while?”
“Of course, Durin. Thank you.”
As we walked into the outpost, I noticed the magnificent craftsmanship of the fort, and when I saw the weapons, I nearly fainted. I had never seen such greatly crafted guns and hammers. They were truly the smiths of eternity. After we had dinner, and had some great ale, Durin and I had a discussion.
“As you can see, we are still a small guild. We still need many members. You fight well with both hammer, and rifle. We would like to have you and your comrades to join us. Because if this outpost falls, then the horde’s invasion will begin, and then many of the forts and towns would fall, without any strong resistance. You know that the horde is great in numbers. The soldier quality may be crappy, but the amount of them, will surely conquer the southern parts of Khaz Modan, if not all of it. Stormwind has already fallen, and we are next. Will you please join us?”
“I would, but I have a personal Quest going on. Since my father was killed in the Old Quarry Battle *, I have sworn to kill as many of those trolls as possible. I used to be a paladin, which explains my hammer skills. Then I became a hunter, since that would be the best for a loner. I am not yet ready to accept myself into a guild. But I may in the future. Please remember me until I do.”
“So you are Eugene, the Troll Slayer?” asked Durin.
“I guess, but I did not know that I was given a title for that battle. I thought I had only gotten a medal for bravery, which I did not accept, since I was blind with rage.” I answered.
“All the dwarves that participated in that battle were given medals. But you were the one who killed the most trolls in the battle, and saved many a dwarf. Everyone in Khaz Modan knows who you are. But then you disappeared for a long time, and no one knew where you went. I hope you know that I will have to report to King Bronzebeard that you were here. But do not worry. I just have to alert the king, when a hero is seen. I will not make you do anything you do not wish to do. This might even get the guild some publicity, learning that this was the first place that you made yourself known. Also I see you are traveling with Deadeye, and Marazar. How did this happen?”
“Marazar taught me the ways of the hunter, and now is my colleague. When I was in Ironforge, I got into a contest with Deadeye and beat him. Are the both of them known around here too?”
“Why yes! Well, I do not know if you or your friends will be famous that long, this will still be very good publicity! I have had only 13 other members for 16 years now. This will surely reinforce the guild! Please do not think that I am only concerned about publicity. I just need more soldiers here. It is not going well. We lost one dwarf about a week ago, and morale is low. A bunch of reinforcements would do the dwarves well. I will make sure that you get as many supplies as you need, and if you do change your mind, my guild is always open to you.”
The next morning we got more supplies, and started leaving the guild. Then one of the dwarves ran to us, and gave me an upgrade on my rifle, I thanked him and we moved on.
“Farewell, Deadeye, Marazar, and Eugene!” called out Durin.
“Farewell Durin and Dwarven Blacksmiths! I hope to see you again!”
Our little group moved on, to the south and beyond.




* The Old Quarry Battle, was a battle in some old quarry between 400 trolls, and 100 dwarves. That is when my father was slain by a troll. It was the largest amount of trolls that was seen not with the main hordes. (refer to my first bio)
