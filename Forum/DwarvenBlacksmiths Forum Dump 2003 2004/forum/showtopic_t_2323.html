Unsure of his name, the young dwarf wandered from town to town, looking for a new life. It had been weeks since he had left the human town to the south, and he was hungry. No matter what though, he would not return to the horrors he found there...

He could barely remember who had found him in that alley, trying to stay alive. He only remembered that she was nice and protected him from the guards who frequently tried to hurt him. They soon became friends and vowed to always help each other. She taught him how to defend himself with daggers, and with his bare hands. He was even learning how to throw a knife!

Since she also did not remember her name, he decided to call her Maria. Maria was a human who also had a mysterious past, but she did not care that he was a dwarf. Maria was also teaching him how to make explosives from salvaged goblin land mines. He soon learned how to make bombs and used them to kill deer and rabbits for food. One day on his usual hunting trip, he found Maria dead at their home. He went into a rage and promised Maria that she would be avenged.

The local tavern always had rumors going from person to person. It was a great place if you had a problem you wanted someone to eliminate. Everyone there hated him, as he was a dwarf, and they were humans. He always made sure that he kept several knives with him and a bomb or two whenever he came here. Just last week he had killed a guy who advanced on him with a sword. The poor drunk didn't even have a chance.

Today everyone was talking about a new guy in town. They only knew him as Shadowtarrasque. He was rumored to have come from Stormwind. He was forced to run here after he had killed a guard and tried to murder the king! Everyone was talking about how he had disguised himself as a sheep and blew the brains out of the guard with a large gun hidden in his mouth. An expert assassin with reason to kill anyone who came near him without a large sack of gold.

The tavern drunks greeted our dwarf hero with the usual grunts and taunts. Luckily, he had enough money to get them interested in helping him find Maria's killer. A large gruff man with wild black hair and a beard came up to him. Although he wasn't used to someone so large to be friendly, he decided to buy him a drink. The large man said "Thanks" and drank deeply from the mug. When he had finished the beer, he turned to the dwarf and said "Bit unusual to see a dwarf in a human town. What can I do for you?"

"I need some help to find my friends killer." The dwarf replied. "I'll be willing to pay you if you can help me."

"That depends on who killed your friend." said the man. "Some people around here I wouldn't go near unless you paid me a hundred gold." The news shocked our hero who had been hoping for someone a little less expensive. Perhaps it would not cost that much to find Maria's murderer.

"I might not have enough money if you have to go near someone that dangerous," said the dwarf, "But maybe you won't have to. Maybe it was just a group of drunks who decided to kill her because she helped me instead of trying to kill me."

The man agreed and told him he would try to find Maria's murderer. Our hero went home to bury Maria and sharpen his knives. He decided to go to the marketplace to see if he could find some gun powder or other explosives so that he could make some new bombs. You never knew when some gang would try to kill you, and it always helped to have a couple mines around in case they were stupid enough to come near him...

The dwarf also decided to buy some bags and wood so that he could sell some food at the market. He also decided that he needed a gun so that he could make his own weapons instead of having to buy knives. He was able to catch several deer and rabbits, and also helped out a friendly farmer who lived near the woods by killing a large wolf who was killing all of his sheep. The farmer thanked him and gave him some money. He also decided to reward our hero by taking him on a fishing trip and teaching him how to fish. "If you know how to fish, you'll never go hungry as long as you're near water," he said.

Our hero thanked him and found out he had enough money for some mage leather bags! He sold his food and bought some of the large bags that could allow him to make even more money than usual. He also bought a small pistol. There were also some friendly traveling engineers in town who taught him how to make his own bullets for his gun. He also found a group who agreed to teach him how to mine if he gave them a couple bombs. He could finally mine his own materials!

A couple days later, our hero decided to go look for that man who told him he would search for his friends killer. It didn't take him long to find him with his head blown off and a message written on the ground. The killer used the man's blood to write the words "I know you are searching for me. I'll give you this warning only once. Try to find me at your own risk. I'll be waiting for you foolish dwarf..."

Next to the body was a name our hero had come to recognize. His blood turned to ice as he saw the name. He soon learned that he would have to learn how to make himself invisible if he was to avenge Maria.

The next day he sold his pistol and bought a rifle that he had made a scope for. He also kept several bullets with him so that he could kill people from a hundred feet away. He would need this if he was to ever kill the man who had murdered his friend. He taught himself how to blend in with the area around him, until he was nearly invisible. He soon found work as a spy and a scout for a party that decided to stop by town for some food and rest. They agreed to take them with him if he learned how to make himself even harder to see. They took him to their night elf friend known as Blood Killer. He taught the dwarf how to blend in with the shadows and be completely invisible at night.

"Be one with the darkness," Blood Killer said, "You are invisible and the creatures around you are all prey." He pointed to a squirrel in the distance. "Look in that direction and then close your eyes. Let me know what you see."

The dwarf did as he was told and said "There is a squirrel in the distance master."

"Kill it my apprentice, but do not open your eyes."

A shot rang out and smoke poured from the gun as the dwarf fired. Two hundred feet in the distance, a squirrel found that it was dead after a bullet flew through its neck.

"You have come far my apprentice, and there is nothing more I can teach you. Travel on your own and search for your friends killer. Keep your eyes open and do not rest until you have found him. He will have known how far you have come so you must strike quickly before he can prepare." At these words, Blood Killer faded from sight and left our hero on his own...

"The dwarf has come far," said the killer, "I wonder how far he will get after he tries to kill me." Miles away from our hero, the assassin readied his fort for battle...

Our hero knew that he had come far enough for people to merely know him as a name, and not as a dwarf. He decided he would take Maria's killer's name and change it to suit himself. Right after he thought this, he set off through the woods with plans of revenge...

It turns out he traveled a couple miles before he fell down the trap. A large tunnel that he did not see which led to a dungeon. At least he had a couple bombs to blow up the cell door. The loud noise alerted the guards to him. They barely got two feet before they were both killed. The dwarf ran through the dungeons searching for the man he had only seen once, the man who was rumored to be the bandit lord of these woods.

Up the dungeon stairs, through a hallway, and past a large door that forced the dwarf to use another of his bombs, our hero made his way to the very heart of the fort killing everyone who got in his way. He prepared his body for a battle he knew would be nearly impossible to win. He had recently made an innocent looking mechanical squirrel that he hoped would win the battle for him. He had learned long ago that underestimation often led crime lords to their doom. This bandit was the best crime lord in the whole continent. Our hero would not let that stop him though. He had made a promise to Maria and he would keep it, even if he died trying to avenge her. At last the bandit lord's door was in sight. It only took a couple bombs to destroy the lock and allow our hero to face his greatest fear...

"Welcome dwarf. I have been expecting you. Did you ever think you could enter my fort without me knowing it? Did you ever think you had a chance to kill me? ANSWER ME!"

He was even more powerful than our hero had thought. Armed with two swords, a knife, and a pistol, Shadowtarrasque stood at the top of some stairs put in for the effect of making his enemies cower before him. Our hero would not answer this evil man who had killed his friend. Instead he let out a scream of rage and charged.

The bandit drew his swords as our hero pulled out his knives. The bandit swung one sword, while he stabbed with another. The dwarf was agile enough to dodge both attacks while he threw his last bomb and his knives. The bomb missed by twenty feet, and one knife flew harmlessly through the air past the bandit. Another knife, however, was able to hit Shadowtarrasque in the arm. He reacted quickly by throwing a handful of darts at our hero. Two hits, and from the looks of it, all of the darts were poisoned. Grey fog seemed to flow through the air as our hero fought the poison. He drank an antidote and fired the last of his shots at random. Shadowtarrasque was only hit by one bullet as it dug into his leg. Another one broke one of his swords in two. This did not stop him, as he saw his opponent had no more weapons.

"Prepare to die foolish dwarf." said Shadowtarrasque. He lifted his sword, preparing to bring it down with all of his strength, but he saw something he did not expect to see walking towards him. "So the foolish dwarf had a little friend with him did he? I see you built him very well. I think I'll sell him in town for a couple hundred gold."

Shadowtarrasque leaned over to pick up our hero's mechanical squirrel, but it ran away from him. He dashed after it, but it hid under a chair. The squirrel was fast enough to let our hero hide behind Shadowtarrasque's large chair, just as Shadowtarrasque finally grabbed the squirrel. The blast was so loud that our hero had trouble hearing for hours later...

Our hero found the spot where Shadowtarrasque had been. The area was just a crater as he reached it. A very big crater. As our hero fell to the floor, he said "I have avenged Maria with your help, my friend. Good bye Number 7."

Two days later, ShadowT7 once again left town as he set out for the dwarven city of Ironforge, and a new life...
