Part 1: Valnirs Quest.

Valnir had six brothers and one sister, as such his family was judged to be relatively small. His eldest brother, Rodrigue, had ridden out, accomplised al sorts of valiant deeds, including slaying a Writhing Wyrm of Rotherham, which had earned him the title Knight of the Realm, his own domain in verdant pasturelands by the river Grismerie, and a smiling blondhaired wife of inpressive assets. The two next olest brothers had ridden off to prove their worth as Knight Errant, and where currenlt conquering the hearts of maidens the length and breadth of Agila Torith. Travellers constantly brought back news of the two brother’s adventures. And as the minstrels sang tales of their exploids, Thibault watched the proud faces of his parents with a sinking heart.

And then there was Girauld, Valnirs older by a year. With is curling fair hair, his good looks, his skill at arms and his personal charisma, he was his parents golden son. Girauld was gifted with the best weapons, speachially made armor and the best grey colt to be his warhorse. On the day that Girauld left home, a huge crowd gathered to see him off. As he watched his brother supr his prancing horse around in circles, Valnir wondered if he where the only person who wouldn’t be sorry to see him go, then felt guildt when he saw the tears running down his mothers face.

For the first few months, news drifted back slowly: Girauld had defeated some goblins, rescued the odd maiden, hunted down a rampaging beast or two. Nothing spectacular really, but enough to keep his parents spirit up. Then Nothing. No news for months and months. Valnirs mother became more and more worried, though his father remained stoical. Valnir, in the meantime flourished in his brothers absence, and concentrated on his knightly training – Swordfighting, horsemanship and learning the codes and rules of chivalry. He was always made subtly conscious of the fact that though his skills were good, he wasn’t quite as good as Girauld, who of course could weild a sword, master a spirited steed, play lute etc. better than anyone else.

A year had passed since Girauld’s departure, and Valnir was nearly sixteen himself. Like his brothers before him, on this sixteenth birthday he was expected to face and pass the tests of adulthood and be declared a man. Then, as family tradition dicated, he would ride away from his home as a Knight Errand, bound not to return until he had earned his spurs as a true knight of the realm.

In truth, Valnir felt no great desire to go out and stamp his mark on the world. He was not by nature very ambitious, and the fire for combat didn’t burn in his veins the way it had for his elder brothers. He was a proficient swordsman, but he viewed combat as the means to an end rather than an end in itself. He took plesure in hunting – who wouldn’t? – but never went out of his way to pick a fight the way other men did, just for the joy of it.

On the eve of Valnirs birthday, gloom hung over the castle like an invisible shroud. His mother hardly spoke to him anymore and it seemed to Valnir that she hated him, though he couldn’t understand why. His father tolorated him with cold politeness, and the only person who treated him like a human being was his sister, Malfeur, who was uncannily perceptive for a girl of her age. “how can you declare what your quiest will be, when you don’t even know what you want” she said to him. “You must absolve the demons of the past beore you can conquer the perils of the furture” Valnir spend the night kneelingon the cold stone floor of the castle chapel, praying to the Lady of guidance. Inspiration struck him as light of the rising sun shune trough the stained glass window, bathing the altar before him in multicoloured light. Now he knew clearly what he must do.

The day passed in blur of activity, during which time Valnir passed all the ritual tests set him, and proved himslef worthy to carry the arms and armour of a Knight Errant. The final part of the ceremony was for the young knight to puplically declare the object and purpose of his quest. Valnir strode up to his parents, removed his helmet, and saluted his father. His mother, he noticed wouldn’t look him in the eye. “For the honour of the Lady, the King and my family,” he announced “I declare my quest shall be to search for my brother Girauld – to bring him back home, if he is still alive, or, if he be dead, to avenge his passing. Thus I do swear on my sword and my honour.”

As he guessed, his statement caused no small amount of commotion. His mother rushed off crying, his father just glared at him, and all the servants started whispering to eachother. “Maybe I should have just said I was going to kill the black boar of Borrobil,” he thought gloomly, “so much for altruism.”

Valnir left as soon as he could gather his possesions together – some battered armor, a plain but serviceable sword and the only horse his father was prepared to let him have, a beast so bad tempered the groms were going to kill it for meat in the autumn. It was raining, and noone could be bothered to see him off exept his sister, who seemed quite cheerful, considering. “take this,” she said pressing a cloth-wrapped bundle into his arms”its mine to give, and it will serve you well. Ride west and search for Melys Gau. I cant help you any more than this, but… good luck” She blew him a kiss, and waved goodbye as he rode trought the castle gate into the great outside world.

When he was finally out of sight of his fathers castle, Valnir stopped his horse and unwrapped his sisters present. It was a sword like no other he had ever seen. He had never heard of such a thing, it must be worth a kings ransom – how could she have come by it? Despite the damp chill of the day, the gold and pearl hilt felt warm to his touch, and swung the sword around, delicate runes along the blade sparkled in the air. Feeling distinctly more cheerful, Valnir strapped on the sword and rode off down the road to meet his destiny.

After seeing her brother off, Malfleur went back to her bedroom and flung herself on her bed to think. There was only so much she could do for the moment. Girauld’s vanity had led him to his doom like a moth drawn to a candle. It was pehaps a risk sending Valnir after him, but her need to be rid of the damning evidence of the sword overweighed any problems that would be posed by Giraulds return. She wasn’t yet skiled enough in the magical arts of prediction to tell whether Valnir would succeed in his quest or not. The Sorceress part of her soul cared nothing for any of her maimly, they served merely to support and protect her while she was young, and gathering her powers, but the part of her that still was a little girl of six summers hoped that he would, because he was a far better person than any of his brothers… and because of the way he tousled her hair when he teased her.

Valnir rode his horse down the narrow, twisting track, night was falling.
He was tired and cold, and not looking forward to yet another night sleeping in the open. At the bottom of the hill, the track turned to follow the course of a shallow stream, then tailed off in a small wooden clearing. In the middle of the clearing, huddled in front of a feeble fire, squatted a grizzled old crone. Valnir got of his horse and walked towards her warily. At close quarters she was particularly hideous, with pockmarked skin, hairy warts, and possibly only one eye, altho it was hard to tell under the mob of tangled grey hair.

“Pardon me for intruding, er, grizzled old crone. I am embarked on a sacred quest, and I seek Melys Gau. Can you pehaps tell me anything that will help me?”

“Eeeeh!” cackled the old crone. “Maybe I can, maybe I cant. Informations not cheap, you know. What can a lusty young lad like you offer an old girl like me in return eh?” She leered fetchingly in his direction, and ran her fingers trough her hair, dislodging a small frog.

“Old Crone, as a Knight I am sworn to observe strict vows of chastity,” replied Valnir hastily. “pehaps there is some other service I can do you in return?”

The Crone stared at Valnir troughtfully, as if assesing him. “Well, Sir Knight, I see you have a big sword, maybe you can put it to some use. There is something you can do for me. Ive lost my little Milou. He ran off to that cave.” She turned and pointed to a dark tunnel mouth in the roks behind her. “I havnt seen him for ages, the poor mite. If you cant find my little precious, at least bring me back his collar, and ill help you on your quest. By the way, you don’t have anything to eat, do you?”

“all I have is half loaf of bread”

“Hand it over then,” snapped the old crone. “And it better be white, I don’t want any of that disgusting brown stuff.”

Valnir showed her the bread, which was abit soggy, but the old crone snatched it out of his hand and started to gum it happily. “well, what are you hanging around for? Are you sure you don’t fancy a quick…”

“Er, no thank you very much,” replied Valnir, backing away towards the cave, which seemed a good deal more inviting than the questionable charms of the old crone in front of him. On the face of it, looking after lost kittens wasn’t the most heroic knightly pursiut, but it probably fell under ‘Protecting the weak’. Anyway, it shoudlnt take too long to find the thing, then he could be on his way.

Valnir made his way carefully down the dark tunnel, steadying himself againts the wall with his left hand. In his right hand he held his sisters sword, which had the peculiar ability to glow in the dark. Broken bones littered the floor and a fould smell wafted up from the tunnel depths. He heard a scuttling sound up ahead. Feeling rather foolish, he put on his best child-calming voice and called out ‘Milou, Milou!’

The scuttling stopped, so he started to walk towards again, shifting his shield protectively in front of him. Another sound, something was definitely moving along the tunnel towards him. He was surprised a kitten could make so much noice, maybe Milou was a dog. The sword glowed brighter, and started to vibrate with anticipation, tugging him forward. Suddenly, in a clatter of stones, a monstrous Beast galloped round the corner and threw itself at him, roaring with fury. Valnir didn’t have enough time to get a good look at the thing but it was large, vicious and had appalling breath. The beast jumped up at him, gnashing its teeth and spraying him with slobber, and Valnir was forced to defend himself.

Teeth the size of daggers chomped and tore at his armor, while the beast raked at Valnirs body with its filthy claws Pushed back against the tunnel walls, Valnir hacked away at the beast for all he was worth, Fighting a Ravening beast wasn’t the same as fighting another human being, its was faster being able to attack with its teeth and two set of claws. With a sweep from its claws the beast tore away his shield sending it bouncing on the floor, Valnir was now fighting defensively, trying to prtect himself with his sword, He was tiring fast, and bleeding from numerous tears in his armor. The beast drew back its head opening its mouth wide to bite the knights head, and in a split second of grace Valnir lunged forward and plunged his sword into its gaping maw, skewering its brain. Stinking back blood sprayed around the tunnel as the dying beast blundered about, eventually collapsing with an almost human sigh.

When Valnir had caught his breath, he limped over to the beasts corpse and turned its body over with his foot. In the fading light of his sword, the beast was a horrible mixture of a animal and reptile, as If someone had crossed a bear with a lizard, and added a extra pair of legs for good measure. Round its thick neck, embedded in the filthy fur, there was a leather collar…

Valnir was woken in the morning by his horse buzzling his face. He gently pushed it away and sat up, winching at the pain. The events of the previous evening were hazy. He remembered fighting the beast, and staggering back up the tunnel, but after that, very little. Badly wounded, and in shock, he must hve collapsed unconscious on the ground. The pain-fevered dreams of the night still haunted the edges of his mind. Confused images of a old crone, a ferocious beast and a beautiful lady with cool, white hands flittered briefly across his consciousness then the memory slipped away.

During the night, someone had removed his armour and cleaned and dressed his wounds. Valnir carefully stood up. He felt weak and shaky, and his body was mass of bruises, but he reckoned that he could still ride. He looked around the glade. The old crone was nowhere to be seen, the cold ashes of the fire and a slight sniff in the air were the only evidence of her presence. His armor lay in a pile on the ground, it was useless, too battered and torn to wear again. He would have to leave it behind. Hopefully if the lady favoured him, he would be able ro replace it soon. Beside the ruined suit of armor lay his weapon cleaned, some bread and cheese, a flask of wine and an enormous bloody claw.

Valnir gobbled down the food his anonymous benefactor had left him, and considered his situation. He now had no food, no money, no armour, and was no nearer to finding his brother, or the mysterious Melys Gau. On the plus side, he was alive still had his horse, and had killed a Ravening beast. As Valnir loaded his meagre possessions onto his horse, he noticed a crude design scratched into the earth. It had been badly defaced by hoofprints, but he was just able to make out a large arrow, pointing across the stream, and the words MAL D’YSCALLE scrawled shakily beside it.

For two years now Valnir had journeyed trough the land of Agila Torith, trying to discover the fate of his brother, which was somehow linked with the mysterious Melys Gau. During the time he had killed many monsters, vanquished many enemies and passed many tests. As a result of his adventures he was taller and stronger, both in body and in faith. He relied upon the chance encounters of fate direction. Though the objects of his interrogations were evasive, truculent, or spoken in riddles, they were never deliberately misleading, and his path took him inexorably south east, across the plains of Bastonne, past Parravon, following the western edge of the Grey Mountains.
At a tiny village called Puy de Velay, on the south-eastern tip of the Massif Orcal, he talked to venerable hermit knight, who managed to recall that a village called Melys Gau had once existed near the Forest of Lorén.. The village had been abandoned hundreds of years ago - for reasons unknown - and must now lie in ruins. The path that once led to the village had long since disappeared, but Valnir, sensing the end of quest, knew exactly which way to go...

After riding through the woods for days, Thibault felt sure he had violated the border between Agila Torith and the mysterious realm of Lorén, yet none of the elven folk nor their Sylvan allies appeared to challenge. Pausing on the crest of a hill to let his horse catch its breath, he looked down into a small, bowlshaped valley. Patchy, low cloud clung to the hillsides, making it difficult to see the valley floor, but he could just about make a cluster of indistinct buildings, which looked ruined, surrouned by a circular area of low tangled vegatation that could be overgrown fields. There was no sign of life, nothing moved, and the place was utterly silent. Spurring his horse on, he rode down the hillside into the mist.

Reaching level ground, he finally broke trough the forest wall into a delightful sunlit valley. Contented peasants waved at him from the neat well tended fields. Fair haired women sang as they tended their cows and sheep. In the middle of the fields lay a charming village, where Valnir was welcomed profusely by the villagers. Before he knew what was happening he found himself sitting outside the inn, tucking into a delicious meat pie, a pint of foaming beer at his hand. As the hot food settled in his belly, and the sun warmed his face, he felt all his cares and worries failling away. He looked at the people sitting around him, they were so friendly, so happy, chatting away in their odd lilting accent. The last time he'd felt so content, so at peace, was back home, when he was youth, before Girauld had left home. Relaxed and at peace, he lent back against the warm stucco wall of the inn and closed his eyes.

Valnir awoke with a start, tempoarily disorentated by the dark unfamiliar room. Then he remembered the village. He must have nodded off after the meal. Where was he? He climbed off the bed and stumbled over to the window. Looking down, he deduced he must be in a room on the upper level of the inn. Strange how different the village looked in the feeble moonslight. The cottages which had seemed so fair in the daylight now looked skewed and dilapidated, their pretty gardens overgrown with weeds. The well appreared to be just a tumble of stones, and the fields were tangled with shrubs and young trees. The scene was blurred and deteriorated as he watched it. Dismayed and disorintated, he returned to bed, but as he lay there, suspended between wakefulness and sleep, he heard a ghostly voice calling to him: Save me Brother, save me!

In the morning, the village was exactly as it had been the day before. The sun shone, the people were welcoming - in fact, the place seemed almost too perfect. There was no dirt, no clutter, no raised voices. All people was healthy, the animals plump, and the fruit and vegetables showed no sign of mould or weevil. And why were there no children? Furthermore, and he hadnt noticed this before, but there was no castle to protect the place. When he asked the villagers how they protected themselves, they were evasive or changed the subject. When he pressed the point, and started to ask questions about his brother, the villagers became increasingly sullen, but finally agreed to send for their lord, who, they assured Thibault, would be able to answer his questions.

At midday, when the sun was at its highest, the lord of the village appeared. Where from, exactly, it was impossible to say. He must have been a old, thought Valnir, watching him hobble painfully up the road, solicitously supported by two of the villagers. The Lord stopped when he reached Valnir, threw back the hood of his cloak and looked the young knight straight into the eye. Valnir froze in horror, and the world turned dark around him. The Lord of the village was none other than his missing brother Girauld horrible changed, old and ill, when he should be young and hale. His golden hair was grey and lanky, his strong body wasted away, his face haggard and pale. "Welcome to Melys Gau, brother" croaked Girauld. "So, have you come to stay, or take me away?"

Valnir lay in his bed that night, too restless to sleep, he had spent hours talking to Girauld, a conversation that had ended in acrimony. His brother refused to say what he was doing in the village, or why he'd never send word back home. He claimed his ravaged appearance was the result of a mysterious illness, and that if he were to leave the pure mountain air of the village, he would surely sicken and die. Little was left of the Girauld, Valnir remembered: this withered old man, was a fragile shadow of the handsome, proud man his brother had once been... and should be now. Even his personality had changed, as if his will had ebbed along with the decline of his body. Finally a terrible coughing fit had forced Girauld to retirem carried out of sight by concerned villagers.

Once again, Valnir woke up in the middle of the night, his heart pounding. Tey again, he walked over to the window and threw open the shutters. The village had transformed itself back into a ruinous state, and a low mist obscured the ground. Valnir was seized with dread, but this time he was determined to investigate. Grabbing his sword he hurried out of his room.

Valnir waled about the village, his footsteps deadened by the mist. The buildings were completly ruined, low broken walls covered with ivy. When he looked back the to the village square, the inn too was a sad jumble of stones and bushes. There was no people, no animals, no life of any kind.

Yet... Something about the square was different. What had change? What was that shape looming out of the mist where before had been nothing? As he turned to investigate, the shape solidified and resolveditself into the form of a huge mailclad knight "HE WHO SEEKS TO RULE MELYS GAU MUST FIRST DEFEAT ME" boomed the strange knight, raising his massive giant axe and striding foward towards Valnir.

"I have no quarrel with you, Sir Knight" replied Valnir backing away. "I do not seek to rule this place."

"Face me, or die!" intoned the black knight. Clanking forward he swept his axe towards Valnir, who barely managed to parry the stroke in time. A mighty fight insued. the opponents were evenly matched in skill, but the Black Knight, incased in full armor was slower, Valnir could react and move faster, but had no shield or armor to protect himself. One slash from the knights massive axe would cost him a limb, or even his head. Valnir decided his best tactic would be to wear his opponent down, and forced the black knight to lumber round the village after him as he darted back and forth. But his opponent was relentless, attacking like a automaton, and its was Valnir who started to tire. A trip over a treeroot costed him a nasty gash on his arm. The magic of his sword was strangly erratic, as if unsure whether the black knight was friend or foe.

Valnir pulled on all his experince, and all his faith, to survive. The fight was hard, and seemded to last forever however. Finally the sky started to lighten and the mist receded. Dawn was on its way. The black knight hesitated, and looked upwards. Gathering all his remaining strength, Valnir rushed forward and dealt the knight a terrific blow across his neck, at the back of his helm. With a ghastly howl, the black knight staggered back, and toppled onto the ground with a mighty crash.

"Yield or die!" cried Valnir. leaping forward to hold his sword at his foe's neck. The black knight moaned in response, and moved his head feebly from side to side. Placeing his foot on the knights chest, Valnir lent forward to cut the lacing on his armour and pulled the heavy helmet free. The pale face that stared up at him was Girauld's.

"Save me brother, save me!" gasped his brother. "now, while i am myself once more, I beg you - kill me and set my spirit free!" Valnir recoiled with horror. To kill his own brother would be a crime against his honour and human dignity. Yet if Girauld were to be believed, his death would break the enchantment and free his soul from the terrible curse that held it here. On the other hand, by killing his brother, would he in turn become the doomed guardian of Melys Gau, and have the life slowly sucked away from his body and soul? Should he sacrifice his own soul to save his brothers? And if he didnt kill Girauld, what then? Would anything be changed? would he even be able to leave Melys Gau himself or would he be trapped here, forced to fight his own brother every night for all eternity, or to one or other them perished by the other brothers hand? As he stood in the rosy morning light, frozen by indecision, the line of a prayer drifted into his mind; And the greatest of the se is mercy.

As the golden rays of the sun broke over the hilltops, Valnir raised his sword abd prepared to strike.

The End.

Vampire Lores
Evil Never Dies.


The year was 859 B.C, a few Acolytes lead by Malus, prepared an ancient ritual, ancient and evil, the underground cult devoted themselves to the dark side of magic. “We must hurry” Malus grumped, “we do not have much time, if we do not find the tomb in time the dark one will sleep another century, we must not fail” the Acolyte continued.

The Acolytes gathered their books, staffs and remains and headed off towards the east, their mission was to find an ancient grave unknown to the human world, old, lost, forgotten… the terror within the tomb would soon be unleashed upon the unsuspecting humans.
“Acording to the scrolls we should not be far away” Malus smiled a sadistic smile, soon the Dark Lady will be with us my friends, soon will our master be with us again, the Acolytes cheered all together, “HAIL the great queen” they yelled.

Days went on, and the search grew thin, Malus was getting frustrated, “Damn it! Time is of the essence, we must find the tomb” he growled… midnight fell and the acolytes seemed restless, then something happened, one of the acolytes hit something hard with his pickaxe, “Sire, ive found something” he shouted, the acolytes worked hard, and soon the top of the tomb revieled itself, “look my friends” Malus smiled and pointed, an ancient seal was shown just above the entrance “finally, after years of searching, we have found her” Malus was smiling all over his face.
“Quickly my friends, it is the right one, we must hurry” the Acolytes started to dig all around the ancient tomb so they could reach the entrance.

Days went on and the tomb was fully dug out, Malus was smiling all over his muliated face, “Finally after all these years my friends, here she is, the Dark Lady herself” Malus entered the tomb slowly, warning signs was written all over the tomb entrance, “death would bestow any who dared entering the tomb” some of them said… Malus laughed, “ofcourse, she is the Queen of the dead” he grinned.

A short sound of something being activated alerted Malus and his Acolytes as a large ironclad spear slammed trough one of the Acolytes and nailed him to the wall. “So, some one don’t want us to awaken her” he smiled.
Then another Acolyte vanished trough a hole in the ground, a large death scream confirmed that he was pierced on several spikes at the bottom.
Malus didn’t care, even if it should take him and all the acolytes their lifes he wanted to suceed.
The dark lady had to be awakened.

Hours went on and several traps were unleased upon the searching Acolytes, until they reached a small chamber where a sealed grave stood. “we are here my friends” Malus glimsed at the grave, “come quickly we must start the ritual at once”.
“Mortis lieber ferdu es errore humanum” Malus started mumbling, as the tomb started to glow.

“Who dares to awaken the slumber of a Vampire,” a ghastly voice thunderered trough the crypt, as the seal exploded and a glowing dark figure was floating in the air infront of Malus. “I do, my queen, you have slept long enough, I am your servant and in your debt” Malus went on his knees and bowed to the great queen Hecate. “Ssso Malussss, bring me to the Valley of the dead, and summon my husband” she hissed, “your wish is my command” Malus obeyed and High Queen Hecate was laid back to rest in a coffin guarded by several Acolytes in a black coach while traveling to the far desert of the land of the dead. “her husband?” Malus wondered why he had never heard of Hecate’s husband before… who was he? What was that… Never then less Malus and his acolytes obeyed and began their travel towards The Valley of the Dead, towards an unknown destiny.

Days went on as the acolytes drew nearer their goal, several attacks had been there to stop them and their queen but all the time they have been struck with some kind of fear when getting to close to the Black Coach, ofcourse, the Coach glowed some mystic green aura and the high queen Hecate was inside it the darkest and most evil Vampire ever to have walked the earth had been reawakened, ofcourse they would fear her, it was more like they had to see it was true more than attacks. Malus did not care, there was no force strong enough to stop his masters when she was in her full strength, but ofcourse until she was he would wov his life to protect her.

At last, The Valley of the Dead was in sight, the heat was extreme as the Valley was far out in the desert, destinined to be forgotten by men, but the high queen had other ideas, “My lady, we are here, were do we start the search?” Malus asked, Hecate smiled “Good then I shall find him myself” she said and arose from the coach and lifted her hands high in the sky and started to mumble something in very ancient Vampire tongue, not even Malus could understand, and there a few hundred meters an gigantic citidel arose from the sandfloor, “He is there servant” Hecate grinned, Malus was in no doubt that his queen had gained strenght already and smiled, “as command we shall find him” and bowed “come men the dark lord awaits us” he commanded the acolytes towards the tomb, an old inscription was written on the door, Malus decided to study it before moving on and it came out like

“The beast lives out of the raging storm in the dead of night, The ravenous blood sick creatures searches for it's sacrifice
Through the hideous darkness it lurches driven by death itself
Only the satisfaction of slaughter will cause it to return to
The darkness from which it came"

No doubt that it was an inscription telling about what vampires are, Malus smiled “lets go, we shouldn’t let him wait any longer” as they entered the tomb Malus remembered Hecates tomb, “Men beware of traps im sure there are some here too” as he said that a sling with a giant blade set lose over the top floor and severered one of the acolytes in two, they froze in fear, but Malus just hissed at them to move on or else Hecate would kill them instead in a way that was thousand times worse than death itself. Then without any questions asked they moved on they entered a small corridor “watch out!” Malus shouted but tolate as the two walls rammed together and crushed two acolytes, blood was running down the walls and a twisted wind of laughter was heard trough the halls, then a dark voice whispered something ancient, it must have been ancient vampire tongue which only Hecate could understand, never then less Malus quickly found out what it meant as several skeleton warriors came running towards them and gutted a acolyte before he could react, “bah” Malus grunted and mumled a word of shadow spell which caused the skeletons to crumble to dust, “move on weaklings” but before they even got the chance to move another trap sprung free and pierced a acolyte trough the belly.

That was enough for the acolytes as they turned their tails and ran, “you fools!” Malus yelled,
Then they froze, as Hecate herself stood in the entrance, she smiled “I can see you have ran into some troubles in my husbands tomb no?” she asked “ fear not young ones and follow me” she continued as she flowed trough the corridors whispering ancient vampire tongue, no traps was tripped and the skeleton guards stood still not moving a inch, “behold the tomb of Valik himself” Hecate smiled as they entered a hidden chamber. “Valik? You mean Valik Shadowblade” Malus asked? “hehe yes Malus, the one and only” Hecate answered…
Malus shaked his head, “ofcourse I should have known, but I thought he was a legend” Malus wondered, “no servant, Valik is real enough and my husband” Hecate smiled. “Now begone your skills cannot awake the dark lord” she continued “as you wish my queen” Malus bowed and left the chamber to stand guard with the remaining acolytes. Again Hecate started mumling in ancient Vampiric tongue and raized her hands high and called upon the dark gods of the twisted nether, lightning thundered from the clear skies as darkness swept over the tomb and everything started to shake, “by Mortis’s dark will thou shall arise Valik ShadowBlade, Lord of darkness and destroyer of dreams” Hecate’s eyes glowed bloodred as her spell continued to boom trough the tomb chamber, moments later everything went silent and the sky was clear again… infront of Hecate a dark shatted figure was standing, confused by the haze and dust the figure was silent and unstable, “you have returned my lord” Hecate then whispered. At once two red eyes could be seen trough the dustclouds, the figure lifted a hand and pointed a finger towards Hecate, “Lady Hecate” the ghastly figure whispered, “Yes my love it is I, you lady and queen, you have slept for 600 years, it is time to return to the realm of men” she continued. The figure stepped towards Hecate and revieled himself, his skin was white and pale, black dark hair… he was in perfect shape. He drove a hand down her cheek and smiled “beautiful as you were 600 years ago darling” then he turned around and walked towards the entrance were Malus were waiting, Valik grapped him by his troath and lifted him high up in the air, “and who you might be human” Valik hissed “I am Malus my lord, I am your servant and the one who awoke your lady” Malus gasped. Valik left the grip and turned “Then I am in your debt, you shall live to see another day” Valik smiled.
