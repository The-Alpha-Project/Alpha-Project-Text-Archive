How quests kinda should look like ... hmmm

Monthly quests:
they should be EPIC in every sense of the word. All players need to be involved and it has to TOUCH them in every way possible.
I am basing the following example on a previous experience in AC1, it is VERY similar but still changed a bit ;P
- There is a worldcrystal (of about half a human's size), created for the sole purpose of keeping an uber critter (admin-controlled) practically immortal. Before the players can even try killing it, they must destroy the crystal.... However, when you pledge allegiance to evil, your guild receives bonuses from the crystal, only a limited amount of people can be bound to the crystal though. This makes sure certain guilds will do all they can to defend the crystal, while the others will try to destroy it. A battle that would last weeks, maybe even a whole month, until the crystal finally gets destroyed (if the defending force is too strong or the attacking force too weak, admins can always ... intervene secretly ;p. Bump def down of crystal or let the immortal creature defend it for a time)

- Then of course you have the whole horde vs alliance thing u can work out. also a bit the same as my last example, let a horde uber NPC (admin-controlled) attack constantly so the orcs will need to follow it everywhere to defend it. Or have a castle give bonuses so the other faction needs to destroy it, etc.

Normal quests:
They don't need to be as epic as monthly quests, but it also needs to give players the fuzzy feeling of "this was awesome, and my reward was good!". for example a little TEAM BASED quest like:
- A powerfull nerubian has risen, and needs to be killed (of course he respawns or is in an instanced dungeon). However, when you enter his lair he hunts you down. He constantly resurrects though, unless aquiring a staff first, deep in his dungeon and guarded heavily. This would require one part of the team to constantly slay the nerubian, while the other team searches for the item. Once found, they need to use the item on the nerubian, whereafter he becomes a LOT stronger but also mortal. You kill him and loot for fubar itam!
Now for a solo quest:
- A powerfull NPC mage in a distant tower asks you to give him his lost gems for a ritual he was preparing. Easy enough so he says, and sends you to the dungeon where he says he lost them. When you enter it seems quite easy, you find a gem fast and pick it up. Then a timer starts and you have exactly 20-or-so minutes to retrieve the gems before the long-dead robbers of the gems come to haunt you (and kill you). When you bring back the gems the NPC will laugh at you for bringing back evil gems that ancient guardians stole and sealed away. Then you need to kill him to obtain a letter which describes an attempt to kill thrall e.g.. U go to thrall, show it to him, hand over the gems and receive a leet reward!

Maybe not fubar quests but you get the idea. There need to be excitement, story, some which require teamplay, nice rewards!
