Here is a little brief breakdown I wrote up in my former AC2 Allegiance's website:

"Update after 3 hours of trying to play...

Well, I think the game is not bad. There are still some kinks, but I will give them a week or so to hopefully resolve. I got instant messaged and it popped me out of the game, but the game don't like you to alt tab, so it crashed... Kind of annoying. My friend said the same thing happens in Final Fantasy XI. Towns have a ton of people, but the toons take forever to show up on your screen. It is kind of weird and I think it might be a Video setting I need to tweak.

I am only a Rating 2 Scout and Rating 2 Blacksmith at the moment as I took some time to run around the areas and chat with some people. The crafting so far seems like it is going to be very fun. I only did the initiation crafting quest and it was pretty neat. First you mine your resource with a pick; then you need to smelt it into bars with some prongs, copper in my case; and then take it to the anvil and use your hammer to shape it.

My gnome's Name is Pablo, so if you want to add me to your buddy list feel free to do so. The buddy list is a cool feature. One thing I hated about AC2 was the non customizable GUI. This games GUI is pretty customizable, but then again it has alot of things that might cause clutter. The graphics aren't as polished as AC2's, but they aren't too ugly. My little Gnome looks pretty cool and I am happy with him, he has glasses like me! You can tweak alot of things on your toon when you first make him, now I need to find my dude a wizard hat like Gandolf.

The game is new, and some things in AC2 I wish they had in this game, but I am gonna see how far I can go and see if it is fun or not. Maybe we can all meet up and do some questing together, or build a house or something."

The level system is alot different than the norm. I don't think lvl 2 is like a lvl 2 toon in AC2, leveling is weird in this game. I so far am liking and hating the game at the same time. I hate the no alt tab thing. That is so stupid. If they do not fix this soon I will probly throw this in my pile of failed MMO's next to Shadowbane, AC2 and NWN (if you consider it a MMO).

Ugh, yesterday I tried to get into the game. I mean I really sat there trying to not think of the bad things. I had my gui setup, my hotkeys setup and a plan.

So I heard the crafting was great, so I talk to my Blacksmith trainer in New Brummel. He gives me a simple quest, just had to travel to another town and report on New Brummel's blacksmith. I guess it was her boss or something. Now finding him is a pain because of how slow the models load onto the screen. I found the Blacksmith sign, went and stood near the Smelter and sat patiently sipping on my Powerade. Alas, finally loading onto my screen was the person I was looking for. Greeted the toon, was told to return to the lady smith in New Brummel for my reward. I head out of the building and start to look for the Teleport, by the way teleporting sucks, which was right behind me the whole time, it just hadn't loaded onto the screen. It appears I go in, wait for the stupid loading screen, and head back to get my reward and experience so I can advance. To make a long story short, I had to wait for the lady smith to load like I did the dude in the other town. It is like this for almost every NPC and PC. It is not my system, I have 1024 ram...

I did a few quests like that but got nowhere really. It was like 50xp per run, which means I had to do like 5 runs to level... Not worth it.

I spoke to a Taskmaster! He asked me, "Do you want a short, medium or long quest?" Figuring the short quest would be a crappy errand task, I went for the long quest. I was told to make 30 Parcels. Each parcel requires like three bronze bars, and a bronze bar requires Ore. So I head off to the resource part of New Brummel to gather ore to make the bronze bars. My dude can carry like 75 or 80 ore. So after gathering 80 ore, I go to the smelter, bust out my tongs and smelt the 75 ore into about 15 bronze bars. Then I smelt the 15 bronze bars into like 3 or 4 Parcels. I had to do this for about 2 hours, maybe longer. I was getting some Blacksmith exp everytime I made bronze bars and Parcels, not much but some. So after finally completing my task I head back to the Taskmaster :ph34r: and guess what he gives me! 50xp! WOOT! After 2 hours of work, I gained like half a level, which was enough to make me level 4 Blacksmithing... By the way the lvl up animation is about as lame as they come. The particles look like crap. After that I seriously wanted to strangle the dude. Now I wouldn't have minded the crappy xp if he had maybe rewarded me with some leet new pickaxe for mining resources. But nope, nothing but the xp from making the stuff and a bonus 50 xp. Then he has the nerve to tell me to ask him again when I want to do something else.

The game needs to fix 2 things, 1) fix the combat, it sucks, its very slow paced and the animation quality of the models is poor. 2) Add some loot to monsters. You can only get cool gear by buying it at the bazaar. Crafters huddle around the bazaar all day. It basically is made for crafters. I dunno who the crafters will craft for when the adventurers leave.

I dunno if I will ever load the game again. You need to use Internet Explorer to load up the game... That was poor and or lazy from the designers.

Personally I think AC2 is the best MMO out atm. The game looks good and doesn't lag. The only thing that is holding it back was the loss of players when they had the lame chat bug.

to add some things:

from the eyes of a dragon
pros:
+ the combat is fast (or at least faster than the other MMORPGs out there).
+ the combat is very nicely animated
+ lots of different skills
+ no death penalties
+ superb graphics, nice scenery. The dragon city dlark (or something like that) is very impressive. The atmosphere is great
+ teleport gates to go to other locations fast. No hours of sitting on a stupid horse like in Daoc. AC1 had this too though but I still concider it a pro.
+ guild support
+ property buying available
+ you can become an adult and ancient as dragon, becoming much bigger AND being able to fly anywhere you want. You should also be able to carry one character along on your back (at least that's what I heard).
+/- Quests give you certain abilities and stat/skill ups
+/- lvling seems to be fast for now. I scored lvl 10 in a day
+ interface is pretty good
+ Seemingly a very wide-spread crafting system. As this is no pro to me, it's kind of one to the people who like to craft. U can create your own spells, armor, weapons, whatever, and you need to look all over for the good stuff to craft with.
+ Very little and very fast loading times

cons:
- bug with combat makes dragons run a few sec before attacking, doesn't really bother me so much but should be fixed. This doesn't happen when a critter attacks you though, just when a critter is running and doesn't attack you.
- alt-tab does not work. (but heck, it doesn't work in a lot of MMORPGs)
- quests are repetitive and have little depth (so far, maybe later quests are better)
- high system requirements
- no decent loot on monsters (cept craft stuff), trading/crafting is the way to go. You could see it as a pro because it adds a huge player economy to the game, but I see it as a con because I fucking hate trading and crafting! ;)
-/+ judging what mrO said, the crafting is boring. Looking at it though, it doesn't differ much from other MMORPG, it's even a lot more wide-spread and it has the same usual boringness of creating stuff all day. It wasn't any better in Daoc and see how much accounts Daoc managed to get.
- Skill points u get are not yet visible! Doesn't matter much to me, as I put every single skill point in "tooth and claw" skill, but it should be fixed fast.


I see no problem with the login process being on the site. It's so incredibly fast, just chose ur char and hit go. No loading game, see servers, loading game, see chars, loading game, IN!
I can not see why u have a problem with that mrO. it rocks. I don't want to see shitty slow-ass game graphics to select my char, I want to play!
