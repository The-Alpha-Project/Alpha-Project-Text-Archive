I sent this to Blizzard regarding my play time as a shaman in WoW.

QUOTE
The Shaman Class

    I played as a Tauren Shaman as my first horde character in the beta. With about 11 days played on that character I thought I would send in some feedback.

    First off, I thought it would be a cool class to play since it was the Hordes 'Paladin' and I enjoyed the role the Paladin played in the Alliance Beta Patch. As a Paladin in the first beta version, it may have been too good as I have seen they have had a rebalance in their abilities. It was fun though none the less.

    So I played as my shaman all the way up to level 39 and I enjoyed it as much as I did my Paladin to a point. As I gained more levels, more abilities and tougher quests, I felt the shaman started to lack, while most other classes kept on par.

    When questing inside of instances namely, you don't really stay in one spot for a long period of time and I was finding myself needing to sit and drink more often because I was using up all of my mana casting everything in my arsenal. Needing to med after every fight is expensive because Sweet Nectar isn't cheap when you buy a few stacks. Also, it holds back the group, who have mana efficient spells and are ready to move along.

    So my first thought was to not drop as many totems during each battle, unless we were taking on a boss or a group of mobs (unoften as most of the game is pull one mob at a time for a 5v1 slaughter). So usually I would drop only my strength totem down for the damage per second buff on the group, use earth shocks to interrupt mobs spells and possibly use a flame shock to dot the mob. If needed I would also heal group members low on health with my lesser healing wave. In only giving part of my all, I felt as if I wasn't playing up to my full capabilities.I got to thinking my group would benefit more had a druid joined in the group instead of me. A druid can cast Mark of the Wild on everyone, which would act like my strength and mana/healing stream totem plus more. They would also have this benefit on them for 30 minutes, no matter where they were. So instead of my strength buff and half spirit buff (mana font or healing stream), they would get a buff on all their stats and resistances. Rejuvination at its low mana cost would also be more efficient than my healing. Druids can also dot the mobs with their arcane spell.

    I came up with a suggestion that you may have or may not have already solved internally in your latest builds. In my opinion the Shaman will go from the weakest class to a comparable class by making a small change to the whole totem idea:

- Have a 'Retrieve Totems' ability. This ability will gather all dropped totems, and depending on how much time they had left on them, give back a % of mana to the shaman.

    Simply giving totems a larger radius in my opinion is not the best solution. Some totems could use a larger radius, but that alone will not help balance the mana cost of the shamans arsenal.

Note I have tried alot of things, from going pure intellect, pure spirit, to a mix of both. Most of the time I find myself in the same dillemma as a Druid with the same gear will still overall be a better part of a group. I at least want my shaman to be as worthy of being in a group than a druid or any other class for that matter. I don't want to join a group just because I can heal and res people when I have a ton of cool abilities I am capable of using. I want to play a shaman, I like the whole idea of a shaman class, but I am disappointed when I see that other classes work a whole lot better in a group.

    Soloing as a Shaman is also very tough to do. After playing my shaman to 39, I made a mage and found I could solo as a mage a whole lot easier than I could with a shaman. I even had fun soloing as a mage whereas with my shaman I got frustrated and found tagging along in a group was my best bet at succeeding. When I tried to solo as a shaman, I had to make sure 100% of the time I only fought one monster at a time. If another monster came, it would be extremely tough to pull out of the battle alive. Note at times, even lower level mobs would be a pain to take on 2v1. The stoneclaw totem was pretty much useless the majority of the time, its taunt just didn't work out right. Maybe you could replace the stoneclaw totem with a stasis trap totem that could act as a minor form of crowd control. Have it stun one mob, or more if you feel it is balanced at a time. You could make the totem act like a sleep spell, which stuns a target for X seconds. Click the ability, select a target, click and then the shaman will throw a totem at a targets feet. The target will then numb and become immobile for a set amount of time, thus allowing the shaman to finish taking on the mob he wants to focus on.

    Soloing will still be as mana intense on a shaman because since you are alone, you will not benefit much from the retrieval of totems, as the majority of the time one battle will last as long as a totem allows.

    So my next point is that Lightning Shield and other personal beneficial spells are overly mana intense. Possibly a lower mana cost for the shield would be in order. Also having the weapon buffs stack with say a flametongue totem would be nice. I mean, if you can combine earth, fire, water, and when they add them in wind totems, why can't the weapon buffs combine with the totem abilities? Casting the Fire buff on your weapon and dropping a flametongue totem shouldn't be too imbalanced as the flame tongue totem proc isn't 100% of the time anyway. Same with the weapon proc, it doesn't work 100% of the time.

    Next topic regards weapons for the shaman. Some nice Shaman axes and maces would be nice to be able to come across in game. Right now a shaman with a dagger seems pretty boring, but it is possibly the best choice for a shaman (or any class as it seems). Daggers are cool, but everyone uses a dagger. Axes, staves and maces would be a nice change in style for the shaman class. Most current maces are only beneficial to priests and paladins, so possibly adding in some styled more towards a shaman would be cool.

    I am sure you get this all the time, but where is Bloodlust? Is this a balance issue? A spell you want people to wait on? It would be nice to get a reply on this topic.

    Note, what I have discussed is my personal opinion on the class. I may be wrong on some topics and will await the changes you made to the class in the next upcoming patch. Thanks for your time.


Thoughts?

I'm playing a Troll Shaman this push, currently at level 36. You make some good points here.

In groups, my role is almost always as the healer, since there seems to be a shortage of priests. Using lesser healing wave, I can heal pretty well since it only has a 1.5 second cast and I have the added bonus of being able to resurrect.

With the new push, priests will have many of their spells made more mana efficient, so the role of a shaman as a healer should be much reduced. So now the question remains, what will be the role of a shaman in a group? The answer has to lie in totems (secondary damage-dealer as well).

Posted today by Kat, shamans will be getting a new totem in the next push. Hopefully it will prove to be useful, along with the increased radii of many other totems. It is my guess that it will be an air totem since the only existing one so far is the Sentry totem, whose 5 minute duration should be increased (I use it to watch for respawns).

As far as soloing is concerned, it is rather difficult to solo as a shaman in the higher levels. Yesterday I was fighting in Stranglethorn Vale, and while I can solo a single creature a few levels above me, when I get an add, I am simply screwed, even when the mobs are of equal level to me. Right now, at 36, I can handle two level 30-33 mobs if I get an add, but if I'm fighting level 35s such as the raptors, I don't stand a chance. Simply put, I die a lot when a roamer comes my way or a mob respawns at my location.

Unfortunately, stoneclaw totems don't work during battle, so there is no form of crowd control or method of getting a mob to stop attacking. Perhaps the stasis trap of Warcraft III or some form of instant stun with a 30 second cooldown will make its way into the shaman's arsenal of totems.

Lightning Shield is indeed extremely mana intensive. However, it may be rightly so, since in its current form, it can be cast in battle, and at 83 damage per hit currently, soon to be upgraded to 116, it may be one of the most mana-efficient damage spells for the shaman (also being able to melee while lightning shield damages the enemy adds even more damage). With it also being cast outside of battle, that is essentially a free ~300 damage on the mob since the mana can be regenerated before entering battle.

We'll see what the changes the new push brings.

Just my thoughts.
