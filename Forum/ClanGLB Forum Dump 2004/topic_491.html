Some notes about what happened with me and alpha.

Hey guys/gals. As some of you know, I recieved an invitation to the WoW alpha. This happened slightly before Christmas last year, and was brimming with excitement. Starting off in the UD push wasn't terribly exciting to me, because I didn't like the graphic style very much....but the gameplay more than made up for it.

I took my character: Spanky, an UD priest to lvl 18 and was having some serious fun. I don't want to give very much away, because I think the less you know, the more you have to look forward to when you actually start playing.

Anyway...some key things mentioning around the time of the UD push:
The AI is amazing, as well as the aggro balance. Mobs attack the right people when they should, and parties are insanely fun. There are areas where you have to raid mob-controlled villages. These villages had patrols, as well as ambushes. To successfully beat them, you have to scout, and find the best way to do it. Running in kamekaze style only gets you killed, even with a few extra levels tacked on.

The priest was a really good class to play, with actual soloing ability, but I still didn't like the UD style very much...seemed a bit too cartoony and uncharacteristic compared to War3.

About a month after the UD push started, the rest of the Horde races were unlocked, and the UD went back into hiding. For this push, I was really interested in playing all the different races, but I chose to wait on my favorite: Orc.

So, I tried out Troll Shaman: Winston, as many of the other people I knew wanted some feedback on it, and a Tauren Warrior: Elroy (Shaman were the new class unlocked this push).

I started out with my Troll Shaman, and had the intent of being an enchanter/herbalist. I really took to enchanting, and felt like it was a really cool skill with lots of potential. Although, I found the style of the Orc/Troll area very cool, as well as the major city: Ogrimmar...it was just organized poorly. The city while visually stunning and immense, was just too spaced out, and I found myself having a bitch of a time finding things I needed, as well as getting to them in a short period of time once I remembered where they were.

Anyways, my troll shaman wasn't very interesting, either. I found the class underpowered compared to my priest, and I had to sit down to recouperate lost mana and health much more. Totems are easily destroyable by mobs, or other players, and they don't have amazing benefits in comparison to the alliance paladin.

Okay, so troll shaman wasn't feeling too good. Yeah, lots of cool scenery...but I liked my UD priest a bit more. I decided it would be a good time to start my Tauren Warrior: Elroy. Elroy would turn out to be my most-played character thus far, and level to 20 (one bubble away from 21 when they closed the push!).

Wow. The Tauren have some BEAUTIFUL lands. Reminds me of upstate New York, in the woods. Great story, great immersion into the environment, and the Tauren Warrior was busting some monster ass. I tried out Enchanting some more, Blacksmithing, Mining, and Herbalism. Before you say WTF!?@#!@ You can do that many skills?!@ Yes...and no... I was constantly out of money and skill points to distribute and I often had to wait to level before i could continue a skill. I ended up dropping herbalism and buying herbs I needed for enchanting, and sticking with Blacksmithing and Mining as my major jobs.

Some key things to note:
Warrior in defensive stance is much better for groups than Offensive stance warrior. Taunt = thx. 2-handed weapons are weak compared to a really fast dagger. You end up holding more aggro, and doing more damage with a super-fast dagger (this will most likely be changed in beta after some number tweaking).
