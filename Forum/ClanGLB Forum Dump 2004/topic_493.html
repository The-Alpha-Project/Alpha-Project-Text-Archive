Day One

OK, so I am kicking it in #ClanGLB on Quakenet when the little envelope pops up in my system tray that tells me I have a new email. Usually it is just another Viagra spam mail, but this time it was Mr. George Wang. George had sent an email to all of the Official Blizzard Fansites telling us it was coming very soon. If you didn't know, I am lucky enough to be apart of that group as I was a very active admin at WC3addicts.com.

A few moments later the real email arrived. I went to the beta site provided in the email, filled in the information it asked for and downloaded the torrent client. The download had finally begun after a year or so of waiting for the damn game to come out. I then kicked back anxiously looking at the download percent every few minutes (maybe more like seconds?). With it 80% downloaded, fellow clan mates Microslave and DrLego were in and going. WaCK, being in the alpha, had been playing all evening fishing with his Gnome Rogue Zala.

Hearing them talk about the game (we were also chatting on ventrilo) made me so anxious I needed to take my mind off of the percentage done, so I went out to play some Magic the Gathering. Thinking a few hours should almost guarantee a finished download when I get home.

After playing some Magic, I get home and find the download had 45 minutes to go yet. Dissapointed it wasn't done, I sat there the whole 45 minutes wishing it would hurry up. Long story short, it finally finished, took around 20 minutes to install (might be less but it seemed forever) and I then looked at all the characters on the creation screen.

You can see all races and classes, but we couldn't actually create a horde character (Undead, Orc, Troll and Tauren). So I then made myself a human Paladin and set off into Azeroth.

Akroma Is Born

Getting home at 3:30am from playing Magic, the North American members of GLB had hit the sack already. I could have went to bed as well, ah I am lying, I couldn't. I had to make a new character, her name Akroma.

Making a Female Human Paladin was pretty much what I had planned so I followed through and did so. Arriving in the world for the first time was just insane.

As soon as you get into the Human starter town you notice a footman with a yellow Exclamation(!) mark over his head. Just as in the Warcraft III campaigns, this means that NPC (Non Playable Character) has a quest waiting for you. As a side note, silver exclamation marks symbolize a quest you can do later.

I walk up to this kind footman and see what he might need. He knew that I was a Paladin and that my name was Akroma! WTF~~~ He must have been watching me through my window while I would change into my wolfskin nighty or something. Anyways, he let me know that his boss needs to speak with me about some trouble around the area.

Behind him was a large building, similar to possibly a small castle of some sort. I entered and right away I see another NPC with a Question Mark(?) above his head. This symbolized that this is the next part of my quest (find my boss). So I go and talk to the old timer and he tells me there has been a nasty infultration of Kobolds around the area. Since alot of the royal guard has been fighting the demons, the kobolds took this time to try and occupy the great lands of Azeroth.

Me as a noble Paladin couldn't stand for that, so I joined the cause and was gonna help fight back versus these candle on their head bastards. I accept his quest, run outside with my wooden hammer and look for some Kobolds to bonk.

Kicking some kobold ass seemed to be my specialty as I killed quite a few of them and had leveled up a bit. Looking around the town and reporting back to the General, I managed to fullfill a few quests.

I also went into a mine and found a great big treasure box. I was so overwhelmed with joy; I ran up to it, right clicked on it, started to pick the lock, but alas the Kobolds started to kick my treasure looting booty! The whole early game I was cocky and didn't think I would ever die. I mean these Kobolds were so stupid they let their friends die even if they were right next to eachother, but NOOOO, steal a little treasure and they go loco on my @$$.

I experienced the ghost mode of death and had to run back to my corpse. You cannot see anything living around you and you are a bit transparent. You move a bit faster as well. So I run back to my corpse, as the mini radar shows an arrow showing me the way to my body. As I get closer to my body, basically whithin radar range, the arrow turns into a mini headstone. Then when you are within 5 meters of the corpse, you get the option to ressurect and rejoin your body. Rejoin my body I did and I killed them bastards in that treasure chest room. Finally looting the chest, I get some crap I don't really benefit from and I move on.

Apart from killing Kobolds, I also had to kill some wolves and collect wolf meat from them for a nice gentlemen who offered me a choice of quest rewards. Most quests also give you an option in choice of quest rewards. Some classes might luck out more than others. As you will see in my next write up, my priest couldn't use more than half of the early quest rewards since she couldn't wear anything but cloth armor.

Finally before Akroma was retired for the night, I had to kill some Bandits across the stream and collect their bandanas as proof. These were the first type of creatures to actually engage you if you got too close. Kobolds just went about their business till you attacked them (or tried to take their preciouseses).

My final quest with young level 5 Akroma was possibly the end of the easy quests. I had to go kill a super bandito with a huge sword. After killing him I needed to take back with me proof via his head. I kill the dude sort of no problem and loot his head and return to my quest giver. I get a fairly nice reward item I can use for more defense.

After this, hmm, hour and a half or so time playing, I think I should get to bed and wake up to play with WaCK and crew early on. So I log off, leave my computer on as I was seeding the beta on the torrent for people still downloading and lay down in my bed.

Tossing and turning for an hour or so, I decide to just stay up and play. This time I make a Night Elf!

MsONLINE version 1.0

So after testing out the human Paladin a little bit, I thought let's see if my Night Elf slot is open (rumor had it only limited accounts could make a Night Elf early on to prevent a huge population of Night Elves).

Lucky ol me, I was allowed to create MsONLINE, a female Night Elf Priestess and venture around the beautifully colored Night Elf lands. The first thing I seen upon entering the world of the elves was the humongous buildings. Trees blocking out the sky, Panthers roaming the area looking for fresh Night Elf blood and of course the expected quest giver.

The quest giver let's me know that though all creatures of the wild are beautiful, that too many of them can cause more problems for the earth. I was asked to slay some Panthers and a few Boars in order to keep balance.

This would be a good test of Blizzard's word on whether every class can solo. In most MMO's the healer class can solo, but he won't live :) Yet my priestess kicked some serious butt... She actually probably plays alot faster than my Paladin in terms of ass kicking ability early on.

All in all, the elven lands are amazing looking. The stuctures are not only unique, but they look stellar.

Sadly though, all nice things must come to an end in order for something better to come along...

Who is MsONLINE?

MsONLINE is a Female Human Paladin who specializes in one handed maces. She wields a shield and uses it to defend herself from the evil that is trying to knock down the followers of the light. Wearing armor crafted by some of the best crafters Stormwind has to offer, she looks sexy yet is well protected from harms way.

Devotion as her primary aura, she gives her group members a boost in defense. Combining a Holy Strike and a Fist of Justice; this attack combo first stuns the enemy, which allows her allies to have a chance to deal damage free from harm and or give them a chance to run if need be; finally she deals out an attack that can end most of her enemies days on Azeroth. In a time of dire need, she can give all of her health to an ally who needs it more than her. A risky spell, but one the paladins must wield in order to keep true to their allies.

One day she hopes to be among the King's Court and protect the royal family at all costs, but until then she will continue to try and help anyone in need for the sake of what's right.
