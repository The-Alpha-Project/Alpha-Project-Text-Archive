    OT but worth a reply, jobs.
    Blizzard, like the vast majority of MMORPG producing companies, are US based. If they do a testing, they want to be present at all times to watch the REAL players reaction to every given thing they made in the past few years. So they want to be game masters. There is that problem of time zones, additional server expense and other more money related stuff that keeps a beta test from Blizzard occuring at the same time worldwide. Now if they had a server in Europe, they (because of lack of the support in Europe, currently) would have to be awake 24/7. On the other hand, if the europeans wanted to play on this beta test, they would have to be online at about 1am till 7am to have some coop fun (okay, I know this is not true, the WoW players themselves will be online 24/7, but for statistic sakes ).

    It's not discrimination, it's more of a logical economic step. Hey, at least they promised one would be here, too .
