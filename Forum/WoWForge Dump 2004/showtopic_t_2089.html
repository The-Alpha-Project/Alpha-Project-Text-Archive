    Re: WoW Beta Testers = No MMO Experience?
    I do agree that WoW will not be the ultimate gaming experience (that's reserved for WoW II, like some other nice chap said ), it is heads and shoulders above other MMORPGs in its features, immersive world and lack of "let's release it now so we can make more money"-executives.
    What I hope for (and what I have heard from the boards) is that while, yes, you can "grind" your way to the top levels, you both make it faster and have more fun if you quest your way up (barring some insanely long times between spawns/overcrowded areas... both problems that can be avoided by going elsewhere).
    I also like a game where all classes can solo and where all classes is supposed to be neat to have in a group - currently, mages seem to be the ones that aren't very welcome in groups, which seem to be linked to the fact that few people have the courage to wait with their nuking, but mages can (and do) group with people.

    As for levels taking long time... it's a delicate trade-off between the "fun" of levelling and the "fun" of having the powergamers flaunt their maxed-out characters. No one wants to see the powergamers farming low level critters "because I need to twink my L3 Priest" within a week of WoW being released, but no one wants to gain level ten after two weeks of hard work, either.
