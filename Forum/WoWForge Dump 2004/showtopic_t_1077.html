    Actually, according to Kat, they arn't even restricting you to one sign up per IP address.

    <edit>
    I dont have the correct link on hand, but there was a post answered on the official forums that said two signups from a single computer would be ok
    </edit>

    However, I wouldn't suggest sending in 10 or 20 signups from the same IP. The rumor is that they have some fairly complex method to determine fake applications and they may use IP address as part of that equation.

    P.S. if they only allowed one sign up per IP Address then anyone with a dynamic IP could get screwed.

    In response to your question dynamite: I can't tell you for sure what blizzard does but I image there is no way in hell they would disquality two different applications based soley on thier IP addresses being in the same class C (i.e. 36.35.128.x). That would lead to ALOT of people being disqualified.

    However, if you are using the application with that ActiveX computer spec thing, while they claim that all it does is automate the filling out of the form, there are a million sneaky bits of infromation that ActiveX could get from your system; which could be used to uniquely indentify your machine.

    However, the above post makes this all moot. If you and your friend are able to sign up from the same computer and IP address. Then one would imagine that a signup from the same computer with a different IP address would also be fine.
