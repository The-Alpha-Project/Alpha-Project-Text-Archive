    Will WoW live up to the hype?
    As many know Blizzard entertainment has brought us the classic games of our lifetime. Diablo, Starcraft, Warcraft, and now World of Warcraft. For most of us here we are truely amazed at the concept of " Warcraft MMORPG". With a truely deep storyline and ever evolving characters, will make this game one of the biggest anticapted releashes.

    Or will it?


    With the overwelming fan community dieing to get ahold of a copy of thier favorite game.. what does this mean to Blizzard?

    Blizzard for years have made great games that every person can like something of them. But coming into the business of MMO games is a big step. The market right now is hot for mmorgps , the fame of the genre has made online gaming exp taken to another level. Games like Planetside, Everquest2, Star Wars Galaxies, Dark Age of Camelot, Asherons Call, and Matrix Online are sum of the many big titans of the MMORPG Industry.

    Blizzard truely is a grade A developer but take on a project this big will have some downfalls.

    Right now WoW is in the early stages of beta testing. I myself have been scrutinizing the beta forums trying to get a perspective of the games engine, style and gameplay. So far its been mixed results. The gameply is not innovative in anyway but offers for the most novice of gamers a good learners curve. The real niddy griddy opnions comes for the more exp. gamers. Talks such as quests being overcrowded, creeps spawn rate, Leveling exp per level and of course the new implemented rest system.

    World of Warcraft like any other game is cluttered with things to fix. The Negative part of this is alot of fans are hearing these negative comments and are basing the game without playing it. If WoW cant live up to what its title bears Blizzards years long project can go down the drain just like Asherons Call 2 did.


    All of this is just speculation and we are still in the early stages of Beta Testing. So we must all wait patiently.

    Re: Will WoW live up to the hype?
    EDIT: corrected minor spelling faults

    I suspect that WoW will be a very successful game, and probably a good MMORPG. It might lack the content - and the whole orientation that goes with it - that keeps people playing EQ for the loot.

    I do not think WoW will be the "XYZ game killer" that some people seem to think - it is far more likely to attract new players than the next level-grind MMORPG clone that comes out. If Blizzard plays their cards well - and chances are that they will, based on their previous games - they can probably even keep a huge number of players simply because they will have found friends and guilds that they like.

    Do I see myself playing WoW two years from now? Perhaps. Probably not as much as I would be playing it would I get a beta key right now, but I will be there, if only to keep in touch with my guildies and friends... and of course, to help (or harrass, depending on your viewpoint) newbies.

    So, as always, take what the fanboys (and girls - let's be Politically Correct here!) with a grain of salt, add what the hardcore nay-sayers have to say about WoW and divide it by two, and you'll probably have something inaccurate but somewhere close by the truth.
