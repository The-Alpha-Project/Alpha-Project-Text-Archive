I would like them to do a AC1 type system, no expansions, or atleast not so often, but with regular monthly content updates to push the storylines along.

    And the number of classes. The number of classes really means nothing, all that matters is how many skills and how each one is balanced. When it comes down to it a class is just a way to force a person down a certian path. Making the game easier to balance (skills, party system).

    A game with 1 class with 100 skills or a game with 10 classes with 10 skills each, esstentailly the same thing. Just when you go down a certian path, say healing magic, one of the games closes off all the other skills to you and gives you the title 'Preist' while the other keeps it much more open ended, which it turns makes it harder to balance. WoW will have the basic 8 archetypes and you should be able to customize from there. ex. I Warrior has three known stances, BattleStance, DefensiveStance, and Berserker. Blizzard could have split the warrior into 3 classes, giving each class only 1 of the stances and a new name (Defender for Defensive stance, etc.) This would have made 2 new classes by adding nothing to the game, 10 is better then 8 WOOO! But they left it open for you decide whether you wanted to become pure Defender of a Defender/Zerker and so on.
