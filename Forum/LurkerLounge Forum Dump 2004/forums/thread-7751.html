Saxywoo,Oct 25 2004, 06:44 AM Wrote:Of course, we won't know what the trees there are until we get them, but before we just start dumping points into the talents, I thought that coordinating the testing would be best.

I suppose, but keep in mind you can retrain talents all you want, so I suggest that individuals experiment freely and try different configurations. Also, I suggest that you attempt to reserve judgement about individual talents until you really try them out. In other words, don't base your judgements on the talent description alone. I ended up liking some priest talents that I thought sounded "useless" in the beginning. And along those lines, don't get caught up in "group think" on what the "best" talents are and what the "useless" ones are. Keep in mind that a group at Blizzard spent a long time thinking up and designing the talents, and at the time, they didn't think they were useless. This doesn't mean that there aren't "useless" talents. It just means that at the time they didn't think they were useless, and it helps to keep an open mind and figure out why they didn't think they were useless at the time.

Also, note that what people often forget is that there really are different player styles. Some talents that are useless to one person are pivotal to another.
