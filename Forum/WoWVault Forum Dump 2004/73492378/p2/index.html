The people complaining about this change really don't get the point...

For any given encounter, anyone with mana was only ever intended to have a certain amount of it. That's why drinking during combat is being removed.

You were never ever intended to be able to have almost unlimited mana during combat. This makes any mana based class over powered.

Encounters are designed based on the fact that the people attacking the mob have a certain skillset. The devs know approximately what abilities the players will have so they can plan the difficulty accordingly. With the old system, the devs had to assume that mages have unlimited mana. This means that all party members have unlimited health. So basically, all your players will live forever if you have enough healers.

With eating and drinking during combat in the game, the only way the mobs in high end encounters can win is to do rediculous amounts of damage. What I mean is single or double shot kills. It's the only way they could possible win. This type of gameplay gets old really fast. It's not that much fun working your way to a boss mob only to be killed in one shot.

With eating and drinking removed, the mobs don't have to do as much damage. With there being a good chance that the player won't get a heal (because the healer is out of mana) the fight can be a lot more dynamic. The mob doesn't have to one shot kill people because the damage it does is not trivialized by a healer with unlimited mana.

You have to realize that the game was never designed with the ability for casters to have unlimited mana. The mobs aren't designed for it and unlimited mana completely removes the devs ability to design encounters. This isn't a feature that the devs intentionally put in game and then removed. They never intended for players to drink during combat. Because players are doing it, they have to remove their ability to do so.

If after reading this you still don't get it (or simply don't agree), then there's nothing I can say. This change absolutely had to be made. It's literally the difference between a game where all encounters are the same (and easily won) to a game were encounters can be different and challenging.
