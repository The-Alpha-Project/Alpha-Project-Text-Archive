"WoW just seems to lack the community aspect that a lot of other MMORPG's have and that's a shame. I logged on in Darkforge today and the first thing i see in chat is a bunch of players verbally abusing each other over such mundane things as quests and locations. Stupid.

Now I'm not someone who likes to group all the time, I like to solo about half of my playing time to. The problem is that for the most part you don't have to group at all in this game...EVER! What I don't understand, and never will apparently, is why someone who doesn't want to interact with anyone would play a MMORPG instead of going out and buying NWN or some other single player RPG.

What's really ironic though is that the players Blizz is building this game for are the same players who are going to hit 60 and then quit. Hopefully when that happens and all the teenage b-netters have moved on to something else they'll take the game in a more cooperative friendly direction. Community really in the end does make or break a game like this. Who here would argue that EQ would still be going strong w/o the strong relationships that have been built over years in the game. Heck, a buddy of mine still plays it even though he really no longer likes the game, what he does like is the friends he's made playing it.

Instead of worrying about players lvling too fast and quitting (durability & regeants), Blizzard should be worrying about building a community that lasts. The players that end up keeping MMO games going are usually the one's that aren't going to play a game where every other line in the chat window is a personal insult or flame. "

"What you're seeing is not entirely WoW's fault. You're seeing what happens when the casual powerwhiners get too influential. Since they don't like grouping rewarding more than soloing with their oft-heard cries of "I don't have time to group!" they managed to force this game to reward soloing as much or more (at some levels at least).

Casual powerwhiners are a pox on the mmorpg industry. This board is exhibit "A". Much much worse than dewds, or ubers, or even cheaters. Most of what they want revolve around making the games easier, faster, more instantly gratifying, more convenient. Or, in other words, dumb it down so they don't get outshined by people who want it more. Most of their suggestions do NOT make the game better, only as I said, easier and faster.

And they won't rest until they've wrecked every game they try. The funny thing is they are usually the first to get bored and leave, but they never blame the changes brought in to satisfy them. Nope. Must be "someone else's fault".... "




I am hoping that with all the 60+ content they add, it will require more grouping and socializing to keep this game longer than 6 months.
