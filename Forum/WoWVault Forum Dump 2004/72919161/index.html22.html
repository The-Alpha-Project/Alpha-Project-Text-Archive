Currently a lot of crafters complain that they don't really have a viable market for thier wares because loot drops are often as good or better than what can be crafted.

In addition, with no item decay/upkeep the market can quickly run dry of paying customers of those items crafters can sell.

How about this:

Make crafted items anywhere from 10-20% better on average (When first created) than most loot drops but also make crafted items decay over time getting steadily worse (Durabilty).

This will give a good demand for crafted items by wealthier players who can afford to buy and maintain such items for the extra stats.

In addition, it gives a demand for smiths to repair these items. (Good money sink)

The idea here is make both crafted and looted items have an advantage one way or the other.

- Crafted items rock for stats but required maintenance (Money sink) to keep them in peek working order. (Good for PvP)

- Loot items, though not quite as good will never decay. (Good for PvE)


One added thing to consider is perhaps make all crafted items bind on aquire and to randomate the 10-20% craft bonus to stats only when they are equiped for the first time.

This puts a bit of random fun in the mix and allows a crafter to sell his merchandise to the buyer not knowing ahead of time if he will get +10% or +15% or +20% bonus to start. You plunk your money down and take your chances. This avoids crafters not being able to sell stuff that is only +5% or some such nonsense.



Discuss....

I prefer the best armor/weapons be quest items. Some people say this leads to everyone having the same stuff. But that isn't true (or doesn't have to be) because quests often involve killing critters. So you could have a quest involve killing the same critter the item would have dropped from anyway.

Plus you can make even harder quests by requiring drops from multiple critters whereas plain old uber loot drops only require one kill. So actually quest items can be made more rare than uber drops if they take the steps to make it so.

But back to crafted stuff...

I'd just make crafted stuff not have to compete with loot by not overlapping what you can make. If potions only come from crafters (and they are worth using) then you know that craft will do well. For consumable craft items that seems easy to do.

For weapons/armor you could avoid overlap in a few ways:

1) Have certain slots only come from crafted, never dropped.

or

2) Have certain level ranges only come from crafted, never dropped.

or

3) Have crafting for weapons/armor be just about item improvement to dropped loot rather than creation from scratch.
