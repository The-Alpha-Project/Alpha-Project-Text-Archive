Date Posted: 10/11 9:27pm Subject: RE: Durability + epic instances = problem
I'm sure they'll tone it down.

The DAoC item decay system never bothered me. I thought it was an interesting idea and had a trivial effect on my gameplay - a minor money sink, a very minor inconvenience, and I think I might've completely worn out exactly one item over a year and a half playing over 30 characters. The positve was that more worn out items weren't very desirable so they'd pretty much leave the economy. When items never leave the economy the economy gets more and more whacked over time - not that any player-based economy I've ever seen wasn't whacked in *some* way.

With WoW's emphasis on fun I'm not sure how the durability thing fits in. I imagine that once it's tuned properly it'll just be something extra you do whenever you go to town to sell or train - adding a little bit of tedium to town runs (as in DAoC). I don't see that as being much fun. I'm playing some D2 for fun while waiting for OB - and almost every time I return to town to sell I "repair all" - it's pretty much pointless seeing as how I have gold overflowing from my vault at all times.

I'd prefer to see them come up with some other way to do money sinks, like charging upkeep for mounts, upkeep for guilds (guild has to pay X based on number of members - each member pays a portion of X base don their level), dues for professions (do you think these forges and things are free?), taxes, or something that doesn't add tedium to your daily gameplay.

Even so, the only characters that really need money sinks are capped characters who are accumulating much more than they're spending - those are the characters that wreck the economy (by vastly overpaying for anything they want just to avoid exerting any effort - like buying low level materials, twink gear, etc). Once you cap out and have everything you want you don't value money very much. It's been that way in just about every MMORPG and RPG ever created.

They need a money sink, plain and simple. Without one the economy becomes extremely inflated.

I think AC has the best method for doing this. You basically have to pay money to play any character. It sounds a lot worse than it is mind you. For instance to use magic you'd have to buy spell components, the highest level one being rather pricey. To use magic items you'd have to buy mana stones.

Actually thinking about this as I write I realise the mana system of AC is exactly like durability in it's functionality.

Some of your items have a mana pool and a burn rate. The item works 100% as long as it has mana. When the mana runs out it won't work until you add more mana. To restore the mana you use a mana stone which can be purchased in varying degrees of strength for a varying cost. These mana stones can be carried around and used whenever in an instant.

So put in variable stats for total durability and degrees of damage an item will recieve from use. This adds a nice touch to the economy.

And the main thing I'm thinking would be great: add in craftable items that repair your items. Have the components need to be purchased, so there's still the money sink, but you'd no longer have to go to town to repair. Also this would add something that is in constant demand from the professions, something I think is rather lacking atm. Have the different professions each produce items to repair different things too, so each profession is equal.
