Well so far it's all theory since we don't know how it'll work in practice.

What the response says, however, is that you only get *some* minus to your honour for *attacking* people much lower in level than you. Notice, *attacking* them. Defending is another matter. And killing people of your own level will in turn award you with about the same amount of plus to your honor.

As I said before, this system is clearly in place to prevent some idiots making a career out of killing lowbies only. I firmly believe you can initiate an attack against 10 lowbies with no trouble at all. It's when you kill 10 lowbies 10 times in a row, when it becomes a problem... just as it should.

Simply stating that killing enemy NPC's that have a quest-specific purpose for them gives a big minus to honor is in my opinion a clear mark of the right sort of attitude. Afterall, if anything amounted to griefing then that did.. preventing people from even getting quests? Come on.

People are overreacting. It's obvious there's no point in making a PvP game where you can only fight others of your own level. Why would you ever want to level in that case, as you would never get any power relative to others? And I don't think Blizzard has made that mistake. They just want to make sure it's actual PvP, not "lvl 60 vs lvl 20 gankfest". For that, I think this is the best solution one could think of.

As I said, I will kill plenty of people who are so much weaker than I am that they have no chance. Yet I expect to be killed by many people such as me as well. I will no be an asshat and grief by stalking the same person over and over again and doing some stupid dancing animations or somesuch over them with the only purpose of pissing them off. I will simply be efficient and kill as I should in a war against the horde. And every now and then, I'll even let the other people go, knowing their lives were in my hands.
