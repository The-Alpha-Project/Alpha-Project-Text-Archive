Here it is, again, for the nth time, spelled out in layman's math in one way it could work but it could really work a ton of ways... Though it's not my job to come up with a method, but merely propose a solution in a format as I have done that could work and that obviously involves very simplistic algorithm processing that is darmatically less than the current area load processing, aggro range processing, and even attack processing that is already in place.

Honor works as honor works, but killing NPCs of your level or higher gains honor.

You lose honor for killing x number of players at a determined to be low level (level-10, level-20, whatever), wherein you actively attack them first and where killing more than x will continue a loss of honor. This could also be tailored to location (if a 60 is hunting in a level normal level 20 hunting ground, for instance). x equates to a number that is found to be a good representation where a person who kills however many of the determined low level range can be considered griefing. x can be modified to an algebraic equation that composes a possible ratio of kills by level, for example if you obtain a 0.75 or higher ratio of level to level kills, meaning you kill more of your level or higher rather than lesser of your level, especially much less than your level, you will not lose honor. There's a few ways where x could be measured in this manner, that's just two examples. Being attacked automatically wipes all chances to lose honor and can only result in the gain of honor. A member of a group attacking a much higher level person will enable the player to kill the whole group without any loss of honor.

- What does this prevent? Nothing.
- What does it do? It creates a goal or direction for players to follow that leads away from needless griefing.
- Is it a solution to just remove all forms of griefing? Nope, just a direction. In fact, players can and would, if implemented, create characters that they use solely for griefing if they enjoying such things, while still having multiple characters to play that can enjoy benefiting from a gain of honor without the need to grief.

This could lead to a color system, if wanted, displaying a change in colors as people approach a lower ratio score or general score and otherwise with a higher ratio score. This would work well with the idea of player titles based on honor level (Sir, Lord, Master, Magister, Lady, etc.).
