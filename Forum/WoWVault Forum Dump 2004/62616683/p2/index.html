I agree, wild fluctuations between race and class popularity aren't half as important to game mechanics as basic faction equality. I think that some of the people with concerns like " the horde is going to have fewer healers" or "The alliance is going to have too many nukers" are doing just what you're trying to avoid with polls/threads like this, and that's focusing too much on individual bits of statistical information and not taking enough time to look at how those facts relate to each other within the confines of the WoW system.
Balance is mostly an issue for PvP, but having equal numbers of people in every career doesn't neccesarily mean that side is "better". Sure one side might trade some healers for Tanks, but as long as players on both sides have the presence of mind to curb their strategies towards the obvious strengths/weaknesses of the over-all factional make-up, it won't matter. Every class has a weakness that can be taken advantage of.
Finaly I think it's important to repeat one of my business professor's Mantras: Unpopular doesn't always mean uncompetative, and I think if Blizzard acknowledges that fact before making any changes in the interest of balance, It's not going to matter if 10% of all WoW characters are night elf hunters and there's only 1 troll priest per server.
