I think that WoW is all about packing every class with as much utility and versatility as will fit. The hunter is no exception to this.

There is no hinderance to the hunter's actual or immersion/roleplay based abilities because of mail armor. They dont sneak. They hunt, not hide. For this kind of woodsman it isnt about trying to go undetected, its about finding that comfortable balance that lets them actually interact with nature.(as a predator :P )
NPC hunter already in the game have pets around 75-85% of their level. I cant say for sure what it is, but it is noticably below their own level.
Rogues have lots of toys already. They're about to get beefed up even more, plus talents. Rogues are more direct in how they deal with their targets. They are assassins. They use themselves and things to improve what they can do. The hunter has tools at his disposal to make use of places, things, and entities outside of himself.
Feign death isnt that attractive of a skill because it gives you the games very unwanted rez sickness. Its also more fitting for a hunter than a rogue. Accomplished outdoor experts know how to react to an animal attack from beasts such as bears and alligators so that the onslaught will cease. Many animals do not immediately their prey and the majority of fights you might find yourself in with animals wont revolve around if they can eat you. Using the same skills against sentients and humans isnt that much of a stretch, especially for a fantasy game.

I have to disagree with both your concept of the rogue and the hunter. It seems to me like you may have somewhat of a bias towards augmenting the rogue instead of judging the hunter on his own merit. If you are in beta I'd suggest waiting for the next patch. If you arent, I suggest waiting for the next patch and then looking into what the rogue class in WoW is.

Oh, you do know hunters use mana, right? They have some druidic aspects that lend themselves to the behavior we see implied by the hunter's list of skills.

This pretty much holds true for any MMORPGH that I've played with pet classes and PvP.

I don't know about what level the pet will be in relation to the owner. Personally, I hope that it will be be the same, but will deminished abilities. One of the main problems with pet classes in DAoC is that the pets are so level in comparision to players in PvP that they either can't hit or do damage at all and are thus not worth worrying about. I don't think that a Hunters pet should do as much damage as say a mage or a warrior, but they should be able to do significant damage to make people and even mobs to realize that ignoring them is a mistake.

To wrap this up I'm going to say that it does look like Hunters are going to get quite a bit and not everything that I would have expected. I could argue that my vision of a Rogue is one of stealth, espionage, and theft not that of a massive damage assassin.
