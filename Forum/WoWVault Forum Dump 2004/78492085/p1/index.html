Date Posted: 11/9/04 2:06pm Subject: RE: WoW vs. Guild Wars - people who have played both....
GW was very pretty, and I liked the premise. Granted, I only played for a few hours, so my opinion is definitely WOW-biased, but you ARE on the WOW boards, you know.

The combat system has a mix of abilities much like WOW. I love that. My frame of reference is AC, where you could swing or fire your weapon or cast a spell. That's it. The number of abilities at your disposal, however is 8. Well, that wouldn't be THAT bad if I could choose which 8 I could use at any given time, but it's only possible to change them in a town. Sorry - I don't want to buy a new skill and then only be able to use it at the expense of others.

You can't jump. You say, "So what??" I say, "It destroys immersion." Seriously, you see a chest or mob 2 nanometers away, but you can't get to it because you can't jump over a cliff or off the trail you're on. The linearity (yes, I made that up) gets old FAST.

I love that there are NPC party members. Great touch, and they're remarkably smart. This helps soloability and makes it possible to tackle things that you can't alone. GREAT feature. However, the limitations on immersion in the world just killed it for me.

Date Posted: 11/9/04 2:20pm Subject: RE: WoW vs. Guild Wars - people who have played both....
I've played both (ST1 for WoW (and downloading OB) and the WPE for GW).

Guildwars PvP is fun, and I usually don't even like PvP. It still has some major balancing issues to work over though such as the Warrior/Monk who seems to tank through everything (get 4+ of those on the opposite team and blah...). Admittedly though it's got quite a while to release, so plenty of time to mess with those. PvP overall was pretty but hectic. Targetting was sorta hard, either by having to scroll through all the targets looking for one or managing to click one with your mouse (or target closest).

I definately like WoW in that you have the capability to use any ability that you have learned at any time. Guildwars limits you to 8 abilities per battle. It does this apparently to try to diversify the basic character types, but I felt it was a really limiting factor. There's all those nifty little abilities that have a special case to use them, but you really can't throw them on your 8 ability bar because you need more generalized abilities.

PvE is what I usually concentrate on and I have to say that while GW is again, very pretty, the 'paths' that you are constrained to make me go nuts. It's like dungeon seige where a little hill/rock/creek constrains you and you can't pass that way unlike WoW where you can walk most all over the place.

I played a healer in GW and I found that I really spent all my time in both PvE and PvP staring at 6-8 red bars on a little corner of my screen for hours on end. I haven't been a prime healer in WoW, but my experiance in party play is that healers have a bit more time to do things.

I know in general it's bad to comment on gamer cultures, but GW just seemed to be heads and tails more elitist and competitive than WoW players that I had met. Probably due to it's PvP focus that naturally draws that sort of player. The only 'friendly' person I really met was another guy who started up with me and also had no idea of what to do (and during the WPE I did run 1 char to 19.5ish, 2 to 18, and one to 17, so I did spend some time in game).

In both games, at least from what i've seen, the grind is disguised in quests which is a good way of doing it.

I might buy GW because of the no monthly cost, and just to goof around with once in a while.. but not as a main game. WoW during ST1 really got my attention so i'll get that one and spend the most time on it.

And FYI, I never played DAOC.
