Date Posted: 10/15/04 9:40pm Subject: RE: What's a Bnet Kiddie & A pos Troll?
Bnet is referring to battle.net which is the medium players connect to when playing multiplayer Blizzard titles.

An example of where the term "bnet kiddie" is derived from is in the online community of Blizzard's Diablo II. This is a standalone RPG with multiplayer capabilities through the battle.net medium. There is no pay-to-play involved as the online service is entirely free. There is a maximum of 8 players per game and the "in-game" community is lumped into various chat channels.

Because it is free, the game is not restricted to an audience that has a credit card/checking account. The game Diablo II was also very much of an instant gratification game in addition to being a worldwide hit. Consequently, there were a number of immature younger players of which people refer to as bnet kiddies. The term bnet kiddies may be used to imply such things as unjustified (and incessant) whines, typing with virtual slang (or purposely using incorrect punctuation/grammar/etc), or just displaying a very immature attitude. Sometimes, though, the term is used so loosely that it is just meant as an insult with a negative connotation.
