How do you implement stealth so that it doesn't equate to complete invisibility and doesn't unbalance games.

Problems with stealth that I see:
- stealth is usually implemented as invisibility which is totally ridiculous and leads to...
- stealth, when implemented as invis, requires ZERO skill - you just press a button and fear nothing.
- stealth can easily lead to abuse of content (like in WoW (where rogues can farm high level minerals in instances with zero competition and zero risk)
- freedom of movement due to being invis - a rogue can move freely at any time even if it's at a slower rate

Things to consider to make stealth reasonable:
- the more a rogue is moving (distance, speed) the easier it should be to detect them
- perhaps stealth should be implemented differently for PvE and PvP - although you still have lots of potential for abuse if stealth is more effective in PvE
- give some critters the ability to see right thru stealth - and place them randomly in instances to prevent farming
- usually stealthers have higher than normal when striking from stealth - but if stealth is made to require player skill and isn't implemented as brain dead invis then this type of damage should be even higher (ie, reward challenge)
- in a game like WoW there are anti-stealth mechanisms - engineered items, potions etc - the thing is, not everyone will have those yet any stealther can still press the stealth button to be stealthed.
- Critters don't seem to vary in their ability to detect stealth - it only seems to be level based. Critters should not only vary in species with their ability to detect stealth but should vary as individuals - that'd keep stealthers on their toes!

As it is, I feel stealth is usually implemented in a such a way to make it too easy to use and too easy to abuse - so the result is you have a lot of idiots that use it as an easy way to do both PvE and PvP, and in particular to gain unfair advantages at both. If stealth actually required some thought and skill there would be a lot less abuse and rogues would become known for their skill and prowess instead for pressing their 'I win' button.

As someone who played DAoC, I can't remember how many times I'd be running across a wide open field in broad daylight just to get whacked by a stealther. Pretty friggin amazing when someone can hide behind a blade of grass and be setup to assissinate a moving target, eh? As a side note - please - someone who played a stealther - explain to me how something like this is acceptable or desirable, even in a fantasy game.

Part of your complaint is that stealth is implemented as full invisibility. In part you don't like it because you get jumped out of nowhere and you offer up 'unrealistic' as one of the reasons why.

The simple fact of the matter is in a PvP game, if part of the purpose of stealth is to give a suprise advantage to the stealther (which may not always be the case) then it purely invisible is the only way to make it work. If the character is there, but dithered out there are too many client side hacks (changing the character models, messing with the gamma/dithering map, using packet sniffing radar) available to defeat it. Put simply anything less than pure invisiblity is effectively no stealth at all.

As to the 'unrealistic' part, it's all in the name. In a world of undead nations and wizards, there are a thousand RP explanations for how rogues 'move unseen'. If you don't like invisible people, then you don't like invisible people and that is a valid opinion. But please stop throwing 'unrealistic' up as an argument.

As for a differnt implementation of stealth, you just have to figure out what you want it to be. Personally, my vision of stealth is to be a counter to ranged attackers. Ranged guys have a natural advantage over melee guys. Namely, they get to shoot them a lot before the melee guys can attack.

Stealth exists to allow the melee stealther to close range without getting shot up. To that end, I see no problem with simply having the stealther be visibly stealthed (mildly dithered) but still visible but unable to be affected by ranged attacks. You'd still be able to target them in melee. Part of this package also implies that they would move at full speed even while stealthed, and probably even have some kind of sprint ability to allow them to close range.

Blizzard didn't do that, and it's not a suggestion for how to change WoW. However, that is an other possible way to implement it.
