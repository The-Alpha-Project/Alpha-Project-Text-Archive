Going to address the last quote first:

"I don't feel that dieing and respawning back at a bindpoint is a death penalty"

(1) It is "dying". Sorry, just tired of you misspelling the word.

(2) I consider it a penalty - especially if the game uses respawning like EQ does (your items stay on your corpse).


Now, to these two:


"Why have a Death Penalty?" & "What does a DP serve for you?"


This has been answered many, many, MANY times in this thread.

For most people, it is the key ingredient in balancing risk vs reward, which makes the fight personally satisfying to them. For a lot of people, it adds to the thrill of the fight, knowing they have more at stake should they lose - it becomes an additional excitement factor that they like to have in their games, because they play games for fun and excitement.

It is also punitive, designed to discourage people from recklessly attacking content that they cannot win against. It is designed to cause players to play more carefully, to put more thought into their strategy and tactics, and to think more carefully about what mobs they attack.


There have been arguments against all of these:

Some people don't feel they need a punishment, that the failure of not winning the fight is enough for them.

Some people feel that it is a GAME, and as such they want their entertainment to be as far removed from adverse penalties as possible. They suffer enough in real life, they play games to have fun - a positive experience all the time, every time.




The thing I get out of this thread is that people who dislike death penalties generally seem to dislike negative experience of any kind in their games. They play games to ALWAYS have fun, and any kind of adversity in a game is frowned upon by them. I can totally understand this rational. If that is what works for you, if that is how you prefer to have your games created, good for you.


The other thing I get out of this thread is that people who support death penalties enjoy overcoming odds, obstacles, etc, and they generally enjoy the added thrill-excitement from "cheating death", so to speak. They seem to prefer their games to more closely emulate real life - and for them not every gaming experience (individual sit-down play-period) *needs* to be a positive experience. They are people who are perfectly willng to accept the consequences of failure and any punishments that the developers choose to associate with it.



It is, at the very basic level, a tale of two different breeds of game players.

And what we have here is a difference of opinion on why death penalties are important, and necessary (or unnecessary, depending on your camp).


Two different opinions. They cannot be reconciled. Live with it.

For my money, I would prefer a system of die-and-wait-for-a-rez or respawn-at-bind-with-all-gear-and-permanent-xp-loss (or debt.) If gear is as important in WoW as it is in EQLive, then the death penalty should be harsh in terms of lost time, or even temporarily weakened attributes, but never with the need for a corpse-run to recover your gear.

A game in which players assume the roles of characters and act out fantastical adventures, the outcomes of which are partially determined by chance, as by the roll of dice."

Special notice to the "assume the roles of characters" and "act out fantastical adventures", and how this contrasts with "watch the blue bar go up" and "watch the character get +1 on their character sheet".

Adherence to this paradigm insists that character progression is not "saved" at level demarcations. To treat the entire life of the character as a single challenge means that "de-leveling" is possible. To treat the game as an RPG, the characters should be able to "save" at a minimum of between each discrete challenge. /shrug
