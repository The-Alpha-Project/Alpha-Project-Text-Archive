Unlike DAoC you may have every "Mage spell" in the game if you are the appropriate level mage. There is no specialization of one line in any class anymore. Every equal level hunter has the exact same abilities and stats as the other, with the only variances being choice of items worn and talents.

Talents is where you get to customize your character. The closest thing to DAoC would be Realmskills. It really doesn't give you much that is NEW, all it does is allow you to make certain abilities or spells more powerful or give you access to a new toy.

You gain 1 talent per level gained, starting at level 10. At level 60 you have 51 talent points.

Date Posted: 9/21/04 11:18am Subject: RE: Compared to Daoc.... and what are talents?
In WoW you have class skills and you have talents. Class skills can be purchased from class trainers or awarded from quests. This half of character development is more like anarchy online than DAOC.

Talents are enhancements to your class skills. You have to decide how you are going to spend your talent points. For example, if you were a mage, you could focus most of your talent points in your fire tree, making your fire class skills more powerful than your ice and arcane skills. These are similar to how Diablo II skills were.

The concept is to allow players to select any and all skills they want and allow customization with talent trees. This helps prevent people from gimping themselves.

Hope this helps.
