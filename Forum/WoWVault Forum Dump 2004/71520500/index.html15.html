Ok, here's my quick little review..

Here's my background..

AC was my first MMORPG. I played it off and on since Beta in '99. In fact, a friend and I are playing it now (because there's nothing better out). I played many other MMORPGs (Including DAoC, AC2, Earth and Beyond, Planetside, SWG and a few others). Basically, I haven't found one that's as good as AC (IMHO).

Now, how do I like WoW?

I think that it's a lot of fun. I really like the idea that questing is how you level (for the most part).

So far, my highest character is level 18 and I've got a second character which is almost 15 (I'm playing him now). I've got about 76 hours play time.

I'm actually really looking forward to getting some friends into Beta (or going into open Beta). I love playing MMORPGs with friends. I don't normally group with strangers because my friends and I like to use voice chat. For that reason, I've pretty much soloed most of my time.

Overall, WoW is a really nice game. However, since it's beta and not all of the content is in yet, it is definately lacking in a few areas.

First off, they are still tweaking the XP and the rest system. Due to recent changes, the XP you get from quests is not enough to level continuously without grinding. Luckily this will be addressed.

PvP still needs a lot of work. On the PvE servers, PvP players can run around and kill NPCs and basically annoy PvE players. Again, this is going to be addressed. Changes are coming with the next patch to help fix the problem.

Basically, there are a lot of little things which still need to be added. The game can definately use more quests (for specific classes, races and levels) and all of the Tradeskills can use a little bit more work (in terms of new recipes and tweaking of existing recipes). So far Battlegrounds, Hunters, boat and zeplin travel haven't even been implemented yet.

Overall, WoW is looking vary promising. If the higher level content (40 - 60ish) is the same quality as the lower level stuff and Blizzard adds all of the promised features, I have no doubt that WoW will absolutely blow a lot of people away.

The thing I like the most about WoW is that is has so much promise. It is going to be released more stable, tested and feature rich than any other MMORPG in recent memory (like the last 4 years). This means that the Live team will not need to spend all of their time fixing the game. I can't wait to see what the game will look like when open Bata and Retail arrive. I also can't wait to see what the live team comes up with once we are in Retail.

They're not affraid to try things (even things that are drastically differnt) just to see if they will work better in game.

I'll take a stab here.

I have tried most of the MMORPG's out right now. Some I got to high levels, some only mid-levels. Games I have played are: AC1, AC2, AO, DAOC, SWG, EQ, FFXI, L2, Horizons.

I have only been playing WoW for about 2-3 weeks. I have a 16 Paladin and a 12 Warrior. I'd be much higher but I've been very casual and I have some other alts in the <10 range. I recently started a Mage and I have a Rogue I am going to work on.

I'll rate some of the systems and I'll probably post again when a new patch comes in, because things can change.

Graphics: They are in the Warcraft III style. If you played WC III and saw the full screen scenes, cut scenes (not movies) you get the idea of the STYLE. It's not photo-like, it has the use of bright colors and a cartoony look. I happen to like this look and it suits the game. The best thing is the game doesn't run choppy or laggy, like AC2 and AO did at times, especially around lots of people. There are amazing Cities like Ironforge and Stormwind that are zoneless, complete with shops, some with upstairs and downtstairs. These shops are decorated, have burning candles or bubbling potions, pictures on the wall, animalskin rugs, etc. The detail is fantastic. In the snowy areas players and animals leave footprints in the snow and your breath steams out from your mouth. The game uses pixel shaders for the water, which looks good but it isn't done yet (water effects as per the devs). There is also another effect where things shine in the sunlight, like glare on road pavers or smoothed areas of snow or ice. The game runs great and screenshots and movies don't do it justice. Spell effects are good but amazingly so and animations are smooth.

UI and Movement: A good solid interface that I think is still being tweaked a bit. It's similar to DAOC and is XML-able. I had no problems figuring stuff out.
WASD is the default. I don't think I remapped anything. Enter to chat. R to reply. You can change the size of the chat text and make new windows. You can also put a transparency around text and change the color. The default is clear, but it's actually a window. You can swim UP and DOWN into water (some quests have parts where you need to go underwater), you can jump. There is a full screen map that is not too detailed and a smaller map inside the compass in the upper right. I would like to note that this map is zoomable but DOES NOT SHOW MOBS or PLAYERS. It does show group members. There are also little notch waypoints in the corner for your corpse or nearby POI's you have discovered. As you discover new areas you get XP and it fills in the world map for you. Entering a dungeon changes the little map and it does map out for your as you walk through.

Sound: Excellent music that changes with the area. No combat music. Terrific ambient sounds of birds, wind, wolves, people, anvils clanking, etc. Combat sounds are good, with different weapons making different sounds. Swords clank, Hammers make a thud, etc.

Quests: So many quests! Some are simple and some are multiple step quests. Some require you to go into a dungeon, some are delivery, escorts, kill tasks, collections. I had a quest to go to a farm and steal 10 pumpkins. There were bad guys all over the place. Then, another part of the quest, I was to deliver this laced pumpkin pie to a prisoner in the basement of an Inn. I went down there, there was a guard and this prisoner. I gave him the pie and he thanked me for the food, he then changed into some kind of creature, he then groaned and died. Quests take you through most of your level, you'll always have several quests going at once. The quests seemed to push you further out into the world, although very slowly. I have yet to see any quests like AC1, where you needed 2 groups to move levers, etc (Like Virindi Amulet or Shendolain). Overall they are really good, only some annoyed me (drop rates). Rewards consist always of some value of XP, cash and many times a reward. It could be a weapon, piece of armor, food or even a buff or new skill. I had to do a quest with my Paladin to get a rez spell. The armor or weapon may or may NOT be better than what you can buy or loot for your level. Some quest items you may use, some you may not.

Classes and combat: I have only dabbled in some, because I haven't gotten past 20. I'm finding the Paladin is decent, but not great. They have no way to do a ranged attack. It seems they are more backup healer and support than anything else. Their skills are mostly protects that last like 20 seconds. I seem to have like 3 attacks and then 20 buffs/protects. The pally uses Mana like a mage or cleric. The warrior I have can use more weapons and is very interesting. A red bar, called rage increases as you fight. This becomes like your mana for your skills. Once you have enough you can then kick off skills. I really can't wait for the Hunter class to be put in. That's what this game needs. There's no real ranged class, aside from mages. There are bows and powder type guns but the hunter class is THE class to be if you are a range freak. I think that will change the game a lot.

PvP: I didn't PVP yet so I don't know how it is. All I know is that Mages are pretty uber right now (IMHO) and range kicks ass this game for PVP.

Overall feel: I have to say I look forward to logging on. I haven't felt that way in a long time (since AC1) about a game. There are some things that make me say "Hey, this kind of reminds me of DAOC, but this is like AC2 and this I have never seen before".

Trust me, it's not a clone of anything I have played, but elements remind you of other games.

WoW in a Nutshell

My Experience:
5 years playing at least one of these: AO, EQ, UO, SB, FFXI. For WoW beta at the time of this writing I have a level 39 Warlock on the PvE server and a 26th Mage on the PvP server. Also 5+ characters under lvl 12.

PROS:
-The most artistically stunning and vibrant world ever seen in a MMORPG, or any Computer game, nothing even comes close. The world builders have done something truly amazing with this game. The shear number of different and interesting places to visit is mind-boggling.
-A design model that concentrates on fast-paced battles with multiple enemies rather than the slow 1 mob at a time approach.
-Extremely impressive instanced super-dungeons that are fun dungeon romps with a group of select friends.
-Fantastic GUI.
-Excellent quest system with quests everywhere. About 40% of your experience comes just from completing quests.
-The best crafting system ever seen in a MMORPG, yes better than SWG. Crafting depends on getting the right materials, not endless trial and error.
-Nice Auction House system that works just like ebay. Better than FFXI's.
-Great Talent system that is used to make two chars of the same class different. (At the time of this writing only 2 classes have their talents implemented.) The talent system is very similar to the skill system of Diablo 2.
-A beast-flight (gryphon, bat, etc) transportation system that is working surprisingly well with the gameplay. Even at high levels, I find myself using this service to travel around the world at least once per play session. Not to mention the incredibly impressive tour it gives you of the traveled areas.

CONS:
-WoW doesn't add anything innovative to the genre. Pretty much everything found in WoW has been done in a previous MMORPG.
-Arguably too much of a solo oriented gameplay. Too many "best-to-do-solo" quests.
-Technically speaking, a slightly dated graphic engine. Where this really shows its ugly head is the non-transparent water and the very low poly count character models.
-A somewhat lack of challenging gameplay because of WoW's almost non-existent death penalty and the moderately fast leveling rate. The game is arguably too easy. Time played = level.

Conclusion:
I'm being very honest here, WoW is easily the best MMORPG I have ever played. The game is addicting as hell. The game, in Phase 2 when I started, was far more complete than most current MMORPGs released years ago. Never have I been so excited to go home and play a game since my first days of UO. Personally, the thing that does it for me is the incredibly artistic world design. I can't believe the effort the world builders have done for this title.

EQ2 aside, WoW will blow all existing MMORPGs out of the water. WoW meets Blizzard goal of just making a fun MMORPG. Of course, EQ2 is starting to look even better, but that’s outside of the scope of this review.

Final Score: 9/10
It would of been a perfect score if not for, of course, the cons I listed.

Before I begin, I want to take a moment to clarify a couple of minor things so that most of you will understand the perspective from which I view this game, and for you to know how much salt to take with it. My first MMORPG was Everquest, which I played for just over 2 years. I then played DAoC for another two years. I’ve also had minor experiences in other various MMORPGs (AO, SWG, Horizons). However, I am anything but an MMORPG expert. I’m not on staff for my knowledge of MMORPGs, but for my willingness to go gather information and volunteer where I can. Furthermore, I’m a huge Warcraft fan and have been anticipating this game for a long time (so I am biased). As of right now, my in game experience resides almost solely on my main character, an Undead Warlock (PvP server) which is level 28. My /played is over 100 hours. My play style in MMOs has been largely that of a “tourist”; I’m a very “casual” and social gamer. Lastly, I just want to make it clear that my intention is NOT to recruit you to play WoW, I’m just sharing my experiences and the impressions I’ve been left with for the last 30 days in Beta. It is NOT a definitive review or prediction of what WoW will be when released.

THE BAD

Might as well “stick it to the man” from the beginning and chase off as many folks as I can. Let’s begin with Bit Torrent. It sucks in conjunction with its usage for patching the game. It does a terrible job, especially when compared to other games in the genre. The Character Creation is weak, especially for an MMO coming out this late in the “game.” The current physical customization is simply not up to par with other games that are out or in production. Another weakness in character creation is the inability to customize one’s stats. Although I understand the concept Blizzard is going for with this, I don’t necessarily agree with it. I’m all for KISS (keep it simple, stupid), but this is too simple. The lack of a Tutorial rubbed me the wrong way. Although I’m a MMO vet and was able to quickly figure out what was going on around me, there are going to be true newbs, and the current series of watered down tips and hints isn’t much help. When it comes to the UI, I feel that it should contain more than just one skill bar. Currently, if you want more than one skill bar, you have to use Cosmos. I’m not keen on going to third party applications, even if Blizzard allows them. The future of these is still to be determined. The graphics to the game use a low polygon count, which leave things looking blocky, which folks don’t like. Furthermore, the water surfaces need some work. Some places it looks ok, other places it looks out of place. And it interferes with the third person camera while swimming.

Generic Dungeons, those that are basically low level no-name places, are carbon copies of each other (eg the “spider mine” in the night elf area and the “imp mine” in the troll area). This isn’t too frequent, but once is too much in my book. I’ve also noticed a small problem with the “uniqueness” of certain creature models. For example, why does a sap beast on Stonetalon Peak look the same as the ectoplasms in Wailing Caverns? While we are talking about models, let’s talk about item models. There simply are not enough different equipment models in the game right now. This is a severe weakness when combined to the weak character creation currently in place. Although magical items and drops tend to open up quite a bit more after 20+, Blizzard definitely needs to add more diverse items and never stop. The NPCs in the villages could be a little more interactive also, and was kinda disappointed with how many of them fail to act or interact. The Quest System is also going to redefine the way people interact with strangers. I labeled this in the bad, because it is my feeling that this is going to hurt the game initially, as folks who are so used to the standard of go to camp A and grind away until Level X and then go to Camp B, etc, are going to have a tough time adapting to the pace and flow that WoW is pushing with the quest system.

My last real complaint and reservation with the game is how PvP is going to be implemented and balanced. In my opinion, this game’s success is going to hinge heavily on what it can do in this area. Until I know more, I am going to assume the worst. Here recently Blizzard has made a couple of PvP policies. While admittedly short term fixes, they concerned me greatly because they were too heavily reliant on GM intervention instead of a PvP dynamic that is self correcting. I also fear that PvE, while even at this stage is rich, engaging, and abundant, will simply not provide enough “end game” things to do for players that are anti-PvP. However, both PvP and PvE are nowhere near complete, so much is to be seen.

THE OUTSTANDING (Beware! The following contains rampant fanboism and strong emotion!)

While the polygon count might have been low, the Blizzard art department and world designers have come together to produce the single most beautiful and unique world I’ve ever seen in an MMO. It is utterly mind blowing. The models are superbly rich in detail and look great! The world has been put together in such a fashion that I was NEVER left feeling like the devs got bored one day and decided to cut and paste their way to glory. As a Blizzard fan, the effect that the world and environment had on me was tear jerking (in a good way!). The most memorable such occasion was when I entered the Throne Room in the Ruins of Lordaeron, the place where a few cinematics occurred in WC3. What a magical feeling to be standing there at the very spot where Arthas had usurped the throne and set very tragic events into motion! The towns, cities, ruins, and simple building architecture (they did an awesome job recreating the actual buildings from the RTS series) leave no doubt that you are adventuring in the lands of Azeroth. I can't stress enough the amount of detail that has been put into these! Its incredible! Furthermore, they do an excellent job of making the world feel inhabited, while at the same time portraying the dark days of the past (via the ruins) and the troubles to come (the militaristic stance of many places). The Sound FX are beautiful and really add to the gaming experience at all levels and activities.

WoW has also managed to marry and unite the concept of MMO (persistent world with 1000s of players) and RPG (role playing game)! At no time have I felt like I was in an MMO and had to go to the nearest level appropriate camp to grind my life away in numb oblivion. There’s always something to do or something to get done. The questing system makes leaps and bounds to really get the player active in the world and story. It puts the role playing back into the genre, without having to be a role player! My first quest was to help fight back the Scourge from the Forsaken starting town! I couldn’t believe it. Not in game for more than a couple of minutes, and I’m already knee deep in the story and struggle that defines Warcraft. The whole game proceeds in this fashion, with the quests gently carrying you through the landscape and deeper and deeper into the conflict. The quests, while not the most revolutionary in the genre, are definitely well written and fun. The quests pit you against your enemy, be it horde or alliance, and not so much against the environment and wildlife. I’ve had so many quests take me into human settlements to wreak havoc that it’s wicked cool! (I’ve had to poison dogs, steal pumpkins, sabotage machines, and even plant a WMD, not to mention killing dozens of hateful Alliance dudes!!!)

Trade skills are really well done. Very simple, but very useful. And the gathering skills really play well and give yet another thing to do while out and exploring. Maps are in game, and they are great! The game encourages you to explore and open up parts of your world map by granting you experience in the process. The Elite/Instanced Dungeons(and generic dungeons) are awesome. I’ve been in three thus far (Shadowfang keep, Wailing Caverns/Cavern of Mists, and Razorfen Kraul), and each one was built with obvious care and attention to detail to provide a unique experience for each. The best dungeons I’ve ever seen, bottomline. Furthermore, the Instanced Dungeons make great places for those folks that just wanna group up and grind away without interference. /1 General Chat! It is so nice, even when you’re not grouped, to be able to quickly call for assistance or just listen to good natured banter that is zone wide. Combat is fun and exciting. It’s not revolutionary by any means, but it is fast and fluid and allows the players options. Death is really well handled, and definitely caters to the casual audience. You basically get to choose between making a corpse run or biting the bullet and sucking up an XP loss. Because the graveyards are numerous and pretty well placed, you are never more than 5 minutes away from your corpse.

The current implementation of PvP, while admittedly very base, is fun and exciting in the aspect that you can actually take the fight to the enemy, and you are not restricted to fighting in a desolate and boring no man’s land. Even the Contested Zones are really well done, with Horde and Alliance outposts and towns dotting the landscape that give the players a feeling that they are actually fighting for something other than some long forgotten and half abandoned castles. Other miscellaneous Knick Knacks such as the Warcraft style corpse skeletons on the ground when someone gets killed, the non combat pets, voice emotes, and various other fluff just adds to the overall fun level.

IN CLOSING

This game has brought back that special “first” time feeling for me, that I had originally experienced so long ago in Everquest. It’s amazingly fun, and the game is not even complete yet. Like I’ve told a few RL friends: I never walk away from a game session in WoW feeling like I didn’t have fun or didn’t accomplish anything. Also, I’ve never felt like I had to level to experience the fun stuff (despite getting constantly mauled in pvp). With that said, this game has a long and bumpy road ahead of it still. Its nowhere near complete, so don't hold your breath!

Regardless, outside some major catastrophe, I will most definitely be purchasing this game and playing it for years to come.

Background:

Played EQ, AC1, DAOC
Beta'd and chose not to play release: Neocron, AC2, AO, Eve

Been playing WoW since the undead phase of F&F Alpha.


UI: B (With Cosmos UI: A)

It's actually a fairly decent UI. You have 12 hotkeys, and a lot of the interface screens are accessible easily. However, it takes a 3rd party custom UI to really make the interface shine. Your abilities greay out when unusable, and they highlight in a clock-like fashion.


Graphics: A

They've chosen a comic book style, it works pretty well, and it makes you feel like you're in a Warcraft universe.


Gameplay: D

There are just too many quests. At first it was cool, because you had something to do. But then so does everyone else. If noone else is doing your current quest, you're on your own. Later on, you start needing groups in order to do the instances. If you aren't in a good guild, good luck! The ideal 5 man group breaks down into Warrior, Priest, Mage, and 2 other damage classes. The great holy trinity of EQ rears its ugly head in a game that really should have learnt from its predecessors.


Miscellaneous gripes:

The rest system should never have been born. It doesn't hurt the powergamer, but surprisingly enough, it really hurts the casual gamer. Yes the casual gamer levels up fast when they play, but they aren't receiving the same income as they are killing less mobs, for the same xp earned.

I used to play a lot with my wife. In one of the pushes they changed the way xp was allocated. If you are both at 0% xp in your level, and one is higher level than the other, and you make a kill that is worth 1% of the xp required for the higher level to level up, then the lower level also receives exactly 1% of their xp needed. The lower level will NEVER catch up while you are making the same kills. We tried to agree to only play together, but it just became yet another pointless restriction to the game.

Travel time is excessive. Some of the later quests make you hop around all over the 2 continents. During a bird ride just today, I unloaded the dishwasher, and put in some more dirty dishes, and when I got back I still hadn't landed. The mage gets to alleviate travel time as they get access to self ports and can summon a portal for anyone else. The warlock does have a summon spell, but it requires 2 extra people to activate it, and yes, this is another stupid restriction.

There is no autoloot. There isn't even a "loot all" button. Thankfully the cosmos UI team added an autoloot, but Blizzard consequently disabled the ability to loot from UI code. Again the cosmos team came to our rescue with a loot helper, where the loot window was moved such that the next item to loot was right under your cursor. All you needed to loot was to click, click, click without having to move the mouse. Looting without this helper is just a pita. Oh, the loot window only shows up to 3 items, to loot more you have to click on this down arrow widget.

Bind on Acquire items. Blizzard's answer to no item decay. This is an outdated mechanic to prevent people who aren't in good guilds to be able to have access to the stuff that the top guilds can get.


Overall score: 8


Summation: This is a great game that will attract many many people, and will be a fantastic hit. I, however, will not be playing.

Ok, after 4 long years I must say... the wait was damn well worth it!

To be frank, this game is THE BEST MMORPG I have ever played, bar none (and I've pretty much played them all).

The game is a pure blast, even in the first few seconds. Currently, my main is a level 17 warrior (focusing on 2H swords and Mining/Engineering) on the Khaz server.

Quests: The first thing I noticed are the quests. They all have an indepth, linking story to them (some larger than others) and are perfect for what they are. Some quests are hunting quests, some are gathering quests, some are looting quests and then there are tons of others that can't really be classified since no other game has done them before (for instance, there's one where you must convince a drunk to give you his special mug by talking to him but then start a bar fight). Almost every turn to look, there's someone giving out quests. Some are harder then others (some require certain tradeskills, groups, stealth, lockpicking, etc.) but most are solo friendly (and I'm about 50/50 group/solo player).

I give the quest catagory a 10/10

Combat: Combat is fun and engaging as a warrior (dunno about the other classes but I'm sure they are too) and you have a myriad of skills at your disposal (some require different battle stances, positions, weapon types, reationaries) but they are very costly as you level up. Combat is fast, intuitive and just plain ole' good fun. There are a few animation bugs (understandable since it's beta) such as position locks and wierd movements but they don't hamper the gameplay at all since it doesn't affect character.

I give the combat catagory a 9/10

Skills: At level 10, you have access to talents and the way they are set up is almost exactly like Diablo 2 skill-trees. There are tons of talents and pretty much any talent you can think of for a warrior, they have em. Talents offer a lot of skill choices/bonuses in combat in addition to your regular attack skills. Talents also help each player differ themselves from the other players of the same class. I, for example, am focusing on brute force using 2H swords and critical strikes. Other warriors might focus on using shield protections while wielding a mace OR some warriors might choose the berserker style and use duel weild and faster attacks. This overall makes your character standout and feel more unique, which I always love.

I give the skill catagory a 9/10

Customization: At first, I was a little disappointed with the character creation since there aren't that many choices (Blizzard has said, they plan on release many more choices before release though). It's better than DAoC and EQ1 but no where near as good as CoH in terms of choices. Items, skills and talents will make the real difference among each player however. As I said before there are tons of talent/skill choices to choose from which will always make your character standout. I only saw maybe 2 other 2H sword warriors besides me and even then, their talents were different than mine. As well as skills/talents, items also helps make your character stand out amongst the crowd. I've never seen 1 character look in the least bit alike so far and that is heavily due to the items. There are TONS and TONS of items so far whethere they're quest, dropped, treasure (from treasure chests), crafted, etc. Every steampunk/medieval item you could think of is in this game and then some. Special spell scrolls, food, weapons, armor, potions, metals, gems, guns, etc. are in this game! From the looks of it, WoW could turn out to have the most items of any MMORPG so far (even more than EQ1). And besides, who cares about what your face looks like if you're gonna be wearing a Plated Helm or Troll mask covering it?

I give the customization catagory a 8/10

Interface: I love the interface of this game for some reason. It's very easy to use and also accomidating even to MMORPG newbies. The maps are hand drawn and are very detailed and fun to look at (as you explore, you open up more areas of the map). There are also tons of customization options for the interface including customizable keymappings, point and click movement if you desire, customizable chats, etc. Overall, very well done and the best of any MMORPG so far.

I give the Interface catagory a 10/10

Tradeskills: Normally in MMORPGs, I never tradeskill because I hate them! But WoW is the first MMORPG where tradeskills are actually FUN! Currently I'm almost a journeyman Engineer and an apprentice Miner. Mining is great because there are tons of ore deposits all over Azeroth and Miners also have a special ore tacking skill where these deposits are mapped out and are on your radar. In order to mine, you first need the skill and a mining pick. You then can go up to an ore deposit (Copper is the easiest to mine, then Tin is the next, and then so on) and you proceed to mine it until the deposit is dry. Once that happens, the deposit disappears and you have to move on to a new one. It's every easy to do and can be easily done while exploring, hunting, questing, etc. anywhere in the world. Engineering also requires mining due to the ore that you need to build special engineering items such as bombs, guns, fireworks, etc. Later on, you can create some really cool and unique items such as mechanical squarrels, goggles, bullets, lock bombs, universal remote, etc.

I give the Tradeskills catagory a 9/10

Stability: Since it is beta, there are still a few bugs around but from 20 hours /played, I've never gone LD, crashed, lagged or pinged at any time. The FPS is at an almost constant 60 (a little less in capital cities). For a stress test, I was blown away at how great the stability was!

I give the Stability catagory a 10/10

Environment: Azeroth... need I say more? Best environment I've ever seen! Each zone is unique and has a very distinctive flavor to it. No loading times at all (except going into instances and traveling to the other continent). The game world is also HUGE! I'd say it's about 3 times the size of DAoC and 1.5 times the size of EQ1 (after the first 2 expansions). And the best part is, when you run around, you actually RUN!

I give the Environment catagory a 10/10

Overall: 9.4/10

Like I said before, WoW is the best MMORPG to date!

First, my MMO background:
MUDS/Shadows of Yserbius/The Realm/UO/EQ/DAOC/SWG/SB/COH/L2

I've been playing WOW off and on for three months of Beta, with multiple 20+ characters and 1 40+ character.

WOW is a very good game, but to me its roots are clear. It is essentially EQ in the Warcraft universe. Many things EQ did wrong, WOW does right. However your goals in the game are identical to EQs, level and acquire items.

If someone is looking for a level/loot game done right, this is the game for you.

The atmopshere and graphics are very good and immersive. Lag has not ever been a problem for me.

The instanced dungeons are very well done, and there is a lot of content. Soloing is viable, but levelling is always better in a group.

Levelling is a mixture of quests, but most are of the whack a mole variety. The storyline is very good.

I do not like the present setup of Horde/Alliance and the limited generic classes. The hero system is a good idea, but again despite being a fun game to play it still is reliant on the same levelling and item acquisition mindset of EQ. That is more a personal choice, than comparatively speaking. Some will love it, I don't.

The developers 3 years and tons of time in EQ is telling, as the game will feel like home to longtime EQ/DAOC players.

The game however, is not at all revolutionary, and is a simple upgrade of EQ/DAOC. It is not at all a paradigm shift. If you want a new MMO with content, but the same TYPE of game then WOW is it.

Obviously WOW is set in the Warcraft Universe, which will be great for Warcraft kiddies, but not as appealing for an AD&D pen n paper guy like me.

To break the game down in my mind

Graphics: B
The graphics are not cutting edge, but are good.

Gameplay: B+
The quests are good. The storyline is good. It is easy to get involved. The game does need more branches and it'd be nice if they implemented a decent reason why Horde/Alliane are separate. This game will have few roleplayers so that to me doesn't seem a good reason.

UI: A
The UI is done well. Very well.

Staying Power: B
I do not think a majority of people will stay with this game, because the premise is the same as DAOC and EQ. It does have great hype, but so did a lot of other games <Hi, Shadowbane>. It will sell very well and get a good base, because it is a very good game.

Originality: D
This game is an EQ clone. There are no chances being taken, as the game is tailor made with ideas lifted from many sources, and implemented flawlessly. It is EQ done right, with a few things changed. If you want a new type of MMO, WOW ain't it

Overall: B
WOW is the game many are looking for, but a lot will take a drive and not find what they are looking for. As good as WOW is, and as much as it is EQ done right, there are a lot of people who do not want to do the EQ thing again.
