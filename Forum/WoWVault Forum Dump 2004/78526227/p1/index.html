Date Posted: 11/10 9:00am Subject: RE: Why would you not want to play PVP?
I like to play my own game and be in control. I don't wish to give someone else control of my gaming experience. When you have hard core PvP, someone can take control of my gaming experience just because they find that amusing. I have yet to see a PvP implementation in a MMORPG that didn't have ganking and griefing.

I don't care too much about dying. One of my character in AC1 died like 500 times. I even left some corpse's filled with loot in AC1 because I couldn't recover. That's the way the cookie crumbles.

What I hate is the idea the I might get distracted by my wife in real life, turn back to the screen and find that someone has killed me and decided to camp my corpse. Not only is my wife mad because I'm swearing, now my character is useless because I can't get away from the griefer. I play games to get away from stress in my life. Not to add stress to my life.

I don't have a control problem. As long as I'm in control, no problem. PvP lets someone else take control of my gaming experience and that is a no go.

I might try out the battlefields in WoW. I don't mind going head to head with people. However, I want the option to exit if I have to and go back to my normal existence.

I agree that PvP can be more of a challenge. However, I think you would also have to agree that PvP brings out the griefers, cheaters, and gankers.

By the way, I love PvP servers. I think PvP servers do a great job of skimming the griefers, cheaters, and gankers off the regular servers. As such they perform a useful public service and should be required for all MMORPGs.
