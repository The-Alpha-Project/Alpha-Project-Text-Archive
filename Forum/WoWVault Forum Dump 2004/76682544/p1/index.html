To Ashen: Mage tactics in any RPG are to remain a bit back from the action; they have cloth armor and low HPs. In the movie linked above I wade into the fray quite often, but I'm not a warrior or a paladin, I'm a caster, and furthermore, I'm not an idiot. If you play a mage in WoW and wade into a huge group of enemy mobs you're not only going to die, but you're not going to do your "team" any good. Believe me, the best tactic for a mage in WoW is to stay as far from the fight as possible while still lending some value to the fight. I'd also like to draw your attention to the fact that I killed over 11 people in that video (damaging over 30) and only died once (and yes, while there was editing, I didn't intentionally edit out any of my deaths). Only an idiot would try and fight huge spawns of level 50 NPC guards, when the whole point is to kill enemy PCs. Until you've played the game and have a clue what you are talking about, I'd suggest you keep your fabulous "tactics" to yourself.

Also, if you haven't played WoW you wouldn't be aware of three other things going on in that video:

-in huge PvP battles, the game can't render all the models on the screen at once, so what might look like tons of Alliance with very few horde around, is in fact a much closer fight than you realize

- twice in that movie (even right at the beginning) I am the victim of an "over-powered" spell called Howl of Terror (an Area of Effect Warlock Fear spell) that causes everyone around the Warlock to run away in random directions for 10 seconds

- the guards in the town we were attaking spawn in packs of 5-10 every few seconds and will chase you until you die, or they are killed

To those of you who didn't enjoy the music: cool, that's your opinion and you're welcome to it; and considering a majority of the audience, I didn't expect that many people would like it, but I will smile and tell you that one day you'll grow up and realize that Limp Biscuit, Korn, Tool and the like is not good music and I wasn't about to put that crap in my video.
