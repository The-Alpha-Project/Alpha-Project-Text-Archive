If you are casting a regular spell and someone hits you, the bar that fills up is reduced. In other words, your spell is delayed. If you're getting hit by one enemy, it is still very possible to get spells off, it just takes a bit longer. If it's 2 or more, it's much harder.

Channeling spells are interrupted completely when you get hit. There are also abilities that will protect yourself from interrupts.

There are 2 different types of interrupts. I know all about interrupts as you can see I have a lot of mage experience

anyways when you start casting a spell you get a little bar. So let's say I am casting fireball(3s cast time with my talents) the little bar on the bottom of the screen starts to fill up. It is much like the crafting bar in DAoC(you mentioned having played DAoC). So the red progress bar fills up and when it gets full it turns green and your spell casts. Now lets say someone starts attacking me mid cast. So this mob or player gets on me when I am 2 seconds through my cast bar(2/3 full) and they hit me. My progress bar will go back a slight bit, say to 1/2 and continue to fill up until it is full. So you can cast through their attack your spell will just take maybe 4 seconds to fully cast rather than 3. So it won't make you unable to do anything but it will cut your dps down. Now if you have a rogue or duel wielding warrior or mob that has 2 weapons that will cut you down even moreso. Also if you have more than one mob on you then you should really forget about casting through them unless its an instant spell. Just to note I also have a fire talent that gives my fire spells a 65% chance to not lose cast time when I am hit while casting a fire spell(I maxed this one out, 3 talent points for the max lvl of it).

Now on to the second type of interrupt. Rogues have an ability called kick and warriors can use charge and I forget their other interrupt ability. there are more than 1 or 2 but certain classes have the ability to completely negate the casters cast. Some of them also then prevent you from casting that spell for 10 seconds or so. As a mage I have a spell called counterspell which will stop the caster from casting a particular spell for 30 seconds(I believe I could be wrong on the timer). Also any stun will stop your casting.

I love the interrupt system in this game. After playing 2 Runemasters, 2 Healers, a spiritmaster, and a bard to 50 in DAoC and RvRing a decent bit with each of them I really love the WoW interrupt system. You don't necessarily want to cast while being attacked but you can if you so choose.

Oh yeah Channeling spells basically they start with a bar full and the bar empties as you cast the spell and it is more of a damage over time. Like say I have a blizzard spell. It is an aoe spell that is a channeled spell. so I start casting it and it does 181 damage every 4 seconds or so over 20 seconds(not sure on the exact numbers) so if I get hit after 8 seconds it will stop casting so I only did 362 damage rather than 905 damage which I would do if I didn't get hit.

Also priests get a spell called Holy Word: Shield. It absorbs all damage done to you and it prevents you from being interrupted while it is up.
