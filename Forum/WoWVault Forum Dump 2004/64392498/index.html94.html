Quinas 
Posts: 82
Registered: Jan 04
 		Date Posted: 2/16 6:43am Subject: Did it occur to anyone that this game could fail?
I had a look at Blizzard's lovely site. They really do hire good webmasters, as all of their webpages are first class material.

To be honest what brought me here is the absolute slew of people who can't say enough about how good WoW will be, how they can't wait for it, how it'll not only kill DAOC but also EQ2 when it too is released.

I remember a long time ago sitting at the North-west Blacksmith's shop in Britain on the Pacific server discussing with the fellow making my armour and the people sitting around him (at least 30% d3wds I'm sure) this new game that's coming out. It was called EverQuest or something. 3D, you say? Sheesh. That'll be laggy. Oh well, it'll definitely be better than all the bugs/griefers/whatever/insert-gripe-here's in UO right? Then it came out and everyone flocked there, and then, (after the myriad problems that arrived at launch) a few years later, people found that EQ had its' long-term problems too. Not just bugs and glitches, but things that made people unhappy, made people think their class was useless, nerfs etc. It was then that some of the quicker people, and certainly the erm.. nice folk at SOE.. realised that you couldn't please everyone. Looking at DAOC and SWG (not to mention the other less memorable "failures" on the way) it doesn't seem to have changed. People still think their class is underpowered, or that this particular nerf spells the end of this game and that, once again, "Enter New MMORPG title here" is going to ruin "Enter Current MMORPG title that everyone is playing here". It's almost cyclic.

Now I don't want to cast dispraging thoughts upon this potentially good game, but if you look through the fact that Blizzard has a good track record (and only in single-player games mind you, I don't consider Battle.net in the same league as MMORPGs), doesn't the MMORPG industry have a bad enough track record itself that WoW could go down the same path as the rest?

Don't get me wrong. I love MMORPGs and can hardly play single player games anymore unless they're very engrossing. But after a few years, a game /will/ get boring, and in the end, you can never really make everyone happy. The very fact that in a single-player game the developer can make you the hero means everything, but in a MMORPG you have to share the limelight with others. Thousands of others. So if someone is happy, someone will always, in my opinion, be unhappy. Whether it's an enemy realm feeling that they're underpowered on a frontier (like in DAOC), or a fellow tank class who believes that this new ability of another class will encroach upon a) his ability to find a group, b) his personal character concept or a number of other things.

The second thing that this issue raises is the fact that WoW /is/ (and I know you'll scream at this) going to be a lot like other MMORPGs. I read sme of Blizzard's FAQ plus TehSuq's very adept description of his experiences at GenCon. Apart from the graphical differences (which I would expect given it's a /new/ game. Would hate to see it look like DAOC or especially EQ so long after they'd been released. Ugh.) I don't see a lot of new stuff. There is new stuff so don't start posting madly to tell me what it is, but essentially all of it is the same RPG stuff we've been getting since D&D.

A lot of stuff has also been pilfered from other games. The /release function, styles, Realm vs. Realm combat (including a lack of communication inter-realm) are all featured in other games, just to name a few. You must understand it's easy to take the best of past games, put better graphics AND add your own cool things.

I guess I'm not sure what I'm saying here lol. Perhaps I just want to warn.. hmm.. maybe that's not the right word.. I guess I just want to lend some experience to the whole MMORPG thing, which I'm sure some (maybe most?) have too, but I dare say some do not. In any case, the main thing I want to say is stop telling me how good WoW will be until you've actually played it, and then until you've played it for a couple of years. Then tell me you're not looking forward, once again, to the "next best thing".

I think that's it. Let the flames begin.

Respectfully, (except to you Albs out there )
