EQ2

Pros

  1. Beautiful game on the higher settings as long as you are looking for that "realistic" look.

  2. Some animations are done really well, spells effects look great.

  3. Character creation is pretty good, with loads of options.

  4. Quests have a good amount of variety to them early in the game.

  5. When in a good group, its pretty darn fun, and looks great with all the swords and spells flying.

  6. The beginning levels are pretty fun and the voice acting is above par, even if you don't always want to wait for the person to say everything they need to say

  7. Felt like I was participating more in a story than a series of quests like in WoW, like my character might actually have an impact on the in-game world.

Cons

  1. While beautiful in some aspects, there were many places where everything just seemed to bland, especially in the cities. I would describe it more as a fake RL world than its own world. Didn't have enough feeling to it.

  2. Despite having all the character creation options, for some reason they don't seem to change the look of your character enough to really be worth it in most instances.

  3. Need to have a group for the game not to be extremely difficult and a long, boring grind. I like to be able to log in for a 90 minutes and get something done and have some fun in-game.

  4. Loading times are a pet peeve of mine, even for 10-20 seconds (even in games like HL2), they detract from the game and ruin the immersion, and EQ2 is full of loading times.

  5. Some of the animations bugged me. Like the running+strafing animation where your character faces straight, but slides left and right. Just wierd.

  6. Even high end systems will have trouble in certain areas, like in towns for instance. Its almost to the point where I would consider changing my video options based on where I was going in-game

  7. You don't choose your skills, they are giving to you when you level, so there isn't really different between characters in the same class at all.


WoW

Pros

  1. Doesn't have the realistic look, but still manages to look amazing somehow. Has a great many artistic touches in the game that just feel right. I feel like I am playing in the WoW world, not a world trying to imitate the real world.

  2. Plays well on even medium to medium/low systems. My 1.8Ghz, 512MB, 32MB GeForce 440 laptop still ran WoW at 1024x768 with most of the options at medium levels and it ran extremely well.

  3. Great quest system, but at the low levels, the quests can be quite monotonous, most of them being "Kill 10 and come back" type quests. However, the quests later in the game get much, much better.

  4. Crafting system is streamlined and easy to get into, while not being overly easy. If you put enough effort into your craft you can create items very useful for your own level, saving you money and keeping you well equipped.

  5. Combat is fun and exciting. Even when I play my Paladin, which is supposedly one of the more boring classes, I can take a fight different ways. Do I use my blessing with a chance to stun? Do I use one with a faster attack speed, or use the judgment to increase holy damage for another blessing?

  6. The ability to solo is awesome. I play MMORPG to be in a living breathing, changing worlds. Though I may spend most of the time solo, I still can group when I want to. If I wanted to play with other people all the time, I would play Counter-Strike online or Champions of Norrath with some buddies.

Cons

  1. Still a prevelance of bugs for a released game. Quest NPCs that will chase after a mob and then no longer give out quests until a GM can come fix them. Quests that become broken under certain conditions and won't work until a server restart.

  2. Things like mail lag can be quite annoying when you have to sit at a mailbox for 5-10 minutes just to send one pice of mail.

  3. Sometimes the leveling seems too fast. Being at level 24 and having a decent group of people in my guild closing in on level 40 can be a little annoying at times.

WoW is pretty substantially better in my opinion. How they will pan out over time remains to be seen, but from everything I have heard I expect the gap to only widen.

I have opinions on all aspects, but the number one thing WoW has over other MMOs is **QUESTS**

They're not only amazing, but they reward you massively, and can provide the sole source of xp and coin to get you through most of the game, literally without ever killing a non-quest mob if you so choose.

However there's a lot of misinformation in this thread that I want to clear up.

Earlier someone said this:

"WoW has a very dry and weak quest system"

And later this:

"Yes, [EQ2's quest system is] much better.
- I can get quests from different sources.
- There are push and pull quests
- quests that originate from drops. Keys, books, poems all spawn quests.
- Some quests are riddles that you must decipher.

So far in WoW the only source of a quest is an NPC with a huge question mark over their head. "

These people simply haven't played the game for long, if at all.

WoW has all of the above and more. In the first 20 levels in the Human starting area alone, you can:
- You can fish up a "message in a bottle" that starts a quest.
- You can read about a captured general on a bulletin in lakeshire (but get no quest for it), then go save him, escort him out and talk to him at the end which triggers another quest.
- Kill a random Defiasbandit who randomly drops a letter that uncovers a plot (follow through with the clues in it to start a quest).

Hell, you can get quests from all freakin' over the place - not just people in town with gold excalamations.

And many WoW quests are *insane* too:

- I did one that appeared to be a "delivery" quest: Go get a fossil from a senile old archeologist and bring it back. But when I got there his excavation site had had been overrun by golems. Being a crazy old man, though he obliviously lead me around anyway looking for the fossil. As he went he chatted about silly things or stopped to look through tents while golems spawned out of the walls and rockjaws jumped from the riggings to attack us. If you survive the gauntlet, and beat the juggernaut that spawns as he's climbing back out of the site, he remebers that he gave it to someone else and sends you there. Dry?

- In an undead camp by a sacrifical altar there are two books on pedestals. If open one you get a quest from it. If you open other, an arm comes out of it and slaps you a'la Evil Dead.

- In a cave you find a hibernating druid (part of the story). If you wake him he'll ask sleepily to be lead to town for he's been sleeping for hundreds of years. But as you go, he keeps falling back asleep on you (complete with falling on his face animation and a little "zzz" effect) and you've got to gaurd him from corrupted forest animals until he wakes up. And of course, upon saving him he kicks off another quest chain.

- I was asked to leave "poisoned" food by a blackwood furblog campfire that would purify them if they ate it. When you do so all of the furbys nearby wander over to it and "poof" become friendly npcs. Then their leader - a satyr - spawns very pissed off, /shouts to the zone that nobody messed with his underlings and attacks you. What's really funny is you can go find your "freindlified" furbolgs wandering around the forest for at least 10 minutes afterwards.

And here's the real kicker:
These are all in the same zone: Darkshore
They're all the same lowbie level range: 15-20.

There are literally dozens more there, too. Search sunken pirate ships. Investigate washed up sea creatures. Climb a necromancers tower, or get mushrooms from a naga cave. There are dozens of escorts, encounters, dungeons and bosses mixed liberally in the "kill X of X" and "take X to X" quests - even at the lowest levels.

WoW quests are anything but dry.

I won't even get started on the amazing instanced dungeons and the crazy quests and encounters associated with those.
