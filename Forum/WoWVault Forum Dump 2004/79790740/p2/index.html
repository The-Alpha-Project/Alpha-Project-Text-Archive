I am a former Everquest II fanatic. I beta tested for 2 months + 32 hours of retail gaming. The gameplay philosophy of the developers, overtime, alienated me from a "casual" perspective. I tried to adapt, but failed. At low levels I could accomplish my goals within a short period of time, but later levels really started to slow down too much for my tastes. I like feeling some sense of accomplishment, even if it only means completing one quest/task.

Techinical points: the graphics are amazing and I love the voice overs. You really do need a good computer to enjoy the visual beauty.

Misc points: I hated the crafting system, too confusing. The class system is pretty good, but diversity is a little too subtle within each base (fighter, mage, rogue, priest).

Overall: great game for people who enjoy the EQ line of gameplay design.

Switched to World of Warcraft over the Thanksgiving Holiday. I did not enjoy the Stress Test, but still felt compelled to play retail. As I leveled up and started exploring areas, the game became highly addictive. The PvE system is more enjoyable than EQII. Crafting is accessible to everyone. By this I mean fun and streamlined. We have a real incentive to learn skills.

Technical points: WoW could use more graphic options, but my system runs flawlessly on high settings. I love the artisitc style. People who complain about the cartoonish aspect are strange. I still watch Looney Toons on Boomerang/Cartoon Network, so

Misc. points: I focus more on the adventure than the actual leveling.

Overall: I haven't experienced this level of fun since my early Ultima Online days. I'll stop rambling my opinion now. Shut up and play!
