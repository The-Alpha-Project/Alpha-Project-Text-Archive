Date Posted: 9/21/04 6:23am Subject: RE: Button for nearest enemy?
The thing about /face was that it was like a lock-on. THATs a good thing folks. It makes it similar to what we're used to in action games. The target graphic/symbol is hidden enough as it is. Being able to strafe around, locked onto the target is better than not. Having the OPTION is a good thing. Its not like you even need to be facing the target to really hit it. Aiming isn't an issue as we know. The mob could be next to you while you're swinging at air, and you're still hiting it. At least with an auto /face command it makes it seem like you're actually engaging the target instead of it just being "in range". Its VITAL for melee and honestly much cooler as a ranged class.

FFXI made it automatic, one of its few good features;) I think SWG did it also. DAOC made it a simple macro to use if you wished. Guild Wars is doing it. Theres a VERY good reason its in those games. Its no accident. WOW should follow suit.

For mass PvP, if theres loads of targets in front of all running through each other, you'll bet it'll be a pain to keep track of a single target, JUST LIKE IN DAOC. Locking onto the target is just one way to stream-line the process and make it BETTER. NOT easier...BETTER!!

Anyone against this doesn't understand why its the little things that make gameplay better. Getting used to something isn't good gameplay. It should feel right from the start.

I fiddled with the click to target or whatever that setting was. It negatively effected other gameplay elements if I recall. It still wasn't quite "right". Can you play the game without a /face command? Of course you can. Would the battles be better and feel more dynamic with it esspecially in PvP? Very much so.
