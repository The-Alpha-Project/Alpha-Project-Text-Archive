    CS is a very tough issue. I don't know if the hype is true but I know that support costs. Verant (pre-SOE) told us that 1 hour CS response time is insanely expensive. I don't know what that means but I'm sure it has a significant cost. The question I suppose is; Can the customer or the vendor afford not to have speedy and efficient support? I don't think so.

    I've worked in a support industry for almost 2 decades now and I can tell you that human nature is still human nature. People are going to demand speedy resolution to issues. It doesn't matter how unreasonable that might be. Something I learned early on is that if you have a customer service perception problem then you have a customer service problem. Period. It doesn't have to be reasonable, that's just the way it is. Also, customers are going to abuse customer service policy. Period. That's just the way it is. Trying to deny these issues is like trying to argue with gravity.

    In DAoC I had good experiences with support. They answered fairly quickly (within an hour) and usually had ways of verifying my issue and resolutions were almost immediate.

    EQ was a different story completely. Guides were a good idea at first and they were very helpful but they were abused and eventually fell out of favor. When I quit you couldn't get the simplest answer from a guide. Getting help from an actual CS rep was far worse. If you could get them to answer you at all, they always gave you the same answer... sorry I cannot help. They had no means to verify your problem. By the time they responded (2 weeks was the max I experienced) the trail was cold and there was nothing they could do. Or so they said. I found out that perserverance and tenacity worked wonders but it sometimes took more than a month.

    Support is very important to me. I think my ultimate reason for quitting EQ was lack of support. I am one of the few that is actually willing to pay extra for speedy and efficient support. Your average person is not. Oh and that doesn't mean that they don't expect it anyway. You techy types know what I'm talking about. Your friend/mother/grandfather/neighbor asks your opinion on what to buy from Dell or Gateway and despite your advice, they save $200 and skimp on technical support. Sooner or later the excrement hits the fan and they are screaming for help that is not avaiable because they didn't pay for it and they are mad as heck and blaming everyone but themselves.

    Bottom line: I think fast efficient support should be mandatory and built into the general costs. 10 to 20 minute responses should be the goal and anything over an hour should be unacceptable. At the very least, (and I think this IS the very least) the vendor should offer some type of premium support for those of us who are smart enough to pay for it. But it better not be insanely priced.

    Yes the vendor is between a rock and hardplace. Such is reality. Welcome to the market.
