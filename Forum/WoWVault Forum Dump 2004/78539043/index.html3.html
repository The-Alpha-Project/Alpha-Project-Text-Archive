Date Posted: 11/10 12:12pm Subject: not facing your target error
There was one thing that the lag last night brought home to me was that a MMORPG is not a real time fighting game. It is a simulation running on a remote computer that reports back the results to my local client which draws pretty pictures. My computer is just a window into the world that is being simulated on another system far, far, away. The one rule that is always constant is that the server always wins. Your character *is* where the server thinks it is, not where your computer thinks it is.

I can see where the players can be fooled into thinking that they can affect the outcome of events in real time when lag is minimized. But having the game tell me I'm facing the wrong direction just tells me that the developers are being overly optimistic. They of all people should know that lactency can vary greatly.

I know that this seems like a trival point but my concern is that the folks who produce these games take reality into account. Lag happens. Interacting in real time (or near real time) over a public internet is just not possible at all times. Trying to make it look like it does it just frustrating to the people who try to play.
