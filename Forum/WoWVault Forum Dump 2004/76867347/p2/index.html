I voted love it, but love is such a strong word.

All RPGs need money sinks. It's a fact. This durability thing sounds harmless in that your gear never becomes useless unless its durability goes completely, which isn't likely to happen often since you visit town to sell and stuff with frequency already.

It also gives the devs another dynamic to work with. It'll give players something else to consider when optimizing their gear - do you go for something with high durability and slightly less +atts so that you don't risk it becoming temporarily useless, or do you go with low durability and higher +stats and take your chances.

Easy, Jamare. A gold sink is always a good thing for the game economy. You can't allow the players to get richer and richer and richer, with no place for the money to go. This is called inflation. It's what makes the cost of things in-game go through the roof as time goes on. The more money people have to spend, the higher they drive up the costs. All this does is encourage gold farming and eBaying. This "pay to repair" is a small sink, but the more sinks the better. They allow gold to disappear entirely from the server. (I use the word "gold" in a general sense, not as the specific monetary unit in WoW.)
