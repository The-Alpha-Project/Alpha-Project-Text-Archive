"I have heard disturbing rumors that it will take 20-40 players to do raids to get any of the good loot items. This doesn't bother me too much as long as the system doesn't promote leet guild camping and ninja looting"

I'm not sure what you expect. For any given raid encounter there can only be so many drops. Raid content has to be kinda tough or there isn't much point in requiring a raid to do it. So, that means that organized guilds with enough people will do the raids until all their people have the gear and it means everyone else is screwed and will have to hope for pickup raids, where ninja looting and general, miserable chaos are very likely.

Ie, raid content isn't for everyone. No matter what you say or think the reality is that the potential for any given player to do raids isn't equal. This is the main reason I don't like the best loot in the game to come only from raiding - it means that mostly the uber guilds and powergamers will ever get that loot.

I can think of one way to make raids less tilted towards those that are already uber.

For one - make raid groups smaller. Raid content needs to be hard enough to make it impossible to do with one group - but it doesn't need to require a small army. 3 groups max should be enough. This would let more people have a chance at raiding.

Secondly - use a loot system similar to LDoN in EQ (hate to make EQ comparisons but LDoN is one of the best things to happen to all of MMORPGing). That is - each raid zone would yield points for beating it instead of having uber boss drops. Each raid zone would have vendors that sell the uber loot for those points instead. Each set of raid vendors would sell different things - and you'd have to unlock the availability of those items by beating that particular raid. Like LDoN, you might be able to use any raid points you have to buy gear from any raid vendor - IF you've unlocked items by doing enough raids for the vendor.

A point based system has numerous benefits. For one, it lest you PICK the loot you get - no randomness. For two, it eliminates the chances of any type of ninja looting or any other form of greed or arguing over loot. It eliminates the vacant feeling you get after beating a tough boss critter only to see crap items drop that nobody wants or needs (ie, it makes every raid victory meaningful).

Many raiding guilds in EQ use point-based systems to determine who gets looting rights at raids. A system like this one incorporates the point tracking for you - eliminating the need to keep track of it yourself and produces the same results! Except you also get a lot more flexibility in what you earn - since you potentially have more to choose from once you earn enough points.

When you're raiding, your goal is typically to beat the end boss for her drops. Anything less is failure. Ideally, any given raid has multiple lesser bosses too to keep things interesting. With a point based raid loot system you could get some points for each lesser boss you help defeat but the majority of your points would come from helping to defeat the big bosses. This would, in effect, be the same as the existing system - because you might get some lesser items from mini-bosses but the real uber gear comes from the end/big bosses.

A point based "loot" system for raiding eliminates pretty much all the negative aspects of raid looting and provides functionality that a lot of raiders have to manually implement. It also opens up raiding to more players than just the hardcore, those in strong guilds, and the powergamers because you don't get punished for pickup raiding.

Lastly - if you did a point-based system that was very much like LDoN then there is *still* a chance to get a good dropped item (and fight over it like normal raid loot) - in LDoN you can get some decent dropped loot in addition to earning points - which just adds to the fun.

Anyways, the LDoN thing in EQ is tons of fun and I'd love to see something like it in WoW. It's actually so much fun that it's hard to believe that SOE implemented it so well.
