I can buy into the Multi-player aspect keeping people out. My girlfriend loved City of Heroes, but she wound up quitting. Not because she disliked the multi-player aspect, but more that she just did not care about it and I think that is a key thing to consider too.

When UO first came out and along with older MUDS, the huge draw was the neat, novelty concept of being able to log into a world and adventure with people across the globe (even if it was lag-a-licious).

It's not really a novelty any more, there is none of that running into the other room to grab your room mate and drag them in so they can see you killing a dragon with 7 other people.

People know about MMORPGS now. I'd wager to say it's pretty difficult to be a gamer and -not- know about Everquest or Ultima Online. It may be not be the fact that playing with other's keeps them away but that they just don't care. In a single player game they can do as they wish, mod what they want and cheat as much as they want too. A casual gamer might wonder why they should give that up just to play with people they don't care about, and to pay to do it on top of that.

I do think there is some room to grow in the market.. it's really only been since the growth of the PS2 that it's become socially acceptable to call yourself a gamer.. and it really only seems to be the last 2ish years that MMORPGs are starting to break away from their stigma of being geeky.
