But if you give the Jack of all Trades that "one spell to make them wanted", does that solve the problem? That being that a "jack of all trades" is not as useful as a specialized class?

With Refresh, those Red Mages weren't being invited because they were so versatile (usually), it was because they had Refresh. Maybe they were invited for their Healing (as most people know healing tends to transcend skill levels better. Half-arsed Nukes get resisted, half-arsed Heals are still heals), but rarely for their nuking and never for their melee.

So then ask yourself, in which situation does a Jack of all Trades really shine? The answer is simple: when there's only one available spot but several lacking areas. For instance, if you're lacking a good tank and damage.....you invite one specialized tank and one specialized damage dealer. Then your bases are covered exceptionally.

But what if you only have one spot, and both damage *and* healing are lacking? That's the only time when a Jack of all Trades outshines a specialized class. You'll get that splash of healing to make things more safer and certain, but you'll also get the extra splash of damage that you wouldn't have gotten if you had invited another specialized Healer.
