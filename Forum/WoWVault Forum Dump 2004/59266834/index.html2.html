This short FAQ answers a few of the most commonly asked questions. Please read this before asking on the board. For a more detailed FAQ, go to the WoW Vault FAQ or check the Official Site for game info. You can also find the Official WoW Forum here.


What is World of Warcraft?

World of Warcraft is an MMORPG game based on the Warcraft universe from the Warcraft RTS games. The game is being developed and produced by Blizzard Entertainment.


When will the beta start?

Beta signups are now closed. Beta is scheduled to begin very soon. Blizzard will be announcing when they send out beta acceptance emails.


How will we know when beta emails have been sent out?

Blizzard will be announcing when they send the emails out. If Blizzard hasn't announced anything and people claim to be receiving beta emails, they are probably joking.


When will the game be released?

Blizzard has not yet stated a target release date.


What playable races are in World of Warcraft?

At release, the playable races will be Humans, Dwarves, Gnomes, Night Elves, Orcs, Undead, Tauren, and Trolls. For more specific information, check WoW Vault or the Official Site.


What classes will be available?

The following classes will be available at release: Mage, Warrior, Priest, Paladin, Shaman, Warlock, Rogue, Druid, and Hunter. For more specific information, check WoW Vault or the Official Site.


What sort of PvP will the game have?

Currently very little is known about WoW's PvP. PvP is consensual, meaning that you must agree to a duel (in arenas) or enter a PvP region (PvP regions make up about 10% of the world) in order to be attacked by another player. There will be faction based combat and players will be able to fight NPCs and take over towns.


What are the system requirements?

The game is being developed for both Mac and PC. Early reports indicate that the game will run smoothly on a P3 800 MHz with a low end graphics card, but that a higher end machine will be needed to take advantage of the game's graphical abilities.


Will there be a monthly fee?

Yes. The monthly fee will cover regular content updates made by Blizzard. The exact amount has not yet been set, but the current going rate for MMORPGs seems to be about $12.95 - $15.95.

*** History ***
The history of the Warcraft universe is long and rich. An entire FAQ could be dedicated to it alone, so I will only give it in brief here, and provide links to the extended versions. If you are not already familiar with the history behind this game, you should take the time to become so. It is an excellent read in its own right and will greatly improve your experience in World of Warcraft.

1. Demons are the force of chaos, and they dwell in the depths of the universe.
2. Titans are the benevolent gods who seek to order all things.
3. A titan named Saergas is assigned to stomp out the demons.
4. If you gaze long into the abyss, the abyss gazes also into you; Saergas goes mad and becomes evil.
5. Saergas enslaves the demons into an army he calls the Burning Legion.

6. Elves awake on Azeroth near the shores of a sea of magic.
7. They live by the sea and start practicing magic.
8. The Burning Legion notices the magic and becomes lustful for the sea of power.
9. Some elves are corrupted by the love for magic and become High Elves.
10. The High Elves come under the sway of Saergas and begin to summon him into the lake of magical power.
11. Two Elven brothers attempt to put an end to this. It appears to mean the destruction of the lake of power.
12. One of the brothers is too addicted to magic to destroy the lake. He secrets a vial of the lake.
13. In the battle to stop the summoning, everything is destroyed. Saergas, the lake of magical power, the Elven nation, and most of it's people. The face of Azeroth is remade.
14. What's left of the lake of magical power becomes the Maelstrom.
15. A few elves live through it, including the two brothers. They form a nation in the new land of Kalimdor.
16. The greedy brother survived and creates a new well of magical power with the sample stolen from the first.
18. The elves are split, some like the well of power and some don't. They ones who don't are exiled. They move to the far side of the world and are known as High Elves.
17. A nature god, Cenarius, and several demi-god dragons grow the World Tree over the well, bind the elves to the night and sway them to become druids. The night elves are created.

18. Much time passes. Dwarves, Humans, Goblins, Gnomes, Tauren, Trolls, etc all appear on Azeroth.

19. On another planet, the Burning Legion corrupts the leader of a shamanistic culture of nobile warriors to do their bidding. These are the Orcs. They are perverted to evil.
20. The Orcs open a dark portal to Azeroth and attack.

23. The Orc leader stages several raids into Azeroth for these magical items.
24. The people of Azeroth think the Orcs mean to do them ill and counter attack.

36. Back in Human lands, the Orcs start to get lazy and loose their will to fight.

38. One Orc is raised by humans and is called Thrall.
39. Thrall visits his people and is saddened. He tours all that is Orc left in Azeroth and is tutored by their best. He becomes a shaman and is appointed leader of all the Orc people.

*** Features ***
1. WoW is easy to learn and hard to master.
2. It is set in the amazing history that I poorly represented above.
3. There are thousands of quests, making it FAR more quest oriented than any other MMORPG.
4. The dungeons and some epic outdoors areas are 'instanced', meaning that when you enter them a private dungeon is created just for you and your group.
5. There is a diverse crafting implementation which provides some of the best items in the game without concern of item-creation failure.
6. You can gain experience just by exploring!
7. The game is fast-paced and focused on being fun, rather than a long, slow level grind.

*** Races ***
Alliance Races: Human, Dwarf, Night Elf, Gnome
Horde Races: Orc, Troll, Tauren, Undead

Note! Starting statistics for the races are not yet revealed.

Abilities:
1. Undead do not have to breathe underwater, are resistant to shadow magic, and are immune to charm/sleep/fear spells.
2. Tauren have an ability called 'plains run', which lets them run cross-country at great speed.
3. Night Elves are resistant to nature, can 'shadowmeld', and find herbs.
4. Dwarves are resistant to frost, can mine minerals well, and can find treasure.
5. Gnomes are resistant to frost.
6. Humans get additional skill points.
7. Orc and Troll abilities are unknown or do not exist.

*** Classes ***
1. Druid. Can heal and shapeshift. Night Elves and Tauren can be druids.
2. Hunter. Can tame and soothe beasts, track, dual wield and use ranged weapons. Dwarves, Night Elves, Orcs,
Taurens, and Trolls can be hunters.
3. Mage. Can cast lots of magic. Dwarves, Gnomes, Humans, and Undead can be mages.
4. Paladin. Can heal, cast 'seals', lay on hands, and a variety of other goody-two-shoes magic. They can also fight very well. Dwarves and Humans can be paladins.
5. Priest. Best healer. Dwarves, Humans, Night Elves, Trolls, and Undead can be priests.
6. Rogue. Can stealth, backstab, dual wield, and perform a variety of other cool, sneaky, high damage attacks. Dwarves, Gnomes, Humans, Night Elves, Orcs, Trolls, and Undead can be rogues.
7. Shaman. Naturalist buffers, casters, and light warriors. Orcs, Tauren, and Trolls can be shamans.
8. Warlock. The summoner. Warlocks probably get the best pets, as they summon the Burning Legion. Gnomes, Humans, Orcs, and Undead can be warlocks.
9. Warriors. My favorite! Warriors can fight better than anyone. They have multiple stances and many neat attacks. Any race can be a warrior.

*** Character Creation ***
1. You do not modify your character attributes or skills during character creation.
2. You can have 8 characters.

*** Grouping ***
1. You are not forced to group, you can solo throughout the entire game.
2. Group size is 5.
3. Groups can be linked to form mega-groups
4. Loot can be divided round-robin or all given to the group leader for distribution.
5. Group experience is currently shared among all, but this is likely to change.

*** Skills ***
1. Skills advance through usage and leveling.
2. All classes can advance to max in all skills. That said, classes have special abilities that make them better at some skills than others.
3. skills can be untrained.

*** Quests ***
1. There are lots of quests! Many, many, many!
2. Quests vary in size from short, to long and hard, to epic. Some span the entire development of your character.
3. Some quests pretty much require a group to accomplish.
4. All your quests are managed in a journal.
5. Quests can be abandoned and started again later.
6. Failed quests can be retried multiple times.
7. Quest items dropped by creatures are shared by all party members.

*** Stealth ***
1. Allied characters will perceive you as a shadowy character.
2. Enemy character will have to detect you using passive game mechanics that will probably never be released.
3. Just because one enemy character sees you, does not mean that his group does.

*** Swimming ***
1. You can swim.
2. You have to hold your breath.
3. Crafters can make things that extend your underwater period.
4. Undead do not need to breathe.
5. Druids can shapeshift into an aquatic animal.

*** Items ***
1. Both crafting and adventuring yield the best items in the game.
2. Gear does not decay.
3. There are level requirements on items.

*** Transportation ***
1. Air transportation, such as gryphons and giant bats, are available between cities along fixed routes.
2. Boats and Zeppelins are based in major cities and can be used to cross to another continent.
3. At level 40 you can get a mount, which you can ride anywhere. BADARSE.

The mounts are as follows:
Dwarves and Gnomes -- Battle Rams
Humans -- Warhorses
Night Elves -- Great Cats
Orcs and Trolls -- Dire Wolves
Undead -- Deadhorses... I wonder if they beat them?
Taureen -- No mount, instead they plains run.

*** Alliance vs Horde ***
1. It is undecided currently if you can have characters on the same server in both the Alliance and the Horde.
2. You cannot use an enemy city.
3. It looks as though you will not be able to swap items with enemies.
4. Alliance and Horde characters cannot cross-group.

*** Game Mechanics ***
1. You do not need to eat to stay alive.
2. You can completely customize the interface
3. Besides epic dungeons and cities, there is no 'zoning'.
4. The game runs on a 24 hour clock (currently!)
5. There is dynamic weather and cloud generation.
6. Different races speak different languages, but you can learn other languages.
7. The game is fast paced but it is not a potion-fest.
8. PvP is consensual, in most rules sets.
9. There is currently no collision detection.
10. Movement is by the WASD keys, but can be modified.
11. Balance is achieved by the rock-paper-scissors method, in respect to classes.

*** Death Penalty ***
1. There is no 'binding'.
2. When you die, you rise as a ghost at the nearest graveyard.
3. Your corpse can be raised by a rezzer, and you will suffer rez-sickness.
4. Or you can travel back to your body and corporate (anywhere within 30' of your body).
5. Or you can be raised by a graveyard NPC for an XP loss.
6. You cannot see monsters or players, nor can they see you, while you are a ghost.
7. That said, SOME monsters CAN see you as a ghost and can kill you.
8. If you die as a ghost, you rise again at the nearest graveyard.
9. As a ghost, you cannot enter a dungeon you do not have a body in.
10. As a ghost, as soon as you enter a dungeon that you have a body in you will corporate.
11. Xp loss cannot cause you to loose a level, though you may go negative xp.

*** Servers ***
1. There will be servers across the globe.
2. You can pick your server.
3. Server names have not been decided yet.
4. Server rule sets (all PvP servers, RP servers, etc) have not been decided yet.
5. You can have characters on multiple servers.
6. The maximum number of players per server is undecided.

*** Technical ***
1. You can completely customize the interface
2. The game can be played on many types of systems, and can be played on modems.
3. It is available for MAC and PC.
4. It requires a 3d accelerator.
5. You can use 1st or 3rd person perspective.
6. You can play the game in a windows mode, or alt-tab and window key out of the game.

*** General Questions ***
1. You can hook up with buddies in different races within a maximum of about five levels.
2. Blizzard is indifferent to purchasing items and characters on Ebay

Q:Trade skills

A: I'm not sure what you want to know about tradeskills. They are for the most part pretty simple. Blacksmithing requires metals, stone, and various gems. These are mainly gathers by mineral nodes that spawn at random around different areas (not completely random in the fact that there is a set number of places they can spawn.) Someone one with the mining skill can extract these resources and then a blacksmith can combine them into different items. Engineering uses the same resources. Alchemy and enchanting require herbs which are gathered like minerals by a herbalist. Most of your recipes are acquired from a trainer, but there are many recipes that are dropped off of monsters or bought from hidden trainers. These dropped recipes tend to be the more useful ones too. Leatherworking requires leather that you have to have a skinner to harvest from "beast" class mobs. Tailors use linen/wool/silk that are dropped off a humanoids. It doesn't require a whole lot of time to level these skills up, but the cost of recipes and resources can be pretty high.
