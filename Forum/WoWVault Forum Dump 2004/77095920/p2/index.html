"A druid in your group automatically gets any spi/int leather, and will roll for str/sta leather as well. So on the off chance some real nice agi leather drops, I'd certianly give it to a rogue before a druid."

This is the type thing that will keep me from joining that group. None of you are in a position to tell me what my Druid needs. You simply have no idea how I play my character and very likely have no idea how a Druid can be played. Far too many Druids roll over and just play the backup healer/buffbot role and refuse to shift. Those Druids are a major part of the rest of our problems.

I play my Druid to its full efficiency. If the situation calls for a shift, I do it. If a group asks me to join in the role of backup healer only, I still expect to be able to roll on any leather item that makes me a better Druid. I could care less what your idea of a better Druid is so don't bother trying to tell me what items I can and can't roll for.

Here is my advice to any perspective Druids out there. First, decide what type Druid you want to play. If you think backup healer is the ticket, consider going Shammy or Paladin. If you are still determined to be a healer Druid, that is fine too. If that is the case, all you will ever need is Int/Spi gear. If you decide to be a shifting Druid, understand that you are a leather class. That means any piece of leather that improves your abilities is a NEED and not a case of GREED. Do not let others dictate your build to you, suggest they roll their own Druid and take their own advice. They would likely have you make a very gimped Druid if you listen to them.

Next, before joining a pickup group, get a few things straight right up front. First, what role do they expect you to fill? If they demand healer and you want to do something else, part company nicely and wish them luck. Seriously, no need to make anyone angry for no reason. Some people are under the impression that all we can do is backup heal. They are wrong but that is the common conception. Either try to help them see it is wrong or avoid an argument. Personally, I will fill whatever role they ask me. That is why I chose a Druid in the first place. Just be prepared to be asked for healing/buffing the vast majority of the time.

The next thing is loot division. Get it out in the open before you go anywhere. If they tell you you are only eligible for Int/Spi leather, then think about your build. If you are a 1-dimensional Druid and only stay in caster form, then there is no problem with this group. If you are a complete Druid and shift (even though you won't in this group) then I suggest you find another group. You NEED Str and Agi gear to improve your abilities in forms, do not let anyone tell you differently. Try this, suggest that Warrior telling you that you don't need Agi armor does not actually need Str gear as he is a tank and only needs Stam gear. Watch his reaction closely.

My personal rules are as follows. I never take cloth, that is not what I am built for. Let the casters roll for cloth. I expect to roll for ANY piece of leather that is better than what I have. I benefit from every possible combo of leather so it is a need, not some greedy desire. If I have the same or better, I pass and let those that need it roll. I only roll for weapons I have trained. In my case that would be Staves, 1-H Maces and daggers. I use Staves for DPS and a Mace/Dagger combo for casting. I am very selective about maces and daggers though and very seldom do I want one that a melee class wants. Usually it is one that me and the Priest want.

To me, that is need before greed. If it makes me better, I need it. If I don't need it or it does not add to a stat I rely on, I pass to someone that can use it. Unfortunately, too many people have decided they know what every class needs. They are usually completely wrong when it comes to Druids so prepare for it. Luckily, I intend to play with guildies and do not foresee any issues. Those Druids out there that will go it alone have my sympathy as I already know what you are in for. Good luck
