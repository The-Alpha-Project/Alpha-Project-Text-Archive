WOW has the sidekick feature that City of Heroes has.


I am eagerly awaiting the release of WOW. I have been playing City of Heroes to pass the time. While CoH is a good time passer it has no lasting appeal. But it does have one great feature. The sidekick feature. For those that don't know what this is I will explain.

Starting at level 10 you can sidekick a lower level character. The character has to be several levels below you at least. Once sidekicked the sidekickee then fights at one level below the sidekicker allowing people with different levels to group together and both be effective.

For example. Bob is level 20 .. his friend Jim is level 11. Once Bob sidekicks Jim. Jim will be fighting at a level 19 effectiveness.

I hope that made sense. The exp scales so that the level 11 guy would not be powerleveled it would be like him killing creature his own level. It simply is a tool to let different level friends hang out together. I cannot express strongly enough how much this feature helped me and my clan mates group together and be effective.

Please someone who is in beta do me the favour of posting this suggestion on the beta boards.


Thank you in advance.

Chuckleslove already nailed it for you.
People run around doing level appropriate quests for the majority of their advancement needs. When we're grinding, we tend to do so in tough environments that the low level wouldnt survive. The sidekick system would make them survive then, sure, but thats an awful lot of work to support an activity that takes up the mnority of our game time and isnt really something the game wants to encourage.
The low level player would be doing themselves a disservice by skipping their own quests and level appropriate activities. Because of that, they'ed be levelling slower, reinforcing the need for them to go back to being a sidekick. Ultimately the system would be a detriment to the style of game that WoW is.

It isnt practical, applicable in terms of what this game is about, or necessary. Levelling is fast. Faster than CoH. More fun I think.
If you want to get a person who plays 6 hours a week to get together and really accomplish a lot with someone who plays 6 hours a day, you wont see that in WoW. Mechanics are in place to make the more casual player's experience faster and more rewarding but they wont be able to keep up with someone actively playing.
Over time though, they have a much less steep level curve to play up.

The idea is not only a good one for COH but would work just fine in WoW as well.

There are a few simple things you can do to make it work just fine.

1) XP is scale just like on CoH so there is no worry about some low level guy getting super xp.

2) Allow the sidekick to take any quests that would normaly be taken at his "Sidekicked" level. However, they only get the scaled XP and monetary rewards (Also scaled) and once the quest is complete, they will still be able to take it when they reach the real level required.

Done.

This allows low levels to group with upper levels, still fight and have fun and get xp/monetary rewards. They only thing they miss out on is any loot rewards.

I don't see that as a problem for the game and as far as you "Does not fit in the lore" naysayers. So what? It's a game and should be made fun for all, at all levels.
