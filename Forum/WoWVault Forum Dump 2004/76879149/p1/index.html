Date Posted: 10/11 7:42pm Subject: RE: HOw many people are in your pvp guild?
Generally for a pvp guild you'd want a small core of people. If you have to many then you'll run into equipment problems in gearing up for pvp. If you're a big zerg guild then the number and quality of players doesn't really matter. But for solid pvp smaller is usually better because it lets everyone gell with each other and get used to fighting in pressure situations smoothly. Basically you'd want to find out what the usual pvp size group will be in wow and have about 2-3 times that number so you can always fill your pvp group. I'm guessing that it will be 10-15 people for wow. Most likely 10 people or 2 groups because over that you have organization problems and below that you'll most likely be outnumbered. 1 group or 5 people is really to small both in terms of keeping players active and the chance you're gonna hit something bigger is pretty great.

Zerging will have its place as will group vs. group pvp. Zerging takes some organization and most people I've played with don't enjoy it as much as a good even odds fight. 10 on 10 is probably much more fun than 100 on 100 since your individual actions have more of an effect on the outcome of the battle. Being 1 mage out of 25 in a zerg nuking won't have as much impact as you 1 mage out of 2 or 3 selectivly picking the proper targets. You're going to get very similar PVP dynamics as DAOC currently has. Small core groups of pvper will go up against lesser equiped/possibly skilled opponents. They will in turn get greater numbers. Nothing is wrong with either side. Nobody likes to lose and so thats how stuff gets rolling.

The only area where this doesn't really apply could be the pvp instanced battlefields. Blizzard really has a chance to shine here. If they nail it good you could see the elite pvp guilds mostly competeing with each other in these and leaving regular pvp to the casual players. This would satisify both sides. The reason the elite guild would mostly use these is 2 reasons. If only 10 players are allowed on each side this greatly favors "gank" guild because 10 super equiped players vs. 10 random strangers is goign to be an absolute slaughter. So random group probably won't enjoy that play experiance and stay away from it. Zergs piss off gank guild because barring the odd time you completely pull the fight out of your ass you just get steamrolled by 3 or higher times your number. So if done correctly you should have no real cross competition with elite players vs. causual which in a sporting way is best for all involved. The new york yankees don't play against 1000 pee-wee players at the same time now do they?

One other game has a system to avoid this as well: Guild wars. They have round robin battles and what happens there is the elite guild move on to the next round after beating the random groups. They spent the next few matches climbing the ladder and are therefor taken out of play in effect from the random groups. So in that system they only bang heads once in a while.

So basically after posting all that maybe you need to clarify what type of "PVP" guild you're looking for. Casual or hardcore? Both will have differnt group sizes to be competetive because no one likes to lose.
