Content Allowed on the General Board
We want to keep this board centralized on WoW Information, so we do not allow much for non-WoW Topics. If you wish to post Off Topic, please visit the WoW Community Board. The reason this is done is because of the sheer amount of posts so we want to keep this one related to game information.


What is Allowed:
  • Announcement/News type threads for MMORPG's.
    Examples:
    -Mythica is closing.
    -EverQuest 2 was just released.

    -Turbine went bankrupt and AC1 or AC2 is closing.

  • Comparisons (within reason) between other games and World of Warcraft.
    -What is better? WoW or DAoC?
    -Do you like the graphics of WoW over AC2?

  • General MMORPG Threads.
    -What do you think is happening with the MMORPG Industry?
    -Are MMORPG's getting better or worse?
    -What new MMORPG's will be released this year?
    -What game will you play to tide you over?

    Not Allowed:
    Should be locked and pushed to the Community Forum.
  • MMORPG Troll Threads.
    -Think SWG sucked, what do you guys think?
  • Non-MMORPG related Threads.
    -Unreal Tourny 2004 was just released, who wants to play with me?
  • Specific MMORPG threads.
    -Should I play DAOC?
    -I hear AC2 is getting better, what do you guys think?
    (Though "What MMORPG's are the best/do you you guys like", would be ok and fall under General MMORPG Topics above)

    Obviously, Moderators reserve the right to make judgement calls and exceptions, when and where we think they are required.

    This short FAQ answers a few of the most commonly asked questions. Please read this before asking on the board. For a more detailed FAQ, go to the WoW Vault FAQ or check the Official Site for game info. You can also find the Official WoW Forum here and the beta forums here.


  • What is World of Warcraft?

    World of Warcraft is an MMORPG game based on the Warcraft universe from the Warcraft RTS games. The game is being developed and produced by Blizzard Entertainment.



  • When will the beta start?

    Open beta will begin Nov. 8th.



  • When will the game be released?

    Blizzard has announced Nov. 23rd as the official release date in North America.



  • What playable races are in World of Warcraft?

    At release, the playable races will be Humans, Dwarves, Gnomes, Night Elves, Orcs, Undead, Tauren, and Trolls. For more specific information, check WoW Vault or the Official Site.



  • What classes will be available?

    The following classes will be available at release: Mage, Warrior, Priest, Paladin, Shaman, Warlock, Rogue, Druid, and Hunter. For more specific information, check WoW Vault or the Official Site.



  • Will WoW have different types of servers (like a PvP server)?

    WoW will have at least two different types of servers: PvP and PvE. Both have AvH conflict to a different degree. Read about the differences here. Roleplaying servers have been mentioned, but Blizzard has not confirmed whether or not they will be included.



  • What sort of PvP will the game have?

    Standard Player vs. Environment Server

    Players have the ability to attack almost all opposing faction NPCs, including vendors and quest givers. If a player attacks an opposing faction NPC, players of the opposite faction are then able to target and attack that player. Whenever an NPC is attacked, players who share the same faction as that NPC will receive a system message indicating that a specific town is under attack.

    Please be aware that some quest NPCs might not always be available. If an NPC has been killed, players should wait for the NPC to respawn.


    • Actions that will enable PvP on the standard player vs. environment server are:
  • Attacking an NPC of the opposing faction

  • Attacking a player who attacked an NPC

  • Aiding a player engaged in PvP

  • Entering enemy cities


    • Players will also have the ability to enable PvP on their own by using the /pvp command. Once PvP has been enabled by a player, no matter what the method, it will remain in effect for five minutes.

    In time, all standard servers will include a consensual Player vs. Player system called Battlegrounds. Once Battlegrounds has been implemented, players will have the ability to attack other players to defend their cities and towns from invasion. We expect the Battlegrounds system to be available later during the beta test.


    Horde vs. Alliance Race War Server

    On the Horde vs. Alliance race war server, territories will be indicated whenever a player enters a new zone. The zone name will now include additional information to inform the player of the territory's status. Additionally, the mini-map will be color coded to indicate who controls the zone:

    Green = Friendly
    Red = Enemy
    Yellow = Contested

    Players will be able to initiate player vs. player combat based on what territory they are in:
  • Players in friendly territories are safe unless they decide to engage an opposing faction player in combat.

  • Players in enemy territories will always be at risk and can be attacked by all players that belong in the territory.

  • All players can be attacked in neutral (contested) territories.


    • Actions that enable a player for PvP status on the Horde vs. Alliance race war server are:

  • Entering or being in an enemy territory

  • Entering or being in a contested territory

  • Attacking an NPC of the opposing faction

  • Attacking another player

  • Aiding a player engaged in PvP


    • Players will also be able to enable PvP on their own by using the /pvp command. Once PvP has been enabled for a player, no matter what the method, it will remain in effect for five minutes.




  • What are the system requirements?

    The game is being developed for both Mac and PC. Early reports indicate that the game will run smoothly on a P3 800 MHz with a low end graphics card, but that a higher end machine will be needed to take advantage of the game's graphical abilities.



  • Will there be a monthly fee?

    Yes. The monthly fee will cover regular content updates made by Blizzard. The month-to-month subscription plan costs $14.99 per month, the three-month plan costs $13.99 per month, and the six-month plan costs $12.99 per month. World of Warcraft subscription fees can be paid by major credit card, PayPal, and pre-paid game cards, which will be available in stores where World of Warcraft is sold.
