Date Posted: 11/17/04 7:03pm Subject: RE: OMG! I cannot believe Caydiem posted this?!?!
<<Won't happen. Tracing IPs back to a specific individual isn't an easy task, being that most ISPs don't keep logs for that long (if they do) and aren't always willing to do the work to track where the traffic is coming from unless legally required.>>

WRONG.

It's not the ISP that has to track it, it's the web server and all they have to do is go to the ISP and ask them who that IP address was assigned to last. And from working with ISPs and with the security industry IRL, I can tell you right now that this request is trivial, as most ISPs have a log going back six months of what IP addresses are assigned to who. And unlike you and I, HD space is pretty cheap for them, so they don't really get too concerned about how big the log gets.

In fact, with most high-speed providers, you don't get a new IP address every single time that you connect. Most people just stay connected and when they do reconnect, the server sees their MAC address and just gives them their old IP address back.

On top of that, he went and bragged about it on gamefaqs.com and all Blizzard basically has to do at that point is subpoena their web logs and see if the IP address they recorded matches what they have. If it does, then it's a slam dunk of a case and if they want to press charges, it's a no-brainer.
