Tobe is now Level 11 and has traveled to the Lands of Azeroth and met up with some fellow mates.


He has learned much about his class and there is much more to learn before he is done.

Healer

That is our primary role in groups
The heals scale up very nicely so far.
Strictly healing will get you killed fast.
Nuker

Nukes damage is very random
You have two types of nukes, Cast time nukes, and Insta Nukes (Level 10)
You have the ability to stregthen your nukes with skill points.
The spell will continue to cast as the mob try's to interupt you.. this is where Rune is important, You don't get interupted unless thrown to the ground.
Rune Tanking

Well I wouldn't advise tanking, but with a good Rune on you can stand there and take the hits while others pull mobs off you.
Rune cast times are very fast.
Its a must for solo'ing
Aggro Machine!

OMG! - AoE Heal Aggro! This is the only thing that hasn't changed in the Hate system between EQ and WoW.
Do I hate this?
Yes.. but what is the alternative?
Rez'r

Yes we ressurect!
Running so far in Ghost form > then Rez
Ressurection is a Huge amount of mana.
DoTs!

I love the DoT line for Priest.
From what I can see so far they are the strongest DoTs out there.
Resistance could be a bit less and I have found creatures that are immuned to DoTs.
You really can't kite dot (well to be honest you Can't Kite at all!)
Regen

Regeneration needs to be less mana IMO.
Regeneration needs to be a faster cast.
RUNE! RUNE! RUNE!

Rune Owns - No getting around it
The Fast Cast.
Pre-Runing is worth the effort
Rune + Travel saves your butt.. (in more ways then one)
I would say its the main source of power for the Priest Class.
Mezzer!

Sleep is cool when it doesn't resist.
Sleep is too much mana.
Cast time is to long
Its a management tool but the long cast time negates some of the value of this spell.
And more!

The Priest Class is coming a long very nicely, I am still in a learning phase.. (Level 11 now) but these are my findings so far. The Class is worth playing and quite frankly I see more Priest Building Groups then other classes. I am currently playing with Tailoring and hoping this brings me more fortune because the spells are very expensive.. but that is class wide.. which IMO is okay.. the more effort you put into quest, skills.. the more money you will make to cover your own personal desires for your class.

As for the Spell lines they are split into two Catagories.. Damage and Healing. The more skill points you spend increases your abilities in the role that you desire. Play style is everything and I believe Blizzard has done a great job in this for the priest class.

I will be posting more as I move a long to the next phase.. and hopefully many of you will be joining me when phase II comes out and by then.. I hope the Druid push is live!!!

My new found abilities and Grouping.

Earlier discussions were on “two different spell lines”. I will go into the Holy Line.

Level 12 as a priest we received two upgrades and one new addition to my Holy Line of spells

Holy Word: Shield
Holy Magic
35 Mana
Instant cast
40 yd range

Instantly shields the target, absorbing 83 damage. Lasts 1 min. While the shield holds, spells will not be interrupted by physical attacks

Holy Word: Fortitude
Holy Magic
65 Mana
Instant cast
30 yd range

Holy power infuses the target increasing their Stamina by 4 for 30 min.

Inner Fire
Holy Magic
20 Mana
Instant cast

A burst of Holy energy fills the caster, increasing his damage by 2 and armor by 60 for 3 min.

Holy: – Beneficial type spells; Heals (direct healing and regens), buffs, self buffs, dispels, cures, Runes and Direct Damage (Nukes).

Shield = Rune. Works for incoming melee damage “Nukes bypass the rune”.

Fortitude: Stamina increases Hitpoints as it does in EQ. The formula is based on Class from what I can tell.

20 Warrior, 18 Pal, 16 Rogue etc.

Inner Fire: Self only buff, increases AC and damage output

AC: (damage reduction) = (.3 * (armor class - 1)/(10*level+89))

03-28-2004, 10:25 AM
The Purple Bar all the way accross the middle is the exp bar. Hovering over the bar tells you your numeric progress into the level, i.e. 3045/8000.

The icons on the left are user definable hotkeys, and can be skills, clickies, spells, food, drink, etc. There is a small up/down arrow at the right of this area that takes you to other pages for more hotkeys

The icons in the middle are all tools and options like quests, report issues, setup...

The right hand side is inventory. Inventory space is very precious so far. It looks like it may be a game balance mechanic, as certain classes require reagents, and reagents don't stack.

There is a big map to the upper rigth that shows cities and town (and your corpse, hooray). Next to it are buffs and spell effects both positive and negative.

I haven't played a caster yet, so I don't know if their interface differes significantly, but Sobe's looks pretty much the same.
