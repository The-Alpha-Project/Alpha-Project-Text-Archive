Well having been a long time beta tester of WoW, I first can say I really enjoyed the experience, the game is probably the best MMO to date.

I am however extremely disapointed that a company like blizzard would ship a product that is quite simply, not finished.

Level 31-60 spells for a lot of classes are not in the game in the current build. Thats almost half the entire character development, I dont think thats an acceptable thing to cut. Shaman for example get a total of 2 new unique spells from level 31-60 and thats it.

There is absolutely no PvP system in place to make it worthwhile or give reason. The honor system, pvp ranking ladder, amoung other things they promised simply did not make the cut. Currently PvP is a lot of graveyard rushing and ganking by level 60s on level 20s with no reward or penalty either way. PvP is a good 1/3 of WoWs content, and it was not developed at ALL in beta, what does this say?

Most classes are done well, but there are a few that still have serious glaring class breaking issues that are totally unresolved. Druids bear form AC is almost half a high level warriors AC and they are not able to perform the role of tank as blizzard said they should, the cat form doesnt have a DD finisher and the DPS is no where near good, warriors get owned by unarmed paladins punching them to death after the "awesome" warrior changes, mages spend a good 25-40% of their time sitting and drinking while soloing after every 1-2 mobs, shaman are still extremely overpowered in PvP, etc..

There are only a few raid encounters in the game currently, a handful at best, that will not keep level 60 guilds occupied for long at all. They say they are holding a lot of raid content back, but even looking at the onyxia raid and how it broke, I wonder why they think they can hold back raid content and have it untested. Are we too experience onyxia type bugs with the instance during retail because blizzard didnt want to release the rest of the raid material to the beta testers?

Itemization is still horrible at the top end of the game. Warriors, rogues, and other equipment based characters benefit greatly post 60 by getting uber raid gear and purple items, meanwhile casters like mages do not gain any significant power by equipment and so they are essentially at their max potential when they ding 60, meanwhile the melee characters keep improving. Moreso, hunters experience a severe shortage of high end bows/guns, some beta players can name the entire list of available ranged weapons on one hand, and they usually are lacking in the damage department. Druids feral forms damage is not based on equipped weapons, so that means they will not increase in damage by geting raid equipment like the warrior and rogue will, therefore making the already large gap even larger.

There are many other issues aswell, and its disheartening that blizzard feels these hugely important issues are nothing worth worrying about and are rushing the game out the door to make christmas sales anyway before its done. Battlegrounds? hero classes? seige engines? I doubt we will see any of them at all.

I love WoW and im as excited as the next person to buy it on tuesday and get playing, but im just disapointed, I had hoped this last patch would solve all my concerns, but it did not.
