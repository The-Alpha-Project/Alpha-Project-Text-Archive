Taolin posted a question concerning the watering down of classes based on PvP performance and posted that he's concerned WoW will end up like most other MMO's and have a bunch of cookie-cutter classes with different names and different equipment just to make PvP fair.

Katricia Responded


In World of Warcraft, balancing for PvP situations (regardless of what server they are on) is done separately from balancing PvE situations.

As an example, in a PvP setting a spell such as stun may be altered to stun the same targeted player less with each consecutive cast. That same change would not take place in a PvE situation.

To further explain the example above:

PvP: A player stuns another player for the first time, the duration of the stun will be at 100%. A player stuns the same player a second time; the duration of that stun will be 50% of what it was the first time. A player stuns the same player a third time and the duration of the stun will be 75% of what it was the first time. A player stuns the same player a fourth time and the targeted player is now immune to stun.

PvE: A player can stun the monster/NPC over and over again without any change occurring.

~Kat :)

I disagree. I think balanced PvP in a MMORPG is a worthy goal. There are plenty of pitfalls to avoid, but the benefits are substantial.

Human opponents add depth to a game. Mob AI and event scripts may satisfy adventurers temporarily, but in MMOGs especially, where playtime is measured in days as opposed to hours, there's a fair amount of repetition. Furthermore, there's a wealth of resources available online for those interested in maximizing their xp gain/time. The result: go to camp, single pull, grind. Or if you prefer raiding, get numbers, do buffs, clear trash, kill boss. There are few surprises for the veteran PvE gamer, intimately familiar with the relatively simple mechanics of PvE combat. PvP on the other hand, involves opponents who run when they realize they're overmatched, instead of at 20% life. It involves stealth for the purposes of making surprise attacks, as opposed to simply bypassing content. Smart PvPers use terrain and battlefield position.

In my opinion, good PvP combat provides more opportunities for players to be heroes. A good PvP system will encourage level-appropriate conflicts, allowing all players a chance at victory, without letting any one dimension of combat overshadow all others. The fewer "iWin" buttons there are, the more exciting the conflict becomes. Level, gear, consumables, class/race abilities, awareness and character control should each provide benefits, but deficiencies in some areas should balance advantages in others. This delicate balance requires effort by the game developers, but if the devs get it right, the players will end up providing each other with months or years of fresh, challenging, memorable content.
