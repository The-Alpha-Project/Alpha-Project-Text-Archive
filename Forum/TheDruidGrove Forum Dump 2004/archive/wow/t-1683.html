In case someone doesnt know how that works. You have various races and 4 base classes Fighter, rogue, healer, mage. Once you get to lv 10 you have to select among with a dozen or so professions before you progress further. So a rogue can be a theif, scout or assassin at lv 10. But wait... A mage can be a Warlock, Wizzard or Assassin... Humm so you can be a stealty assassin or a caster assassin. And that stealty assassin can specialize in bow, dagger or sword... Or the wizzy assassin can specialize in staff or dagger (or if elf sword). Then you can also quest for runes so you can memorize to access up to 3 Diciplines (specilized skill trainers) that give you limited skills that enhance yours or give you skills other classes have (track, heal, weapons). So you could have a mage assassin with a pet, tracking and charm. Or a Warrior with Fear, heal and group buffs. By the points you invest each level you decide if you will be more defensive, offensive, melee, caster, pet class or healer.

Confused?? Basically you make decisions throughout your toons life as to what his ablilities are, making him more unique to you.. That would be like EQ, each spell level letting you select some but not all spells for your class but sharing more spells.

WoW will start with probably 3 classes War, mage, priest and then let you progress along a skill tree, specializing along it based on your race till the character is more unique than in EQ but less than in SB. I did not see any theif class but may have missed it.
