Its been the debate for almost five years in Everquest. Other games have attempted to find some way to thread the needle...and unfortunately failed. Either coming out with a bland game geared towards casual play (SWG) or a game geared so much towards uberosity as to make it unfun (Eve).

Now World of Warcraft enters the fray with their own ideas and attempts to maintain both the interest of the Casual player and the interest of the Uberguild players.

Weighing in on the casual side is their stated goal of having every class be solo capable, instanced settings, and a plethora of quests (Yet, fortunately, staying away from the generic and repetative quest generation modal of DAOC, E&B, AO, SWG, and others). They've also powerful equipment will be available to the soloist and small groups through hunting and crafting. Then again, Sony says this repeatedly as well about EQ.

Aside all of this is the promise, yet, of guild sized and even server sized events. Raid targets. Promising the best of the best gear to the raid players, as it were.

As seen already, the Casual vs Uber debate begins anew.

Having been on both sides of the issue during my EQ days, and now looking back through neutral eyes, I can only say this.

A game needs both. You need plenty of worthwile things for casual players to do, or you will lack your player base.

However, you also need a steady stream of raid encounters for the "uber"players. Without it, the game lacks meaning and purpose. While a casual player may resign himself to never reaching that level...more frequently the casual player will find himself at one point of his "Career" trying the uber route. To see how the "other half" live and to try and get his hands on some of that "phat lewt". You need something for the "little people" to dream of and strive for.

Games like SWG and Shadowbane, for instance, lack that. When you wake up one day (usually in the first week or two) and realize you own composite armor already and the best weapon your profession can use...you wonder is that really all there is?

Everquest provided the players with various levels of difficult to aquire gear. Which is one big reason why they managed to hook and keep so many people. From the days of the original class armors and planar armors, to the latter days of Ornate, there was a progression of things to strive for in the game.

From all accounts, WoW understands this lesson from EQ. The questions now are, have they learned it and how well can they implement it? Its difficult to balance gear for casuals which is good gear, yet to create raid only gear which is worth creating a raid to obtain...yet isn't so far beyond what a casual player can hope to get as to breed dissention.

As of now, I have faith in the developers of WoW to design challenging and dynamic raid content. I hope they can do the same for the casual player as well. By all accounts they certainly aim to do so.
