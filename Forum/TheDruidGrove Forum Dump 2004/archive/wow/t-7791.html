From the world of warcraft forums here (http://www.worldofwarcraft.com/thread.aspx?FN=wow-general&T=42264&P=1&ReplyCount=12#post42264)
It's not really accurate to think of the Scarlet Monastery as Endgame. We never intended for players to be stuck or hover at a level 39 level cap. That is a by-product of the game being in testing. Dungeons such as the Monastery are mid-level content that we expect you to level through in a relatively short period of time.

We have a tremendous amount of high level content planned -- true high level content -- for solo players, groups and raids. None of that content is accessible to you in this Beta yet.

One day you salty Beta testers are going to look back and laugh that you once considered the lowly Scarlet Monastery *endgame* content.

As for too much low level content, well personally I don't think there is such a thing as *too much content*. I think the true problem would arise if people started running out of quests and had to grind exp for multiple nights before doing a new quest.

Lastly, if the leveling is relatively painless in the lower levels, it encourages people to try out new races and classes and absorb the *entire* WoW experience, rather than just a small fraction of it. It's a lot of fun to log in a new character and run through completely different quests in completely different zones and be able to actually make meaningful progress.

Don't worry, we have plenty of zones and dungeons slated for the high levels. We know players are going to spend a lot of time maxed out or close to max. We will provide them with plenty to do. Until then, enjoy the ride =)
