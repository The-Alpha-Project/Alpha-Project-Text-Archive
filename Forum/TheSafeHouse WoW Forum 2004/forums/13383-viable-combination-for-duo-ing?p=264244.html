        some basic combinations:

        Warrior/Priest - Warriors *never* lose aggro, and the priest's other non-healing spells find good use here.

        Warrior/Paladin (or Warrior/Shaman) - offtanking, extra melee damage, heals, it's all good. While paladins can main-heal for a duo team, remember that their mana pools are much smaller than priests.

        Rogue/Paladin (or Rogue/Shaman) - see above. Usually you'll end up splitting the tanking between the two of you, though, as paladins lack an effective taunt mechanism and you'll be too focused on DPS to want to waste energy and time feinting.

        Shaman/Priest (or Paladin/Priest) - slightly more interesting. Back and forth healing is the order of the day here, with a slightly more castery flavor to the entire affair.

        a bit more esoteric:

        Druid/Paladin - paladin tanks, druid heals. fairly basic, except the druid has more damaging spells than the priest, but no resurrections (which the paladin does have)

        Druid/Warrior - not quite as optimal as above, but it still works.

        Druid/Druid - this one is hilarious. Thanks to the fact that druids can be, alternatively, warriors or rogues, you can have a real round-robin. One fight, you play the healer, while your partner turns into a dire bear and tanks (with total aggro-lock, i might add). Next fight, you switch. For added fun, grab a third member that can heal, and one of you tanks as a bear while the other deals damage as a panther - with basically all rogue specials at your command, you're a damage machine. This combo has nearly everything in versatility, too - you can both shift to panther to sneak together through stealth-required areas to nail a quick assassination mission, or shift to druid form in a tough battle for an emergency heal. The druid's only real weak point is the lack of any resurrection spell.
