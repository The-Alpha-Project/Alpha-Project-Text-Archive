        Just about any Duo works well. I'm very partial to Paladin/Mage duo. Mage does incredible damage, summons water for the Pally, and can open locked chests they find. Pally heals, can Rez, and Tank...plus can dish out some pretty hefty damage himself.

        Warlocks are among the best Duo partners out there, mainly because having a partner means they can really let loose - something they can rarely do solo. Warlocks can also keep a steady supply of bloodstones and soulstones going to a group, reducing any downtime that occurs due to death and substantially increasing the chance of success on hard runs.

        Warriors I cannot recommend playing at all. You'll find youself frustrated quickly. In EQ, all a warrior is, is a meatshield. You learned the role and lived with it...or re-rolled as another class. In WoW, warriors have tons more abilities, and being essentially forced into Defensive Stance (the only one which lets you Taunt) while in groups means that 2/3rds of your abilities seem wasted. You're doing a lot less damage than you could be doing and generating enough rage to do things is problematic if you are in a good group. It's frustrating.

        Rogues are a decent duo class, if your partner is another rogue or really just about anyone except a mage. If your partner is a mage, you may end up feeling a bit useless as a rogue -- things either die before they reach the mage, or they are a bit too tough to handle. Finding an in between spot can be difficult. Just about any other class makes a better partner for a rogue.

                      Shaman/Priest combo another to try

                      Well, this weekend a friend of mine and I started up a Shaman and Priest on the PvP server. We're now level 21 and 20, respectively (I'm the shaman). PvE wise, we don't die much and put out quite a bit of damage. In fact, it's now my favorite duo. Being able to heal each other makes us nigh invulnerable. And with the combination of Holy Word:Shield and my Stoneskin totem, we've got defense WELL taken care of.

                      Offense is also well taken care of. I nuke well enough, and melee really really well. I'm using a dagger and my Rockbiter buff. It adds +11 to every hit right now, so with a dagger being a fast hitter, I have a hell of a damage output melee wise. I also have that nifty damage shield that's unresistable as far as I can tell. The Priest has a hefty amount of nukeage himself, so his damage output is nothing to sneeze at either. And in WoW, there's no disparity between the weaponskill of a melee class and a caster class as far as regular hits go. So it's actually worth it to melee as a caster. Warriors and Rogues still have styles and special attacks that put them ahead in the melee category, so don't worry about that. I'm just saying that getting out your weapon as a caster is still a decent amount of damage output and well worth it.

                      I'm frankly surprised about how well this combo has worked out. I truly recommend it.

                      I can't comment on PvP yet, as we're too low to make a dent on the level 50's we typically see. It's rare that we see anything in our level range, so haven't had that experience yet. They're looking at taking out the resistance based on level bit in PvP, though. So hopefully, when I cast a lightning bolt at an Alliance punk, they'll at least feel a tickle as opposed to nothing at all. :D
