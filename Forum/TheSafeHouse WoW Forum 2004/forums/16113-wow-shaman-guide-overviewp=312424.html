    WoW Shaman Guide/Overview

    11-19-04, 08:05 PM
    I spent the last few hours writing up this little novel, mostly from the top of my head. If anyone has any input, it is very much appreciated. It may at one point get submitted to a few choice fan sites if I can polish it enough. A lot of this info is through reports and other people, since my shaman never got above 18 in beta, but I have spent a lot of time talking to older shamans.


    -----


    This guide won't list the full spell list, the full talent list, or any very detailed talent builds. If you want that, go to thottbot.com or wow.allakhazam.com, and search around on various forums. It also won't explain the very basics of WoW, so it expects you to know what such things as "cooldown", "instant cast spells", "threat" etc is, and how they affect gameplay.

    "Shaman
    The old phrase “jack of all trades, master of none” is an excellent way to describe the Shaman; they are the only real “hybrid” class in World of Warcraft. While they do not excel at any one aspect of their repertoire, they function passing well when performing most tasked called upon by a party. Need a support healer? Looking for a secondary tank? After another damage-dealer? Have an empty slot that needs someone to support the group in general? The Shaman can provide. They have a number of utility spells as well that help make life easier for any party."
    This is the semi-official description of the WoW Shaman. It is pretty accurate, as far as class descriptions go. The shaman is really a versatile class, probably the most versatile class currently in the game.

    Weapons:
    Shamans can use 1-handed maces, 2-handed staffs, 1-handed axes, and daggers from the get-go. The last two require training at a Weapon Master. In addition, you can get a talent that allows you to use 2-handed maces and axes, that you can get at level 20 at the earliest.

    Armor:
    From level 1 to level 39, shamans can wear cloth or leather. Leather is superior to cloth for armor rating, and has mostly the same stats, but gear upgrades happen so quick that you will often find yourself wearing cloth pieces while levelling up, because that's just what dropped at the appropriate time. At level 40, you can learn to wear Mail, which raises your armor rating pretty significantly. Mail armor tends to have less caster-friendly stats though, so you may find yourself wearing a good amount of leather to boost your casting stats if you decide to be a casting shaman. A shaman can also wield a shield, which pretty much doubles your armor class if you get a good one. The shield will cause you to do quite a bit less damage though, as two handers (axes and maces in particular) are far better dps than equal 1-handers.

    Races:
    Shamans are restricted to the Horde side. Orcs, Trolls and Tauren can be shaman. These classes have various racial traits, that you can find listed elsewhere. These traits are all good to the shaman. In particular, orcs' axe damage boost and resistance to stuns, trolls' berserk mode (for fast heals at low health) and tauren's 5% hp boost and war stomp are good abilities for Shamans.

    Spells:
    Shamans get a large variety of spells. Three types in particular will be your bread and butter:
    Shocks: These are instant cast spells on a shared global 6 second cooldown. You get three of these:
    Earth Shock: Your strongest shock. Deals good damage, and interrupts spell casts, leaving the target unable to cast a spell in that school for 2 seconds. Also produces a large amount of threat. Great for pulling casters, interrupting casts, dealing damage, and pulling aggro if tanking, all in one.
    Frost Shock: Your second strongest shock. Deals good damage, and snares the target.
    Flame Shock: Probably your weakest shock overall, but the best of them for damage per mana, so still very good when playing nuker or soloing. Adds a DoT to the target for 3 ticks after the spell hits.
    The shocks are augmented through the Elemental line of talents.

    Totems: These are instant cast totems summoned by the shaman, and stick around for 30-60 seconds depending on the type. They come in 4 schools, Earth, Wind, Fire and Water. You can have one totem up from each school at a time. With a few exceptions, these totems all have 5 health, but they have 0 cooldown, so can be dropped immediately after being destroyed.
    There are too many to list here, but some of the most useful ones include:
    Earthbind Totem (Earth): One of the first totems you get, and you never get an upgrade to it. Thankfully, you don't, as it's wicked powerful all your life, at a very low mana cost. It snares any enemy within 10 yards, and refreshes it every 4 seconds or so. Good for stopping runners or escaping in PvE, godlike for killing anyone in PvP. The fact that you never get an upgrade means it's less than 1% of your mana pool at level 60 to cast.
    Stoneskin Totem (Earth): Damage dealt to group members within 20 yards of the totem is reduced by a fixed amount. Excellent at low levels, somewhat less useful at high levels.
    Searing Totem (Fire): Shoots at enemies within it's range. Acts as a very mana efficient but slow source of damage, much like a DoT.
    Mana Spring Totem (Water): Restores mana to nearby party members for 1 minute. Costs 50% more to cast than it returns for the shaman, so only efficient when grouping.
    Healing Stream Totem (Water): Restores health to nearby party members for 1 minute.
    Grace of Air Totem (Wind): Boosts the agility of any nearby party member by a LOT. Reportedly adds 20% dps to your average level 60 rogue, and helps everyone else as well.

    You get a multitude of other totems as well, like taunting totems, resist boosting totems, strength boosting totem, AE DoT and nuke totems, etc. The ones above are the most essential ones, however.
    Totems are boosted through their respective Talent schools.

    Healing: Shamans are decent healers, and get two main heal spells.
    Healing Wave: Your first heal spell, and by a small margin the most mana efficient one. Takes longer to cast for each rank up to the fourth, where it maxes out at 3 seconds. Not a bad choice if you have time to cast it, but generally overshadowed by the faster Lesser version.
    Lesser Healing Wave: You get the first rank of this at level 20, and it's your bread and butter heal spell. At 1.5 sec cast time, it's your most versatile heal, and almost as mana efficient as Healing Wave.
    Chain Heal: Heals your target for a non-efficient amount, but then bounces to up to two more targets, healing 50% less for each bounce. Good for downtime healing if several people are hurt, but rarely used in combat. Somewhat more useful if secondary healer and less reliant on mana efficiency.
    Heal spells are augmented through the Restoration school of talents.

    Other spells/skills:
    You get a lot of other great spells too, including:
    Lightning Shield: A very strong 3-hit self damage shield, and one of your most mana efficient ways of doing damage. If you choose the appropriate talent, it gets obscenely good for soloing and pvp.
    Weapon augments: These add certain effects to your weapon. The best of these is Rockbiter Weapon that adds significant dps and increases threat generation considerably. Flametongue Weapon is great for added dps when not tanking, and the others can be very good situationally.
    Ghost Wolf: Wolf form that increases movement by 40%. Can be lowered to 1 sec cast time with talents. One of the few movement buffs in WoW, and very strong for escaping unwanted/losing PvP battles.
    Lightning Bolt: Generic nuke with longer range than shocks and better mana efficiency. Spends some time getting to the target, and has a fairly long cast time. Decent for pulling or sustained ranged damage.
    Chain Lightning: Works like Chain Heal except with damage. Very very strong for group PvP, especially if augmented through talents.
    Reincarnation: A passive skill that allows you to rez yourself back to your corpse once every 30 minutes (modifiable by talents), obtained at level 30. This is a convenience tool that will save your group from wiping 100% in an instance every so often, but doesn't really add any raw power to the shaman. It's still a very nice skill to have though, and helps make shamans desired even for the hardest instances, where their lack of specialization would usually make them overlooked.

    Talents:
    You get three schools of talents like everyone else: Elemental, Enhancement and Restoration.
    Elemental: This school focuses on boosting your damage spells and totems, but have a few talents that affect other spells indirectly. The shock augments in particular are great, so is Elemental Focus and Improved Chain Lightning (if you PvP). The final talent here (Elemental Mastery) is generally not worth the loss of other abilities unless you are going for a specialized nuker - in which case you should probably have been a mage. Most PvP shamans will have a good amount of talents here for fast damage.
    Enhancement: This school boosts your physical skills and physical based totems. Improved Lightning Shield, Improved Rockbiter Totem are skills most soloing shamans drool over, and Improved Ghost Wolf is wicked for PvP. A number of the other talents (Thundering Strike, Flurry, Parry) are extremely nice if you are going for a tanking build, and for general soloing. The final two talents are questionable. Stormstrike is terrible at the moment, but Improved Grace of Air Totem can be very strong. Unless you are going deep for the tanking talents anyway though, they're not really worth it.
    Restoration: This school mainly boosts your healing and healing related skills and spells. It has very good mana reducing talents for heals and totems, as well as a very powerful ability in Nature's Swiftness. Improved Lesser Healing is also a very strong talent. A lot of people, both PvE and PvP will go 21 points deep here for Nature's Swiftness, some even deeper. The final talent, Mana Tide Totem, isn't all that strong and requires you to take several mediocre skills, so I doubt we will see very many shamans going that deep.

    Overall, shamans get a few very powerful Tier 2 and 3 skills in all schools, but mediocre tier 4+ skills. Therefore, most shamans will probably have 2 or 3-school builds. A common tactic for retail is to go heavy Enhancement for the levels 1-50+ (easy soloing), and then spec to a more healing or nuke specced build after that.

    The shaman in general:
    The shaman is a very strong class, and like I said very versatile. They are a powerhouse in 1v1 PvP, solo well above average, and can fill multiple roles in a group. At low levels, they are extremely powerful since they can basically do anything almost as well as anyone. At 50+ though, their versatility begins to hurt them as they aren't specialized enough to be efficient main tanks, efficient main healers or efficient nukers. They are still decent offtanks, good second healers, and decent damage dealers, as well as still being very powerful in PvP. Their PvP prowess will be limited somewhat at advanced stages, since most serious people will probably use targeting macros to kill totems almost instantly. A single hunter or wand-wielding caster with such macros can shut down multiple shamans' totems pretty efficiently, making the shamans either abandon the totems or face massive mana drain.

    The versatility also makes the shaman a very advanced but fun class to play. To quote a certain company, "Where Do You Want To Go Today?". At low-mid levels, you can do it all. The vast amount of options rewards skilled play and good understanding of the game. It is certainly a very nice class, and doesn't really lack anything big today. Some players are suggesting a talent that allows shamans to wear Plate, so they could be alternatives to Warriors also at high levels, since there are no such alternatives on the Horde side. Such a talent would have to be buried very deep in the enhancement line, possibly requiring 45 or even 50 talent points placed there, but it wouldn't be terribly unbalancing if it required the shaman to give up most/all of his talents from other schools.

    Unfortunately, the low level strength, versatility and pvp power has given the shaman a reputation to be the latest "god class" of WoW. Whiners and forum trolls everywhere demand the nerfage of the Shaman, and min/maxers with 20 minute horizons loudly declare they will all be shamans at launch. This will likely result in a few serious nerfs, and an amazing amount of morons playing Shamans. I agree that at least the Earthbind Totem needs a tweak. It's fine that it's powerful, especially when people learn to kill the totems, but it should definitely not cost 40 mana at level 60. A limit to the level it could affect for each rank would be nice, and then it could be upgraded through 4-5 ranks until it finally affects level 60 players.

    In any case, whatever Blizzard decide to do, I hope they strongly consider the strength of the shaman post-50 when making changes. Hybrids are always powerful at low levels and in easy content, but suffer when the need for specialization increases. Reaching level 60 is very quick in this game, so that is where the focus of the balance changes should be. If they perform a kneejerk reaction based on the whines and nerf them to be more balanced at low levels without considering high level effects, the shaman could become a very weak class at the top levels. If they do it with some finesse and skill, the shaman could remain a very fun and interesting class for years to come.

    - Elerion, WoW beta Shaman
    Entharion, semi-retired Everquest enchanter

    re: WoW Shaman Guide/Overview

    Well written. I played a shaman to the mid thirties and every other class except the paladin to at least level 20 in beta. I think I would say without reservation that the shaman with talents was the strongest overall character of the lot (although not by a massive margin by any stretch).

    The Shaman has the ability, if well played, to adjust to a changing environment better than almost any other. A stam/int build ensures exceptional survivability and the class can be played solo in so many different manners that it is simply hard to not come up with a winning strategy at any given point in time. Lightning Shield with healing alone can allow you to take much higher level mobs out (at a grave loss of efficiency) and decent melee plus healing and the damage-shield make for wonderful overall efficiency. With the changes to mana regeneration the Shaman is also well suited to front-loading some spells/totems and regenning to full mana again during even a brief fight or pair of fights.

    In all, I really like the class and will certainly be playing it in retail, although I'm still debating if it shall be my main.[/spoiler]
