76. Re: Lineage 2 is Awesome | 2/9/2004 7:21:35 AM GMTST
You really shouldn't compare Lineage 2 and WoW. Lineage 2's main audience isn't the same as WoW.

Lineage 2 just like Lineage 1 is focused primarily on PVP. It is true that Lineage 2 has good graphics. It uses the Unreal Engine just like Ultima Online X does. However, graphics aren't everything as AC2 proved. As far as character customization goes, it's way overrated. SWG had very detailed character customization, but the truth is no one notices all the small details. In EQ especially after Velious, you separated yourself from other characters through your equipment. The same is true for Lineage 2. The armor and weapon models are extremely detailed will probably the method in which people will differentiate each other. The class system is by no means simple. There are plenty of classes to specialize. It's very similar to AC2 in that sense. Finally, while content may seem sparse. A lot of content is focused on high level gameplay which most people never reach in beta. Personally from playing Korean beta, I feel gameplay did feel a bit dry. But hack and slash is nothing new. In fact, that's all mmorpgs are basically anyways with notable exceptions. All you do most of the time in EQ, DAOC, FFXI is hack and slash. Quests just happen to be a nice break from that routine. Again PVP is the focus of Lineage 2. I actually had a lot of fun PvPing in Lineage 2. Whenever I got bored of leveling, I'd visit n00b areas and shank them. At high levels, pvp is actually very different. Guilds capture and command castles. Fully controllable mounts that also act as siege add a new element of strategy to PvP and a lot more fun than controlling those DAOC siege weapons. At this point in the game, the gameplay can be quite fun and robust. It's certainly not for everyone. I'm sure it doesn't appeal to a lot of WoW fans .But it wasn't made for WoW audience either. It's really targetted for the Korean audience. If you look at the games that are very popular in Korea, the most successful ones are ones that promote direct player versus player competitions(i.e. Lineage, Starcraft), involve strategy and online. Boxed games don't do sell very well in Korea with the exception of Starcraft.

WoW is much more about storyline. I'm not saying pvp isn't important in WoW but it's certainly not as emphasized as in Lineage 2. A lot of EQ, AC players are drawn in by storylines. It gives a mmorpg a focus bigger than hack and slash. I love the low system requirements on the game. It's basically reassurement that the game will run smoothly on my system. Far more important than high detailed computer slowly graphics. I would write more about WoW, but it's pointless since most of you keep up on this game anyways.

Finally as for EQ2 and Middle Earth Online, I don't expect anything good from those games. A lot of EQ's subscriber base is excited about WoW and not EQ2. First, the game has really high system requirements and that will certainly alienate some of their subscribers. The graphics don't impress me at all. You get to fight highly detailed versions of EQ1 mobs like Wasps and Rock Giants. Oooh.. Very Exciting. The models look like plastic dolls. Considering that SWG was a piece of crap, I don't believe SoE will do fare any better.
Middle Earth Online that game will suck. I can you this, even the developers don't like playing the game. However, that could easily be explained by the fact that Vivendi is in charge. Blizzard and Sierra have hate relationships with them as well Not to mention the same staff that did AC2 will be doing Middle Earth Online. The current AC2 staff doesn't have any of its previous AC1 staff. AC 1 was awesome. The graphics may have sucked but the developers made a fun game that had an engaging storyline and GM's playing an active part in that storyline. AC2 was a piece of garbage.
