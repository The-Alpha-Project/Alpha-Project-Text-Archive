68. Re: What are some good books to read? | 4/3/2004 3:34:39 PM GMTDT
I'l put another vote up for Song of Ice and Fire, the first book of which is a Game of Thrones. It is a very well done fantasy novel in which you will be suprised, disturbed, sometimes even angry. In this book not every character is guranteed to live. And not every death is a heroic sacriice. Sometimes you are just SoL.

This is not your classic hero versus bad guy fantasy novel where the hero gets magic sword/magic ring/whatever to kill the bad guy before some terrible thing happens. In this book you really have to ask yourself who he bad guy is, and even after reading three books, I am not sure if I've found anyone who would clearly fit the classic definition of good. It's all shades of grey.

If Sci Fi is more your thing, I recommend dune. Wow. Terrific series. I have read it several times over. I really love the origional series, and have mixed feelings about the new ones.

About "The Davinci Code" which was mentioned before. Feel free to read it. But remember what it is. It is, despite what anyone will tell you, a work of FICTION. It is based loosly on fact. Yes, the two "secret" organizations in the book do actually exist, but a lot of what he says is utter crap, and not really sustainable. He presents as fact things that only have the weakest evidence. The book is NOT as well researched as many would claim. It is a good book, if taken as a suspens thriller, and parts of it fooled me. But please don't act like the secrets revealed in the book about the truths the organizations protect are ACTUALLY true. They are not, or at least have next to no credible backing.

The fact is the basis of the book is that constantine re-wrote the bible to suit his needs. It was he who made Jesus into a god (nobody believed that before, he claims) and he destroyed all the old copies, thus eliminating the role of women in the church and a bunch of other nast stuff. And early christians supposedly simply accepted an Extremely drastic shift in their fundamental beliefs in stride and didn't save a single version of the origional bible. Sounds plausible. (BTW, this isn't really a spooiler. one of this is an real revelation in terms of the book. I avoided talking about anything you don't find out in the first fifth of the book.
