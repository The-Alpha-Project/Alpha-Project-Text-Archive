258. Re: WoW Economic Management Challenges | 1/5/2004 8:30:14 AM GMTST
I read the whole thing…

I’ve played on EQ, DAoC, Ultima, and a very small amount on FFXI. I also played Diablo, D2, D2:exp, Warcraft III, Starcraft and SC:exp. I beta’d for SW:G, not that they listened (pretty much all I can say). I think this is my first post…I haven’t read this much on economics since the pre-launch SW:G boards (back when the game was going to be cool).

I think that some of the basic consumables should be immune to inflation / deflation. I realize this does not work in a real world model; fortunately this is not the real world. Everyday vendor items like minor healing and manna potions should be the same price all the time, because the new characters will be the only ones buying these. As levels progress the stronger stuff will be needed, and the economy can affect them just fine without possibly disheartening the newbie. The low-end items that newbies must purchase to survive should be immune to economic trends.

I don’t think the concept of item decay should be thrown out completely. While the concept of having the Worldcrusher ™ hammer eventually break upon the head of an Orc seems unreasonable, a rusty dagger should eventually fall apart. My idea is upper end (Soulbound) items are incorruptible; they will last forever. Normal vendor-purchased items and low to mid level player-created items will also slowly decay over time. High-end player-created items will also be incorruptible. This will create a very slow but natural money sink that will grow organically with the population, yet allows the really important stuff (to you) to remain yours forever.

Why not have a generic consignment option for already-created vendor NPC’s? While selling your product to the merchant items that he might be inclined to sell (a Weapon merchant for weapons, Herbalist for herbs, etc.) may activate a button called ‘Consign’. The vendor agrees to sell your stuff for a price, and in return he gets a cut. In this model items that are not consigned are out of the market and back into the M6. Items that are sold get a cut from the merchant and also sends gold back into the big pool.

I would like to point out that it should be the privilege of high-level characters to hoard money. They probably lost real-life health and real life friendships to make that imaginary coin. Instead of finding ways to get them to spend money on ‘Cruises’ or taxes (I get enough tax in real life thanks) have them spend money on things they may find cool, like having some holy / unholy aura cast over their house. It probably won’t do anything, but if your house looks cooler than your neighbor’s, that can be cool. It can also inspire your neighbor to get something similar, which can start a lot of money flowing back into the big pool.

Nice comments Arakim. WoW you read the whole thing, and understood it, and incorporated previously stated ideas into your post, impressive. For economic correction mechanisms that operate in the opposite direction however (i.e. to correct the problem of player held wealth being lower than target) maybe the impact of the correction could fall more heavily on newer (poor) players than experienced (rich) players.
