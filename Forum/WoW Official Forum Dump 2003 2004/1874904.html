 1. 2 Problems in WOW retail... | 7/21/2004 5:42:01 AM GMTDT
Ahh, I know what you are thinking how can I gurantee thats these 2 problems will exist after all beta testing is complete and WOW goes gold? Simple, these 2 problems exists in every MMORPG to date and have destroyed quite a few, the 2 problems of end game PvP and the economy. No I am not worried about quests, content, leveling too fast or slow, the rest system, PvP nerfs and/or balance issues, these can and will be corrected some quickly and some after retail.

Lets look at the classic PvP problem...
You "did your time" and reached the highest level attainable in the game you feel you got the best "build" and the best equipment and you are ready for full blown PvP. The first question you have to ask yourself is why? Some will PvP to grief, some will PvP to "defend your realm" and others will PvP just because they are mad Bush smoked Kerry in the election, the point being you will most likely at one point or another whack on another player... But why, is there a reward for "defending your realm"? You cannot ask for a % of the victims coin as a reward or you will have the majority of peeps using the in-game mail to send all but a few gold to an alt character before they PvP. You cannot ask for a random item... you got to be kidding if you think Bliz is gonna let a group of 8 lvl 50 peeps take 10 secs to kill a solo lvl 50 that was gathering herbs or something and then take an "epic" item that he hours upon hours to obtain, or payed a ton of gold for that ubah leet crafted item... "just restrict which items can dropped from the body" BEEEP wrong answer, you will have mages and rogues going into PvP naked.(trust me I have seen this before, and no they were not even wearing a thong)

O.K so whats the reward? Satisfaction you PWNED someone? nah that wears off after a few hundred kills... A system will need to be implemented(something like DAOC's realm abilities and PvP exp system) so that peeps keep that monthly fee coming well after they hit the highest level. Lets say you are 50 and you PvP another level 50, and you win you would get say... 10 PvP exp points. This should be on a sliding scale to +/- 5 levels of you - you 45 them 50 you would get 20 exp points - you 50 them 45 u only get 2 PvP exp if they lower then 5 levels you get NO PvP exp points this will really slow down the idiots that are 50 whacking on lvl 44 and under peeps as they get nothing for doing it... what about group PvP? Thats simple, lets say u have 4 level 50's and 1 level 40 and the level 50's think they are going to "PvP power level" their friend... the group goes out and kills a group of 5 lvl 44's each would get 0 PvP exp point and the level 40 would not get any as well, why? because in group PvP you would use the highest level member of the group to determine the how many PvP exp points are recieved. This would encourage peeps of around the same level to group together and fight in PvP together. Of course group PvP exp points would be divided up between the group.

What about when you lose in PvP??? "Who cares ill just get rezzed and keep coming at ya again and again I dont care there is no death penalty"(see I can read your mind cant I?) Well same scale... only you lose the exp points(but only at half the rate, this would guarantee you keep getting PvP exp points if u atleast hold your own 50% of the time) --- You are lvl 45 and a lvl 50 whacks ya you only lose 1 point --- you are lvl 44 and a lvl 50 whacks ya you lose NOTHING --- you lvl 50 and a 45 whacks ya you would lose 10 points, same sliding scale as if you won but only half the loss. But what about the idiots that keep "griefing" the town guards and get rezzed and do it again and again and keep dieing but dont care because they lose nothing? Again simple, when u die in PvP or get whacked by an enemy realm guard have the items and armor you have equiped take 5X times the "normal wear" damage, of course you can always get your items repaired but are you really gonna waste a ton of coin running into the guards again and again? most peeps will not, this will also make peeps think twice about going into PvP, but not be too harsh that would discourage it. If repairs are really expensive mabye only 2X or 3X the normal wear damage.


What do you do with the PvP exp? Well theres always the clasic +1 to this atribute for X amount of exp and the next lvl you can get +2 for 2X exp and so on, or for something really big like 10,000 exp points you can get that "unique mount" that goes 25% faster then any other, there are enough creative minds at bliz to figure out the rewards...

The economy...
Bliz has already stated that they want money sinks to keep the economy atleast half decent, but having unlimited skill points and unlimited trade skills will trash an economy quicker then "all I can spend gold on is repairs and gambling." I really dont mind the "unlimited skill points" because i guess you could learn to speak all the languages or somthing but haveing every character have every trade skill at the highest lvl is simply stupid. I read the beta forums alot and there was a thread about a peep putting 10 items on auction for 1 copper each and only sold 3... this items you could sell to the NPC merchants for 5 silver... Not only do the auctions have to be "connected"(of course horde city connected auctions for horde peeps and alliance city auctions connected for alliance peeps with Booty Bay not connected as too cater to both sides) but they have to be meaningful... The above example means 1 of 2 things are happening. 1, peeps are not looking at auctions(why look at auctions when u can have all the crafting skills maxed out and make your own stuff) 2, peeps are not seeing all the items because the auction cities are not connected. Bliz in the end will probably connect the auctions too the cities so this leaves us with crafting. A character should be limited to 4 trade skills per character, I mean come on peeps with 8 characters per account this still gives you all the trade skills, just not all on 1 character. Yes Bliz more charcters means more leveling, more leveling means more game time, more game time means monthly fees will be being paid for a longer period of time... Of course some skills should not count against this limit of 4 such as "survival", others like "first aid" should. Also wep crafting and armor crafting should be seperate. And all this stuff about "the best boots at lvl 30 might be crafted boots but the best boots at level 32 would be dropped boots" isnt gonna work... I know you want crafters to have a meaning in the game but you do not want every single player to have to count on them for the best items right? The solution is easy, make all the best armor and weps crafted items, but the best earrings, belts, rings, and other items be drop items, this also opens up the opportunity to put earrings, belts and ring crafters in an expansion down the road....

P.S. I know this might sound like I am "whining" but my opinion of WOW has changed in the last few months from "I am going to get the collectors edition the day it is released" to "well I wanna see what it is like in open beta before I buy it" No, am not mad I did not get into closed beta I mean if there were really 300k signups, what were my odds? I know Bliz is working extremely hard on WOW, but you cannot recreate the wheel, you must make it more round, lighter, faster, and stronger... and no meaningful PvP with a risk/reward PvP system(hey who said I was not a futures trader see that slip in I did = risk/reward) and giving everyone unlimited trades skills is just gonna "tank" WOW quicker then greenspan tanking market by saying "we are headed for another depression in the next 3 months"...

P.P.S If Bliz does fix most of the problems in beta, and ONLY the peeps that signed up for beta bought WOW could you imagine the server count? I mean lets say you have a server load of 3k peeps and 300k signed up? MAN THATS 100 SERVERS!!! Would be the most popular game for the next 10 years....... LOFL 100 servers X 8 peeps per server, man someone is gonna be ebaying 800 characters LOFL...
How can I fly like an eagle when I am surrounded by turkeys?

 5. Re: 2 Problems in WOW retail... | 7/21/2004 6:45:02 AM GMTDT
It is true that PvP and economy have been two rather poor aspects for many MMO's in the past (minus Planetside of course). I've played many MMO's over the years (Asheron's Call, AC2, FFXI, Planetside...) and granted PvP is fun at first and a great way to test your mettle but it does need a driving factor at times (more than the person you wanna kill is a jerk, etc). Granted the whole Alliance vs Horde is quite the aspect for it but personally I believe an exp system, or some other form of rewards for killing ppl, is a great way too help keep the feud/hostilities going for the players. Also we must not forget PvP is not restricted to the big and the bad max - or near max - level characters. The sliding scale should solve that problem but just the thought of doing PvP from lvl 1-max means alotta shinies... Perhaps the upgrades or whatever you could spend your exp on should be on an increasing scale of cost so you could spend it at lower levels and higher levels evenly throughout your PKing career. The risk/reward system idea is great for the most part but the only problem is: spent PvP points. Maybe make it so they act as a passive bonus. This way they can't be spent but still have a point too being collected and a detrement too being lost.

The economy may not be so bad how Blizzard has it thus far. It could get bad where everyone has all the trade skills at max and the market is getting flooded so a limit on the skills may be needed. Not so much that the skills should be restricted to how many each character has but how many of the same item can be crafted in a certain period by the same character. Nothing insane just some sort of cap to keep crafted items from being everywhere. And why should it matter if a crafted item is best for a character for a while? Not every character is or should have the best. The reason it's the best is cuz it is rare or something. Granted if it is crafted the likelihood of that is low - unless the best crafts are rare recipes - but it could be a bridge item/an item to tide a character over for a few levels. The new item types seems a good way to solve the issue but Blizzard needs time for that... poor buggers under so much stress.

So a cheer for Mongooser and the marvelous ideas and a big cheer for Blizzard for the hard work that is sometimes so unappreciated!
