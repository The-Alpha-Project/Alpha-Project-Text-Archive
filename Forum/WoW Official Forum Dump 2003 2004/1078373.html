<Begin Legolas bashing>

Walking swiftly through a glade, you suddenly hear the quickening footsteps of a large party of creatures heading your way. Glancing around fearfully, you almost wet yourself when a band of blonde elves, all nearly identical to each other, step out from beneath the shadows of the surrounding trees.

"We draw near, you can feel it," the presumed leader of the party says, his eyes opening and closing in an odd pattern of surprise, antagonism, and affection.

"Wh-wh-who are you?" is your stuttering reply, as you squeeze your legs together in terror.

"We are the Legolasi."

"Jesus Christ!"

"So some have called us. We--" The leader stops speaking as he and his band start licking themselves clean.

"So that's how you do it. Have you told Aragorn?"

"He would not understand," the leader says. Almost as an afterthought, he adds, "I am an elf."

"I would never have guessed. Is it contagious?"

"Telling you that was a diversion."

"From what?"

"I am uncertain, but don't tell me you weren't impressed by the cool way I said it."

"You've got me there." You subtly look around at the other elves, each of whom is talking to himself as though someone is listening. "Why are they doing that?"

"It is a kind of self-gratification experience. If we do it enough, good things may come."

"To those who wait?"

"We are not good at waiting. We tend to be premature."

"So I noticed when you went snowboarding at Helm's Deep, when you could have killed more with your arrows."

"I am an elf."

"Yes, we went over that."

"We must move on; we cannot linger!"

"Is your wife coming, or something?"

"I have not the heart to tell you. For me, the grief is still too near."

"Sounds pretty bad. Look, she can't have reached the Eastern Shore just yet. We still have time to...talk."

"It is not the Eastern Shore that worries me. A shadow of a threat grows in my mind. Something draws near, I can feel it."

"Is this about that self-grati--"

A loud horn sounds in the distance.

"The horn of Gondor!" the elf leader cries, his eyes lighting up.

"Oh no. Don't tell me Boromir's involved in this whole thing. Look, I can go to him, help him out..."

"You would die before your stroke fell!"

"Man, I didn't mean it that way!"

"Hush. I think the White Wizard approaches!"

"Is that a euphemism?"

"I am an elf."

"Dear Lord."

Suddenly, out of the underbrush crashes a tall burly man, holding an oddly-shaped horn. His eyes rake over Legolas, and he smiles. "My precious."

Legolas, either smiling or frowning, says, "You look terrible, Bory."

Boromir chuckles. "I'm sorry I'm late. I was with Arago--"

"Aragorn! Where is he? Speak up!" Legolas' eyes widen and narrow again, quite dramatically.

"It's not what you think."

"You lie!"

"No, really, he and I were just talking to your, er, wife, here..." Out of the bushes comes a tall, graceful creature.

"That is one of the Mearas, unless my eyes are cheated by some spell."

"Yes, he has a lot to tell you."

"It cannot be!"

"But it is, my love. He told me something disturbing about you, too...About a certain accident you had, once upon a time, involving a tree knot."

Legolas, quick on the uptake for once, says, "Shall I describe it to you? Or would you like me to find you a box?"

"We have trusted you this far; you have not led us astray. Forgive me. I was wrong to despair."

"Isn't that my line?"

"It may be."

"Are we cool?"

"We wants it."

Getting a bad feeling about what might come, you slowly start to slink away.

<End Legolas bashing>
