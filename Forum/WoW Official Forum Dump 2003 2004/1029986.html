163. Re: Q&A 01/07/04 | 1/17/2004 1:35:34 PM GMTST
I feel that people are nit picking what looks to be a visually stunning game. I agree with the points made in reference to relationship to &quot;buffness&quot; of fighter/rogue/magic-user classes. A mage or rogue should not look like the Incredible Hulk, nor should a fighter be a scrawny tooth-pick or look like Jabba the Hut. I am for a happy medium. Each class should have a standard body model, with two more possible selections that are variations on that model. So then at generation, for let's say the Warrior, you would select Standard, Athletic(Thin but muscular), or Huge(Hulk-like). My solution would allow for things like a blacksmith-mage that forges enchanted weapons, who would definitely be more muscular than the run of the mill spell caster. There are also warriors who rely on speed and skill as opposed to the biggest weapon and brute strength(Are they over-compensating for something?). This would easily apply to all races and allow some variation in character appearance, and most likely eliminate the animation concerns of the devs as they would still have control over a limited number of models. This WILL entail more work for the devs, but you guys DO get overtime don't you? ;) (Sorry if you don't) Perhaps also some modifications could be linked to these selections, that would help further define the play style of the character. Huge might get a bonus to strength, while loosing some speed/dexterity, while standard has no bonus/penalties one way or another, and thin has a bonus to speed/dex while a penalty to strength. This could be initial affecting only the starting base stats, or more accurately reflect the choice of the player, affect stats as they progress so that a thin character can never become as strong as a huge or standard character without the use of magical items, potions, or spells. Similar rule applies to Dex/Speed. The height factor is almost irrelevant as to the fact that it will probably not be that noticable in game play except between races with significant height differences. This could be addressed by making the &quot;Huge&quot; player models slightly bigger than standard or thin player. Other than that every model stays the same height. That is my solution that I believe will make almost everyone happy.

Good luck and good times to the devs.
