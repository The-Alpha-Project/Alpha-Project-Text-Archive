1. No Risk Trade Skills? | 11/29/2003 6:23:10 PM GMTST
What do you think about this?

"Recipes will work every time. If you collect the proper ingredients to create an item, you don't have to worry about them being wasted due to failures. There's also no need to constantly experiment in World of Warcraft to find a combination of ingredients that will work. A recipe clearly lists what it will allow you to create and what raw materials are required. There's no guesswork (or surfing through dozens of Web sites) involved…"

I'm not sure if I like this idea or not... on one hand I know how frustrating it can be to gather expensive materials only to lose them (and the related time) to a "failure" in the crafting process, but on the other hand the risk involved did keep things interesting. The title of "Master Crafter" will be meaningless if there is no risk involved in gaining it.

Another thing that seems to be implied here is the fact that all items made from a particular recipe will be identical, regardless of the skill levels of the crafter/gatherer/miner/etc. In this case we'll see the market flooded with thousands of items with the same properties and no Tradesman will be able to make a significant living.
