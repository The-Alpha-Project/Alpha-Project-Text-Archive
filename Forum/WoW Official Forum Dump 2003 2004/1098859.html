    139. Re: Just Curious | 1/30/2004 3:07:31 PM GMTST
    A few things I really enjoyed:

    1. The magic research system of Asherons Call 1. I truelly enjoyed having to "learn" new spells. I beta tested AC and I found it one of the most exciting things about the magic system. (see my hate list further down for how this got ruined)

    2. The evolving monthly story line of Asherons Call 1. The first MMORPG to actually bring you fresh content every month.

    3. The changing of the seasons in Asherons Call1. I can not say enough about how immersive this was. The fall harvest festivals were some of the best times I ever had. We had pumpkin carving, cooking contests, scavenger hunts it was a blast. The physical changes to the landscape with all the leaves turning brown and gold gave you a sense of being there (didn't matter that the graphics weren't that great compared to todays games). Then to have it all roll over to winter with snow on the ground where there was none a month ago. The inclusion of seasonal mobs like the Snowmen during winter (the Giant Snowman was awesome). The scarecrows during the fall with some of them just being a scarecrow and some actually being animated made hunting in some of the harvest fields creepy. I'll say it again CHANGE OF SEASONS is AWESOME!

    4. The implementaion of PvP brought to us by Mythic. RvR warefare is probably the best concept for PvP EVER thought up. The consentual aspect made it perfect and brought MORE people into the PvP fold then any other idea out there.

    5. Keep/Relic taking in DAoC. Quite simply one of the coolest things to be in a game. Having your guild banner fluttering above the keep walls and all of the NPC guards wearing your tabard was cool. I loved the shiffting back and forth of the keeps the need to defend them against the godless hordes (Once I became an Albinian I played it well..) Grabbing a Relic and having to run back through the enemy frontiers was ALWAYS a blast. Now that is adventure on a large scale.

    6. Battlegrounds in DAoC. The addition of level specific areas to hone your skills in RvR.

    7. Epic Encounters. Quite simply put in Asherons Call the gods came to visit. There were epic battles, events that must be completed. Once this occured (like the killing of Balzheron) the storyline moved on. In fact the story line couldn't be advanced UNLESS people took part in the epic quest. I can't say enough about ONE TIME events that leave you with a sense of accomplishment. You could look back and say you were a part of something. Unlike many MMORPGS that provide static Epic adventures (EQ, DAoC and their epic mobs, I mean how many times can you kill the same epic mob before it loses some of its uniqueness?) the ones in AC1 came and went never to occur again. They were stories you could relate to the younger Derethians who weren't on Dereth during that time.

    8. Wandering NPCs. Quite simply put the NPCs that rove the lands, walking the roads between towns all of them with their own quests and stories to tell.

    9. Attention to detail (graphically speaking I should say). Some of the simple things that add immersiveness. I beta tested Asherons Call 2 (the best piece of eye candy out there) and one of the things that struck me as very cool was the realistic water interactions. If you stepped into the water you made little waves, when you swam you splashed and created dropplets etc. The swaying of the trees, plants, grasses in the fields when you hear wind blowing. Things like that just totally immerse me into the game.

    10. Role Play Only servers (this is actually number one with me) the freedom to be away from the kids who name them selves things like BigOlBoobies and choose a female toon really makes the game more enjoyable. Not having people talk about last nights sporting event in public chat is great. Being able to hold RP style event (see Harvest festivals, Winter Carnivals above) KNOWING you aren't going to be deluged by someone walking by publicly denouncing you as all a bunch of freaks,

    Now for the things I disliked:

    1. Free For All PvP. UO lost me when I first gave MMORPGs a swing. There was nothing worse than to have to worry constantly about getting griefed by some kid with nothing better to do that gank someone 10 levels lower than him.

    2. Games that work in "windowed mode" meaning that you can have access to your desktop during the game. Asherons Call 1 allowed this which enabled enough smart folks the ability to create Macros, Hacks and Sniffs to be able to wreck the unique systems they had in the game. Doing this allowed the base to post the magic system hacks/sniffs inside of 2 months of release. It runined a very unique magic learning system. The fact that MS/Turbine choose to allow this to continue is what ruined the game for many.

    3. Nerfs as the way to "balance" characters. Toon balancing should occur in beta NOT 6 months after the game has been released. Once you are gold any and all "balancing" should involve raising others up not tearing one class down.

    4. Lack of RP Only servers. I might have bought and payed for SWG (I beta tested it and knew what was wrong with it) if they had included a RP Only server. I at least would have known that I could try creating an enviroment where there were like minded people trying to do the same thing...

    5. Artifically Enforced Behaviors. SWG forced social interaction by requiring people to return to town to get healed. Forcing people to spend hours of wasted game time sitting in a cantina watching a wookie dancing is not my idea of interactive "fun" gameplay. There are ton of examples but in reality what it adds up to is any game mechanic that is designed to force people down a certain path to make the PC work inside the framework of the game. When it is artificial and not very organic it detracts from the game not adds to it.

    6. PC dependent economies. SWG has failed utterly to provide a robust economy by not providing any competition to the Player Created items. If it isn't created by a PC then you are pretty much relegated to junk items.

    7. Item decay based on time instead of use. Time based decay is stupid and an artificial game mechanism designed to stimulate the economy. The problem is it doesn't take into account the casual player. Item decay based off of use is more realistic and is flexible enough to match every players behaviors.

    8. Tedious Crafting.. enough said.

    9. Grinding for xp

    10. LAG. If you design a game to have 200-300 people all in the same zone and in the same area (keep and Relic raids in DAoC) then you dang well better be sure that the game can support it. It is as simple as that. As cool as the whole keep and relic taking events were the whole process was seriously marred by Mythics inability to fix this ONE issue. Seeing as how the issue was tied to their engine (modeling details drawing the whole zone for you even when it wasn't visible) this should have been an issue they could at some point have taken into consideration and fixed.
