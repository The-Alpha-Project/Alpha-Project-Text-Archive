I understand some of the distress people have about paying to play. It was a big deal during the release of UO. People couldn't understand how they could be charged to play a game they had already bought in the store.

The reality is that when you buy the game in the store, you are paying the entrance fee. Then you pay per month to upkeep that membership. Usually the entrance fee helps to cover the developement cost of the intial product and then the monthly fee covers the upkeep and maintainence of the product.

Each may have some profitability built into it and rightly so. As much as Blizzard's goal is to bring thier boyhood dreams to reality, it is still about making some money. It is the essence of a capitalistic enviornment. It is this very environment that continues to push and bring new and exctiting features to content.

Think of this form of business in terms of a sporting competition. Take away the ability to win and the event becomes pointless. Nobody will strive to do better because the essence of competition is based on the win. So everyone produces the minimum and you will be unhappy because there will be nothing new or innovative.

As much as everyone wants this to be free, it will not. MMORPG's are not a cost free service. They require different server techonolgy and upkeep. To expect this to be very cheap in your mind, is to expect them to give you a service at a loss. That is foolish at best. To expect to have them offer this at thier cost is selfish at best. If you would deny the game maker a profit based on the hard work and service they offer, then you are wanting nothing but a free ride.

If free rides is something you think you deserve, then you are lazy and deserve the slap in the face you receive each day for that thinking.

I do not pitty or feel sorry for those who can not afford "Entertainment". I spent several years of my life with very little income. I did it without fancy "Entertainment". If you can't afford the service, you don't get the service and no amount of "Poor Me" whining should change that.

You control your life. You and you alone. If you want to improve it to afford luxuries, you can. If you choose not to. Then you choose your path. Life may deal you some bad cards, but you control how they are played. Take the steps to change your postition or suffer the consequences of them. You decide.

Ive seen those who have had the absoultely worst hand turn them into a winner and Ive seen those with the best hand play them into a complete loser. Which are you? A winner or a looser?
