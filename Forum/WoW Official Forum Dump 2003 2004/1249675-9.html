161. Re: Death Penalty Discussion | 2/21/2004 8:13:17 AM GMTST
Ioren, you make a point few would realize (hell, I didn't think of these exploits until you mentioned them).

Q u o t e:
1. A character runs through a difficult area, dies, then runs back to their corpse, ressurecting themself a safe distance away from creeps with no penalty, and repeats that process until they are all the way through the difficult area, with no penalty. Back when the death penalty meant being rezzed instantly at your bindstone, you would not be able to do this. This is "death-travel" in a different guise! (By adding a currency % loss on death, multiple deaths in this fashion would add up quickly add discourage this exploit)


Assuming that the currency penalty does not happen, this could be curtailed by simply limiting the number of ressurections in a row one could do within a time limit. Also, one could simply allow them to do it once and only once in special dungeons and other quests where "Find The Item" is the goal and the deaths after that are made outside of the dungeon giving others a chance to do the quest or just not allow you within for a period of time (5-15 minutes?)

Q u o t e:
2. Since characters get 30 feet to resurrect themselves, can that location be atop a building or mountain? Will this give players the ability to leapfrog their way across otherwise unpassable terrain or directly to the top of a building, say where the boss to that area resides and skip fighting all the enemies within the tower? (By adding a currency % loss on death, deaths in this fashion would discourage this exploit)


#2 would only be a problem in the early beta when some restrictions like that havent been created where impassable barriers are literally impassable, a solution to this if your corpse was surrounded 30 feet in all directions with mobs in a corner of an impassable structure (i.e. tower wall or a mountain) you can possibly choose a spot proportionatly farther away from the mobs so that you may escape from the corner.

Q u o t e:
4. Since ghosts can float over water easily, can I swim 1/2 across the ocean (or another suitable body of water), die, and run back to my corpse and swim the remainder of the journey? Or better yet, go the entire way as a ghost? I am assuming as a ghost as long as I get to a town with a binder NPC I can pay exp and resurrect myself there at the graveyard? (Normally impossible travel over large bodies of water?)
(By adding a currency % loss on death, deaths in this fashion would discourage this exploit)


A way to keep an exploit like this from happening would be to, once again, set a limit to the number of ressurections directly at the body you can have and/or have a stat reduction after death no matter how you revive. Also, you could prevent someone from going entirely across the ocean as a ghost and binding to the next graveyard available by forcing the character to physically go to the graveyards and bind there, or in the beginning of the game, go to the one closest to that town anyway.

Thought a currency penalty is a good idea (well, great really), it does not necessarily have to be implemented to fix the problems that you discussed
