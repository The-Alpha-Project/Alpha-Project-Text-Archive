71. Re: a PVP system (long read) | 10/24/2003 12:56:09 AM GMTDT
It doesn't look bad, although I don't think a player should recieve any penalty for killing in self-defense. And I don't think it fits very well into the Warcraft universe, which is based on warring factions, which are already polarized for us, so I feel the emphasis should be on the factions, not the individual agendas of player-made guilds.

For the PvP server (NOT the others, because it's just not realistic, gotta give way to the PvE players) I would prefer the PvP conflict to be 80-90% factional. Allowing players to fight their own faction makes the system much more complicated, as you now have players able to "break the law" so to speak. Now that they can break the law, you need something to enforce the law: be it NPC patrols, placing a bounty on their heads for PCs to collect, triggering NPCs guards to bar or kill outlaw players entering allied cities - safe only in some neutral towns, or all of the above.

I know I'm skirting your system, hunter, because you seem to be proposing it for all servers, which is simply asking too much. My hope for greater PvP options rests in the PvP servers, where I DON'T want blizzard to just turn enable PvP for everyone with no consequences.

That said, I like your idea for dealing points to murderous characters to determine whether or not they get flagged as outlaws and also possibly determining how much bounty they get. I don't know about players clearing their names by killing monsters, though, depends on how much you like same faction combat, I guess. I think players who go against their faction should have a hard time, as bandits would get treated in any society.

NPC hunters hirable for PCs - nah... that defeats the concept of PvP and gives rich players an advantage, it might fit for a vanilla server where players don't want to PvP.
Anyway, just my thoughts on the subject.

fun system, but way too complicated. Here is a post I recently wrote in another thread that outlines the basics of how PvP can be instituted on regular servers without interfiering with standard play. (this system is from Asheron's Call the original)

As you said, all zones execpt for spawns and the likes are PvP areas... but


You start as an NPK and you have to defeat a quest in order to become a PK - Once you have done this, you can only fight other people who have done the same or similiar quest. It makes it totally optional but your still able to interact with people who are not PK's. Of course, don't forget that there was an NPK alter/quest that you could pray at in order to return to being a NPK.

I think it is a flawless system and I see no reason why it should not be implemented into every MMORPG. It does not presuppose balancing or any other of the aspects of PvP, it only allows players to try it out without having to start a fresh character. And it is not permanant!
