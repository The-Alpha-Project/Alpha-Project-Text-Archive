You know I have a very nice way of settling this whole name dispute (Blizzard, you may want to listen in on this one, you're free to use it :p):

All names (in WoW) consist of three parts:

First name, nick name and last name:

The First name is very strictly regulated, it must be a appropriate to roleplaying. It is only shown in a person's profile and is used by certain NPCs when spoken to. Roleplayers may refer to each other by their first names.
The first name field in the Character creator comes with a standard randomiser (which accounts for race and gender) that non-Roleplayers can use if they're not overly interested in their characters' first names.

Nick name, or screen name, is the name other players see floating above your head, it's the name used for sending 'tells' and such. It's a lot less strict than the first name, but players have to realise it's a nick name, not a character name. Hence using 'Feon Stormguard' as a nick name, despite it being a perfectly acceptable RP name, would be discouraged, if not disallowed, while less 'RP' names such as 'Kaotik' would be acceptable.
Think of a Nickname as being the same thing as 'Stormcrow' and 'Gandalf' are to Mithrandir (names that he is known by, but that that he wasn't born with). Sorta like in AO.

Last name: Potentially always random, or choosable just like the first name.
Potentially when you choose a last name, you can then have other characters you create on the same account carry the same last name (not necesarilly tied to race, on account of adoption and such).

This would allow people who want 'non-RP' nicks as well as RP-ers to coexist without yelling at each other about names. It would also make your nickname independant of your character's 'real' name.
