I really like this bit.
Q u o t e:

Can ghosts see other ghosts?
Yes. Players may also find ways in the game to see ghosts. Lastly, we would like to eventually add ghostly NPCs that may have quests for ghosts or world lore.

It seems to me that this is actually pretty clever. Now some may see this as further 'wussyfying' death, by giving some a benifit to being dead. However, this is a 'softening' independant of the penalty of dying. That is, you can't just go X experience/time lost + Y fun gained from dying = Z net loss/gain. You still experience a death penalty, you still have obstacles to overcome. But you get access to more content. It's somewhat like the afterlife in many religions. Death is STILL a bad thing, even if there is a heaven afterwards. There being a heaven still doesn't soften the penalty of dying. What it does is alleviate the frustration caused by it.

In fact, it would seem to me that due to this alone, Blizzard has opened things up to allow for a harsher penalty for death if they wanted to, because they can reduce the frustration in another fashion.

Q u o t e:

This death penalty isn’t harsh enough <insert reasoning here>
One of the fundamental philosophies we have had throughout the development of World of Warcraft is to remove overly frustrating elements from other MMORPGs and make the game FUN. Of course, you cannot take this too far, since you also need players to respect the world which is why things like falling damage, death penalties, and other elements exist. If players do not feel like they are overcoming obstacles then the game does not feel rewarding enough. We would rather make various encounters in the game be the difficult and challenging part of the game, not trying to decide if I’m willing to lose 1-8 hours worth of progress to replace equipment or experience already earned in the game. Most of the development team are hard-core gamers themselves, so we do plan to have plenty of challenges and rewards for players that choose to take them on.

Trihell,

1. You can't fly to areas you haven't been to yet. And if you can get to high-level areas, no matter how tough it might be to do, if you can do it with no penalty, you will surely eventually reach a town with vendors that offer high level items for sale. Now, either buy the items for your personal use, which will pay off heavily down the road, or, intentionally travel to the higher level areas to purchase these items and then sell them to all the players that aren't able to get there at that point in time for huge profits (at least until the situation changes).

2. In some very rare cases, it may allow you to aggro a boss monster in order to pull it out of the tower entirely, and to your party who wait in ambush to kill the boss. As was mentioned the max time it would take is about 10 min to get back to your corpse, so if a tower has three levels that take 5 min each, and a 4th level with a boss at the top that takes 5 min to kill, would you rather spend 10 min getting from the graveyard to the tower and an additional 20 min clearing the three levels and the boss, or, 10 min getting to the tower and an additional 10 min waiting for someone to run back, rez at the top, pull the boss, and kill the boss in 5 min, for a total of 25 min? Now imagine if each level took more than 5 min, maybe each level might take 10 min. What can I say, I'm the type of gamer that will do that kind of stuff to powerlevel faster =D

The trick is now gonna be scaling the penalty until it is "just right" to deter the vast majority of death traveling.

4. Many of my examples are intentionally crafted with full knowledge that the chance they will be abused is slim. I simply chose to come up with every possible objection for the sake of argument - and to promote a viable alternative, a penalty of currency.
