1. All those who want ogre concept as reality | 4/2/2004 2:08:10 AM GMTDT
Please post if you agree that the playable ogre race as described here-
http://www.worldofwarcraft.com/info/races/two-headed-ogre.shtml
-as blizzard's april fools joke, is something you would want to see for Real.

I don't care about April's fools. Ya it's great they are having a laugh but lets look beyond all that.

I've been playing mmorpgs for 5+ years now starting with UO. Instead of loving the genre more and more over the years I've learned to mistrust and hate it. Mmorpgs getting worse and worse as time goes by. Becoming less and less creative and inspiring. More and more repetitive and mundane.

WoW has seriously become one of my last best hopes for the entire mmorpg concept. It isn't just how pretty the WoW world looks (and it looks better than i could ever hope for any mmorpg). I know that after all this time it takes more than eye candy to keep me interested in a mmorpg. It is blizzards's creative uniqueness in its gameworld, in its Purpose for playing. Its the idea that playing an undead is a DIFFERENT experience than playing a gnome, not just an APPARENT one. I look at WoW and see all kinds of different worlds waiting to be explored, not just different power sets waiting to be tested that just happen to have different player skins over them.

The ogre concept is the pinnacle of achievement in this feeling of awe and wonder that only WoW now inspires in me. This is truely a concept that no other mmorpg has ever thought of, that no other game at all has ever thought of to my knowledge. It is someone finally saying "lets take a chance on something new and exciting!" It makes my imagination and spirit soar because i just dont know what could come of it!

Will I be stuck with a jerk who is always arguing with me? With a guy who is never around? Will i be known as the wacky useless ogre who is fighting with himself as often as helping my allies? Or the super useful ogre because he both casts spells And fights? Who knows! And that not knowing is a good thing. Its a great exploration that I can't wait to discover.

And what if my ogre game is "terrible" because the person I'm teamed with quits after day one never to show his face again? Well I can either go on as the ogre who's other head became permanently comatose and still be about as functional as any normal one-headed player, relishing in my uniqueness and difference, roleplaying my strange half slumbering ogre. Or I could start over and try again and hope to get a better ogre partner the next time. But I know the risk! I know the possible rewards and the possible headaches (pun intended) and if I want to avoid those things then I can become any number of the other single headed races.

I see no reason at all that this incredible concept which is meant only to be a joke should not be made a reality. All it can do is improve a game and create an experience that no other has. All it can do is inspire and excite!

MAKE THE OGRE COME TRUE FOR ALL OF US THAT STILL DREAM OF THE CREATIVE MMORPG!

15. Re: All those who want ogre concept as reality | 4/2/2004 2:41:52 AM GMTDT
I would LOVE to see this become a reality in the game. It would be one of the most innovative things to hit MMOGs in years.

I have an idea how things like movement could be handled. The two heads would have a box of "permissions" that they could negotiate between the two of them, a series of "checkboxes" to control things like walking and other possible shared actions, such as evoking hostilities against a mob, joining a guild, etc. The two of them could work out which head has which permissions at any given time. In this fashion, the two of them could easily work these things out and understand who has control of what at any given time.

There have also been some complaints levelled which I think are, frankly, a little absurd. One person asked, "What about when one head casts a spell to change forms?" Well, disable such spells, for crying out loud. These ogres would already be more powerful than the average character: they wouldn't need further advantages, like a warrior with the benefits of a strength form, or the technical issues and permissions that would be assosciated with them. Simply circumvent the issue by not allowing an ogre to cast such spells.

The idea of implementing two headed ogres would be an immense challenge, and there are lots of small issues like this that could stand in the way, but if they are dealt with one at a time, I believe there is a LARGE segment of the WoW population that would be interested in playing this kind of character. Don't blow the idea off because it's wild or unconventional. Lots of people could have a hell of a lot of fun with this, and it's something that could be a great part of an expansion or other ambitious addition to the game. For crying out loud, Blizzard: can't you see the positive reaction this has been getting? This is something people are interested in. Give it a chance.
