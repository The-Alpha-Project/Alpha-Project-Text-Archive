37. Re: Who played DAoC, and what did you think of it | 1/19/2004 6:49:11 PM GMTST
I have played EQ, AO, E&B, SWG, AC1, DAOC and out of all those MMORPG's DAoC has been my best experience, but it's still not that great of a game.

Like many have said it was REALLY FUN when the game first came out for the first year or so and before spellcrafting, alchemy, and realm abilities. It was fun because people were so nice and you could find groups easy and the classes were awesome. The community was great unlike EQ when a nice item drops it goes to a person who NEEDS and will USE it.

Then lets travel down the timeroad to ToA (Tides of Atlantis) their most recent expansion. It introduced some new ideas that sound good on paper, but aren't good in game. It made RvR more unbalanced then was before and made EQ style camping and time sinks. One of the great things of this game was that leveling was fast and fighting was fast with little downtime and sometimes none with the right group. ToA also split many servers apart I know on my server Iseult Midgard it basically made all the guilds seperate. Most stuff in ToA is soloable or 1 groupable that most guilds did their own thing. The community is very bad and is so also on my other servers because of ToA. Thats why I have pretty much quit DAoC. Also now a days when you start DAoC it's very tough to get anywhere because all the level 50 people which is usually at least 85% - 90% of the players on a given server use /level for their alts. So a new person has to solo from 1 - 20 sometimes 1 - 30 on less people servers. And then even after that they usually have a hard time finding groups.

DAoC was good early on and in it's prime, but since then it's turn for the worse. If you wanna try out DAoC I would suggest that you dont. Unless you have some friends who are willing to level with you and help you out. Hopefully WoW wont travel DAoC's road and 2 or 3 years down the road have everyone max level with little to do in the way of excitement.
