Can any PC diehards offer a genuine reason for keeping it off Xbox besides there own insecurities and the fact that they, a minority for business considerations in this instance, think it sucks?

Team strategy doesn't happen much without voice communication, least that's what I've learned playing Ghost Recon on PC and Xbox. Sure, arranged teams can perfect their techniques time and time again, but random teams are usually everyone for themselves. Simply because playing and typing both require free hands, on the fly strategy doesn't happen much without voice communication.

The same can hopefully be said for team strategies in WoW. Most hack and slash rpgs don't allow for team play beyond the obvious tank and ranged dynamic, but if group of monsters in wow act as intelligently as they do in warcraft, target prioritization and focus firing will have a big impact, and more importantly require instantaneous communication for acting in perfect unison. Sure you can work on these things between battles with just a keyboard, but unless this is the exact same group you always go with, there won't be a point.

Now there are plenty of reasons for not wanting voice communication, you can't really roleplay with it for one, but it offers some strategic advantage if everyone can talk. Also, for some people, a $150-$200 console is quite preferable to several hundred in computer upgrades, a new video card alone can run you $300-$400+.

I understand that a lot, well, most of you hate consoles and the xbox in particular, and still resent Blizzard for not putting SC:Ghost on PC, and hate consoles and their owners even more as a result, but all of that is fairly irrelevent to the question of whether WoW on Xbox is a good idea.
