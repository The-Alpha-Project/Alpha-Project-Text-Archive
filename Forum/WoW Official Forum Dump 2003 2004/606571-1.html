1. Intelligent Music | 9/28/2003 5:41:31 AM GMTDT
In some games, they use synchronized music that changes in an intelligent manner. For example, there could be a beat, a harp, and some strings while you're in a town (not much action). When a thief comes out of nowhere and starts a fight with you, the tempo gradually increases and some new tracks come in, some guitar, maybe a catchy bass track. When the thief gets you to your health bar in red, the music tempo increases more, and some short, high pitched strings come in (think psycho only more easy to listen to, or the Matrix Reloaded high strings).
And if you win the fight, the music drops back gradually to medium, and then low action. Volume can also increase very slightly to give this effect. There would be a different intelligent music for each area.

This is a VERY old feature for games to have. (Think Mortal Combat 1, the music changed when you were going to "finish him"). It's been adapted far more now though.

This is an awesome possible feature for a game to have nowadays. Music is a very important part of the game and as you know, can make or break some games. Music in this way can also help draw the player into the action even more than before.

If it were possible, would you want WoW to have this feature? Or would it just be more annoying than its worth? What if you could toggle whether it would be 'on' or 'off' while playing? Or do you not care too much about the music your games have, as long as it has music and it sounds OK.

12. Re: Intelligent Music | 9/28/2003 9:01:08 AM GMTDT
I myself have been thinking through and hoping to make a prototype of the system you are talking about here. It makes me think of X-Wing, where the music would change ever so gradually as a Star-Destroyer warped into the sector, and as more and more Tie-Fighters were launched against you and closed in, the Music would get more and more fast-paced.

I've thought of several ways to do this, although a very knowledgable man that I talk to in England has informed me that most of these methods would be quite laggy indeed. I disagree with him, but knowing him, he's probably right.

One way was to have the music broken up into Cells, each of like 2-4 measures in length (about 4-10 seconds) and they would seemlessly string together to form a song. As something started happening, say the person mounts a horse, a new set of cells would work there way into the song and it would seamlessly change to that situation. Then the player gets ambushed, and maybe a timpani intro gets put to next in queue followed by some combat cells.

Another way, although much harder to program, would to have the game actually write the music in real-time, using MIDI type technology. Basically it would write the music in MIDI format (which is like 50kb for a 3 minute song so there wouldn't be much lag from that, it could be all client-based too, so it wouldn't take up much bandwidth) with a custom instrument list that's stored in the game file. With this method, you could do things such as tailor the timpani to be struck at the exact time your sword hits the enemy shield for example, or have a cymbal sound when you get knocked over, and the music could interweave with the animations and make some cool effects. It would be very hard to program, but fun. One problem is that the instruments would have to all be pre-loaded, so it would take up a whole lot of RAM.

20. Re: Intelligent Music | 9/28/2003 2:01:59 PM GMTDT
This feature about changing the atmosphere by adjusting the music has been around for a while. It's called directMusic and has existed since DirectX 5 I think. It works like this: The developer of the game creates a few patterns, and a algorithm which controls the order of the chords, and the variation in the melody. The composition has levels, level 1 being low-paced, with only a few instruments, and for instance level 10 being hectic with lots of drums and other instruments. A good example of the usage of this technology is Dungeon Keeper 2. I honestly must say I never heard this in another game though.
And it doesn't take much resources to load etc. if it is based on a midi pattern.
