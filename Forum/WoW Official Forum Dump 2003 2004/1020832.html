Hmm, lot of these have been from D2. While I do have some funny stories from D2, I'd rather tell one from SWG. If you've never played SWG, they have a language system where anyone can learn all the languages on the first time you create, which most people do. This one wookie however, would actually chat in wookie, so even though you know wookie you still can't understand him. Also as far as playing music in SWG goes, if you all don't play the same song it sounds terrible. I'm just copying and pasting this from someone else's story on the boards:

There's a Wookiee player who gets past everyone knowing Ssyriwook(the wookie language) by speaking in grunts and howls and having his Trando colleague translate for him. He'll also "think" simple abstract emotions for our benefit. For example -

At the Fallen Star I was in the band on stage. We're all playing starwars2. Enter Wookiee and Trando. The Wookiee steps up on stage and produces a slitherhorn.

Wookiee: "Urooga rugh rugh." (brandishes slitherhorn)

Trando: He wants to join your group.

Wookiee: "Roogh." Blinks expectantly.

So we invite him in, he joins, and starts playing his slitherhorn. Something sounds terrible. He's playing starwars1, the wrong song. Stop, we tell him, that's the wrong song.

No change. We're playing starwars2, we tell him. Do you know starwars2?

The Wookiee stops playing, flails his arms around, "Arrrauugh raugh rarrurargh!" and starts playing starwars1 again. We turn to the Trando: what did he say?

Trando: "Um...you better play what the Wookiee wants."

So one by one we switch songs until we're all playing starwars1 together. For a while no one says anything. Then a thought bubble appears above the contentedly toodling Wookie:

"(HAPPY)".

It might have been antisocial if it wasn't all so silly. Eventually he thought "(HUNGRY)" and got down from the stage. But when our leader switched the band to folk he howled and flailed until we went back to playing starwars1. His Trando buddy tipped us very well.
