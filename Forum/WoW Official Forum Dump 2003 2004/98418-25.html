Actually, the main reason for this post is to point out one thing only: we have no rights. None. Playing WoW will be a priviledge we recieve in exchange for money and on the condition that we abide by the rules. Stop paying, no more for you. Break the rules, same thing. You don't have a right to play, you have no right to any name (rp or d00d,) if Blizzard makes a game where you have to pick your name from a list, well... its their game. You don't have to play, they don't have to let you.

Now, people here have been saying that they should be able to pick names like they could for battle.net. Clarification: those are account names, not character names. I know this point has been made a couple of times, but it seems to have been overlooked. A character name is not the same as a "handle" on battle.net. As an example, in WC3, let's say your account name is b00g3r. Now, when you go and play WC3, you do not get to choose the name of your Blademaster... that is done for you. He will be named one of the following:
Q u o t e:
Hero Names: Tojara, Nikoro, Kajind, Mikasa, Samuro, Akinos, Mazuru, Yozshura, Daisho, Kigami, Arashicage, Moogul the Sly, Jubei
Your Far Seer will be named one of the following:
Q u o t e:
Hero Names: Gar'dal Grimsight, Negal Fireye, Kazil Darkeye, Magis Coldeye, Bale Bleakstare, Gorr Grimwolf, Kag'ar Winterfang, Nazgrel, Morg Wolfsong, Kazragore, Fenris'ar Gul
So, by letting you choose your characters name, WoW will be more flexible that a battle.net game. (I know that is a silly argument, but so is comparing WoW to battle.net in the first place.)

In the recent issue of GMR magazine, the issue of the World as a Toy concept was mentioned. Blizzard wants people to have an immersive experience in the World of WarCraft, such that they will want to spend time there goofing off when they are not adventuring/questing, basically doing nothing, with their avatars hanging out in pubs. They are really (so it seems to me) encouraging people to suspend their disbelief, let themselves feel like they are part of this highly detailed and artfully rendered world. I'll be surprised if just about everything about the game isn't designed to support this immersion, from the NPC conversation interface, to emotes; from the trading system, to craft skills; and from character customization all the way to... name rules.

Now, knowing Blizzard (which I might not, actually, at all,) I would guess that humorous names, like Cutlet Porterhouse the Tauren, will be allowed... even encouraged. I really do not think that l33t, d00d, etc. will be ok, nor do I think that zugzugzugzugzug will be gracefully accepted. I think you will be able to pick a first name and a last name, and only the combination will be required to be unique.

One other guess I am going to make (and risk being proven wrong about) is that there will not be different types or classes of server - everybody will have the same rules. No RP server, d00d server, PK server, etc. I am basing this off of the way Blizzard has answered questions about these things in the past. No committal answer has been forthcoming, but hints have, I think, been dropped.

Blizzard will be asking things like, will any players be unhappy about seeing a name like b00g3re4t3r in World of WarCraft, and if so, how many? Will players be upset by names like Krom Stonehoof? What about Cutlet Porterhouse? Then, they will set guidelines for names that will, in their opinion, keep the vast majority of players happy. I think they will totally not be concerned about people not playing the game because they cannot have names with numbers, or whatever. The simple fact is that no one except a total weirdo, regardless of what they say here, will avoid playing the game because of the naming rules, whatever they are.
