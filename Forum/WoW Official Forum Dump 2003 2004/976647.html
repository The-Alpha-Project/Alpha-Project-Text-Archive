1. A Role-Player's Plea [Or Several] | 1/14/2004 9:51:54 AM GMTST
There have been several threads already about role-playing issues, but few of them have seen Blizzard responses. I've even seen at least one rather large "anti-roleplayer" thread, which isn't surprising, since I have seen a fair number of players with that attiude elsewhere (especially in Shadowbane).

In essence, I have one over-arching request. Blizzard, please keep role-players in mind as much as you reasonably can when designing the game. Do not forget this small, but significant part of your player base. Please give us the tools to make this the strongest online role-playing game. There are many devoted role-players out there that are seeing World of Warcraft as a potential home, and there are several aspects of the game that I believe will strongly impact the game's desirability to such players.

Game Master Events. There have already been a number of threads on this topic and supporting this concept, but I have yet to hear any official discussion of their presence or absence, and the role they might play in the game itself. The variety and nature of Game Master Events varries greatly for game to game, and many of us seem to be interested in knowing how they will work in World of Warcraft. What frequency might these events have? What should we expect to see?

Guilds and Community. Will there be any simple way of finding other role-players? A /roleplay on command that could be used with a LFG interface would be one simple way. Role-Playing Forums are also another excellent way, or simply through participation in Game Master Events.

Player Events/Volenteers. Please try to keep event leaders in mind. Work with us if you can; let us know if we're doing things the way you want us to. In most cases, a role-player would be more than willing to work with the development to try to make their event fit into the established storyline more strongly. Let us help you. Give us guidelines for submissions, and you'll surely see a great number of quest ideas, proposed events, and other contributions. Please work with the role-playing community to make this a great game.

Background lore. This is what made the role-player presence so strong in Shadowbane, and what ultimately has caused many role-players to feel betrayed. Shadowbane made many promises, and the vast majority of them were never realized. The game's background is very highly developed, but since the departure of in-game events, the storyline can no longer be truly developed on an "official" level.

Advancement of the story in general is very important to many role-players. I cannot speak for anyone beyond myself, but I will say that I believe most role-players enjoy the feeling of an evolving world, and that seems to be one of the primary draws of many upcoming MMORPGs. Will we, through events or otherwise, be able to affect the story of the game?

Static Quests. Please include at least a couple "hidden" quests if possible, and perhaps even a few quests that once completed, are truly finished. Respawning is perhaps one of the worst things for role-playing possible, in my opinion, because it damages any sense of having a lasting effect. A role-playing justification of *why* people respawn helps, especially if it could lead to an ultimate goal -- in Shadowbane, respawning was the result of a cataclysm, and many players spent their time trying to "fix" the problem.

Flavor. This includes putting background stories on NPCs, having a few taverns here and there where NPCs will drink and dance, etc. Will there be dice or card games that can be played in WoW? Flavor items and such are also important. Allow people to put up temporary tents for festival shows. Let us pay bards to play music for us or tell us stories. Allow us to participate in a Gladiator's Arena with a built-in ladder for victories. Please consider implementing a flag to let people know you're looking for someone to role-play with, or running a role-playing event. Quest Points. Raid Points. Character titles for achievements. The possibilities are endless.

We want intellegent enemies. Eventually, any static monster with any number of hit points and any amount of damage dealing ability can be slain. When I took down Beliathan for the first time in DAoC, I was told by CSRs,"No, you didn't. He's invincible." I'm sure they would have given the same answer to the people that killed the Sleeper in EQ if GMs hadn't been watching that three hour long battle. While these are very memorable experiences, the most memorable ones for me aren't killing the big invincible monsters -- they're finally conquering someone or something that's on your own level, and quite possibly trying just as hard as you are. This is one reason why main characters like the Lich King should be, IMHO, GM characters and not Epic Raid mobs. Once a hundred raids have slain the Lich King, he too will have lost his value. The beauty of GM Events is that the Lich King isn't going to sit around at a spawn point and wait to be slaughtered, he's going to call his armies of undead to his defense, or use strategy to topple the army with his own formidable abilities. Please take this into consideration.

These are just a few thoughts, and I'd love to hear comments on if any of these would be possible and/or are planned for inclusion in the game. While the human mind has a potentially limitless capacity for creativity, sometimes it's nice to have some tools to help out. =D I would love to see World of Warcraft live up to the image I have in my mind, or perhaps even surpass it.

Other role-players, please post your thoughts, requests, and ideas here.

Blizzard, please let us know if we're barking up the wrong tree here. =D
-Salaris, the Arcane Paladin

In the 1970's Gary Gygax and Dave Arneson created the first ever role playing game, and introduced it to the world, and Dungeons & Dragons was born. They defined role playing games and created the industry in one single act.

Role playing games are about interactive story telling, where players have an impact on the world. This sort of environment is NOT found in a game like EverQuest.

People may role play there, but it is nothing but role playing in a glorified chat room with graphics. Not too unlike role playing in a chat room anywhere else on the internet.

What we are discussing here is the premise of role playing in it's purest form akin to Dungeons & Dragons. It was done in the original NWN on AOL, along with other games such as Multi Player Battletech from GEnie, and even in EQ in the beginning through various assorted in game GM quests. Role playing games are not about phat loots or leveling.

Where "role playing" is concerned, there's nothing credible about EverQuest in it's current state. If anything its a prime example of what does not belong in a role playing game.

20. Re: A Role-Player's Plea [Or Several] | 1/15/2004 12:53:23 AM GMTST
I think some types of role-playing in this kind of game is essential. I'm not a role-player by any stretch of the imagination, but that doesn't mean I don't like grouping and talking to role-players.

I believe a lot of the role-playing should be left to a persons imagination. If a NPC doesn't have a backstory then make one. If Blizzard doesn't have enough RP events, then start them yourself. Need to play games? Then make some. I've seen a lot of role-players doing this in other MMORPGs.

Make your role-playing fit the game. Don't expect Blizzard to make the game fit everybody's style of role-playing. Blizzard can give you tools but I don't believe these are really needed for people who truly like to role-play. A good imagination will beat anything Blizzard can add to the game.

With that said, I am unsure whether a seperate role-playing server is good or not. I remember how DAOC's RPG servers were at first and it caused a lot of fighting between Hardcore role-players and Casual role-players. I remember when people sent massive tells (and filled forums with their rants) because a group of players decided to try the RP server and didn't have authentic names. They had fantasy names but they didn't research the internet to find names harcore players thought appropriate.

With this type of attitude I don't believe a seperate RP server will be feasible because the Hardcore role-players will drive-off the people needed to make the server possible.

On the other hand, a RP server guarantees that you will find other people who are willing to put their R/L aside and take advantage of WoW's content. I can see how a role-player's experience can be ruined by comments like:

"Look what I got and you don't"

"I'm better because I'm higher level"

"Wonder how much I'll get for this on E-Bay"

"Where do you live? I'm going to kick your butt"

"Ok, I'll call you tonight after I get pizza"

When seen all the time, I'm sure it doesn't make for a pleasurable atmosphere for role-players.
A seperate server may be the only useful tool a role-player can use.
