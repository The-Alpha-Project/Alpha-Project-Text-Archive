1. Journy of a Paladin: A Story in the WoW | 1/23/2004 11:44:26 PM GMTST
Part I
Daldar Senturus had just finished his training in The Knights of the Silver Hand. Though the holy order had been driven from the land of their birth, Lordaeron, they pledged that they would retake their fallen homeland and purge the evil that now rested within their native soil. The knights had set up a training camp in northern Azeroth just south of the dwarf-controlled lands of Khaz Modan.

Daldar’s camp had been filled with dwarf, Gnome and human alike. Since the invasion of the scourge, The Knights of the Silver Hand had begun to allow dwarves and Gnomes among their ranks. Daldar’s commander, Aldareous, had expressed favor toward Daldar. Aldareous had given Daldar a sword engraved in runes telling the story of Lothar, and his final battle with the orcs. He told Daldar that has father would have wanted him to have it.

Daldar had never known his father but his mother had told him that he had been a paladin. Daldar had joined the Silver Hand in hopes that he would be able to find his father and be united with him. His mother had always been kind to him but had been killed by the undead when the last remnants of The Silver Hand had left from Lordaeron. He had always loved her and was only 16 at the time of her death. Daldar didn’t like to think about his mother or her tragic death.

Daldar sat on the back of a warhorse as he rode into the city of Elwynn. He was clad in silver armor with the symbol of The Silver Hand embroidered into it. His mount was also wearing a set of armor that was done by the same smith so that the horse’s armor and the armor of Daldar would match. In the left side of the beast there was a shield that bore an identical symbol as on Daldar’s breastplate. On the same side of his horse, Daldar had the sword that he was given by the commander of the training camp he had attended.

On the other side of Daldar’s horse he carried his war hammer, the true weapon of The Silver Hand. Daldar’s sergeant had always told him of the importance of a strong and sturdy hammer that could crush any foe that would dare stand in the way of a knight of the Silver Hand.

Daldar neared Elwynn and when he reached the city he tied his mount near an inn and dismounted. He finished securing his horse to the railing in front of the inn and he thought to himself that he could surely find work here. And when he turned around there was a man standing behind him. The man was the first to speak.
http://www.blizzard.com/wow/ScreenShot.aspx?ImageIndex=43&Set=0

“Hello” he said. “I am Marshal McBride and I would like it very much of you if you would do me a favor.” Daldar was amazed that he could find work here in this city without even having to try, at all!

“Afternoon, I’m Daldar Senturus” he said, “What is it that I can do for you?”

“It’s these light damned kobolds you see. They’ve been runin’ around terrorizing my caravans and I need someone to help me scare them out of here,” Daldar was about to say that he would do the task as it was the job of members of the Silver Hand to help citizens of the country and to better the people’s liking of the order “of course I will pay you for any troubles that you would have.” Daldar would have expenses if he were to stay here, wouldn’t he? It was at the mention of money that there was no doubt as to if he would do it or not.

“Yes, of course I will do it.” And with that Daldar left into the woods to rid the woods of the kobolds.



In the steamy jungles of Stranglehorn Vale two magi stood in a clearing flanked by hills on all sides. The first mage was an old man with a long gray beard. The second was a young woman with black hair and a glowing spell staff. She was fighting off a jungle raptor, a beast kept by the trolls and used as war tools to track down their enemies and then to kill them once found. The other mage was fighting off a pair of trolls one with a long, barbed spear and the other with a large battle-axe. The first mage dodged thrust after thrust of the spear from one of the trolls and parried the axe blows with surprising agility for a man of his age. The second mage continued with her own battle with the jungle raptor. The beast’s talons flew through the air, and the mage skillfully deflected them from the air with her staff. Once more the raptor’s tale swung at the mage, and the mage fell to the ground dodging the beasts blow. She rose to attack the beast. She spoke a word of power and her hands glowed with charged energy and an arc of lightning soared from her rune-incrusted staff and crashed into the beast’s chest stunning it and forcing it to the ground.
http://www.blizzard.com/wow/ScreenShot.aspx?ImageIndex=54&Set=0

She spun to meat her next foe and if she had waited only a second longer a troll javelin would have plunged itself into her unprotected back. She swatted it from the air with the same skill she had shown when blocking the raptor’s talons. Once more she had muttered a word of power but this time instead of lightning the troll that had thrown the javelin had burst into flame.

The older mage was faring just as well as his young apprentice. Parrying attack after attack from the trolls and then dispatching the trolls just as fast.

The leader of the trolls, Gesh`vu Stonegaze, had finely ordered the retreat of the onslaught of trolls. He had been given his name by his troops for his watchful eyes that never showed any emotion, even during the fiercest of battles. None of the trolls hesitated in fallowing their leader’s orders to fall back.

The pair knew that they couldn’t hold out much longer. The previous battle had left them both drained of the energies required to cast their spells. They knew that the trolls would be back to collect their human reward that their employer had paid heavily for the mercenaries to capture. They knew that once they were captured they faced little chance of survival from the warlord who sought the blood of any mage in retribution for the death of his son by rouge magi.

A few hours later the trolls returned to collect their bounty, and this time they wouldn’t fail.




Daldar rode of the back of his mount after his battle with the kobolds, both his sword and mace stained with the beasts’ blood. He returned to the village to find McBride waiting in the town square with Daldar’s full payment.

McBride thanked Daldar for his work and said, “There is a man in that tavern there who would like to see you,” he said, pointing to a building to Daldar’s left “He’s wearing a gray robe, odds are he’ll be writing in a tome when you see him.”

“Thank you for the work sir,” Daldar said.

And with that Daldar left and headed into the tavern. As he entered the small building Daldar looked around and found the man in the robe with his tome. He sat down next to the man and said, “I heard that you wanted to speak to me.”
http://www.blizzard.com/wow/ScreenShot.aspx?ImageIndex=32&Set=0

The man in the robe put down his book and said, “Yes, I figured that if anyone would help me it would be you.”

“And why would that be?” asked Daldar.

“We will get to that later, but now tell me about yourself.” Said the old figure.

“I don’t see how that’s relevant,” Daldar said.

“I suppose you’re right,” he said “ now, I have a job for you. How would you like to take a little trip to Stranglehorn Vale?”

“That depends on what you want me to do there. Take a message to someone at port: okay. Go on a mad, useless, damned quest into the untamed jungles: not a chance in all of heaven.”

“I’m afraid it would be the second one lad, but in no means would it be useless.” Said the robed figure.

“No, forget it. Not a chance.” Daldar said. He was preparing to get up and walk out when the man said, “There would be an excellent reward if you would do this for me.”

“How excellent would this reward be?” Daldar said.

“Very excellent.”

“What do you want me to do?” Daldar said easing himself back into his seat.

“ I want you to rescue two of my magi. I’m afraid that I’ve lost contact with them.” He said.

“Now hold on,” the paladin interrupted, “I am not going to go risking my life to save some damned mage. And I’m not going to work for one either.” Daldar began walking out the door when the mage said, “Too bad, your father did. You look just like him. Did you know that?”

The paladin stopped when the subject of his father was brought up. “You knew my father?” Daldar said while still in mid step.

“He risked his life to save me,” The caster said but also purposely neglected to mention that there was also a woman involved. “My name is Rhonin.”

Part II
Daldar pivoted on his right foot turning back to the mage. He walked toward him sat down and said “What do you know about my father?” in a harsh tone to Rhonin.

“I know that he gave his life to save me as well as many men during the invasion to retake Khaz Modan from the orcs.” Answered the mage.

“A paladin would never give his life to save a mage,” Daldar said, “but to save the invading armies… I will trust you, mage,” Daldar paused for a moment and then added “for now. Tell me about my father.”

“You know,” said Rhonin “it wouldn’t hurt to be a little kinder.”

“Tell me about my father, please.”

“I suppose it’s a start… I will tell you all you want to know about your father after you do my job. All of my accounts are in this book,” he said pointing to his tome, “While you are gone I will put all accounts of your father into another tome and then give that to you along with five-hundred gold pieces.”

five hundred gold pieces for one quest! Daldar thought. Just what would he need to do to save these magi?

“I will give you the funds that you will need to get to Stranglehorn Vale and back,” the mage continued, “You will Ride west until you get to the sea between the Two mountains where a transport will be waiting for you. From there you will sail south to Stranglehorn Vale where you will land at Blackwater Cove, a port city established by humans many years ago, although it is now controlled by the goblins. From there you will head west to the top of Mt. Glavale, the tallest peak in Stranglehorn. That is where I last had contact with my magi. You’ll find them there in a troll city at the top of the mountain.” The mage handed Daldar a sack of coins and said, “Good luck, boy.”

Amazed that the mage knew all this Daldar stood up and said, “If you’re not telling the truth to me about my father, mage, I’ll have your head.”

Rhonin paid to attention to Daldar’s threat and returned to his tome, and waited for the paladin to return with has two magi.




Daldar arrived at the city that he had been told to be in and headed toward port. He saw a large ship docked at port. He went up to the vessel and asked one of the sailors where the ship was headed. “We’re takin’ a shipment of goods to a bunch of goblin’s down in Stranglehorn Vale,” the man on the ship answered, and for a moment Daldar found himself speechless at the man’s answer. It was just as the mage had told him. “Do you know if I could buy passage to the jungle with you?” asked Daldar to the sailor. “I can’t help ya lad, but if you talk to me cap’n ‘buot it the he may be able ya do so.” Answered the sailor.

http://www.blizzard.com/wow/ScreenShot.aspx?ImageIndex=49&Set=0

“Of course,” Daldar said to himself. “Do you know where I can find him?” he shouted to the sailor.

“Aye,” said the man “he’s in his cabin,” he said pointing to the aft of the vessel.

“Thank you,” said Daldar heading for the ramp up to the ship. The paladin climbed the ramp, and turned right when he reached the top heading for a large door near the aft. When he got to it he knocked and from within came a deep voice saying, “Enter!”

He came into the room to find a dwarf sitting at a table hunched over a collection of maps and charts. “What can I do for ye sir?” the dwarf’s voice boomed.

“I’m looking for passage to Stranglehorn Vale, do you think you would be able to help me?” asked Daldar, “I can pay you for any troubles it may cause you.”

“Aye, I can do that for ye.” The dwarf said with out looking up “We set off at dawn tomorrow. The price ‘ill be one hundred ‘n fifty silver.”

The next Morning Daldar boarded the vessel and set off to Stranglehorn. His journey was rather uneventful.

When Daldar reached Blackwater Port Goblins swarmed all around him trying to sell him this and that. One in particular, named Throg, was quite persistent. Throg was trying to sell the Paladin a gem that he clamed was able to detect the voodoo sprits of the trolls. More than once the thought to step on Throg had crossed Daldar’s mind. Throg was shorter than most of his companions and Daldar thought that no human could blame him for squishing Throg if they had known the little nuisance.

Daldar started for the mountain in the distance. By nightfall Daldar had almost reached the summit but decided that traveling in the dense jungles at night was not a good plan as well as the fact that if he waited until morning he would be able to surprise the trolls while they slept. Dladar set up camp, laid his hammer down and took off the sheath containing his sword and set it next to the mace.

Lying down as slid his hand to the hilt of his dagger as he did every night and waited for day to come and the battle would begin. He thought about the prospect of learning more about his father. And then he fell into sleep.

Before the sun had rose Daldar was already up and preparing for the coming battle. He put on his sheath, secured his shield and lifted his hammer, and started toward the troll city. As he climbed the last stretch of the mountain Daldar heard two trolls talking.

“Every night, the same thing every night. Why do they botha’ keepin’ us up here? Nothin’ eva’ happens here.” Said one of the trolls.

“It’s ok mon, our shift’s almost ova’.” Said the other.

“Aye, guess you bein’ right.” Concluded the other Troll.

And then Daldar hurled hid dagger at one of the trolls. The dagger plunged into the troll’s vile flesh and the troll fell to the ground as Daldar charged to the rescue of the two magi as well as an unsuspecting dwarf.

3. Re: Journy of a Paladin: A Story in the WoW | 1/23/2004 11:45:17 PM GMTST
Part III
Daldar charged up the dark hill. The second troll still bewildered by the sudden death of his comrade. As Daldar reached the second troll he smashed its face in sending blood flying through the air. Daldar reached down to extract his dagger form the now lifeless troll. He pulled out the now blood stained knife and returned it to its sheath.
http://www.blizzard.com/wow/screenshot.aspx?ImageIndex=1&Set=12

Drawn by the commotion several more trolls were drawn out. In the chaos Daldar was able to take out another two trolls before the remaining ones could reach their weapons. A troll javelin was hurdled toward Daldar. He knocked it from the air with a quick swipe of his shield. Another troll carrying a large axe in either hand. He struck once and Daldar stopped the brow with his mace. He struck again with the other hand and it was deflected by Daldar’s shield. The troll struck a third time, now using both his axes to strike down Daldar. Daldar stopped one with his shield and ducked under the second. The troll had now exposed himself and Daldar took no time in hesitating, striking his foe with the maul. Seeing another three approach him he threw down his hammer at one striking it to the ground. Daldar drew his sword as it was much more suited to close combat as well as its ability to strike multiple foes at a greater rate of speed.

One of the trolls with a spear thrust his weapon at Daldar, but it was of little use to him as Daldar deflected it just as quick running the sword through the troll’s gut. The second charging troll outraged by the death of his comrade lunged at Daldar making the option to deflect the blow not available. However, Daldar did however manage to dodge out of the way just in time and as the troll flew by at full speed Daldar thrust his sword forward giving the troll the same death as his companion.

Battling his way around the courtyard of the troll metropolis slaying troll after troll, Daldar’s opposition would be forced to retreat. Daldar gave no pursuit to the trolls, returning to retrieve his maul. He picked up the maul and sheathed it on his back. Daldar moved toward the inner city of stone leaving the outer wooden rims void on any life. He knew when he entered the city that he would only find more trolls, but he would also find the prisoners he sought to rescue.

Suddenly, a shower of spears and arrows rained down of Daldar. Daldar quickly reacted jumping into a nearby hut. The trolls wouldn’t make reaching the inner city an easy task. Daldar looked for a back exit from the hut but the effort was useless. Daldar decided that if he had no other exit, his only option would be to make one of his own.

Hacking and slashing at the back of the hut with his sword Daldar finally managed to cut his way through the wooded structure. He ran behind each hut, planning to take the trolls from behind as they expected him to be in the hut.

Daldar climbed the face of the inner stonewall. As he came up he saw all the trolls waiting for him to emerge from his nonexistent prison. A troll came from behind them; he was the chief of the clan. He wore an elaborate headdress and carried a spear with barbed spikes protruding from the razor sharp tips. “WHAT ARE YOU DOING YOU FOOLS?” he shouted. “DO YOU KNOW NOTHING? Before throwing your spears this time, burn them, and set your foes ablaze!”

The trolls all did what he said lighting their spears with the fires held by another troll. They hurled their blazing spears at the hut and set it ablaze. Daldar decided that now would be the best time to strike, just as they thought the human would be emerging from the burning hut. With the swiftness of one of the many tigers inhabiting the jungles, Daldar threw himself up onto the stone face. Daldar charged at the trolls cutting them down where they stood. The one with the double-sided spear shouted something incomprehensible to Daldar, “Osh fal mudalerum! Osh fal mudalerum!” The trolls retreated from the wall, running into the inner sanctum of their ancestors.


Daldar was in close pursuit of the trolls, that is until he had found what he had come for. There, in a bamboo cage were the two magi. At first neither saw Daldar but then he began to walk toward them. The woman said, “Help, please help us, we must get out of here or they will kill us.” At the sound of his apprentices voice the man looked up. Daldar lifted his sward and said get back. The woman did and Daldar smashed his sward through the thick bamboo, it took him three tries before the bamboo gave in. he did like wise to the cage the old mage was trapped in, this time only swinging his blade twice before the bamboo was smashed. “Thank you,” said the old man “now we must depart and be away from here as soon as we can.”

As the trio was leaving a forth voice erupted from above. It was a rough voice, the unmistakable voice of a dwarf “And what about me, lad?” The three looked up and saw a dwarf hanging in a cage above them. “Were you just going to leave me here to die?” he said. “No, no,” Daldar said, “I’m sorry, I didn’t know you were there.”

“Oh, It’s fine, lad. Just as long as you get me down from here!”

“How?” Daldar asked.

“There, to your left,” the dwarf said, pointing to a coil of vines on a winch, not far from Daldar’s feet he saw a vine coiled on a wench, just as the dwarf told him. The vine was draped over a tree branch and led to the top of the dwarf’s cage. Daldar walked to the vine and razed his sward above the vine and looked at the dwarf questioningly. “Yes, yes that lad, go ahead, cut it!” the dwarf said. Daldar let the sward fall on the vine cutting it in two. The dwarf tumbled to the ground still caught in his cage. When the cage hit the ground it shattered and a befuddled dwarf lay on the ground. All three ran to his side and then the dwarf stood up exclaiming, “Glad that’s at an end!”

The dwarf stood tall for one of his kind, he had a rough face and a braded beard. He wore a green tunic and he wore spiked braces incrusted with runes. His boots were leather with spiked toes that matched his bracers. His belt held many compartments, including a now empty holster for a pistol.
http://www.blizzard.com/wow/screenshot.aspx?ImageIndex=3&Set=23

“Good,” the elder mage said, “then we must go find our belongings.”

“Any idea where they might keep them?” asked Daldar.

“No,” said the mage.

“I do,” said the dwarf, “it’s this way.” The dwarf started back toward the lower level of the city. The trio now turned quartet headed down a flight of stone steps leading back to where Daldar had come. Once they had reached the bottom they headed forward several paces and turned into an empty hut, this one much larger than the one Daldar had been in only minutes ago. Daldar suspected that this was the troll’s barracks. The four, headed by the dwarf headed to the far end of the room and stopped in front of a large locked chest. “This is it,” said the dwarf, “ our gear is in here.”

“How do we open it?” asked the young mage.

“Paladin? You think I might be able to burrow that hammer of yours?” asked the dwarf.

“NO! You can’t do that!” yelled the female mage. “You’ll crush our staves!”

“Don’t worry lass, I’m just going to crush the lock.”

Daldar handed the Dwarf his maul. The dwarf took it and bashed the lock open, handed the mallet back to Daldar, and opened the chest. He reached in and pulled out a pistol, smiled, spun it in his hand and returned it to its case. He then pulled out a pair of staves and handed them to the two magi, and then finally pulled out an axe over half the size of his body and said, “Let’s go!”

The four ran out of the hut and were fleeing into the forest. As they were about to leave the outskirts and leave the trolls in their dust, their he was. The troll carrying the double-sided spear stood waiting for them.

“Did you fools think you could get away from the trolls this easily?” asked the troll “Gesh`vu will NOT allow you to!”

“Run,” said Daldar, “ I can take him.”

The three ran into the forest, as Daldar stayed behind to fight the troll. The troll rushed Daldar but was unable to strike him; instead Daldar had managed to cut the troll’s spear in half.
Now armed with the two halves of his spear he used them lake a pair of swords, swinging one after the other at Daldar. The troll knocked the sword from Daldar’s hand. Daldar was reduced to fending the troll’s relentless onslaught with only his shield. Daldar struggled to reach the sheathed hammer on his back finding that every time he reached for it, the troll struck at the wandering hand.

And then it happened. The troll’s spear pierced Daldar’s gut. Daldar fell to the ground, mortally wounded. The troll knelt over him and said, “Gesh`vu told you that he wouldn’t allow you to flee so easily, and now look at you. You are at Gesh`vu’s mercy, and Gesh`vu will give you NONE!”

The troll pulled out an intricately forged dagger. Its glow was beautiful in the morning sun. A disoriented Daldar could only gaze at the dagger and then he faded into the light as it struck his heart. He was dead.



The three ran into the forest leaving, Daldar to deal with the troll on his own. As they fled the sounds of battle grew less and less until they were no more. The elder mage stopped and said “We will hold here until our ‘friend’ arrives.” The other two stopped. The dwarf was the first to speak. “So, what are your names?” he asked. “I am Vigour,” said the elder mage.

“And I am Amplara,” said the younger. “And what would your name be?” she asked.

“My name is Brann.”

“The brother of the King of Khaz Modan?” she asked.

“Do you know of any other?” Brann asked.

“But I thought the trolls captured you and killed you!”

“Well, about half of that would be right, lass.”

“Amazing,” Amplara said.

Vigour gasped. “We need to go back, now!” he said, “something’s happened to the paladin, but to save him we must make haste!”
