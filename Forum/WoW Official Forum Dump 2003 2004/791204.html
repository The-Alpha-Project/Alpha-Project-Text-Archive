1. Dumb Players = Warrior Class? | 11/27/2003 6:31:04 PM GMTST
Do people who are dumb players or have a poor understanding of class strategies tend to play warrior classes?

A comment on the board reflected this view recently with the remark:

Q u o t e:
I'm guessing that's why you play "tank" classes (stay in the front and soak damage, no thinking required)


Certainly there are some folks who believe this about the warrior class. Do you feel this way?

Debating this point will assuredly raise the following underlying issue:

What is the role of a warrior in a group? Damage absorber or damage dealer?

While I clearly believe based on EQ and Shadowbane the warrior is a damage absorber - not everyone believes this is their role in a group. Certainly the warrior controls aggro - or tries to - but in many games the warrior is weak at drawing aggro compared to other classes so this task requires group coordination with the fighter.

Whatever you believe the role of warrior to be based on previous experience, what role to do you see for the warrior in WoW?

I believe the role for the warrior in WoW is as damage absorber. However, WoW may present options for warriors to develop as better damage dealers than damage absorbers. If one developed their warrior this way based on gear, attribute and skill point allocation - he could be a "damage dealer warrior" but certainly no "tank", and would pale in comparison to other damage dealers in WoW (Wizard, Rogue).

If 'dumb players' have adopted the warrior class in other games, will this be any different in WoW?

This assumes of course that you believe "dumb players" have gravitated to the warrior in other games. Based on EQ, I think this may be true at very low levels (1-20) but beyond that I think quite the opposite is true. In WoW then weaker players may gravitate to the warrior at low levels, but as the game advances with warriors reaching higher levels - I don't think this would be true.

There may be a very basic point behind this whole discussion:

Which do you believe to be more challenging to play: a class with few strategic options or a class with many strategic options?

I tend to think if you play a class with many strategic options - while fun as hell - you do not develop the same fine tuned appreciation for the dynamics of the game. For example in EQ, I often found shadowknights with great stratetic options to be poorly utilized on a situation by situation basis - they tended to tackle most problems the same way whether they were assigned as damage dealers or tanks in a group. The same can be said of playing a 'cleric' vs. a class with many strategic options one of which includes healing. I am toying with this - I am not certain.

This is all speculation and yes most of us realize the game is not out yet ;P
