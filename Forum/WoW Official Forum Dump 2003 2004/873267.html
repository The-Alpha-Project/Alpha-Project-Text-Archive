EDIT: What cavalier1969 said but more patronizing ;P

Listen, I can't even begin to explain to you guys how much more there is to The Philosophy of Gender (in MMORPGs) than what comes through in this thread. My intention is not to be patronizing but instead to spread some more information on what a wide subject Gender actually is.

First, pop a google search for my nick ("gender studies"). See what kind of literature you come up with. See if you can find a literature list from the gender studies courses available at your local college.

Second, go to your college library and borrow what seems to be the central pieces for this subject/course. Heavier "classics" would be Simone de Beauvoir's "The Second Sex". Relating to this I would also like to recommend Toril Moi's analytical biography of de Beauvoir called "The Making of an Intellectual Woman". A great historical feminist piece is Thomas Laqeur's "Makin Sex", Stuart Hall's "Representation -- Cultural Representations and Signifying Practices" is also a useful read for those interested in the media (which includes computer games). Moreover, recent must reads include Judith Butler's "Gender Trouble" (that's as heavy as de Beauvoir) while beginning readers might look for just any introductory book in gender studies. Valerie Bryson's "Feminist Political Theory" is a nice history of the genre. Oh, must NOT forget Connel's "Masculinities". Especially the chapter on "body reflexive practices" (my translation -- I only have the Swedish version at my house) does draw an interesting paralell to the original poster trying to find some safe & sound standards for being a "normal" heterosexual and not some "sick" transexual. Really, calling anyone "sick" because of sexual standards is rather medieval.

The titles mentioned are just some off the top of my head and might not be apt for introductory purposes.

The philosophy of gender runs a whole lot deeper than neurotically being afraid of wearing a dress because "whoa dude, it might do things to your head -- I'm no homo". To me, the philosophy of gender (and feminism) is about questioning the biological standards of sex that modernist society uses to justify gender roles and, in turn, oppression.
