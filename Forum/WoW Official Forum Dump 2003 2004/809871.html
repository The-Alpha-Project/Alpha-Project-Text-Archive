While a lot of you are stipulating at the cost of running a server, I think a lot of you are overexagerating. Depending on how Blizzard design the game, it will *not* require computers that rival NASA. A fair few MMORPG developers are accutely keen on their bandwidth costs, and so make as much of the game client side as possible and minimise traffic between PC and server.

On average someone playing a MMORPG will only transfer 20mb an hour. Its a huge amount if you add up 500,000 players or whatever you estimate... but firstly not all of them are going to be online at the same time, and secondly blizzard is going to use multiple server realms. They should not need big gigantic servers to run WoW when they are dividing it up like that.

A monthly fee WILL be required because over time whatever profit they get from selling the game retail would eventually be dwindled down to nothing over the years without one. My personal upset is not really with the monthly fee -though I hate them- but the fact Blizzard will also charge a retail price. On a monthly fee alone, the game would still make a profit and server costs will be covered. The retail is being charged because Blizzard knows they can, and will make a fortune out of it.

From a gamers perspective: the game costs so much. There are plenty of brand new decent games that are multiplayer and LAN capable, with also single player elements. Then when looking at the MMORPG scene, there are several decent games which are downloadable which you pay a monthly fee for... there are cheaper alternatives, and it is not obvious yet that WoW will be heads and tails better than them.

Say what you will, but WoW has yet to prove itself as a good decent game. We havn't had a go at the closed beta, we have just seen screenshots and heard of some of the features.The fee will alienate some *not* because they can't afford it, but because they think their money is better spent elsewhere.
