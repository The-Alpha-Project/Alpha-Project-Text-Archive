1. Total Warlock Coverage! With ALL Spell Stats | 4/3/2004 9:19:11 AM GMTDT
Hey there again!

Last week, we recieved lots of positive response on our Detailed Listing of the Mage Character Class, including all spell Stats ( http://www.infoceptor.com/wow/classes/mage.shtml ), so as promised, we bring you the Warlocks, except this time, it's even better!

The guide includes stats of all of the Warlock spells released in the beta, as well as some spells that are not yet released! We've included Cost, Damage, Cooldown... you name it, even a Damage/Mana column so you can see which spells are most efficient. There's also screenshots of the spells in action included

There's also on write-up on Trade Skills, Warlock Pets, and the General Warlock Playstyle. I think you will find it very much worthwhile!

http://www.infoceptor.com/wow/classes/warlock.shtml

Here's an excerpt:

Q u o t e:

Pets:
The Warlock would be a weak character class if not for their ability to keep a permanent 'pet'. Unlike conventional spells, a player cannot purchase Pet Summoning Spells from Warlock Trainers. Instead, whenever the Warlock reaches a certain level, his local Warlock Guild will give him a mission, which upon completion, gives him the ability to summon a new pet.

For example, a Human Warlock would be given a mission to retrieve a stolen spellbook from Defias Thugs. Which leads finally to the ability to summon an Imp

Each type of summoned creature is of the same level as that of the caster, and hence improves in combat ability as the Warlock improves. You can bring you pets to special trainers who can train them with new abilities. For instance, the Imp can learn higher levels of the Firebolt Spell. Pets can be given basic commands such as attack, as well as specialised commands to use their spells.
