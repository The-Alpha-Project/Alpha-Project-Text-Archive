312. Re: Just Curious | 1/31/2004 5:55:14 PM GMTST
People don’t play RPGs for what they (their characters) are, but what they could be. That is the primary reason they pay for the experience - it is hope. This does not exclude the path or process taken to achieve those goals, in fact, the challenge of the journey (or metamorphosis from starting character into player envisioned character) is where the enjoyment comes from. Unfortunately, games have turned the journey into the level grind or treadmill. New MMORPGs should make the game fun and exciting in spite of the level treadmill. Do that, and you’ll have solved the number one problem with all current MMORPGs.

Game makers should see the character like a painting, the player like the artist, and the game itself like the medium. The artist has a mental image of his/her painting before it is completed - so has the player a picture of the character. They are limited by the medium, as they are also guided by it. As a maker of an MMORPG, your duty should be to make that medium as least restrictive as possible in order for each player to craft their own unique character, but not such a free-for-all that they are given no direction and are doomed to create ‘bad art‘. It’s complicated at best, impossible at worst, but I believe that it can be done with more success than past attempts have shown.

Keeping things as brief as possible;

EVERQUEST
Like:

1. The vast variety of the environments and creatures in the game. Regardless of the theme
you enjoyed chances were good it was there. Exploration of new areas was fun when I played
(initial and Kunark expansion).

2. The stupidly incredible amount of items in game, and figuring out what each last one was
for, and they all pretty much had some use, be it crafting, quest, or combat.

3. The roles each character class had in the Eq world in the beginning were very well
defined.

Dislike:

1. The game was designed to demand more and more of your time as you progressed in levels
and was designed to be addictive. In the end you had to give more of your real life time to
Everquest to keep playing, or quit it. (I quit, but as we all know, too many tragic stories
abound the Net)

2. Everything in the game was more or less designed to be a time sink. Literally
everything, and it was obvious and utterly not fun.

3. The storylines in Eq quickly degenerated after the Kunark expansion. The game lost a
driving storyline, and without it, became simply "the grind".

4. The graphics quality is poor, and the artistic merit of the graphics is questionable.
The same goes for EQ2, although I see that the graphics are high technology, they still look
bad to me because they are bad artistically.

5. Bad music/sound effects

ASHERONS CALL 2
Like:

1. The game world was beautiful at full settings and again fun to explore.

2. I liked being able to pick the skills I learned and improved at level ups.

3. Ongoing storyline that was well integrated with the quests and overall game.

Dislike:

1. There were not enough players in the world, it was at first interesting, because running
into someone was an event, but later became tiresome as grouping became necessary.

2. Not enough variety in the environments and character races/classes.

3. Crafting system interesting but ultimately poorly balanced.

4. Big empty world.

DARK AGE OF CAMELOT
Like:

1. The classes and races were interesting and the quest system an improvement on that of Eq at the time.

2. Crafting was well done in respect to weapons and armor.

3. The ongoing warfare was between the three sides was great.

Dislike:

1. Overall, the game had a very medicore and average feel to everything.

2. While the crafting was well done, it did not have enough variety.

3. The three sides were never well balanced so warfare between the three was not always
fun.

SHADOWBANE
Like:

1. I loved the idea that you could build property and cities in game.

2. Character creation was more involved than most of the other games of it's type.

Dislike:

1. Poor graphics and poorly designed interface.

2. Lack of content.

3. Terrible amount of bugs and gameplay issues.

4. Game totally unbalanced.

FINAL FANTASY XI
Like:

1. The world is beautiful and the "feeling" of a Final Fantasy world comes across clearly.

2. The world is full of stories and the quests are really well done, especially instancing
boss fights.

3. The class system allowing you to combine 2 classes at once, and have your character try
all classes without restarting the character was fantastic. Furthermore, at least some of
the classes are atypical of the MMORPG mold.

4. The auction house works extremely well, it is automated, efficient, especially being
able to see previous sell prices for your items.

5. The "PlayOnline" mini-browser/e-mail client. I personally liked this little PDA type
program for organizing friends and such out of game. Could definitely use a chat function,
especially guild chat.

Dislike:

1. The extremely party-heavy game balance.

2. The interface, while learnable, is still terrible.

3. I could feel the grind coming with every level, and therefore it was time to quit. In
short, the time sink element of the game's experience point and skill systems intruded
exponentially in my fun as I progressed into the game.
