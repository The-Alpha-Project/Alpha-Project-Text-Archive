68. Re: The perfect WoW PvP-wise (long) | 2/2/2004 11:30:37 PM GMTST
@ elstudo:

To #3 - the point is the geography of WoW. It is *not possible* to block off a area byblocking an adjacent area, there is always more than a single way to a place. So, if one area is blockaded you can still go around.
In addition to this a enemy force would need to block a area INSIDE of a enemy fractions land to force players to make a detour. By doing this they are vulnerable to a mass assult from all sides.
Blocking a area in their land is not really different to the usual situation there - there will be people hostile to you there either way.

Hunt for raw materials - then he has to improvice. If he has the skill he could sneak into that area, trying to avoid attention (basically a covert ops), if he hasn't he could pay someone from his fraction to do it. Alternativly, providing he can speak their language, he could try to find someone from the enemy fraction who is getting the rew metrial for him - for a price.

Bashing lower levels: Again, we have not really a clue how exactly the power distribution per level is in WOW. Knowledge how it's in EQ does not really help there. For example, any camping experience from EQ won't be of any help in WoW, since camping won't be present there due to the instances.
The reputation thingy someone mentioned earlier will also help there. If someone is known regulary bashing lower levels he won't be very popular. Neither in his fraction or the other. Actually this gives me an idea:


What about some kind of reputation score? Let's assume every player starts with a reputation score of 100, if his is killing someone with more than 5 levels under his current level he will loose this amount of levels from his reputation score. Similar, if he kills someone more than 5 levels over his current one he will get a bonus of his reputation score by the surplus levels.

The reputation score should have a minimum of 0 and a maximum of 300. It would be visible openly by everyone, be it friend or enemy. If someone is a good PVP his repuation would reflect it, same if someone is a lowlife only hitting on weaker people.

A slight problem with it would be once someone has reached max lvl. He won't be able to get any further bonus points to his rep score.
