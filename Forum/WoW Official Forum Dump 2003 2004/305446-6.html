Well, it looks like most people understood the point of my post, but a few people still don't get it. Let me try to spell it out again.

Yes, I understand that some game developers like to spend all their spare time on their forums talking to the fan community. It is not something we spend a lot of time doing. Our developers spend most of their day working, and talking about the game. I'm sure we could argue philosophy all day whether you need to build a great community for your game, or whether you build a great game for people to build a community around, but we would both walk away disagreeing.

Team 2 is already working many long hours so that we can get the beta ready. Any time spent away from work means the beta gets delayed by a little bit. Blizzard is not some huge company, we don't hire people to take screenshots or even to moderate the forums. Anyone that posts on these forums (myself, Geoff, Mike, etc) are doing this of their own free will, no one asked us to do this. Our primary reason for having these forums is to give our fans somewhere "official" to discuss our games, and somewhere where our developers can go and see what the community finds important and what they like and dislike about our games. It is not a big communication tool between us and the fans. I will continue to try to stop by occasionally and make a post here and there, but brand new information is not going to get broken on the forums, I'm sorry.
