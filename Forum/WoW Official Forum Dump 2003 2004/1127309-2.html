27. Re: The perfect WoW PvP-wise (long) | 2/2/2004 9:27:47 PM GMTST
I think the proportions of people who want FvF and the people who want the proposed carebear server is not what most people think it is. I actually think at least 50% of people want FvF and not a "battleground" PvP system.

I'd like to attribute DAoC and SWG to helping people understand the joy of PvP while minimizing the negative aspects of it. DAoC really allowed people who disliked PvP to avoid it altogether. But there was the underlying theme and story that revolved around it. I often found people who originally only played DAoC for the PvE ended up trying out the factional PvP and loving it. They loved the teamwork aspect of it, the danger aspect of it, the thrill. They didn't have to listen to the enemy smack talk them. They knew when they entered the frontier to expect someone trying to kill them.

Then there was SWG. SWG may have had an unbalanced and poor PvP system, but still more than 50% of the players participated in it. Why did they participate if they don't enjoy PvP? Because it fit the storyline. If you wanted to roleplay your character then in most cases that meant you joined the Rebellion or the Empire. But SWG also let them put their feet in and test the waters before jumping in. But just like DAoC I found people who weren't into PvP normally who enjoyed fighting as part of a team and as part of the main storyline of the game.

So I think the number of people who see the merits of a factional PvP system has vastly increased from before these two games came out to now. I think some people will never take a shine to it and they must have their way. But I honestly think you could split the servers 50/50 and have half of them FvF servers and the other half carebear servers with "battlegrounds" and all servers would fill up.

I don't think those of us that want FvF are in the minority anymore.
