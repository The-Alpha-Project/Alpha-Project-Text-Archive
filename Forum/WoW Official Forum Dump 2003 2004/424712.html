68. Re: RACIAL PVP | 8/18/2003 4:55:43 PM GMTDT
The horde/alliance theme is definately going to have a heavy role in the storyline of the game. How that will actually transfer over to game mechanics remains to be seen.

Ultimately, enjoyment is the most important factor in a game. If being unable to group with members of the opposing faction turns out to be a gigantic irritation which detracts from the play experience more than it enhances it, then I expect Blizzard will remove that restriction.

However, at this time, I expect Alliance and Horde to be unable to group, I expect there to be quests on each side which support or enhance this underlying theme, and I expect a few PvP areas to contain factional PvP. In addition, I expect GM events to center heavily around this opposition.

I just also expect there to be a huge part (the majority, in fact) of the game which does not fall under the umbrella of this theme. The majority of the game will be day to day adventuring, questing, dungeon crawling, etc. The war will always be in the back of the mind, and be an underlying current in what you do, but it's not going to be the primary focus of the game, as far as I would guess. Adventuring in a giant world, and becoming a hero yourself, will be the focus... with the war with the Horde being merely one of many settings in which you can do that.
