I've been hooked on online gaming since I discovered Subspace in '94. I then moved on EQ, and have briefly played other titles thought the years; such as UO, AO, and DAOC. Through out my gaming experiences there is one factor that has remained constant amongst my favored games: working as a team to achieve a goal. In Subspace, the goal was capturing flags – roughly two 10 man teams duking it out. In EQ it was 60-72 man raids working together to take down NPCs. Both were enjoyable and fulfilling experiences in their own right.

Unfortunately my most unloved moments in gaming are related to large scale team oriented events as well. Namely, the lack thereof. In EQ, time zone played a huge factor in which raid mobs one got to encounter on a regular basis. The early bird gets the worm. In EQ, the early bird always gets the worm. A guild located on the west coast nearly always gets the last pick for their nightly encounters, if any are left to choose from.

Things to like:

THE PEOPLE WHO PLAY WITH YOU. Allowing morons, griefers, bad manners, etc. is bad. No tolerance please so people can enjoy the game.

The sense of accomplishments. It's nice to be super powerful solo and all but MMO games should put the challenge in team accomplishments, too.

Things not to like:

Large time and manpower commitments for accomplishments. I don't mine several hours with 20-30 people to do something but 9 hours and 80 people to get something done is excessive.

Unnecessary and over the top graphics/processing. Looking nice does not mean being the most insanely taxing game on processors and memory.

Excessive penalties on failures. Risks vs Effort is a tough balance, but you can not have things too easy for rewards and too harsh for punishments. There should be risks with adventure and dying, but not so excessive that it is nothing but frustration.

So far I've never played any graphical mmo's. However, I have played a number of MUD's and other text based games which are essentially the same type of thing without graphics. Anybody else who has played these know's how addictive they can be despite the lack of pretty pictures.

Speaking from experience playing these I would have to say my biggest problems were not having a choice of being pk or non pk, running out of things to do, not being able to solo and unretrievable corpses.

With pk or PVP it's fun to do if you want to, bu if you are just looking to explore the world and have a good time then constantly worrying about someone tracking you down and killing you takes away from that. Fair pvp I have no problems with. It's when people multiple levels higher then you repeatedl kill you that it becomes discouraging.

Running out of things to do is usually a problem for any game. Waht happens when you finish the last quest or gain your last level? There has to be something left to do, or the gme quickly loses my interest. That being said, gaining levels is quite fun if tied to the storyline. Something like diablo 2 has remained popular because of the IMMENSE variety of items and difficulty of getting to very high levels. Unfortunately the quests are so mundane most people consider them obstacles. I wouldnt mind it taking 3-4 times longer to level in d2 if the quests were longer and more involving.

Being able to solo is also very important. If you can't run out and have some fun by yourself then games go downhill. Yes it's fun to party with other people, but everyone needs some solitude and solo hunting once in awhile. If it can't be done then the game becomes frustrating. This ties in with unretrievable corpses. You run out and accidently get smacked by a high level mob, you respawn but cant retrieve your body as if you try you just get cut down over and over. Not exactly exciting and prevents you from continuing on.

What I hate about MMO's
-Lack of unique dungeons with interactive sections for switches, doors, mutations (Light and Shadow versions), puzzles.
-Lack of Unique "Boss" monsters in their respective "Rooms".
-Lack of Quest completions on the first kill for random item drop. (I.E. Get "Orc Scales" from a certain untold creature at a certain time from a game called FF XI)
-Lack of Character development
-Lack of Player vs Player Interaction
-Lack of Multiple Economies for Multiple Cultures (1 Universal currency is lame) with changing exchange rates for investors
-Lack of varying monsters, (I.e. Sheep, Wild Sheep, Crazy Sheep, Smelly Sheep) with no different models or colors to signify differences
-Lack of any real point (Single Player RPG's grant storylines and fun factors in their methods)
-Lack of Leveling (unless its D&D, where each lvl = Massive changes usually)
-Slow Leveling is a pain
-One on Six Players, one on one opponents get boring. Need more Monster "Groupings" where the monsters work as a Party.
-Lack of Diverse Inventory
-Lack of Unique Classes

Whats good in MMO's
-Capability to involve players in branching storylines and getting people to re-act large interacting twining "Mega" quests if they get it right.
-Lots of Players grants lots of potential opponents and allies
-Big world
