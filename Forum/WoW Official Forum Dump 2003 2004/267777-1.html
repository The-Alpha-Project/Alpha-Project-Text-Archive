1. IDEA FOR RACE | 6/27/2003 9:16:42 AM GMTDT
Another race could be a nerubian. REMEMBER that not all nerubians were killed by the lich king and some still exist in Northrend...well then again...that IS what the storyline in the instruction book said...BUT, I don't think these guys should be forgotten just yet.
I just figured Blizzard has currently made a member of each race as a playable character that resembles a unit (i.e. Night Elves- archers, huntresses, etc.--- Humans and Dwarves- footmen, riflemen, etc.--- Orcs and Tauren- raiders, spirit walkers, etc.). I noticed that the only race not contributing here are the undead, but since the undead are just walking corpses, the only unit there that hints at a connection to an original RACE are the crypt fiends. SO I SAY a Nerubian should be a playable race, because after all, they're not exactly extinct yet.
BESIDES!! BLIZZARD claims that they are a company enforcing diversity and maximum enjoyment for players right?? SO why not be the first company to make an MMORPG that features a playable race that is NOT BIPEDAL?!?!? I mean THAT IS WHAT MAKES practically every MMORPG seemingly repetitious. ALL OF THE RACES THEY PROVIDE are HUMANOID in shape, AND WALK ON TWO LEGS!!! All in exception to this one game that had centaurs as a playable race, but I doubt people have heard of it. So what you end up is with characters that attack the same way, walk the same way, and practically do EVERYTHING the same way, because their character designs are the same, just with a few minor alterations to the face and skin, and a little nose putty hear and there!!
BLIZZARD has already CHANGED all that because they have dwarves and tauren who look so darn WEIRD that they have to seem different.
What I mean is, make the game SO unique and totally different, that when a player selects a race, not only will they LOOK different, but they'll feel different because that's how diverse they are. I feel that adding a Nerubian as a playable race will only ADD to that diversity, because they, in my opinion, have the most bizarre look of any other race. Think...REALLY REALLY BIG arachnids, WON'T THAT be cool?
Then again, I feel like there will be this enormous wave of negative feedback I'll recieve from people. BUT I think crypt fiends are COOL. They are great units in FT and are my favorite to use. It would be GREAT if they didn't just disappear forever, in the first WC game they appeared in. IT's like Destroyah from the godzilla series, he was blown to pieces in Godzilla vs. Destroyer, and was never brought back again by the creators to appear in a second movie....even though he had TOTALLY kick butt design and character concept.
I feel the same way about Nerubians, they've got a totally AWESOME design and story behind their race, so...just think if they were actually playable?? You've got these four legged arachnids walking around. You start the game in a cold northern region, with a special race ability to bind flying enemies up in webs. They could have something like character specific weapons like CLAWS or hand held scythes. If you select their hair color, being spiders, their entire bodily color would change. WE all know how colorful spiders really are. And since I doubt anyone can design clothing for them, you could change the color patterns in the hairs so you've got spots,and patterns that can be JUST AS unique as changing clothes. Eevry other race can change clothes, so you got Nerubians who could change those hood things they wear on their heads, and their bodily hair patterns. WOAH!! I can really ramble...sorry, I can get really expressive about these things. Feel free to bash my statement with negative comments if you want, BUT I still think BLIZZARD should think twice before adding ANOTHER bipedal race to the game. If they want to strive for the UNIQUE and DIVERSE they gotta think about turning their characters upside-down, and doing some REAL internal change from the orthodox, LOOKIE!! I LOOK LIKE A HUMAN BEING WITH A LONG SNOUT AND SCALES!!! Or.....LOOK AT ME!! I LOOK EXACTLY LIKE A HUMAN EXCEPT I GOT LONGER EARS!!!!

Pit Lord:
Story: The fall of Archimonde and the Burning Legion four years ago had left a massive rift in the galactic and dimensional distortions of the world of Azeroth. With the gates and paths of dementia thrown into chaos, the ancient demonic gates that were used by the Legion had been shut down, cutting off all means of return to Kil' Jaeden or the almighty Sargeras. The small bands of the Burning Legion that have remained trapped have been systematically terminated by the defenders of Kalimdor, the Night Elves. Now thrown into confusion and terror, the Pit Lords, the once mighty commanders of the Burning Legion, cower like rats to the very corners of the planet, hoping to avoid the hunters who pursue them. Now, many have taken refuge on the now abandoned capital of the spider kingdom, hoping to buy enough time for the opportunity to find a way out of Azeroth. Now that foyr years have passed, the Pit Lords have gathered enough strength to leave their stronghold in Northrend find clues to their escape. But they are cautious, for they are horrors, and abominations in this world. Great care must be taken, or all shall be lost.
The Pit Lords are part of the Burning LEgion so they are treated as such. Pit Lords possess powers and might that are unmatched by any other race, but they come with a great handicap. They must constantly avoid night elves, orcs, and humans wherever they go, or they will be turned on and killed. Most vendors will close their shops ro Pit Lords, and great care must be taken not to enter heavily populated towns. Pit Lords must get their items and supplies from other races such as the mercantile Naga, from monsters, or even other players. Possessing great power, such an option as attacking other players is a welcome strategy. Remember, Pit Lords are somewhat like fugitives. Be very careful.
Race Specific Class:
Destroyer- (base stat-strength)
As the name implies, this class focuses on both physical and magical capapbilities that focus on nothing more than causing an immense amount of pain upon the Pit Lord's foes. In fact, his attacks are so powerful, they have the ability to harm the evironment around them, leaving a wake of destruction that is truly characteristic of the Burning Legion. Their abilities also supplement the idea of PKing, since their only means of acquiring supplies are from other players, monsters, and random merchants. The catch is, their magical abilities have a fiery motif, but are actually composed of forces from the void, making it possess elements that are not from the world of Azeroth. These massive Area of Effect Spells, are so powerful they make damage even magic immune enemies. But, there is a great drawback to this. The Pit Lord's existence in the world of Azeroth is very taxing to its own soul. Since the nexus of the Burning Legion has been lost, the Pit Lords must use their own soul as that nexus, greatly taxing their lifeforce. Whenever the Pit Lord is engaged in combat or another vigorous activity, it quickly loses vitality, so one must take great care not to overuse the Pit Lord's weakening influence.
