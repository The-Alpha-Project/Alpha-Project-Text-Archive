47. Re: Just Curious | 1/30/2004 7:30:14 AM GMTST
Dark Age of Camelot:

The beginning bored me to tears. A game should hook you right off the bat. DAoC did not do that for me, so I left it.

Shadowbane:

I dislike running for miles and miles without any idea why I'm running for miles and miles (same with DAoC of course). I don't mind running for miles and miles if I can have a legitimate chance at discovery...discovery that I can safely receive at whatever level.

If I'm level 2 and I've gotten to a certain place without any danger to myself along the way, I should be meant to be there...and not killed the second I meet a monster, which might not happen for a while. Good game design would prevent such things from happening. I'm a fan of level zones; they give you a concrete goal, but they can easily backfire if you don't have the content necessary for keeping the interest of people in whatever zone they happen to be "stuck" in. Zones should be natural barriers (like mountains), otherwise...they suck.

Neither game emphasized quests. I didn't like that. Quests entertain me, but challenging quests entertain me more. I've gotten the impression Blizzard is kind of making the majority of their quests ones that don't require a great deal (if any) thought to complete. Give me quests I can only complete using special emotes in certain zone (be the zone a tree or a house or a forest--I don't mean "zone" in the way some of you might think), and the only reason I know that special emote is because I am a clever fellow.

I never want to run out of goals, and I want to feel like I'm being fairly treated in my pursuit of those goals.

I want branches; I want trees full of branches! (Ahem.)

Frankly, I've gotten all of my enjoyment from MUDs. If I enter a new area in WoW and I get a nice text story to read, if I want to, that'll be nice.

Give me some complexity if I make the effort to find the complexity (like the emote quests I mentioned).

There's just so much...

Likes:

-Everyone got the reward for killing a quest monster unlike EQ.
-World was highly detailed.

-Movement SUCKED all the characters were slow as hell unless you had a minstrel/bard/skald.
-All the armor looked the same except for coloring which was player customed through dyes.
