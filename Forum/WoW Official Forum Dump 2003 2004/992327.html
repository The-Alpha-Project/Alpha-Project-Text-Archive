1. Economy and Housing Blizz plz read | 1/17/2004 12:21:28 AM GMTST
This is being posted for Blizzard Designers mostly.

I have recently read Gamespies Article and although this game is on my every waking thought, I truly believe you need some more insight to these two areas.

Lets start with my experience and some background of my experience with MMO's.
I began with Ultima Online: The second age
(For those of you who don't know much about UO it was before they did the Felucca / Trammel split w/ Pking and camping running a serious muck.)
Then UO: Renaissance and then Lord Black Thorn's Revenge (with a brief break).
I then tried Asheron's Call and played through to AC: Dark Majesty.
And Finally Everquest (I jumped in there about 6 months after the release of The Shadows of Luclin)
I also Beta tested Horizons.

I after I caught wind of WoW to be released I stopped playing MMO's because of 2 reasons: Economy and Player Housing. In my own honest opinion these 2 subjects will kill a great MMO.
I have seen and heard the same arguments across all those games.

Lets begin with Economy...

What keeps the players from spending cash? Why? How can we change this?

WHAT KEEPS THE PLAYERS FROM SPENDING CASH?
Player ran vendors have been core issue across these games. There is generally too much freedom left up to the players here. I.E. I have a mid to high-level character (can venture safely from the newbie and have a few months experience playing).
I know that there are a few locations where a (i.e.) particular reagent spawns for a highly used spell by most spellcaster races. This semi rare spawn is generally camped. I spent the better part of a Saturday evening collecting 100 of this regeant. Lets say this item has a base cost of 50 gold bought from an npc vendors rare item list. If I sell it back to a vendor I probably would get 75% of its cost. Ok I start up a player vendor at my house to sell this item to people.
Ok, here is the problem.
I turn around and feel that since I spent say 10 hours of game time collecting this item I should take this into consideration and sell this item at 2,000 gold each!!!
Most people can't afford it so they tend to hoard their cash to pay these prices because they know the spots to get them are camped and would rather have fun playing than trying to acquire it. Which in turn forces them to overprice items they find from hunts to buy these other items. This has a tremendous snowball effect. Which I am totally for sure you will experience when this game goes live.
Suggestion to overcome:
Give all items in the game a base price i.e. a database of item values, script the player vendors to not exceed more than 25% of its base price. Lets give an example to those who are not following to closely. I.E. A Large Bat wing is a magical component for a mid to high-level group buff spell. On a rare item npc vendor list it sells for 50 gold pieces. The large bat can be found in a particular area with a moderate spawn time, but usually has alot of traffic for its wing. I a mid to high level player spend the next 10 hours camping / hunting this creature for it's wing to sell it on my vendor. I collect 100 wings.
On my vendor menu when placing this item I set a price of 300 gold per wing (because I spent all day hunting it and had to deal with the crowds of kill stealers and campers. I WANT MY FAIR SHARE is usually the gripe) after hitting enter I noticed that he did not try to sell it for 300 but for only 63 gold!!! OMG now the wing is set at a fair price and even though I am mad I cannot get 300 I still make a profit at which anyone can still buy. The formula looks like this (50 gp base price + 12.5 gp (25%) rounded up = 63 gold
What did this really do?
1.) It is deterring camping because I don’t think I will sit and hunt them for 10 hours anymore.
2.) It still promotes player spending and competition with player pricing because it still goes above its base value.

HOUSING!!!!!
This one has had me slamming my keyboard for years.
How many times you bought the deed to a house and gone exploring to find that there is no more land that will fit your house? Only to see on eBay that the 63,000 gold piece deed you have is being sold by someone with it placed for 1 million or more in gold?
There is only two ways to quell the rapid selling of land space in an MMO.
1.) Design enough land mass that every cd key per game can buy and place ONE house.
Or
2.) Use the same Database /pricing and script it into the house menu. So if a house is for sale when I pass by I open the house menu select buy it withdraws the money from my bank and deposits it into the owner’s bank.
Lets be real for just a min.
This is a fantasy game and last time I checked there wasn’t a real estate broker back then.
Or
3.) SCRAP PLAYER HOUSING ALL TOGETHER

Thank you for your time and I hope at least a few Blizzard peeps read this.
Keep up the great work guys and gals!!

One last thing if you want to really sell this like no other mmo out there, $10.00 per month is the key.
