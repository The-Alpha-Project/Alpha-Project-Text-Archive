1. A way to prevent Ebaying in a p2p MMORPG | 1/24/2004 7:41:07 PM GMTST
I've read a lot about how a few people selling there l33t items for $$ turns into an entire company having multiple people farm mobs all day. Then suddenly massive amounts of gold and all the elite items from that game are being sold for money. A lot of people and most of the game companies want a way to prevent this.

I think I’ve come up with a solution to the problem but before I go make my millions I wanted to hear what you guys have to say :)

My Idea:

If the following system was implemented people would be unable to get $$ via mmorpgs.

All items will have a value associated to them by the game.

1 value point will be equivalent to one piece of gold.

Gold will be limited throughout the game; every monster will not drop gold.

A trade cannot be made unless the value of the items being traded equals the value of the items being received.

Items cannot be dropped by players – they can be deleted by them.

The people who maintain the game will not ignore the economy, they will actively monitor it to spot and prevent cheats/hacks/glitches etc.

--
Now people still might be able to cash out via accounts, the only solution I can think of for this would be to check who owns the credit card and make sure that they are the person who owns the account and then if that person ever changes they could disable the account. This last bit would only work if the game required a CC#.

It would be a lot of work to stop people from selling/trading accounts and to stop people from power leveling characters for $.

I don't see the difference between Joe owning account X or Mike owning account X or Mike leveling character A then Joe owning character A.

It wouldn’t impact the economy like item trading does -- if anything it would cause more people to play the game, hopefully something the game maker would want.

What companies could do though, is actively monitor the net (wouldn’t take more than one guy.. if Joe EQ player can find out about PLing service X then Mike who works at EQ sure can) and the game for people selling/trading accounts and PLing characters and then shut down those sites/people. (Shutdown the person meaning ban them or otherwise prevent them from breaking the EULA again)

This is on the WoW boards because..
Wow is an mmorpg and everyone knows what happened to Diablo II (massive ebaying -- the ability to turn items into cash turned the wrong people onto the game and it became a hack fest) and no info has been relased as to if blizzard will condon selling items/accounts/etc or if they will allow it -- so far no company has really been able to prevent it and most have just caved in like Mythic.


So what do you think about this system actually working?

yes it will prevent people from giving stuff for free but it will also keep things fair imo.
