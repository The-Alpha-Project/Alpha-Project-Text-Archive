 41. Re: Can anybody tell me Why SWG sucks | 7/29/2004 10:27:48 PM GMTDT
EQ: I played EQ for about a night. I spent the entire time in the newby zone. I did one quest, and it took 2 hours, and I didn't even complete it because the NPC I was supposed to talk to had been killed by a MOB. I didn't get a single new skill, and I reached level 6. I didn't get any better equipment, I didn't meet anyone, and there was no one there to help me, players or GMs alike. I hate EQ with a fiery passion that has lead to by extremem biased against EQ2.

SWG: I got the trial version of SWG. It was thoroughly entertaining for about a week. The newb quests weren't fun, but they were something to do, and it was cool being in the SW universe. Some random player gave me a speeder bike (100% MANDATORY, usually cost around 10,000, which takes about 2 or 3 days to make in the nube zone) and he gave me 50,000 credits. That was great. But there was nothing to do with my money! I traveled all over Tarooine (the starting planet) and saw all the "points of interest." They were just places from the Star Wars universe put into a game. Not very exciting. I stopped playing after about 2 weeks when I realized the only new thing I would ever see would be a new "class" after about three months straight of playing. Like someone else said before: huge but empty.

Planetside: This game really is cool. It's repetative, and there's almost nothing to it. But it is fun. I only recently stopped playing it after about 6 or 7 months. I've got lots of good memories from this game. There are many very cool weapons and different equipment you can use. You don't really have long-term goals other than "level up" so it's a lot easier to just play the game and have fun. It can get very frustrating sometimes when you're getting zerged, but whatever. Just go somewhere else. This is the one SOE game I've played that I enjoyed.

My final opinion about SOE is that I'll keep giving them chances. EQ2 COULD be a good game. I'm one of those people who think it looks like Barbie dolls and clay, but look at all your favorite games from back in the day. They look like crap, but were wonderful. I'll get the trial version of EQ2, but chances are (due to past experiences with SOE and Blizzard) I'm going to just stick with WoW. If EQ2 is anything like the first, I'll hate it.
