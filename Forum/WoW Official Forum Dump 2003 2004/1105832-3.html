Q u o t e:

How does group experience work?
Group exp is still being evaluated. The current system is in its simplest form with everyone sharing the divided experience for a kill.


Well I'm glad you're at least open to some change. This quite honestly can MAKE OR BREAK this game on its own. I've seen too many variations and issues not to comment-

Straight divided exp: you'll have a LOT of people soloing throughout their career. There has to be at least SOME group experience bonus, and you might have it grow with the size.

Speaking of, do we have a group limit number yet??

I *really really really* suggest granting a bonus to grouping experience. Yeah less downtime, but unless you're killing 2-3-4-5-6! times as fast as a solo person.. why group??

EQ had this issue in spades, I was an enchanter in a guild, and even most of my friends/pals didn't group with me (some friends huh??) until late in the game. They'd rather just get free cra.. err clarity. Later they needed me as crowd control went from an ok extra to CRUCIAL (and then to nothing as you got higher in level as everything had WAY too much magic resistance, at least before later expansions.)

Now it's not enough just to tweak the group experience either. Why? Loot.

Example from Anarchy Online:
Experience from grouping there ROCKED! You could out level solo efforts big time, particularly in large groups. Even disparate levels joined up for this experience fest so unlike EQ level wasn't quite as critical. However loot BIT! You'd level extraordinarily, but the ONLY way to get real money and loot pretty much was solo missions - even group missions were more beneficial for the quest initiator, not the whole group. This also goes to the heart of group quests, it sounds like here though each member might get the bits right?

Q u o t e:
Will each member of a group receive a quest reward if they participated in completing a quest?
If each member of the group accepted the quest from the quest-giver, each member would also receive the quest reward.


Still unless each group member can choose the reward though, what a mage picks will be a lot different than what a warrior would like to pick.

So what to do?
Have some balance - grouping exp should be as good if not better than solo exp. Meanwhile loot ideally shouldn't be THAT far behind solo loot. Ideally group quests will be created so that it's not a bunch of guys going through the motions for a pal - they'll get some of the good stuff to.

If quests and solo work exceed grouping (I'm not talking about early stuff, but as people reach mid-high levels I'd hope grouping is a POWERFUL thing and not a sideline affair, particularly with the way PvP sounds like it might work out).

I'm glad to see we don't have any silly over the top classes like Necromancers in EQ. I'm trusting Blizzard to keep this balance. For some reason people were so proud of what they could do with them where IMO yay for you they were easily the most powerful character. But if I wanted to play a solo game at least *I* wouldn't get online and pay a monthly fee!
