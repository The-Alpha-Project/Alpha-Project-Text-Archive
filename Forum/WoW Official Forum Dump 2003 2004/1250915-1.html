143. Re: Death Penalty Discussion | 2/21/2004 7:29:43 AM GMTST
Personally, I think this ghost system adds a wonderful dimension to the game that has been unexplored up until this point (to my knowledge).

I think it mostly balances itself (for those critics calling it too whussy). Why? Well, if you die in a place where you were obviously too weak to be in, you can go back there, respawn 30yards away, but you'll still have to deal with getting yourself out there once corporeal. This could become horribly frustrating, or impossible, and thus respawning at the bind point with xp loss might be the only way to go.

Beyond this, I imagine that what was indicated by some areas having monsters that could attack ghosts, would be that some areas (higher level), you will NOT be able to venture to as a ghost, and thus will be forced to rely on a resurection. With the ghost system in place, I do suggest that resurection takes some sort of XP penalty. That way, the game becomes more difficult, and dying becomes a bigger deal as you become more powerful. Either that, or true resurections without penalty, should take some rare resource to accomplish, thus possibly enforcing a barter system to gauge how much extra work you'd be willing to do to avoid the XP loss.

As for people purposefully dying - what's wrong with that? I think it adds something to the game, not detracts. This would be especially true if the world through the eyes of the dead is sufficiently rich (as seems to be indicated). I personally think blizzard should go as all out as they can with the stuff only available to the dead. Maybe even some areas that you can only get into or explore as a ghost. (!!!)

(By adding a currency % loss on death, multiple deaths in this fashion would add up quickly add discourage this exploit)
