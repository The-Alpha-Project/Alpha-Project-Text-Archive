188. Re: Just Curious | 1/30/2004 6:25:29 PM GMTST
I've got about six years of MMORPG experience, spread roughly evenly between UO, EQ, and DAoC. Personally, I'd have to say:

Ultima Online:
Best features: Thriving economy, easy to disguise yourself, not "level-based."
Worst features: RAMPANT thieves, exploitation, plague of player housing, insta-killing guards, twitch-based PvP, potion-fest, flavor-of-the-month imbalances, macroing, too solo-oriented, full loot on death

EverQuest:
Best features: Good atmosphere, huge world, extremely deep world ("maxing out" your character takes forever, so there's always something left to accomplish)
Worst features: constant item inflation, 6+ months of time required before you can "do the fun stuff," nothing can be accomplished in less than a 6-hour playing session, mandatory classes, rampant imbalances, tremendous downtime, incredible boredom at the high end (EQ DEFINES the term "level grind"), no PvP, griefing, quests are set up to be competitive, not cooperative, broadcast channels filled with spam

Dark Age of Camelot:
Best features: Great player economy, useful tradeskills, cooperative questing, best PvP system yet (rewards for winning without costs for losing), fewer mandatory classes, good guild/alliance system, much less spam (except in towns), fun siege gear
Worst features: BORING tradeskills, horrid crowd control ideas (instant CC in PvP), combat is too level-based, class balance came only after 3 months of PvP combat, zerg-dominance in PvP, about 50% of the world unexplorable to any one character, stupid AE imbalances (GTAE and PBAE through walls --> nearly invulnerable keep defense), stealth vastly overpowered, buffbots
