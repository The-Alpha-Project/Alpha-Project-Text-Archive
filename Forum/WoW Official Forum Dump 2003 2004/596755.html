I visited some EQ2 info recently and I was very surprised. Not sure if this is new info - or just something I never knew to begin with.

They appear to have the same message as Blizzard.

1. Game focused on users new to MMORPGS
2. Log on and actually complete something within 2 hours.
3. Smaller group sizes and smaller adventures
4. Quest logs and book guides

In essence a more user friendly system targeting those new to the genre, while providing content that allows people to have fun immediately upon logging on - unlike EQ.

The dungeon instancing that WoW refers to is now implemented in EQ's latest expansion - and appears to be EQ's best expansion ever. Again I am surprised.

What is really neat here - and this is the ONLY feature I have heard Sony talk about WoW has not (to my knowledge) is the ability to assign new properties to your gear based on your adventures (you collect "augmentation points"). This is a nice way to become unique and upgrade that gear you don't want to replace with something else that is more "uber" (there are limits here of course).

Yup, maybe EQ has stolen these ideas from WoW. Not arguing that - I suspect they have but I don't solid time line information to compare the companies.

I suspect WOW is already a success for one reason already - it is causing major changes in the existing genre as exhibited by Sony.

I think we all stand to benefit.
