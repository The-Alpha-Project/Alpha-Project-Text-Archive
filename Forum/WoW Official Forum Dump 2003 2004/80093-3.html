60. Re: $12.95 A MONTH IS INSANE!!!!!!!!! | 4/26/2003 11:42:27 PM GMTDT
i think the fee will vary from country to country. those of you who know economic theory should know that a flat one price will not earn blizzard the most dough.

for example, for most other countries apart from the g7, most ppl earn less than $5000 annually. how do you expect them to fork out $240 for a game, a luxury at best? hell, even korea, blizzard's asia stronghold, the per capita earnings is only $10,000 equivalent (compared to the US of $30,000+). cybercafe rates are like $1 per hour, mcdonalds much cheaper than mcdonalds in the US. no way in hell will you get subscribers for $20 a month.

blizzard is also picking up a strong audience in china, but the per capita is even lower at about $4000. blizzard will lose a lot of audience if a flat rate of $20/month is charged throughout the world.

most likely, in the g7 countries, a much higher rate is charged, like $20 or so. for other countries, especially if there is an asia server, will be much less...like $5-10 equivalent (maybe a promotion of some sort like annual subscription of only $60, but if you want to pay per month, then the fee will actually be higher, like $10 or so). that is what most US magazines do...they offer heavily subsidized rates if you subscribe annually, instead of buying on a per month basis at the newsstand.

oh...for those who are not economics/business majors, think of it this way. the game has been made regardless of subscribers. so to recuperate your cost/get the profits, you want to spread it over as many users as possible (but must not exceed the cost to run the server maintenance, etc - which is your marginal cost). and to do that, you really need stagged pricing (because not everyone is as rich as the americans). if $20 is charged, then perhaps you will only get like 50,000 or $1,000,000. if you charge $20 on 50,000, then lower your rates to $10 for the next 50,000, you earn extra $500,000 (and maybe incur only $200,000 extra cost to maintain a new asia server and gamemaster team, which means net additional profit of $300,000).
