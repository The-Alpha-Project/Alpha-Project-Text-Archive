134. Re: What I learned at Gen Con today. | 12/14/2003 6:44:29 PM GMTST
Holy smokes, such an incredible public outpouring!

Time for a reply to ALL of your comments.

For those of you who appreciated my work, whether you made a "thank you" post or just read that whole freakin' monster, I can honestly say that your appreciation made my labor worthwhile. In the first twelve hours, this post got about 10,000 hits! In my wildest dreams, I wouldn't have guessed that would happen. I'm truly flattered. All I can say is, "aw, shucks."

Some answers:

Regarding authorization to repost this information: Feel free to link it, copy it, post it, summarize it, change it, lie about it, or falsely attribute to me random "facts" you make up. =) All I ask is that if you do quote or reference this information, you at least mention somewhere that you borrowed info from TehSuq. Beyond that, I officially declare this public information: enjoy it.

Regarding the prospects for a short beta: the dev I talked to said that beta will take as long as it takes. "If we get 10,000 beta testers who say 'it's great, ship it,' we'll be shocked, but we'll end beta and ship it." In other words, WoW will ship when WoW is done, and not even Mr. Blizzard himself knows when that will be.

Regarding screenshots and game movies: Blizzard was very tight on the reins when it came to video. Several times, I heard the PR guy politely request that a person not take any video when they had a portable camera that looked like it might be able to do video. I personally took 256 megs worth of USB storage capacity with me just in case, but was told (to my dismay) that the setups they had on display had no capacity to take screenshots or movies. Of course, with the 8-mile lines to play for 10 minutes, asking everybody to stop for 1/2 hour while I got some good screenies would have made me even LESS popular there....I did get a press kit, and I just sent about 70 megs of exclusive Blizzard concept art and screenshots to Kheldon for Stratics to host. They will be available at http://wow.stratics.com/ and they said they would get the new stuff posted as soon as possible.

Regarding conflicting information: I have two theories about this, and they're probably both true to some extent. First, the game is apparently in QUITE a bit of flux at the moment. While the backbones seem fairly stable, the details are being changed, tweaked, discussed, revised, and reconsidered on a week-to-week (day-to-day?) basis. As a result, much information was simply unavailable. I saw several of the Blizzard reps express uncertainty about a concept, only to find themselves pressed for details which they then reluctantly made "best guesses" about. These "best guesses" could differ from one rep to another, and unfortunately the uncertainty expressed by the reps wasn't always adequately understood by the fan. The natural result is different fans posting different "facts" about the game. Example: the highest level in game is currently undecided. However, that wasn't good enough for some fans, and they asked the reps what they thought/expected/hoped the highest level would be. Some guessed 60; others 70. If the fans then reported those numbers, they would conflict.

Secondly, on the same topic, not all the reps were equally knowledgeable about the game itself. This is logical enough -- if you go to a car tradeshow and ask the spokesmodel about the inner workings of the engine, he may not have any idea about the true answer. But if a design engineer is standing on the side and you ask her, she may very well give you MUCH better information. Some of the reps at the WoW display were devs and others were PR reps; the knowledge levels were quite varied. (NOTE: *please* do not read this as an attack against anybody; I thought the Blizzard PR reps did a fantastic job at keeping the event running smoothly and making sure everybody went away happy, despite the fact that they knew less about the specific details regarding the game itself).

On purchasing skills: most skills seemed to follow a dual-layered system. When you spend skill points on a skill, you "unlock" the ability to advance in that skill, either by gaining the skill outright or (for level 2 or 3) by increasing your maximum capacity within that skill. Practice will then increase your abilities with that skill. Example: level 1 mining gives a player the ability to mine, and also to increase their mining ability to about 150 skill. Level 2 mining raises that skill cap from 150 to 250, and level 3 mining raises it again to 350. (The numbers are arbitrary, but that's the general idea.) You don't automatically become a great miner by spending 30 seconds at a mining trainer; rather, you get the ability to BECOME a great miner.

Regarding Teleportation: Mages will be able to teleport more than just themselves. There was even talk about a Ritual that would open a stable portal between cities that might last a long time (the dev said a week, but that the length of time could be greatly changed for balance reasons). Any player could then go through that open portal (not sure if you'd have to have explored the destination first or not.)

Regarding Shamans: I didn't get to play one, but I think someone else did (21_blessings, maybe?). There is some new shaman information floating around now about things like the various totems available, but none of it comes from me because I found out nothing about them.

Regarding "taxi" travel: all I've seen about taxis so far were screenshots and a few dev comments comments, but it SOUNDS like you hire your own personal taxi. Therefore, there's no waiting for the 10:15 Griffon to Jersey; you just hail one. Boats and trains will probably operate on a schedule, though, but that is just idle speculation.

Regarding leatherworking vs. clothworking: From what I could tell, it sounds like leatherworking creates leather armor only, not clothing or stylish useless gear. Tailoring, on the other hand, seems to have both a practical side (creating cloth armor for casters) and a frivolous side (creating shirts and pants that are just for show, with no armor or magical benefits).

Thanks again to everybody who took the time to read my comments.
