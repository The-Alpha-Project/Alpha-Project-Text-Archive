Rob -

First of all, thanks for the continuing replies. Secondly, one question - will dieing in ghost form have any penalty attached to it?

Lastly, I wanted to say that I really like the way WoW is treating the death penalty issue. If there was a single thing that "ruined" the fun level of Everquest, it was the harshness of the death penalty. Post 50 (and especially post 60) players were forced to group to fight the extremely challenging mobs in the planes, and oftentimes they were so challenging that one mistake could wipe out an entire party. The death penalty never reflected the ease with which you could be killed...recovering from death, especially a party wipe, could mean a very long process of doing a CR, getting rezzed, getting back to full health / mana, rebuffing, and oftentimes finding a new camp. It was anything but fun, and it took you out of the action for far too long...a single computer slowdown in Valor might mean half an hour of rezzing and rebuffing.

I can only boggle at people who are trying to say the death penalty should be more severe. Not only does a harsh death penalty only succeed limiting the amount of fun you have while playing, but it can also dictate exactly how people play the game. A harsh death penalty forces people to group more to avoid death; it forces people to stick to the few skills that they might be absolutely best with, and never try new ones for fear of dieing; it forces them to come up with innane and repetitive strategies to use in order to defeat mobs, and to stick to those strategies no matter what; it makes people terrified of exploring new areas (I doubt that most level 60+ characters in EQ had ever visited even half the zones in the game - they stuck to what they knew to avoid death); and it creates a community where specific classes are considered the "good classes" to group with, and the others are shunned.

Q u o t e:
If you burden the death penalty with severe penalty then everybody starts getting all conservative and $!@% with their characters and nobody ever does anything unless it is "safe."


/agree. Thank you =)
