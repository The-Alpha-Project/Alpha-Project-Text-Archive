172. Re: A Role-Player's Plea [Or Several] | 1/21/2004 8:33:08 PM GMTST
Nice to see a relatively productive thread here. A few more cents to throw into the pile....

Immersion into character is in very few cases an absolute, and each individual defines "roleplay" at a certain point on the spectrum of this immersion. While one person or guild may take offense at any breach of character regardless of channel, another may consider a basic idea of a character history in keeping with the world's fiction sufficient RP. A key issue with regard to the viability of roleplay in a massively multiplayer environment is to what extent these divergent groups of players are forced to interact with one another -- the ability, or lack thereof, of players to isolate themselves from other players at their discretion. This isolation is also relative, but in order for a game to support RP there cannot be a negative circumstance for choosing to disregard other players who have a different play style.

In Shadowbane, any guild was able to declare war on another and thereby have the opportunity to destroy its buildings and other assets, which represented a significant time sink in terms of purchase and maintenance. This caused SB in general to be a game of numbers, with the expected resulting negative impact on small roleplay guilds. I made a difficult decision to ally with a larger guild with a somewhat incompatible interpretation of roleplay because it was the only way I could see us, as a small RP guild, being able to participate in the conflict we enjoyed. This arrangement didn't prove fruitful, and we're no longer in SB.

Ultima Online, by contrast, did provide for some level of isolation simply by disallowing players to do one another any truly lasting damage, and by requiring guild wars to be consensual. There were no roleplay regulations and no roleplay server, and we had our share of 133+ d00dz, but we made RP work regardless. The issue is really a PvP one, but my belief has always been that PvP and RP are not mutually exclusive. While my guild and I are strict roleplayers, we also enjoy PvP, and in fact consider it an integral part of roleplay. RP provides a context and motive for combat, while PvP is a tool to resolve in-character situations. I would find immersion without a game mechanic for character conflict pretty boring, and I quckly tire of the generally repetitive and predictable PvE experience. Fantasy characters should fight one another -- most fantasy stories revolve around conflict, and the Warcraft world is absolutely rife with it.

The issue, to my mind, is not that of a roleplay server or a set of naming restrictions, but a PvP one. There will always be people in a massively multiplayer environment who you would rather not associate with for any reason, be it RP based or otherwise. As long as World of Warcraft's PvP system allows us to do this (the individual instances of quests and dungeons are a step in the right direction) and does not punish us for it, we should see a healthy RP community in WoW.

178. Re: A Role-Player's Plea [Or Several] | 1/21/2004 9:34:31 PM GMTST
Q u o t e:
So, for those 'roleplayers' who think that its okay to group with members of an opposing faction... well you are either still breast feeding or listen to Michael Jackson. Take your pick.


I essentially agree with this point, but keep in mind that there are situations where Horde/Alliance cooperation can be justified. I'm not sure if you've read the Warcraft RPG books, but the general idea is that Horde/Alliance (at least at the time of the RPG) members are not always going to kill each other on sight.

Rather, they are generally hostile to the opposing faction, and in open war situations they'll gladly fight, but if a group of orcs runs into a group of humans it doesn't necessarily mean they'll immediately come to blows. A good example in the RPG book is a short story where a human who's caravan has just been destroyed ends up joining a mixed-race group of demon hunters. Since demons are generally the enemies of *every* faction, cooperating against them -- as in the final mission of Warcraft 3 -- is completely justifiable.

In general, however, your point is sound. There are people out there that are completely "carebear" and will never raise a weapon against another player, even if it makes sense for role-playing reasons. I suppose there would be real pacifict characters in-game, but I doubt that's the real justification. After all, many of these same people probably slaughter humanoid NPCs all the time. It's a very, very fine line. I just do feel that there are cases where Alliance/Horde grouping could make sense. That being said, I don't believe the mechanics of the game will support it.
