1. Lore: Tichondrius and the Frostmourne | 12/2/2003 9:15:45 PM GMTST
The Frostmourne was made by the Burning Legion along with the Plate of the Damned and the Helm of Domination. They were all placed inside the Frozen Throne, the former prison of the Lich King, Ner'zhul. Now, the Nathrezim (along with the rest of the Legion) knew what the blade was and where it was supposed to be in.

When the Undead campaign of Reign of Chaos starts, Arthas is visited by the leader of the Nathrezim and a close agent to Kil'jaeden, Tichondrius. He tells Arthas finally what he is wielding and what it is meant to do. The Nathrezim are known as a very pessimistic race, always assuming the worst. My question is, why was Tichondrius not worried when he saw that the powerful runeblade, that was still supposed to be inside the Frozen Throne, had been kicked out of the icy prison and given to the hands of a mortal? Shouldn't he, being an eternal pessimist, have been suspecting that the Lich King might have plans of his own?
