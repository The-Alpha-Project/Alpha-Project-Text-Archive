Well....
I guess someoen must ask what genre.

I would put Lawrence of Arabia on the short list. Based (loosely) on a true story, the Dune series was an admitted sci-fi take on this story, and for the military types this is a classic example of special forces type op before anyone knew what special forces was about.

On the DVD short list I would consider Band of Brothers as well. Not a movie per se, but better than most movies.

Saw some anime on people's list. For short anime Voices of a distant Star is a good example of packing a lot into 26 or so minutes. I am more biased towards anime series over movies, so I would say give Last Exile a look, since the production quality is better than most movies mentioned anyway.

Braveheart is a pretty good film, regardless of the historical inaccuracies and other flaws.

Other good movies:
The great escape (wow, a role where Bronson actually ACTS in his career, great overall cast)
Saving Private Ryan (Vin Disel in an actual acting role? another surprise)
The Shining (Jack Nickholson version)
2001 (holds up pretty good over the test of time)
Raiders of the Lost Arc - it invented modern action movies.
The Duelists - a surprisingly good movie with (for once) quality swordplay. Ridley Scott's first movie.
John Carpenter's the Thing - horror movie that holds up very well over time and still can be scary, which may be the best test for horror flicks.
Signs/6th sense - a horror movie that isn't quite a horror movie. A good example of a suspence director learning to become effective, but stil lacking the final bit of payoff at the end to make these truly terrifying. (sort of spielberg meets hitch$!$%)


The Best movie ever? not a dang clue. Maybe the extended Return of the King?
