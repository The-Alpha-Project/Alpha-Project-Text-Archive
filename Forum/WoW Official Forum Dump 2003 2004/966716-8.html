Heh, I suggest some disguised blizz worker should repost the ideas they like into their suggestion forum, then it will be legal :P

I think the babysitting and pandaren pupil fetching would be fun :)

I'd also like to point out some things to keep in mind with quests.

Several ways to achieve the same goal

It seems that people forget about rogue's stealth, mage's invisibility, druid's ambient form. These things mean that you don't actually HAVE to fight, you can sneak past. You don't have to kill the guy to get a key - you can steal it or pick the lock. You might even persuade or taunt people to do something unexpected, like to help you or go away to fetch the sword and meanwhile you can pick the place clean (but I don't know if there is diplomacy skill in WoW).

The point is - if you want to fight, you can just go and creep/hunt. When you go questing, then the objective is primary and how you do it is secondary. Which brings us to the next point.

Exp for not only killing

I think that quest creeps should probably give 0 exp. You would recieve it for picking up a quest item. This way you may steal it, you may kill it's current owner etc. And none of the possible ways is enforced by giving more exp than others. Or some hybrid scheme where you get total of X exp for completing the quest and the creep exp is substracted from it or something.

Teaching the ways and history of Azeroth

Most of the players will know next to nothing about the world around them. Even those who have read Warcraft novels (like me) and played warcraft games. And of corse there are plenty of people totally new to Warcraft. Quests are a good way to make people understand the world better but in a casual way, paralel with some evolving plot.

There are couple of good suggestions about pilgrimage, paricipating in druid ritual etc. These and other quests should involve bits of lore or be based on historical events. But the chunks of conversations should be skippable for the sake of more experienced players - like "I know this already."

Different moods and attitudes

You should be able to react or deliver the same message in a number of ways - basically good, neutrally, evil. The NPC's responce can bethe same for all of them - "Of corse I'll help you. I won't let the innocent suffer." or "Alright, I'll see what I can do.", "Gah! You are such a pathetic whiner, but I'll look into this." But occasionally the different attitude should lead to different chat subsets. Or even affect wether or not you'll be able to get quests either by pissing someone off or intimidating them to give you a task.

This is rather cosmetic, but certainly adds quite a bit to the game. Actually it's more general suggestion and not only for quests.

153. Re: List your quest ideas for Bliz. | 1/23/2004 10:23:59 PM GMTST
"Keep Away"
Length: 0 - 6 hours
Level: Any
Description:

There was a little goblin who made all sorts of gadgets. One thing he made was a small automated machine to make high-quality, large bags. The small machine could only turn out a bag once every six hours because it was so small and the bags were so large. Still, this was enough to make it valuable. Disputes erupted and some even fought for possession of the machine. Enough blood was spilled so the machine became cursed to cause war wherever it was. So a skilled mage enchanted the machine so it would not stay in one place for long.

The little machine drops with the first monster killed in the world. The carrier of the machine looks distinctive so other players know he/she has it. The carrier can be attacked by any player anywhere but can only attack those who attack him/her first and those who he/she'd normally be able to attack. When the carrier is killed, the machine drops. If the carrier logs out, the machine leaves and is dropped again with the next monster kill.

The machine also has a timer that counts down 6 hours. After six hours, the player finishes the quest, a really large bag is given to the player by the machine, the timer resets, and will spawn with the next monster kill. If player A is killed with the machine, player B can pick it up and timer keeps ticking. Player B could get the machine with only a few seconds left to go, or maybe the full 6 hours to hold onto it.

This would add a nice but rare PvP feature. If you don't want to be attacked, simply drop the machine and let someone else take up the quest. If it sits on the ground for 10 minutes or so, it disappears and will drop again with the next monster killed. If the 6 hours run out while the machine is on the ground, it will simply put it on the ground.
