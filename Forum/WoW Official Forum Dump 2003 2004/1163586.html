362. Re: Best Videogame Ever | 2/7/2004 12:36:35 AM GMTST
In no particular order......

  • Morrowind (PC and Xbox)
I've read through ninteen long pages of "what's the best game ever?", and only four people listed this? Something's wrong here. The freedom, the character progression, the gameplay.... almost* everything about this game is just awsome!

  • Halo (PC and Xbox)
It wasn't perfect, but it was very well done.

  • WarCraft III: RoC and TFT (PC and Mac)
This is the only RTS I've actually enjoyed. Before any of you say "What about StarCraft?", I found it bland and boring. Too bad I hardly got go play the second one though, when my family was moving to California, I went down to my aunt and uncle's house while my parents were packing everything up. And my aunt and uncle made me get rid of it because they thought it was too much like D&D...... lousy SOBs t_(>.<).

  • Contra (NES)
I actually don't remember much about this game (I didn't play it that much) I just remember that it was verrrrry fun.

  • Otogi: Myth of Demons (Xbox)
A very fast, and very cool "run around and smash stuff" ninja-ish game. Great graphics, geat gameplay, you can smash up buildings (note that your character is a human sized guy with a sword or pole or hammer), you can smash enemies into the ground leaving craters :D, it's just very well put together.

  • Metal Arms: Glitch in the System (Xbox, Gamecube, and PS2)
This game is very well done. It's a third person shooter that plays like an FPS, you can take over other robots, blow off enemies' limbs (or just damage them so they're only hanging by a wire), if you damage them enough your enemies will scream and run away, it's fast and has awsome combat, really funny sometimes... it's just very cool.

  • Tom Clancy's Splinter Cell (Xbox, PC, Gamecube, PS2, and sort of on GBA
This game kicks any Metal Gear game's ass. Awsome gameplay, controlls, and graphics. Very well done.

  • Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six 3 (Xbox, PC, and I'm not sure about other consoles)
I'm not sure what makes this game so fun, but it does a good job.

  • Chrono Trigger (SNES and PlayStation)
The best game of its, and most likely our, time 'nuff said.

  • Half-Life (PC and PS2)
I only played the single player, but that's all I need to call this one of the best games ever made. I love the whole theme of it, and how all the "cinematics" are in-game. This game had me hooked right from the time I was riding the tram into Black Mesa.

  • MechAssault (Xbox)
Mass destruction, awsome multiplayer, great controlls.... what more could you want from an action game?

  • Insert any game I forgot here



* = I don't like the fact that Bethesda didn't include Xbox-Live downloads or the official plug-ins with GotY.

365. Re: Best Videogame Ever | 2/7/2004 1:09:16 AM GMTST
Super Mario 64 hands down.

This game was revolutionary.

Not sure if it was the first fully 3-D game, but it sure was the first awesome fully 3-D game.

No game I can remember created quite a stir - not just among hardcore gamers but casual gamers and people who've never ever played videogames save for this one.

In terms of creativity and innovation this game blew everything away in its day.

In a close second would Zelda: Ocarina of Time.

To this day I've yet to play (and doubt I ever will) any console games that were able to replicate or supass the entertainment these games produced.
