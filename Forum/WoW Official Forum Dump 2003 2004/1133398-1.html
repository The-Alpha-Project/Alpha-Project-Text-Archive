11. Re: Will Your Beta Key Be Set To Your Computer? | 2/3/2004 5:04:50 AM GMTST
The CDkey in MMO's is tied to the game account, not an individual computer. It's not required to load the software. The CDkey is a one time activation code that sets up an account with the game. You then select a user name and a password, and from then on, to play the game, you link to the game and access your account using your account name and password.

You can link to the game from any computer that has the software loaded. However, that's not necessarily going to be good for the beta if you play on a machine other than your own.

Another thing: ANYONE with the software can access your account if they know your account name and password. Make sure you absolutely trust anyone you may be tempted to give this information to. Ask anyone who's played MMORPGs and you'll hear stories about how people have had all their items/cash or reputations completely stolen or trashed by someone using their characters improperly.

EDIT: Bah, actually read the thread instead of just the thread title. I'm betting that Blizzard wants to know what rig you are running for the beta, and getting the information from the sign-up means less work in setting up/updating the database for those who get invites.
