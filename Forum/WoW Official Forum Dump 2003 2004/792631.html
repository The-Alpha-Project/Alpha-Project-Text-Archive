That is a hilarious point. I certainly saw that play out in Shadowbane - very true. In EQ every character of the same class is the same - unlike the options WoW offers - so in EQ you did not see this much.

We might take a different angle on this:

1. What is "easy" to play
2. What is "easy" to level advance

The warrior in the eyes of many people is easy to play - but I don't agree with this at mid to higher levels. However, I think we might all agree that this class - which is soooo group dependent - is a real pain to level. He can 'solo' at the newbie levels - but that quickly fades as an option (EQ, Shadowbane).

This is why I think high level warriors, in EQ at least, are sought after. There are not many high level warriors because it takes so much commitment to get a warrior to a high level. However, there are plenty of high level hybrids (druids, shamans) and solo enabled classes (e.g. wizards) at least in EQ.

If your experience with other games is similar you might argue the paucity of high level warriors is due to:

1. Because they are mind numbing to play
2. Because only "good" warriors find groups and even then it takes great dedication to advance your level.

Whichever the reason #1 or #2 I think explains what I tend to see:

There seems to be a vast number of low level warriors and diminishing few warriors at the higher levels. This may be consistent with NinthArchon's observation that:
