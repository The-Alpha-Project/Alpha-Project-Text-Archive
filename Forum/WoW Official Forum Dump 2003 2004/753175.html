30. Re: +_+ Semi Solution To PvP +_+ | 11/18/2003 12:09:35 AM GMTST
Heh, some people on my thread pointed me here.

I'ma just copy and paste my idea.

I was just thinking this might be a good idea...

A kind of a cross between DAoC frontiers and zone control. All the land would be divided into a bunch of quadrants, with each server opening the quadrants would belong to the corresponding realm. Any quadrant that has at least two sides not bordering allies would be an open PVP area. It could be taken over by the opposing faction.

Some quadrants would be sanctuaries: Newbie starting areas and major cities. These would always be non-PVP areas.

The corners would be the most susceptible to attack, as they always are open PVP. Quadrants that are heavily guarded and probably closer to the center would be have huge dungeons and big points of interest in them. Say, Blackrock Spire might be a quadrant that would be of high value.

To defend a quadrant, players could set up towers, build walls, and even recruit guards. Int the center of the quadrant, they could set up a fortress of varying cost and effectiveness, from a mot and bailey to a full fledged seige resistant castle. To seige one, enemies could bring catapults and other seige weapons to battle DAoC style. To capture the quadrant, the invaders might have to take an object from the most heavily defended room in the fortress, destroy the fortress completely, or kill the main guard (a la DAoC keep lord.)

The side with more quadrants could get special rewards to all its people, like and exp or cash bonus. Some quadrants might come with the 'one per server' items that people so desire.

If the war becomes hopeless for one side (almost all quadrants stolen) the winning side might have new characters more susceptible to PVP damage from the opposing faction.

What do yall's think?

31. Summary of what has been said to this point | 11/18/2003 1:07:11 AM GMTST
Ok, good thoughts.

Alot of the things posted on this thread are really similar to that, only instead of "zones" of control there would be a line of battle where armies of npcs would constantly be fighting eachother. This line could be moved through the actions of the players, by completing some quest to rally more support on their side, accomplishing some espionage mission behind enemy lines, or just hacking away at enemy front. This will let a wide variety of players with different ways they want to play all get into the battle.

The line would move slowly, and as it got closer to one capital or another, it would become even harder to move. There were a few ideas as to how that would be accomplished, but I would say that the best was to increase the concentration of defending npc fighters and decrease that of attacking npcs the further from equalibrium that the line line got. This way, the chances of actually reaching striking distance of an enemy capital, while existant, would be very, very slim.

Towns could be upgraded (like you said, defensive structures, more guards, etc) by contributing to the local economy (ie, buying and selling products to and from local vendors) or something to that effect.

All areas in the game would be faction PvP, except during peace, and during war times you could not bind at normal bind points when on the enemy's side of the line of battle.

Then there could be a system through which players could toggle the war on and off for the whole world. A series of very long and complex quests could begin talks for peace between the heads of each race, then a time of peace would decend on the land. After a certain amount of time had elapsed, there could then also be a set of treacherous quests, where the goal would be to betray your enemies and restart the war. This way, if a kingdom is losing the war too badly, the players can attempt to bring on peace, and if dedicated PvPers get tired of peace and arena only fighting, they can go and restart the war. This would of course be a repeatable cycle, with a period of no war/peace related actions available for a certain amount of time after each phase begins.

Also, during the peace times, it was proposed that some world-wide event, like an Olympic tournament, be held to keep people's attention and make things more enjoyable. There is another post relating strictly to that topic here:

http://www.battle.net/forums/wow/thread.aspx?FN=wow-general&T=752362&P=1&ReplyCount=8#post752362

if you are interested.

I think covers the everything. All summarized, nice and neat for your reading pleasure (minus all the funny stuff...sigh, I just can't win...).

Ya, I agree. There should be more to FvF than just taking over land. Faction perks act as amazing rewards and are great as a means of encouragement.

The rank of Knight or General would be somewhat of a status symbol, showing your involvement in the FvF conflict. Hopefully, as JoeMoses mentioned, your title (a faction perk) could give you access to places others couldn't.

I would personally like to see a large military post or building within one of the major cities which only players with certain titles could enter. Different sections of the building would be open to different ranks. For example, to get in the door you would need to be at least a footman/grunt. Then, access to higher levels of the building would require higher ranks. Also, on each level you could get different missions corresponding to your rank.

One more thing that I would like to see is the higher ranks having more control over the FvF battle itself. Perhaps the highest ranks of the alliance/horde could organize these times of war and peace that we discussed earlier. Also, higher ranks should be able to make missions/messeges as to where help is needed on the battle line. For example, high ranking members could go up to an NPC guard in some random town and a menu would pop up in which allows them to set up a mission/message type (i.e. defense, attack, supply, reconnaissance, etc.) and a location. The menu could then check whether the submission was valid (i.e. so you couldn't have an attack mission/message against a town that was under friendly control). The NPC would then begin giving out these missions/messages to passing players.
