81. Brainstorms and Rambling-Open PVP-Murderers | 11/12/2003 1:00:33 PM GMTST

From what I gather WoW will be a hybrid, there will be open zones and there will be closed zones. The question then becomes how much of the world will be each kind? WoW right now seems to be about 90% closed to PVP, and 10% open. That is, most of the world is safe and non-PVP. These areas are assumed to be under powerful government control where order is imposed so strongly that even the most murderous rogue is too intimidated (or just realistic about their own survival) to even think of pulling a weapon on a person. The remaining areas are all arenas and battlegrounds specifically for the purpose of PVP. These PVP Areas are so obviously for the sole purpose of PVP combat that entering them amounts to giving consent to any and all PVP. An area dedicated ONLY to PVP isn't fully exciting, because it is not ambiguous. When you are there, you are there for one purpose, tournament-style PVP.

A PVP-closed monster area would be like a sanctioned hunting grounds or a tract of forest owned by a local Lord, and would offer quests and hunting for non-PVP players with no danger of PVP. So far it seems that's what almost all the wilderness in WoW will be.

What would be more intriguing would be if perhaps 30% of the world were "Neutral" areas where PVP was possible, but there were also quests and other objectives to draw PvE players in too. AKA areas that are not just arenas for PVP. An example of this might be the Slums of New Stormwind or an abandoned temple. Treasure and dangerous players both.

That being said, I like many of the interesting ideas your summary of Lineage (which I like learning about but haven't played.) The thing I liked most was the suggestion of the Criminal/Chaos system. If that Criminal system was amplified greatly, like crimes punished 3X more, I think I could accept 50% or more of the zones in WoW being neutral, PVP-open. Let me think of all the other gameplay points I like from thinking of this system.

I like that it differntiates between murder (non-consensual, PK) and consensual combat (consensual, PVP, dueling). I like how it punishes murderers and makes their life more difficult. I think the criminal punishment for even one murder should be high, and subsequent murders getting exponentially worse until the murderer's life becomes a living hell.

What are some good ways to punish murderers? Have town guards attack them on sight. Make shopkeepers charge them more. Reward other players with EXP, Gold, and chances to steal the murderer's items if caught for "bringing them to justice". (Killing a murderer not considered murder.) Maybe the game should offer a moderate experience reward for higher level players who protect younger ones from murderers. Well they would automatically be rewarded for any murderers they brought to justice. Essentially, people who chose murder would be choosing a gameplay experience of always being hunted and being on the run. Even if they were very strong murderers, it would be fun for the "Just" because the rewards on their head would keep getting larger and larger, so that posses would eventually form up to bring them to justice. If a murderer was too strong, a GM could form a posse to hunt them down, bringing along NPC guards if necessary. It might be fun to have a few notorious murderers at large with large bounties on their heads.

Of course you have to balance that out with murderers racking up a huge bounty and then letting their friends execute them for the Gold/ XP reward. The consequences for notorious murderers being brought to justice would have to be larger than just respawning- perhaps a 20% penalty to experience and all stats and level (or percent based on their crimes) would prevent murderers from giving up their lives in exploitative ways.

What would be other fun gameplay consequences for murderers? Maybe they could be "handcuffed" and dragged to a courtroom where a jury could decide their fate? Perhaps they could be given a sentence and pennance where they could work off their guilt (or just plain executed in front of an audience). Maybe their resurrected murder victim could be allowed to kill them back while they are handcuffed and defenseless in exchange for a clean record.

Murderers might whine that this is too harsh- but you always have the option NOT to murder an innocent victim, and instead take your aggressions out on a consenting arena gladiator or in faction combat. And it's more freedom than simply having the server not allow you to murder anyone at all.

Another brainstorm would be that the Murder penalty could be greater or lesser depending on how close you were to a major city. Zones could be rated like 90% lawful, 50% lawful, 10% lawful, etc. This would represent the long arm of the law being limited-- if you wanted to prey on victims who wandered very far into the wilderness, you could do it with less of a penalty because it would be harder to find out about. The penalty would be you'd have to live a murderer's life way out in the wilderness. Non-murderers who managed to wander all the way out to a 0% lawful zone without enough strength would have no one to blame but themselves. Newbies would be protected because they might spend their entire time in Closed, 100% lawful zones and enjoy the game that way. In 0% lawful zones, murderers would act as intelligent, dangerous mobs that would be a known and accepted risk for any heros venturing out that far.

Faction-based killing against member of a rival faction would be considered a just act of war, not murder and have no murder penalty assosciated with it. Joining a faction or clan that was at war would be an act of consent for all faction PvP. Faction PVPs could fight in any area that was not closed, or not 100% Lawful.

This is getting long, but if you're still reading you're hardcore and don't care.

Option- Injuring. Hurting someone in a way that does not result in their death would have only a very mild penalty. You could only do this in an area that was not PVP-Closed, aka less than 100% lawful. This would be a way to warn or intimidate the annoying. Maybe there would even be an attack on your GUI for warning shot, that deliberately does only 10% of your total damage. If you damaged someone though, and they died for ANY reason within say 1 minute of when you did that damage, it would be counted as Assisted Murder for a rather large penalty. (So if you hit them for 10% and they managed to suicide on a mob within 1 minute you'd be liable.)

So in closing, I like the idea of having large areas of the world open to faction PVP and even PK murderers, but only if a murderer's life becomes a living hell as a consequence of non-consenual PK.

82. Re: Brainstorms and Rambling-Open PVP-Murderers | 11/17/2003 3:50:27 AM GMTST
I think Blizzard's goal is to have a much more sophisticated storyline than merely different factions trying to kill each other.

In fact, such a dynamic would limit the content available to any given player, because they would not be able to venture to the cities and regions controlled by other factions and fight the indigenous monsters. If I choose a human player, I still hope that I'll be able to travel to the Undead city at some point in the game.

Also, the storyline Blizzard has set up seems to hint at a much more interesting story than merely a bunch of player characters killing each other. The storyline in Frozen Throne ended with a bunch of uneasy alliances among factions with complex interests.

In fact, the whole Sylvanas/Forsaken storyline seems to have been specifically designed to create a faction of undead that could plausibly align with the other groups in WoW.

It doesn't seem like Blizzard set the story here for the purpose of establishing a one-dimensional Orc hunt as the context for WoW.

I would hope that PvP quests are part of the game, and that there is some device for facilitating feuds between guilds.

I would also like to see events within the game that set groups of players against each other, within the context of the overarching story. But I see no reason to allow high-ranking parties of griefers to ambush lower-level parties that are working on a quest.
