50. Re: Dynamic PvP zones | 6/24/2003 8:05:02 PM GMTDT
____________________________________
Faction PVP Battlezones
____________________________________

1. CONTROL – Who gets to decide when and where the battles occur?
  • PLAYER CONTROL-
    I don't think it would be as much fun if GMs controlled who and when certain zones were attacked.
  • FACTION POINT SYSTEM-
    There has to be some sort of faction point system. Those who do the most to protect their empire or expand it should be the ones deciding who and where to attack. Maybe they should even be given the ability to give messages to the local town criers to make announcements to people in the local towns.
  • FAIRNESS-
    There will be 3 factions. How to prevent 2 of them from ganging up on 1? Well, it works in DAoC, so I don't think that is a problem. Usually if one faction is kicking butt then the other two will try to gang up, which is perfectly fine and only makes it more interesting!
  • INTERFACTION COMMUNICATION-
    In general, I hope they limit the amount of communication between factions. I don’t think /tell should be permitted between opposing factions, only local /say or /shout would be options. Trading should not be permitted either, unless through some sort of Black Market bazaar, where the prices are premium and/or there is a Faction Point risk associated.


2. BATTLEZONES – How they could work.
  • CAPITOL CITIES-
    The capital city and the surrounding areas should be immune to PVP and sieging. This would be the area where new characters would spawn, so they would not even have to worry about griefers.
  • DURATION-
    Each battlezone, once declared, could last a week. I think this is probably the most appropriate time. Each siege should be voted on at the beginning of the week (probably on a Monday) and the rest of the week the Battlezone is created and fought over.
  • PVP LIMITS- Only the two factions at war are allowed to engage in combat. Healing, buffs, and the like are not permitted from the third faction to either side. If they want, they can merely only observe. Any PVP outside the battlezones is prohibited, except for consentual dueling or group dueling.
  • CAPTURE-
    Each battlezone should have a key objective. Probably there would be at least one small town with a keep for defense. Inside the keep there would be NPC faction guards and a lord ala DAoC. Once this lord is killed the attackers have taken the zone. A new lord is spawned loyal to the occupying race, along with a complement of guards. The lord and guards should also be of the same race as the occupiers, and might not be as strong as the homeland’s previous defenses. It should be just enough that any small incursion (less than 10 or so?) from the enemy could be stopped without help of PCs. The townspeople however, should be of the homeland’s race i.e. they would be slaves of their enemies until the town and zone is once again liberated.
  • OCCUPATION-
    They could have many fun possibilities with occupation. The townspeople could periodically revolt and refuse to sell merchandise for a short while to their captures, or there could be an increase in taxes due to a distance penalty from your home faction. Each town could perhaps contain a unique item or grant an ability to the occupying faction, like in DAoC. However, I’d rather see bonus faction points for each faction member that visits the new lord and pays homage of sorts, vowing to protect their new conquered territory. Each successive kill would garner further bonuses. This would foster occupying PCs to hunt in the nearby zones and be exposed to different terrain, architectures, and creatures! I think this system would be much more satisfying and interesting than DAoC, where the only way to view the different empires are to create 3 characters on the 3 separate realms.
  • LIBERATION-
    How would the homeland faction be able to gain back their territory? Well, it might be a good idea for that land to be open game. They could attack and caputure it back at any time. This might seem unfair for the occupying faction, however. Instead, maybe it would be better if the homeland faction had to delcare that territory as a Battlezone when the time came, and take it back.


3. FACTION POINTS – How to get them and what they do.
  • FACTION COMBAT-
    Every time you kill an opposing faction member in this battlezone you are granted faction points based on the level or skill of the one killed. For groups, these faction points will be distributed, as would PVE xp.
  • FACTION ITEMS-
    Each faction could have unique items available to those who want to exchange points. These could be symbolic (badges, medals, clothes, etc) as well as utilitarian (weapons, armor, mounts, etc.)


A system like this is something I've only dreamt of. I'm sure it would be very difficult to implement, but I think if this was possible, it would capture the attention of all MMORPG'ers out there. Aside from implementing, testing would be very extensive. This would be a great way to limit PVP to 10% of the map. Granted, that 10% would be dynamically allocated across the map! I'm glad there are people like Bladefist. who have great ideas like this for games. :)
