    293. Re: Just Curious | 1/31/2004 12:37:25 AM GMTST
    Sorry to duplicate my post, but this is where it actually belongs =P

    I have actually thought about this A TON during different betas to try and give feedback about what I think is going well or missing.

    I have played Everquest, Asherons Call, Dark Age of Camelot, and Star Wars Galaxies (I have tried and tested a whole lot more than that, but those are the only ones with any time invested). I have a few things that I found must be in a MMORPG to make it fun:

    Community Love:
    This is first and foremost in any MMORPG. If you don't have a good community then the game will lose my interest. You have to feel close to the people around you, not hugs and kisses, but that you are united together in your struggles to advance. SWG did not have this at all, EQ was the best for me, and DAoC was a close second.

    Character Love:
    You have to feel some sort of affection for your avatar. Either pride in his/her hard earned abilities and equipment, or really feel like it represents your personality to the community.

    You also have to feel like it is different from everyone elses. DAoC failed in this for me. My Paladin looked just like every other Paladin out there. If it wasn't for the pride I had in the Blackfist Clan emblem (which was a differentiating factor) I really would have tired of this aspect fast.

    You should also feel like your character is a "bad @ss". You should be able to come back to past hunting areas feeling like, "Oh yeah, I can kill everything in this zone". The dynamic spawns in SWG killed it for me, why go anywhere when you can spawn mobs your level by running around the same area you started in? Useless.

    Oh, and your characters class should be well defined so that your role in a group is clear, as well as the benefit you bring to a group. I hated it in SWG when people would just form groups, they didn't care what you were because you might as well have all been the same. The classic "zerg".

    Progession in Zones:
    You should feel like the zone/area you are in is not only challenging but is a mark of your characters progession. I had a friend in EQ who was always ahead of me and I would see his location in my /friends and be like, "Wow, your in the Plane of Fear? Thats crazy." (This was near the beginning btw, I think my necro could solo in fear now =P).

    In SWG you could go just about anywhere at any time. I want to feel like new areas are available to me because I am finally high enough to go check them out. An actual GOAL.

    Loot:
    Every mob, or every "boss" mob you kill should have the chance to drop something good, no matter how small the chance. You should get that small excitement like a lottery ticket every time you fight something new or unusual. Kind of like when you fought a boss in Diablo, you are really hoping something cool drops, and when it doesn't you figure your karma will build up and you will get something cool next time.

    DAoC did this well. I remember fighting some undead dudes soon after release. We had been fighting there for over an hour and out of nowhere this "Lion Embossed Helm" drops. It is FAR superior to anything any of us had at the time so we vowed to return and fight those undead until we all had some "Lion Embossed" armor. It was one of my favorite MMORPG experiences.

    Polished:
    The game experience should be polished. It should be immersive as possible, with as few immersion breaking bugs and graphics flaws as possible. SWG and EQ both failed miserably at this, DAoC was pretty good. I like to "FEEL" like I am in the world, if I am running around with my arm sticking out of the center of my shield I don't feel that way (EQ anyone?).

    Gameplay Evolution:
    The game play and way I play my class should change as I get higher. An excellent example of failing and succeeding here is SWG and EQ.

    In SWG I felt I was doing the same sh|t when I started as when I maxed some skills. That game play didn't change for me, it was just more of the same. My strategies didn't change, my styles didn't change, and they way I fought was the same.

    In EQ I played a necro. The way you play a lvl 1 necro is night and day from playing a 30 necro, and a 30 necro is so far from playing a 60+ necro its almost a different game. I LOVED the way my styles and strategies would change depending on available spells and the mobs I was fighting and what zone I was in.

    Replayability and Class Difference:
    These two go hand in hand for me. If I get bored of playing a Mage I should be able to make a Rogue and have a completely different role and gaming experience. In EQ I started with a Warrior and switched to a Necro later, I played the necro for FAR longer than I did the warrior because it felt more my style and was a completely different experience.
