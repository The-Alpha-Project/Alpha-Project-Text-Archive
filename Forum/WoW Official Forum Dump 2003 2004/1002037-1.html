1. BIG IDEA: travelling circus. | 1/18/2004 6:24:15 PM GMTST
I had an idea for a VERY fun recurring world event: a traveling circus.

The basic idea is that once per real-world month (on a random day, or perhaps always on a weekend), the circus comes to town. Starting one real-world week before, fliers and advertisements would go up in the city where the circus would be held. It would travel through the land, appearing in the major cities in turn, but every month it would appear in one city for each faction. It would last for 24 real-world hours, and it would have all SORTS of events for players to do:

I. Quests. An incredibly obvious choice would be to have some quests associated with the circus. Many present themselves at a glance, and coming up with more to add to the list would be EASY:

- “Hear ye, Hear ye!” Starting a week before, the circus manager seeks players to pass out fliers advertising the circus. You apply to the manager and he gives you 20 fliers. You then go to 20 DIFFERENT targets and give them the flier. Note: at least one of the targets MUST be a player, not an NPC. When you finish your task, you get your reward, which could be a free pass to the circus, some coin, or something similar.
- “Well, That’s Natural….” How to put this? Circus creatures are messy, and somebody’s got to clean it up. That would be you. You are given a canvas bag (just an item) and told to fill it with doo-doo. Traveling to the various animal cages, you can gather up droppings. The bag holds 20 “surprises.” When you fill it and return to the animal trainer, your reward is that you can keep the bag (a slightly-used medium-sized container).
- “The Broken Hammer.” During set-up, one of the circus hands broke his hammer while raising a tent. Fix it for him and you will be rewarded with a new hammer recipe (for smiths – medium-level hammer with a unique appearance). To repair it, you’ll take the pieces he gives you and find one or two more parts, then rework it at the forge.


II. Contests. Now THIS is the part that I think would be most interesting to the player community, particularly the roleplayers. There would be skill- and luck-based contests during the event with some sort of reward attached to it. (Incidentally, they would also be potentially good money sinks for the economy. Heck, if the circus is popular enough, you could literally have people saving up for the week before to go to the circus.) Examples:
- Steeplechase: bring your own mount, of course. This will be a race through the forest. It starts at a preset time, costs a large starting fee (say, 250-500sp), and involves a good 30-minute race through the forest, requiring you to hit certain beacons as you go. All players start at the same time, no magical speed-enhancements or buffs are allowed, but the player can use any means necessary to win the race. No non-participants are allowed on the course during the race. The first player to finish the race is awarded a unique-looking (and slightly faster than normal) mount.
- Snipe Hunt. Calling all Hunters: the circus has captured one of the legendary Snipe and will release it in a deep forest. All Hunters who are interested must pay an admission fee (again, it should be steep – 250-500sp) and when the starting gun is fired, they can enter the forest. The snipe does not show up on the mini-map radar, moves VERY quickly, and flees from players whenever it can. This means that the contest will last quite some time until one player accidentally flushes the snipe towards another. The contest ends when one Hunter captures the snipe. The reward is obvious: the Hunter can keep the snipe as a pet. Snipes are a unique pet, looking different from all others and being ever-so-slightly superior to normal pets. The only snipe trainer in the world travels with the circus, and training a snipe is quite expensive; however, snipe are smart creatures, and can gain slightly more skills than normal pets. They also have a WIDE variety of skills to choose from, so that a snipe can be the pet of your choice.
- Logrolling: two players enter, one player leaves (the other swims out). A log is placed in a nearby lake. Players pay to play (smallish fee – say, 5-25sp). When the contest begins, the log starts to spin. If the player falls off, he loses. The winning player gets a ribbon (not wearable). The player can cash in five ribbons to “take on the master”: the circus has a logrolling champion that is INCREDIBLY hard to beat (say, 5% chance to win, or lower). Players who defeat the champion are awarded Logrolling Boots, a unique-looking item that is strong for the player’s level.
- Pugilism: a good old-fashioned boxing match between players. They pay to play, and fight until one falls. Winners earn a Silver Glove (not wearable). Cashing in five Silver Gloves allows the player to challenge the champion – again, incredibly hard to beat (5% chance of succeeding or lower), but winners get Golden Gloves, wearable gloves that are unique looking and strong for the player’s level. Also, the player’s unarmed combat skill increases by 1, possibly exceeding the cap.
- Scavenger Hunt: one for all levels. Each player gets a list of items and must travel through the city finding them. Players pay to compete, and their results are timed. (For each player, all items have randomized locations – no buffing or grouping is allowed during the contest). The fastest finisher gets a 10% experience-point bonus throughout the coming month.
- Skill Tests: players with certain skills compete against one another in once-per-circus tests of skill. These tests will include Quilting Bees for tailors, Sword-making Contests for smiths, Potion Brewing contests for alchemists, Cook-Offs for chefs, an herbalist/miner contest to find a rare ingredient, obviously a fishing contest, etc. The reward in each case is a unique recipe, a very rare ingredient, and/or a one-point bonus to the skill, possibly exceeding the cap.

The list goes on and on. There could be class-specific events, level-specific events, carnival contests with novelty prizes (imagine a gnome wearing oversized sunglasses), an actual three-ring circus, unique food that players can use in the coming month, unusual-looking gear to buy, unique skill trainers that can only be found at the circus, a zoo with rare creeps, and so much more.

In days of old, it was an exciting and anticipated event when the circus came to town; there’s no reason why that couldn’t be the case in WoW, too.

mr.sellars said:
Q u o t e:
i doubt that even blizzard will invest that much effort for a once per month event
While I admit there would be some not-inconsiderable setup costs involved, it would all be a one-time cost. After that, the circus would run itself. Think of it -- basically, it would be a monthly GM-type event that required no GMs to run. Plus, since it would be monthly, announced in advance, and well-known by the players, it would be something that people could look forward to all month long. If it were truly fun and exciting, I think this would add a great deal of "flavor" to the world. Of course, this is the sort of event that Blizzard could start small and build over time, just by adding to the existing code. That way, it would be as simple or as complicated as they wanted it to be, and it could grow as quickly or as slowly as they wanted it to grow.
