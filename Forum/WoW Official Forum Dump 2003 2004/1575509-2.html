For anyone who believes that .999~ is really just a decimal point followed by infinite nines, and that that isn't evidence that .999~ is equal to one, consider

1 - .999~. We can all agree that this equals
.000~1: an infinite zeros followed by a single 1. However, if there are infinite zeros after the decimal point, you can never place the 1. So, we place a decimal point, then infinite zeroes...but we can't ever place the 1, so we are stuck with .000~.

That implies that 1 - .999~ = .000~ = 0.
