273. Re: Best movie ever. | 1/14/2004 2:10:14 AM GMTST
Best movies (in my opinion):
Matrix trilogy (YES, the ENTIRE trilogy)
Beetlejuice
The Nightmare Before Christmas
Lord of the Rings trilogy
Star Wars trilogy
Kill Bill
Old School (f'ing hilarious)


I can't for the life of me understand what some people disliked so much about Reloaded and Revolutions. The only thing I can come up with is that they were hyped to the point where expectations were so incredibly high that there was no way it could meet them, no matter how great the movies were.

I personally had no problem with the actors. I hate Keanu Reeves as much as the next guy in most movies, but I thought he did a fine job in the Matrices. Hugo Weaving was incredible as Smith, one of the most memorable villains ever (for me anyway). I'll admit there were a few bad apples - some of the characters in Zion come to mind - but overall, I thought the movie was well cast and that everyone fit their parts.

The action sequences in these movies are second to none. The freeway scene in Reloaded exemplified all that car chases should be. Whether it was the agent jumping from one car, crushing it, over to the next, or one of the Twins phasing into the car and having a knife fight with Morpheus from the back seat, or Trinity weaving through oncoming traffic on a motorcycle, or Morpheus fighting an agent on the top of a speeding semi, this scene was absolutely incredible. Throw in the three well done Neo vs Smith fights, the lobby scene, the battle for Zion and a few others, and you have action that puts most other movies to complete shame.

The story started off strong in the original Matrix and obviously left the biggest impression on most people because it introduced the concept of the Matrix. Someone else in this thread mentioned that this movie is overrated in part because this concept of the 'Matrix Theory' isn't new. So what? It's quite new to most people and was still a great premise for a movie.

People complain that Reloaded was too light on story, and this may be. However, what story there was I found to be very good. The entire twist that the Architect revealed towards the end of Reloaded was great, though I think most people missed it because they heard him use some big words then tuned him out.

However, while critics largely liked Reloaded, Revolutions took a big hit from them. I would agree that it is likely the weakest of the three, but it is still a great movie (outside of Trinity's overly long death sequence).

As for the other poster here that complained about 'the Oracle talking too much and she could have just said it in one sentence'...please. There are elements of philosophy in all three movies - I've seen entire books written on the symbology and philosophy of the first movie. If this goes over your head, I'm sorry, but it adds an extra layer to the movie for many of us.

I don't know why this bandwagon (not just here, everywhere) has started against the second two Matrix movies, but I am completely baffled by it. I found both movies to be thoroughly entertaining.

Typing any more would likely be a waste, as I doubt many of you have read all of this anyway. ^^
