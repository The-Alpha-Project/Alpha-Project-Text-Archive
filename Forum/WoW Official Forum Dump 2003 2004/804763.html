BananaRebellion

I think that when a player crafts an item the ingredients used to craft the item should go to treasury at the time of crafting, and the finished item should be pulled from treasury at the same time and put into the game world. This way treasury will have constant information about quantities of ingredients being used in crafting and the quantity and value of items being crafted by players on a server. This information should be very useful in economic planning.

However, should a crafted item have a hidden value equal to the hidden value of all ingredients used in the crafting? To actually craft an item will require collecting and combining the right quantities of ingredients with the use of a recipe and will take time and possibly a crafting facility or building (e.g. use of a blacksmith building to craft armour). Recipes will also require time and effort to get, and some recipes may only be obtained through difficult quests.

So if a finished product has the same value as its ingredients, what is the motivation to craft it? Why not just sell the raw ingredients to a vendor for the same price and save a lot of trouble? This is why I would expect crafted items to actually be worth more than their base ingredients. Of course I don’t know if it will be like this, the game hasn’t even gone Beta yet so who knows what Bliz is up to with their economic planning.. :D

By the way, I have thought of a way to encourage movement of wealth from M3 and M2 to M1. That is, to encourage players to hold less gold in chests and in the bank and carry more gold in character inventory. Of course the amount of gold held in character inventory will depend partly on how much gold monsters drop when killed, as this gold will need to be carried back to town and will therefore be in inventory for a while. However, Bliz will want to use monster kill drops mainly as a tool to adjust movement of wealth from Treasury to Players (that is from M6 to M3).

So, to encourage movement from M3 and M2 to M1 I suggest we look at items and services available for purchase outside of towns and cities. Firstly let’s assume that if you buy something from a vendor in town you can either deduct the purchase price directly from your bank balance, or you can make a withdrawal from the nearest branch then walk over and make the purchase. These types of purchases don’t encourage you to hold gold in inventory once you leave town, they encourage you to use wealth that is already banked.

However, if (as Bliz has hinted) there will be services and equipment available for purchase outside of towns (and away from banks), then the more valuable the out-of-town equipment and services are the more gold players are going to want to carry in case they are given the opportunity to make such a purchase far from any bank. For example, it seems likely that there will be trainers who live in the wilderness and offer special skills training to players who seek them out and can afford the training fees.

By encouraging players in this way to carry currency around (instead of banking it all) Bliz should be able to stimulate spending in the WoW economy. Because as everyone knows, money in your wallet is much more likely to be spent than money in the bank or money that is invested. Encouraging players to carry currency around will also stimulate player-to-player trade outside of towns and cities, as players will be able to buy stuff from each other without travelling to the nearest bank.

Also this way players will have gold in their inventory ready to drop (and presumably go back to treasury) if they are killed, presuming there will be gold loss death penalties. By the way, did you guys see the latest tradeskills addition to the WoW homepage? It says "all of these abilities fall under the heading of tradeskills, and specializing in certain disciplines by selecting and perfecting them could allow your character to seize an important role in the dynamic economy of the world." Just what we’ve been talking about for the last month. :P
