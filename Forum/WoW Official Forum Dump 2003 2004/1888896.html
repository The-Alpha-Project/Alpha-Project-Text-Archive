1. Yes it does seem pretty easy. I wanted to say no, but I think that's only because I'm used to the leveling curve.

2. Priests own big time. Their shield is so overpowered it's ridiculous. Don't get me wrong, I'm not whining, because I play a priest as well, an undead priest. When I play with my mage, and a priest casts shield on me, I kid you not, a mob or player 5-10 levels above me can barely do any damage on me until the shield wears off after 1 minute. Blizzard has stated that they know the shield is unbalanced in the pvp section of the beta boards, and they plan to tone it down I assume.

3. It doesn't right now. There are no racial talents in the game except for undead being able to breathe underwater and them being resistant to polymorph. When racial talents are fully implemented, I would assume that Blizzard will most likely give some kind of engineering bonus to gnomes.

4. Keep in mind that pvp is in its' early stages. A couple days ago, Katricia had a fansite chat which is posted on the beta forums. During this chat session, she stated that their will be lots of support for group raids, zone raiding, and lots of changes to refine pvp. I would wait a while before judging World of Warcraft's pvp.

5. Ok, here we go. Blue items (forget what they're called) are all bind on acquire mostly, except for a few items. Green items are all bind on equip. Souldbound means, once you equip a green item, it has bound to your characters soul (soulbound). Once you pickup most blue items, you will get a message saying "are you sure you want to pick this up? (some message like that). There's a good reason for this too. Lets say you are in a group, and it is your turn to loot the corpse. You try to loot an item and it comes up with that warning message. You take a closer look at the item stats and notice that it's for the priest class when you're a mage. You're like oh, I can't use this and it'll be no good to me since it'll be soulbound once i pick it up. You then tell the priest that this item is bind on pickup for priest, and then you let the priest loot the item.

Using soulbound is good for the economy. It helps keep the market less saturated wtih specific items and helps retain the value of good items. How this works is that most blue items don't even enter the market. IF they're bind on pickup, you can't sell it, making those items virtually impossible to get unless found. This makes that item worth a lot even though you can't sell it. It retains its desire for people. This also makes the other blue items that aren't bind on pikcup, but bind on equip very costly and their market value is very high. Blue bind on equip items are very rare. Green items being bind on equip also helps keep the market less saturated with these green items. Instead of allowing people to continusally sell the same item over and over, this item binds to you when you equip it, which in return takes it out of the market, allowing for this item to retain its market value for a longer period of time.
