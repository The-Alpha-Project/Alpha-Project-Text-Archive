FFXI was an above average MMO, but it wasn't a great one. It certainly was better than most of the trashy bandwagon attempts that have been tossed around lately like Horizons or Shadowbane, and I would put it above SWG on customer service dept. as well.

However, don't go into FFXI thinking you're going to be totally immersed in it. Despite a few cut scenes to give you a feel of being part of the story, alot of FFXI has these little reminders that you're just playing in a digitally created pseudo-universe, and not an actually character who's part of a living fantasy world. It's little things like not being able to swim in water, not being able to die when you fell off cliffs, knowing that there are no evil players out there who are your enemy (I HATE MMO's where you can't choose to be evil or good chars), are things that turned me off from FFXI. Plus the constant and redundant level grinding.

To be truthful, FFXI has a lot of things that made EQ bad, but very few things that made EQ good. Like I said though, it is better then most of the crappy ones that have been released lately.

Personally, I'd recommend waiting until WoW for getting into the MMO bit, but that's just me. If you're still wanting to get your rocks on for online gaming, try checking out City of Heroes. I've been hearing lots of good things about it.

 50. Re: Getting FFXI? | 7/26/2004 8:47:23 PM GMTDT
Ok to start off I played for 5 months with 2 characters, a Galka on the Ifrit server and a Hume Male on the Lakshmi server. Now if you have friends to play with it can be very fun, but if its just you then its just downright aggrivating. I leveled Monk 35, Dragoon 40, Warrior 32, and Beastmaster to 23 for my Hume. For my Galka I leveled warrior to 37 and Samurai to 34. Its fun for a while, till you look around and realize what you're doing. past lvl 10 unless you are a BST(beastmaster) you basically HAVE to party to have any hopes of leveling. As Cavalier mentioned its very camp based, and a party breakdown usually looks like this:

  • Healer (White Mages, Red Mage with a white mage sub is common at lower levels, Summon/White Mage can also work)
  • Tank (Paladin, Warrior, or Ninja)
  • Puller (Usually a thief or ranger)
  • Support (Bard or Red Mage)
  • Damage Dealer x2(Black Mage, Ranger also fits here too, Dark Knight, Dragoon, Monk, Samurai, and Warrior (most effective using Great Axe))

    And if you're looking into it because you love FF, its not really FF. I bought it with the same notion, a WCIII buddy convinced me into it and I came in thinking it was going to be good because I liked FF. Wrong. Dead wrong. The only thing it really shares in common with FF is that its party-based, job names (a lot of the jobs are drastically different) and the creatures (Chocobos, FF summons, etc).

    Things I disliked:

  • Forced to Party- Yep no solo for you unless you are a beastmaster, or else it just goes VERY slow.

  • Chocobo Fees- First you have to get to lvl 20, (and lvl 10 with your sub if you want the invites needed for 20) Then you must sit in Jeuno in a very tedious quest feeding grass to a Choco. Once that pain is over they don't give you a choco, they just give you a license to rent them for a fee.They vary from the amount of business they get, and a lot of the time are about 500 gil on average. (Jeuno they've reached about 1100 at most)

  • Subjob- Once you hit 18 you need to turn in 3 uncommon drops off 3 different mobs, depending where you do the quest. Turn them in and you get the ability to have a subjob..sounds cool at first but then (yay) you get to go level a new job! After that you have to level it to be at least half of whatever job is your main or else you can have trouble getting invites. Also, you are pretty much forced into having a /war sub if you are a melee, /smn if you are a whm, etc.
    Idiots- Depends on the server you go to, though they seem to be everywhere. Morons who name Mithra OPENBETWEEN, Taru who hump legs, oh and the ever popular nice people who train 10 goblins to the zone.

  • Economy-This is my favorite. Low level items in a shop cost an outrageous amount of money (50k for a standard sword?) unless you have high fame. So, enter the Auction Houses. They were a good idea really, but people overprice things a LOT(Some of the items can go up to a few million, but those are justified a bit by the Notorious Monsters you have to hunt for the good stuff). A +1 version of an item can cost x2-x4 times the regular item and its only about 1 point extra damage or 10 delay, an unnoticable difference.

  • Farming- Ties in with the economy, you basically have to either camp a NM(Notorious Monster) for hours hoping you can get it before the Thief or Ranger gets there, or hunt dozens of small weak enemies for their drops. The latter is usually better as they're bought constantly for crafting.

  • Square-Enix plays favorites- Its been a common belief among NA players that the company favors JP players above them, turning a blindside to reports of Japanese botting (lots of complaints, check almost any English FFXI board) and rudeness. If reported to a GM, the NA GM's say they can't handle anything dealing with a JP being rude to a NA. However, the JP GM's can deal with a NA player being rude to a JP player.

  • Same Mobs everywhere- Sheesh they barely even change the color, if at all, just new name and level thats about it =/.

    Now after that rant, things I liked:

  • Ability to change jobs at your Mog House.
  • Auction Houses(just the concept, the rest, see above)
  • Linkshells-Nice take on guilds

    To sum it all up..I came in thinking it was going to be great but now I can't stand it heh. I'm glad though as this was my first MMORPG and it gave me a glimpse into the genre.

     54. Re: Getting FFXI? | 7/26/2004 10:05:57 PM GMTDT
    FFXI is a fun game. I got to level 50 as a samurai before I quit, played for about 2-3 months if I believe. It is a *very* group based game. about the only effecient times you can solo is from 1-15. After that, everything's grouped based. As others have said, if you got friends that will be playing it with you, go for it, as it's a much better experience playing with people you know.

    The only other thing that's hard to get used to at first is the keymapping. It takes a while to get familiar with, especially if you've played other mmorpgs, but it's not impossible.

    About the only thing I hated with the game was the fact that, though it's slow leveling, you mainly end up fighting the same mobs over, and over, and over, just with different names. It can be very droning at times, and then again, very fun.

    And, if you're a tradeskills kinda person, you'll be *very* busy. The tradeskills in the game are very, very deep, but expect to put a lot more money into getting them up in level, than recieving cash from them. Unless you're fishing. fishing's a good way to make money early on, and thensome.

    Also, the quest/fame system is really fun. Basically, the more quests you do, the more renowned(sp) you become in the game with other NPC quest givers.

    For the few months I played it, I'm glad I did, mainly because I like having a broad spanse of games though. It was quite a fun game at the time, as there was nothing else interesting on the market yet. And again, if your friends will be playing/trying it out, go for it. All the more fun with people you know, and can play/screw around with.
