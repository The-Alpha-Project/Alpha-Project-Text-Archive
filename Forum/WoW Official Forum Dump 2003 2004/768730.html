Hunters pets are permanent, the

Call Pet: skill literally revives your latest pet with a cooldown period if it dies (can even be clicked during battle) or will summon it from wherever you had it guard

but no, I do not think hunters should get a player heal spell, they don't get mana to my knowledge, and shouldn't be given spells unless it's a hero ability, they have the first aid secondary skill like everyone else, it would be unbalancing

they have mend pet skills with different cooldowns for healing their pet if they wish to play a "pet-priest" type character.

they should not get a resurrection spell either (or any spell as I said) definitely not one that affects players, that is the shaman and priest spell ability (confirmed shaman so far via SS's taken at E3, but probably priest too).



There are no mechanical pets, that was a teaser by blizzard that is misleading, if there are, yes it will "stack" with hunters pets, read up on the multiple types of pets in Everquest to understand this concept, swarm pets, and a charmed monster, or swarm pets and a regular pet.

no a hunter needen't be the best at duel wield, in fact far from it.

The way a hunter acts will definitely alter the way he fights, if you're a solo character who's often bologna'ing about nothing, you're probably going to go gun/pet

A grouping hunter may very well (if you acquire good melee weapons versus a ranged one) dual wield and forego nearly all ranged skills (there are a lot of them increasing damage for us)

but your job isn't to do the best melee damage of the group, that is literally your pet's job, pets/mobs are always known to be at least as powerful as the players who command them (usually more so, ie: sickly necromancer summoning a golem) although your melee damage is a contribution, you could likely contribute as much, or more, following the "power tree" of skills for ranged damage.
