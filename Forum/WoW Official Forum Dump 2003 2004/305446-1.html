1. Stop with the "Update Forum" posts | 7/14/2003 6:34:15 PM GMTDT
We know you would like to see the forums and website updated more often. We can't suddenly start releasing more information just because you want it so. Here are some of the reasons why we do not release a lot of new information

- New info is usually given to gaming magazines first since they reach the largest population. Magazines will not give us covers or even write articles on WOW unless they get to break new information.
- As I'm sure you have seen, the MMORPG business is very competitive. If we release lots of cool new mechanics and features, then there is a very good chance that you will see some of those new features in other existing MMORPGS before we can even release our game. I have been told face to face from some developers that they watch our website and forums for this very reason.
- Even new screen shots and movies are exhausting to make. The last gameplay movie took over a month to produce and many people on the development team were involved. Every time that movies and screenshots are created, it is less content being created for the game. Creating a screenshot is not as easy as pressing the "print screen" button. We like to stage the shot to show something interesting, not just us fighting some random monster that everyone has seen before.

So...all I can do is ask that you understand that we really do want everyone to know everything about the game as soon as possible, but there are factors that go into these decisions. Once we get close to beta, you will see lots of information released since we know that once the beta goes live, that all the information in the game will leak anyways :)

lastly, all a ton of "update now" threads really do is to push all the interesting discussion threads down to page 2, so don't be surprised to see them suddenly get deleted or locked from here on out.

-Rob

I've mentioned this in other threads, but not too long ago, and we are only talking about 5 years, info was only released about a game in gaming magazines, official web sites told nothing.

SOE was the first to really give any kind of info about their game, and it took several years after EQ's release before they started doing so.

"Regular info updates" is only something that's happened in the past few years.

Go back to about 15 or more years ago and you were lucky to see info about a game more than a few times a year, if at all.

Go all the way back to circa 1975, only about 25 years ago (although that's near the birth of gaming) and if you had a screenshot or some text on a game box you had a serious amount of info about a game. Most games were a plain solid color with nothing more than the name of the game.

Gaming mags that gave previews weren't even really around until the mid 80s, and even then there were only a few. Previews were still relatively unheard of until the later 80s, and news websites didn't start showing up until the early 90s, just about 10 years ago.

If you need regular feedings of info about something, like a little chick in a nest peeping for food every 5 minutes, something is wrong.

There is at least 6 months to release, calm down.

I would love to see more info too, but I'd rather they spend their time making the game and have it even just 10% sooner than to see a new screenshot or skill every few weeks.

Go ahead and look at other games. I've got my eye on about 6. Of them, only 2 update their official site, and the top one is here for WoW, the 2nd under that is EQ2, which hasn't been updated at all in about 3 months, which is the 2nd update they have ever done since their announcement of EQ2.

There is a big difference between regular updates, and telling us every little thing they are doing that may or may not be around in the final version / release their full code to everyone before WoW is done.
