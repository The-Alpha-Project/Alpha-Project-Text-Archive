 11. Re: What is MMORPG to you? | 10/21/2004 11:25:47 PM GMTDT
Well to me the RP aspect of MMORPG's seems to be more of an afterthought than anything else. So is PvP. The RPG element in MMO's is totally subpar compared with the single player RPG's out there.

The focus is almost exclusively on fighting the AI and developing your character with lootand experience in order to remain competitive.

The PvP aspect is also subpar because of the focus on character developement and lack of objectives:

1) Because of the fact that you go up in levels over time, your character is either overpowered or underpowered in comparison to most of your opponents the majority of the time. Most of the time, the outcome of a competition between yourself and opponent is determined by your level and gear, rather than skill. It makes for pretty dull PvP.

2) I think having something to fight over is more important than having a reward system. The PvP in 1942 wasn't awesome because you got rewarded for kills, it was awesome because there were strategic locations to fight over in order to win, and you had to work with your team in order to do so.

So far the nice thing about WoW (the only MMO I've ever played) in contrast to my normal online experience (CS, BF1942 etc.) is:

a) Character developement. While it hurts PvP the way it is right now, it's very nice to have a charcter that you can develope and tweak to fit your taste.

b) The community. I can't remember alot of people that I played with in the FPS shooters, but in WoW, you get to know the people on your team. At least you remember the aliases they use and what kind of teammates they are.

You also get to know (and despise =D) your enemies as well.

Where I'd like to see the genre go:

I'm hoping that one day MMORPG's move away from the whole xp + loot model of character developement and adopt a more "real-world" model:

Right now, the Pres of the USA is the most powerful person in the world. But in a 1v1 fight with me, it could go either way. Bush isn't powerful because of the gear he wears and the combat abilities he has, he's powerful because he controls the world's largest army and can launch nukes at his enemies. =D

You could make a MMORPG that is based more on your characters standing in the fantasy world and the resources at his disposale rather than xp + loot, and I think it would be a hell of alot more fun.

The only reason Newbs would be in the woods hunting wolves and stuff would be to collect furs. There would be no classes, instead you would choose a proffession and then perform the applicable actions in-game to develope it. Rogues would join a thieves guild and do thieve-type quests or freelance robberies. Merchants would focus on gaining wealth and power through trade. Mages would join a mage guild and become powerful by training, going up in ranks in the guild and searching for arcane knowledge... you get the idea.

In the end, the people who spent the most time in the game wouldn't be powerful because of their abilities and gear, they would be powerful because they could influence the actions of empires (legit or criminal), have armies and fleets to control and were insanely wealthy.

And finally, PvP would be meaningful. You and your buddies and associates wouldn't be fighting enemy players simply for bragging rights, You'd be fighting for territory, shipping lanes and trade routes, recources and stuff.

Basically, character developement would be totally dependant on interaction with players and even npc's, instead of grinding mobs all day for xp and loot.

Now that's what I would call a MMORPG.
