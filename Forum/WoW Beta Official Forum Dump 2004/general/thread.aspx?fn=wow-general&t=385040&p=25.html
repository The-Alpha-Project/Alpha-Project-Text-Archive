 481. Server segregation is a huge mistake | 10/15/2004 9:22:38 AM GMTDT
Restricting player choice of server is a huge mistake. I am a European player. I have seen the latency differences between my client and my US guildmates' when playing on US servers (negligible to none usually, with local servers to choose from I could have alternates there). I have also experienced being shut into a server along with other language minorities, and being shut off from my guild, friends since many years with whom I always play if I have a chance. I have been in environments where everyone around me spoke only Italian (which I don't) or Dutch (no, I don't speak Dutch either) - will you believe me that I'd rather be locked into a US server?

The billing and privacy argument is weak. Do business - adapt and profit.

For an illustration of how harmful (to the game, to the company and to the customers) this decision of segregation can be, witness the fate of Horizons. 6 months after release, in a game riddled with difficulties, the most prominent complaint among those affected (i.e. all European players and many Asian/Australian players) was still the segregation issue. It was the most frequently quoted main reason for quitting, and this in spite of the EU operations uniformly quoted as being far superior to the US operations. People should have been flocking to the EU servers - but their friends weren't there.

Can you see a message here? Players want their friends!

I'm sure localized servers are fine and good and all, go ahead if you want them. But do not restrict player movement. It is stupid and will hurt your business, more than any similar decision. Social context is everything in MMORPGs.

I sincerely hope you reconsider this decision. It will certainly affect my decision as a customer.
