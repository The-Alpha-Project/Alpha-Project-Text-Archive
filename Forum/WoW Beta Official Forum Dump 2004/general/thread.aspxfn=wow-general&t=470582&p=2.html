I don't know about you guys, but I think the fun I had in Seb was only nostalgic. Over the years i've forgetten all the bad parts and only remember fun community aspects.

It really was great that you "grew up" with the same people there. And during Kunark and early Velious, that was pretty much the only place you can level to 60. So everyone from lvl 54 to 60 was there. I frequently grouped with the same people, made friends there that I still talk to 5 years later. Fighting trash on the way to the camp, and holding it, and then fighting back out. And man was it so sweet when you dinged 60, it felt like such an accomplishment because there were only about a few dozen people who had hit 60.

But there was also the overcrowding and sometimes undercrowding. How many times have you sat at zone in waiting for a priest to appear, only to not find out and not be able to leave since you were a lvl 54 Warrior and would die trying to zone out? Or die at a camp and not be able to find a rogue or a necro to grab your corpse? Or have your corpse sitting at zone in for a day because there's no one to rez it. Or being trained by some nutjob and having to zone out, ONLY to be killed 2 feet from the portal. Also pretty much everyone spent lvl 54-60 grinding there, since there was no one else to go level at the time. 54-60 took around 2 months for the average person to do. Two months in the SAME dungeon, man.

I just remember the good parts, and don't think about the parts that made me want to stab people in the face. Maybe that's why I still remember the experience fondly instead of being bitter. Just something to think about, I guess.

A few other comments. I'm not trying to bump my own thread, honestly.

Firstly, I just now saw this. I think it lends credence to my argument.

http://www.vanguardsoh.com/forums/showthread.php?threadid=6191

Further, currently a huge issue with WoW is that every character looks the same, almost. This is ridiculous. I know this is going to be unpopular with players, but there has to be content locks. There should only be so many people capable of getting some items, so much of the time. Otherwise it WILL lead to this cookie cutterness. If a very large portion of the unique, good items in the game is from non instanced dungeons that are frequently quite populous, it will strangle the inflow of these items into the economy.

For example, one person wants to do one named which is currently being done by others. He doesn't want to have to try to find it when its open, so he just does not bother with it. Those who do have patience and wait til they can get a piece of that area will get their item, and will be distinguished in a small way from others. Enough of this and you will have a much more item diverse populace.

And I don't mean wait as in 'wait around for an invite to group.' I simply mean some people will leave this entire zone and not bother with it, while some will be patient and work on other things nearby until they can be a part of it.

There is something to be said for groups that can survive for 24 hours without having any of the same people at the end of those 24 hours. Groups can replace people who need to leave, which is a major issue right now, for in many instancess, especially lower level ones. If a member of your party leaves, and you don't have a warlock, your basically stuck and either have to wait for another person to run, or leave. A hub zone eliminates this also. There are so many advantages to these zones in an MMORPG, you can't begin to list them all.
