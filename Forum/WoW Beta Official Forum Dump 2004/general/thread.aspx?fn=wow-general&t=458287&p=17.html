Certainly, First of all let me clarify a few things. I understand that, in the classical sense, strategy is not really involved in WoW.

Here is “a” definition of strategy; I’m not saying it is “the” definition:

“The science and art of military command as applied to the overall planning and conduct of large-scale combat operations.”

This is as applied to the military, however I understand that there is a lot of gray area with strategy and tactics especially when expressed through our less than effective method of communicating.

That being said, typically, in the classical definition, strategy is only what happens on a large scale. But since there is technically no “large-scale” in WoW this is how I relate strategy and tactics towards games such as WoW. Again, I understand this is debatable so don’t get hung up on the classical definitions of tactics and strategy or you will not get the point I am trying to get across:

In WoW, I consider strategy to be basically the planning and preparation that happens before an engagement. I consider tactics the maneuvers we make in the engagement.

I’m also not saying WoW is completely devoid of tactics but I do believe that the speed of combat makes tactical choices (maneuvers we make in the engagement) matter less than planning and preparation that happens before an engagement.

In regards to the topic of “hiding race/class info” it’s not that I think hiding this info will create more strategy but rather that the scale will be tipped more towards strategy than towards tactics.

For example, when there is a warlock on the field alliance must be concerned about AE fear. If Alliance doesn’t know there is a warlock on the field until he casts AE fear it is too late. There is not enough time in WoW combat for many tactical changes to be made during the engagement. If you do not know what you are facing, with the amount of time the engagement is going to last, there is even less chance of changes to be made during the engagement.

Additionally there is something else I said in another post, which is pertinent to this:

Q u o t e:
I’m not saying this is the case for all games (that showing level and class is a requirement for tactics), I am saying that in the context of WoW it WILL reduce the tactical element.


In certain systems not showing the level/class of a character will actually create more tactical combat (by the definition I am using for tactics as it applies to MMORPGs). However in the context of WoW it will not. This is much the same as why you cannot globally say “looting must be included in PvP” because looting CAN work in certain systems but not all systems.
