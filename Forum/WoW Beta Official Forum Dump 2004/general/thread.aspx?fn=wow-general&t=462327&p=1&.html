 3. Re: ./suggest Idea for Vendor Trash | 10/21/2004 6:55:28 PM GMTDT
Two things. One should be that for a tradeskill repair, it shouldn't be a secondary. Repairing should be part of another skill, or a primary in and of itself.

The way you listed the repair kits, it looks like Blacksmiths get the most benefit (by far) and Engineers get the shaft. Plate/Mail wearers tend to get beat up and need more repair, and blacksmiths have 3 corners of the market. On the other hand, ranged weapons almost never get damaged.

I think either the work should be spread evenly, or one trade gets the whole market, making that profession all that much more appealing. I'm not sure though, just my thought on work spreading.

As for repair kits. This is an excellent idea. It still puts the money sink in place (since you can't sell the vendor trash you destroyed in making the repair kit). Perhaps the repair skill can be a sort of disenchant function, where the item is destroyed and you get the parts for making repair kits. Better parts from better items for better kits.

One last thing. Since repair is supposed to be a money and not a time sink, repair kits should stack very high. Something like a heavy repair kit can be made from very valuable vendor trash (averaging around 50s in trash components per kit), returns 35-45 durability to its item, and can stack to a maximum stack size of 50 or something. This way, the kits are more expensive than going to the repair vendor, but are also MUCH more convenient.
