Yes. I actually talk to a druid friend of mine I group ALL the time with. I like druids..don't get me worng..but after talkin to him I feel as though both ROGUES and DRUIDs are shafted in this game.

Why? Neither one has a defining ROLE in a GROUP.

Mages: outdamage rogues, better CC
Priests: outheal druids, better CC

A druid cannot replace a warrior with bear form.
A druid cannot replace a rogue with cat form.
A druid cannot replace a priest with healing.

A druid is really only useful for secondary healing..ie keeping the priests alive and filling in for healing every now and again. But why take a druid of a priest? No reason.

Same thing with rogues. Why take a rogue when you can take a mage who can AOE, do as much damage, and has poly for better CC?

It sucks. Both should have a defining role in a group. Not one where the group can't survive w/o them mind you, but one where people would say "Hrm...rogue or mage? Either works" "Druid or priest? Either works".

I'm not asking druids or rogues to be the same as priests or mages..why have classes if that were the case? But lets put things into perspective:

Why have more classes when the 3-4 primary ones do the job better? Why not just play a priest? Why not just play a mage?

Yes I know they are different and there are more things then just healing or straight out DPS.

But this is what I suggest for DRUIDS:

1) Give them a CC spell that is the best in the game. Keep the others as is...but make the druid the best CC in the game. That way ppl will say "Druid or priest? Well druid can heal pretty well (not as well) but has better CC..so they balance out".

I think if given a GREAT CC then that will make druids wanted in groups.

I won't even touch on the rogue thing, other then the simple fact that both rogues and druids seem to be in the 'same boat' when it comes to groups and the roles we both play.
