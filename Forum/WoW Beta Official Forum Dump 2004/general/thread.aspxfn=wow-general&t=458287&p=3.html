The problem with "masking" classes is that it benefits certain classes over others. Specifically it benefits the "fighter" classes. How?

1) Fighters (warriors, rogues, paladins) can hide their armor by wearing Robes, so they "look" like casters. But, the reverse isn't true. Casters can't wear armour that "looks" like plate.

2) Certain classes, like Druids, Shaman, Warlocks, and Hunters have class specific traits that identify them. The ones most effected are Warlocks and Hunters because we have pets. It's easy to peg us. Druids and Shaman's have the same problem, but to a lesser degree with their forms.

So, who does this change benefit? Rogues, Warriors, and Paladins primarily. They can "feign" their class look. Give a warrior a staff and a robe and they looks a LOT like a mage.


Now, if you could make all Warlock pets invisible until attacked and make it so we can wear armor that Looks like other kinds of armor, like Leather and Plate, then you might have a possible change here. But, I don't think any of that would happen for other reasons.
