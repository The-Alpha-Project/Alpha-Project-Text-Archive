Whisker is easy to pick, with nearly no risk to the herbalist, however (showing up frequently near level 35 mobs), same with Blindweed. I can pull 20 blindweed/whisker in about an hour on beta2 while reading a book.

Herbs are free, we're paying for the cost of the vial. As long as you sell for more than the vial is worth, you are making a profit. Now, beyond that you're placing a price on your time spent collecting. I don't value my time at 20g/hr, so I'm not charging 5g/stack for Greater Int. Since I can do other things while farming herbs, or simply sell what I have leftover when I'm in the area and pick them.. I make a healthy profit, my potions ALWAYS sell (because 1.5g buyout for 5 Greater Int is something people will pay, and quickly). Now, if you want to use beta1's inflated economy as your pricing scheme, fine. On beta2, Truesilver Bars don't sell for more than 1g/bar, MAX. At the same time, they were going for 3g/bar on beta1. Too many 60s willing to inflate the value of everything by buying for alts/powerskilling, and having enough money to make MATERIALS worth a lot, but PRODUCTS not worth much at all.

Personally, I charge the default auction value of an item, with double that as the buyout. That will always be my pricing scheme, if I feel an item doesn't move enough product (arcane potions, for example, aren't valued high enough and don't sell fast enough to make), I don't make them without a commission. But why should I screw over fellow players, unless I want to make the economy inflate to a point where due to me charging 5g for Greater Int potions, I then turn around and need to pay 5g for Runecloth Gloves (which I bought for 1g.. love what happens when NOONE gouges.. we pay reasonable prices all over the market, because you don't NEED to make 5g/sale in order to afford other player made goods, thus you can afford to sell low)

Attempting to insert inflation into an economy for short term personal gain results in long term global loss.
