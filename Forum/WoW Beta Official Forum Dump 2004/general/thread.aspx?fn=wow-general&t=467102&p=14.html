 271. WoW's Death Penalty is Game Making | 10/23/2004 2:53:32 AM GMTDT
When I was in my early 20's somewhere around Ashenvale, I hit one of those awkward patches where my character's power was a bit behind that of the mobs I had to face (Druids get tough around the 20's, 30's etc.) I died for the fourth or fifth time in a row trying to get my corpse back from a spider infested area (the spiders snared). About the fifth time I cracked and started cursing and blustering... and then stopped mid curse. I remembered I wasn't losing XP - four years in EverQuest had trained me to feel pain at each death.

I said out loud "Relax, this is WoW, not EQ. It doesn't matter". The feeling of relief and the return of fun was comical. I was still chuckling at myself when I finally managed to die close enough to the edge of the area to make it out safely.

I /bugged the area (it needed definite tweaking), but was full of praise for the death system. Getting my corpse back cost me time, not time and experience. Because of that, I had fun, not pain.

Huge difference. Please don't change it.
