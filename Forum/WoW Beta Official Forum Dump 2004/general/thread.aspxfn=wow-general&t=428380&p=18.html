 344. Re: Hit or Miss? :) | 10/21/2004 7:13:34 PM GMTDT
There has been no final sign off on this from the dev team, but from the exchanges, it does not seem there is any one issue that this can be pegged on.

It seems to be a combination of things coming up.

1. NPCs can and sometimes will have block and parry skills (usually not both) without the use of a weapon or shield. A successful block/parry in these situations will be reported as a miss.

2. Rogue special attacks have had their range reduced from 7 yards to 5 yards, meaning special attacks will not operate from the previous 7 yard distance, causing long time Rogue players to have to adjust their distance from the NPC before a special attack will hit.

3. After using Charge, Warriors are not beginning their melee attack right away, causing Rage to not build up as it usually does, causing players to believe they are missing. This is a known bug.

4. And of course the Evade issue.

I personally believe that experimenting with your position in relation to the NPC will clear up many of these miss problems players are reporting. (Assuming it's not #3 and #4).

Thus if you are seeing abnormally high miss rates, move your character around the NPC, or try moving closer or further away from the NPC and see if that does not clear up the miss rates.

***** NO! NO! NO! - Tyren please read! *****

You've listed a few mitigating factors, but I believe 90%+ of the problem is this:

******************************
- If the client thinks you are out of range, your attack is reported as out of range.

- If the client thinks you are IN range, but you really aren't and the server rejects it, then your attack is reported as a MISS.
******************************

Mob / character positions are often a bit off from where they appear on your screen. This can result in the client thinking you are within attack range, while the server's data verification (to prevent cheats that would send an attack from a hacked client no matter where you are) shows that you're not within range.

Ever tried to line up your character with another? The two of you will see each other in different relative positions. You move directly in front of them on your screen, and they'll tell you that you're behind them... or off to the left...

Or this one: You're meleeing a mob with another melee character. You have aggro, and you slide your character around to position yourself opposite the other player (so you're in backstab position for when/if they manage to get aggro). The mob SHOULD just spin in place based on your motions relative to where it appears to be on your screen, but instead it makes other odd motions, showing that it wasn't really where it appeared to be.

The server can't realistically send updates every time anything moves a small amount. Not sending constant updates is a beautiful efficiency move when you're talking about things at a distance or playres running across a zone. A meter here or there doesn't matter. But during combat it can be critical.
