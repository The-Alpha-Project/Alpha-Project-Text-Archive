 5. Re: Alternatives to priests? | 10/11/2004 7:57:42 PM GMTDT
Druid: 3.42 hitpoint per mana heal.

Priest: 3.23 hitpoint per mana heal.

The only reason you "need" a priest is because so many foolish players, including druid players themselves, let expectations govern their perception.

Since druids, shaman, and priest will all heal similar amounts with their equal mana pools, the question of the best healer falls to damage. Since the three class's melee damage stacks up as shaman - druid - priest, that's the order in which they are the best healers. If not much healing is needed so that the healer has about 25 mana per second to spend on damage, the priest catches up and all three are roughly equal. But if the surplus is lower than 25, or as it increases beyond it, the druid gains most with their awesome nukes and the priest loses ground quickly with their inferior nukes and melee.

Blizzard has righly caved to player concerns that all 3 healers heal equally. The problem is that they've not redistributed other capabilities accordingly. We come up with the problem that shaman are the best healers, with the most melee damage, with the best nuke line-up, with the most armor, and with totems. Conceptual redesign is needed in order for 3 equal healers to be truly equal classes.
