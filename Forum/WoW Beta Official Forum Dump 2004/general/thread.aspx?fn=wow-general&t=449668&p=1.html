 12. Re: Gamestop claiming NOV 16th?!! | 10/18/2004 4:18:02 PM GMTDT
Hate to play the devil's advocate here...

For one thing... both EB and Gamestop's online stores list the ETA as 11/15/04... which means that yeah... the stores would receive them on the 16th... if it were being released then.

My gf worked in a GameStop previously, and while she was working there, she placed a pre-order on it... this was in 2003... when the ETA was March something or other... then it was May... then it was July... now it's November...

As Beta testers, use your brains... do you REALLY think the game is going to be in stores in Nov 16th? One month from now?

If they started Open Beta TODAY then it wouldn't even be a month long. However, what is the purpose of Open Beta? Is it not to do a final crash course in debugging, by saturating the servers with players who could conceivably pick out any minor flaws simply by sheer dumb luck and probability, while at the same time testing the server systems themselves for stability?

How long do you think Open Beta *needs* to go for, if the Blizz Team feels that the game is adequately developed for release after the next patch or two and the purpose of Open Beta is just to whet the appetite of the general gaming pool, do a final crash test and check the server abilities?

And how much more money do you think Blizzard is willing or able to throw at the server costs before they start bringing in some cash themselves. Let's not forget, while closed beta is costing them money, and it's a necessary and reasonable development cost, Open Beta is going to cost, per day, a HELL of a lot more. The bandwidth costs alone, stretched out over a several month period could be enough to drop the game development into the red... (not saying for sure... but you never know).

Is 11/16/04 LIKELY? I don't think so.
Is 11/16/04 POSSIBLE? Yeah... it is.
Is 11/16/04 PERFECTLY TIMED to come out just after EQ2 and give it a run for it's money and probably thoroughly drub it in the holiday season? Definitely.

Finally... Are the marketing departments of every major software development group in the world so drastically out of touch with reality that they expect their company's video games to solve world hunger and be released a month after development begins? Well... not far from it.
