Some people seem to be suffering from 'selective reading'.

Its NOT Open-beta coming soon.

Its Open-beta signups coming soon.

Many non-mmorpg testers (and even some mmorpgs) might be used to 'open beta signups mean immediate downloads and fast action'. This is NOT always the case. Signups mean taking applications, generating keys, getting an idea of what you've just opened yourself up to (How many servers are we gonna need, guys? How shall we propogate the software downloads?), and THEN, after this and other considerations are taken into effect, do the floodgates open, and the downloads begin...And even that might be 1 week prior to the doors opening...!

Its good business sense to run that in parallel with the end of closed beta...Why have 'down time' from a developmental standpoint when you can minimize it by getting everything ready?

Now, I know many people feel that 'WoW isnt ready for open beta'. Well, sure, in its current form, it isn't. The devs know this, too. Heck, have you EVER known Blizzard to release a game that is unfinished? They are FAMOUS for holding back release dates in order to 'make sure they got it right the first time'. But remember that we don't have a copy of their timetable. (Hey, that would be nice to see, though). We have at least one more patch coming prior to open beta (possible two, maybe more).

And seriously, how many here who are planning to buy in release would WAIT and NOT buy the game in the predicted form (All talents added, active quests finished, etc) at the day of release? Content WILL continue to be tweaked and added beyond release date. Expect alot of micro patches. Expect a few macro patches.

OK, I'm done standing on my soapbox. Well, almost. People, just relax. Enjoy the closed beta (which has to end, eventually). Enjoy the open-beta (which has to start someday). If you have fundamental problems with the game, post your concerns; thats why we have forum access in the first place.
