The problem is that death can be used as a way of avoiding/skipping content.

It also makes you fearless in PvP (if you are in the mood for PvP)...you have nothing to lose.

Some examples:

1) You have a quest to kill a boss (outside of an instance) that is deep in a keep.

You all charge up to the where the boss is, running past all the mobs. You all die near the boss. You all come back and resurrect because there is no penalty.

You just used the death system as a way to accelerate game play. It was a reward not a penalty.

2) You are in an instance facing a difficult encounter with many mobs. You know your party cannot defeat it alone.

You send the priest away a safe distance. Everyone else in the party focus-fires on one mob and kills it..then they all die. Then the priest comes back and resurrects everyone. Rinse repeat until the encounter is more manageable.

You all died, but lost nothing (but a little time). You were rewarded because the encounter was easier using this tactic.

3) You need to kill a boss for a specific drop. You all rush in to kill the boss but are sure to die by all the adds. You kill the boss quickly, loot the head (or whatever) and die.

Again, the death penalty was irrelevant. You accomplished your goal because you had nothing to fear.

Summary: No death penalty means, that people will ignore death in many cases as a means to an ends. In fact, they will exploit the fact that there is nothing to lose and reap the rewards because of it.

There needs to be a harsher penalty than a slap on the hand and a corpse run (or rez sickness).
