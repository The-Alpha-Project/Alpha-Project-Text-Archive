 1. Undead pro's/con's are they worth it | 10/22/2004 5:31:14 AM GMTDT
was asked today if the undead benifits are worth the trad of of having some undead only spells work against you.
i tried to be unbiast at the start but the more i typed the more i relized the undead have it fairly good. for each abality that can be used against them they become immune to two other classes spells. did i miss something or are the undead just whiners?

So what are some other thoughts, i would like to see an undead post as my view is obviously skewed and i would like to report both sides.

here is what i posted, while not the most unbaised thing i have ever writen i believe it is acurate


In my opinion yes.

There are basically 4 spells to talk about.

-turn undead,
probably what undead complain about the most. This is a paladin fear spell that cause the undead to run; it is similar to both the fear spells and intimidating shout.
I think being invulnerable to other fears is a bigger advantage. I mean if it was just a trade of warlocks being able to fear you and paladins not, then no big deal. but you are immune to not only warlock's fear spells but also warrior fear spells, also many creeps can not fear you so questing can be easier.

-exorcism
undead complain about this a lot. Basically it is a high dmg 30 yard ranged spell that a paladin has.
No real benefit here, but what people do not realize is that exorcism is not mana efficient and in most cases if uses it, the paladin will run out of mana. A holy strike is for more bang for your buck. It is also the paladins only ranged spell. Also the range is only 30 yards where most mage spells are farther than that. So it's not a supper long range spell.
The only time I really use it is against some one running away since I have no other ranged attack or way of slowing them down.

-track undead,
great skill and very handy for tracking undead down . however it can be very misleading, I have often saw only one red dot only to find that the undead is in a group with many other non undead people...oops. It can not track sheathed undead characters and it does not tell between npc undead and player undead. Also the range is fairly small, it basically can see as far as I can see, but it does prevent them from sneaking up behind me.

-shackle undead.
This is a priest spell that only works on undead and makes them unable to attack for a short time. This is one root spell as a trade off they are immune to polymorph and sleep. So being vulnerable to one root and invulnerable to 2 roots seams like a good trade to me.

after all that it seams like undead have it fairly good when you consider they can also breath under water and thus have an edge fighting in water as they can simply dive and the alliance can only engage them for short times as that is all there breath will allow.
