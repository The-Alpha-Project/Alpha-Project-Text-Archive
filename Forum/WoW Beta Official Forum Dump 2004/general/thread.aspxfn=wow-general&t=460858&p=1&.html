 1. WOW armor and weapons need addressing | 10/21/2004 6:22:15 AM GMTDT
I am concerned about the item content presently in WOW. It is rather stale, and has grown more insipid as I continue to venture further and further into the world of warcraft.
I initially posted on this very subject several patches ago. Blizzard, in other posts made it quite clear a good deal of item content had yet to be added, and such subjects were being addressed. Well, a few patches have come and gone and while items have been added, the core of my complaint has not been addressed in anyway.

I have now come to the disappointing conclusion that Wow will ship with a lackluster item system. It seems too late in the development process to really change this, though I’m far from distraught. It strikes me that the very nature of an MMORPG is a game of growing content. Still, this is an important issue to me, so I’d like to readdress this issue one last time prior to release.

---original post---

I’m hesitant to bring this topic up. I’m sure it’s been discussed before, ad nauseam, in different permutations. But seeing as this is my greatest concern in regards to the development of the game, I’ll attempt to address it. Simply put -

“I am greatly disappointed in Wows item system.”

Wow items are largely dull, there are very few modifiers, ESPECIALLY at lower levels. There’s no real deep level of item randomization that adds that true level of personality to gear. “Oh look another + X to one or two of my five basic stats,” and that’s about as good as drops get for me. I’m not going to get into the issue where it seems everyone wears the same crap.

Now I am sure it is a difficult process, balancing forged items, with random drops and so on, managing both trade skills and lootable gear, one that is in some ways uniques to MMORPGs. I don’t know if it’s this dynamic that has turned Wows collection of items armor and weapons so stale. But I find items largely boring, in fact exceedingly dull.

Now at this point, although this always ruffles feathers, I feel the need to point out a game that’s item system was bar none, the best I’ve ever experienced, of course - Diablo (I am clearly aware that Diablo was not a MMORPG and thus would not be directly transferable to this system). This game brought me back time and time again. Not the varying skill trees for the available classes, not the few quests per act, or even the horde of monsters with interesting and varied tactics, strengths, weaknesses, etc, it was the item system. I won’t go into too many particulars, but “here is a game that got the item system right.” I mean just a few of the possible random item modifiers: ethereal items, mana/health leaching, socketed items, slowing, open wounds, piercing attacks, knock back, the list could go on and on.

Now one large criticisms of the Diablo system is that characters where largely the sum of their items load out. I know many people don’t want that. I don’t want that. But does that mean that I should never be excited about the random item drops. Cause I “NEVER” am. The only reason I kill creeps is to meet quest objectives or collect loot so I can “sell it to vendors” to pay for my powers. I appreciate that questing is such a big part of this game, and grinding isn’t necessary. But man, killing legions and legions of monsters was so much fun in Diablo. You just never knew what was going to drop. It didn’t matter if you were first level or 90th; there was always some exceedingly useful item to get you to the next drop.

One of the most important points to address of course is that none of my half a dozen characters have surpassed the 31st level. I’m sure that a lot of the items I see on higher level characters, or have viewed on thotbot, will stir my interest. Though as far as I can tell higher level players farm specific items, where’s the high level randomized loot or the varying utility of different unique item load outs that beg for variety? That being said, even if higher levels will offer the sort of item variety that I’m interested in, which ostensibly they don’t seem to, why does it matter that my characters are lower level? Should I not find the loot wearable at my level to be satisfyingly unique with some degree of utility? While the high level loot maybe as exciting as Diablo’s uniques, sets, rares, crafted, jewels, runes, rune words, etc… (Though I find this tremendously hard to believe), what about the lower levels!

There’s a lot to keep me coming back to this game, but it often wears quickly
Enemy AI is often largely flat, though I often find enjoyment in refining the combat process. Quests lose there charm the second, third, fourth time through. And while characters offer a lot, they could be so much more with an interesting and unique weapon and armor lay out. I really want to see a weapon system that offers a vast array of possibilities, one that characters aren’t entirely dependant on, but adds the kind of spice Diablo is so well known for.

If any players can assuage my fears, please do so. Tell me how items are of interest prior to level 30, or tell me that the higher levels present a system of great variety, one that keeps you guessing and has you just itching for that next item drop.

My final comment, this game is a blast, and highly addictive. But as is the case in a Beta, it is what I perceive to be its flaws that I am obligated to write about. And here I face what is clearly one of my favorite games, one that has much more in store for me. And if they could address this issue, on a level that is feasible for an MMORPG, I can’t imagine a more addictive experience.

---end post---

Quite simply by widening the base of item modifiers you can achieve greater variety of armor and weapon load outs. Thus, adding real flavor, something that accompanies player choice. Not a system that forces the player to choose the best loadout for their class, but one that suggests there are different loadout that would offer distinct disadvantages and advantages at any given time.

And before you comment please remember

This post is not about Diablo, please don’t make it, when I originally posted this several people felt they needed to reiterate what I’ve already conceded. They are two distinctly different games with different limitations. This post is about WOW and how it falls so painfully short of what I know it to be capable of.

I’ve continued spending most of my time among several different character classes, and I’ve really enjoyed the taste that this beta has allowed me. But in my experience nothing has changed in the item system fundamentally since I began my beta voyage. I’ve even spent a great deal of time inspecting high level players to see what I had to look forward too, as well as perusing thotbot, and I’ve got to say, it doesn’t get much better.

 18. Re: WOW armor and weapons need addressing | 10/21/2004 11:48:16 PM GMTDT
I do see your point, and I also agree that there is a lack of interesting equipment.
However, as for the reason, I'm completely on the other side. Namely, I believe there is such an insane amount of random drops that it takes away the uniqueness of items.
A little example:
There's a monk's cloak of the monkey, monk's cloak of the gorilla, monk's cloak of the wolf, monk's cloak of the bear, monk's cloak of the whale.
The list goes on and on.

You could say there's a large variety of the exact same item, which is boring. It would be far more interesting if there was just one monk's cloak with a unique look and a unique set of stats bonuses. These bonuses can include pretty much anything, not just ability score bonuses, armor bonuses and resistance bonuses. They could have item-cast abilities (something which made EQ items interesting), boosts to class abilities and spells, bonus proficiencies/skills/talents, etc.

IMO, there should be less magical items than there are now and they should be more rare and harder to come by. Normal items should be more common (doesn't mean there can't be some difference in stats in them). When someone is using one of those rare magical items, people should go 'ooh!' and 'ah!'. Not 'hey, I got something similar with different stats'.
Most of the non-quest items are largely random and totally meaningless. Pretty much any humanoid of level X can drop a monk's cloak of the monkey or some other animal. There is no thought behind it why that NPC would have such an item. It's just an item to be an item, an item to boost stats. Meaningless.

When in EQ, if someone got a certain item you knew he would have been through a specific hell-hole in the darkest corner of the world to get it. In WoW, the item could come from anywhere, and the only factors involved in getting it would be killing a monster of a certain level and being lucky. This is uninteresting to me. Items dropped should be dependant on more than that. Things like the NPC's race, faction (like the Defias) and area should be taken into account.
Yet another point to make, is that items can be worn by too many. There should be more class and race restrictions on weapons. This would make more distinction between classes. Different options to use items would be a plus for me.

All of those things put together, you'd get something like this.
The monk's cloak of the owl is unique in it's kind. In looks, in stats and in name. This type of cloak was traditionally worn by the monks of order of the owl, and only those with spiritual powers would benefit from it's magic. Their monastery was based in one specific area, but it was cursed and now the monks are all undead. Many of their cloaks have been torn through the ages and became worthless rags (a common variation without any magical benefits), but in some rare occasions their magical powers remain. If a priest, druid, shaman or paladin ventures into the ruins of the monastery and is lucky enough to find one of these intact cloaks and equips it, he will benefit from enhanced stamina and spirit and he will enjoy an increased effectiveness of his healing powers.

Of course, for all classes there should be plenty of choice in items of the same level, so that people could choose their own preferences in looks and stats. But it would really help the game a lot if items were more specific and meaningful, not just dull random variations of the same item found everywhere around the world.
