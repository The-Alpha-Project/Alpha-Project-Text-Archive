Eh, the Druid is the equivalent of the FFXI RedMage. When that game went live, RedMage was pretty useless in groups, because they couldn't nuke like a blm, or heal likw a whm, or attack like warrior. They could debuff well, but that wasn't enough to invite them. They were only accepted to parties if the group was desperate to find their 6th member and get going.

That all changed when they gave them two very special abilities unique to only the Rdm class. One was swapping your mana pool and HP pool every 10 minutes, the other being a casted (self and others) MP restoration spell. This made the class viable, and people actually started inviting them.

I'm not saying I want this game to be like FFXI (I hate that game, btw), but adding some special ability to the Druid to give them that final "oomph" into usefullness is really needed. They need 1-2 new important, useful spells/abilities that you can't find in the 4 classes they emulate. Until this happens, they'll always be a gimp in groups.

PS: These kinds of discussions were prevalent in the JP FFXI forums when the game was first coming out too, people saying that they pretty much could fill any role, so they shouldn't get unique abilities. That side quickly dimished when the game hit live status and people realized just how useless they truly were. Blizzard will end up adding to this class 2-3 months after release, mark my words.
