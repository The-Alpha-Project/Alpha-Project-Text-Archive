 1. Talents I'd like to see removed. | 10/23/2004 7:16:49 AM GMTDT
These are things that I don't like being talents, and I think aught to be removed. My reasoning behind this is I don't want talents to hyperspecialize characters, to the extent that if you aren't specced in something, you can't do it worth a damn.

I don't like talents that just simply increase effectiveness of some aspect of your class. Talents, to me, should give new abilities, or new effects to existing abilities, not just increase damage/armor. If the aspect of the class is lacking without these abilities, then maybe the ability should just be folded into the class by default. Here are some examples from the druid lines that just need removed, replaced, or added into the class by default:

Improved Moonfire - 5/5 points
Increases the damage and critical strike chance of your Moonfire spell by 15%.

Moonfury - 5/5 points
Increases the damage done by your Starfire and Moonfire spells by 10%.

Predatory Strikes - 5/5 points
Increases the Attack Power bonus from your Cat form ability by 20%.

Strength of the Wild - 4/4 points
Increases your Strength while in Bear or Cat form by 12%.

Improved Healing Touch - 5/5 points
Reduces the mana cost of your Healing Touch spell by 15%.

Gift of Nature - 1/1 point
Increases the effect of your Healing Touch, Rejuvenation, Regrowth, and Tranquility spells by 5%.

Improved Rejuvenation - 5/5 points
Increases the effect of your Rejuvenation spell by 15%.

Randomly browsing through the warrior trees, here are a couple from there:

Anticipation - 5/5 points
Increases your defense skill by 10.

Shield Specialization - 5/5 points
Increases your chance to block attacks with a shield by 5%.

Toughness - 5/5 points
Increases your armor value from items by 10%.

I'm sure there are examples like this in all the talent trees, and I just don't like them. For a druid, if I don't get the balance line, my nukes just aren't up to par. Talents like improved starfire (which gives a chance to stun when casting starfire) don't make a balance specced druid just plain better at nuking, they make him more versatile when nuking. But, if I balance spec, then I don't have predatory strikes/thick hide, and my cat form damage and bear form armor just plain aren't as good as they could be. Gear can already seperate a tank warrior from a damage warrior, but you can at least change that. Come retail, you won't be able to, and non-defense specced warriors will probably have a bit of trouble tanking at the same level.

But, if all those abilities were replaced with something that gave an effect, or ability, instead of just more damage/more defense, all your other skills would still be just as useful as those you talent specced. Just the talent specced ones would have more utility, have fewer weaknesses.

Anyone with me on this count?

 7. Re: Talents I'd like to see removed. | 10/23/2004 7:55:49 AM GMTDT
The entire point of talents is to allow you to specialize your character. My ice mage shouldn't be able to throw fireballs as well as a fire mage. It shouldn't even be close. It should be to the point that I only leave the Frost spells in special situations, like against cold-resistant monsters, and I'm less effective during those times. The reason for this is because diversity makes the game more interesting, and having different abilities from the guy standing next to you (even if he's of your same class) creates diversity.

So I don't agree that talents shouldn't simply increase effectiveness of some aspect of your class. I would, however, agree that there shouldn't be talents that you must take. There should not be a "anyone who doesn't take talent X is gimped and undesirable in parties" situation. The solution to that isn't to remove talent X, it's to make talents Y and Z just as good.
