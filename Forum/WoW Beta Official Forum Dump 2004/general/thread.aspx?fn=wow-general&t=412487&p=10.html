Alright, I'll try to make this post clear and un-biased, and in a way that everyone can understand.

This is my take on item durability:

First off, item durability is not yet tweaked. The complaints that seem to be coming from people are that
a) items lose durability too quickly
and
b) repairing items costs too much.

a would be considered a problem if you have to go back to town JUST to repair items. In my play experience, I have never had to do that post-patch. I am always going back to town to return a quest, vendor items because my bags are full, craft, etc. When item durability is tweaked, I'm pretty sure this will be the case for all. Just fix your items every time you go into town, and it should be a non-issue.

a and b would be considered problems on the money-side of things. Once again, values will get tweaked so that the intended effect of taking money out of the economy without being too much will take place.

When item durability is implemented AS INTENDED, it will not make the game any less fun. You simply click a button at a vendor to repair all of your items while you are making an already necessary trip to town, and you're good to go.

Unless the split-second (perhaps seconds for those of you with poor mouse skills) it takes to click the button reduces your fun level, it won't even matter as far as fun is concerned.

When beta testing, think of how an idea is INTENDED to work within the game and give the developers ideas/suggestions/feedback on how to make it happen. Do not say "this system sucks, remove it now." If a system gets tweaked and still doesn't work, then the development team will probably remove it.

Now, another question people seem to have is "how will this effect the rich more than the poor?"

Rich players will generally have better gear. Yes, I understand that random purple drops happen, but those are rare and you'll probably have ONE really good item, while a rich player will probably have ALL really good items.

Great items take more money to repair. If a player has 10 great items, those items take more money to repair than 10 good items. Casual gamers or poor gamers should not get hit nearly as hard.

Poor gamers with great armor are rare. If you go through instances enough to get all the good drops, chances are you've gotten quite a bit of money by vendoring other items from those instances and money drops from mobs. If you play long enough to get great drops for all your slots, chances are you've been accumulating money all that time as well.

The end goal is to have a system that keeps the gap between the poor and the rich at a minimum.

To help Blizzard attain that:

Don't worry about your items going down 1 durability in a 45 second period of time. Focus on how long it takes for say, half of your item durability to decay. If that time requires you to go back to town specifically to repair items before you have to do other things, it's probably too short and Blizzard needs to know.

Casters will take more hits when soloing than while grouping. Players who solo more than group should not be penalized for soloing. Somehow this needs to be balanced, and I'm sure Blizzard will address this.

Thanks for reading this overly long post. If you have doubts about the system currently, re-read this, have an open mind, and think about what the system WILL be and help get it there. =P
