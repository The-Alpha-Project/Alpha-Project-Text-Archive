 1. Scholomance w/ 5, challenging, lacks rewards | 10/17/2004 7:46:15 PM GMTDT
Last night, we decided to give Scholomance a shot with a group of 5. We successfully completed the dungeon, unfortunately I was quite disappointed in the end result.

Our group included:
Myself: 60 Mage
Rytrearc: 60 Warrior
Croner: 60 Paladin
Parl: 60 Priest
Kuradoberi: 60 Rogue

The dungeon isn't too difficult, it requires a lot of communication and trust in your group abilities. The respawn rate is short, so it leaves little room for mistakes. In-fact, one mistake and it's all over. The paladin and priest roles were of great importance on this run, I wouldn't choose any other combo.

The First room isn't too difficult, the task ahead of you, is to clear as fast as you can so you run a lesser risk of adds for the battle in the next hallway.

We moved on to the next area, which housed the dreaded "summoners". This didn't pose a problem at all for us, Rytrearc and I worked together to pull the mobs, I would cast silence (causing the summoner to run forward) then sheep to follow. Should an add res beside us (usually UD), Parl was waiting with shackle and we moved through this area quite easily.

In the next area, we had to face the annoying ice adepts. Silence, sheep, resist aura along with Kura's DPS and we were out of there in no time.

Here's where my disappointment begins.

The Key boss drops a 10int/10stam uncommon shield, quite a disappointing drop for anyone of level 55 or higher.

The Lich dropped a 15 cold resist robe with some added frost dmg boost, another vendor item.

The other bosses leading to the final battle, dropped a piece of a leather set (2 bosses dropped the same piece), one boss decided to have fun and gift us with a rare haulberk which was definitely a great find with awesome mods, but unfortunately it was mail and not plate.

The final encounter was by far the most challenging, as he would teleport one of us to another room. The oddest thing about the strategy we decided on, was that, for the first time, I was begging for the agro. Croner threw me a seal of fury, which definitely made that boss hate me, port me, and we ended up in a successful run.

Overall scholomance is a great dungeon. I would like to see the drops changed somewhat, to be of the same caliber as Stratholme and BlackRock Spire (upper). The respawn rate was okay, it definitely helped in keeping us focused and moving forward.

In the end, the group admitted it was fun and challenging, despite the rewards. But... I will be happy to forego another 5 hour run in this dungeon with a group of 5, and settle for a raid group, which cuts the time in half. It's not like I have anything to lose, I completed the quests in last nights' run :p

Incidentally, I was messaged a lot last night, during our encounter, others questioning our choice in party members. Warlocks wondering how we could possibly do this without a soustone, and rogues wondering why a paladin and not another rogue, mages as well.

My answer? We had the best group for the run. Croner was a life saver (literally), which resulted in a no-wipe situation. Without a knowledgable and quick paladin, this would have ended up in a wipe. Another important part for the paladin, his ability to throw agro my way, so I could bait the teleports from the final encounter leaving our healer safe and the others to continue the attack. Parl was quick with assisting crowd control, there were many times, where Parl had to play the main role of CC, definitely a challenge for the priest.

I'm sure others who have completed this dungeon with 5 people may have a different strategy. But this way was definitely the win for us.
