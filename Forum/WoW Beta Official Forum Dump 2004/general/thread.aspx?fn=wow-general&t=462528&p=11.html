This entire thread is a fantastic read and I wish it were stickied.

As a part-time uberguilder/part-time casual player during my EQ career, my initial reaction to Fires Of Heaven in a tag was negative. Not in that I thought the original poster was an evil person, but that I believed I knew his outlook on any game (must.. take.. years.. to.. level.. and nobody but me and my pals with 100hrs/wk to raid should have anything good). Often, that perceived/presented concept of 'my way or the highway' would result in an angry, abusive poster dead-set on trashmouthing anyone who disagreed (as evidenced by any number of EQ guild/server boards).

I've ended up refreshed and encouraged by Kruegen/Greuken's attitude in this thread, and I'd like to thank him for taking the time to offer the initial post as well as keeping a somewhat even keel in the following responses. Your representation of your guild and, indeed, the high-end EQ mindset in general is to be commended.

Adding my vote on the topic: I'm a fan of the ease of soloing, but understand (moreso now) the lack of grouping challenges. As the sole beta tester of a large group of EQ players, set to the task of review as to whether we end up in WoW or EQ2 or what-have-you, I've had to note severally that our original EQ setup will afford, in WoW, an absolutely ridiculous level of ease for what content (<40) I've covered so far.

I don't believe increasing the difficulty for the soloer accomplishes a balance for groups any more than I believe balancing classes in PvP helps the natural PvE player (it's quite the reverse).

Someone mentioned D2 'smart' AI vs. groups, in that the enemy adjusted to suit what was coming at it/how many were in the game. If doable, this is perfect.. coupled with EQ's group xp bonus style of cooperation reward, it seems capable of covering the vast majority of the issues.

I think that open beta will serve a purpose beyond marketing; if the majority of the new players level at a blinding rate, I believe the overall experience gain will be adjusted downward for release (assuming ob/gold don't coincide, as has been suggested in a pure marketing scenario). Should this watermark translate into the solo/group environment, perhaps more attention will be paid to that aspect as well.

It's better for a gaming company to be able to offer improvements/bonuses/perks in patches and expansions than it is to have to nerf severally out of the release gate. Rather basic, that.

Thanks for reading, and thanks again to K/G for his calm and intelligent discourse.
