 221. Re: Suggestions To Fix Item Durability | 10/16/2004 6:15:38 PM GMTDT
After reading through an amazing number of suggestions on durability, a couple things come to mind.

1. Everything tends towards chaos. Aren't the laws of thermodynamics wonderful?

I have zero problems with items wearing out and even breaking. However, I would like to see a person with the correct tradeskill to be able to repair/maintain items for a percentage cost of having a master/vendor fix the items. Say Thud the tauren shaman takes leatherworking so he can be self sufficient and make his own armor. Shouldn't he be able to get a field repair kit and fix his leather armor? If he has quality 40 pants, maybe they would be 38 or 36 quality after he gets done, or he can have a vendor repair it and keep the 40 quality at additional expense. This also increases the out in the field value of skilled artisans.

2. TAXATION - The rules of governing bodies says that those in power will tax those out of power in order to provide security for said people. And those who tax the peons too much cause revolts.

Perhaps there should be taxation (money decay). For those who are rich, get on the cash hamster wheel and pony up some gold for the war effort! Taxation should have enough of an exemption so that a person can purchase the items & things they need, but face it, once you get to max level, and have ubertastic epic items in each slot (example - EQ Time gear), and you are trained to maintain your items, all you are looking for is cash to buy the velociraptor mount, or whatnot. I think that taxation is MANDATORY to prevent macro program users (They exist in ALL MORG's) to abuse the game like the IGE and YANTIS macro users have done to EQ and SWG and many other games.

One final thought... If flight is a tedious thing (and 5 minutes to get from point a to point b can be) then add a money sink of teleportation. The rich guys will pay the fee to save downtime. I like the hearthstone idea (with a half hour cooldown though), and the suggestion of a return stone was also excellent...

FYI - The ABSOLUTE BEST travel system I ever saw was in Asheron's Call 1. You had 2 town bindspots, and 2 dungeon bindspots. The benefits of this were extreme... You could be a casual player, log in for 20 or 30 minutes, dive into a dungeon and enjoy the experience and port back to your own apartment/cottage/mansion, log out and resume real life.

Compare this to DAoC that required a 10 minute ride (25 to 30 to cross a country) to get anywhere or EQ that requires significant amounts of time to get anything done.

I think that if magic exists, some wonder-mage will have figured out teleportation. If implemented, I think that it can be tweaked to provide convenience without sacrificing gameplay & balance.
