I think druids are great as secondary healers, and some can be very capable of primary healing if built around int/spirit such as items of the owl.

I can heal most groups that have a decent tank and setup, but if you ask if I am even 3/4 of a priest 3 levels below me, the answer is HELL NO. But, if you have no one around, then a good druid is fine to a degree.

To get back to a group config that is often used in instances... War/priest/Pali/Mage/Rogue. This is typical, however, you can replace a druid with the pali or rogue just fine, since the druid can heal better than the pali, and the druid has MOTW which is one of the best buffs in the game, if not the best.

I choose not to tank as a druid, but some of my fellow friends are excellent tankers, I dunno, I enjoy healing. One last thing about my druid, is that I have high level engineering, and during the last patch, I was able to self rez, and then use Goblin jumper cables around 30 percent of the time to save a raid hehe. Now, since rez is lost, if I live by sneaking safely, I can still use em.

So even tho we don't have a rez like a pali or have seals, we have a great buff and can easily replace a pali in a good group as secondary healer/buffer/nuker.

One last thing on this and this in no way suggests anything against other classes such as locks or hunters, but Blizzard has done an amazing job with all classes, IMHO, so that you can substitue that "typical" group with almost any config now with good players and make it work.

Heck we did a temple run the other day with my alt mage and flew through it including Eran. The group config was as follows:

War 52
Mage 55
Priest 56
Priest 58
Warlock 55

Worked like a charm. I know this tangents from the main topic, but I always like to show how almost anything can work when people are good players and know how to work well in groups!
