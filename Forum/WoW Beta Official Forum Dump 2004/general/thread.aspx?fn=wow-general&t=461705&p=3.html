What I see is druids saying they cant get groups and other classes saying they love having druids in groups who are willing to fill the role that is needed (often times healer).

One of the bonuses of being a versatile class is that you can get groups easily because you fit the "versatile" role. You help things go more smoothly. Only desperate groups will invite you as primary healer, tank or DPS.

If you are a druid and you always want to get in a group as a tank and never can then make a warrior.

If you want to join a group to fill whatever role you "feel like" for the day then this is a problem with your attitude, and not something Blizzard can or will solve.

You need to learn that grouping is mutually beneficial and if you won't play the role of Versatile then they will find someone who specializes in what they need that you wont do.

Probably the #1 thing keeping druids out of groups is the attitude that people see in alot of druids that they won't do what needs doing in a group, so even though they might need a druid's skills as a healer, they wont invite a druid because they are afraid he/she won't do their job.

The fact that druids are mostly wanted as healers stems from the age-old problem in MMOs that not enough people choose healer classes.

Druids: Instead of complaining that you can't get in groups as a tank, be thankful that you can get in groups as a healer and versatile and learn to play those roles well so that you can level. Eventually your tank will die from an overpull and you will find yourself in dire bear form and save the shaman to keep your group alive in the instance. Until then, let the warrior do his job and you do yours.

Regardless of the above, stop whining that you can't find a group that will let you do whatever you feel like at the time.

They're good for PvE, hell they're awesome at it (and some people think they're required, too), but in PvP they don't have the tools to adapt, and they have limited options in what they can do.
