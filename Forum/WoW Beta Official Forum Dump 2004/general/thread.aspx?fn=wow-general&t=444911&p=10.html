Finally home, long day at work.

I am not dodging questions at all and will address the questions in this post. Might be true I sound like a broken record, didnt realize it, so I will elaborate on my concerns. As for me being a griefer, not at all, I think ive been very blunt about the way I play and I dont sugar coat it at all. I dont go around looking for low levels to kill, I actually dont look at levels at all unless a group is coming at me. Do I kill lower levels? absolutely we've all done it. Do I go around looking to only kill lower levels? Not at all. To be honest I havent been playing my main at all for the past couple of weeks, lvl'ing up my warrior in Kalimdor.

Now to the important stuff.

Let me start off by giving some ideas that will correct certain problems with the Honor system how blizzard initially said it would be implimented. To summarize what they said; if you kill anyone 5 levels below you, or above you, then you get an Honorable Kill, anyone lower then 5 levels then you get a Dishonorable kill and if you continually kill lower level people you will eventually become attackable by your own faction (i could be mistaken with this attackable thing).

The obvious problems are, can you defend yourself if a newb attacks you? Of course, and easily fixable.

Can you defend a fellow Faction member if you see him being attacked by two peeps? I think you should be able to defend him regardless of your level, the alternative is to just stand by and watch our brothers die. Which would really suck and kill the game in my opinion.

So lets say blizzard impliments that if a Faction member is attacked, doesnt start the fight but is attacked first, then anyone can assist him. This seems like the best call imo. Because if you allow a lvl 20 horde to attack a lvl 30 alliance and that makes the lvl 30 attackable by all other horde, then you will have high lvls escorting newbs around just to get "Honorable kills" as the newb initiates the fight and if the lvl 30 fights back, then hes attackable. Doesnt seem right.

So if you can defend Faction members who are being attacked which is an honorable thing to do, and u do get honorable points from it, it raises a rogue scenario that can be abused.

Rogues will be able to stealth nearby to a lower level and as soon as he is attacked by someone close to his lvl range then the high lvl rogue can quickly help and get an honorable kill. You will end up with rogues atop the PvP points ladder.

The Ganking problem I honestly dont see how it can be fixed. FunHooves said that if you wait to attack a person that is engaged with a mob and is low on health, that it is a smart PvP move. Well thats what Ganking is. And it is what people complain about, it isnt fair, and can be abused to get Honorable kills. WoW will turn in to a game of a LvL 50 watching another LvL 50 or higher from a distance, just waiting till he gets low on health, and then Gank! "Honorable Kill"... Which is lame.... and isnt honorable at all. I think ganking is lame, but there is no need to fix this. The only way to fix this on the PvP server would be to impliment rules like the ones i posted earlier. A rule like this, You are not attackable while hunting/killings mobs and you have 15 seconds after the last mob you killed,15 seconds to regen before you are attackable by a PC. This type of rule would be very lame, and you would have to sit there and wait for your enemy to get ready. That would suck. But is the only way to fix the Ganking issue.




The honor system will never be fair for another reason. Lets say the rule remains 5 lvls below you for an honoralbe kill. What will happen is lvl 40 peeps will go hunting in lvl 35 areas and if you increase the lvl difference for honorable kills, then it will only add to this problematic scenario.

Now what I recommend is implimenting a very complex Honor system. One of Defending and Raiding for points. A system that rewards Higher levels for going to Defend low lvl areas against Griefers. A system that rewards raid groups with Honorable points for attacking relatively close lvl range Towns. The higher the level of the town you attack the more Honor points you get. So you get a raiding system that rewards you more and more as your character progresses in level and skill. In order for a high level to want to defend lower levels who are being attacked, there has to be some incentive, what iff a lvl 60 gets 3 Honor points, as opposed to the regular 1 point he would get for killing another high lvl. Then the LvL 60 would go defend the area. This system punishes griefers b/c they wont get any points if they kill only newbs, and they will also be viciously hunted by other high lvls b/c there is pretty much a bounty on their head, a extra Honor points bounty. If the griefers kill more then 15 newbs in an hour, then they will be trackable by all races/classes of the opposing faction for the next 3 hours, but Ingame time, so they cannot log off. And if griefing means that you will be hunted or if it means that you will have to be afk somewhere and wont be able to play. I guarantee you that it will deter you from wanting to grief.

I see Griefing just like stealing. Its impossible to make something unstealable, but there are steps you can take in order to make it harder to do and to discourage it. That is the approach I think blizzard should take with the honor system.

Possibly the best way to get Honor points should be in Battlegrounds, since it seems like it will be more organized and LvL oriented. Should be relatively fair. Put rules in place that reward players for hunting griefers or higher levels killing lower levels. Maybe have the player who kills a lower level get a + next to his name, so that it indicates that this player recently killed a player significantly lower level then they are, and that you get extra honor points for killing him. Possibly the points should even increase if he continually kills lower levels, so you would end up with a player name like FunHooves +3. Which means that he has killed a lower level player recently. And you will get 3 Honor points for killing him. So if a high lvl passes by Hooves and sees this extra bonus for killing him, then it is more likely that he will be engaged. For players with a + next to their name, anyone who attacks them should get the Honor Point. As opposed to regular Honorable kills which only the person who gets the last hit would get it. It would definetly be best if the kill was given to the person who did the most dmg to a PC.



But the Honor system isnt in yet, so its possible that some of these rules have already been changed by design. I continue to post in hopes that we can continue to help blizzard with their design and giving them ideas and showing them Pros and Cons to certain rules.

Please feel free to post how you feel the Honor system could and should be implimented, and lets positively critique it.
