 1. Instance-only dungeons are stifling community | 10/23/2004 8:09:22 PM GMTDT
Don't get me wrong. I enjoy instanced dungeons, quite a bit. However, the static 'Indoor = instance, outdoor = non instance', while good for continuities sake, is making it hard for communities to grow! I doubt you will find many people who would have ever enjoyed Lower Guk, Sebilis, Velketor's Labyrinth, and a multitude of other dungeons without the sense of community that you got there. There was always multiple groups in multiple parts of the zone doing their thing there.

While I don't think its a good thing to camp one spot, that was the only real downfall of EQ's more commonly used dungeons. Especially Sebilis, with a zone exit in a different location than the entrance. Anyone who cared to and had business belonging there could exit, and yet the zone in was usually safe. And on top of that, you could have one or more raids occuring there at the same time as well, such as one raid doing Juggernauts, behind another doing Trakanon. These mega-dungeons are what built EQ's community and gave it the staying power it has had, over these years, not the outdoor areas, and not the instanced areas.

While it would have to be more beneficial to move than stay in one place, and do so without stepping on another groups toes to be feasible in WoW, this could be a huge improvement over EQ. Obviously there will always be the bad element, the people who will either accidentally due to lack of intelligence, or intentionally due to a slight or just evil tendencies try to ruin your experience, this too was an important part of community building. At the same time, one group might just happen to rescue another, building bonds between them as well.

Further, groups would form and launch INSIDE the zone they wished to do, as opposed to the capitol city for them (IE, location of the AH), further strengthening the sense of being in a live dungeon.

I hope you will considering creating some non instanced super-dungeons in the future. They are the living, breathing heart of a MMORPG, and what gives the immersive feeling of Massive to many, many people. Thank you.

Rombus, its true that Battlegrounds will be a popular, non instanced gathering grounds for many people. However, I simply garner no enjoyment out of team PvP. The only form of PvP I, and many others, actually enjoy is 1 on 1. And due to the very nature of PvP, reputations will not be built on skills PvE, or politeness, helpfulness, or true PvE griefing on the other side of the coin. If there is a large degree of randomness involved, then each area might be equally interesing to groups of a certain skill, level, and the power overall of their group.

I'd like everyone who played EQ to think back. How many reputations were made and lost in Sebilis, Guk, Karnors, Velketors, or the like? Plane of Valor is the worst thing that every happened to EverQuest, in my opinion, by 'opening up' the dungeon concept, removing the nameds, and making high level experience be a thing of outdoor grinding. Thats also when the playerbase went to hell. So many players learned how to play in Dungeons. They made EQ. Nagafen raids? Vox raids? They were icing, Guk was the cake. 40 people could be there and it would not be busy. 30 and you'd rarely see another soul. 18 people could explore the entire zone, 20 minutes apart, and never see each other.

Many examples to consider when thinking about how to possible make this viable. Another thing to think about would be Guk's layering system. 4 wings on the top layer, counting the zone in, with a central hub leading down to the next level, to the exit, and to each wing. And a lower level with a linear progression that would sustain 3 different groups who would be constantly moving. Sebilis was the biggest success against camping EQ ever had, I think. While that is to say, it was only a mild failure, it was still possible to make it very enjoyable with just a little tweaking.
