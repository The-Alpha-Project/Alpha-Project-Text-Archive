i have two things to say, first of all being my opinion on the fears that brought about this decision.

what i think theyre trying to avoid is what we know as a server zerg. all the players overseas and in any foreign nation buying the game online would all be rushing to play on the only servers there are, north american and korean. i know these will be the only servers there are because apparently vivendi cant cough up the dough to handle a worldwide release like almost every other major mmo to date. but its too late to change that now, so oh well.

now, the problem with this, is that when the servers in other nations are ready to go, the foreign players will migrate back to their regional servers, leaving the population on the north american servers deflated. and the last thing they want is to have too many servers and not enough players on them, thats losing money.


the second thing i have to say is my own personal opinion on just how terrible this decision was.

to me, one of the amazing things about online gaming, and the mmo genre in particular, was that it seemed to break down geographic and cultural barriers for players worldwide. i could log in and adventure with someone halfway across the world just as though they were sitting right next to me.

ive spent the last 3-4 years of my life playing mmo's in my spare time, and ive met many people from other nations, some of whom used english as a second or third language, and i feel that these experiences did nothing but enrich my gameplay experience. i am dissappointed, to say the least, that such interactions will not be possible, or at least not be encouraged. to me, some of the 'magic' of the genre would be lost through such a decision.


i am personally "lucky" enough to have all the memebers of my guild living in north america, so this policy will not effect me directly. however, i dont think that i should be considered "lucky" because we have no international members of our guild. i do, however, have many multinational in-game friends, players who have been playing the beta test for months and months, players who youre basically shutting out of the release servers until you guys can scrape together the foreign servers.

i implore you, vivendi, reconsider this. there are many customers (myself not included, but that doesnt make the statement any less true) who are losing their faith in this game over decisions like this, customers who very well may not purchase the game at all. blizzard is a company that has gained the respect of gamers all over the world, over the course of many many years and many successful games. youre on the verge of ruining those players respect for the game, and the company.

to some people, respect is worth more than the cost of the upkeep of the additional servers youll have to create.
