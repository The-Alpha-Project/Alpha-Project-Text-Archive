You all COMPLETELY fail to understand that it is never possible to remove solo advancement in an MMORPG. EQ had part of the idea and WoW more or less the same - advancement from 1-10 is VERY fast. After that point mob progression lowers quite fast. The level 12 mobs you wiped through at 10 wont apply to, say, level 18. At level 18 you'll be killing level 19 mobs. At level 25 you'll be soloing equal level mobs for the same difficulty as those level 12s at 10 or those level 19s at 18. You get the idea.

By level 40 or so you're forced to kill lower level mobs to solo at the old rates, or if you'd like kill higher level mobs for more downtime but equal exp gain.

Thus opening another issue - exp scaling in WoW is VERY low but mob power is very high. Its much more efficient (by a huge margin) to kill mobs within 1-2 levels of you compared to mobs 3-4. The exp difference is nowhere near enough to validate the extra time involved in killing and then resting.

Too many issues, too close to a wipe, far too close to release =(

Making it viable for every class to solo, along with a horrible anti-social quest system. Unlike other games which PROMOTE social interaction, World of Warcraft PUNISHES it in a myriad of ways.

Talents make sense too. When a class has a primary role, wouldn't it make sense that all 3 talent lines further that class role in different and unique ways. The gap is not as large for weak equipped players, but a well equipped priest with Holy Talents in comparison to one with Shadow Talents, there is a huge difference in the amount they can heal. How can you design content that will allow a Shadow Priest to keep a group alive, but not completely trivialize it for a Holy Priest? Groovy.
