There has been no final sign off on this from the dev team, but from the exchanges, it does not seem there is any one issue that this can be pegged on.

It seems to be a combination of things coming up.

1. NPCs can and sometimes will have block and parry skills (usually not both) without the use of a weapon or shield. A successful block/parry in these situations will be reported as a miss.

2. Rogue special attacks have had their range reduced from 7 yards to 5 yards, meaning special attacks will not operate from the previous 7 yard distance, causing long time Rogue players to have to adjust their distance from the NPC before a special attack will hit.

3. After using Charge, Warriors are not beginning their melee attack right away, causing Rage to not build up as it usually does, causing players to believe they are missing. This is a known bug.

4. And of course the Evade issue.

I personally believe that experimenting with your position in relation to the NPC will clear up many of these miss problems players are reporting. (Assuming it's not #3 and #4).

Thus if you are seeing abnormally high miss rates, move your character around the NPC, or try moving closer or further away from the NPC and see if that does not clear up the miss rates.

My theory, which is VERY important for Rogues.
Special attacks now depend on your normal hit chances, IE they are based on your weapon skill. This is a change, and I can confirm with fairly detailed proof this changed. But that isnt the main issue.

The main issue is Rogues dual wield, and their weapons suffer a 20% miss rate tacked on to the normal 5%. Meaning, a Rogue is 5 times as likely to miss an attack, as a 2h wielding warrior. This explains why our specials miss in such high numbers.

We are seeing this as a DIRECT result of the change to special moves hi rates being moved from whatever the criteria was before, to now being based on our weapon skill.

For easy proof of this change, ask a lvl 60 warrior to equip a weapon he has 1/300 skill in, and have him try to disarm, sunder, shield bash, rend, or hamstring a lvl 30 npc. You will find that he will miss near every special attack, but, he will gain weapon proficiency for each attack, which is wholly different than things used to be.
