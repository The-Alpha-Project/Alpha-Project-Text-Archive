No, the problem is all perception. Many sit here and expect each new instance that comes out to be the next upgrade path for their characters. Stop and think about it, though. At release, all of these instances will be open and available. Blackrock Depths, Spire, Stratholme, and Scholomance will be what you gear your character up on in preparation for raid zones and the next expansions

This is all the baseline for the rest of the game to come. At release, you will get a piece or two from BRD, a piece from Scholomance, maybe two from BRS. You will gear equally from all the instances, rather than farming them all to get all the best drops while waiting for the next instance to open, as we have done in this beta.

There is no problem other than the one created by the time bottlenecks we've had waiting for content to come.



I like your reasoning on this... in beta, we've waited long weeks in between pushes/patches for them to release new content, and those at the top of the food chain rapidly devoured new instances in hopes of some new uber loot, but most find the gear to be equal to or only slightly better/worse than other already released dungeons of the same level. In the open game, players will have all the finished instances available and can pick and choose between them, not waiting for each as if it's a progression in better gear up the chain.

I also believe in my paranoid little gamer mind, that Blizz is actually holding back the best stuff from us until they release the game. It makes no sense for them to put the top loot tables into the game during testing phase, this leaves zero left to discovery and luck in release. I am glad they're doing this (if they are) so that I have something great to look forward to when re-doing these instances again the next time around in the real game.

I think the object right now is not to get the top line gear from these instances, but rather, to judge the difficulty level, playability, time factor and possible bugs/exploits of them before release. Yes, loot is the driving motivator, but in beta it's a fairly meaningless bunch of pixels once last day hits and we're all level zero again.
