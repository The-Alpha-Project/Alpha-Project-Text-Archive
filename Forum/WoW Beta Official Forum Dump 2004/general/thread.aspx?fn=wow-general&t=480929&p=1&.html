 5. Re: Why is crafting tied to "Adventurer" level | 10/26/2004 5:53:24 PM GMTDT
This was changed during the move from Skill Points to Professions. In the old method, you would "buy" your tradeskills with Skill Points, which were acquired every 400-600 XP that you earned by killing monsters. There was no limit on the number of Tradeskills you could learn. Thus, in effect you were forced to level up in order to tradeskill. However, the problem was that high level characters would grind away their Skill Points and become one-man crafting armies. This was destroying the economy.

When the Skill Points were removed, we were restricted to only 2 Professions. However, the problem became this - A person could still be a one-man crafting army, except that all of the tradeskills would be on Alternates. The Main character would gather and get money, and then give it to the level Alts who would do all of the crafting. To fix that potential problem, you must now level-up in order to access the higher tradeskills.
