 1. Things I'd like to see... | 10/20/2004 4:25:44 PM GMTDT
Getting closer to open beta and release soon after it I wanted to make a post on things that I would like to see by retail, in the first few months after retail, and in the first expansion.

Before Retail:
Hunter and Paladin talents should be implemented as well as a solid set of pet abilities for both Hunters and Warlocks.

Horde lands balanced to have a similar level progression as alliance lands. Meaning more quests with rewards of similar power for the horde side. Also more herbalism spots in lower level horde areas. Similar to the herb propagation in the lower level dwarven lands.

Graphical updates to races so that such things like a tauren's mane of hair and the undead's spine dosn't poke through their armor and cloak. Also a bit more variety to the male tauren dance.

Broken abilities fixed to match their description such as the Shaman grounding totem.

Paladins, Warriors, and Bear-Druids to have better agro-management.

Some spells should have an added benefit of being 'mana friendly' meaning that mana is still regened while being cast. This is primarily for mana-regen abilities such as a warlock's drain mana and lifetap. This could also be an added benefit on select spells to give them a bit more use over their stronger counterparts.

A frost-talent for mages in the 10-15 required points area that is fairly decent. Having all the very nice forst talents requiring specing fully in frost tends to keep many mages from specing at all in it. Having something for partial frost specs might help out.

Warlock talents need a bit more work. With dark pact as the end talent in affliction many warlocks feel they have to fully spec affliction to be the most efficient. Demonology needs more balancing and tweaking to the abilities but destruction is fairly balanced if much less favorable to a warlock than affliction.

Warlock Felhunter should be added and another high level pet should be considered. Infernals are a signature demon that have been tied with Warlocks almost since their conception. Another rank of a summon similar to imp (but with same abilities, higher hp, and much higher mana and costing a soul shard) would be valuable, it could be effected by the improved imp talent as well.

Battlegrounds implemented and working well. A good battlegrounds set up would definatly make me chose PvE server over PvP. Nothing like all the pvpers going to a few select areas for battle rather than wandering around aimlessly pot-shotting lower levels or higher levles without much organization.

Months after Retail:
A fairly major storyline event roughly every month with added content (some decent some fluff) would keep the World of Warcraft alive. WoW has such a rich history and world that it would be a shame not to do so. It should be detailed and not just a 'GM playing a notable npc and killing players'. The best example of this is Asheron's Call though Final Fantasy XI is fairly good at this to. EverQuest is NOT a good example of this though Ultima Online had some good events.

Added 'fluff' and enjoyment factors such as musical instruments to play and perhaps added dance animations (such as a /dance2, /dance3 or perhaps something more specific like a /funkydance or /discodance). More fireworks and 'fun' abilities and items added to classes and tradeskills. An example might be an engineered disco ball that foars above a charecters head for a few moments as they dance.

Storyline content above and beyond the current quests would be appreciated. 'Missions' from FFXI are an excellent example. They gave players a feeling of being tied to the game and making a difference. Often I did them just to see the next cutscene. Blizzard should also consider using cutscenes for some missions. They pull a person into a story much more at certain crucial intersections. They should not be used often but only at certain important points.

Tradeskills should continue to be better fleshed out and balanced.

Higher end raid instances and events should be added. These don't need to be in by retail since it will likely take a few weeks to a month before a guild has a solidly leveled up base of players. The best example of high end raiding in any MMORPG I have played is EverQuest, I think World of Warcraft can far surpass EQ in this area.

Hero classes. They should live up to their name of requiring 'Heroic' feats. However they should be able to be gotten by any player with the determination and will to do so. (Not like SWG's Jedis, more like the 'Advanced' classes in FFXI, but a bit more higher level than that).

It would be nice of a variable water level system could be implemented in some areas (A tidal system) that varied in tide each day meaning water might be low enough to allow access (without water breathing) to certain areas or might reach high enough to allow swimming to normally inaccessable areas. This could perhaps be done with lava as well. Meaning that when the lava is low areas that were completely inaccessable before would be open. This would be best to test in instances, for lava it might be wise to use a much shorter time than with water since part of the challenge of an area might be to kill the enemies and get across the path before the lava rises back up.

What I would like to see in the first Expansion
I would like to see a pair of new races. One alliance and one horde. A 'large' alliance race as well as a 'small' horde race. The best races I can think of are the Panderans for Alliance and the Goblins for the Horde. Goblins tend to be neutral but with their natural competition with Gnomes it seems like the horde fits them best and it would be easier to fit them to one side than both. In addition it gives alliance a very unique looking large race (like the horde's Tauren).

A new class should be considered depending on how soon after WoW hits retail that the expansion comes out. If over 9 months there should be a new class, if under 9 months then the new class should be put off till the second expansion. It should be shared between alliance and horde. Two different classes one to each faction would be too difficult to balance against each other and would take much more work to do. A new class would add something for players who have 'done it all' to do. As well as allowing lower levels or those just starting to try something the higher levels haven't yet.

A good chunk of new areas to explore, quests and such. A new continent perhaps though not the frozen continant. That should wait till a second or maybe third expansion.
