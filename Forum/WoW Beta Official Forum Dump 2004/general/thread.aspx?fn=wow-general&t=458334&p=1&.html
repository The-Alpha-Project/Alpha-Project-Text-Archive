Interesting post by Sekira on mmorpg.com... I have their permission to post it here..
----------------------------------------------

Firstly, taking notice that the honor system hasn't really been introduced yet, some of the points I am about to make, could be addressed in a future patch. But right now, I am assuming that the current PvP structure will remain the same, only with the addition of honor points.

Currently, there is very little distinction between the two factions, in terms of pointing them out from one another in PvP. But more importantly, there is too much of a distinction within each faction, which in pvp, is the absolute reason why people take part in it. By taking part, I mean acknowledging that you, or your group has the upper hand. This is the main reason why people partake in many battles - the simple knownledge of at least thinking they have the upper hand.

But on to my point about distinction; it is very carebear'ish (as much as I hate the word). There is so much certainty of the outcome, based on current distinctions between the factions, the fight is over before it starts. Ok so yea, what's my point?

Say I am a level 26 Human Paladin in Hillsbrad, and I come upon a level 24 whatever. Now, with the honor system (I am assuming for this post, the threshold is -5 levels before you lose honor), being only 2 levels below me, I would gain honor. But as I said above, if I am going into battle knowing I will most likely win, why would I ever go into battles knowing I would most likely lose? But going back to where I said, "there is very little distinction between the two factions".

Well lets say that level 24 I was talking about is tauren , class _____, name ____., guild ____ .And I think to myself, what's up with all the info? Sure this might be fine for the PvE server... but this is PvP, where both factions are at a constant, all out war. So why the formal description., as though you were describing my buddy dwarf beside me. I do not socialize with the horde, so why would I care what guild they are in?(Would I find their website and taunt them?... or complain about their pvp tactics, no) Or, should I base whether I take part in a fight based on what classes I'd be fighting against, since some classes are harder for others, and vice versa.

No...the point of PvP is to simply kill the other faction. The name(this is debatable), the race(this can stay), the class(definitely should not be in), the guild(this is debatable), and the exact level(to an extent): none of it should matter. The less information given about the other enemy, the less certainty there is going into the battle, and less certainty going into battle, means a greater chance for a surprise or unexpected result - which is what pvp should be about.

Lets say the threshold for getting honor is -4 lvls, and 5 or more lvls below, you lose honor.(note: this can be tweaked to whatever is best)

5 or + lvls below -- cons light green -- you lose honor
3-4 lvls below -- cons blue -- you gain little honor
-2 to +2 lvls -- cons yellow -- you gain ideal honor
3-4 lvls above -- cons orange -- you gain more honor
5 or + lvls above -- cons red -- you gain max honor

So if the enemy cons yellow to me at lvl 26, he could be anywhere from lvl 24-28, and there is no certainty to his level, other than in that range. So there is no way of telling the outcome, for it could either be an easy fight, an even fight, or a hard fight. But it will cause people to take more risks, since they will not know the the exact lvl they will be fighting, or the class (some are obvious, others aren't, until it's too late). Now im not saying that -5 is THE threshold lvl, but I think it would be somewhere around there.

I hope you read through these issues and ideas, and please comment on them. The main point I was trying to make is that the current pvp system is too formal, causing pvp to be unbalanced for the reasons above. If the above ideas I suggested were implemented, I'm sure pvp would seem much more balanced, simply because of less knowledge about of other faction, and theremore much more risk is involved.

-----------------------------------------------



Just a thought...
