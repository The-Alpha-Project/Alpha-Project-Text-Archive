Maybe it could work like this:

1) The hunter would be required to keep the animal as a normal pet for awhile (or a long while depending on the level) until a "mount counter" reaches maximum, at which point the animal is ready to become a mount. Then-

2) The hunter can't sell the mount directly to another player. He has to sell it to the stable master or mount seller or whoever, for a decent price. The mount seller then can sell the mount for normal mount prices.

That way there is still the mount money sink, the hunter gets something out of it (money and new skills learned from the animals), and there won't be a bazillion of the same mounts everywhere.
