I agree to an extent. Lets go back to the eq joat the bard. Ya he (or she) wasnt really great in anything, but a good bard was very usefull in a group twisting songs.


Bards had the same problem at first. Their hastes didn't stack like they do now, so they were useless compared to Shaman and Enchanters, they had little to none of the crowd control they currently have, they had none of the spell damage buffs, and their group mana/hp regen was subpar for the longest time. They had the same problem druids do now...they could do a lot of things, but were outclassed in all those things.

Eventually bards got things that were "unique" to them though. Overhastes made them useful even with another haste caster. Songs like Rizlona's gave them a very unique ability that was pretty handy. Their mana regen songs, which stacked with Enchanter spells to begin with, became much stronger. They were given enhanced crowd control powers to make them a viable alternative to Enchanters. Fading Memories gave them a powerful escape and pulling tool to augment their lull songs and brought them to the forefront of the difficult pull scene.

So, yes. Bards and Druids have a lot in common. The problem is that at this time Druids are like Bards in the very early days of EQ. They just don't have much to set them apart from the classes that they're (poorly) copying.
