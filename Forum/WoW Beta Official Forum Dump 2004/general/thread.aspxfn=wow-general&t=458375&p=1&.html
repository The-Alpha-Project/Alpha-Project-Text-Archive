 7. Re: Cross-racial Factions - Choosing sides in PvP | 10/20/2004 7:06:07 PM GMTDT
Q u o t e:
Do you think it would be a good idea for an alliance character to dub themselves a traitor and move their human to side with the horde or vice versa? The ability to cross factions with another race.


No. First, it goes directly against the lore. The two sides aren't at war because they disagree on ideological points. They're at war because orcs hate humans, humans hate undead, etc etc. The fact that you've renounced your side is meaningless. You're still a human, or whatever other race, and the other side will never accept you. At best, they'd use you like a tool, never giving you something like acceptance, just having you serve their ends until you're dead or you get sick of being used that way.

The other reason is that, while there still are individuals who do reject their side and try to serve the other side in the Warcraft setting, they are few and far between. Again, from a lore standpoint, there are no more than a handful of individuals who do this. Letting players do it would cause many, many, many people to switch sides. The Alliance would no longer be a human, night elf, dwarf and gnome group. It'd have swarms of all the races in it. What would be left would look nothing like Warcraft, as it'd be all the races on both sides against the same races on the other side.

And, anyway, there are other ways to ensure the sides remain balanced. Incentives which shift organically depending on the population distribution to give the underdog an advantage, for instance.
