 1. WoW's solo emphasis destroys group play | 10/21/2004 7:04:01 PM GMTDT
Part of me wanted to write this message, and part of me spoke up and said that it would be a waste of time, as these things are already set in stone. So take it for what it is – a retrospective, not a crusade for changes that will not happen no matter how much I wish they should. Sorry to my friends at Blizzard, but stripping everything bad AND good about MMOG's is not the answer.

Warcraft’s greatest strength is its game play at the individual class level. In most RPG’s – online or off – the individual roles are pared out and dumbed down to the basics of warrior hacks and slashes, priests heals, and wizards blow things up. Single character RPG’s tend to bore me to death for that reason – in Morrowind, you either chopped things up or roasted them from afar. In Everquest, unless you were a leader, you basically just followed people around and mashed a button or three. Warcraft addresses all of this – every class has a variety of methods of performing their role, and most classes mix up their skills constantly. Even though it stills boils down to routine most of the time – mage snares, fireballs, fireblasts, scorches twice, fireblast again, etc – it stays interesting.

Unfortunately, all of this breaks down in Warcraft for one reason: The game is so overly concerned with preserving the sanctity of being able to advance quickly and easily through solo play that the system falls apart in groups. The game has to go so far as to have special areas sectioned off for people who choose to go through the bother of playing together. But what happens when you form up a group to do anything other than those limited, out of the way and time-involved designated grouping areas? The game play goes to crap. Mage nukes – once. A rogue might get off a swing or two. The warrior never gets rage. Priest classes have to abandon healing for their (mostly) sub par DPS. Concepts such as tanking and healing become completely extraneous, and only fast damage now matters. How much mana (loot) a caster has or how great a warriors armor are become completely irrelevant. Simply put, the massive glut of soloable creatures is so stupidly trivial for grouped players that grouping is useless. Why would I add another player while I’m off doing some random quest somewhere, when it means that not only would completing the quest be totally trivial and boring, but also that I’d finish the quest so quickly that I will spend more time traveling back and forth to the quest giver npcs than I would actually performing the quest! It’s pretty odd for a massively multiplayer game to say that grouping is detrimental to having fun, but that’s the case in WoW. Its more boring to group than it is to stand around by myself doing the same actions over and over, mob after mob, for the vast majority of the games content.

And of course, a major result of this is WoW players SUCK at grouping. Most of the time, grouping makes the game so stupidly easy that when a group does finally encounter a difficult situation, they have no idea how to deal with it. You mean we need to all damage one mob at a time? I should save some mana for healing? Resist totems have a purpose? Defense, wtf, that sucks, I don’t do much damage that way. It is incredible how bad WoW players are when they are actually faced with a group-worthy challenge. I DESPISE the vast majority of players that I group with in this game, because the game does not teach them what to do. And this is because most of the time groups are killing mobs meant for soloing – since SO MUCH of the game is meant for solo play, and only solo play. If being able to solo in every single non-instanced zone of the game is so uber important that it overrides all other design concerns, then you should at least have created a system where grouping does not therefore trivialize the hell out of everything. Yeah, DAOC had that bring a friend mess, which I thought was pretty clunky and odd. In WoW that would just translate into AEing the hell out of everything. At the very least, creatures in WoW could have their hitpoints increased (doubled) to the point where a 1 on 1 fight is still soloable, but not three at a time, or ten single mobs in a row without rest. At least this way a group might have time to push two hotkeys before their target dies. (This leads to another issue as well – the kill rate. In many of the cave systems, two players who are not working together can keep an entire population dead before it beings to respawn. This in effect makes the game smaller, as a few players can take up a lot of real estate.) But back to the grouping issue – is there going to have be some weird hack put in place so that grouping outside of designated elite dungeons isn’t so damn dull? Pull a solo mob, it sees there’s five of you, so it whips out a can of spinach and turns into Popeye? I don’t know. But man, is this game damn boring to group in as it is now. I refuse to group people even as my priest if I am not planning on going into an elite dungeon. The only way I can get any challenge at all out of the game is by soloing non-elite quests that are meant to be done in group. That’s so wrong.

As I opened with however, I suppose it is too late. The soloers have won, and the people who play multiplayer games to actually interact and cooperate with other people just have to be happy with their little roped off smoking sections in the corner of the playground. I think it is hilarious the designers believe they can just suddenly throw in raid or “challenging” content at the end of all of this solo leveling and suddenly have players wake up and understand how to work together as a team. You can see how well that works with nearly every post about the higher level instances on this board. Suddenly there is no option of waiting until you are ten levels higher than the intended design to do a dungeon, or of bringing three times as many people. The training wheels are off – and look at the reaction. And sadly these places they complain about are like sleepwalking for people who actually have teamwork experience.

In conclusion, there’s just too much damn emphasis on soloability for you to expect these same players to suddenly work well in a group when they have to. These people are lost sheep when they encounter these places. At the very least, mobs fought as a group should not behave the same way as all these millions of “any idiot can solo me” sissies that the game is full of. It makes grouping too damn boring and non-functional.
