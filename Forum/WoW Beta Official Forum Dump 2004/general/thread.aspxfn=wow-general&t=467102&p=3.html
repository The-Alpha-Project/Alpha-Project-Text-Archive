Q u o t e:
I hate to say copy CoH's death penalty but out of all the mmorpg's, in my opinion it has the best system hands down.

For those of you haven't played CoH, when you die you incur an experience debt. The debt is paid off as gain experience, so for example, you die and are given 1000 exp. debt, the next mob you kill gives you 100 exp., 50 exp will go towards your experience pool, the other 50 exp go towards your debt pool. If you haven't played with this system it won't sound that amazing but trust me, every (and I mean every) person I have ever talked to has agreed it is a great death system. What is nice about it is One, you are always gaining experience even after death and Two, you never go backwards in your experience. An example of what makes this system nice is: Say you are one kill away from your next level that you have been working towards all day, bam you die, well even though you are going to have to work off that debt, all you have to do is kill 2 more mobs and you still level, you don't feel like gee now I have to work another hour or two to get the experience back I just loss to finally get that level.

For WoW, I would love to see them incorporate this system and the only modification I would make is that if you go back to get your corpse you elminate 1/2 of the debt pool or if you get a rez you eleminate some other fraction of it (mabye low level rez starts at 10% reduction).

Even if they don't use a system similar to CoH's system, I beg them to make death mean something, people need to fear death!!


This was done first in Earth and Beyond. I am unaware of any other titles that did this system beforehand. However, they allowed the debt to be decreased by 10% every hour your logged off, until it was low enough, and was completely removed. So I think E & B's system is superior to COH's since they have other ways of removing the debt. It was an alright system, but it is still in fact something that takes away your exp.
