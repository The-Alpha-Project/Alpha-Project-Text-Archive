 1. L2's Farming/EBaying Problem | 10/20/2004 3:21:26 PM GMTDT
So I was perousing the L2 boards (what can I say, I'm a sucker for punishment and I enjoy the anime), and I came by a post ranting and cursing about the rampant CN adena/equipment/ebay farmers (a rant it may be, but 100% true it is).

One person suggested people try WoW if they want to see less of this activity in game. Now their comment was perhaps a single line amongst long drawn out paragraphs of back and forth.

The following posts proceeded with the usual flaming and L2 >>>>>> WoW crap we're all familiar with here as well.

In any case, people would rant about how equipment and gold farming will be so much worse in WoW and how D2 was hacker heaven and so will WoW, yadda yadda yadda.

Well it was quite apparent from the get go that these people don't have a clue about a single game mechanic of WoW. Heck, even I know how L2 works, I followed it for a while, and I played the beta for about 2 months, so I figured if any of them happen to come by the forum I'd enlighten them.

- Gold farming may happen, but due to dungeon instancing, you won't have to see it, or even particularly care about because you can earn your own money easily enough.

- Equipment farming can't happen because all the best gear is Soulbound on pick up, so even if the gosu CN Adena, Inc pro-farmers were to leave L2 and come here, they cannot sell any equipment they farm except for the occasional Hydrocane or Toxic Revenger, etc which are by no means hard to acquire to begin with.

- Forced server policy will make it more difficult for CN's without contacts in NA to play on US servers, but I realize they force servers in L2 as well, so it's only a minor argument.

I very much enjoy the L2 threads because the ignorance makes me laugh, but it's really quite sad that they think the pro-farmer CN gosus will come to WoW and magically cure all their woes, when it's simply untrue. L2 offers farmers every incentive to farm 24/7:

- Ridiculously overpriced gear with very little loot drops, and if the loot does drop, you can't use it because it's below your level anyway.

- A Karma system that prevents legit players from exacting revenge on the farmers themselves without losing all their gear in the resulting feeding frenzy of turning chaotic.

- A TOS that permits griefing.

- Administration that won't kick the farmers even though they say it's against the TOS to share accounts and bot.

 15. Re: L2's Farming/EBaying Problem | 10/20/2004 5:35:16 PM GMTDT
I played Lineage 2 for a while.. and there's significant differences between the two economies. Extremely significant. These differences are what will make botting and ebaying much less likely in WoW.

First off, the ratio of power to currency is MUCH higher with Lineage 2. With WoW, the equipment you pay for is always a few stat points higher than what you could just as easily find or quest for. And even the most expensive equipment you could buy at the time (for your level) is usually only a fraction of what your entire savings are. With Lineage 2, money equals power. there will always be some piece of equipment you can (and will) use your money on, and it will always require days of saving cash to get it, and it will always use up all of the money in your possession. The equipment is necessary to buy, therefore a large income is necessary to have.

Secondly, Lineage 2 was intentionally coded so that people would have to, quote them, "farm" to get your money. They specifically say that you need to fight mobs lower than you in mass amounts. I considered buying Lineage 2 currency off of Ebay because I was soooooo tired of returning to the same area over and over and over for days at a time, just to buy a new weapon.. and I'm a cheapskate with actual money, so that's saying a lot. In WoW, earning money is more of a side-effect of leveling (for me at least), so I enjoy every moment of it. I mean, if people wanted, they could easily change focus from leveling to earning money, but it isn't anything people need to do to play the game.

Thirdly, the system exploits. There are plenty of dungeons in Lineage 2 with high density spawning. And I mean high. I wish I could find my old screenshots folder, but I took a few screenshots of my running through a low level dungeon.. I had literally 35 mobs on me in less than a minute. Fast spawn times, too.

Now, there is a level separation system similar to WoW's (people were unable to earn any experience or money from mobs if they were 5+ levels higher). The problem is that people are capable of de-leveling in L2. So, after a night of botting, all they had to do was slap a town guard, he kills them instantly in self defense (since guards of all towns are the equivalent of say... lv 75 guards in our towns), they spawn instantly right next to the guard, rinse/repeat. Took 15 minutes a day, tops.

PLUS, the evildoers had found several loopholes in the enforcement of the GM's. I had paged GM's on several occasions... 15 give or take.. and they let me know that the only determining factor for a bot to be punished is if the GM's page them and they don't respond. So, they would host multiple computers with multiple accounts (believe me, it was still profitable), and do one of two things: either host bots on every computer, and keep an eye on them once in a while, or have the secondary computers use an assist bot on the manually controlled character.. having it follow them around, hitting whatever they hit. It was painfully obvious, too; the three player models that followed the person controlling did exactly the same movements at exactly the same moment. It would be impossible to move in synch as different players across the net like they did. I kept begging the GM's "please, just watch them for 10 minutes; that's all the evidence you need. They're botting as we speak." No go. All 4 players responded to their pages, so that's proof enough that they aren't botting, according to them. I guess the ends didn't justify the means, because that would mean 4 paid accounts lost. Though, many of my guildmates and I quit for that reason... so maybe not ;p.

Oh, plus the equipment isn't restricted as much as ours is. The equipment goes in stages: from level 1-20, there's about 5 increasing grades of equipment that anyone in the level can equip.. so a level 1 can wear what a level 19 is wearing. Then the next range is from 20-40. So lv 20's were fighting lv 20 monsters with lv 39 equipment.

The thing that I hated the most was that... they knew it was going on. They just didn't bother doing anything about it, and if you ask me, it was all for the money.
