I have used water walking to escape mobs, players, and npcs several times and it is the one way shaman get a chance to run for once. We used to have earthbind but that was nerfed and can be destroyed, and is often resisted. Frost shock slows your enemy but only one so it is useless in situations where you are either out of mana(thus running) or getting traing by 2 or 3 mobs(or alliance).

Mages have blink and polymorph, rogues have sap and sprint, warriors charge, hamstring and fear-shout, paladins holy-shield and some form of stun, warlocks have fear, druids have entangle, and hunters have that confusing arrow and they are still incomplete so who knows what they will have.

A few things to note that were mentions but most people may not understand. Our biggest defining quality, totems, are useless in the water. In the BB instance, they drop 50 feet below. Water walk can be dispeled by a priest, we can be polymophed(and drowned) by a mage, all a warrior has to do is hamstring us and its game, a rogue can sap us while invisible(regardless of me having catseye elixirs), and paladins can stun as well. These are all forms of counters again waterwalking in the most extreme case(booty bay). The horde and shamans have a slight advantage on water. This is not a big thing because little of the games content lies on water. In booty bay we cannot kill people from the water unless they enter the water. So if people do not want to be at a disadvantage all they have to do is not fight us out on the bay. If you take this flavor away from the Horde or give it to druids...you are effecting the diversity of the 2 sides. I like to have at least ONE buff I can give someone so I can get the occasional int or sta buff in return. I understand you cannot ever make a shaman a buffing class because that would make the horde severely overpowered. This is a buff the horde can have that is an advantage in some area, but definately counterable, and not overpowering. As some people have said, most people are not used to dealing with this tactic, and have no strategy to fight it yet. I would say I still get killed over 50% of the time when I am water walking by either a mage, rogue, or worst of all a warrior.

Recently the shamans have had nerf after nerf after nerf, from ghost wolves, to facing using spells, to earthbind nerf. While I haven't complained about those, if you take water walking there is little use for a shaman besides a ghetto priest who is a nice addition to group raids on instances.

One top of everything I just said, everyone please stop with the insults. I know we all want our class buffed and our enemies classes nerfed, but remember, We are testers of this game, our goal's should be Blizzards goal, and that is making WOW the greatest MMRPG ever.
