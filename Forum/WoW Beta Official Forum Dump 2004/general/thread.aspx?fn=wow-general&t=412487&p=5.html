I love the rest system. Usually get a minimum of 2 full bars because I only play at nights.

The thing that makes rest good is the fact that it's a bonus--you don't NEED it. I think durability should be done in a similar way, as I said earlier--instead of items becoming COMPLETELY useless after long use, they could become less useful. Not a slope, but a complete drop-off at the end. When 0 durability is reached, the item simply becomes less useful.

It could even add more depth to some items. If the items simply break when they're finished, that's it--they do nothing until fixed. If they simply lose effectiveness, the amount that remains when they hit 0 could be something that differs on certain items. 50% (or 60% or 40% or ANY %) could be the norm, but there could be variations.

There could be a dagger that has massive damage in normal mode, but has zilch for durability--so for the first hit or two, it does really nice damage. Then when it's got 0 durability, it does maybe 10-20% of that.

Conversely, there could be a mace that lasts -forever-. When it finally does run out of durability, it keeps 75% effectiveness.

I just think that it would be a lot preferable to them becoming totally unusable. Means that if your weapon breaks down in the middle of a dungeon or something, you don't have to run back to town, you just keep going with a bit more difficulty, and fix them up when you get back.
