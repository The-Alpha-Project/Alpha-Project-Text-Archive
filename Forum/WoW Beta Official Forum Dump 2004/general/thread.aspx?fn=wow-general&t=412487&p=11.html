*cough* Diablo 2 *cough*

I would agree with this philosophy, If everyone has godly items and can kill enemies in a single hit, the game in question can get very, very, tedious and boring for some. Others however still seem to find a simple joy in it, after all there are still people playing Diablo 2 are there not?

The fine line you have to tread, however, is the line between what happened in Diablo 2 and what happened in games such as everquest etc. I'd say the WoW team has made another successful step along the tightrope, but they have to watch what they do after this. I think there should be some tedium that is somewhat voluntary.

I honestly think the idea of the level cap to be somewhat limiting. If gamers really want to be hardcore, i think we should let them. I've always wanted to see a game with i nearly limitless level system, this of course produces 2 major problems:

1) Balance: If player X has no life outside his bedroom, he could get to level 200 after, say, a year. This would make him nearly unstoppable in any type of PVP situation, and of course, stonger PCs require stronger NPCs to keep the gain entertaining (Meaning more work in the long term for blizzard). This could be solved in numerous ways, one way is the idea of decreasing returns, after level 60, each level progressively becomes less and less helpful, to the point where people do not see the benifit of continued grinding. Why bother to have these levels at all? People like the sense of progression, growth, etc. Being limited to only growth in equipment is a detering factor that will lead many people who've reached max level to grow disinterested in the game. Even with a strong PVP game.

2) Content: Already mentioned in the above, the idea of blizzard having to provide new areas of exploration for increasingly high level players would be a bad move on their part, as they are mostly dooming themselves to an infinite amount of work.

Possibly instead of raising the level cap, the developers could expand on less troublesome systems. After level 60 maybe people can gain experience for extra talent points, making their character more robust, yet not really making them unbalanced.

Also, the idea of a more custom magic/skill system comes to mind. After all, how powerful were the mages who first created the spells we use today? At some point classes should be able to forge new ground for themselves and others, creating spells/skills that are unique to their style of play, etc. and this type of feature could require more than just the monster grinding experience that you have to do from levels 1-60. "war exp" could be a requirement, making PVP seem more useful for high level chars. Rank systems among classes (IE: "Archmage", "squire" "knight") could also be created in addition to the idea of gaining honor experience via the PVP system. (think of level 60 as getting your masters in college, you should still have the ability to get your doctorate)

This would obviously be implemented in an expansion or huge patch, as the amount of extra work these things could take are mind-blowing to a programmer (trust me, i know)

Still, i think that hardcore gamers should be taken care of once low level gameplay is perfect, and thats one thing we can be sure of, blizzard will try to make it perfect :)
just a few ideas, i have more.

LOL

Reading through the various points in this thread has been an emotional rollercoster! Some of you guys have stated points on the verge of viability; most of you have not. The trend here seems to be with item durability - so many of the beta player base are concerned that durability is going to be that camel breaking straw I've heard so much about. And apparently, somewhere throughout the course of this conversation the word "tedium" got thrown in to describe everything from item durability to meditation to travel. Let me throw MY $.02 in for a minute here.

Item Durability
Quite honestly, WoW's durability system is HARDLY something to whine about. I have never been against a durability system in MMOsm, as testament to my 2.5 years (or thereabouts) of dedication to DAoC. But if I have to compare the two - like so many of you seemed to have done - and if I have to further investigate the similarties of WoW to Diablo 2, I come to the conclusion that WoW is MUCH closer to Diablo 2. There is no CON & Quality system in WoW. You don't have to worry about your items getting worse, not only as you use them, but also as you repair them. Blizzard effectively avoided the Level-oriented degredation system in DAoC by simply putting level requirements on gear. CHEERS!

On top of the fact that it's not even that severe of a system compared to some of WoW's predecessors, the fact also remains that repairs aren't even all that expensive. Just the other day, I repaired all my Priest's equipment for the first time since the system was put in, and it only cost me 12sp. 12sp?? I don't know how much it is for leather/mail at this level, but even if it's, say, 2x or 3x more than what I pay to repair cloth, 3 X 12 sp = 36sp, that's still not a whole lot of money. And "tedium"? I just don't see where the tedium is in swinging by the vendor to "Repair All" my items for a meager sum of money in the town that I was already in since I had to vendor my day's kills and turn in my quests anyway! For the life of me, I just don't see how that's tedious. After EverQuest, DAoC, Lineage 2, and FFXI, I haven't found a single thing in WoW that's a "tedium" by any stretch of the imagination.

Travel
I noticed several of you are clamoring for some form of teleportation scheme in-game, saying that the current transportation systems are too slow, that 3-5 minutes of travel time is simply too great of a temporal chasm to be worth filling.

Maybe there's something in the water, but this again seems to be an argument spawned out of boredom and some deeper level of personal anxiety suffered ventilation through impersonal communication media and anonymity. If 5 minutes of flight time (over a gorgeous landscape, by the way) is too much of a crunch for you, I really don't know what to tell you. The Griffon/Wyvern system is, I think, one of the cooler features of the game so far. Blizzard could have very easily stuck with a DAoC-esque horse system - how boring! Instead, they gave us a beautifully rendered, eye-in-the-sky transport system that gives you something to look at while you travel for all of 3-5 minutes of your time. The Tram was a neat idea, too! I just hope they have some alternative for the Horde. And let us not forget to mention that, not only does Blizzard actually give you a transportation system to use, but they also allow you to personally own mounts! DAoC never did that, and it still hasn't. You get public horse transportation and djins - that's it.

Honestly, guys. I think many of you need to step back and look at what you have and what you could have had if some other company were making some other MMO in some other year past. WoW has genuinely looked at what worked and didn't work from other MMO's and tried its hardest to balance between them. You guys talk of how big of a "tedium" certain aspects of the game are when honestly you have no concept of what real gaming tedium is. Lineage (and its sequal) is a tedium. EverQuest was a tedium. Even DAoC, which I hold above all others minus WoW, is a tedium. They all had their time on my screen, but they all lost their luster after some time. Compared to these other titles, WoW is fast, it looks good, it plays smoothly, the software itself is relatively solid (for a beta), the servers are fairly stable (again, for a beta), and those features that so many of you are heralding as "tedia" and "unnecessary money sinks" simply aren't.

One thing comes to mind faster than anything else while reading all this. It was something a teacher of mine once said after half the class complained about their grades on a test that he curved 20 points:

"You know - if I gave each and every one of you ten thousand dollars, each and every one of you would complain that I didn't give you a million."
