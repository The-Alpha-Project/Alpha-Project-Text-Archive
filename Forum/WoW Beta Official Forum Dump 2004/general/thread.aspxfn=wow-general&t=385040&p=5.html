Let me make a comparison...

When SOE moved all... ALL their servers to a single facility in San Diego, I laughed. I laughed HARD. I was thinking ... man, people are going to be pissed.

Then, about a year later, after the deafening silence, I stopped laughing. Noone was complaining.

See, it's not about server placement anymore. It's about raw connectivity. If someone in Germany could play on a server in San Diego and have better connectivity than me (in Oregon) then you begin to realize that there really aren't many internet lag issues.

Just for grins, I looked up a major ISP in Germany, the first one I found was 1and1. First off, it was 3 hops farther away to me than SOE, and www.worldofwarcraft.com was 3 hops farther than that (at least, at 16 hops it timed out).

A ping to SOE returned 80ms average.
A ping to a German ISP (as above) returned 150ms average.
I've played WoW at "yellow" connection status, usually playing at 200-300ms reported lag. (though as I look at this now I'm getting 130, to be fair).

My points? I barely notice lag with this engine. Even if I did it's tolerable. Kudos on the incredible engine. But the real point?

...You could host these servers in Germany and I probably wouldn't notice.

I will grant that on Blizzard's defense, the problems with a non-regional server are exacerbated by -any- internet trauma. The more hops, the more likely. To counter that, look above. I get just as many hops going across the US to Blizzard as I do connecting to Germany. It's very very hard to justify less lag on the pure basis of region.

Edit: If this is truly the reason for the separation of servers, then I would highly recommend Blizzard put in a PING meter in the server/realm selection screen, regardless of region. It is highly likely that users in the east/west coast could have better connectivity to servers across the US than their own home state. Even in the US, it wouldn't make any sense.
