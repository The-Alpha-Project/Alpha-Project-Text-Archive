 22. Re: L2's Farming/EBaying Problem | 10/20/2004 6:39:08 PM GMTDT
Check out an article in the 10/18/04 Newsweek entitled "Gaming the Online Games." Here is a quote from it:
Q u o t e:
[K's] specialty is Lineage, a medieval fantasy game, and he plays seven characters simultaneously on four computers seeking the spoils--magical swords and the like--that allow victors to move up to higher levels. At 27, he says he couldn't find a normal career out of university, so he turned to this gray-market profession, earning some $4,000 a month winning and selling virtual booty to other players.
The article also says that there are more than 200 companies doing this, with total yearly revenue estimated at somewhere between $83 million and $415 million.

As far as farmers jumping from Lineage to WOW, it's a business to them and they'll go wherever the money is. WOW will obviously be a very successful game, so if they can find a way to profit by it they'll certainly try. It doesn't mean they'll abandon Lineage though. I'm sure Lineage will continue to be farmed as long as people can make money doing so.

Blizzard has already done the most important thing, and that's build a game that discourages farming:

1. The best items in the game are bind-on-pickup, so you can't sell those. Blue and purple bind-on-equip items are sellable, but since they are very rare drops there is no way to farm them quickly or reliably.

2. There is no real way to monopolize spawn and corner a market. Anyone can do the instance dungeons. Rare items drop off any mob, not just certain mobs that could be camped. WOW is designed such that no one is denied a fair chance at good loot.

3. Money is useful, but doesn't carry the same value as it does in L2.

4. The game is designed to be fun from levels 1-60, not the kind of game that is a boring, pay-your-dues grind until it actually gets fun once you are level-capped. That reduces the motivation for people to buy a level-capped character on ebay.

5. WOW is not the sort of game that lends itself well to the sort of 7-boxing that farmers like to do. I would think it would be harder to 7-box effectively in WOW than it would be in a high downtime, slow-paced, grouping-required MMO.

Hopefully these characteristics of WOW will make farming less profitable and thus keep the commercial farming to a minimum. I'm not sure it's possible to make it go away entirely, though I hope Blizzard tries.
