 1. Idea I got from Korean servers | 10/16/2004 7:42:01 AM GMTDT
When servers are down, one contemplates all sorts of things concerning WoW. It seems that WoW will not be up until tomorrow morning, so I got to thinking. Yeah, I know it's a dangerous habit. ^_^

But here goes. After looking at the unprecendented amount of PvP on the Korean servers http://www.worldofwarcraft.com/thread.aspx?fn=wow-pvp&t=23827&p=1#post23827 , there is a way to perhaps encourage more robust PvP on all servers.

The idea is this. Let there be some sort of flag in each main city; for example, the king in Ironforge, the high priest in Stormwind, and the arch-druid in Darnassus, Thrall in Ogrimmar, etc. would be the targets. If a faction from one side successfully kills a target or targets in these cities, make it so that all hearths or mage portals cannot be made to that particular city for a duration of a week or so. This will be very such inconvenience for all faction members, thus it will force all members of the faction to defend all cities whenever there happens to be a raid.

Another way would be that, if any of these targets are assassinated, either the successful faction gains some bonus, or the opposing faction will lose some bonus. What I mean by this is that, for example, if the horde was successful in assassinating the Ironforge King, then all the horde members will gain +5 all stats for a week when facing the alliance or vice versa.

The last way would be, and I am sure this would be the least favorite option by all, that if a faction loses one of the targets to the opposing faction, then the vendor price in that particular city goes up by 5% or so for a week.

All these three options will encourage all members of the faction to defend their own at all times.

What do you think?

Please share you ideas to encourage more organized PvP on the US servers.
