Erm, no. You don't understand how it works. My buddy's a manager at EB, this is how it works.

EB collects preorders. The game companies use that information to decide how many boxes to send out initially (to save themselves money and prevent making too large a run). EB uses then uses this info to decide how many boxes to ship to their individual stores. The stores generally get their pre-orders plus a percentage, assuming the game delveloper made a large enough initial run.

So when you arrive on game day, EB (frys in your case) sells ONLY the extra ones to people who just walk in off the street. If you have a pre-order, they hold a box for you. If they only receive enough boxes to cover the pre-orders, you'll be out of luck if you don't have one. They WONT sell the game if it will mean they wont have enough boxes to cover the preorders.
