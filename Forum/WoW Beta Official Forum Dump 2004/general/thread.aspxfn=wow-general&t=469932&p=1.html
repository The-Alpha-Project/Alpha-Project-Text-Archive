alright, one of the problems that the devs are having with the hero classes, is that once they're in, everyone and their mom will have a hero in WoW. the devs dont want that to happen, they want the heroes to be in smaller numbers compared to other players.

for those of you who played FFXI, you know about advanced jobs. in FFXI you pick a base job (6 options), at lvl 18 you may choose a subjob, and at lvl 30 you may start quests for advanced jobs. some of these quests are impossible for lvl 30's, and you have to wait til high 50's to get some of the jobs.

now the good thing about it was that the advanced jobs were in no way better than the normal jobs (white mage, black mage, red mage, warrior, thief, monk) i played a monk/thief and i wouldnt have traded my character for anything else. many people still wanted warrior instead of paladins (paladins were better tanks) because warriors had more group utility and could play any role in any renkei. monk/thief were still preferred to dark knight/ninja/samurai because as a melee damage dealer, they were just as good - if not better.

so, although it was cool to have my lvl 65 dragoon with my own dragon pet, or be a high level dark knight with sweet looking evil armor; i still chose to stick my mnk/thf, and i owned harder than most advanced jobs.

i would like to see the same thing in WoW. make heroes high level content. make heroes very hard to get. make heroes look cool. make heroes unique. but one thing you shouldnt do, is make heroes superior in game play to other characters.

It seems like when it comes to hero classes, the title hangs up discussion more than anything else. Look at a possible mage -> Archmage progression. Leave out the word "hero" and just examine what that upgrade would mean...

A mage masters the arcane, fire, and frost aspects of magic, and upon mastery titles himself an "Archmage." He's obviously not your fledging level 1 mage and is very powerful, and as such he's capable of mastering more of the magic arts such as summoning elementals, learning to boost the magic potential of those around him, or whatever skills a "higher tiered mage" may have.

He doesn't necessarily have to be a world saving hero like those we know from WC lore, such as Grom or Tyrande. He's just advanced for his race/class, and as such can do a few better tricks.
