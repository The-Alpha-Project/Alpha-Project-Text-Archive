 9. Re: Ogre-What's up with Orgrimmar low FPS? | 10/12/2004 8:20:59 PM GMTDT
They probably need to check or place blocking volumes inside the walls of the city. Not sure if it is the same in their engine, but in UT2003/2004 at least brushes will not block any more. Meshes dont block drawing. It is left up to a blocking volume.

So what happens is as you look toward a wall that has a room behind it, and a room behind that one, your PC ends up having to "draw" those rooms behind the wall. Basically looking into a building could lag you, even if you arent looking down the hallway.

Ths short story is that with blocking volumes between the sections of city, your computer isnt having to draw out the warrior area or the bank area ot whatever area you are NOT in at the time. Makes FPS much better.

 17. Re: Ogre-What's up with Orgrimmar low FPS? | 10/12/2004 9:48:26 PM GMTDT
We are requesting that anyone currently experiencing problems relating to frame rate drops in Ogrimmar send their dxdiag to wowsupport@blizzard.com for further diagnostic. In the subject of the email, please add an Attn: Cjones. Steps to create a dxdiag.txt have been pasted below.

1. Click on Start.
2. Click on Run.
3. Type in dxdiag and press Enter or click OK, this will open the DirectX diagnostic tool. Run the DirectDraw, Direct3D and DirectSound tests found under the Display and Sound tabs before creating the dxdiag.txt file.
4. Click on the Save All Information Button (save this file to your windows desktop and name it dxdiag).
5. Attach the dxdiag to the email.

Edit: Thank you to everyone that provided me with the necessary requested feedback. We now have enough information to properly investigate this matter further.
