 1. WoW - Lack of depth? | 10/21/2004 11:13:29 PM GMTDT
Though I'm hoping alot of this will be added closer to release and after - One thing I notice in the current version is the lack of depth in the game.

For instance, SWG (though horribly rushed and poorly executed) had lots of features like player housing, guild housing, player shops (you could even hire NPCs for your shops), setting up harvesters, and even founding entire new player-based cities.

In WoW, the only reasons people are in towns are to trade and meet. There are taverns, but when's the last time you saw a group of players sitting around chatting there?

The game needs more depth, more "extras", so that it is not 100% fighting monsters, trading, and making items!

Somebody suggested adding poker to taverns, which is a great idea, but it won't be done because blizzard is too worried about younger gamers. Now, WoW is expected to get a Teen rating, but playing Texas Hold'em with game money is too risky for that audiance? What about just adding non-gambling card games?

And what about playing housing? Guild halls? Organised MOB attacks on towns?

I'm hoping that more of this is added closer to/after release (I'm also worried about the amount of content they have schedualed for "sometime after release" but that's another thread...) but it's things like these that make WoW not just a single player game where you happen to run into others sometimes. I mean, they've already made it a solo-focused game, taking out alot of the fun for guilds in the first place.

Does anyone else agree with me on this?
