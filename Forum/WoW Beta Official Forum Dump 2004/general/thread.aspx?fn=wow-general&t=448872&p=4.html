Wow, this post had a lot more replies after I logged off last night! Here's a little roundup of reviews for the new sites that were added:

http://www.legendsofazeroth.com:

PHP-Nuke site, so there's not a lot to rate. The skin is nice, but the header doesn't really fit. Try having a header that is compatible in resolutions all the way up to 1600x1200. It's not hard to do, just make one site of the top image fad out into a generic pattern and have it aligned to one side of the center. It's a good looking site, especially for a first attempt. But that header doesn't fit it well.

http://www.dogma-guild.org:

I actually hadn't seen this web site before, but I fell in love with a lot of the aspects behind it. The concept are being used as much as it is has a really nice, smooth, effect. All of the images are crisp, and as a result they look incredible. The only minor complaint i'd have is that this is an out-of-the-box portal, but i'd commend the webmasters for using something must better than PHP-Nuke. Also, I wish the other characters in the header were in color, it'd make the page much more vibrant. But that's being -really- picky, I love the look of this page.

http://www.blackknightsclan.com:

100% custom, but not 100% original unfortunately. This design looks like a standard out-of-the-box portal. The nice thing is that it's not. But to the naked eye that's what someone might think. It's a good looking site, but again, not the best.

http://www.lordsofdecay.com:

Someone elses first attempt at a web page? It's nice, especially for a first attempt. I commend the webmaster for having all of the pages hand coded rather than using PHP-Nuke. You'll learn much more about being a webmaster doing that. If I had any suggestions though, it would be to use something a little more crisp for the font, try Verdana, Geneva, or Arial.

http://www.deadly-sins.org:

Good looking site, very simple and that's my preference. It's also expandable to work in any resolution, that's a nice feature. Two suggestions though, try having that sidebar that floats on the side of the page aligned as a vertical sidebar on the right hand side of the page, you could probably achieve this by adding an 'align='Right'' inside the <td> tag. And instead of using such a vibrant, bright red, go for something darker. And finally, add a different link color, the stock HTML blue is never pretty! Good job though! :)

http://www.avatarsoffear.com:

Good looking site! It's a shame that it's a PHP-Nuke, but at the same time the skin is so beautiful it's definitely worth overlooking. Good job, keep it up, i'll definitely be keeping an eye on this one.

Two of my favorite since last night:
http://www.avatarsoffear.com
http://www.dogma-guild.org

    *Cries* Another PHP-Nuke website. You're right about one thing, your webmaster did do a good job with the skin. But hard to review because of the PHP-Nuke aspect of it... which brings me to a whole other point that i'll discuss below:

    Why I dislike PHP-Nuke:

    It's pre-fabricated, no matter how much you customize it to be your own, it never is. Plus it's comparable to drag and drop. The learning curve is about as steep as the bunny hill at any ski hill, and once you've reached the top of it, you haven't learned anything. Try -reading- the PHP code if you're looking for customization and adding in unique features doing so. PHP-Nuke sites wouldn't bother me at all if I saw that people had spent time in the backend coding features that aren't out-of-the-box, but that's never the case. Aside from the skinning, PHP-Nuke sites are all generally alike. Rating them is like rating a forum skin. The content is always the same, but you're judging the wrapper.
