 1. Why do rogues and hunters have money sinks? | 10/21/2004 4:15:29 AM GMTDT
Why do rogues and hunters have a built-in money sink while other classes do not? Non-mage casters (Pallys, Druids, Warlocks, Priests and Shammys) have a slight money sink because they may need to buy drinks occasionally, but this is technically optional, and Warriors and Mages have no inherent money sinks at all. I'm leaving out food and durability since these affect all classes. I suppose a warrior could make the argument that as a tanker it spends more on food and durability, but that's unproven and still doesn't explain the mage and other imbalances.

I used to think rogues had to pay for poisons to balance the strength of the ability, but that doesn't make sense to me anymore. That suggests that poisons are a "bonus" ability and that with poisons, Rogues are over-powered. A loss of some money isn't really a fair way to balance a damage bonus, especially for PvP. However, if poisons are taken into consideration when balancing the rogue for PvP and PvE, then they are unfairly required to pay for an ability that is a key element in the class' functionality. The same is true for hunters, who require food and stables for their pets and ammo for their weapons in addition to drinks.

Is there a reason for these inconsistancies? Is this fair class balance? Is this good for a balanced game economy?

Alternatively, rogues could acquire poison ingredients from vendors for free. Story-wise there could be a quest that awards the rogue membership in "SI:7" with free poisons as a membership perk. And a little creativity in the hunters' story could help them get around unfair costs as well.
Just sayin'

The money sink issue does seem a little odd.

Mages need nothing

Mounts are only required for some classes. Training has similar costs across the board, scaling up for classes who have fewer abilities.
