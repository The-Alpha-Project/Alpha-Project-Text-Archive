I am getting the feeling that the OP's point was missed by many people. Let me try to point some things out.

The OP likes to group.
The OP likes to solo.
The OP does not want NO SOLO'ing in the game.
The OP does want more group education earlier in the game.

I think he is exactly correct. From level 15 to level 50, the instance content is pretty simple. Let me sum it up for you.

Mobs have more HP, but don't really do anything special, so it is easy to bring a tank and a healer and a few DPS players, and the instance is yours.

Boss mobs have one or two special tricks that make them difficult. Somebody else figures them out, it becomes common knowledge that in order to beat Whitemain and Mograine you run into the back room so you can fight them one at a time. With that little trick, no skill is required to complete the instance.

OK, at level 50 the sunken Temple is a little harder. 2 elites, and 4 regular mobs are common pulls. Black Rock Instances are 5 times as hard as sunken Temple and suddenly require people pay attention, be patient, and do their job to help the group.

Why do you think it took until Black Rock was created for the developers to realize that SoulStones needed changing? 'Cuz that is the first instance that people were wiping ALL THE TIME. Why do they always wipe? 'Cuz they don't play together well. Why don't they play together well? 'Cuz the way they have been playing since level 1 was working, and they don't want to change it now. After all, they got to level 60 that way....

The OP brings up a great point... which to me is "Why should the only "Hard" dungeons or encounters be created for level 60 content? Why shouldn't there be a "Hard" level 30 encounter, and an "Extremely Difficult" Black Rock Depths style encounter for level 40?

So yes, there is something that the development team can do. Make the instance less trivial at earlier levels so that people who achieve 60 will be used to working together to complete a dungeon instead of just grabbing a level 60 guild mate and zerging the dungeon. People used to make raid groups to zerg dungeons, now they just grab a level 60.

Grouping in any game, even EQ, is a totally different dynamic than soloing. One cannot assume that everyone who'll buy and play WoW will have any grouping experience from a previous MMO. It's an adjustment and it takes time to learn. And that's okay, because before you can effectively group, you have to understand the strengths and limitations of your chosen class. So just look at the first 30-40 levels as your opportunity to really understand and build your character.

But I have to agree with Grueken that the way WoW is currently presented to us, grouping doesn't work very well due to the fast-paced game mechanics.

That being said, raid content hasn't been added either. To quote Jeffrey Kaplan from his article on raiding:

"We aren't going to bog people down in the normal leveling process, so we expect that there will be a lot of people at maximum level and they'll be anticipating some truly epic challenges."

This tells me a couple things.

1 - people say leveling in this game is too fast. Duh! If you read this you'd see fast-leveling is intentional.

2 - At level 60 you have seen only the tip of the iceberg.

3 - If he feels us gamers are expecting epic challenges, and they're planning on providing that, you're certainly not going to waltz into raid situation solo and last very long. You will need to learn how to group.

I'd encourage people to read that article in full, and then decide if you still feel that grouping isn't a part of playing WoW.
