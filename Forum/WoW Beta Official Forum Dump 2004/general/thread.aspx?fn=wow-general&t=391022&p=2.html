You guys need to understand that most of the items marked "post-release" in this Q&A were never planned for release. We know that this game is going to be going on for years to come with lots of cool features and content to be added over time. Don't take this to mean that we are ditching everything "cool" just to make some dates.

For example, we never planned for Bows to be player created within the current lineup of professions, but we do wish to add more professions over the lifetime of the game along with new zones, classes, etc, etc.

It should also be noted that some items were initially planned for post-release like Auction houses and mailboxes, but we moved them into the shipping product instead. Unfortunately, features are never "free" and it causes of other planned for features to slip.

-Eno

It's still beta so there's nothing wrong with that, but I may have a problem paying $<subscription fee> a month for a game in which I occasionally fall backwards through the ground and have to wiggle to get out, or while climbing mountains, occasionally get locked in the falling animation while I run around. I know it's not possible to make a game, especially one this huge, totally bug free, but there are a few problems that I hope will be cleared up before you begin asking the subscription fee you need to keep adding content.

Not that I am an MMO god but I have played my share since UO. Two things that are important to every MMO, but that none of them have gotten close to right on day one are:

The most important 'feature' is class balance. Every class needs a clearly defined role and must bring value to a group and ADDITIVE value to a raid. There will always be a most powerful class but no class should be clearly more powerful, or more in demand, than the rest. How do you think WoW stacks up here? It really is much better to iron these things out in beta as opposed to when people are paying time and money for their characters and will hold a grudge when you nerf their investments. An ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure, or something like that.

The other important thing is plentiful, tested high end content. WoW is sorely lacking here.
