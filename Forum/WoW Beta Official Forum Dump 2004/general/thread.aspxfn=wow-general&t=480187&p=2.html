He's not saying that we need racial and hero classes to be IMPLEMENTED before release. He's saying we need to know what the different racial abilities and hero classes are going to be. We need the information to make an informed choice.

Consider: at release hero classes and racial abilities aren't yet determined. You were considering playing either an Orc Warrior or a Troll Warrior, and decide to go with the Troll.

6 months later, when you've put in countless hours acquiring raid-level gear, honor points, level 60, trade skills, not to mention a reputation, racial abilities and hero classes come in. The troll racial ability is "Summon Rubber Ducky" and the troll warrior hero class is "Fairy Godmother". The orc racial ability is "Whirlwind of Death" and the orc warrior hero class is "Deadly Juggernaught of Uberness". Think you'd be pretty pissed off?

Or to use a not-so-exaggerated example, suppose you choose orc rogue over undead rogue and it turns out the orc rogue hero class is a Thug and the undead rogue hero class is an Assassin. Some people might want to play a Thug (which would be more of a light tank than a stealth killer) but you *really* wanted to play an Assassin and were thinking orcs would probably get it, and now you've got 6 months of playtime down the drain. Probably what you do next is cancel your accounts and play something else.

Or suppose you wanted to play a Demon Hunter and assumed it would be a warrior, so you play a Night Elf Warrior. It turns out to be a Rogue 6 months down the line. Same deal.

If we can't adjust to it post-release, we sure as hell better be able to make an informed decision about it AT release.
