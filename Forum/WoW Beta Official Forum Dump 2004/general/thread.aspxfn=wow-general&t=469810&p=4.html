 70. Re: So I got to play the EQ2 beta today... | 10/23/2004 8:23:14 PM GMTDT
I got to see EQ2 beta on a friend's computer last week also. I never made it past character creation before I was completely turned off.

While the graphics and detail are insanely detailed, all that detail ends up looking like CRAP because it's poorly positioned on the body or done with some sort of claymation tool. Now granted, some of WoW's armor and body structure needs work, but I felt that for all the minute detail and airbrushed facial features EQ2 put in, they could have focused half that much attention on the gameplay and done far better.

As others have said, the game has been "dumbed down" and has a very console game (PS2 or Xbox) feeling to it without the fast movement a gamepad offers. Movement is awkward with the keyboard and I feel like I should have a PS2 gamepad instead, much like FFXI did... The whole storyline has been heavily cut/simplified (all the gods are dead!) and you're either strictly 'evil' or 'good' and you start in Freeport or Quenos respectively. Long gone are the cool starting cities like Neriak and Felwithe. The zones are really small and boxy feeling... but granted I saw very little of the starter world. My friend played more extensively out of curiosity and says all the adventuring and exploring has been taken out of it and your completely guided/restricted by the quest and game plot progression... no freedom to do whatever you want really.

But back to my least favorite thing, and 50% of why I play certain games. The character looks. EQ2's races have all been morphed into bizarre looking puppets... straight out of a muppet reproduction combined with Pixar claymation technology. Maybe this appeals to some, but I thought everyone looked very squatty and awkward. Long gone are the tall and austere looking high elves, replaced with some strange Walgreens $2.00 knock-off barbie doll copy. The females have a pasted on red doll smile, and tiny heads and arms with giant torsos and legs. The males, well lets just say the Rainbow Coalition will probably like the victorian era vampish "fop" style they prance around with...

Ogres now look like frighteningly dumpy-fat trailer trash ex-convicts with bad complexion and teeth, and Trolls look like Ferenghi on steroids, complete with gaping shark-like mouths full of conical dolphin teeth. Iksar look like "Fish-man" out of a comic book, with gills and a pug face... long gone are the scary looking lizard features.

The worst of all is humanoid hair. They use some sort of airbrush tool to make skin flow smoothly over awkwardly designed frames and skulls, but the hair seems to have been created by a playdoh factory machine. Literally, rolls of dough are blopped onto the head and face in various detailed styles that do not blend or mold with the head as if hair is "growing" out of the skull or face, but rather that it's been applied with a putty knife and colors smeared in. It's absolutely absurd looking.

So why am I being so critical when WoW's character features/clothes graphics are fairly simplified and undetailed? Well that's exactly it... if you're going to create a game that shows THAT much minute detail of skin, hair and clothing, then you better be damn sure it's very realistic looking and attractive. EQ2 looks awkward and overly-done to me... all that detail does nothing but magnify how awful they look and move. The armor pieces don't even seem right on the body, with zillions of bands and loops and links all over the place that are unnecessary and gaudy looking.

I don't know what market they're trying to reach with this new look and concept, but it isn't the market I'm in, and I do not intend to play this game. The only 2 races out of the entire lineup that even slightly appealed to me were the totally revamped Erudites who look oddly out of place with the rest of the races, something more out of a science fiction space game (Anarchy Online style) with alien like eyes and bald purple heads complete with glowing skin glyphs, and the Rotonga, the supposedly 'evil' rat-men, who quite frankly are the CUTEST thing I've ever seen, straight out of Stuart Little... I have no idea how they're supposed to be evil... it's like saying gnomes are scary! =P

I don't think this one's the game for me... despite it's bad looks and limited gameplay, there's no PvP whatsoever and unlike EQ1, I don't foresee this game holding the market for years and years with content and challenge to make up for lack of player conflict.

Big Thumbs Down.
