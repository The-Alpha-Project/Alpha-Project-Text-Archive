Orc priests do not make sense from a lore standpoint.

As others have pointed out, the Orcs were, on their homeworld of Draenor, shamanistic, in tune with nature, and we can infer that they weren't the slavering "horde" of bloodthirsty savages that they were when the all popped through the Dark Portal and started murdering everything they could.

It is a moot point whether they were once "peace-loving" or not. The Night Elves are "peace-loving" but they've been at the center of most of the major conflicts of the last 10,000+ years. Every race, except for the vile Undead, could characterize themselves as "peace-loving" if they wanted to.

The Orcs that we get to play in the game all come from Durotar. Durotar was founded by Thrall, who led the enslaved survivors of the second Great War to Kalimdor. Durotar Orcs, and the clans that followed Thrall, were FREED of their demonic bonds when Grom Hellscream died at the hands of Archimonde's Pit Lord (I forgot his name) in Warcraft III. One could even argue that Thrall's personal rediscovery of shamanism and his reconnection to the spirits of the land also contributed to the Orcs finding the power to overcome the demonic shadow of Guldan's betrayal and his nefarious dealings with the Burning Legion.

Aided by the one race of trolls that wasn't insane, as well as by the Tauren, Thrall and his orcs founded Durotar, their new home.

As a player, you cannot play any of the Orc clans that remain corrupted - Twilight's Hammer, Blackrock, Dragonmaw. These clans are, so far, proving to be beyond redemption. These clans should not be confused with the clans that comprise the nation of Durotar.

You are a Durotar Orc. It makes sense that you cannot be a mage or a priests, but, rather, you embrace the shamanism that is your heritage, or indulge in the demonic arts of the warlock like so many other races.

Remember that the Durotar Orcs are only viewed as "the enemy" by the Alliance, because Humanity was incapable of seeing the orcs as anything other than savage animals. Even Medivh pleaded with King Terenas to recognize the only TRUE enemy of the living - the Burning Legion and their armies of undead - but his pleas fell on deaf ears. That's why he instructed Thrall to pull up stakes and leave the Eastern Kingdoms.
