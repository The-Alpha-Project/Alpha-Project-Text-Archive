 1. How do you instance/dungeon? | 10/26/2004 5:55:43 PM GMTDT
Over the past week, I've been delving outside of my usual group/party to play with others and see if perhaps I am missing something in instances/dungeons. It's amazing how things are a bit out of whack in a pick up group, as they are called, compared to that of a group of friends.. Why is that?

My Style...
1. I'm usually the party planner, so I determine first, how many people are needed for the amount of time I want to spend (usually 5 or 10).
2. Next I determine, how many of what class is necessary to achieve a successful run.
3. Coordinate the group, successful run...

Keep in mind, I have a huge friends list, I also have friends who have a huge friends list etc... Finding a balanced group, isn't difficult. I play with all classes, hunters and druids included. Often times, I get tells, Take me! etc etc.. and I have to respond, we already have 2 of X Class. sorry :(

Enter my experiences with pick up groups... Here are few areas I've found during these experiences that have been, in my opinion, reasons for a non-successful run.

Leadership Spam Syndrom
Everyone's a leader, and there are few followers, ending up in a lot of miscommunication, chaos and death..

Commando Tactics
X decides this is the best way to do this, and I'm not listening to others ideas, I'm doing it this way.. He/She fails to sell his idea, he/she decides to practice his idea, he/she dies, group dies..

Ego high
X thinks to himself how every person in the group sucks and they should do <y> when <z> happens. He refuses to speak out what works, instead he sends me a tell that they suck, then laughs at the others. Because he's lost in his thoughts he agro's another group and the group wipes.

Religious Belief Structure
X believes that having 4 priests in a 10 man raid is a more successful saving grace than having a well balanced group.

Proficiency Challenged Players
X has no concept of when it is appropriate to heal, taunt, shackle, AoE, sheep etc...

Lack of Confidence
X claims Y has more talent than he does so X sits back and watches the battle. X gets agro, X runs into next mob group, X dies, group dies.

Explorer Factor
X is only here because he's never been here before. Therefore, do not expect X to heal, shackle, sheep, taunt, AoE etc... because X just wants to explore the dungeon.

Curiosity killed the cat (and the group)
X sees something white around the corner, X rushes ahead of group and peeks around the corner. X gets agro, x dies, group dies.

I'm not denouncing pick-up groups in any way whatsoever, what I would like to see is more thought into putting a group together, rather than just getting what you can. Relying on desperation measures to obtain a group, will only leave you with more problems on your run. I've played with a ton of people that I have never played with before, over the past week.. Many of which I will be happy to group with again.... Even if they did fit the categories I posted above... I will still group with them, eventually, they will figure out, that a group = a group and there is no place for a solo run in a high level dungeon.

I was just messaged in game in regards to this post, the question was:

Q u o t e:
How can I expect them to get a good group when limitations on good <class names> exists?


I never expect to get the best of any class, nor do I expect to get the worse. I have a lot of priests, mages, paladins, rogues on my friends list, not because I believe they are better than others, but because they are my friends.

I treat every class with respect. When I enter an instance with other cloth wearers, I have respect for that cloth wearer. If I note that the priest still wears a belt from Scarlet Monastery and I boast a belt from Upper BRS, I will gladly allow the priest to gain the dropped belt item with 1 more additional intel than I have, as that will be of more benefit to him than to me. (Guess what? That priest will come with me again, on my next run, and my next run, and my next run...)

I made a mistake once, and that was thinking I deserved a drop over everyone else, because I worked hard for my Seal of Ascencion. Reality is... They worked equally hard to get me through Upper BRS so they too should receive equal benefit. I will never again assume that I deserver greater than others... I'm thankful that the friends I made in game, forgive me for this stupid decision.

I take care of my friends in-game. If I know that X wants a new wand, belt, sword or any other type of gear. I will note that and make sure that I think before I roll on something, they may "need".

The only people that do not make it to my list of people I love to play with, are the ones who have no respect for the others in a group. You know these ones right? The X class that knows how to play your class better than you. Or the X class that rolls on a BOP Plate Item when he can only wear cloth, then claims it's for his alt. Or, perhaps X who thinks no one else has the right to roll on Y item, just himself. ugh.. I can't stand it when X tells me that I cannot talk on a run... WTH dude... This is an MMORPG, I wanna SOCIALIZE!!!

I'm not saying that you should give up rights to everything so the others can have better gear. I'm saying, if you wish to play more with X then you should rethink how you're behavior affects your future runs with them. Think about it, if you were a priest, and second wind dropped in BRD and a Paladin wins the roll, would you really remember that Paladin as someone you absolutely must group with again?

For me, instance runs are a ton of fun. It's more enjoyable when you have a group of people that are equally interested in having fun and have respect for other classes. Neme is one of the greater examples of this philosophy... Neme can find benefit in any cloth gear that drops in a high level dungeon. But out of respect for the cloth wearers, he will not roll. I have to scold him to roll... sigh

Instead of sitting around whining that there isn't enough priests/healers in-game. Think about how you can get the advantage over others when it comes to healers. I for one, have healers that log on and look for me before they choose another group. Why? Because there is fun, benefit and respect for them... It's not an ego boost, many people I know behave the same as I do, it's because of this respect that I will continue to group with them.
