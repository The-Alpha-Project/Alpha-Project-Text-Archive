 1. Suggestions To Fix Item Durability | 10/12/2004 5:11:48 AM GMTDT
I find the aspect of item durability a necessity for MMOs that wish to avoid inflation. Avoiding inflation allows for a working currency and voids the possibility of a frustrating barter economy; thus, this will have a very positive effect for the game in the long run.

With that said, I do believe some changes need to be made to make item durability coalesce more effectively with WoW. One of the first issues with item durability is that it requires an individual to often make a trip to town in order to repair one’s gear. This has a very negative effective on instances and raids where a trip to town is not so conveniently made. One of many possible changes can fix or at least ease this burden on the players while still allowing the repair fees to still act as a feasible money sink. One possibility is that players could buy a tool that allows them to repair their gear; this would eliminate the need for town runs altogether. Another possibility is to just heavily increase the maximum durability of items; this change would have to be large enough so that players never have to worry about repairing items, even when on the longest of raids. Finally, a third possibility is just to make the item durability rate scale down slower but cost more; this would keep the price effectively the same but ease much of the hassle that currently accompanies item durability.

The only other suggestion I’d like to add is that item durability for items that lower than “blue” or “rare” quality should not have a durability fee associate with them. Furthermore, items of epic quality and above should have a much steeper repair fee than blue items. The money sinks for character that have no blue items are already very effective and leave the character with little excess gold. It is also important that casual players can worry less about item durability while hardcore players who enjoy higher quality items are much more effectively targeted by this new money sink.

All in all, the durability system is a step in the right direction; however, some refinement is most certainly in order.

 14. Re: Suggestions To Fix Item Durability | 10/12/2004 6:39:08 AM GMTDT
My problem with durability is that it unduly targets tanks and soloers. Tanks will end up getting the most durability decreases to their armor out of everyone in the group, and will still take weapon durability drops at the same rate as other melee classes. However they aren't allotted any more of the money that is split. Soloers tend to get even less money per point of durability lost than tanks in an instance (at least for melee classes, may be true for soloing casters as well). It's not right for a tank to ask for more money than anyone else in the group, each member of the group is doing their equal part of the job, yet tanks are taking the brunt of the cost in item wear.

The way I see it, the more powerful and expertly crafted an item is, the more durability it should posess, the slower it should degrade, and the more expensive it should be to repair. Unique items should be invulnerable (have infinite durability). Thus, grey items should have the lowest durability, slowly scaling up for white, then green, with blue having even more, and rares having the most durability. Say a white item has 20 durability, but only costs 1 silver to repair, then a green item with 40 durability that costs 10 silver to repair, and a blue item with 80 durability that costs a gold to repair. Perhaps also have the cost scale upwards exponentially as the amount of durability that needs to be repaired increases. Durability should also degrade slow enough that it never becomes an issue -during- a raid as long as it is repaired before-hand. An item, under normal use should last a good 4 hours per level of quality. (grey 4 hours, white 8 hours, green 12 hours, etc...)

I've never been a fan of the concept of item rent in MUD's, which is essentially what durability is for items in WoW (difference being that you only pay as you use the item with durability). I understand the need for money sinks in an economy to prevent inflation, I just think there are other, better ways of creating money sinks. There's another post that was made that gave a number of teriffic ideas for money sinks. I think things such as rent for housing, special feed for mounts that increases run speed, more 'consumable' items, and things of that nature provide better alternatives for money sinks than durability. The idea is, create money sinks that people -want- rather than money sinks that people don't want. Make people want to spend their money on item x that only lasts so long so they have to keep buying it, rather than forcing people to pay money to keep their gear in working order.
