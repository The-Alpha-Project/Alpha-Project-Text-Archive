 496. Re: Balancing | 10/23/2004 12:39:27 AM GMTDT
Three points worth making here I think.

1) The Booty Bay example is frivolous in any event- the guards can be leashed by jumping into the water *whether or not* waterwalking is allowed. In fact, it isn't very hard to snare the one or two guards in an area, wreak unholy havoc, and then take a leisurely stroll out of aggro range.

The manner in which that analogy was put across is clearly disingenuous for this reason and one more- this spell is rarely an advantage against anyone except a priest trying to run away. Anyone who wishes to rob the waterwalker of a tactical advantage merely has to dive under water to make melee closing impractical.


2) There cleary have to be utility abilities that distinguish, firstly Alliance from Horde, and secondly Horde races from one another. Also it is worth noting that it is well in keeping with the notional concept of a shaman to allow them to grant such a utility ability to their team members.

The ability is also fun, and in 99% of cases is used for nothing apart from convenience. There are other far more pressing issues of PvP balance at stake than shamans with waterwalking.


3) The original poster is a priest- a class that can levitate, DoT, heal and charm. If a fun utility spell like waterwalking can be scapegoated as a reason for "leashing guards" in Booty Bay (clearly misleading, the guards can be leashed with or without that spell)- then what is stopping me from crying out for a removal of priest levitation.

After all, it adds nothing to the priest class short of an easy escape route from PvP. An escape which given the wide and substantial array of tools available to the priest can arguably be unjustified. Why am I not calling for such an absurd change? Because I recognize that these abilities are fun, useful in the PvE solo game, and are rarely if ever the deciding issue in PvP.

I submit that waterwalking does little more than allowing an escape already permitted to priests. Furthermore, I see no reason why simply because the Shaman has an ability buff which can be distinguished that it is by necessity an imbalance. Paladins give their groups seals- druids can buff, why should shamans join the warrior category of not being able to provide ANY utility benefit to someone not in their group?

The "Booty Bay" justification has I think been substantially debunked. Furthermore, if the game's PvP testing microcosm is Booty Bay of all places then I think an extremely skewed view of the issue is being presented.
