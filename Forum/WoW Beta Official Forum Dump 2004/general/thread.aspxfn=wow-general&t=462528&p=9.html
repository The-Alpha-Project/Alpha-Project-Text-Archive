 178. Re: WoW's solo emphasis destroys group play | 10/22/2004 6:26:13 PM GMTDT
It's true that taking a 5-person group to do solo quests trivializes that content, but I think not many people play that way. My own playstyle is to do a solo quest here and there during the day whenever I get an hour or so to play, and then meet up with friends in the evening for an instance run or elite quest. I would guess that many semi-casual players are going to play in that way, and WOW's "mixed" content suits us perfectly. It is less suitable, I suppose, for people who expect to be grouped with 4 other people all the time.

Regarding non-elite mobs getting tougher when tackled by a group (Diablo style), that's an interesting idea. I can see some problems with it, though. Many "solo" quests are really better for a duo and can't really be soloed at the quest's actual level. If I attempt a quest and find it too tough, it's nice to be able to recruit a friend and then do it. But if the mobs get twice as hard when I do so, there will be no incentive at all to bring a friend. The quest will still be too hard. I suppose you could alleviate this somewhat by making the difficulty increase by 50% instead of 100% for each player added to the group.

Also, a system like this would encourage people to help each other without grouping. That is, have 2 people do the quest (say it's the type where you just need to fight through a bunch of mobs to reach a certain location) but stay ungrouped so the mobs don't get tougher. That would be lame. And if you make the mobs get tougher just becaue another ungrouped person is fighting nearby, that makes it impossible for somebody to solo if there is a group nearby in the area. The reason Diablo's system worked well was that it wasn't massively multiplayer and there weren't player collision issues since anyone was able to get their own "instance" at any time.

Overall it's an interesting idea. I don't think I would support it because the game as it is suits me perfectly, but it wouldn't bother me too much if they did something like that as long as the difficulty scaled up by something like 50% per extra person added rather than 100%. But making such a big change so late in the game would make a ton of rebalancing and tuning necessary.
