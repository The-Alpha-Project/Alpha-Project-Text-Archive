 1. Rogue Problems - Summation | 9/27/2004 10:15:37 PM GMTDT
I assume, Kalgan, that you are in some fashion involved with the development of the Rogue or the talent system. If not, could you please pass this link along to those in charge?

The crux of the problem in dealing with how to balance the Rogue stems from several positions taken on how the Rogue should effectively be used.

There are only a few main staples for Rogue builds. Variations exist, but we could call those hybrid builds for the sake of arguement.

Builds
  • Stealthy - Opening Move Build - This build is designed around doing an incredible amount of damage during the opening moments of combat. Enough damage, in fact, to make up for the decreased amount of damage dealt over the latter part of combat. Ambush, Garrote and interesting uses of Vanish affect the overall outcome of this particular Rogue.
  • Sustained Damage - High Crit Rate - This Rogue is designed for the long haul. He will hurt you at the beginning but not as badly as his counterpart might. However, this Rogue's strength stems from his ability to crit more often and dish out Sinister Strikes and killer Eviscerates. Adrenaline is pretty much a must for this Rogue.

    There exist a few cross talent options that dilute the effectiveness of each of their strengths but stil garuntee a satisfying outcome and give each Rogue a sense of "designer" combat.

    The problems come into play in varying degrees based on what kind of Rogue you are asking the question to. (The question being: What are the major problems Rogues are having with the current build?)

    A stealthy Rogue will say that the new PVP stealth system makes it very difficult for him/her to manipulate the battlefield and be the true "silent killer" that he/she wanted to be. He might also say that Vanish does not make him entirely "Vanish". Which causes this Rogue a lot of angst.

    The sustained DPS machine complaints stem mainly from him/her watching the DPS of the other classes and wondering why the amount of damage he is dishing out isn't at least on par with other less DPS oriented classes.

    Several options and indeed all of these options could be implemented without swaying the Sin wave that is the Rogue class to the extreme opposite end of the power spectrum. I think a few nudges here and there are what are needed.

    Rogue Improvements:
  • All abilities that require the Rogue to be behind his opponent should discount Armor either Completely or take into account only a % of the opponents armor. - The Rogue has maneuvered on the battlefield, he has a tactical advantage because he is behind his opponent and in many cases has his opponent dead to rights (stunned/sapped). This would not drasticly improve Backstab/Ambush (based on % considered) but it would improve Rogue performance against High Armor types for PVP and Instance Mobs for PVE.
  • Vanish should VANISH. The Rogue has a DOT, fine, he is hurting, bleeding out and in trouble, but he has Vanished. He is gone. His opponent cannot see him. If he had a spell targeting him or a fireball is midair, it misses. The Rogue is no longer there. Area effects should still hit the Rogue, but not knock him out of Vanish. This has a two fold effect: It improves spells like Faerie Fire and makes the Druid want to use them more often and it makes the Hunter's Flare and Mark a far more attractive PVP countermeasure.
  • Daggers, like Axes, in the hands of Rogue, should BY THEMSELVES be higher crit % rated. The Talent a Rogue adds from specialization should be icing on the cake and should make the sustained DPS machine even happier.
  • Stealth should be based on Subtlety skill, distance from target and line of sight. Spinning the camera should not result in seeing a not so sneaky Rogue approaching from the rear. If you aren't facing him, there should be a 0% chance of seeing him. If you are facing him, there should be a good chance you'll see him. (i don't know the limitations the coders face so this could be wishful thinking)
  • A major complatint against the Rogue is the Rogues ability to stun continueously. Cheap shot should remain and even Gouge, but Kidney Shot needs some work.
  • Pre-meditation is not on par with other 30skill requirement talents. 2 pnts, when compared with other skills that garuntee 2 pnts from crits or dodges or finishing moves make attaining the Supreme Power in Subtlety not so Supreme. I would be for changing this skill to something usable during combat. I would love to see a skill the teleports the Rogue instantly behind and facing his opponent's back. This makes the Subtlety Rogue a tricky opponent in melee or spell vs melee combat. Give it a decent cooldown and its not overpowered. I cannot see giving up Cold Blood for Pre-Med, but I would give it up for "Assassin Step" or something equally cool sounding. =)
  • While we are on the 30 pnt builds. 10 Energy is not attractive enough to give up Adrenaline or the "reset" attained in Subtlety. I could see an Evil 5pnt requiring "Finishing Move" that is attained at the top of this Tree being far more attractive.

    Have I left anything out here guys? If so I will edit and add more pointers.
