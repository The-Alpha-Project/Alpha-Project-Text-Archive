 1. The Class Role | 10/23/2004 9:06:35 PM GMTDT
Many of you have asked about your role in groups. I'd like to address that question today. The developers have attempted to make as few rock-hard “roles” as possible; all classes should complement each other. A priest is not just a “heal-bot”. When there are classes that can tank and classes that can heal, however, role definition does appear in some form. Please don’t take this post as the One True Way to Play. Explore all the abilities open to your class. Experiment with different class combinations in groups. We’re not telling you how to play the game – one of the fascinating practices of this genre is the ingenuity exercised by the players every day!

The stealthy Rogue is an up-close damage dealer. Like the Mage, they can rip through mobs quickly and brutally using their wide range of combination moves. They are also worthy scouts, being able to sneak past monsters if they are skilled enough to see what lies beyond. And there’s no one better than a Rogue at the end of a dungeon when a locked chest or door stands between you and hard-earned riches.

Please keep in mind that there is not meant to be one "master" of any role. In a game with this many classes, our goal is to make the classes roughly equal in efficiency when filling the roles they're meant to fill, though the gameplay experience may vary between classes. Tanks are not meant to be carbon copies of each other, for example; they should all fill that role efficiently, however, enough so that they are viable and wanted in groups for that role. Each crowd control class should be able to manage small groups at times; they may simply be more effective in certain situations. We are aware that some classes do not currently fill the roles given to them; the development team is hard at work bringing them closer to their planned purpose.
- Caydiem -
Assistant Community Manager, WoW

But what does that entail, exactly, for the rogue?

I'm sorry, but to me it is all pretty vague. I understand that we get a variety of skills and abilities to use to adapt to situations. The problem is that those kills and abilities are *too* weak to be able to rely on any one ability for that situation.

Lets take Sap for example. It's a CC ability, yes. However, the problem is that it is unreliable for the rogue to maintain. Mainly because the rogue has a one-shot chance (two with vanish, and even then that is a 10-min deal on recharge) to control one mob. If the rogue misses, or accidentally agro's more mobs and blows that chance to sap, it's over. That is the reason most people take a mage or warlock for needed CC. Their CC method is fast, accurate (within the limits of the spell), and can be recast many times in one instance.

Another example is damage. You just stated that the "stealthy Rogue is an up-close damage dealer."
That is all well and good, but we are a sustained damage dealer, meaning we do the most amount of damage over a long period of time. With current fights lasting a whole 15-25 seconds, that means dill. Zero. Zip. Nada.
Only another reason why people generally will pick heavy fast damage over a rogue.

[Edit] Let us not forget that we are also single-target fighters. Most of our damage comes from staying on one mob to be most effective. Some situtations make it hard for us to do that, thus we end up switching targets and reducing our possible damage. I encounter this a lot using a 'stun build' when I need to stop casters from casting. Other classes do not lose their chance to do more damage by switching targets.

All in all, the fact remains that as a class, we ARE balance. We just are not balanced with the other classes out there.

At least in my opinion.

 8. Re: The Class Role | 10/23/2004 9:41:15 PM GMTDT
While scouting is certainly fun and in many cases useful, chances are that there will be several people in a given instance group that know the instance well enough not to need scouting as Rogue's can offer it. Unless future instances have dynamics beyond simple roaming mobs, I do not see scouting as nearly as useful as crowd control, especially since scouting either creates downtime as the group waits for the scout, or it sacrifices the Rogue's damage as teh Rogue scouts while the group fights behind.

Locked doors are laregly irrelevant, since Rogues cannot pick many of the 'important' doors in instances, and the rest all have keys that you will generally pick up on your way down with little added effort. Add in seaforium charges and picking locked doors is hardly a reason to invite a Rogue into a group.

Locked chests are a bit different, and I have not dealt directly with the 'named' chests in higher level dungeons. Do these chests have key drops, and are some unpickable by Rogues? The named chests are the only ones that really drop anything of value, according to Thottbot, and if they often have keys as an additional option, or especially as the only option, then picking chests is also not much of an enticement for groups.

Which leaves us with damage, which we are certainly capable of doing, assuming that mobs are not inorganic high armor types, or large groups that require area effects to be effecient, or that put out short range point blank area effect damage during combat, or where you need to control multiple adds, since all of those situations are better handled by a Mage.

Certainly Rogues can offtank periodically with evasion and gouge, and blind is occassionally useful, when it is not parried, but all classes have useful five minute abilities, and a Rogue's lot seems far less group friendly, since evasion requries that you keep aggro for 15 seconds, and blind gives no visual inidcation to prevent group members from breaking it without even knowing that you blinded.

Q u o t e:
In a game with this many classes, our goal is to make the classes roughly equal in efficiency when filling the roles they're meant to fill, though the gameplay experience may vary between classes.


If efficiency is so important, why are two out of our three 'roles' not related to efficiency at all? Scouting is in fact the very opposite of efficiency in most cases, because without the occassional roaming adds, five minute abilities rarely get used to their full potential, and things are certainly less fun. I cannot think of many situations where fighting through roaming adds would be less efficient than spending time scouting and waiting for the roamers to not be a direct threat.

As far as I can tell, Rogue and Mage damage is comparable in many situations. However there are more situations where a Mage is superior to a Rogue than vice versa, because as far as I know ther are far more large groups of mobs, high armor inorganic mobs, mobs with point blank area effects, and certainly more sheepable mobs, than highly resistant mobs, or reasonably sappable mobs, or non-sheepable mobs where Rogue offtanking is preferable to some other means of crowd control.

The bottom line is that Rogues need more versatility in groups, and we need our current skills, such as Sap, Blind, Vanish, et al, to actually work in ways that make them worth the time, money, and inventory space that they currently require.
