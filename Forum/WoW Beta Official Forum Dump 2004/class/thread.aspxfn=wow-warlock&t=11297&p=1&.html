Ok, here are a few things I was thinking about while I was shaving on how to make the infernal a pet and not have people cry nerf when warlocks get them.

One idea I had to even out the amount of pets we have and when were such as this....

Level 1 - imp

Level 10 - Voidwalker

Level 20 - Succubus

Level 30 - Felhound or Felhunter (what ever it is now)

Now this is where it gets interesting

Level 40 - Felstead and minor infernal (requires 5 soul shards to summon, as well as 30 min cool down) or a VW that is meant for PVP (meaning taunt works on players, and our enemies will actually want to kill them instead of going straight for the warlock)

level 50 - Infernal (requires 8 soul shards to summon with 1 hour cool down) or Felhound for PVP

Level 60 - Higher Infernal (10 soul shards to summon with 2 1/2 hour cool down) and Nightmare

Different levels for infernals as we progress that cost more soul shards to summon and a long cool down so warlocks are not summoning them every chance they get and we dont ignore our other pets.

Another idea, to make it so we are not summoning an infernal 24/7 would we have a new spell that we would created a stone that would capture a infernal stone from the one lands in Kalidimor where we can kill them (sorry cant remember the area's name) with this stone we would need to use it either 2-4 times to full fill it up, once done we are able to summon an infernal. Also, we can only carry one stone with us at a time which means we wont use it often and we would have to travel back there to refill it, this would be for level 50-60.

The 2nd last idea, that I see for us that would make it interesting, and make warlocks wanted for raids, would be is that we are not able to summon an infernal unless we are in a raid party of 20+ for every 20 players in the raid party, 1 warlock will be able to summon an infernal, if I am correct, there can be 60 people in one raid party? so that means 3 warlocks can summon 3 infernals for the party. Once again, this is for high level warlocks.

The final idea would be that it requires 5 warlocks to summon one infernal, like our summon players except it has to be 5 warlocks, not one warlock and 2 other players.

So those are my ideas behind infernals.
