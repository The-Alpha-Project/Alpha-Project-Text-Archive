OK, let's run your numbers. Dazzling Mithril Rapier

http://www.thottbot.com/wow/index.cgi?i=11641

and Battlefield Destroyer

http://www.thottbot.com/wow/?i=12206

I'm assuming a character at level 43 that hits every time and never crits. This makes the math easier, and we'll assume to hit and crit chance are equal with both swords. Given that assumption, misses and crits cancel each other out. I'll also assume that full Crusader Strike (75) is up on the extra Holy Strikes the rapier gets. We'll also assume that HS is activated the instant it cools down.

The rapier has a swing speed of 1.7, so it takes 6 swings to get 1 HS. It takes 10.2 seconds to fire. The destroyer has a swing speed of 3.1, so it takes 4 swings to get 1 HS. It takes 12.4 seconds to fire.

Given these numbers (and unlimited mana, to make math easy and scale well), the rapier gets 58 HS to the destroyer's 50. The rapier gets 8 more, at an average damage of 129 + 75 = 204. Total extra damage of 1632. Over 600 seconds, that is a 2.72 DPS advantage for the rapier. But, according to Thott, the destroyer has a DPS that is 8.2 higher than the rapier. That's a 5.48 DPS advantage for the destroyer. Over 600 seconds that is 3288 more damage from the destroyer. Without CS and HS, the advantage would be 4920 for the destroyer.

Let's try some other numbers:

two 2-handers - Frost Tiger Blade - speed 3.4, DPS 26.2, level 35

http://www.thottbot.com/wow/?i=3927

Gutrender - speed 2.6, DPS 27.5, level 36

http://www.thottbot.com/wow/?i=8348

and 2 1-handers, Widow Blade - speed 1.9, DPS 22.9, level 38

http://www.thottbot.com/wow/?i=11780

Olmann Sewar - speed 2.7, DPS 21.1, level 36

http://www.thottbot.com/wow/?i=1957

FTB - 10.2 seconds per HS - 58 HS
Gutrender - 10.4 seconds per HS - 57 HS
Widow Blade - 11.4 seconds per HS - 52 HS
Olmann Sewar - 10.8 seconds per HS - 55 HS

Both of these 2-handers give you MORE Holy Strikes than the 1-hander.

Revelation:
This shows us that lower speed is not necessarily better. The best speed is actually one that has a multiple that is slightly over 10. So 2.6 (10.4) is better than 2.4 (12.2).


As for 2-handers being the weapon of choice for most situations, well, that's personal preference. I currently use a 1-hander (Olmann Sewar) and shield. I used to use Electrocutioner Leg and shield, along with Archeus when I wanted to use a 2-hander. When my 1-hander out-DPS'd my 2-hander, I sold it.

Given the parameters above, a 2-hander will always out DPS an equivalent 1-hander. This will usually end the fight faster, and taking less damage by ending the fight sooner might balance out taking less damage by using a shield. A 2-hander will probably be clearly superior when taking on a caster instead of someone who uses physical damage.

This makes the DPS on normal hits higher than listed for the short battles, but it evens out with long ones. The Holy Strike and Crusader Strike I used are the ones that a level 43 Paladin would have.

12.4 seconds, normal hits (no special attacks)
DMR - 360 dmg (29 DPS)
BD - 537.5 dmg (43.3 DPS)
The Battlefield Destroyer does 49% more damage.

12.4 seconds using Holy Strikes (2)
DMR - 618 dmg (49.8 DPS)
BD - 795.5 dmg (64.1 DPS)

12.4 seconds using Crusader Strikes (5) + Holy Strikes (2)
DMR - 923 dmg (74.4 DPS)
BD - 1038 dmg (83.7 DPS)
The Battlefield Destroyer does 12% more damage.

62 seconds, normal hits (no special attacks)
DMR - 1620 dmg (26.1 DPS)
BD - 2150 dmg (34.6 DPS)
The Battlefield Destroyer does 32% more damage. DPS would be as listed except the DMR was halfway through a swing while the BD got an exact amount of them.

62 seconds using Holy Strikes (6)
DMR - 2439 dmg (39.3 DPS)
BD - 3031.5 dmg (48.9 DPS)
The Battlefield Destroyer does 24% more damage.


Clearly, the more a Paladin uses Holy/Crusader Strikes the less difference there is between slow 2h and fast 1h weapons, especially with shorter battles. This reveals a flaw in implementation.

I want to follow this up with a discussion about damage reduction. First, let's look at the armor damage reduction formula, from thottbot.com:

http://www.thottbot.com/wow/?formula=1

(damage reduction) = (.3 * (armor class - 1)/(10*level+89))

At level 41, my AC is about 950 with a shield and Devo. My shield is 164, and Devo is 130. So here are some numbers (all numbers are approximate):

2-hander - AC 650. Reduction of about 39%.
2-hander with Devo - AC 780. Reduction of about 47%.
1-hander with shield and Devo - AC 950. Reduction of about 57%.

With Devo on, that's a 10% reduction of total damage using a 1-hander with a shield, or about a 19% reduction in actual damage.

How do I figure that? Let's say a mob hits for 100. A 47% reduction gives you a hit for 53. A 57% reduction gives you a hit for 43. Taking 10 off of a hit for 53 is more significant than taking 10 off a hit for 100.

If you run more numbers, you find something very interesting - the higher your AC is, the better it is to get even more AC. Each unit of AC blocks the same % of the total damage, but blocks a higher and higher % of the actual damage.

Using this formula, an AC of approximately 1664 at level 41 would block all damage. At 1336, you block 80% and take 20%. Adding a shield to that to get to 1500 will block 90% and take 10%, but your actual damage will be halfed!

The conclusion - the more AC you have, the better it is for you to go 1-hander/shield instead of 2-hander. The more AC you have, the better it is for you to use Devotion Aura.


I noticed these results last night when I was switching between Retribution and Devotion. Then I stacked on the shield and saw my damage taken drop a lot. Taking 40 per hit instead of 50 is only 10, but it means you can stay in the fight for 25% longer without healing or running. (40 hits to 1600 HP at 40 per hit vs 32 hits to 1600 HP at 50 per hit)
