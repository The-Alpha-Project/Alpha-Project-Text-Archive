We got a lot of feedback that the last round of trees overly-incentivized builds that were heavily spread between the tabs (too little specialization).

There were several factors behind the decision to move the weapon specs farther down the tree...

1. We didn't want to lure players into commiting to a particular weapon type too early in their character development, when they have very little knowledge of magic items in WoW (remember, when the game goes live players won't be able to re-spec talents easily, and the vast majority of players will be new to WoW and thus not have had any prior experience with the characters).

2. We didn't feel that the more direct dps-related talents were well distributed accross all tiers (in combat and assassination). It felt like there was too much dps in the early tiers and not enough in the later tiers. In theory, if the dps is pretty well distributed then lots of builds become viable.

Q u o t e:
Then solve the problem by setting a level requirement in order to do certain things without forcing the player to over commit in a tree to do it. Problem solved.


I would say that's a pretty exceptionally bad solution.

First, it complicates the system that much more since talents would potentially have tier requirements, pre-requisite requirements, and level requirements. The system is already complex enough.

Second, it means a player trying to work their way down the tree would be forced to take a talent they didn't necessarily want in the long run in order to get to some talents farther down in the tree, only to later double up in the earlier tier getting that talent they actually wanted but couldn't get before because it was placed prematurely in the tree with a level requirement.

And no, it wouldn't make it better to allow them to spend the points, but not let them get the benefit from the points, since that's even less understandable then the above, and even if they did get it, would frustrate the player since we'd be giving them weird time-delay talents that they could pick now, but not use until later.

So, problem solved? Definitely not. If what you really want is another talent that increases crit chance or something like that, it would be far more reasonable to just ask for something like that then propose mucking up the system in order to jimmy a talent into a tier it doesn't belong.
