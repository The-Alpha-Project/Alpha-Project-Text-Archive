 22. Re: Why I respecced to destruction | 10/19/2004 10:28:08 AM GMTDT
I think the fact that people can't agree which tree is better, means these 2 trees are reasonably well balanced against each other.

Destruction isn't bad at all, you can side step the bad skills.

Devastation+Holocaust = 6 credits spent for an average of a 5% damage improvement across a fair number of spells. That's about typical for something at this level in the tree.

Inferno - Most class trees have this equivalent skill, 10% on one class of magic, and it's good.

Shadowburn - Good.

Range - Always useful.

Cataclysm seems out of place. In this tree, you should be killing faster, not more efficiently. Reduced mana costs belong in the Affliction tree.

Improved Searing Pain - this is very poor. It's a 10% average improvement to ONE skill (assuming you get Holocaust). Kind of pricy to optimize this skill, given that it's kind of weak on damage, and has a built in taunt.

Improved Immolate - Poor. The initial damage it is improving by 25%, is only 33% of the total damage, so you are only getting about an 8% improvement to ONE spell from this. In that respect, it's even worse than Improved Searing Pain.

Assuming Improved Immolate and Conflagrate get improvements, I would get Cataclysm, Bane, Shadowburn, Devastation, Destructive Reach, 2 in Searing Pain (would prefer to avoid spending these), Holocaust, Improved Immolate, Inferno, Conflagrate, for 32 points.

In Affliction I'd go Corruption, Life Tap, 3 Suppression, 5 Fel Concentration, 2 Grim Reach. Last 2 credits I haven't decided what to do, maybe another Suppression and Amplify Curse.

I like Demonic Embrace, but it seems to go against a Destruction warlock, which is about killing quicker, rather than living longer. An extra hitpoint/mana buffer doesn't do anything for you unless you find yourself needing that buffer.

Bane/Intensity/Pyroclasm seem to go together, but AOEing is way too situational. I'll live with the default implementations.
