It's move should increase swim by 50% or more (talent possibly). Also having some kind of sonar (works like tracking but only in water), sonar-stun (AoE centered on Seal), whirlpool (like entangle but in water) or other unique water-based spells would be welcome.

As a marine biologist, I look forward to more underwater quests and exploration.

The fact that the 'travel' form is a cat is redundant and I agree another form, like a bird, would be much more welcome. Variety should be the key to the Druid. With automatic feather-fall ability of course.

Another vote here for cat-form finishing move, as well as more bear abilities. Perhaps allowing the swipe to knock multiple people down. Or a charge that knocks multiple people down or stuns. Bears are BIG afterall and should have that to their advantage.

I don't understand this 'multiple bags of alternate equipment' thats needed for shifting. That really sounds wrong for the flavor of a druid. Skip the middle-man on that and just make the forms better.

Would love to either use Wands or Bows.

I'm not high enough to have seen any of the portals into the Dream, but that sounds amazing. Can't wait.

Rez is a tough one. I think we're already much better than Priests, in that we can take and deal much more damage, but we should have some kind of rez as a back-up. I really like the Reincarnation rez idea. The pull-spirit near body is also great and can at least help with runs; with the resurection timer this wouldn't be too powerful either.

I actually love the 'jack-of-all-trades' role and I've never had anyone deny me a place in a group because I was a druid. Mark and Thorns are great buffs and being able to heal, sneak, fight, whatever always seems welcome.

If they made all our forms as good as the Warrior/Rogue/Priest then everyone would want us and no-one else. So we can't be quite as good. But we should still be able to fill those roles effectively when we need to. That's going to take some serious balancing, game wise. And so far, its the best Druid I've ever seen in any computer game.

So kudos to the development team for the great start and for letting US help with the design. Can't ask for more than that.
