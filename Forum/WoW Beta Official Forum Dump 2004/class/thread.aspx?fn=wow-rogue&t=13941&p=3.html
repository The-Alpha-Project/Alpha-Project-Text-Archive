 47. Re: Best all round rogue, talent setup...? | 10/22/2004 2:47:58 PM GMTDT
As nice as it would be to have a "best" whatever talent set up, you're not going to find it. It is all preference in the end. Blade Flurry/Adren builds work great but if you don't enjoy it, what is the point right?

Blade Flurry/Adren allows you to deal some nice damage to a mob while working off a mob next to it. For places where your getting pulls with more than one mob it is an excellent set up to go with. I don't like it much for PvP so I won't comment on that. However, Blade Flurry is a 2 min cooldown, which isn't bad but Adren is on a 6 min cooldown so it does limit you to when you can use those skills to be dealing that optimum damage.

Assassination builds, mainly going for building combo points fast with Cold Blood handy is more stable damage, not really relying on a skill with a cooldown works quite nice PvE if your using maces/swords. You can build 5 combo points as quick as 3 attacks ending it with Kidney shot or Evis which will restore 25 energy(If you took Relentless which I don't see a reason not to) and possibly starting you off with a combo point as well. Cold Blood on a 5 point Evis does nice damage as well but even without it your doing a good amount of damage here. PvP, I find this much better suited when facing single opponents. The only problem is your relying on your crits to give you these combo points. If your unlucky in a fight and don't crit at all(Which doesn't happen often) then half those skills are of no use.

Subltey, I don't usually go too far into this talent line as I don't find the later skills as useful as the other lines but going with this line it is easily assumed you'll be working with Ambush/Backstab. I don't particularly like this build with instances but for soloing purposes it works out very well. Ambush/Backstab openings can deal out a large amount of damage almost killing the mob in that one shot, you just can't do that on elites. Even though it is a large amount of damage it is unreliable since if you don't crit your damage is pretty pathetic. PvP wise, if your landing a crit you can basicly take out a mage and vanish out of there before the group realizes what happened if you play it right. In the end thoguh, this is a really unreliable source of damage. If you crit, your gold, if you don't your in a tight spot.

These are the most popular routes most rogues tend to take. For instances I usually pick up Assassination/Combat talents letting me build my combo points fast to do my damage and it works decently for PvP when I don't respec. However, for soloing and PvP I tend to stick with Ambush/BS builds. Of course thats my opinion and I pick my skills according to how I play my rogue. So in the end it really is how you enjoy playing since each route offers a different play style but as long as you enjoy that style of play you'll be fine.

As for what you suggested, my opinion on a few of the skills:

Deflection/Riposte, PvE wise really isn't that important. For the most part you can avoid ever taking aggro so the extra 5% into parry doesn't play much of a role nor will Riposte. The points have better uses in other skills. Soloing, it'll help, till you run into mobs who don't carry weapons which happens pretty often. While it is a fun skill PvP but your going with a 10%(Parry never seems to increase so I'm assuming 10% after Deflection) isn't much to go on.

Camo, it isn't an important skill in my opinion. The extra speed stealth is nice but not really needed. People will still run faster than you do stealthed regardless if you have it or not. The skill is just for convenience sake and 5 points is a lot to pay for just that.

Remorseless is a little difficult to say really. Since the nerf of it this patch it isn't as useful as it used to be but not a horrible skill either. If your soloing, without Rapid Concealment you won't be using that skill much. In instances with mobs back to back you'll have more of a chance in using it but only if you land the killing blow. In my opinion those 3 points are better spent in Ruthlessness.

Really off the top of my head, I believe if you want to go all around. Meaning if you don't know who you'll be fighting next or what type of place you'll be fighting in, going with talents such as Ruthlessness, Lethality, Seal Fate would be best. Since it gives you quick combo points to do some good damage with be it mob or player.

Kind of a long read I know, but you asked for an opinion so there it is.

Edit:Forgot to mention, come retail without being sure on how we'll be able to repsec I'll more than likely go with an Assassination/Subltey build for Ambush with Seal Fate/Ruthlessness/Cold Blood. Honestly, while I do have a build in mind I'm constantly switching around talents here and there to adjust.
