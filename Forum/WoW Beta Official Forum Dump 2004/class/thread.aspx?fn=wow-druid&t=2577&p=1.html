 1. Summon Drink as a Druid Ability | 7/30/2004 8:34:17 AM GMTDT
I primarily play a mage, and although I enjoy it I believe the present incarnation to be out of balance with both the environment and other classes. To that end, I have tried to think of the best way to bring them back into balance without sacrificing either dps or their crowd control, abilities that I value highly.

While doing so, it also occured to me that it would be possible to kill two birds with one stone so to speak and with that in mind, I believe the best suggestion is to move summon drink off mages and onto druids. Here are my reasons.

1) Currently mages stack too well. The best group is a warrior, a priest, and three mages. Even after rogues are buffed so they do similar damage against a single target, it is likely the best group will remain one warrior, one priest and three mages unless aoe spells are nerfed Severely . Having one class take up three out of five groups in the "ideal group" is a recipe for disaster. Actually, its a recipe for a guild full of mage twink, which is what I am seeing now. /wave Cadorn, Scarlett, Byd, Parody etc.

2) Summoning water gives the druid a way to contribute to the group independent of their present form since it can be summoned ahead of time.

3) It addresses one of the present limitation of forms, namely that they are expensive to slip in and out of. Forms could remain expensive; however, being able to summon drink would largely mitigate that penalty so that one could change form, down a drink, buff the party and shift back.

4) Summoning water would promote class interdependence. In order for the other dps class, rogues, to do anything approaching their max dps, they must have the support of a tank and a healer. With the exception of encounters where mobs are have a ranged attack (rare) or snare immune (really rare) a mage needs only himself. If summon drink is moved off mages and to another support class, that would provide a massive incentive for mages to group with that support class. This is a GOOD THING.

5) Summoning water gives a druid a viable role in a group even if a priest is present. If Everquest taught us anything, it is that if one class is perceived as the best healer then for “challenging” they are considered the only viable healer. Druids in WOW are intended to be diverse; however, that diversity means they are always going to be secondary healers, especially for “challenging content”. Druids are going to need a place in such groups and the ability to summon drink would give them that.

6) Due to the size of their mana pools druids gain less from being able to summon drink than other classes such as priests or mages. This limitation is a check to the power of this ability in solo situations.

7) Summoning food and water fit within the RP aspect of a druid. This is a small point, but I feel nonetheless that it is worth mentioning.
