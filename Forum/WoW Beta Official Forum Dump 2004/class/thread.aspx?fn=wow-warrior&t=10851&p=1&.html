 5. Re: Why isn't dual wield as good as two-handed? | 10/18/2004 5:30:07 PM GMTDT
At my level, I have a 51 dps 2 handed axe, a 41 dps one handed axe, and a 36 dps offhand axe.

If all hit 100%, the dual wield would do more damage (41 + 18)=59 dps.

However, there's the inherent miss chance.

2hander: 51 dps * .95 (5% miss chance)=48.45 dps.
Dual wield: 59 dps * .76 (24% miss chance)=44.84 dps. To equal 48.45 dps (that the 2 hander gives with the 95% chance to hit), with that inherent dual wield miss chance, you'd need dual wield weapons of 63.75 dps (backsolving is great). There are 40+ dps offhanders available, but you'll have to do some serious instance delving or hope for an epic world drop to get them. At the same time, there is a 76 dps 2 handed sword out there (epic from BRS) and some 60 dps instance dropped 2 handers.

 8. Re: Why isn't dual wield as good as two-handed? | 10/18/2004 6:13:12 PM GMTDT
From my experience the DPS has generally been a wash. They balance the weapon DPS based on the miss rates set in game. There are weapons available at top end that will make dual wields DPS the same as top end 2h weapons.

The difference is dual wield gives steadier dmg and rage generation as even though you miss more often you are swinging around 3-4 times as often so the misses even out faster. 2H weapons get a lot more spikes in damage as two misses in a row cuts out 6-7 seconds of swing time but two hits in a row puts out more damage than dual wield is likely since DW will have missed a few times in that same timeframe.

DW also lets you proc more often as you get 2 weapons and gives you more stat bonuses, but in reality they balance around this and 2h weapon procs are generally stronger and they have bigger bonuses on them. So I think thats mostly a wash too.

The specials really favor 2h weapons though.
