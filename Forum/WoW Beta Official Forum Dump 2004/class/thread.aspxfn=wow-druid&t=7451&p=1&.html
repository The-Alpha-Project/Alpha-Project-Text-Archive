 2. Re: Good Trade Skills for Druid? | 10/10/2004 1:09:43 AM GMTDT
Those are pretty much the two to choose from. Engineering might come in useful, but doesn't really fit with the class.

I've had both; I started with Alchemy and Herbalism, then started buying stacks and stacks of leather to train up Leatherworking. When they announced shortly thereafter the change to professions, I decided to make a change and go for leatherworking and skinning, which I had trained up to artisan levels.

I found though, that I really missed my potions. The few craftable items that I would actually wear were easily attainable at the Auction House, while the potions I had come to depend on often were not. Coupled with the sheer tediousness of the leatherworking recipe quests, which involve killing over 1000 turtles, I didn't really enjoy it.

I was trying to solo Margol the Rager, an elite mob two or three levels below me, when I decided that it would be simple with a Nature Protection potion. Unable to find one for sale, I made the decision to go back to herbalism/alchemy.
