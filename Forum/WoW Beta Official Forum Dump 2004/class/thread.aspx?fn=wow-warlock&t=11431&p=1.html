 1. Write-up of current Warlock bugs / issues. | 10/12/2004 4:44:32 PM GMTDT
Feel free to add your experiences and I'll try to keep the list updated. Remember to give both problem and possible solution.

--------
Bugs
--------

1. The Succubus keeps repeating her shake-grin-spank routine every 2 seconds. This gets VERY annoying to listen to, and you end up turning off your sound.

2. When casting a Shadow Bolt, if the target is out of range when the casting finishes, you sometimes get locked up and cannot cast spells. Same thing with Searing Pain. Workaround: press ESC.
Error message: "Another action is in progress".
UPDATE: This is ALL Warlock spells that require a target. The lock-up happens when a monster is out of range or dead when the spell finishes and is cast.

3. Curse of Agony is now considered being the Warlock physically HITTING the monster. This gives it a chance to crit, but it also interrupts any drains that might be running, every tick.
This makes it nearly impossible for a Warlock to get a Shard while using Agony. Possibly ALL physical damage cancels drains. I've tested with Succubus, she didn't seem to cancel. Also interrupts non-monster Drains such as Health Funnel. Note: Only your OWN Agony will cancel Drains. Another Warlock's Agony on your mob will not affect draining.
UPDATE: Fixed!


4. When Night Fall procs, all drains are cancelled. UPDATE: Fixed!

5. Channeling spells seem to get stuck in casting animation on different occasions. There are a couple of ways to avoid this, either by casting Health Funnel or recasting the channeled spell just as it is ending, but neither are all that great for permanent solutions.

6. Amplify Curse boosts all curses for 10 seconds.

7. In certain cases, using the Voidwalker (maybe only in instances?) will result in getting locked in combat mode.

8. Spirit might be less effective for Warlocks than for other classes. Can someone confirm? UPDATE: I just tested this, it DOES NOT seem to be the case. Let me know if you have results suggesting otherwise.

9. If attacked while on a mount, sometimes, the pet will attack the mob, although in passive mode.

10. Warrior and Priest fear now land on Undead. Not directly Warlock-related though, unless someone can confirm whether Warlock Fear / HoT works on Undead?

11. After testing with Curufin, we've come to the conclusion that Hellfire STILL does not stun at all, when you have the stun-talent. Update: Same problem with Rain of Fire.

12. In the talent window, the Improved Drain Mana displays the damage done as being 15%, both when trained once and twice. It should work properly, this is purely a display bug.

13. When using the Imp's Blood Pact with the Improved Imp talent, the added Stamina is not shown when hovering over the buff. The stamina IS added correctly, and it IS calculated corretly with Demonic Embrace, again, this is just a display bug.

14. Improved Drain Mana unlearns itself upon zoning, much like Pyroclasm last patch.

15. The damage done by Improved Drain Mana cancels the casting of Drain Mana itself, e.g. it works like Curse of Agony.

16. The Improved Healthstone talent adds damage to Curse of Agony.

17. Improved Drain Soul has a chance to proc about 50% of the time, even without talents invested. Can anyone elaborate ? UPDATE:
If you have points in the affliction tree below Improved Drain Soul, but do not have points in Improved Drain Soul itself, it acts as if you have one point in it.
Rank 1/2
Improved Drain Soul
Requires 5 points in Mastery
Gives you a 50% chance to get a 100% increase to your Mana regeneration for 10 sec if the target dies while you drain its soul. In addition your Mana may continue to regenerate while casting at 50% of normal.

18. Certain members of a 5-man group did not get Blood Pact. Possibly a display bug. The "regular" display bug does not show no icon at all, it shows an icon with less stamina than what is actually added.

19. People are having trouble gaining a shard from Shadowburn. Apparently the highest rank DOES grant a shard upon killing, but two people around 50 do NOT get shards. Could more people give feedback on their results? UPDATE: It seems this spell is working correctly. People confirm that all uses vs exp-giving mobs have been succesful.

20. Improved Healthstone only works on your own Healthstone. Unclear whether this is a bug, or just an issue. Either way, it needs to be changed.

21. Improved Drain Soul does not trigger on rank 2, where it has 100% chance to proc. This could be due to the bug where it unlearns itself. Can anyone confirm otherwise?

22. The Imp refuses to attack, and simply runs around. Is anyone able to reproduce this?

23. The Imp's Blood Pact is not always working on all members of a party. If the icon is not there, neither is the actual buff, so it's not just a display bug. Workaround: relog or wait.

24. Could be intended, blue text please: Regarding Improved Soul Drain
Right now, if the target doesn't die from the actual drain soul damage (the shadow damage it does every 3 seconds) then you will not receive the Improved Drain soul bonus, no matter what the source; be it your DoTs, another party member, your pet, etc.

25. The range bonus from Grim Reach is not being applied to Curse of Agony, Curse of Exhaustion, or Fear. Will further test when the server is up again.

26. Level 4 of Shadow Mastery does not improve the damage of Corruption, Drain life or Drain Soul. It does improve Shadow Bolt

27. Shadow Mastery damage increase is not reflected in the de-buff hover icon on the enemy mob when casting either of the 3 above skills.

28. Certain spells are not able to be recast until the first one runs out. You get the "a more powerful spell is already active" message. So far, this has been experienced with Corruption and Curse of Exhaustion.

29. Soulsstones possibly do not clear hatelists in instances. Could this be because there is no leash range in instances?

30. When Night Fall procs while casting a Shadow Bolt, it goes away again as soon as the bolt is cast. It needs to either make the current bolt instant, or the next one.

31. With Improved Curse of Exhaustion, CoE still only shows as 10% reduction, on the icon under the monsters hp bar.

Issues
----------------------


Blizzard Employees please note: This is not 'just' a suggestions thread. This is a thread containing REAL issues and problems with the Warlock, and our attemps to fix these problems. These are not some "possible" additions to the Warlock, but things that DO need addressing asap.



A.
Problem: We still need offensive weapons vs Undead melee classes. When facing Undead melee, our only choice is to run and hope to get away.

First fix: Give us some sort of root / stun that is NOT pet-based. We need to have a chance regardless of what pet we have out, if any. When fighting Undead it becomes painstackingly obvious how much we rely on fear in duels. We shouldn't have to be a one-spell-wonder.

Second fix:
Suggested by Dubb:
Right now, I'm Horde, but I can understand from an Alliance stand point the problem with UD being immune to Fear, that's why I want to suggest another alternative. One of two things, Demonic Sleep or Demonic Root, Demonic Sleep would put your target to sleep for the duration of 10 sec at level one just like Fear, and have the same skill scaling. Also make sleep break upon taking damage. As for Demonic Root, it would be like Druid root only you could make the roots look all shadowy, maybe make it shorter then the Druid one. I'd like to see one or the other.


B.
Problem: Sacrifice got changed in all the wrong ways. The change was, no doubt, meant to stop Hellfire bombing, which WAS an exploitable imbalance. I, along with most other Warlocks, believe it was the wrong way to stop it though. Instead, it should have kept its total immunity for 10 seconds, but instead not block Hellfire self-damage. Currently, it does not function as a get-out-of-jail card, but as a bit more hitpoints than the Warlock already has. Concider this: Whenever you need it, it's because you're dying fast. At 54, I have nearly thrice the hitpoints it absorbs, so is another 33% really going to help me ?

Fix: Turn it back to it's original state, but make it so it doesn't block hellfire self-damage. Problem solved.


C.
Problem: Soulstones got screwed. Instead of a time of choice within 5 minutes, they're now destined to go off after 5 secnods. Problem is, 5 seconds is rarely enough for all monsters to be gone. This is especially a problem if you die near patrollers and want to rez when they're the furthest from you. Another issue is in PvP where anyone killing a Warlock can just wait around for 5 seconds and kill him again.
UPDATE: Many people claim this was changed because Soulstones were used to skip content in dungeons. Wake up - this is what Mind Soothe is being used for !

First fix: Either the 5 seconds timer needs to be changed to something more, or it needs to be reverted back to the holder choosing when to rez. If the fixed timer is kept, it needs to, AT LEAST, give the holder a choice when killed in PvP. There's no use in everyone knowing to re-kill a Warlock X seconds later. Other fix suggestions: allow the user to return with a higher % health and mana and/or without rez sickness.

Second fix:
Raijion's suggestion:
  • Leave the 5 second automatic firing of the soulstone.
  • Bring the player back to the body in ghost form.
  • Implement a 30 second res timer from the point at which the player returned to their body as a ghost.


    D.
    Problem: horrible layout / system of Demonology talent-tree.

    Fix:
    Suggestion by Stormbull:

    Demonology

    - Make all of the stone talents require the other. Here's my breakdown of how I would revise all of the shard -> 'stone' talents:

    Master Conjurer - 2/2
    Improved Healthstone - Requires 5 Points in Demonology (and requires Master Conjurer 2/2)
    Improved Firestone - Requires 10 points in Demonology (and requires Improved Healthstone 2/2)
    Improved Spellstone - Requires 20 (twenty) points in Demonology (and requires Improved Firestone 2/2)

    All in all, you spent 8 points total improving your stones, and you need 20 to get into improved spellstone. It's almost the same cost, and it makes more sense, but this frees up the tree (in terms of places to put talents) for more important areas -- the CORE OF DEMONOLOGY AND WARLOCKS, their pets.

    Here's what the revised Demonology tree should look like (according to me):
    Tier 1:
    Master Conjurer
    Demonic Embrace
    Improved Imp
    Tier 2:
    Improved Healthstone (2/2 Master Conjurer Needed)
    Improved Health Funnel
    Improved Voidwalker
    Fel Intellect
    Tier 3:
    Improved Firestone (2/2 Improved Healthstone Needed)
    Improved Succubus
    Fel Stamina (5/5 Fel Intellect Needed)
    Fel Domination
    Tier 4:
    Master Summoner (1/1 Fel Domination Needed)
    Improved Enslave Demon (brought down from tier 5)
    Tier 5:
    Demonic Sacrifice
    Improved Spellstone (2/2 in Improved Firestone needed)
    Unholy Power (5/5 in Fel Stamina needed, brought down from tier 6)
    Tier 6:
    Soul Link (1/1 in Demonic Sacrfice needed)

    Look at all this space we freed up for good talents! Almost all of tier 6 and now we have place for a talent worthy of 30 points to spend in this tree! Not only that, but now we have more options during the lower levels for this tree, instead of "i'll find a way to spend 30 points".

    Banish was "fixed" in all the wrong ways. Now, the spell only effects Demons and Elementals, rendered useless in PvP.

    Suggested change:

    Banish
    - Increase Mana Cost
    - Return (1) Soul Shard cost
    - Make it last 30 seconds (no random timer)
    - Have it work on all types of targets (humanoids, beasts, etc.), only one target can be banished at a time per warlock.
    - Make it 0 cooldown (due to upped cost/soulshard)

    My basic reasoning for this is because Warlocks need to hold more CC power than they currently do in PvP. Mages have polymorph, Warlocks should have banish. The reason behind the 0 cooldown is because if it costs a soul shard to use, the warlock has to ration it out with his other shard-requiring spells. If he wants to do nothing but banish, he limits his arsenal to that + regular spells.

    So what? I've never heard complaints about Banish in its previous form. Polymorph lasts 50 seconds if untouched and gives a high HP regen. Why can't Banish last 30 and take a player out of combat -- especially at the cost of a shard? What was the problem with this spell before? I felt it was a Warlock staple.
