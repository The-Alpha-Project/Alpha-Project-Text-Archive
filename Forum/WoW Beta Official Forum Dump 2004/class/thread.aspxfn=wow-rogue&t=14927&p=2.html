Stormbull obviously made a mistake and wrote shaman under cloth when he meant druid. Otherwise, he's correct about class to armor breakdown. Of course, since the distribution of players to classes is clearly unequal, it's not quite right to say that armor drops should be in the same ratio. If nothing else, paladins and shamans are going to be less common because they can only be played on one faction or the other. However, that would only further imply that plate and mail drops should be less common than other drops (for each faction, only three classes can wear plate or mail, vs. five classes for the other two armor types).

Anyway, regarding rogue roles: Caydiem's post indicates we have three, namely damage, scouting, and unlocking things.

Damage: In my opinion we are doing well enough in this department; I haven't fought excessively high AC mobs in quite some time, so I think they addressed this either by changing how armor works or by changing a lot of mobs. I would say that we don't have quite the "wide range of combination moves" as you might think: we only have three combo point generating moves, one of which is rarely used in groups while dealing damage (gouge), and any given rogue tends to only use one or the other in groups based on their talent build and weapon choice. More combo generating abilities are needed. We're doing ok with number of finishers, but it would still be nice to see a few more which flesh out a rogue's utility. For example, I'd like to see a snare ability that lasts a bit longer than kidney shot.

Scouting: As some people have pointed out, scouting by itself is not very useful in dungeons, unless you change how dungeons can work. Once you learn the placement of mobs and where wanderers are, etc., being able to scout ahead doesn't matter. The mob placement and such can be easily learned via message boards, guildmates, or having just done the dungeon before. In short, scouting abilities need to be useful while actually in the group. If you lump sap as a scouting ability, that's clearly an example of what I mean by useful while in the group. Another useful ability of this sort, or content changes which make it very valuable to have a stealthed person (i.e., something beyond the simple wandering mob mechanic, such as perhaps pulls that would otherwise have to be done blindly, or doors a rogue could shut to delay mobs from joining a fight for a few seconds, or so on) would be welcome.

Unlocking things: As noted, there aren't enough locked things right now. Also as noted, since it's been posted that others are supposed to be able to unlock things via engineering, the ability to unlock no longer remains a class role. It's tricky though: you don't want important content which *requires* a rogue to do, but if the stuff that's behind locks is purely optional, then that makes this ability a lot less worthwhile. Certainly we need to see more locked chests with decent loot, but we also need to see more pickable locks which allow groups to go places they'd otherwise not be able to go, or at least save the group time. BRD has a good example, in that rogues can pick the jail locks without needing the key, but on the other hand that ability saves only a small amount of time (about 15-20 mobs worth) because the key is so easy to get, and that short amount of time can be justified for many groups by the extra loot you get by killing the key holder.

Anyway, overall I think the rogue "class role" is decent, I just want the last two roles stated to actually be meaningful and significant to a group. If you have a mage who is an engineer, for example, the value of scouting (with current dungeons) is vastly outweighed by the value of polymorph alone, never mind a mage's other advantages over rogues. The same could be said about any other class who can do a lot of damage -- their other utility beats scouting, and for the moment lockpicking (which, as I mentioned, is partially mitigated as long as you have an engineer).
