Kat, make sure to check out a number of the other recent posts on this board, since there have been a lot of great ideas already put out there.

I've broken my post into various sections, because it turned out to be really long.

---------------------------------
Here are my suggestions:

Warlocks need to have a clear vision of what we are about. Of all the classes, Warlocks are the only ones that wil lintentionally damage themselves to hurt someone or something else. We are the most extreme risk-takers, commanding the most fearsome demons as our slaves and servants. We deal with the blackest magic, the forbidden stuff. We tear the fabric of reality without regard for the safety of others or the stability of the universe. Because of the personal risks we take and the need to pay for our magic in blood and the stolen souls of our victims, Warlocks should be capable of the most terrifying magic around.

Mage stuff, while very potent directly, is tame and predictable. They categorize and define all their magic in strict recipes and exact rituals.

Priests operate under moral codes (of either good or evil) and find their magic is bound by religious tenets.

Warlocks need to have risk and power under a very tenuous balance. Our spells should have serious limitations (require soul shards and damage our health) with superior payoff (some of the most awe-inspiring spells this side of Hell). Warlocks should resemble blood mages on some level, always putting their own blood into the magic, and combining this with a victim's soul to appease the demons that fuel their power. Warlocks should have some of the coolest graphics around because their magic is definitely *not* vanilla.

The best suggestions I have seen so far have to do with a complete re-envisioning of the class.

1. *** Soul Shards ***

Get rid of the soul shard component for spells. Get rid of mana points. Instead, replace both of them with a soul energy meter that fills up when you use Drain Soul on a monster. Instead of getting a shard, you now get soul energy. All spells, except a few, will cost soul energy to cast, representing the change to make all spells require soul shards to cast. A few spells need to be "free" to cast in order to get the first level of soul energy.

Exempt spells (costing no soul energy):
- Life Tap (converts Health to a minimal amount of Soul Energy, capped at 25% SE)
- Drain Soul (steals soul from a victim as he dies to fill the soul energy bar)
- Death Coil (converts Health to Direct Damage)
- Summon Imp (spell costs Health, but not Soul Energy)

Soul energy never drains away until it is used.

Alternative: If the switch to soul energy isn't made, then more spells should require soul shards to cast and more spells should have hp costs to them, and Soul Shards will need to stack if they are used for more spells.

Henceforth, I will assume (1) isn't going to happen and describe things as if soul shards are kept in game, but still encouraging use of hp in spellcasting.

2. *** Curses ***

Warlocks need more curses so that multiple warlocks grouped together have various combinations to employ. Currently we have 5 curses:

Curse 1 - Weakness (reduces mele damage from foe, works okay)
Curse 2 - Agony (DoT).

CoA needs to deal more damage. Shadow Word: Pain does faster damage currently. If warlocks are supposed to be masters of DoT, this seriously needs upping. I like the suggestion that damage increases the longer the curse is up. Perhaps at low levels, CoA deals constant damage but at higher ranks, the curse deals accelerating damage. I wouldn't go quite double, but maybe 150% of each previous damage. E.g., 20, 30, 45, 67, 101, 151, 226 etc.

Curse 3 - Recklessness (increases damage taken and damage dealt of a foe. also prevents fleeing.)

Currently the "no fleeing" isn't consistent. It stops running in fear, but it doesn't stop "tactical positioning" that casters use. The spell *should* work to "bind" a foe to its current target (as if /following), sort of like a semi-root spell. The foe can only melee-attack its current target. If not in melee, the foe can only move closer to its target, not farther away. This gives the spells a PvP element as well, to prevent fleeing, unless the target also flees or the curse is Cleansed. While so Cursed, the foe is immune to all Fear effects (not currently how it works). This way a warlock has to be sure he wants to give the foe Recklessness, because it prevents him from using Fear later without first switching the Curse.

Curse 4 - Tongues (doubles casting time, makes victim speak Demon).

This Curse needs to cause the target to lose mana over time as well, and give a 5% chance to cause spell failure while so cursed. I haven't tested this in PvP yet, but it should force the player to speak ONLY demon in all channels while so cursed. Curse of tonuges also needs to double the mana cost for spells.

Curse 5 - Elements (increases elemental damage taken)

This Curse works poorly right now. I Cursed half a crowd with CoE once and then cast Rain of Fire. At rank 4, RoF deals 138 damage at a time. The Cursed mobs took 144 instead of 138. I think that's a 0.5% increase in damage. Curse of elements needs to work on ALL elemental damage, and also add 50-100% duration for effects. Freeze root spell from mages needs to last longer. DoTs need to last longer. I assume CoE affects Fire, Water, Ice but not Shadow or Nature. At higher ranks of this Curse, Shadow and then ultimately Nature need to be included.

New Curse
Curse 6 - Curse of Disfigurement.
The target is made to resemble an ugly, molten version of him or herself. Friends turn on the victim and attack in horror.

For NPCs, this causes the target to generate threat to nearby NPCs.

For PCs, this causes the PC to become unrecognizable by his PC allies and can now be targetted by their own area attacks. (Still cannot be directly targetted to prevent grief).

 90. Re: Warlock Suggestions (Channel spells) | 7/24/2004 11:05:59 PM GMTDT
5. *** Channel Spells ***
Here's where I think warlock magic and mage magic need to differ a lot. Mages spend their time in schools and libraries. They are used to dust and bookworms being the most fearsome things they encounter on a day-to-day basis. Therefore, it makes sense that if they get smacked they would stop casting a channel spell.

Warlocks on the other hand, are routinely dipping into the nether planes, going toe-to-toe with demons (which they enslave and force to do their chores, like the laundry, mowing the lawn, etc.) They are used to seeing horrors all the time and really aren't very jumpy. Furthermore, since all their magic requires a bit of blood, a slice of spleen, a portion of my heart, a shard of someone's soul, they really are used to taking damage. After all, a relaxing nap for a warlock is being suspended on meathooks above a rolling pool of lava. Ever vacationed in Hell? Warlocks go there in 1st grade. Therefore, warlocks should not have to worry about channel interrupts from regular combat. Special attacks designed to interrupt a caster are another matter. You can ignore pain, but a warlock still can't ignore the full-nelson that rogue just put on him, or the kick to his casting hand that stopped the spell. Warlock channel spells should only be interrupted on special attacks. This is a compromise between the previous way and the current way of handling it. Excessive damage (e.g. Crits) should have a chance of interrupting as well.

Life Drain - The ultimate warlock experience is looking into the eyes of his dying enemy and gloating as the enemy desperately tries to fight back. Like tormenting squirrels and spiders when they were young, grown warlocks love tormenting their victims in battle, and nothing torments more than knowing your own life force is keeping your hated enemy alive. When a warlock gets into battle, he loves to take it face-to-face with a tough guy and let the foe begin to disembowel him. Then, while the foe has his sword in the warlock's entrails, the warlock reaches down, and twists the sword a little more, and finishes his incantation. Suddenly, the foe's own life force begins slipping away into the warlock's outstretched hands. The warlock becomes invigorated while his foe withers and dies. Afterwards, the warlock sells the sword to a local merchant. Warlocks need to be able to channel life from the very enemy they are fighting without fear of interrupt.

Mana Drain: Oh, the ultimate. Casters battling it out for mana! Leave as is, but maybe increase the steal rate? It's not much of a mage-killer as it is. (Maybe make it more effective against NPCs?) COmbined with Curse of Tongues, warlocks are beginning to approach a viable animosity between mages (wimps) and warlocks (with the kahunas).

Drain Soul: Leave as is, unless switching to require more soul shards, and then maybe creating a system in which sometimes a soul can be taken from a lower level enemy as well (currently cannot collect a soul shard unless you also get experience from the kill).

Rain of Fire: This needs to be a very potent spell in the game, interruptible only by Kick and related skills, like all other channeling spells.

Hellfire: Hellfire should be the supreme spell of WoW. It should be unparalleled by all other spells, since this is essentially tearing open a rift to Hell itself and allowing the fiery contents gush forth onto you and your foes. In addition to the standard health cost to cast, Hellfire will continue to cause fire damage to the Warlock. Currently there is a countdown on the spell, and then it is a channel spell after that. As such, the hate generated by this spell is so much that it rarely lasts more than 1 round, making it pretty weak. Instead, this spell needs to become a DoT, not a channel spell. It should be the most powerful DoT in the game, and it should be very risky for the warlock to cast. What better way to show your devotion to the dark arts than to embrace the flames of Hell yourself, taking enough damage to kill your mortal frame, secure in the knowledge that you will surely take many enemies with you. Each time a warlock casts this spell, his survival should be in doubt. Make it play out its duration no matter what, dealing damage to warlock and enemies alike until the spell is completed. At the end, few should be standing and the warlock should have about a 50% chance of also being alive. (So be equipped with a soulstone!)

Hellfire also needs a graphics change: Make a rift tear open, no longer under the control of the Warlock. The rift shows scenes of Hell within. Demons and Succubi dance in green flame. Imps gambol and prance. Fire spills forth and surrounds everyone and everything. Flames make combat difficult to see. When the World rights itself and the rift closes, the flames die down and then the carnage is revealed. Is anyone left alive? Surely not.

6. *** Miscellaneous Spells ***

Banish: When I first cast Banish, I was surprised that the monster wasn't actually Banished. It was still there, in fact. Not living up to the name... So Banish should work like this:

On NPCs, Banish needs to banish the monster to another plane of existence. The mosnter is gone, poof. It leaves no corpse. It leaves no loot. You get no experience, and no quest credit. Obviously this can only be cast on monsters that have not been tapped by someone else (they must have a red name bar), and Bosses are immune, as well as certain types of monsters like demons and dragons. Monsters more than 5 levels above the Warlock have very high resistance chance. Monsters that do not give experience to the Warlock (low level monsters) have no chance to resist. Monsters within 5 levels of the warlock have a low chance to resist. This is ulimate CC, but you lose something by using it (exp, credit, loot, etc). It still costs 1 shard to use.

On PCs: All PCs have a chance to resist. If the spell succeeds, the PC is transported to Hell for the duration of banishment. The PC can now see visions of Hell (demons, devils, etc., cavorting about). They cannot see the normal world, as they have been banished. When the banishment ends, they return to their original location. The Banishment causes no damage. The PC can eat and drink while in Hell, or cast spells. Banish cannot be Cleansed or debuffed in any way.

Fear: Fear should not work on anyone Cursed with Recklessness.

Ritual of Summoning: Just fix the bugs in this spell.

Life Tap: Remove the "Health Too Low" warning. If a warlock converts too much life to mana, let him die. Warlocks are about risk.

Enslave Demon: Currently there's no reason to use this spell. My demon pet has more hp, has spells and costs the same to summon (1 soul shard) as stealing another demon. Except for the CC aspect of it, there's no gain, and even then, you have to get rid of your own demon pet first. I suggest Removing the Soul Shard cost of Enslaving demon, and replacing it with a significant health cost to enslave (instead of tempting the demon with a soul shard, you offer it your own blood. Fresh and heated to a tasty 98.6 degrees Fahrenheit, the demon cannot resist and comes to serve you for a while.) This way, one advantage is that you can enslave even if you have no soul shards at the moment.

Also, I would like to see the enslaved demon be a little more powerful. Perhaps it should have some spells. When I enslaved my first VW, the first thing I did was look for its spells to sacrifice it for invulnerability :) of course, that would be too powerful, but maybe you could give it a 25% chance of working, and if it fails, the enslavement is broken immediately and the demon turns on you (so not a good thing to do when you really need it. Like the theme of Warlocks should be: High risk for High reward). Succubus Seduce could work like that, too. Attempt to use ahs 25% chance to work, 75% chance to backfire.

More demons need to exist for enslaving. More than Warlocks ever get access to on their own. Maybe an area with all sorts of devils and demonic monsters, that warlocks can enslave for a while. Also with cool powers. Also with a chance to backfire.

New Spell: Death Ward
Warlocks know that death is the risk for any endeavor they take. Therefore, they have devised a risky spell to protect them against the inevitable.

Death Ward lasts for 1 minute when cast. It takes health and mana to cast. When Death Warded, the warlock has a 5% chance to escape an otherwise crippling blow. IF the warlock dies while Death Warded, there is a 5% chance that the warlock, instead of dying, will be returned to full health. Obviously a warlock cannot count on this occurance, but it could be a real boon if it does occur.

The penalities and cost would still remain the same. 30% Movement/Attack speed debuff, soulshard cost, chance to break every 5 seconds, etc.

9. *** Overall vision ***
The way I see warlocks, as demonstrated above, they should be the ultimate risk takers in exchange for the ultimate powers. A warlock should not be one to fear death. In fact, Warlocks should welcome death as a necessary part of their existence. Warlocks need to risk death and injury with just about every thing they do. There should be no failsafes (Life Tap, for example, should be allowed to kill a foolish or inattentive warlock.)
In exchange for such high risks, there has to be a large payoff. The rewards should be apparent in the wow factor of what warlocks can do. If you're willing to take those risks, and if you succeed, you should be able to Banish things to hell, Call forth the molten flames of the underworld, Summon demons and enslave them to your will. Tear apart the fabric of reality to pull things through it.
