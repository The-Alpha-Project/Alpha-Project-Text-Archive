well, I'm pretty much the same level as you (32), so I'll tell you how I do things.
This is my advice, after doing WC, shadowfang, and gnomeregan with my 'regulars', without any real problems.
Stop using pw:s on the tanks so much. It's good to start off before a pull, as it doesn't count towards your own aggro. Otherwise, avoid using it except on casters and yourself. The only time I use it on a tank, is if he's suddenly dropping quickly or he is in red health and will die before the cast time of a heal. It's less mana efficient then an actual heal.

That said, I'm still not sure if multiple Flash Heals or one Heal has more aggro. If they heal roughly the same amount, I figure they should have the same aggro, but I haven't quite figured out if there is a difference. I've alternated between spam healing flash heals, hoping for a crit and inspiration to trigger, and just using the bigger heals on the tanks.

So, for aggro management, I can't give you too many tips. I've just concentrated on mana efficiency. I have the pw:s talent, so I can cast it on myself very often if I have mana. With fade to cover the holes in my pw:s strategy, I've rarely had too much aggro on me with the warriors I group with.

However, one suggestion, if your not already doing it, is to 'alpha strike' all your spells. What I mean by this, is that I try to cast a bunch of spells at once, and then wait as long as possible before casting again. That way, your spirit regen kicks in more often. If the tank is taking damage, and no one else. I just wait till he's 40% or lower, then heal him up to full. It'll normally be 10 seconds or longer until he's back that low, so I regen a bit of the mana back.

Now, it's not always that easy. Your asking about the situations with 2+ mobs on a pull. In those, people other then your tank end up getting aggro. I still try to alpha strike my spells. If the mage is getting hit, I shield them, then renew. If the tank has damage too, I cast whatever heal will heal him to 99% or so. If others have damage, I just heal the $!@% out of everyone, mana permitting. I do gain aggro alot, which is when I throw shield up and/or fade. My tanks can normally get those off me before I take much damage. Since everyone just got back up to full health, I've got a buffer until they are in need of a heal again, and that lets my regen start kicking in (I've got pretty high spirit for my level too).

Other then group wipes, I don't even remember dying in an instance. I'm sure it's happened at least once, but it's lost in a haze of dead mobs and successful runs :)

The PW:S technique described in the first post is bad because of agro transfer. I think the poster doesn't understand agro transfer, so I'm going to explain it in the hope that it helps.

At the risk of being pedantic, let's do a little thought experiment. Assume you have two players, no mobs, and one is almost dead, the other a priest of course. The priest heals up the almost dead guy, no problem.

Now, rewind and add a mob to that picture. If the guy you're healing has a mob agro on him, that heal ends up having the side effect of producing agro on you. It's referred to as an agro transfer because you didn't do anything to the mob, but it hates you anyway. If they have had time to generate as much agro as you just transferred onto yourself, you're gonna be self-healing.

It's better to heal, shield, or buff (yes, fortitude is a fair amount of agro) when there's no mobs agro on anyone than when there are. That probably sounds mind-numbingly obvious, but by doing a PW:S after the pull vs before, you're demonstrating that it's not.


Now, your post also demonstrated a second problem, which was your tank's ability to keep multiple mobs agro'd on him. I'm assuming normal CC techniques were not an option, but that doesn't excuse the tank from doing their job.

Unfortunately, I frequently group with warriors who don't see it as their job to hold agro on every awake mob in camp. Heck, sometimes they don't even hold agro on the mob they're fighting. You need to find some way to explain that they aren't doing their job as well as they can.

Caveat: I do not play a warrior in WoW. I have played the tank (warrior) role in other games, so I have a strategic understanding of how it works, but the actual game mechanics in WoW are something I only know from talking to warriors who do their job really really well.

That said, there are several ways for warriors to keep agro on multiple mobs. They don't just have to be able to hit a mob to generate agro, they have debuffs, and they have taunt. It's fairly straightforward for a warrior to debuff/taunt all of the mobs, and he (or she) should be doing that on incoming with their battle shouts, as well. As long as everyone assists, the only person the warrior has to outagro is your heals. Fade will help there, too, and our heal agro reduction talent is nice if you have it.

If people aren't assisting, the warrior has to split their attention more, and that's usually a recipe for mobs everywhere. It's easy enough to know when that's happening, because you're having to heal the whole group instead of just the tank.

Oh, and Renew rocks if you have the talents that improve it. It's a perfect heal when the damage someone is taking isn't enough to justify a 'real' heal. I tend to break up my hotkey bar like so:

Renew gets used in 3 spots:

1) I don't have time to cast a real heal on you, but you need a little help anyway and I have a decent amount of mana. You get a Renew and then I go and heal the person that really needed the love. Hopefully I have time to get back to you before you die. If I'm very low on mana, I might just have to let you die in this situation.

2) You're getting nibbled at, and I can't justify the poor efficiency of Flash or the time for Heal/GHeal. If I end up being wrong, I have the option to give you a bigger heal later.

3) I want to even out damage spikes so that my big heal lands with safe margin. Any time a high HP tank is taking damage so fast that it's worrying me I can't heal it in time, assuming they're not already close to dead, I slap a Renew on them to give Greater Heal a bit more time to land. If they're already low then it's HW:Shield and Flash Heal time.
