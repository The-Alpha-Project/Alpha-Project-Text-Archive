Caeles, you are absolutely incorrect in this statement. Perhaps the way you play a warlock leads you to believe this, but there are many things a warlock can do that a mage cannot.


  • Pets - Imp for additional stamina, damage shield; VW for an offtank that can AoE taunt if needed; succubus for good damage, CC abilities; plus the possible additions of Felhound, Infernal, and Doomguard (speculated)

  • Life Tap - I think we all know how useful this spell is when you get into a tight spot, where a mage has to either drink or hit a mana potion when s/he runs out of mana, both of which a warlock can do too
  • Banish - like sheep, but for demons/elementals
  • Fear, Howl of Terror - very situational, but can be used in a pinch when you NEED crowd control NOW
  • Ritual of Summoning - handy for when you need to switch a group member out
  • Drain Mana - great when that NPC caster is close to an empty mana bar and you want to guarantee that they won't get another spell off
  • Death Coil - though I don't have it yet, this looks like a great panic button

Did I miss anything?

Q u o t e:
Priests aren't concerned with warlock's mana being full, they are concerned with life bars dropping, and when they see a life bar dropping, they don't care what's going on, they'll heal you (natural priest instinct).

Priests, or all healers for that matter, need to be in tune with the abilities of their group members (this is actually true for every player of the game, but moreso for healers). One of the most useful abilites of a warlock is the ability to convert HP to mana. As the healer, it is your job to know this and not to be triggerhappy with the heals when your warlock hits the Life Tap button. A good healer will know that a warlock is capable of healing himself if he wants to, and should just let him do his thing. I'm not saying that a warlock should never be healed, but I'm saying that the healer should be able to recognize the difference between mob-inflicted damage and self-inflicted damage. I realize this takes more thought than "heal the player who's life bar just dropped", but this thinking will make you a better player and will change your natural priest instinct for the better.

I hate to compare this game to EQ, but the Life Tap -> Drain Life is very similar in utility to how necromancers worked in that game. They would have a spell active that converted HP to mana every tick, and would rely on draining life from mobs to heal it back. Healers in EQ became accustomed to this, once they understood the incredible mana regen that could be obtained using this tactic. Something similar should happen for warlocks in WoW.

Like Kelena said, she's not counting on heals to keep her mana coming in. In most cases, it would be foolish of a warlock to do so, since the heals should be going to the tank during combat. If the healer has mana to spare, he might as well heal the warlock, but the warlock shouldn't be counting on heals to fuel his casting habits. If your warlock overuses Life Tap and dies because of it, that's his fault, not yours. Warlocks can do just fine healing themselves after a Life Tap, and it makes the group more efficient and speeds up kills over time. That's what we're all trying to do, right?

PS - I'm totally undecided if I will go the Affliction or Destruction routes when the game hits retail. I didn't come here to defend Destruction talents because it's something I personally do.
