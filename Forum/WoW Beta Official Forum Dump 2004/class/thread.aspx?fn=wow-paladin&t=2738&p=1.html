 1. The type of Paladin I'ld like to see | 7/28/2004 10:33:21 PM GMTDT
Now this is not meant as anything more then my thoughts on paladins. I don't even remotely expect any ideas here to be used, especially since they would require a complete rediesgn of the class.

When most people think of paladins in an RPG, they think fighter that can heal. I am not saying everyone, but most do. They think it's a cross between a fighter and a cleric. I feel these 2 classes (paladin and cleric/priest) have very little in common, other then serving the same diety if the case may be.

Where I feel cleric/priests are the servants of a diety, I see the paladin as the diety's "arm in the world". The fist of justice if you will. The paladin does not seek to convert non-believers, but rather detroy any blaspheme and abominations to his diety. To bring down those who openly oppose his diety and to justice according to his diety.

A paladin should never run from battle nor should he ever look the other way when duty calls. He needs to be a zealot for his god. He must dispise all that is deemed evil by his diety and must always heed the call of his god.

Now this would be extremely hard to implement into gameplay and would definately take a good role-player to do it properly. However, there are some ways to achieve a good deal of the above in gameplay.

First off, the paladin should not be geared towards being devensive nor a healer. He is the holy equivalent of a barbarian. He rages with no regard for his own well being. He does not "hold out as long as he can", but rather takes as many of them out with him as he can.

To do so, get rid of mana IMO. Repalce it with "holy favor" or whatever you want to call it. As you fight red creatures, you gain favor to use special abilities. Yes I know this sounds a lot like the warrior rage. However, when you fight yellow creatures, you would lose it. You would not lose the favor by being Idle. Running from battle would make you lose all if not a substantial amount of favor, perhaps even having the ability to go negative with favor. Dying heroically (sacrificing yourself for the party) would increase your favor upon being ressurected.

The abilities, as stated further above, should be more offensive in nature, but only work on red targets. An ability like holy strike is good, but I would like to see things that are debuffs as well and an ability that takes all your favor and focus it on one enemy in a very devastating attack that leaves you fairly vulnerable.

Auras fit in well as I see paladins as leaders and ones who inspire those around them. I am undecided on seals.

I would personally get rid of resurrect, although I can see keeping it for balancing reasons. However, I can see the paladin becoming a "ok hide around the corner and res us" character in PvP.

I would reduce the healing capabilities. The paladin should be focused on clensing the world of evil, not tending to peoples wounds. He should not heal himself in battle. His focus should never be on himself. Heck I would love to see paladins and even warriors in some cases become enraged and when they do, have the health bar completely disappear.

In the end, I feel paladins have become mini clerics that can wield a sword. They have to long become about staying alive in RPG's rather then being the selfless individuals on a mission to erradicate all evil they encounter.

 3. Re: The type of Paladin I'ld like to see | 7/29/2004 12:37:51 AM GMTDT
My first paladin I ever played was pen and papper 1st edition D&D .. so every paladin I see after that I tend to feel that is what they should mostly represent.. Stildar's idea of a paladin resembles it to some degree.. Paladins can heal but it is very weak.. paladins were blessed by their dieties to give them added protection (bonus to saving throws, protection from evil, lay hands which was actually a weak heal in its own regard, just instant and consistent), minor clerical spells, weakened turn undead, immunity to fear (because paladins did NOT run from battle), cure disease once per day..

These were some of the things paladins had in addition to wearing the armor and using the weapons of fighters (what warriors were called in D&D).. paladins could not train in the weapons to the same degree as warriors (they could not specialize) Real strength of the paladin came with their use of powerful holy swords that were only usable by the paladin.. such as holy avenger and the paladin's mount was special as well.

Paladins were fighters with less training in martial combat but had added protection from their diety.. (alot of added protection)

In a game where everything has to be balanced they would be hard to implement in the same way because a paladin was a much tougher individual to kill than the fighter.. and if they had a holy sword were feared in battle as well.. the reason for this was due to how one had to play the paladin.. you HAD to be lawful good and you HAD to stay that way through your actions or else you would lose your blessings (all spells skills and weapons usable only by paladin) and be a watered down fighter with no weapon specialization for the rest of your career or until you were resolved by a cleric of equal or greater level (no easy task).

In this regard I have always loved the paladin because it presented a new challenge other than kill the beast. You had to play him right even when there was no beast to fight but how you handled yourself in town with the citizens as well. Oh.. and you had to tithe a percentage of all your coin earned to your church. Although this paladin would not be balanced... a paladin wasnt the equal of his peers he was the leader due to the fact that he had great power.. like I said this power came from the cost of being a certain type of individual who practiced his faith 24-7.

Before anyone flames me with how this would be unfair, I just want to say that I am just pondering the good old days.. not asking for this type of character in a mmorpg because you do need balance in such games.. it would be fair imo if one could actually uphold the high standards and the population of such a class was fairly rare (which paladins were in D&D, hardly anyone I knew tried to do the paladin.. I loved that part also.. being one of few) I guess that is what I miss most about paladin.. anyone and everyone can play one and they are no longer the virtuous warriors they are supposed to be.. gets under my skin seeing someone who is supposed to be a "paladin" acting like a jerk to others.. The paladin doesnt represent what it is supposed to represent anymore..

/salute fellow paladins

Well have a nice day folks.. I'm finished rambling =)
