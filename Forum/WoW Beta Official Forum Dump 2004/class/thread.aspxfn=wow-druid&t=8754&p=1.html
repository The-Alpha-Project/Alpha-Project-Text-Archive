 6. Re: How would you balance Druids? | 10/14/2004 11:54:13 PM GMTDT
To balance druid, I would first kick the original poster out of the club, because any minor reference to nerfing forms is about the dumbest idea ever. Druids are about forms, if you don't like shapeshifting, don't be a druid :-P We're the only class that can really do the shapeshifting kinda thing, and without it, we're just mimicing a mage or a priest, and poorly.

What I would do:

1. Direct damage move for cat form.

2. Agility effects cat form damage.

3. Int and spi will be converted, in a portion, to str, agi, and stam when shapeshifted. Not fully, so getting agility only gear will still be better than getting all spirit and int gear if you spend an aweful lot of time in cat form, but a 2/3 conversion or something.

4. Give the ability to shift between cat and bear.

5. Add a stun somewhere else in our forms. Starfire and the stun talent are kinda a joke, and bear form having the only 2 stuns aside from that isn't good. Plus, they're on decently long timers. Perhaps put it in cat form, since cat form is kind of the "anti-mage/priest," or perhaps put it in caster form, to avoid the forms bias (don't want to make caster form completely useless...).

6. Do some talent adjustments. Combine a few talents (like cat and bear faerie fire, the chances to get additional combo points/rage talents) so that we have a few more points to spend, OR (and this one is a bigger one) remove the restoration tree almost entirely. A few of the things can stay, but replace most of it with a fluidity tree. Put all the stuff like swiftshifting and reduced shapeshift cost, and things like that in there. Maybe make the cat>bear transition be a talent in there. Add more talents to the feral tree to compensate that are bear or cat specific, but make this line concentrate on making shapeshifting completely fluid.

7. Make nature's swiftness not generate that client-side 1-sec delay (does it? I can't remember), cost a few hundred more mana, but be on a 10-15 second timer. Makes emergency healing expensive, but honestly, you need that in later instances, and priests have HW:S which works a lot faster.

8. Give bear form something to compensate for not having a shield. Either a shield-like effect, a talent for it, or simply add a lot more hp/ac to bear form.

9. There is no 9, I just like even numbers.

10. Make tranquility an ultimate spell. Uninterruptable by regular attacks (possibly by virtue of a talent), 1 hour cooldown, mana is used while channeling. Restores 20-30% of each person in your group's hp and mana PER TICK. So, it is usable in an emergency to heal your whole group, as it's fast enough. However, it leaves you with no mana at the end. Thus, having 2 healers, a druid is a phenomenal choice, as he can regenerate the other healer when needed, as well as heal the group. Could make it remove poisons, diseases, and curses too.

-FunHooves

Edit: I forgot 1 other thing.

Unless we do the drastic redesign of the restoration talents, incorporate predatory strikes and thick hide into the forms without needing talents. It's ridiculous that we should have to spend points on that, since they're almost required to make cat form worthwhile, or tank decently with bear form.

 18. Re: How would you balance Druids? | 10/15/2004 9:22:29 AM GMTDT
I still like my suggestion that got ignored from a while ago, to make druid forms a lot more like warrior stances than "Other Class Lite".

Basically how it would work is:

Give the class a base set of abilities and spells that work throughout all the forms. (can leave travel and underwater like they are, since they're specific-purpose). Edit mana cost and possibly casting time depending on which form you're in. Make 3 sets of spells/ability, 1 for each form. Cat/Bear form abilities would cost mana, and we woudln't have to worry about rage/energy.

Cat form would not be rogue-lite, but would increase attack speed, up melee DPS, but would lower your armor. Would still have some heals, but no healing touch. Basically would be limited to the fast-casting type spells. Should do at least as much melee damage as a warrior, but nowhere near a rogue, and be fragile as hell.

Bear form would slow your attack rate but up the per-hit, putting this form in 2nd place for melee damage. This form would get stuns and extra armor, maybe to hunter levels. This form would get the slower casting spells, but would get an innate resistance to interruption, so you could cast in melee. Should be easily beaten at both melee DPS and tanking ability by warriors, but should be good enough to tank-mez (offtank) some stuff briefly, and to keep you from going splat too rapidly. There should be some form of taunt, but make it a) long recycle, and b) mana intensive. Should make it good enough taunt to get 1 thing on you reliably, but only 1 mob, once during the average encounter. That way we definitely don't step on a warrior's toes.

Caster form would have the cheapest mana costs for all spells, and would have the broadest spell lines. Nukes/DOTs would do more in this form than in any of the other forms. Lowest melee DPS of the 3 forms. Middle-pack armor, about like it is now. This'd be the jack of all trades form, and be the best for doing any damage at range.

Shifting would be instant, and allow you to change from any one form to any other form, but would cost at least some mana. With a forms setup like this, there'd actually be reasons to swap back and forth depending on what you need to be doing, especially since you wouldn't lose all casting capability by being in the alternate forms. By scaling back the rogue/warrior class-like abilities, we avoid being a "lite" version of one of them, and balance out the power increase of getting spells in alternate forms.

*shrug* Almost certainly too late for them to do that now, but wish they'd have listened to me months ago. heh.
