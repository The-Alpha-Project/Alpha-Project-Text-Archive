 1. Spells, Abilities, Talents, Undead and more | 7/29/2004 8:34:08 AM GMTDT
Something really dawned on me today and it really came into flution in the middle of a battle later on.

As I have stated before, a bulk of the Paladins problem comes from being a specialized class in a specific 'field' that has little demand.

The bulk of the Paladins skills cater towards killing undead, while other abilities are greatly enchanced when facing undead.

Some specific examples of spells geared situationally to Undead Killing are:
  • Exorcrism
  • Turn Undead
  • Judgement
  • Aura of Retribution
  • Seal of Righteousness
  • Sense Undead

    This means almost 1/4 of all the spells/abilities the Paladin has cater towards killing undead.

    Further Holy Strike and Crusader Strike both are most effective against Undead.

    When I was toiling this around in my head I realized something that I feel is very insightful. Catering towards killing one type of creep does not sound like something that should be a class ability, it sounds like something that should be a talent.

    That said, I challenge anyone to show me another class that has this many spells dedicated to killing one specific type of creep that is as uncommon as the undead.

    Something that rang true to me about this statement comes right out of a Warrior talent discussion thread. The concensus on the thread seemed to be that no warrior should ever be forced to persue any specific talent or caption developement path; that all talents should equally have the ability to improve your character. This same thing was reitterated recently by Kat in the main forums.

    With this in mind, we have a class that has 25% of its abilities steadfast locked into killing one specific type of creep. We have this talent for killing undead and because of this, we have 25% of our skills and our only 2 major attacks rendered ineffective against the vast majority of what World of Warcraft has to offer. Yes, I know Holy Strike is a powerful attack, but the mere fact is it excels at facing Undead.

    Playing the numbers game above, its no reason so many people complain about the Paladins design. It's a fundamnetal flaw.

    What I am going to suggest may sound very harsh but I think it may be a very good solution to this problem. I am going to suggest all Undead Skills be disolved as class trained skills and be converted into Talents.

    Systematically speaking, the skills should be converted as follows:

    1.) Exocrism: Changed into a passive enchancement for Holy Light that does a percentage of damage to undead based on the rank of Holy Light and level of Exocrism.

    2.) Turn Undead: Added as a either a passive attack skill or added as a activated skill in Teir 2 or 3.

    3.) Judgement: Added as a 7th tier ability

    4.) Aura of Retribution: Added as a passive enchancement to Devotion Aura

    5.) Seal of Righteousness: Added as a passive enchament for Domiance Aura

    6.) Sense Undead: Totally scrapped. You can learn it at the tracking trainer. Maybe Paladins could learn Sense Undead and Sense Demons for half the Skill Point cost.

    This, in essence creates 4 passive abilities and 1 activated ability and still maintains all the undead features. In comparison to what kind of things the warrior picked up, none of what I have suggested here is really unbalanced.

    By flushing these abilites out of the class trainer list, we can now add things that will be more useful to Paladins throughout the vast majority of the game. We can add Zeal, we can add Smite, we can add Scarfical Strike, we can add Holy Bolt or whatever else would flesh out the gap created.

    I believe this a good suggestion for allowing Paladins to have their cake and eat it too =).
