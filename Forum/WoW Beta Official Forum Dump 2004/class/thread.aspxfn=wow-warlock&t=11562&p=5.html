What he's talking about is this:

Aggro range and run-distance have been tightened, so creatures chase you for a shorter distance and they won't aggro *again* once they begin their run back.

Here is what you do.

Lets say you want to reach Point X. Point X can be reached only be a long and tedious fight through many mobs.

However, inevitably in dungeons and instances there are spots where monsters do not travel or travel only rarely. We'll call these places 'rest points.'

Instead of grinding this out the "normal" way, you grab soulstones and charge straight in, making no real attempt to avoid aggro. You don't go looking for it, but you don't really dodge it either.

Obviously you typically send the tanks first, as they'll draw aggro first and take the beating. A warlock could (prior to this patch) try this trick by nuking the VW first--but you'd better only be about 20-25 seconds from your destination (assuming invulnerability, healthstone, and high-end healing potion.)

So you (or you and your group) charge towards Point X. Inevitably you aggro too many mobs and die.

The goal is to die at a pre-defined location (a rest spot) where you know monsters do not typically congregate.

In the old system, you waited for monsters to leave, then used your soulstone. In the new situation this is more dangerous, but can still be easily tactic'd around. Most of the time a mob will kill you and immediately leave the area; the only exception to this might be tightly-confined underground areas where mobs can't easily run back.

The current "5 second system" might make this a headache, but it isn't going to make it at all impossible.
