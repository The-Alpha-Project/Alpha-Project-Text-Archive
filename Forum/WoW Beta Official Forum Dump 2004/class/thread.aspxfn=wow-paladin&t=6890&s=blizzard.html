Q u o t e:
I would like to request some Druid-specific questions from you folks.


Busted. :D

Thanks Cay for opening this discussion. Here are my questions, some are more open ended than others, so please forgive if they're a bit too broad.

I guess the first question that's really on everyone's minds lately is What roles are the developers aiming for the Paladin to fill? Currently it seems as if Paladins are being pulled between support and primary tanking, with neither role being *quite* fufilled. Case in point are the Seals of Sacrifice and Reckoning, which allows the Paladin to "tank by proxy" as I've taken to thinking about it, albeit with consequences of spreading out healing - which might not be beneficial to a group make up.

Since that's a pretty broad question, I'm going to assume that our role will shift towards being a primary tank (given that we can wear the best armor in the game.) That being the case, then;

1. Will Paladins be naturally suited to holding aggression for their teammates as Warriors can currently? Or will we be required to spend Talent points to gain the ability to reliably hold agression?

The key word there is reliably, right now a Paladin CAN hold good agro if they get a good Holy Strike off with Seal of Fury - however, since Holy Strike can be negated (missed/dodged/parried/blocked), it's not quite as reliable as groups might like. Obviously not even Taunt is 100%, which I think is a good thing.

2. Will Paladins see an increase in utility/abilities?

Right now, our Horde counterpart is shaping up to be one of the most complete and interesting classes in MMO history. I'm really amazed at how the Shaman class turned out, and in part, I feel that's due to their unique abilities that break the "Hybrid" stigma of being inferior to multiple classes. I definately am not asking for a nerf to Shamans, but I'd like to see that same model applied (with an obvious tilt towards team protection and mastery of evil to fit lore) to our precious Paladin. :D

3. Are the Developers aware of the 'boring soloist' issues?

This is a loaded question, and I'm sorry, it's the only way I can find to phrase this concern. Our abilities give us a lot of power in certain situations, not least of which is PvP. However, this same combination of defensive abilities makes for uninspired solo game play - despite the fact that we solo very well due to being able to win nearly any war of attrition. I think that many of us might be willing to trade a bit of that efficientcy for a bit more engaging solo play, ie., "More buttons!"

4. Fun question - Are we going to see Tomes for our ranged slot?

I am so incredibly excited by this concept, even just another class of items with passive benefits would be awesome - it's just one of those fun things that's so ingrained into Warcraft lore, the Holy Knight with a Good Word strapped to his armor and a bigol' hammer, ready to proselytize to the masses the Glory of the Light... or the hazards of walking in the Shadow.

*glee* I'm all giddy just talking about it. :D

I'm sure other posters are going to have more specific questions, but I feel like those are our major areas of need.

Thanks again!

Edit: Extra question here, this came up previously in a post about making INT affect our DPS;

Right now, Paladins are more or less forced into specific stat builds, namely, Intellect is of high importance to Paladins due to the high mana use to get the most bang out of our abilities and to be able to attack a few times with Holy Strike and have enough mana to maintain seals and be able to heal in a pinch. Are there any plans to help allievate this? I'd like to actually see some low mana, low damage abilities that will favor STR/STA type builds for those Paladins that want to have a bit more damage and still be able to tank (but obvioulsy not be able to churn out the Holy Lights and Exorcisms.) Since Strength is one of our primary auto-rising stats, are the developers aware that INT is given a bit more priority?

Thanks!
