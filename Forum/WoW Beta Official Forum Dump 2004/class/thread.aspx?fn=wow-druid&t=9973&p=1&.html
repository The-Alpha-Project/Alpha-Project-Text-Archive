**quote**

Personally, I'm tired of all the misconceptions with both people that have played druids and people that have not. The majority of people see druids as gimped, but no one knows how. Devs are some of the very few population that does not think druids are gimped. I am hoping this post will answer any questions as well as clear up any misconceptions. If you want to reply, I ask that you READ THE ENTIRE POST BEFORE DOING SO.

The first thing that must be stated is WHY there is a problem because many people think otherwise. The reason is simple: Druids make up a very small part of the population. How small? This is very hard to calculate, given that you have to throw out people that have just started with a class and people that no longer play the class. You can no longer check in game because the search button is broken to only list 49 people (Unless this was fixed). So what I did was check Thottbot. Thottbot is definitely not the most accurate of devices, but it does provide a good estimate. On the PvP server, druids rank second to last for those level 50+. The only class they beat out is hunters, who do not have talents and are still a relatively new class. Priests are three times as numerous. On the beta server, they are third to last (Beating warlocks and hunters). What does this say? Most people would rather not play a druid into the high levels, less so than even priests or warlocks.

Now that that is established, we must find out WHAT the problem is. Why do so many people not want to play the class? The answer is simple, and it has been stated many times: Druids are lacking in groups. There is more than one reason for this, however. Actually, there are quite a lot. Let me explain.

Now, what are druids? They are the jack-of-all-trades. They can do everything, but not anything as well as the class they are supposed to mimic. First of all, that is a false statement and anyone that says it should be shot. Druids cannot do anything at any given time and thus, are more aptly named as the jack-of-one-trade-at-a-time (As another board member put it). We are a lesser class by even shapeshifting, which is supposedly our class defining ability. This is acceptable, because that is what our class is supposed to be. However, hybrids, or jack-of-all-trades, are usually less desirable in groups because they cannot do anything as well as other classes. This fundamental problem with hybrids is easily fixable. Point being Shamans and Paladins, who often do get into groups. Why is this the case? Let us look at point number two.

Druids have no original abilities. Bear form is directly copied from a warrior’s list of abilities. Cat form is directly copied from a rogue’s list of abilities. Even worse, our so-called “class defining ability” that is shapeshifting is in fact, not very class defining given that they are merely a direct copy of other, better classes. So why would someone want to choose a druid for a group? They won’t. Because they don’t add anything. That is the difference between druids and shamans/paladins. Why would a druid choose to go into a bear form when a warrior can do it better? There is no reason. Therefore, druids are saved for the emergency situations when things go awry. That is NOT a viable role of any class, nor should it be. And people have been asking from day one: Give forms original abilities. Otherwise, there would be very little reason to use them in groups.

Going back to the first point, I will like to add that I do not think druids are vastly underpowered or even close to it. At level 40, I could take down a level 40 named elite. Same level, non-named elites are easy. That’s without ever using forms and/or entangling roots. If a druid chooses to actively use forms, he or she could start in cat form, sneak up, and dish out damage. Once his or her hp is low, they can switch to caster form and heal up, while still bashing away. Once the druid’s mana is low, they can switch to bear form to continue. By the time the druid’s hp gets low again, the druid’s mana should have recharged a bit and can switch back to caster form. In other words, druids are impossibly hard to kill or win a fight against. This does not work in groups, however. This method of killing is incredibly slow as it relies on a battle of attrition. In groups, you are given a more defined role. Thus, this would only work if half your party is dead and you’re facing a single mob.

Next, there is the most obvious reason druids are lacking in groups: a lack of group abilities. It started with just entangling roots, but more problems occurred with the addition of talents. Now, we were given a whole set of useless group talents, with eight entangling roots talents. Not to mention the abysmal self res talent, which basically plastered a big sign on druids stating “SOLO CLASS HERE”. Thankfully, this abomination was taken out. But the damage already had been done. And still, the self res talent was replaced by an equally useless talent, which is so bad that people [b]preferred[/I] self res. Imagine that. To make matters worse, both travel form and the cat speed increase were labeled “outdoor use only”, which eliminates yet another talent. Some may argue the same about mounts and ghost wolf, which is true, but the point remains: Druid abilities are being taken away indoors. It’s also worse for druids because of the fact that we have nine useless indoor talents.

Only three unusable indoor abilities, along with a few talents, right? Wrong. It still fails to address that our so-called group abilities are lacking to the point where they are unusable, even though they were meant to augment our abilities in groups. Point one – Tranquility. The HoT is much worse than the instant PoH that priests have, along with the ten second channeling. Even with that though, it still has some uses – except that it has a ridiculous ten minute cooldown, which just makes it unusable. Same problems occur with Hurricane.

Now, the last two paragraphs can be taken as a rant, because they probably are. The point I am trying to get across however, is that druids were given solo abilities and really not enough for groups.

I will concede one thing: we are decent healers in groups. This doesn’t solve a thing, however. We are made to be able to do everything decently, yet all we are good for is healing. Some attribute this to a lack of healers, which is partially true. However, given that a party can sufficiently heal, druids are still mainly only good for healing. That, in a sense, has become a druid’s role. Otherwise, we’d see more warriors complaining about a druid’s tanking ability, in which that complaining is solely reserved for priests, whether they are valid or not. In addition, bumping up an ability’s effectiveness is a very easy fix. However, it still doesn’t fix any problems. Let us use paladins as an example. They are said to be lacking in the DPS department. Blizzard, seeing this, decides to bump up all of the paladin’s damage dealing abilities and then labels paladins as a “finished” class. Does this fix anything? No. At least, I doubt any paladin would be happy with the change. They will still be clamoring for a new offensive spell so they wouldn’t have to spam the same one over and over again, as well as having no ranged attack. Let’s look at another possible fix. Instead of the current line of paladin offensive spells, they are removed in favor of a warrior’s offensive spells that have been given “paladin” names. DPS is now bumped up, but the class is still not fixed. No, it has simply been turned into a warrior. What if, instead, no fixes were made at all, and the class DPS was only fixed through talents? Nope, still doesn’t solve anything. A class shouldn’t be fixed through talents.

Yet sadly, druids were attempted to be solved using all three methods. And none of them worked or will fix the class.

**end quote**
