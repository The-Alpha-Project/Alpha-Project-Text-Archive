 42. Re: Granularity of energy regen is too low. | 10/12/2004 10:18:12 AM GMTDT
I'm going to bring up some of the few negative points to higher granularity, if only because I don't think that anyone is going to realize what some of the side effects of increasing granularity are going to be until it is too late. Now I'm not sure these negative points are enough to outweigh the benefits of increased granularity, but they definetly detract from it.

Information
Energy Regen = +20 every 3 sec
Energy Cap = 100
Sinister Strike (SS) = 40 energy (w/ talent), no cooldown
Gouge = 45 energy, 5.5 second stun until hit (w/ talent), 10 sec dooldown
BackStab (BS) = 60 energy, no cooldown
Ambush = 60 energy

Apparent Energy Cap
Consider for a second the time upon which you engage an enemy, for example:
Case 1:
You stealth up to an enemy and use Ambush at the beginning of a 3 second interval that is -60 energy putting you at 40 energy until the end of the 3 second interval.
Case 2:
Now consider if you had done the same thing, but you had done it at 2.9 seconds into the interval, meaning you have 40 energy for the next .1 seconds rather than the next 3 seconds meaning you will almost immediately regen 20 energy, putting you at 60, which will mean your effective Energy Cap is 120.

With increased granularity, the effect of an apparent energy cap will be decreased.

Timing Required
Increasing granularity will reduce the amount of monitoring, calculating, and the timing required when using abilities, for instance the choice between using a lower energy ability like SS when at 55 energy or waiting 3 seconds to gain 5 more energy to use a BS.

Exaggerated Example
Now if anyone failed to understand my first example, here is an exaggerated one to make it even more apparent, now first lets say that the energy regeneration rate was changed to 100 energy every 15 seconds, same rate, lower granularity.
Case 1:
You stealth up to an enemy and use Ambush and immediately SS at the beginning of a 15 second interval that is -100 energy putting you at 0 energy until the end of the 15 second interval.
Case 2:
Now consider if you had done the same thing, but you had done it at 14.9 seconds into the interval, meaning you have 0 energy for the next .1 seconds rather than the next 15 seconds meaning you will almost immediately regen 100 energy, putting you back at full, which will make your effective Energy Cap 200.

Apparent energy cap will only really make a difference in relatively short fights and will be undeniably negligible if the fight is longer than 30 seconds.

I believe the spacing of energy regen was put there on purpose, for the exact effect each of us realize.

Energy rate is fine the way it is. Certain combos work better than others, and if you use it all up, thats 2 seconds you cant do anything.
