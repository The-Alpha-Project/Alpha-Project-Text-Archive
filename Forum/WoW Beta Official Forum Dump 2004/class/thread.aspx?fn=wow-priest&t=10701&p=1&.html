 1. Should Healing talents be replaced? | 10/23/2004 7:53:05 AM GMTDT
I think one of the issues that face both priests and druids is that they often feel pigeonholed into spending their talent points on healing abilities.

Healing is basically the core ability of both classes - maybe it should be left alone. The healing lines for both classes could be geared towards non-healing group utility; things like better crowd control, buffs, debuffs, and group DPS. This would allow feral druids and shadow priests to fill the role of main healer in instances, but let restoration and holy priests coulld contribute just a little bit more. Not so much that others would demand a holy/restoration charicter, but enough to be noticable.

As an example, the holy line could keep things like combat resurection and improved flash heal, but also add things like AC buffs, debuffs, and maybe a talent that lets the priest reduce the 5 second mana regen rule after casting smite or holy fire.

I think changes like these would add a lot of color to both classes, and make non-healer builds viable for both classes.

EDIT: This is assuming that the core healing abilities of both classes would be balenced with each other.

Priest - Primary Healer/Moderate Nuker DPS
