 29. Re: End Game druid as of now~ | 10/12/2004 3:54:37 AM GMTDT
There's a lot of issues with druids right now...

Hurricane - Our only AE costs 31 talent points and is only marginally useful, AND didn't proc with Omen of Clarity last patch? (That could be a bug.) It's damage is mediocre, it can't be directed, and the attack slowing is so low as to be unnoticeable. We're not asking for it to be super-powerful, we're just asking for it to be VIABLE.

Tranquility - Useless. Period.

Indoors - A druid indoors is just pointless. We lose a lot of functionality, and now even our only escape spell (travel form) indoors. Even if entangling roots WORKED WELL (which, btw, it still doesn't), the fact that it is the ONLY ROOT IN THE GAME that cannot be used indoors just makes it pointless.

Hibernate - A sleep spell for the two least-common enemy types in an instance. I can think of maybe two instances I've done where this would be moderately useful. (WC and ST.) At least its SOME form of viable CC, certainly better than roots.

Cat Form - Cat form is NICE when you get it at 20. At 30 its decent. At 40 its pointless because Dire Bear far outpowers it. The only redeeming quality of it is stealth. Beyond that I generally don't use it anymore. Either the attack power needs to scale up as you level, or we need Dire Cat or something at 40 along with Dire Bear. We also need a DD finishing move in Cat form. Cat isn't even a viable replacement for a warrior's damage at 50+. Talents SHOULD NOT be REQUIRED to make forms viable.

Bear/Dire Bear - I find Dire Bear to be nice at 50+. I still use it primarily for soloing, but I have tanked with bear/dire bear, and its not bad. A warrior can still do a better job, but at the very least a Druid isn't a HORRIBLE tank. We can fake it pretty well.

Entangling Roots - Can't use indoors. Rarely, if ever, holds for longer than a second. Why bother? The only nice thing about it is that with talents its casting can't be interrupted. Roots being overpowered? HAH. IT'S PATHETIC. Remove the DoT, make it work indoors, and just make it flat-out WORK, and then it may be useful.

Travel form - Its lifespan as a good spell is 10 levels. Then its our PRIMARY escape spell. Except now we can't use it indoors. So now we can't use it to get out of a bad situation indoors. In essence, we can't get out of a bad situation indoors at ALL now. Either move this to level 20, and put dire cat or something at 30, or make it work indoors again. This patch has severely limited its usefulness.

Shields - Not really NECESSARY, but they'd be nice.

Innervate - Seems nice at first. Then you notice the 6 minute cooldown and the duration of only 20 seconds. Not much better than self-res.

Druids need a LOT of work. Forms (mainly cat) are not nearly as effective as caster. (FunHooves will disagree.) The feral combat talents are used primarily to make forms viable... I had always thought talents were there not to make something viable, but to actually make it BETTER if you so chose. Abilities and spells and such should be viable WITHOUT talents. Right now it seems to me that Druids are the "can't" class. We can't root indoors (unless you're facing the two least common forms of instance dungeon mobs). We can't do this, we can't do that. WHAT CAN WE DO? Shapeshifting in groups is almost always not wanted, as you're there to assist in healing (or as the primary healer). No one ever says "Hey, we can't find a mage/rogue/warrior, let's grab a druid that should work fine."

There's just no purpose to us in a group. I think it should say something to Blizzard that it took a BUG for people to finally want a druid for their instance dungeon groups. As it stands currently, I can't recommend that anyone play a druid. The disadvantages currently far outweigh the perceived advantages. I say again, we need work.

Edit: I forgot to mention that we're still basically the only class that has people carrying around 3-4 sets of equipment just to be effective in combat.
