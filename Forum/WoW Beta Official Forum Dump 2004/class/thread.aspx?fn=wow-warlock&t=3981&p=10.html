Idea for modifying channeling:

Perhaps add two bars, one for the actual "concentration" (channeling) and one for the "duration."

Channeling spells would have 2 bars, one on top of the other.

The top bar would be the "concentration bar." This bar would start out full and it would decrease (from right to left) upon being hit and keep decresing on each successfull hit untill the point that the bar is completely drained and the spell would be interupted (just like the current spell casting bar but would recharge alot slower). The number of hits that would cause the spell to be interupted and the rate of recharge of your concentration bar would scale based on the duration of the spell. For example: longer duration channeling spells would take more hits to interupt but your concentration would recharge slower. So, if one mob out of a whole pack attacked you it wouldn't ruin a whole AE spell in one hit. If all the mobs decided to come after you then the spell would be interupted quickly. Single target channeling spells would take fewer hits to interupt but your concentration bar would recharge alot faster, so if you were being hit you could still continue to channel the spell for more then a few hits, without any talents. You could then have a talent that increases the concentration bars recharge rate and/or the amount of hits you can take; but would never make your channeling spells completely uninteruptable, unless you specialize in a certain spell with talents to make it that way (like how the mages arcance missile works at the moment). Sprit could even have an effect on the recharge rate of the concentration bar (would be another good reason to pile up spirit). Of course, all abilities that currently instantly interupt spellcasting would interupt channeling spells as well.

The bottom bar would be the duration of the spell and would act like the current spellcasting bar(the bar fills from left to right untill the spell is completed).

Re-posted here from another Thread.

Best Warlock damage spells are Immolate, Life Tap, and Drain Life. Everything else is worthless.

=========================================
I have been doing an analysis of our Spells, including DOTS, curses, and DD spells. I did the analysis of spells from the 20-32 level area and can post the detailed calculations if necessary. Based on my analsys of, I have come to these conclusions.

Curse of Agony - Totally Worthless. If it goes its ENTIRE course, it is the most mana efficient spell {3.27 dmg/mana}, but mobs simply do not last for 1.5 mins so the efficiency and effectiveness of this spell declines exponentially the shorter the time the mob is alive. Plus the total damage that this spell can do over any time period is horrible compared to ANY other spell. Your dagger would do better as long as its DPS is above 4. Best case, this spell does {3.27 Dmg/mana} IF the mob lives the entire 1.5 min duration. If the mob only lasts 45s this drops to {1.62 Dmg/mana} or at 30s it drops to {1.09 Dmg/mana}.

Drain Life - Our BEST spell period. (See Combo with Life Tap below) However, this spell is not very mana efficient alone. At level 30 it has a {1.07 dmg/mana} ratio. Even so, it is still slightly more efficient than Shadow Bolt (our DD) at {1.06 dmg/mana}. Drain Life does 870 / 1740 / 2610 total damage over 30 / 60 / 90 seconds respectively. Compare the 1740 damage in 60 seconds to Curse of Agony's 240 damage during the same period. Drain life does have signifcant mana to damage cost, but combined with Life Tap, it turns into a SUPER DOT (see below).

Immolate - Our 2nd Best spell / DOT. Immolate 4 does {12.57 dmg/sec} compared to {29.0 dmg/sec} for Drain Life 3. But is much more mana efficient at {1.75 dmg/mana}. It is generally more desirable than Shadow Bolt in most cases because it takes less time to cast and doesn't generate as much of an immediate amount of agro as Shadow Bolt (107 initial Damage with Immolate vs 169 total damage with Shadow Bolt). With a 21 second damage duration, this spell will last easily for solo fights and decently for group fights. The initial DD component helps to make this spell viable in groups and prevents it from loosing too much efficiency if the mob dies before the 21 second DOT runs out.

Corruption - Good Mana Efficiency at {1.90 dmg/mana}, poor damage. With a Duration of about 45 seconds, this spell is usable in a solo environment, but loses its mana efficiency when the mob is killed before it completes. At 45 seconds, corruption does about 344 damage (including cast time). However, drop the time the spell is on the mob to 30 seconds and it drops to 229 damage and a {0.81 dmg/mana} ratio. With a better than 6.5 DPS dagger, you are better off stabing the mob than casting BOTH Corruption and Curse of Agony.

Shadow Bolt - Our only DD has the BEST Damage per second but the WORST dmg/mana ratio. While shadow bolt does fast damage, a Warlock generally doesn't want to generate this agro in such a short time in a solo fight which will happen when chain casting this spell to get the most damage quickly. Based on a 10 second fight, this spell could do 563 damage with a damage to mana ratio of {1.06 dmg/mana}. Drain Life will do 290 damage (less agro) during this same time with a {1.07 dmg/mana ration} using less mana, generating a more consistant agro and having the side benefit of increasing the warlocks health when not full. Shadow Bolt should ONLY be used when it is essential to kill the mob quick (i.e. attacking you) or maybe in a group situation if you can get it applied before the mob gets too low in health to be of value.

Drain Life / Life Tap Combination - SUPER DOT! This combination nets a cost of 5 mana, a gain of 41 health and a 145 dmg dot over 5 seconds (6.5 with spell refresh). This results from Lifetap 3 being a 130 mana gain with 104 health loss. Drain Life 3 uses 135 mana to DOT a target 145 damage over 5 seconds, while increasing the warlock's health by 145. The 130 mana gain of Life Tap minus the 135 mana cost of Drain Life results in a net mana cost of 5 mana to cast Drain Life. Drain Life then does 145 damage to the target, plus increases the Warlock's health by 145. The 145 health gain from Drain Life minus the 103 Health loss from using Life Tap results in a gain of 41 health. This combination also manages agro better because if the target attacks the Warlock, the channeling of the Drain Life DOT immediately stops, removing the agro generator. It also doesn't have an immediate direct damage component like Immolate.


Based on this analysis, it becomes clear that Curse of Agony is a worthless spell in the Warlock arsenal and Corruption is only minorly useful in solo battles. The true staples of the Warlock are Immolate, Lifetap, and Drain Life with Shadow Bolt for immediate takedowns.

What the Warlock needs most is 2 things:

  • Faster DOT's that provide additional effects like snare, weakness, an AC reduction, an agro reduction, decreased agility, etc...
  • Better Torment for the Voidwalker (and taunt on other pets). It barely works for most battles now and requires the Warlock to manually cast it to be effective. My pet for some reason ALWAYS decides it isn't necessary to him to cast Torment when the mob is attacking me.


Solo: Send in Pet. Weakness > Force Pet Torment > Immolate > Force Pet Torment > Life Tap > Drain Life > Force Pet Torment > Life Tap > Drain Life > Force Pet Torment > Rinse...Repeat

If nothing else, Blizzard please tweak our dots a little this next patch.


Note: The damage calculations are based on total spell time. Cast time PLUS Duration to take into account the advantages provided by insta-cast spells.
