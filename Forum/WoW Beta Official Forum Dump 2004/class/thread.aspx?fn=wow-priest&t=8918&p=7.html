 130. Re: Priest Questions for the Developers | 10/15/2004 9:01:08 PM GMTDT
Here are my thoughts and questions RE: the WoW priest...

1) Given that Shaman and Druids have become excellent healers with a very full set of utility type spells, what is planned for the priest to make it what it should be - the best healer in the game?

2) In light of the above, what is planned to make the priest class attractive outside its healing roll?

3) WoW priests appear to be a cross between the traditional Cleric and Necromancer rolls of many RPG's. One feature of each of those rolls has often been the ability to siphon HP's away from mobs, coverting it either to hp's or mana for the priest or their group. Is there room for this to be expanded upon in WoW? Perhaps alongside the Vampric Embrace talent?

4) Perhaps give Priests a "Sloth" type ability, spell or talent? This could be a short duration slow/snare, which would aid both groups and individual effots slightly. Giving priests something to bring to the table beyond just healing and damage is important. Having multiple skills to work with is key to maintaining interest and fun.

5) Why not give Priests an ability similar to Mind Soothe that clears all aggro from a mob (let's call it Mind Wipe or something). This would make a Priet's fear line much more useful, as it the Priest could fear a mob, then Mind Wipe the target so that it did not run away and aggro more mobs. Put a high recast time on it if need be. 30 seconds or a minute, whatever. In PvP, the affect could simply be to make the Priest invisible to the affected target for a few seconds.

--------
Talents
--------

My thought is that currently the Priest talents are "spread too thin" and aren't beefy enough to make mouths water. Here is my recommendation...

Make the holy tree specifically healing abilities and improvements, and some late game specials based upon Smite. Give priests some group curing abilities, some AE heals, some abilities to average the group's health, setting them up for an effective group heal. Do things to make healing dynamic and fun, especially at later levels.

When I look at the Holy tree, I see mostly table scraps that I don't really want to chew through to get to the actual "meat" of the tree.

For example, Smite is just bad compared to Mind Blast. It's too slow and I very rarely use it at all, despite the refresh time. My thought is that this spell and its talents need to change in some way, to make it more attractive. Lowering the cast time might help, also making it more effective vs. undead or specifically "evil" creatures would seem like a worthy change. If players are going to invest talents points in Smite, the spell itself needs to be worth using. Divine Fury should be moved up the tree quite a bit, say 10 points required. That might be enough to make me abandon Mind Blast and the Shadow Tree.

Addtionally, I am against basing other talents upon the chance to get a critical heal. For only a 5% increased chance to critical, it hardly seems worth it to get Holy Specialization, Spritual Healing, or Inspiration. Priests need to be reliable, and really... I'm not going to base a character upon a +5% chance to do anything.

Improved Resurrection is largely useless in my opinion. The amount of times someone has died and I have needed them out of Rez sickness 25% faster has been 0. Combat Resurrection is the right idea however. I think allowing players to get this ability at around LvL 20-25 would be superb, just as they start to explore most instances, where grouping in an intense environment against elites kicks in.

Subtlety is the first skill in the Holy Tree I think is superb. This should definitely remain, but be moved upward IMO. This could be useful to a grouping Priest right at Level 10. And it really isn't overpowering in any way at that level as I see it.

I would like to see multiple levels of Holy Fire. That ability simply doesn't scale well as a character gains levels.

I would like to see the Improved Fast Heal line be converted to all healing spells and if need be moved downward. I like Improved Prayer as is, as well as Improved Healing. It wouldn't even bother me if these abilities were 5 slots and scaled over a broad range of levels.

The grand finale of Holy Nova just doesn't cut it. Things down there needs to be pretty darn good if someone is going to invest that many points in this tree, and I just don't see this ability living up to that billing.

My ideas for the bottom would be an AE heal/cure of some sort, or an ability which averages group hitpoints, setting the Priest up for a group heal next. Additonally, some sort of ability based of the Smite line for damage on a long timer, perhaps a Harm Touch of sorts (this might be appropriate for the Shadow tree too). Things like that pump more diversity, and more fun into the art of healing.

---

I find the Shadow Tree to be much better, however I would greatly prefer to see more of the traditional Necromancer/Undead Mastery type roles here.

My main complaint about this tree is that Mind Flay has been made largely useless for soloing now. Priests need a way to consistently stun, snare, or root an opponent while remaining mobile, even if it is only for a few seconds. I felt the previous version of Mind Flay achieved this well. Now it is nothing more than an annoy to use nuke that sort of helps when an enemy attempts to run, but that's it. Priests are fairly flimsy, so either we need the ability to immobolize or snare all types of enemies for a minimum of few seconds while we move away. Shield helps, but its not enough quite often.

I would like to see Vampiric Embrace expanded upon. I like Shadowform in some ways, but it seems to depart from the role of a Priest a bit too much - unless we are indeed meant to be somewhat of a Necromancer type class as well (which is fine, just needs to be flushed out more if so).

Personally, I wouldn't mind seeing Mind Pain removed from the game completely, and replaced by another DoT type spell that stacks with our current DoT. I wouldn't even mind if one DoT required the other one to be on the target, perhaps combining the two could make one sort of "super DoT". This would put more emphasis on Smite (which would also need to be changed a it), but also free up room in the Shadow line for more interesting abilities along the lines of Vampiric Embrace and Shadow Weaving and siphoning. Even if it was less than the total DPS available from chain nuking with the current DoT on the target, it gives the player more options to Heal, Melee, etc... which promotes more fun, more dynamic play.

---

As for the Discipline Tree, I think it needs to be changed to hold more supplemental and complementary type talents for Shadow and Holy Abilities, as well as the current Discipline spells.

Silent Resolve for example seems wasteful when Shadow Affinity exsists, and Smite is not high threat. Improved Power Word Shield should have no requirement, nor should Power Word Fortitude I feel. Additionally, Mediation seems buried by things that are not necessarily abilities that most healers would want to invest in, but the ability itself is something almost all of them would like to have.

For the points required for Inner Focus, I think it should be a 100% chance to crit.

Meditation is too far down and buried by 1 to 3 point abilites that don't seem all that helpful to me. Perhaps adding a snaring or immobolizing ability above it would make discipline more attractive. Mental strength seems about right to me.

In closing, I know this is a difficult class to balance, but I also have faith that it can be done and done well. The key points in want to emphasize are...


1) The need for dynamic and creative ways to heal.
2) The need for priests to contribute to the group beyond healing and resurrection with debuffs, damage, and crowd control (even if they are temporary or minor effect).
3) Maintain the ability for Priests to effectively solo and compete with other classes in PvE and PvP.
4) Keep things FUN at all levels, which doing 1-3 properly should allow.
