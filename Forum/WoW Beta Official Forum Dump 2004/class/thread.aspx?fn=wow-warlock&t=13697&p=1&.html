 1. Firestone Proc Rate Tested, Results. | 10/22/2004 10:13:07 AM GMTDT
As part of our melee discussion, people were commenting / asking if using a Firestone and a proccing weapon was worth it. Lets look at that, shall we?

I currently am Lvl 55. I did my tests in Tanaris against Lvl 41-44 mobs which I can easily solo even without a Pet. I cast no spells against them.

Testing
I use a Hanzo Sword (chance to wound for another 75 damage), a Greater Firestone (no points in the Talent) and have a Fiery weapon enchant.

The number of "swings" refers to the number of hits scored against a monster. Misses were not counted.

Total Number of Swings: 206
Number of Mobs killed: 10
Number of Wounds: 10/206
Number of Fiery Weapon: 40/206
Number of Greater Firestone: 61 / 206

Percentages:
Chance of Wound: 4.85%
Chance of Fiery: 19.4%
Chance of Stone: 29.6%

Chance for *a* Proc: 53.85%.

All three can stack on top of each other.

Average Damage done to mob by melee: 45.
Average Proc Damage:
65 Wound
40 Fiery
70 Firestone

Average Added Proc Damage: 58.3

Conclusion:

These are only preliminary tests. However, based on them, we can see that (at least against mobs of this type and level), having the arrangement I described gives a warlock a 50% chance to land an extra hit (statistically) worth more than his own melee damage.

Hopefully the proc rate information is useful.

Also, to all the naysayers that claim warlocks should never melee, you'll note that the 31.63 additional damage per hit is adding roughly 70% to Deathstrider's melee damage output. If he were to use the Improved Firestone talent (91 avg damage per proc) the boost would be about 37.85 additional damage, or roughly 84% more damage in melee.

Consequently, this is a pretty solid arguement for why you should not get Improved Firestone, since the added damage would only add an additional 8.2% (1.84 / 1.70 = 1.082) to melee damage. Melee damage is a small portion of our total damage to begin with, so burning 2 talent points to boost it by 8.2% is pretty much a joke. That, and you need to have 20 points already invested in the Demonology tree to get it in the first place.
