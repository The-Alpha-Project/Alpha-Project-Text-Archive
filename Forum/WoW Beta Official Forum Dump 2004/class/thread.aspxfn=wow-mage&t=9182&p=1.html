Pyroblast has limited use as an opening spell, especially if you went 31 points into fire to get activated crits. If you did, you can't get PoM though, as it costs 21 points and we only get 51 total. But PoM+Pyro is another option, allowing more points elsewhere.

Either way, it's a pretty disappointing spell, considering the following:

The level 60 Fireball does an amount of damage between 561 to 715 if not resisted. The level 60 Pyroblast does an amount of damage between 715 to 891 if not resisted. So a Fireball landing at the top of its damage spread does exactly the same damage as a Pyroblast landing at the bottom of its damage spread. And you get double the casting time for that priviledge. Not taking into account resists or crits, it is actually possible for your high cast time, high recast time, many-talent-points-to-aquire, super-nuke to do the SAME damage as your common, free, standard nuke. That's not right.

Pyroblast's only saving grace is the extra 268 dot damage it does in the next 12 seconds, but how many fights do you engage in that last 18 seconds in a group? I'm hard pressed to get a 6 second spell CAST before the target is dead, let alone waiting another 12 seconds for the dot to do its work.

Even solo I usually kill faster than that, so some if not most of my dot damage gets wasted. It's one use is as a boss mob nuke used once or twice per encounter.

Just another example of PvP screwing up the game for PvE.
