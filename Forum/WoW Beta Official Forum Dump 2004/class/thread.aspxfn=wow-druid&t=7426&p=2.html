 22. Re: Druid = Good PvP? | 10/11/2004 7:48:18 PM GMTDT
I dont have much trouble in 1v1 against people roughly my level or lower, except for paladins and the occasional warrior. I'm a balance/nature spec, and so spend all my time in caster form, except for prowling through mobs, etc. Also, I'm a pure spirit build, so burst casting is your friend.

Against casters (warlock/priest/mage): Almost always win.. the ability to heal and do consistant dmg really destroys these folks. Warlocks seem to focus on the chain fearing, but our nice HoTs take care of any worries there until you are immune.. its still obnoxious though.

For priests, their dmg spells arent too painful, so try to keep them on the defensive and run them out of mana, while keepin rejuv or a free (OoC) regrowth on. Once they are out of mana, the game is up.

For mages, its all a matter of surviving the first wave. Granted their spells hurt a bunch.. but as long as you are able to pop a heal, they are low on mana and you havent even gotten started.

Melee is a bit more tricky::

Warriors are easy if you get the jump (at least with my build).. just root/starfire/moonfire/root etc.. If they get the jump with charge/MS.. you are definately at a disadvantage, but can usually pull out before death sets in. Spam natures grasp as soon as stun wears off, pop a rejuv while crawling away.. once at an adequate distance cast an ER, and start the nuke/root/repeat deal.

Paladins are a problem. With the ability to negate root, and with large hp and bubble/LoH to refill it, they are the worst of your worries. IMHO, its not even worthwhile to fight a pally.. just get him in combat, swap to travel, and outdistance him.

Shamans are another story.. Seeing as Im horde I dont get the chance to jump/be jumped by a shaman, but I have engaged in a number of duels. Ive noticed that their totem to break snaring doesnt work on ER, combine that with the fact that their damage spells are shorter distance than yours.. and its pretty much game over. Please correct me if Im wrong on the root deal, but I tested it out on a few occasions with the same results. It helps to take the fight away from their totems, as theyll either have to recast and use up more mana, or simply not follow you.
