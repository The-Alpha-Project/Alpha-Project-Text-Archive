I've read through most of the posts made and I'm going to disagree and agree with some of the stuff mentioned plus throw in some new ideas hopefully. The suggestions and improvements I'm going to list do not take into account the current talents in the game. They are meant to bring warlock up to the same level as top classes are without the added benefit of talents.

DOTs: Stacking yes, absolutely. Have priest and Druid dots stack too. In fact all dots even if they're weapon dots and two people in group are using the same weapon. With the current system pure DD based classes "Mage" are benefitting the most. Level the playing field by allowing DOT stacking for every class with no cap on the number of stacks. So if X number of a class are casting on the same target, they can have X number of the same dot going on that target. All except curse dots, namely Agony since it is a curse.

Someone mentioned DOT combos generating an extra DOT with curses. While it's an interesting idea, DOT stacking is a much simpler and effective solution which as I mentioned would benefit all dot classes not just warlocks.

Also on the subject of dots. It's annoying to find or craft items that say: Adds X damage to Fire/Shadow/Nature spells and effects and have it only work for DD spells. Once again this system is skewed to the DD based classes "Mage". If my Shadow Hood says adds 6 damage to all shadow spells and effects, well then why doesn't my Corruption/Agony have an extra 6 damage every tick? Or if the Robe of Power adds 4 to all magical attacks and effects, how come my Immolate/Corruption/Agony/Rain of Fire/Hellfire don't get that 4 damage bonus each tick? The only thing affected is my DD Shadowbolt. So allow these item bonuses to work on dots, wave spells (blizzard/rain of fire/hellfire etc.) This would benefit all casting classes not just "Mage".

Dain Life: To the person that suggested the first tier, level 14 drain life be removed. NO! As little life as it draws, it's still useful for fear tactics and when you're low on life. The only thing about drain life is that the absorbtion on it should be 25% more at all tiers. Secondly I'd like the wave damage to be shown again as it was a long time ago. Have the numbers show above the targets head as the life is being drained like it used to be displayed. Right now I can't even tell if the spell is draining the amount is supposed to be draining. On the subject of channeling in particular, it would more suitable to have it part of our talent line that we could decrease the chance of a channel spell breaking on hit, like Mages have for their Arcane line. This method should be employed for all classes that have channel based spells. It's fair right across the board.

Drain Soul: I agree the damage dealt by this spell doesn't make it worth the mana cost to use the higher level versions. Perhaps if the damage dealt was done every 2 seconds instead of 5 it would make this spell more useful to upgrade. Secondly it should work on the same Talent system but not as a separate branch. You spec in the Destruction line to have channeling interrupt less up to the equivalent of the mage interrupt decrease then it affects both Drain Life and Drain Soul.

Someone suggested Invisibility... I gotta say no to that one. It's a Mage defining spell, and there's no justification for the warlock to have it. We can see invis, and that's balanced.

Banish: Great spell and unique effect. Timer is too long on it as others have mentioned. Drop it down to 10min timer.

Soul Shards: Make them stack already. Stacks of 5 would be perfect. It's so annoying having no inventory space. With Quest items clogging up space and if you toss on a tradeskill's regeants, I have no bag space left to pick up the good items that drop every now and then. Soul Shards aren't some peripheral thing to the warlock class, it's an integral part of our spells. At least shamans can cast a whole spell line of totems based on one totem regeant. 5 different warlock spells require soul shards. Pets/Banish/Summon/Healstone/Rezstone. That's 6 of them you have to be carrying around if you just want to have the ability to cast one of each of those spells. This also alleviates the reclaiming pets concern. Once warlocks can stack these things, it won't matter that traveling gets rid of a pet or two since they could afford to keep at least a stack of these in their backpacks at all times. I would personally keep two.

Curse of Recklessness: I never use this curse. Weakness is almost always preferred. I'd rather time a shadowbolt right as a mob flees than take away my ability to fear it unless I cancel it out with another curse. As someone mentioned above, if a mob is now immune to fear then it should also be locked onto the target it has when recklessness hits. For PvE this curse would become very useful. It also alleviates the voidwalker taunting problem. In groups, warlock would become much more useful since he could keep mobs off the healer by putting recklessness once they're on the main tank.

Curse of Agony: I think this curse is fine, I use it when fear kiting and to stack curses in groups. Its an extra dot. The timer is a little long, but I'd rather have this spell left as is in favor of fixing the other spells. All this needs is a shorter timer, but it isn't such a big deal.

Hellfire: This is a great spell idea except someone forgot to think it all the way through. First of all it takes 2 seconds to cast before it even begins channeling. Secondly it requires channeling. Now it also deals damage to the caster which already has low hitpoints and at the same time generates alot of hate. Plus it also breaks when you get hit. So this is a pretty useless spell. The core concept is great, but the implementation makes it a waste to buy and suicide to use. So first of all, since it is a PBAoE why does it have a casting timer on it? The damage is dealt over 10 seconds so it's not instant, and thus not overpowered. Make it an insta cast, even on the move. As a channeling spell it limits movement and thus also limits radius after time of casting. Well instead of it being a 10 sec channeling spell. Have it as a 10 sec buff effect only self targetable. Yah, you read right. Instant cast like a curse but it's a buff effect on the caster. Meaning he could run around on fire if the targets are moving around. Secondly it could be canceled as all buffs are by a right click before the duration runs out. Also, here's a poignant question. Why would I choose to deal 390 damage to a target while dealing 260 to myself at first rank? By that time I can cast a 150 shadowbolt without ever hurting myself. So why not make the damage dealt to damage taken ratio something plausible and advantageous? Say 10 damage to the caster per second or even no damage at all to the 39 dealt out? With these changes all of sudden Hellfire is a useful and potent spell. And since we get it so damn late anyway, it might as well be useful and potent.

Curse of Weakness: It was already mentioned that this curse doesn't seem to work properly and I'd like to reaffirm that. I too have noticed that it only reduces damage by about 75% of what it is supposed to according to the description.

Sense Demons: I have to disagree with the person who posted that it should be removed since it costs only 5 skill points from the trainer. I think most warlocks would agree that they'd rather pay 60s for the spell than 5 skillpoints. Skillpoints are far more precious than money. So no, don't remove it. Instead move it to level 40 along with Enslave Demon.

Enslave Demon: Useless spell. No ifs ands or buts, just useless in its current form. As I just mentioned, move this spell to level 40 since this is the level when warlocks don't get a new pet. I assume since Felhunter is supposed to be level 30, that Infernal would be level 50. Therefore at 40 the warlock gets Nightmare, which isn't a pet. So put Enslave Demon at level 40 to make up for the lack of pet. Increase the mana cost substantially and make it a permanent thing. With the exception that the warlock can't enslave something above his level. No chance to break free, nothing. Just simply another method of getting a pet. This would still be a limited use spell due to the rarity of demons among various zones.

Imp: Remove the AI on this thing. It should just sit where it is and do what it's told. I'm sick of it running off for no damn reason sometimes. I understand it has to get in line of sight, but the imp has caused several group wipes in instances because I took my eye off it for a brief second. Everything else is fine about the imp. The damage shield could use a little more damage but that's about it. This pet would be fine for both PvP and PvE.

Voidwalker: This pet is great, and by far the most widely used. However I agree with a previous poster that instead of a mana bar it should have a rage bar, and torment would be rage based. The other thing is that this pet deals a max of 9 damage to anything of equivalent level to it, at any level. That's pretty sad. An enrage ability that was previously mentioned would bring this pet's damage to an accetable level. That still leaves the Voidwalker with very limited PvP usefulness. I suggest adding a Hamstring type attack to its list of powers. One of the most annoying things as a warlock is chasing down a fleeing enemy. I basically have no way of really catching them or slowing them down. My only hope is that I can get a fear off before the range is too far and then hope they run towards me. I don't agree with a previous poster who suggested adding a curse to our spell repetoire that would slow down movement. That would make things too easy. However a pet ability would limit the use of this snare ability and would make the voidwalker a desireable PvP pet. Warrior has rushing charge/hamstring to deal with fleeing opponents. Priest has insta cast Mind Blast and insta cast dot. They can simply chase and nuke till the runner is dead. Mage has frostbolt/nova/sheep. Druid has Roots/sprint in catform. I read through most of the feedback here and tried to offer more or less unique contributions. Bear in mind that I think if all these suggestions were implemented, the result would be an overpowered warlock. I see the class as potentially going in different directions, so have offered different kinds of feedback.

First things first: get our remaining pets done and in the game before working on anything else for the class other than major bug fixes.

Emphasize the training of core class skills. Require players to specialize in one or two rather than maxing all of them. This will provide for some diversity among members of the class; we’ll have warlocks who specialize in curses/destruction or destruction/demonology, etc. Training could be similar to Shadowbane’s, where you have a set number of trains per level and “spend” them at the class trainers, or like UO’s, where you set certain skills to go up (or down) as you fight, keeping others locked where they are. Implement a fourth skill (Summoning) that affects pet damage and overall effectiveness. This type of emphasis on class skills and specialization should be extended to all classes.

Each pet should be able to handle most conceivable situations, both PvP and PvE, while requiring different playstyles from the warlock. Here are a few suggestions:

The imp is a ranged attacker that buffs the warlock to be better able to tank for him, with a significant group stamina boost and a minor damage shield. He should stay within a certain radius of the warlock, and should not run in the event that he draws aggro—generally, only inexperienced or poor players do this in a group. His buffing should be more significant than it is now, enhancing the warlock’s melee capabilities dramatically. WC3 style immolation, that damages all opponents within melee range of the warlock, would be a nice addition. An increase in attack power and additional regeneration are other possibilities, or perhaps something that prevents the warlock’s spell casting from being disrupted by normal melee attacks. A warlock with an imp should be able to tank mobs of his level reasonably well.

The voidwalker is the reverse of the imp, and serves as a tank for the warlock. Many suggestions have been made for improving the VW’s function in this capacity, but I think all that is really necessary is that he have some way to effectively get aggro off the warlock if he draws it for whatever reason. I suggest an ability (Infuriate?) that would clear the subject mob’s hate list while forcing it to attack the VW. Cooldown should be relatively significant, perhaps in the order of five minutes. In PvP, it would force the targeted player to attack only the VW for something like 10-30 seconds, depending on rank. Targeting would immediately switch to the VW, and other hostiles would be non-targetable for this duration.

The succubus uses her abilities of beguilement and seduction to better allow the warlock to take his opponents down quickly and efficiently. Like many, I think a slight increase in damage is in order, and I also suggest that her AI be changed so that she more actively positions herself to use Lash of Pain. A vampiric aura like the WC3 Dreadlord’s would be appropriate and make her more party-friendly. She might do well to have a bit of a mouth on her, like the imp does, although her comments would be more sinister.

Warlocks directly channel the infernal energies of the Burning Legion, and should therefore have the most potential of any class for massive damage in a large area: AoE damage. Our AoE’s should be more devastating than the mage’s, simply put. We should also have AoE versions of curses and drains, as well as some form of shadow damage AoE.

Hellfire should damage all friendly characters as much as it does the warlock, while getting a significant bump to the damage it inflicts to opponents—this should be the most destructive AoE in the game by a wide margin, as has been suggested, but it doesn’t make sense that it damages the warlock but not any allies in the area of effect. I also think this should be uninterruptible except for attacks that would normally interrupt spellcasting. The visual effect of Hellfire needs work as well. My suggestion would be for the ground to crack and split, with gouts of flame pulsing up and setting all the affected opponents on fire, while less prominent flames lick at the lower portions of the warlock and affected allies.

Debuffing should be another of our specialties; make curses more significant overall. Give us a snare in the form of a curse—Curse of Lethargy, perhaps. Allow Curse of Recklessness to be cast on allies as well as enemies, as it could be considered a buff in certain situations.

Increase warlocks’ melee capabilities overall, turning us into more of a hybrid melee-caster class. Allow us to wear leather and use a greater range of melee weapons. The ability to use poison would fit well with the class flavor.

Eliminate Drain Soul. Add its soul-stealing effect to both Drain Life and Drain Mana. If an opponent dies while either drain is active, a soul shard is generated.

Add a persistent visual effect to Demon Armor, like a glowing green demonic sigil at the warlock’s feet, so that we can immediately tell when it has expired. This spell is vital the warlock’s survivability and should never be down for more than a moment.
