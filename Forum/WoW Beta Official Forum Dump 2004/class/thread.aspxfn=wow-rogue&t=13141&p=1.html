 1. Essay: Daggers vs. Swords/Maces. | 10/14/2004 3:56:28 AM GMTDT
Hello. This topic has come up many times in the past (both in the forums and in-game), I thought I would post a thread and give my thoughts and opinions on this much-debated concept. Please keep in mind, that these are merely opinions. Many of the ideas discussed here were taken from the Blizzard rogue forums, as well as from my own tests. Any responses on this topic would be much appreciated, especially if you disagree with me (just please don’t flame me). I know that many of the things stated in this post will be obvious to many of you, but please, bear with me. If you do disagree with me, please give us a good description of the reasons and opinions that you have, so that we can keep this topic going. That being said, I hope you find this post informative, if nothing else. :)

Here we have the question that often plagues a rogue: are daggers best, or should I learn to use sword or mace? Why would I make this choice and what benefits would it give me?

Well I am here to try to shed some light on this subject. So here goes.


PART 1: Which should I use?

First of all, I would like to ask you all to think about the rogue ability that you use most in combat. In my opinion, that would be Sinister Strike (SS). Lets take a look at the SS description so we are all up to speed on it (this is thottbots latest description): "An instant strike that causes X damage in addition to your normal weapon damage. Awards 1 combo point" (where X is a number based on the current level of SS).

There are a couple key points to this description that I will discuss. The first main point being that the damage SS deals is based on your weapon damage. This is main hand weapon, not off-hand. So, the next question to ask yourself, is what weapons can a rogue train that do the most damage? Well daggers go right out the window at this point. Swords and maces deal much more damage than daggers, hands down. Now don’t go getting all flustered about not using a dagger yet, I’ll discuss daggers later. But for now, lets focus on swords/maces.

Before I get into swords/maces though, let me just explain something about weapons to you. When the SS description says, "weapon damage" it refers to the # listed next to "Damage" on the weapons description. NOT speed, and NOT damage per second (DPS). Just plain ol' weapon damage. Just to quickly explain, DPS is based on a formula regarding weapon speed, and weapon damage. You will find that when comparing 2 different weapons of different speeds and damage, they can often have the same DPS. For example, a dagger with 1.30 speed dealing an average of 35 damage, may do (and I'm just guessing) 25 DPS. Compare that to a dagger with 1.90 speed dealing an average of 55 damage, it may still do 25 DPS. The reason for this is because a faster weapon hits more often, but deals less damage. And a slower weapon hits less often, but deals more damage. So over say, 5 seconds, both weapons will deal the same amount of damage, and thus the DPS for these 2 very different weapons will be the same. Of course this varies from weapon to weapon, and from level to level, but this is just to give you a general idea, of how 2 weapons with totally different speeds and damage ratings, can sometimes have the exact same DPS’s.

Ok now back to swords and maces. So you train swords and maces, since they do more damage than daggers. Now which of these 2 choices deals the MOST damage? Well that’s debatable. At one character level, you may find that swords deal more damage at that level than maces, and at a different level, you may find the opposite to be true. All in all, I believe that sword and mace damage is approximately the same. So which is better? That’s up to you. Find whatever is best for you at your current level. Keep in mind some weapons will have additional stats and abilities on them, which may increase damage (agility and strength will help with attack power and attack damage). Also keep in mind that the combat talent tree allows you specialize in melee weapons of different types. So you may decide that you want to go with one or the other just so you can specialize in it. It all depends on your talent build. The end result should be, that the weapon you choose for your main hand, should have the highest DAMAGE possible for that level (not DPS, and not fastest speed).

You will find that when trying to find a weapon with the highest damage, that slower weapons will have higher damage #s. This is to balance out the DPS between faster weapons and slower weapons, as explained above. So you will always want to search for a sword or mace that has the SLOWEST speed, as this will give it the highest possible damage.

Now I have heard many complaints that you don’t want to use a weapon with a slow speed, because it wont hit very often. Now let me tell you 2 things that should help to dispel that fear:
1) As explained above, the DPS will be the same as an equivalent weapon that strikes faster and deals less damage. So your DPS will be approximately the same if you were using a faster weapon.
2) The SS description says "an instant strike". This is true, because as soon as you use your SS attack, it INSTANTLY attacks. If you use the talent "adrenaline rush" you will find that you can attack over and over, just as fast as you can mash the SS key. So when using SS, weapon speed is irrelevant. You can use SS just as fast as you can press the key, provided you have the energy to do so.

So since weapon speed shouldn’t bother you quite as much anymore, I recommend going with a sword/mace that has the SLOWEST speed, in order to get the highest damage output from using SS. This is all I look for when weapon shopping for my main hand: slow speed, and high damage. The highest # that the damage can do (weapon damage is always shown in a range, such as 15-27 damage, or 48-92 damage) is going to be the most damage that you can deal with SS with that weapon (not counting the X damage that SS adds to each attack). So shop for a weapon that has the HIGHEST possible damage at the top end of the damage range. In the 2 examples given above, 27 would be the highest damage, and 92 would be the highest damage, respectively, that those weapon ranges can deal.


PART 2: If I don’t use a dagger, how do I use my special attacks?

I know this is another major concern for people who use Backstab (BS) or Ambush. Let me present 2 ideas to you, that should help you to not fear being daggerless:
1) For those of you who use Ambush or BS, here is a solution to have the best of both worlds: equip your dagger (again you want a dagger with HIGHEST damage range, and slowest speed, in order to deal the most damage with these abilities), use your special attack, then switch to sword/mace. 2 patches ago, weapon switching (which has always been possible) was made extremely easy. If you right click on any item that you can equip, it automatically equips the item, and swaps it out for the one you previously had equipped. This works for weapons as well as other equipment you can wear. So use a dagger to start the fight, then switch to mace/sword for the rest of the fight. Easily done.
2) Unless you have a talent build that is designed to enhance BS or Ambush, Cheap Shot (CS) is the best opening move to go with. Keep in mind that you can use CS, Garrote, and Sap with ANY type of melee weapon, not just daggers. Generally, CS is best because it stuns your target for 4 seconds, (AND gives you TWO combo points) and you can usually deal more damage in that 4 seconds than you can with Ambush. However, like I said above, you can make talent builds that significantly increase the damage of Ambush, in which case you would want to use that instead of CS (because you can do more damage with that 1 talent enhanced attack, than you can in those 4 seconds of CS), and then swap out your dagger after the initial attack for your sword/mace.

Another arguable point, which I’m not going to go into much detail about, is Backstab. Generally, SS can deal more damage than BS, for the same energy cost. BS is expensive, and requires that you use gouge (in order to get behind your opponent). For the full energy cost of 1 gouge and 1 BS, you should be able to deal even more damage with multiple SS’s alone. Again, if you beef up on talents that help enhance BS, then this may not hold true for you. Also, if you like to use BS because you group a lot, then BS is something that may work better for groups, depending on the situation. But over-all, SS is a better choice, in my opinion.


PART 3: Off-hand discussion.

I have seen many rogues out there who use all types of weapons in their off-hand. I have seen rogues dual wielding 2 swords; I have seen rogues using a dagger in main hand and mace in off-hand; etc. When it comes to what weapon is best to use in your off-hand, this is something that I don’t have a definite answer to. However, I can point out some various ideas I have about the subject, and let you make your own decisions on it. Please give me feedback if you have found something to work well or not to work well.

First (and I don’t have the exact numbers on this), off-hand damage deals significantly less damage than main hand damage. I believe its somewhere around 50% less damage. How exactly this % affects your overall weapon damage and DPS, I don’t know. But I do know that the weapon speed is unaffected.

So, I have 2 ideas of what kind of weapon is best to use in the off-hand:
1) High DPS, which could be anything really. A slow sword or a fast dagger; whatever you can find that has the highest DPS rating. Most swords or maces can NOT be used in the off-hand however. You have to find a weapon that says “one hand” instead of “main hand”, which is rare. There are some quests and some rare drops out there, but they are few and far between. If it says “one hand”, you can use it in either hand. And far as I know, any dagger can be equipped in the off-hand. So again, I would suggest going for the highest DPS weapon you can get, to equip in the off-hand.
2) Fastest speed, which is usually daggers or fist weapons. Because your off-hand weapon deals less damage, poison or a weapon with a damage proc is a good way to counter that. Poison/weapon proc damage is NOT lessened just because it’s in your off-hand. So a weapon with poison or a damage proc will have a better chance of proc’ing if its got a fast speed on it. The more often the weapon hits, the more chances you get for your poison/weapon proc to go off.

Far as I know, Julies Dagger is a decent high-level dagger that is both fast (1.30 speed) and has decent DPS. If anyone finds a dagger or fist weapon higher level than Julies, please post about it here. Also if you find a dagger or fist weapon that has a faster attack speed than 1.30, please post it.


As a last note, I want to mention that there is a great online talent tree, where you can customize your talents and play with them to see how things turn out. There is also a section for viewing other “submitted rogue templates” which I have found to be EXTREMELY helpful for giving me ideas for talent builds. I especially like the 4 and 5 star rated builds (unfortunately, they haven’t updated the page to include the latest patches talent changes, but I’m sure that will be soon to come):
http://wowvault.ign.com/View.php?view=Talents.View&category_select_id=7


In conclusion, it is my humble opinion that daggers, swords, and maces all have a place in the game. Daggers work best for your starting special attacks, and swords/maces work best for SS and deal the most damage in combat. You can swap weapons (and other equipment) in the middle of combat, without penalties, and without any downtime during the fight. Using a sword/mace for your main attacks after the start of combat will greatly increase the damage you deal, and I’m sure you won’t be disappointed. :)

 17. Re: Essay: Daggers vs. Swords/Maces. | 10/19/2004 1:10:38 AM GMTDT
There is a key point people often miss when comparing sword-mace builds vs dagger builds. Using SS with a sword may have higher dps than a Ambush-backstab dagger. However, there is a dmg threshhold you need to figure out, to see which one is better.

For example, my Ambush Backstab builds often put 1400+ dmg on a target instantly, with 800 more following very soon. A sword build with say an Argent Avenger Starts with maybe 600 dmg, gets regular melee hits, then you can follow a couple more SS hits. I run well over 50% crit on Ambush and Backstab. I run 25% crit on SS. If I need a very fast battle, the ambush backstab method is the way to go. If its a fight vs something that is going to take a while to kill, often the SS route can be better.

An easy analogy to illustrate this point. If my life expectancy is 4 seconds, I can often put 2500 dmg on a target before I die with a dagger. Same situation with a sword, Ill be lucky to get 1500 dmg in 4 seconds. One is clearly better than the other in this scenario.

So, when faced with the question of pvp, when I need to put the most amount of dmg on a target in the least amount of time, Ill go daggers all the way.
