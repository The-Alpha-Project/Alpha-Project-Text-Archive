 1. It's my considered opinion that forms are... | 10/14/2004 12:40:36 PM GMTDT
Useful and fun in PvP, at least. Haven't tried feral in an instance because I'm scared of what would happen to my parties without me healing.

But forms are much more viable than they were several patches ago. Feral charge would be excellent if it actually prevented spellcasting for the 5 seconds it says it does, and maul would be excellent too if it, well, did any damage.

I'm about halfway through getting a decent set of gear for shifting forms at this point -- if you can, pick up the druid-only helm which gives +5 to rage and +20 to energy when you shift in -- it's mislabeled, giving you 15 rage and 40 energy. Combined with talents, this turns into 35 rage and 80 energy, enough to drop into bear, charge, stun, demoralizing roar, drop back out, toss an instant healing touch on whoever's getting hurt, hit cat, backstab a bit, hit some DoT finishers...

It's not fixed. And it's not great. But there's potential. Potential to be so very, very nifty. At this point all I'd really like to have is some sort of hamstring analog for bear form (not going to happen) or a way to add a solid punch to a hit, some sort of instant-cast (not next-hit) strike that does a decent amount of damage. Like Mortal Strike, but it wouldn't scale with weapon damage because we don't have weapons, and another type of finisher for cat form. A stun would make me twitch in my boots with glee, and a spell interrupt would make me chortle with delight. A DD finisher is too much to hope for.

It's definitely still less effective than balance / restoration, and you'll never be the same type of instance healer with feral (no Omen), but secondary healer is a viable role if you go down Restoration enough to get Swiftness.
