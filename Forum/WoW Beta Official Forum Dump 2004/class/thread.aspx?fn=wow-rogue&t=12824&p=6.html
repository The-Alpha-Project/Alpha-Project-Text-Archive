I agree with everything in this thread.

1) My first question - Are there any plans to fix the Warrior's "Overpower" skill so it doesn't guarantee them a win almost every single time? Seeing as how our dodge rate pretty much guarantees them a huge-damage crit against us every fight.

I'd love to see Overpower changed to work off a Parry. It's a guaranteed crit - so the higher their chance to parry, the higher your chance to get a FREE crit.

When you run the numbers, a warrior's chance to crit against a rogue (or any mob, really, but specifically rogues) is like this.

War chance to crit + Chance they'll dodge and give you a free overpower crit + any other bonuses from gear/talents = total crit %

Seeing as how my rogue has a dodge chance of something like 12%, and an added 50% with evasion, this means in a fight against a warrior he has at ANY TIME a 12% to 50% chance of a GUARANTEED successful crit against me.

By contrast, with full talent spec in combat and a tiny bit in assassination, my chance to crit is 19% - entirely dependant on chance. And against a warrior with plate, this is severly inadequate.

2) My second question - Can we get an idea of the dev's plans for leather armor? I'm entirely against the idea of giving us mail/plate, but we have no advantages to wearing cloth armor, and Leather as it stands is not doing the job. We need more neat stats, resists, procs, and drops for leather.

3) Final Question - I'm sure the rogue community as a whole would love to see some kind of breakdown of the vision for the different weapon types. Speed and damage for daggers, swords, axes, etc. all seem to blend together, and right now there appears to be no difference in dps between a rogue wielding two superfast daggers and a warrior wielding a 2h axe. I realize the key point of combat is DPS, but what differentiates a rogue with X dps from a warrior with the same amount?
