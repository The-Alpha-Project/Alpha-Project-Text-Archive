 1. A realistic look at paladins | 10/16/2004 5:42:13 PM GMTDT
First off, let me state that I am a dwarf paladin and thus, am only making arguments from what I have knowledge off. That being stated, let me begin.

I am tired of the complaints against paladins. You know what I am talking about... "we are too weak, our seals are useless, we are too strong in pvp, we don't have any talents, etc."

Let me tell you about how to play a paladin from 1-24 and why I've had no problems with this class.

As a dwarf paladin, you start out in Anvilmar. Just like any starting city, grab as many quests as you can. The xp and financial rewards are well worth it if this is your starting character. IF you are twinking and have mailed yourself 10 gold to outfit for the next 20+ levels, do the quests anyways. Probably the best quests I found in anvilmar are the ones the result in the boar slayer equipment and there is also a tome retrival quest from graelin[sp?] that nets you a pretty sweet weapon. From anvilmar, follow the [northern?] road and you will get these quests. I wouldn't attempt the retrieval quest until around level 4 or so, since you have the possibility of getting jumped in the cave. When I did the quest, there was a priest trying to do the quest as well, so I had some help. Around level 5-6, you should think about heading through the tunnel and up to Kharanos. There are tons of gather/xp quests to do in Kharanos, and if you are planning on becoming a cook, you can pick up a quest to do beer basted boar ribs. Personally, I pick up every cooking quest I can get. Gather quests are annoying, which has been stated about 100000 times, but I find that consumable items sell well and once you can make thistlethorn tea[rogue energy potion], warriors and rogues will love you if you bring some high hp/energy restoring stuff to battle. At a high enough level, you can make mana recovery stuff as well [according to recipes I've seen?], so that is definetly something to think about. However, if this is your first character, you might just want to think about selling off stacks of meat/ribs/etc in the auction house to make money so that you can keep yourself properly equipped. It's really up to you!
But back to Kharanos... perhaps the best quest I found in K. was the wendingo quest. Not only is the xp good, but if you are a skinner, this is a perfect time to kill, skin, and skill up. I have found skinning to bring nice profit. On beta server 2 a stack of light leather sells for 2-3 silver and once you get high enough, medium leather sells for 15-20 silver. It might not seem like much, but it adds up quick, especially once you can afford/quest some 10 or 12 slot bags.
I believe I stayed in and around K. until about level 10 or so. IF you explore up around ironforge [and to the east I believe] there is a place called steelgrill depot. This is where you can get the evershine quest, which results in a sweet axe for being a low level. Just like any other quest, this involves running to place X [brewnall village], killing X mob[s], running back, etc. Honestly, I've always picked up every quest available, especially as a new character. Not only does this break up the slow xp you get from grinding as a paladin, but it is lucrative finacially and xp wise. I've NEVER had a problem staying well equipped for my level doing quests and selling skins, and I have some of the best quested/AH gear possible. Depending on how much money you have, and whether or not you did the evershine quest, I would go to Ironforge and train in one-handed axes. It's kind of pricey at your level to train in 1 handed/2 handed swords at this point and it's really not that helpful to you until around 13+... Why 13+ you ask? Because you can do the vagash elite quest to get the coldridge hammer which is going to be your friend [2 handed mace] until you can get something better [Trogg slayer from gathering idols quest in loch modan/Theismar is a nice upgrade, but then again you have to go to stormwind to train 2 hand swords if you want to use it]

I would definetly try to buy some gear from the auciton house before heading over to Theisamar [you should go there around 10-11+, preferably at least 11] There is plenty of nice ac gear with stats you can get from questing in Kharanos and Theisamar and if you have been saving your money like I suggested, you will have no problem finding a group to help you do the trogg progression quests. I am not going to go into detail about every quest, you can use www.thottbot.com to look up quests, but some of the best ones to do in Theisamar are the trogg killing quests, chok-sul at 17+ [nice mail and ring reward, but make SURE you have a group that knows what it is doing, this quest is HARD], and gathering idols [2 handed sweet sword reward] Around 13+ you can also opt to travel to stormwind and head over to westfall to do the defias quests ... I didn't know about westfall until almost level 17 and I kind of regret not doing the quests over there earlier. Once you hit 20, this is where you really begin to fall into your element. You get exorcism [undead dd spell] and you can really just wail on undead 2-3 levels above you if you have the proper gear and know how to play. I got lucky and found some level 28-30 people in duskwood willing to help me do the high level duskwood quests, but if you are feeling adventerous, pick up some of the low level duskwood quests and head over to the trainquil gardens and work on your undead slaying skills. If you are level 20 and doing this, you WILL have downtime... you will miss ALOT and use up alot of mana healing/casting exorcism to kill the skeletal mages/warriors. Stun and crusader strike are your friends [not only against these guys, but against everything] You can stay in duskwood, go to the stockades in stormwind, run over to redridge mountains ... any of these areas are good for 20-24... you can also head back to loch modan and run over to menethil harbor to do some of the wetlands quests, which is what I am currently doing at level 24. I wouldn't recommend running to the wetlands solo until at least level 20-21.

Now, my thoughts on seals/spells/auras.
I ALWAYS use devotion aura [AC buff] ... sometimes, if I get jumped by 2 mobs, I will use retribution[damage shield], but this is rare. When fighting undead, i will cast the +60 attack against undead seal and pull with exorcism and lead off with a crusader strike/stun or just crusader strike depending on the mob. Most of the time I will CS with a 2 handed weapon for maximum damage and then switch back to a one handed sword/mace and my shield to give fast attack+high ac. If the mob you are fighting hits fast, I recommend going for the high ac and setting up a hot button to equip a 2 hander so you can switch gear every time crusader strike refreshes. I use healing aura once in a blue moon and hardly ever solo. I'll use it to top off friends in instances if I don't want to waste mana after a fight when the rogue is scouting, or MAYBE when im soloing if I'm a little low on health, but I don't want to pop a heal off because I see two mobs coming my way that I'll need full mana for. This is ALL personal choice and it really depends on your play style. A good rule of thumb is to attack creatures that don't hit really fast, that are your level or +1 -1 above/below [unless undead, then you can do +2 or +3 above] ... you DO have devotion aura, lay on hands, etc that will help you get your butt out of trouble many a time... I can't recall how many times this has saved me , but it has been alot.

Your role in a group, depending on who else is there is to either be the primary tank [if no warriors are around], primary healer [if no priest or druids] or to be a secondary healer/support class
Yes, the biggest problem with being a tank is our aggro management, especially if you have trigger happy mages or warlocks... it IS frustrating to not be able to maintain aggro and to have to pop off 3-4 heals and a seal of freedom on your mage/warlock just because X mob doesnt realize that you are a badass with a big sword and shield and that they should be chewing on you, not your cloth/leather wearing friends.

Yes, we don't have talents yet, but this has not slowed me down any. I am able to solo effectively, quest effectively, and always find a group to help me do a hard/elite quest if I ask nicely. If you know how to play your class[rezzer/healer/butt saver], then people will begin to see that you are an asset. Also, taking the time to do hard quests to get good gear is a sign that you are dedicated. People WILL inspect you and you will get respect if you have certain hard to get items equipped.

I've experimented with other classes as well ... druid,rogue,shaman... and yes, I like the diversity they offer, but I have faith[haha] in the paladin. I really think they bring something unique and interesting in playstyle to the game, even though they are not complete as a class yet. I DON'T think they are over-powered in PVP... I'm on beta 2 and I always duel when I can... I can take out rogues +1 +3 levels above me if I'm careful and keep them stunned, mages, let's just say you should be able to take out a level 26 mage if you have decent resists... warriors on the other hand, +3 levels above you will be a struggle, especially depending on what stance they use and what gear they have and if you are dueling another paladin, prepare for a LONG fight. Yes, it's annoying to fight us and have us pop divine aura when almost dead and heal back to full health, but that's half the fun , right? ;) Buy yourself some invuln potions if you want to complain about us doing that and have bandaids/health potions ready...

Like I have said before, there is no reason you shouldn't be able to make money by farming as well... probably the best 2 things to pick up are skinning/mining or skinning/alchemy. Personally I'd go with skinning/alchemy because you always see dead mobs lying around for you to skin and if you have a guild mate or friend alchemist, they can make you some nice potiosn from the herbs or you can sell the herbs in AH.

This is hopefully to help dispell some of the myth about the paladin that we suck. Yes, there are aggro management issues and useless seal/aura issues to deal with, but everything has a time and a place. By providing POSITIVE[ even in a negative form, but with numbers/pictures/proof] feedback, perhaps we can help get this class polished to the way we *think* it should be. If you have the patience, tenacity, and gumption to play a difficult and unfinished class, give the paladin a try. Our ability to survive and find a place in a group will help you on your quest to fame and riches across the land :)

Something to keep in mind is that you're still only level 24. Classes change rather dramatically in as little as 10 levels - amd Paladins are no different.

1-30 as a Pally you feel invincible, you kill things about the same speed as non-mages/rogue, and there aren't many areas where bad aggro management will hurt a group. 30+ your low damage starts to show, as the higher level you get the longer it takes you to kill things. 40+ areas start to put pressure on aggro management, which you have none of, and you're taking 2-4x as long as other classes to kill things. You get the picture.. its a constantly evolving playstyle - you can't take a small snapshot and say "Hey, this class is fine - I don't see why everyone complains!".
