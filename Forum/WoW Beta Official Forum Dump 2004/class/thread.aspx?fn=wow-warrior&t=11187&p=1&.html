 15. Re: Blizzard: What is the Warrior's role in W | 10/22/2004 3:20:07 AM GMTDT
Basically, the role is melee combat.

Rather than talking talents, I'll talk stances, and assume that talents are selected in accord with stances and the goals embodied by your stance selection.

- Battle Stance: versatility. You can take damage decently, and deal damage decently. You don't have any special abilities to keep the enemy focused on you, or any extra-special killing abilities (aside from Mortal Strike, which is widely regarded as much better than the other top-level talents).

- Defensive Stance: tanking. If you spend your time in defensive stance, you'll be harder to hit, but have a harder time landing a killing blow (especially since you'll likely be wielding a shield). Your skills in this stance are focused on a couple of debuffs and the ability to focus monster rage on yourself and then survive the damage.

- Berserker Stance: it's not even close to complete yet, though maybe with the coming patch... but it's clearly meant to be about the heaviest damage output. Whether this will be by stronger techniques or by having multi-target attacks, it's the most offensively powerful stance, but it suffers a defensive penalty in that you take more damage.

No matter what, you can tank better than a rogue (except while he has Evasion active) by virtue of wearing much heavier armor, especially at high level. In terms of not being hit, a rogue can come to a similar point as a warrior, but between the higher armor value of plate/chain vs. leather, and the higher hps (esp. with the large +stamina plate/chain armors), you can endure more damage.

Now, damage output wise... you'll have consistently significant damage, while rogues seem to have to rely on special abilities to deal damage. However, they can do this fairly often, since energy regenerates fairly swiftly, and they can regularly begin a battle with full energy, whereas we have to work fairly hard to have full rage at the beginning of an encounter.

Rogues also have more versatility in many respects; a couple of non-combat roles including scouting, the ability to break roots/snares and vanish, which allows them to begin an engagement in melee range, for which we depend on a stupid opponent or charge.

All we can do is be on the front line, so we have to do that well. There are people, descended from EverQuest, who want us to have pathetic damage output because our job is to tank only. Now, if we focus on tanking, our damage won't be great; not only will it be a single one-handed weapon, but we'll be more concerned with good armor and shields than with good weapons. However, we have the option to ignore tanking (still be decent at the sustaining damage part by virtue of heavy armor, but not be hooked up with proper abilities to keep aggro), and go an offense route. In this situation, we'll get better consistent damage than rogues, but not such massive hits as they are capable of (5-point eviscerate, and so on). In addition, they have the means to approach a foe unawares without hugely advantageous terrain and a stupid enemy... we have to be able to use Charge to really get in close without being blasted or our enemy being fully prepared for us. Rogues can strike without warning and do all sorts of nastiness.

That said, I'd still like to see Protection/Defensive Stance warriors become more viable in PvP, as the "damnit, why won't you die already" type of enemy. Some form of "defend my ally", as suggested by someone I don't remember and can't be bothered to find the post and check up on, ability would be good; I already would hate to be the warrior who gets disarmed in PvP.

So our role is whatever we want it to be, as long as we're on the front lines. And we can find a group that's ok with it.
