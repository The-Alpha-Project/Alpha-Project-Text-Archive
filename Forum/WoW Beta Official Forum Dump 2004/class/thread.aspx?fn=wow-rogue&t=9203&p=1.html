Q u o t e:

A dagger rogue kills faster and more reliably than a sword rogue if you're both using = dps weapons. This is mathimatically proveable and equates directly in game.


You keep saying this, and you are wrong. DPS = DPS no matter what. What IS mathematically provable (using statistics) is that the damage from the daggers will be less "spikey" (less variability in DPS over multiple fights). Misses are percent based (meaning, in a fight where you miss 10% of your hits with a mace, you will miss 10% of your hits with a dagger).

Example:
Dagger-> 20 DPS, 1.5s = 30 damage/hit avg
Sword-> 20 DPS, 2.5s = 50 damage/hit avg

Let's say the mob has 300 hp and you miss 10% of the time. Keeping it simple (not adding any damage bonuses, etc):

dagger: needs 10 hits to kill, so 11 swings (10% miss rate), 11 * 1.5s = 16.5 seconds

sword: needs 6 hits to kill, so 6-7 swings (10% miss rate means no misses or one miss, since 10% of 6 is 0.6, not a whole swing), (6->7) * 2.5 = 15->17.5 seconds.

Already you see the variability. If we could chart this with random numbers you would see the mace killing faster at times, and slower at times, but over the average they end up being the same. When a good DPS parser for WoW comes around we will be able to confirm this. 20 DPS = 20 DPS.

Now lets talk about a couple secondary factors:

1) Poisons. Obviously, this favors daggers.

2) Armor. High armor will hurt daggers much, much more since there is a component of the damage reduction that is linear (supposedly, nobody seems to know for sure). Assuming it IS linear, even partially, it is in your best interest to hit as hard as possible rather than a bunch of small hits, since you are losing X damage off the top of each hit.

3) Specials. Do I even need to go here? Specials hit on their own timer, independant of your weapon speed. Obviously favors big weapons.

So, which is better? It depends on what weapons you have and what talents use use. There are way too many variables to give a clear answer, at least at this point. I use both, but curently a sword since I have a really nice one for my level. Being able to respec based on what I have at the time is nice, but it will suck come retail.

High lvl warriors will forgo A LOT in DPS to get a high damage weapon. The warrior favorite, Mortal Strike, is an instant cast attack that does 220% weapon damage. That means it is a "free" hit when timed right. You'll get a lot more mileage out of 220% with an axe that can max out on 247 damage than a dagger that can max out on 82 damage. The advantage that warriors, in particular, get with 2h vs 1h weapons is ridiculous. Rogues, to a lesser degree, can see big returns by going slow sword + ss.


So, no, DPS is not the end all for weapons. Damage per hit and attack speed are very, very important.

Proc abilities like poisons favor high speed weapons. Instant attack abilities with % bonuses favor high damage weapons.
