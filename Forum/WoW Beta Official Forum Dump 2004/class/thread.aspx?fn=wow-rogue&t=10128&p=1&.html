Here's the deal with races. It doesn't matter when comparing stats. the only thing that matters is the look and any racial abilities that a race currently has. Why? 4 extra dex at level 20+ means nothing. Currently (unless blizzard changes this), all rogues will gain the same stats every level regardless of race, horde vs alliance or any other such thing. It's stupid that is what they chose to do.

Pick whatever you like more and prefer to play.

Swords vs daggers: that's a tough question. Daggers/backstab won't be useful until later in levels when you get some high damage daggers and also are able to spend 20+ talent pts on getting backstab crit up and worthwild.

I recommend using swords if you can until you are at a point where you will be grouping (which backstab is good for) and at a point where you can spend the talent pts if you DESIRE to ..on backstab abilities.

Poisons: you will need to complete a quest to get the poison skill. On the horde side..I have no clue wat this quest is. On the alliance side, you will need to have help to do this as it can't be done by a level 20 solo. Once you do obtain the skill however, you can put poisons on any weapon that YOU own. unfortunetly we cannot coat other people's weapons.

30-40 charges will usually last you 20-30 minutes. I do run out of charges before the time has expired though..most of the time. Depends on how much combat you are doing though.

I only use instant poison for the sole reason that the deadly poison (which is a dot that stacks), takes time #1, and #2 is it is a dot and that negates gouge if I choose to use it (which I use gouge all the time being a backstabbing rogue). Plus I almos talways perfer the instant damage rather then damage done over time. (ie ambush vs garrote)

My hope is that blizzard will end up doing a different racial stat chart for each race/class combo, so that at level 60, you have a pretty big variation in stats (ie a troll might end up with 50 more agility then undead, whereas an orc might have 50 more strength then the troll). Something to that effect...
