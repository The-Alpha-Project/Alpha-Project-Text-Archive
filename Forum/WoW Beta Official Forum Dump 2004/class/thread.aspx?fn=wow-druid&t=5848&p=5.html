Well like I said, on the support classes they hinge on res. Druids with a res and paladins w/o would kill paladins and remove all these complaints about druids being worthless in a group. Same for priests.

Therefore, druids need a way to heavilly compensate for res. I am strongly in favor of a res of their own, or more - redesign how all res skills work so they're all unique.

Priest res - Somewhat short to cast, considerable mana drain, and res sickness. Players come back with 20% HP.

Paladin res - Somewhat moderate mana drain, but a very long time in which they must channel their spell. Causes res sickness. Ressurects TWO players instead of just one. Players come back with 10% HP.

Druid res - Moderate length cast time and moderate mana drain. Vastly reduced length res sickness, and players come back with 20% HP.

This way, Priests would be most useful for post-battle resing, or resing characters in a fight quickly.

Paladins would be more useful for resing lots of people at a time, without much of a mana hit, but the extended cast time would dedicate them to the task. Paladins would be wanted in large groups such as raids mainly.

Druids would be average, much like everything else about druids, but with a MUCH shorter res sickness timer, allowing their patients to enter the fight again more quickly. Unfortunately they neither get a fast cast time nor low mana drain, so they are most limited when it comes to casting res multiple times.

Just to me they sound pretty balanced this way. Different types of battles would require different types of medics.
