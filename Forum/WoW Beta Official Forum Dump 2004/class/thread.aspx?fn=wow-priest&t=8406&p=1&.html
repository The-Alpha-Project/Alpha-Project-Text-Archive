 1. Item "crack" or lack thereof | 10/9/2004 6:39:25 PM GMTDT
Lately, I find little reason to play my priest. Setting aside the problems with the Holy tree we all know about, the main reason is the rareness, similarity, and uselessness/lack-of-coolness of caster loot.

When I do play my priest, usually to help a friend try to get an item, cloth drops seem way more rare than leather, mail, and plate. And on top of the rarity, once cloth finally drops it has to be shared with mages, priests, and warlocks, and since it takes a raid group of ten to do upper Blackrock Spire or Stratholme in a reasonable amount of time, there are usually a lot of other cloth wearers with which to compete. Other classes have to share drops too, but it isn't as big of a problem when it seems like every second drop in Blackrock Spire or Stratholme is plate.

Another huge problem is the similarity of caster drops between the various instances. Now this isn't necessarily a bad thing, since a cloth wearer can be reasonably equipped with an item or two from each high-end instance. On the other hand, why should a priest or other cloth wearer bother going to a particular high-end instance more than once?

After the initial thrill of discovery and fun of beating a new instance, there is no item 'crack' to keep a cloth wearer coming back since chances are any loot will be similar to an item from the Sunken Temple that is much faster and easier to get. For example, let's look at the leg slot and compare risk vs. reward for all the high end instances.

Kilt of the At'alai Prophet
115 Armor
+4 Strength
+9 Stamina
+18 Intelligence
+18 Spirit
req. level 50

Haunting Specter Leggings
119 Armor
+12 Intelligence
+28 Spirit
req. level 52

Wolfshear Leggings
127 Armor
+4 Agility
+11 Stamina
+20 Intelligence
+20 Spirit
req. level 56

The Postmaster's Trousers (part of a set, unsure what bonus it gets with a full set)
127 Armor
+12 Agility
+20 Spirit
+20 Intelligence
req. level 56

Spiritshroud Leggings
131 Armor
+31 Spirit
+10 Stamina
+6 Intelligence
req. level 58

These items are virtually identical, except for the amount of difficulty and time it takes to get the higher level ones, which are arguably worse than earlier ones. I could have done a similar comparison with just about any slot (compare Vestments of the At'alai Prophet vs. Robes of the Royal Crown), but the contrast is more stark with the leg slot. I also could have added the Rainstrider Leggings from the Hinterlands quest to kill the Prophet in the Temple, but I'm mainly concerned about instance drops.

All of these items give around 40 total combined intelligence and spirit. It wouldn't be quite so bad if one was 40 int, one was 40 spirit, one was 30 int and 10 spirit, one was 30 spirit and 10 int, and one was 20 int and 20 spirit, etc. That way, players could at at least choose what balance of stats they want to go for if the higher ones are not given more total int/spirit than the lower ones. As it is now, it doesn't really matter which legs a cloth wearer gets.

And finally, there is the way items are designed with "Equip: X" bonuses. For cloth classes, the +stat bonuses are the main benefit of new armor. For melee classes, the main benefit of new armor is the upgrade in armor. This hurts cloth classes since items with "Equip: X" effects generally have fewer stats. A tank will be happy with an item that has "Equip: +150 armor", and a rogue will be happy with "Equip: +2% chance to score a critical hit." An item with "Equip: Increases damage and healing done by magical spells and effects by up to X" is usually worse for a cloth wearer, because X is generally such a small number that it comes nowhere close to making up for the lost intelligence and spirit. To complete our example of the leg slot:

Skyshroud Leggings
125 Armor
+8 Stamina
+8 Intelligence
Equip: Increases damage and healing done by magical spells and effects by up to 17.
req. level 55

"Up to X" benefits spamming small spells (something the 5 second rule was supposed to discourage) more than big spells.
"Up to 17" is insignificant on a 2500+ hp greater heal. If I dumped all my mana on greater heals while wearing these, it would add up to the equivalent of an extra Flash Heal. I'd suggest changing caster items to "Up to +%" or change them to effects like "Equip: Decreases casting time by X" or "Equip: Decreases mana cost of all spells by X%."
