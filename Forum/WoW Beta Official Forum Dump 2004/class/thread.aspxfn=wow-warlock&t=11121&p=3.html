Since very vew people actually want to post USEFUL ways of using these talents, I thought I'd go against the flow and show people how you might actually apply these things in-game.

Demonic Embrace - To whoever said this is a pure PvE tree, you missed the very first skill listed. Decked out, this gives you an extra 15% stamina, which will give a substantial boost to your HP (at the expense of 5% of your spirit). I don't PvP much, but I do know that HP is king. Even PvE this has its uses because we can simply lifetap to make up for the spirit loss.

Improved Imp - Deck this out and combine it with the Destruction talent of reducing his cooldown to 1 second, and you have boosted the damage output of your imp to 2.5x what it is unenhanced. Plus your DS reflects back more damage, and you're giving your group more HP from Blood Pact, both of which will make you more popular in a group.

Improved Voidwalker - OK, how many of you were screaming for a more effective taunt on this guy pre-patch that are claiming that this tree sucks now that it's out. Enough said.

Improved Succubus - Props to the OP for posting this list, but the description on this talent is lacking. It boosts the duration of Seduction and Lesser Invisibility by 8%. It also increases the effectiveness of Soothing Kiss and Lash of Pain by 8% per point. That means, fully decked out, her LoP is doing 24% more damage. I can't run the numbers on it, but this may stack with Unholy Power for an additional 15% boost.

Fel Stamina - More HP for pet = more survivability for warlock.

Master Summoner - Nothing spectacular on its own, but use Fel Domination first and you take a pet from a 10 second cast time down to 0.5! If your pet dies unexpectedly, you can quickly get it back out and into combat and perhaps come out of the battle victorious (mostly useful for solo play).

Improved Enslave Demon - Again, how many people were crying pre-patch for a talent-based boost to Enslave Demon?

Unholy Power - Fully decked out, this is a 15% boost to the damage output of your VW, Succubus, or Fel Hunter. Yes, this is a nice talent, though it requires 30 points dedicated to this tree to max out.

Improved Spellstone - For the gung-ho people out there that want to keep dropping VW bombs.

Now, I do agree that some of these talents lack the luster that we had hoped for. Demonic Sacrifice seems flat out useless (remember though that if you have this you won't be using your pet as a mana sink), as does Master Conjurer. Soul Link doesn't seem bad on the surface, but again, it requires a ton of points to be able to use it. Improved Firestone could be useful for people that like to solo with a dagger, but on the whole it will be an overlooked talent.

Note that these talents DO mention the Fel Hunter on the in-game description, so we will very likely be getting this pet shortly. The Infernal isn't mentioned, but that doesn't mean we won't be getting it.

My whole point of this post is that if you look at each talent in this tree objectively, you'll see that it is not useless. In fact, there are some nice synergistic relationships that some of these talents have with other trees (i.e. Demonic Embrace + Improved Life Tap will turn your health bar into a mana battery). It's our job as beta testers to approach these additions with an open mind and not be so quick to jump on the "OMG this sux!" bandwagon. You'll see that this tree makes warlocks more powerful than they were before.
