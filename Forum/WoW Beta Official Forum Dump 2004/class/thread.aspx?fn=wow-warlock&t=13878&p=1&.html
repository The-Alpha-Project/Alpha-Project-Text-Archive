 5. Re: How many of you change your build? | 10/23/2004 6:33:44 PM GMTDT
So far, the only swapping around of it I've done is to see deeper down trees to see if they're interesting.

And so far, Affliction is the only thing that interests me. Yeah, I have the bonus Stamina demonology one, and the boosted Voidwalker, and everything else in Affliction.

Destruction is a real turn-off for me because I get to choose between junk and junk to get up to what I'd want (Shadowburn).

But to me, that's more because we have the three dullest talent trees out there, with mostly "take skill X, make it a little better" and that's it, so there's little reason to go anywhere but the most-developed one.

Affliction has most of the new, cool improvements. Channeling not being broken, instacast Corruption, Life Tap, a new (if weak and needing trainer upgrades) DOT with Siphon Life, and so on.

Destruction, while it does seem to cater more to the grouping warlock because of its emphasis on nuking and AOE spells, has a horrible layout, and doesn't add as much new functionality as Affliction does.

Demonology doesn't *change* anything. It makes our existing things better, faster, or more efficient, but since our pets (with the exception of Mr. Blue) are still needing redesign to make me want to use them, I don't care.

We don't really have any of the interesting broad-effect ones -- the closest we get is making our AOEs stun. Dull, and limited situational use. We have very few "make spell X do something new" abilities -- Nightfall and Improved Drain Mana.
