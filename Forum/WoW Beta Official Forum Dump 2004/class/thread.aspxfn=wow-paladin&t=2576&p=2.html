I don't know how many people just play the front end tank or how many people actually sit back and be the healer.

Right now, me and my guild mates are into leveling our secondary characters and when playing our secondary characters, we don't have anyone playing a priest. That tosses the roll of primary healer to me.

Yes, I am known for playing tanks, yes I am known for being in the fray kicking everythings butt that moves to next Wednesday, but I find playing the healer in these situations has given me a great deal of insight to the Paladin especially in the realm of mana conservation.

If used well, the seals actually conserve you a great deal of mana. Even after moderately long battles, I still tend to walk out of most battles with 1/2 to 3/4 of my mana bar if not more.

The seals actually give the Paladin a great deal more to play with when it comes to keeping your party members alive.

By and far, the most conservative way to heal is to keep aggro on one allies and chain heal him wiht a lower spell. In the Paladin trick box, you have a great deal of things you can do to sway where the battle is if you aren't in the middle of the fray. You have one spell that generates threat, one that reduces and one two that complete remove you off the hate list. I am finding this to be a serious advantage over the priest. Beyond that seal of recoking is almost just as good as Renew or Regrowth.

If I have to fight in a battle, I usually limit myself to Crusader Strike and Hammer of Justice. Only in a few situations do I pull out Holy Strike.

Playing like this, I have been able to gun as primary healer in Monastery and in Zul'Farrak so far. I am quiet interested to see how much further the Paladin can be pushed in this respect. Its almost at the point where I can do a better job then priests and both the ability to run away and the ability to stay and fight both make tide turning moves in many battles.
