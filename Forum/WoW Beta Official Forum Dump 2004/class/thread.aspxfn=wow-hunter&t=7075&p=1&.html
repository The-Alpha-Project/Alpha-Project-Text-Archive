 1. Suggestion to improve Raptor Strike | 10/7/2004 10:38:11 AM GMTDT
Many people agree that Raptor Strike is a pretty bland skill for the Hunter to use as of right now. It may or may not be a already in the works of being revamped or replaced, but I'd like to suggest some ideas that would make it fun and more useful.

One of the things I like best about Raptors are their dangerous hook-like talons, leaping large distances and slashing into their prey with agility grace, so to speak. Unfortunately, the raptors in WoW don't exercise this trait too much; most of their attacks with their talons are just hop-and-slashes at melee range. So here's the proposition: I suggest turning Raptor Strike into a 'ranged' melee attack, in which the Hunter leaps at his target with a jumping attack with his/her melee weapon, then hops back to minimum shooting range. Here are some reasons why I think this change would be welcomed:

1.) The Hunter is supposed to focus on ranged attacks. While it has an arsenal of ranged skills and special, it also has dull and underpowered melee skills that take away from the gameplay and/or flavor of the Hunter, and are often unemployed in serious combat. This change would give the Hunter something that no other class has: a damage-based melee attack that can be executed from a distance and still supports ranged attacks, which would allow the Hunter opportunities to use Raptor Strike where it counts.

2.) Warriors have Charge, which while not being a damage-based melee skill, is still a melee skill that can be executed from a distance, and compliments the Warrior by moving him swiftly into attacking range where he best excels in combat. For the Hunter, this changed Raptor Strike would allow him to include swift melee attacks in-between shots, while keeping him at the distance to still use ranged attacks, where he best excels.

3.) Escaping melee- range combat can be encumber some at times. While the Hunter can employ other skills to get rid of focus on him in times of combat, he still has to back-step eight yards/turn around, run eight yards, and turn around again before he can employ ranged attacks, making combat a hassle at times. In time where the Hunter can't scapegoat his pet to attract focus of his enemies (Ex: PVP combat), the only resort to getting to ranged combat is to Wingclip, run, turn and shoot one or two shots and Wingclip again once the effects of the previous wear off and the enemy's at melee range again. If Raptor Strike was changed as suggested, this would give the Hunter an easier and quicker way of escaping melee combat, if not a new alternative. The Hunter could use Wingclip followed by Raptor Strike, giving him an eight yard distance between his enemy with a speed penalty applied, allowing them to run further back and gather enough time for more ranged attacks to where he best excels.

4.) The Hunter is a survivalist and master of the wilds, learning and thriving from the beasts he hunts. Attacks with names suggesting that he emulates the behavior of beasts in the wild should do so to fully add flavor and a unique gameplay to the Hunter. Mongoose Bite (Although underpowered IMO) does a good job on following with this point, but Raptor Strike doesn't do so well. As of now, all Raptor Strike is a simple spinning attack. Raptors don't spin around before biting or clawing their enemies, making the name of the attack misleading and inappropriate.

I believe these reasons support a needed change for Raptor Strike so that it plays a more valuable and useful role in the Hunter's combat. I've read and I agree with others' stance on the Hunter's melee and lack of class design. I feel this improvement would be a good start on steering the Hunter into his own class. But more overall, I think it would also make playing a Hunter more fun and exciting. I believe players would enjoy watching their Hunters attack and employ maneuvers that employ the cunning and adaptation that Hunters are renowned for. On an ending note, I'd like to suggest a graphic improvement should Raptor Strike be changed this way. I've seen skills used by bears/bear-form Druids that uses an opaque bear model animating a claw attacks, adding flavor and excitement to the skill. Implementing an opaque raptor model along with the Hunter's movements in Raptor Strike would complement the skill and flavor of the class further more.
