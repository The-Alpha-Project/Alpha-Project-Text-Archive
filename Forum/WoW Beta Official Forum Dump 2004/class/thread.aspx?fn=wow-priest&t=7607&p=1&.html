 1. Shadow healing, a different priest dynamic | 10/5/2004 3:29:37 AM GMTDT
Warning: long

First off, WoW experience has been limited (stress, and now this phase). I'm mostly going off of discussions and comments with friends and by people on other messages boards, who are all power gamer types. So, please, any higher lvl priests who notice something wrong in my reasoning, let me know.

Anyways, what the general consensus seems to be, is that Shadow spec is for soloing and holy spec for grouping. Obviously, once your max level, your not going to be doing a whole lot of soloing anymore. So, all of the priests are holy speced (for the most part, various builds exist) who want to group alot.
I plan on doing the same, but I think it's a shame that the shadow tree is going to be effectively wasted. Especially because of how cool Vampiric embrace seems. I remember reading that talent and hoping to make a dark healer of sorts. Then, everyone I talked to informed me that it's effectively gimped for the higher level instances.

What I propose, is that Blizzard flesh out that playstyle more. Make vampiric embrace a little easier to get (tier 3/4 instead of 5), and/or add a bunch of other similarly styled spells to the priest's arsenal.

The only challenge, is making the spells worthwhile enough to be worth casting, and to keep them from being overpowered that no one goes Holy.

Some spells for reference.
At lvl 60, our last heal is Greater heal.
Mana: 960
Heals: 2396 to 2674
but, I believe Flash Heal is used more often
Mana: 380
Heals: 812-958

At lvl 60, one our nukes is Holy Smite
mana: 295
damage: 371-415
Let's also look at the dot.
SW:P
mana: 470
damage: 1136 over 24 seconds.

With those figures in mind, I tried to come up with some spells that fit into those figures. I think, there could be 2 kinds of single target shadow leech spells. One, which is more efficient, and does the damage up front, but the heal aspect is a dot.
For example:
Mana: 450
Damage: 400
Heals: 600 over 20 seconds

The other, which would be less mana efficient, would heal your targeted recipient right away, and have a quick cast.
Mana: 400
Damage: 400
Heals: 400

We could have a buff that we cast on someone. That person receives all the heal benefits of our leech spells. If we cast on multiple people, it's spread out among them then.

Another option is to keep vampiric embrace at it's current spot on the tree, and make it stack with all the shadow spells I'm suggesting That way, it'd do damage, heal, and do another 20% of the dmg in heal if vampiric embrace is up.

There'd need to be some balance issues worked out, of course. But, a smart priest could balance vampire spells and regular healing to still keep his party healed, and have more fun then just watching health bars. I don't want it to replace the standard holy heals, but just give us a different way to do the same role we're supposed to play.
Soemtimes, it might be better to add extra dps to kill some mobs quicker, which would save health. But, you still need to be healing somewhat.
Other times, the damage may come in too quick, and you gotta switch back to the holy heals style.
This would allow the druid/priest heal role to be balanced better too. Currently, seems like it's hard for blizzard to make druids a viable healer without making priests unneeded. But, with hw:S and the ability to add dps while still healing, it would make us a more versatile healer. A group could get by with a druid just playing straight healer, but they'd lose the extra dps of a priest.

Agree/disagree, comments?
