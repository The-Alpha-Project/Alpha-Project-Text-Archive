 1. 2h weapons vs 1h weapons. | 7/21/2004 1:39:25 AM GMTDT
At the moment, I find a huge damage disparity between two handed weapons and one handed weapons.


Surprisingly enough, the one handed weapons are generally higher with regards to damage output.

Why is this? The DPS equation used for weapons is [(Max + Min)/2]/Time. This is of course an average equation, which works great. What is not taken into consideration is the variance between the two styles of weapons.

A damage curve for a two handed weapon would appear to be much flatter as compared to the one handed weapon, while over time w/out holy strike, the weapons should be equal, but you'll have some REALLY bad fights and some REALLY good fights, where as with the one handed weapons, your fights should be centered around a pretty average fight every time. (Most of the damage population will be close around the midpoint.)

The major problem becomes noticable when Holy Strike is brought into play. Holy Strike is the ONLY ability we get that helps pull our DPS over that of a warrior (maybe - not sure on this). But to make Holy Strike useful, one must use it as soon as it pops. The problem is Holy Strike goes off on your weapon attack, not upon button press. So, with a slower weapon, lets say 4.0 delay (two handed) the minimum reuse time for Holy Strike is 10 seconds. The maximum reuse is 14 seconds (this assumes that you swung right before holy strike became available). That is a 40% delay between your optimum holy strike time. This is HUGE when one considers a long horizon.

A low delay weapon (lets say 2.0) on the other hand shares the minimum value of a 10 second refresh with a maximum of 12 second reuse. The two second difference between these weapons will be astonishing with respect to damage lost on holy strike.

Any how, as anyone can see, this really does reduce our effectiveness in battle and really reduces what numbers are crunched when Blizzard begins "class balancing".

 8. Re: 2h weapons vs 1h weapons. | 7/22/2004 11:30:04 PM GMTDT
Certainly, you lower your chances of being in a "bad luck" melee fight by swinging a faster weapon, because the odds of missing 3 times in 6 seconds is less than missing 2 times in 6 seconds. But conversely, your odds of being in a "good luck" melee fight are also lower, because the odds of critting 3 times in 6 seconds is less than the odds of critting 2 times in 6 seconds.

It's very simple math. In the long run it balances out, but you'll tend to see a wider range of results with a slower weapon because you're getting fewer swings.

Here's an example. The numbers are completely made up, but I'm using them to illustrate a point. Let's take 2 weapons, both doing 10dps. 1 swings every 2 seconds, 1 swings every 3 seconds. The faster one averages 20 per hit, and the slower one averages 30. Let's also say that you crit 10% of the time and miss 10% of the time. In a 60 second battle you'd get:

fast weapon: 30 swings. 3 crits, 3 misses, 24 hits. Damage total of 120 (3 * 2 * 20) + 0 + 480 (24 * 20) = 600 total damage.

slow weapon: 20 swings. 2 crits, 2 misses, 16 hits. Damage total of 120 (2 * 2 * 30) + 0 + 480 (16 * 30) = 600 total damage.

Again, I stress that this is a made up example. But I did it to show this point - the math is the same. Speed doesn't matter in general. In specific cases (guy has 18 HP after 60 seconds, so you kill in 62 with the fast or 63 with the slow; guy has 22 HP after 60 seconds, so you kill in 64 with the fast or 63 with the slow) you can make the numbers do whatever you want.

Bottom line - DPS is a good gauge of the long term average damge that a weapon will do. Most 2 handers of equivalent level will outdamage 1 handers because they have higher DPS. But you lose a shield.

Another example: let's say you have two weapons of identical DPS - one a 1-hander, the other a 2-hander. The 1-hander has a speed of 1.7, and the 2-hander has a speed of 3.2. This is pretty close to worst case for Holy Strike - the 1-hander can HS again at 10.8, but the 2-hander has to wait until 12.8. Over 120 seconds, the 1-hander can use HS 11 times and the 2-hander can use it 10 times (again, worst case for the 2-hander). At level 32, that's an average of 100 more damage.
