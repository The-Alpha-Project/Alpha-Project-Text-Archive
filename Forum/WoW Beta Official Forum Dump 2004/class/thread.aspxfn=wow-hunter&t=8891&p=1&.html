 1. Some ideas to make the hunter more enjoyable | 10/15/2004 3:37:41 PM GMTDT
The fundamental problems with the hunter (from what I have seen) are the low DPS, severe PvP weakness, ridiculously low pet damage, the inability to track, the inability to use stealth while stalking prey. I’ve attempted to address some of these concerns (as I have read). Blizzard could correct the DPS problem by simply creating arrows with elemental effects on them but this would still do nothing for the hunters ranged uniqueness as anyone could use arrows. Additionally, the pet needs to be more of a fundamental threat to anyone who chooses to attack a hunter (PvP). That is why I suggested a grapple attack for the pet, making is so no damage is dealt but the opponent is put in great danger by being immobilized. As a primarily ranged fighter, it shouldn’t be unreasonable for the hunter to be able to improve the range of his attacks (possibly through talents). Most hunter spells/abilities I have not changed, but the ones I have either changed or added I have tried to explain as clearly as possible. Please don’t flame as these are merely suggestions for both Blizzards and our benefit, but I imagine constructive comments are helpful to blizzard…that being said I do like the “sneak peak” talents that blizzard posted earlier.


PET ABILITIES
Call Pet: (unchanged)
Beast Taming: (unchanged)
Dismiss Pet: (unchanged)
Revive Pet: (unchanged)
Mend Pet: (unchanged)

Equip Pet: (new) allows you to equip barding (armour), saddle bags, and special weapons to your pet (metal claws, spiked plates, etc.), making them a formidable fighting ally

Tame mount: (new) this ability allows the hunter to track and tame a beast that would be suitable as a mount.

Grapple opponent: (new) when the hunter is in melee with an opponent he can command his pet to grapple the opponent allowing him to back away or continue to attack. The grapple lasts X seconds before the opponent breaks free – an ability like this is needed to correct the hunters innate PvP weakness, preventing people from simply ignoring the pet and attacking the hunter


ASPECTS
Aspect of the cobra: (new) the hunter takes on the aspect of the cobra, increasing his attack rate (melee/ranged) by X%. This is recommended when the hunter finds himself in an overpowering situation and needs to strike fast and furious. The effects stack with other rate enhancements (i.e. quivers)

Aspect of the Cheetah: (modified) The Hunter takes on the aspects of a cheetah, increasing movement speed. This can be used to travel or to back away from monsters so you can use your ranged weapon. – currently the stun and extra damage taken make this a useless option in combat…mobs always seem to be able to catch up to me even if I have this active with a healthy lead.

Aspect of the Pack: (modified) (group aspect) the hunter is able to confer on members of his group the aspect of the pack. Through the use of commands and precise co-ordination the group then becomes a far more effective killing machine. All members of the group have their attack damage (range/melee/spell), stamina and spirit increased by X%. The group essentially becomes one large opponent due to their coordinated attacks. (again, this improves a hunters usefulness in groups dramatically)

Aspect of the wolf: (new) the hunter is able to take on the aspect of the wolf allowing him to stalk prey unseen but reduces his movement speed to 60% of normal. Attacking or generally provoking an enemy cancels the aspect. (I don’t think the hunter should be able to confer this ability on group members but if it was, it would make things reeeeeally interesting…imagine a group of 5 stalking prey like a pack of wolves. Perhaps if it was used in a group setting there would be a chance that it would fail exposing one or more of the members)

Aspect of the Beast: (unchanged)
Aspect of the Monkey: (unchanged) – (should be improved with talents to make mongoose bite useful and make the hunter more valuable in groups when he can confer aspects on other group members – warriors/pally’s/rogues would love us)

BEAST SPELLS
Scare Beast: (unchanged)
Eagle Eye: (unchanged, but must be fixed as it rarely works…we always get the “no line of sight” message so I personally never use it and it should be useable indoors. I tried to cast it while standing on a balcony and I got the “does not work indoors” message)
Beast Lore: (unchanged)

Beast stalker: (new) this spell allows the hunter to use his senses and keen insight to track hostile beasts. When activated, beasts show up on the hunter’s mini map allowing him to plan his attack and stalk his prey.

Beast meld: (new) this spell gives the hunter the chance to learn a beast’s special/unique attack (similar to the beast taming ability). The hunters armour is reduced by 100% for the 20 seconds it takes to learn its ability. The hunter can only learn one attack at a time and the ability will remain with the hunter for 60 min (with a refresh of 30 seconds).


MELEE SPELLS
Dual-Wield (passive): (unchanged)
Raptor Strike: (unchanged but more damage would be nice…the extra damage at the highest levels is fairly small)
Wing Clip: (unchanged)
Mongoose Bite: (unchanged but should have an improved chance by improving aspect of the monkey with talents)

Ranged Combat
Multi shot: (modified) Fires multiple missiles all at once hitting a given target with three arrows or bullets. (Improved with talents. i.e. 4 arrows, 5 arrows…)

Head shot: (new) While under the aspect of the wolf (stealth) the hunter is able to take his time in stalking his prey (aim) and make a devastating shot to the head of an opponent killing it instantly. Head shot has a 10 sec “cast” rate and a 60 min refresh time. The percentage chance is 15% plus or minus the difference in level’s between the target and the hunter (up to a max of +/- 10%). For example a level 40 hunter stalking a level 50 opponent would have a 5% chance to kill an opponent (15%-10% = 5%), where as a level 40 hunter stalking a level 33 opponent would have a 22% chance (15% + 7% = 22%). A failed shot misses the target and generates a large amount of agro. (There’s risk and reward and it would be a valuable ability to a group looking to fight a horribly powerful boss or if a group member (mage/priest) was in trouble being attacked by a mob)

Imbue arrow – frost: (new) The hunter is able to magically imbue arrows with cold dealing an additional X damage and slowing the targets movement by Y% for Z seconds

Imbue arrow – fire: (new) The hunter is able to magically imbue arrows with fire dealing an additional X damage and burning the target for an additional Y damage for Z seconds.

Imbue arrow – disease: (new) The hunter is able to magically imbue arrows with a debilitating disease dealing an additional X damage and damaging the target for an additional Y damage for Z seconds. Additional beasts that come within 2 yards of the afflicted beast catch the disease as well possibly spreading the disease even farther.

Imbue arrow – lightning: (new) The hunter is able to magically imbue arrows with electrical energy dealing an additional X damage. The lightning will jump to any other nearby beasts (within 5 yards) and deal 50% of the initial damage to them.

Auto Shot: (unchanged)
Serpent Sting: (unchanged)
Viper Sting: (unchanged)
Arcane Shot: (unchanged)
Scorpid Sting: (unchanged)
Volley: (unchanged)
Hunter's Mark: (unchanged)

TRAPS
Immolation Trap: (unchanged)

Frost Trap: (unchanged – even though is has extremely limited usefulness since damage breaks the trap. I’ve used it once, and that was just to see how it worked. Personally I think it needs improvement, perhaps it should deal damage or be similar to a mages frost nova and keep an enemy or several enemies in an area immobilized for X seconds)

Explosive Trap: (unchanged)


OTHER
Throw net: (new) The hunter magically conjures a net of vines and thorns that he then throws at a given target, immobilizing it for X seconds and dealing Y damage from the thorns over the duration of the net.

Alpha leader (passive): (new) The hunter takes on the persona of an alpha leader allowing him to lead and guide members of his GROUP through subtle body language and hand signals. This allows the hunter to cast any of his known aspects on other GROUP members. If an individual leaves the group they loose the benefit of the aspects.

Disengage: (unchanged)
Feign Death: (unchanged)
Flare: (unchanged)
Rapid Fire: (unchanged)
Mail: - The ability to wear mail armour should be lowered to at least level 30. The hunter has to spend a large amount of money on pets, arrows, food, and equipment. Lowering the mail requirement to 30 would give the hunter the “breathing room” to spend slightly less on his armour (leather) just to throw it out at a certain level when mail is available.


TALENTS – Several of the above spells/skills/abilities are very open to improvement from talents. For example, the three trees could be:

Pet talents: blood frenzy (increase the pets attack and defense), matriarch (allows the hunter to call multiple pets at a time), Special attacks, undying loyalty (when the hunter is physically attacked by a mob the pet enters a berserk state to protect his master by increasing the frequency of its learned traits/abilities like growl/bite), etc.

Ranged combat: Improved fire damage, improved cold damage, improved lightning damage, improved disease damage, Recover projectiles (allows the hunter to recover X% of projectiles imbedded in a corpse he killed), increased ranged attacks, etc.

Beast talents: empathize with pet (the hunter experiences a boost in stats/damage/armour/speed – whatever is appropriate for the pet he has) while his pet is active. This makes the pet more of a companion than simply a meat shield and the pets death will negatively affect the hunter with some sort of decrease to a stat/armour etc., improved stalking, improved aspects, Increase the number of active aspects (this would allow a hunter the ability to have multiple aspects active, and at higher levels when he has alpha leader, confer multiple bonuses on group members), butcher beast (recover a small amount of food from a slain beast for his pet)

Jaerogoth, while I do agree with some of the abilities you have suggested, such as pet equipment, and perhaps pet grappling (if dependant on pet str/size and opponent's str/size), several of them not only step on the toes of other classes but thoroughly grind the heel of the proverbial hunter's boot into them. I can only assume that you haven't played too many classes beyond the hunter, which is fine, but it does mean that you need some to obtain some outside perspective from players of other classes, and here is some.

The following are the suggestions that I have noticed have some serious problems, the reasons why, and what I think could be done to make it feasible:

Head shot: This ability is simply overpowered, the only way it could ever even appear to be balanced is if you were stunned for like 10 seconds after you attempted the shot, regardless of hit or miss, otherwise there is no real risk involved because you can simply hit aspect of the cheetah and run away from your target only to come back and try it again later. Abilities that can kill independantly of HP, armor, resists, and stats in general simply cannot be balanced to everyone's liking in an mmo setting, in layman's terms, it pisses off everyone who can't instakill.

Aspect of the wolf: Were this ability to be implemented it would effectively amount to an open-handed slap in the face to rogues everywhere followed shortly by a swiftbackhand. Not only would rogues' class-defining ability no longer be unique to them, but hunters would be able to dole it out to anyone in their group. Here is an ability I have been thinking about for hunters that would be much less offensive and coincide much better with the hunter ethos:

Stalking: Hunter activates stalking on his target, the target shows up on the hunter's minimap and the hunter gains stealth versus the target in a similar manner to rogue's lvl 1 stealth (60% move speed, easily detected by higher level enemies, and more easily detected the closer the hunter gets to his target, any attacks made by the hunter or on the hunter remove the effect as well as any casting done by the hunter). However, the stealth is only versus that target, meaning that you are not stealth from any other players or mobs, also, if the hunter is within the line of sight of the target an icon is placed on the target's buff bar called "stalked" so that players being stalked know that they are being stalked if the hunter is not careful to stay out of the player's LoS through terrain usage or maintaining a position behind the target, even if they do not actually detect the hunter's exact location.

Alpha leader: This is nearly as much of an insult to paladins as Aspect of the Wolf would be to rogues, paladins are the group benefit/leader class of WoW, not to mention they are already pretty miffed that hunters are getting talents before paladins, maybe if it allowed them to place an aspect on one other party member instead of the hunter it would be acceptable.

Throw net: This ability would step directly into druid territory, copying one of their most notable abilities (Entangling Roots). I could see this as a reagent-based "oh crap" instacast spell, 10-15 second duration, possibly randomized duration, 5 min cooldown, but I could never see it as Mana-only or low cooldown, also the damage would have to be negligible just like Entangling roots.
