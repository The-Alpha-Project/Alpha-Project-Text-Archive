 1. 2 Skills to easily balance the paladin | 7/27/2004 3:31:23 PM GMTDT
I was thinking about the disadvantages vs advantages of this class. Paladins are not pure tanks and therefore an emphasis on damage is not the realm of a paladin. Sure the "one hit wonder" can fit, but that should be the limit in terms of damage. The paladin's main role is usually that of group function in almost every game. The main weaknesses of the class prevail as well in these games. One being the inability to dish damage the other is being constantly kited by casters. So here are 3 unique skills that I feel may help the paladin without reaching beyond the role of a paladin.

1. Knockback:
It's coded in the game already so it's not hard to create. This would balance the paladin vs warrior class. Knockback would be nice to have to give the paladin enough time to heal himself and maybe cause a little fall damage. Put it on a 60 second timer so it'll be available in every other fight.

2. Opposite of Knockback:
Give the paladin the ability to literally pull opponents to him. This works against casters in pvp and angry mobs in pve. Allow it to merely interrupt a cast and cuse no damage. Give it the same range as a casters spell with a similar timer to knockback. The paladin would at this time be an increadibly fun class to play.

3. Lance: (overkill?)
Since paladins cannot equipe ranged items, it would be nice if we can put something in that slot. How about a lance. The only time we could use this lance? When mounted. Allow the paladin to be a true knight. Allow a single powerful hit while mounted. Once the attack is made the paladin must continue the battle on foot. What better skill would there be than to be able to actually charge into battle on what is aptly called a charger?
