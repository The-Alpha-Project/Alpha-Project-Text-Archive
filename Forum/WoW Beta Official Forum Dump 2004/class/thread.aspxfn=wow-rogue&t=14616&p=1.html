Too many Stabs. If you mean me, I'm going with a backstab build lately. I got the shanker from BRD and I finally have a dagger I like.

Combat Talents (8 points)

Improved Gouge - 3/3 points
Improved Sinister Strike - 2/2 points
Improved Backstab - 3/3 points

Assassination Talents (31 points)

Malice - 5/5 points
Improved Eviscerate - 3/3 points
Ruthlessness - 3/3 points
Relentless Strikes - 1/1 point
Lethality - 5/5 points
Improved Kidney Shot - 2/3 points
Improved Instant Poisons - 5/5 points
Cold Blood - 1/1 point
Seal Fate - 5/5 points
Vigor - 1/1 point

Subtlety Talents (12 points)

Master of Deception - 1/5 points
Camouflage - 4/5 points
Opportunity - 5/5 points
Improved Ambush - 2/3 points

Even though I put a couple of points into ambush, I open with cheapshot unless it's a cloth wearer.

During the initial stun I get one backstab in then wait. Against players I will kidney shot, trying to time it to land as the stun wears off. Against mobs I will just wait and gouge, backstab, and the kidney shot with five CPs. With that long of a kidney shot stun I can usually get in two backstabs. Normal mobs are likely dead by now, if not sooner.

I put a couple of points into improved kidney shot to lower the cooldown from 20 to 16 seconds. It seems to really help in longer fights.

I like the instant poison because at 30% it lands quite a bit and does good damage.

I went with camo over MoD because the speed at which you move also has an impact on your stealth - I think faster stealth results in fewer stealth detection checks. Also, against players it isn't always not being seen that's important. The key is to get your opener in before they do something to remove your stealth. Moving faster helps that.

The other change I made recently is to shelve my offhand sword, which was painful. It's an assassination blade with nice base damage, something like 65-130, and gives me an extra 1% chance to crit. Problem is that it's a bit slow and it doesn't land poison very often. I went back and stuck a widowmaker in my offhand and now it lands the poison a lot more. I put snare poison on my offhand. It's fun when mobs and players try to run from me when they're snared. I get some free backstabs in.

Anyway, I've tried a lot of different builds but I'm sold on a backstab build now, as long as I have a good dagger. The shanker has great base damage, 51-95, and that makes a big difference with backstab and ambush.

Well, it really depends on what you plan on doing. If you're going to be doing lots of solo PvP which is kind of spread out, then probably you will want Preparation.

However, for higher-end instance play, I would say that being able to reset your cooldowns every 10 minute is far less useful than an extra combo point 40% of the time when you backstab. Realistically, the only cooldown you are going to reset most of the time is Cold Blood. So...Cold Blood Ambush, get some CPs...Cold Blood Eviscerate? This is great for PvP, but not very useful when you are fighting packs of elite mobs.

I think your sustained DPS would be higher with Seal Fate. Also, it is just that much easier to get the 5 CPs to bust out a full Expose Armor or Kidney Shot, which again are very useful in instance play.
