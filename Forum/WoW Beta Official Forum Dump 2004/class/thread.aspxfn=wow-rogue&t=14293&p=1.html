 5. Re: Am I missing anything? (build) | 10/19/2004 9:44:46 PM GMTDT
If you're using daggers, BS is definitely the way to go. My average non-crit damage with a SS is probably around 80ish, my average non-crit damage with BS is about 120. If you throw in a 30% crit chance bonus to BS, it becomes far more attractive.

Also, the energy cost is only a difference of 15 if you do not get Imp SS (which, by the way, I would recommend picking up). Even with the talent, it's still a 20 energy difference, so base non-crit damage output per energy is still about the same.

Of course, BS isn't the end-all be-all, since you have to be behind a target. For that reason I would still suggest investing in Imp. SS, as well. But, at least for instance play, any warrior worth their salt is taunting the heck out of the creatures, so you will almost always be able to backstab the suckers.

 9. Re: Am I missing anything? (build) | 10/20/2004 10:51:10 AM GMTDT
in PvE, i agree you use SS all the time. In PvP noone is going to let you stand there and SS them to death (only exception would be a warrior or paladin -- in which case you are likely to get owned anyway). Its also relatively easy against most people to jump and dance around to hit a backstab here and there. In addition, you rarely get a one-on-one battle. Its either one on many when you have the initiative and they are fighting mobs or many on many. Many on many allows plenty opportunities for backstabs.

Here is one of my combos i use when catching a caster preoccupied (talking to a quest guy or engaged in action)

Ambush
Backstab (people don't turn around nearly as fast as mobs)
Need some energy regen
Backstab again (you'd be suprrised... also sometimes people just start running when they realize they lost > 60% of thier hitpoints).

At this point you have 3 or 4 combo points (if you got initiative). Eviscerate should kill most casters by now. Once i get premeditation and more assasination talents, i hope to open with premed -> ambush or cheapshot -> backstab if necessary, eviscerate or kidney (5 point) -> vanish and preparation if necessary and repeat.

If they turn around, gauge, backstab. Only thing you have to worry about agianst casters is insta fear and CC.

BTW, Take whatever I said with a grain of salt. I'm only a lvl 38 rogue right now with the associated hillsbrad / stranglethorn daily PvP fights.
