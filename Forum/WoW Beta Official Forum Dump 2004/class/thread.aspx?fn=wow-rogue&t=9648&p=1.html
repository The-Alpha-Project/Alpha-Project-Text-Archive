 12. Re: Mages with +dmg, Rogues without +crit% | 9/28/2004 2:58:42 AM GMTDT
There's a HUGE difference with a crit and +damage.

Look at the numbers a second. A crit does double damage. Crit an ambush with the right talents and you get 1200 damage.

Now, mages DO have good spells, and they do alot of damage...but the difference is you add 11 or even 30 damage to a spell and you're adding 1% damage to every spell they cast. Stack those up to 150 damage bonus (I don't think anyone can obtain this, but just say) and you're still only looking at about a 10 - 20% bonus to damage on spells.

Crits are much difference, if you crit it's +100% damage automatically. To further complicate things we do different damage with different abilities...if you crit a dagger strike you might gain 80 damage, woo hoo. But crit a 5 point eviscerate or an ambush with good talent chioce and you're easily adding 600 damage.

Rogue crit percentage is already running just under 1/4 chance (25%). Adding 12% to that means instead of 25 out of 100 strikes doing 200% damage, you're now looking at 37 of 100...that's very very significant, and no where NEAR what a mage could reach even if EVERY piece of his or her equipment was + damage.

Personally i'd like to see a BUNCH of +attack and +damage and haste equipment added for rogues, not more +crit. Balancing +crit is HORRIBLE...it's like making a class that relies upon DODGE to survive...one battle you'll kill the mob you're after and the next you'll die horribly while the mob is still at 80% health...same mob, same combat, different rolls.
