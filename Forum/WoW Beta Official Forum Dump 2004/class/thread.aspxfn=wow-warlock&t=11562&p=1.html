Caydiem,

I think you'd do better to raise "issues" rather than questions. There's a lot of concerns we have that we'd like to hear dev responses on.

Six months after starting a warlock, I have very little idea where Blizzard is trying to go with it. Our talent trees are rather scrambled, with some skills useful but too expensive in terms of talent points, some of them just plain too weak to be useful, some of them moderately useful (buffs to Health Funnel, buffs to Firestone), and a few fabulous, stand-out talents like Fel Concentration or Improved Corruption.

The problem with talents like "+15% to Spellstone damage absorption" is that assuming I specialize in Demonology, I'm still Lvl 40 before I can open the talent, Lvl 42 before I can max it.

At Level 42, even a 30% boost to spellstone absorbtion is a boost from 400 to 520. That's less than ONE spell's worth of damage--and it cost me ALL my talent points to date (and 64% of all the talent points I will ever receive) to unlock.

Dark Pact has the same problem. An entire tree of investment, and I can get 250 mana back? Whoop-de-doo. I'd rather drink a potion and get triple the mana. For that kind of specialization, I expect to be able to yank my Pet's entire mana POOL, first point invested.

I dont' mean to sound whiney or negative, I think we have some GREAT talents--but we also have some that make no sense, or that make no sense in the position they are in. So my question for the devs would be, when will these mistakes be fixed or addressed?
