 2. Re: Keeping Aggro? | 10/21/2004 8:02:07 PM GMTDT
In multiple mob situations, as long as your party is only attacking the same target as you, you should have problem retaining aggro. A single demoralizing shout or thunder clap will turn any untouched mobs attention towards you. As long as no party members then attack them until you do, they will stay aggro'd to you.

To keep the primary target on you you have two options. The offensive route assumes you deal notable damage. If you can, simply keep whacking him. You can throw a hamstring on him just in case he turns to one of your party members, giving you an extra second to put a mocking blow on him or commence taunting. The other route, if you dont do real damage is to stay in defensive stance and taunt him. If you lose aggro, taunt more. Use bloodrage and inner rage to build rage when you have none, although it shouldnt be a problem keeping some with multiple mobs hitting you.

If area damage is being dealt, the problem gets more complex. Throwing out a few demoralizings and thunder claps will work for a bit. Beyond that, you have to prioritize targets. Say if you have 3 mobs, 2 of which do real damage and 1 is just annoying, keep aggro on the first two using attacks and taunt and let the third annoy your friends for a while. If you have 3 damage dealing mobs, stay purely defensive, cycle through all targets keeping them all taunted and throw in an area challenge for good measure. If one of them turns away, toss some taunts on him or a mocking blow.

Dont forget when using mocking blow that if you cant kill him in 8 seconds, the effect will wear off shortly. Use those 8 seconds to inner rage and throw some taunts on him so that you retain aggro when the mocking effect falls. Oh, and dont forget that if things get ugly, intimidating shout is always a great failsafe.

 4. Re: Keeping Aggro? | 10/21/2004 8:18:23 PM GMTDT
Again it depends on what your party is doing. If you can do as much or more damage than either of your party mates (not both), then your melee alone should be able to retain the aggro. If theyre drawing aggro from mobs other than the primary target, they shouldnt be dealing AE damage without expecting to be hit.

Talents start to come into play beyond this, such as the protection tree's defiance which increases your natural aggro output while in defensive stance.

Personally I have the arms tree's tactical mastery maxed, so when something runs away I can switch from battle stance to defensive stance, throw out 5 taunts instantly with the 25 rage that carried over, and switch back to battle stance.

It depends on how your character plays and how your party operates. Intelligent mages and priests will learn how to gauge how much aggro youre generating and play accordingly to keep their aggro low. For instance if a priest in your party is healing like mad, hes going to get smacked around constantly. If he heals only by necessity and you counteract his aggro, everything will go well.

One trick for managing targets so that your party doesnt go swinging at random mobs is to teach them "F1-F". If you're the party leader, and they hit F1 followed by F, they will acquire your target as their own. This prevents people from throwing damage on unaggro'd mobs.

Aggro management really grows into more and more complex situations as the game goes on and it depends on the whole party, not just the warrior. The warrior however is the cornerstone and the failsafe of any aggro management structure.

 5. Re: Keeping Aggro? | 10/21/2004 8:37:01 PM GMTDT
Without reading any of the response (see if they match up with what i'm about to say...) Here goes my assessment of keeping Aggro...

First off, It is a TEAM effort in keeping the aggro on the Warrior and/or the Tank.

To many player will attack at the same time as the warrior. In doing so, this does not give the warrior time to get a head start on the aggro. Let there be a lag between the warrior intial attack and the group attack. This will let the warrior gain the majority of the aggro and easier for him to keep farther into the battle.

The mages have very hard hitting spells that will pull the mob off the tank if the tank has not built up enough aggro.

There should be at least a 5 sec difference between the tank attack and the group attack.

Also when the group is attacking the mob be sure and attack the same monster that the tank is attacking, sure some times you will have to take out the ocasianal mage/hunter mob that the tank is not next to first.

When multiple targets are involved, the Tank needs to use his AOE spells every so often to keep the mob focused on him, since the Healing spell do generate aggro too. Tank needs to Taunt basically every other attack to ensure the mob stays on him.


Also, if another person does pull off a monster by accident, that person needs to NOT attack that mob (which would generate more aggro) and the Tank needs to pull the monster off of him with Taunt or what every he is able to use. This will take about 1-3 rounds to pull the mob off of him, depending on what is being used...Taunt will generally pull him off right away, if no taunt then you are looking at about 3 rounds to pull him off, if the person is not attacking the mob.
