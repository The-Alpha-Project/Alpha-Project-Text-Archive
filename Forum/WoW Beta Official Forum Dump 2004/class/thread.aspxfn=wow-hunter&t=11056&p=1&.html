 6. Re: Please allow Hunters to Tame Mount | 10/27/2004 4:25:55 PM GMTDT
Q u o t e:
I wish you people would quit posting that 'getting 100 gold is nothing', and tell the rest of us players HOW TO DO IT!!


1. Some abilities are worthless. Find out which ones they are beforehand, and don't buy them.

2. You don't absolutely have to be using the latest greatest ammo. Jagged Arrows in particular aren't really worth over 3 times the cost of Razor Arrows. Wait until you've got plenty of money before you start buying them.

3. If feeding your pet is making a significant dent in your savings, you're doing something wrong. Either your pet is dying too much or you need a different pet.

4. As a Hunter, you can solo 99% of all outdoor quests. Take advantage of that.

5. Make sure you're leveling your trade skills on efficient items. Armor kits usually work well for leatherworking, since they have a low resource cost and you can use them.

6. Take a moment to learn what mob-dropped items are worth selling at the AH at what you should just vendor. Stacked trade items like cloth usually do pretty well at the AH.

7. As a final method of gaining money once you've reached level 40 or so, pick a plentiful monster that drops a high amount of thick leather and farm it. Turn the leather into Nightscape Tunics (requires Leatherworking of 205), which sell to a vendor for 56 silver, costing only 7 silver to make. Several hours of this should net you enough money for your mount.

8. Also, don't use really fast bows/guns.

 7. Re: Please allow Hunters to Tame Mount | 10/27/2004 7:27:15 PM GMTDT
Farm lower lev humanoids. Quick kills, good solo exp, they carry $$ and drop cloth.

Stacks of tailoring material sell disproportionally well at the AH due to low cloth armor drops and the high # of cloth using PCs, and the necessity of lower level tailoring components for higher lev tailoring recipes, which are heavily used due to the lack of and demand for cloth armor drops...

In other words, tailoring PC's would rather buy linen, for example, in the AH at overblown prices [at least overblown to those low lev PC's farming and AH'ing linen] than go farm some lowbie area for materials. So stacks of silk, even wool, sell for buyout prices of 60s, 70s, etc. Mageweave, 1g. If you can farm runeweave but don't have a mount, I pity you. :P

The fact that those same tailoring material drops are used for First Aid helps keep demand even higher.

This will probably be fixed some come retail as it's economically skewed, but due to the number of core issues that cause this, AH'ing tailoring supplies will probably still be one of the best money generating schemes.
