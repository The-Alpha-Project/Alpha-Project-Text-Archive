 1. An Idea For Seals | 7/28/2004 9:40:59 PM GMTDT
1.) Seals are permanent buffs (Exception: Invluns.)
2.) Any seal may be activated at a time, but the same seal may not be activated twice. (Example: 2 warriors are in a group, but only 1 may have fury, assuming both want it.)
3.) Seals have no refresh time (Exception: Invluns.)
4.) Give these seals an aura level/slightly higher effect.

What this does: This allows us to have specialized buffs on certain classes in our group without having to worry about constantly reapplying them. This methood allows the same seal use as it currently does, only 1 may be active at a time (Ex. You can't have the same seal on multiple members.) This also avoids the death of a party member making a long recast time on the seal a huge negative factor (when looking at the increase in time of seals to an increase in recast time.)

Drawbacks: Makes the class a little easier to play, trivializing the attention needed to operate at maximum capacity a la Cosmos and autocasting.

Advantages: Gives the paladin the ability to do other things in a group than being a full time buffer while everyone else is having a good time fighting/healing.

A second idea would be to keep seals their same power, or slightly more powerful, and allow the paladin to have only 1 seal active at any time. Meaning only 1 person may be buffed.

Disadvantages: Paladin could easily become the most efficient solo class in the game. (Unless the seal counts as a mana decreaser over time - slowing the regen rate of mana.)

Advantages: Once again, ease of use would be there, but that is one of the few I see, other than just the cool factor of having a powerful ability to bestow on others.


I'm partial to just the idea, since we can benefit everyone instead of just one, making us more desired in a group.

Comments?

 18. Re: An Idea For Seals | 7/31/2004 4:31:54 AM GMTDT
Wow, this is a really cool idea. Probably just a restatement of what has been discussed, but here's how I'd like to see this idea:

-Paladins can place seals only on themselves or on current group mates. Players who leave the group lose any seals they have received.

-A seal costs a specific amount of mana to cast and lasts for 30 minutes. This 30 minute duration does not include Seal of Protection, which still lasts just a few seconds.

-A paladin can only maintain one seal per target. Casting a second seal on a target overwrites the previous seal. (e.g. A paladin casts Seal of Fury on a player. If the paladin casts Seal of Sacrifice on that player before Fury expires, Sacrifice replaces Fury and resets the 30 mintue timer.)

-A paladin can only maintain a given seal on one target at a time. Recasting the seal on a different target removes it from the first target. (e.g. A paladin casts Seal of Righteousness on a Rogue party member. Before it has expired from the Rogue, the paladin casts Seal of Righteousness on a Priest party member. Righteousness is imemdiately cancelled from the Rogue and placed on the Priest.)

-As with auras, players can benefit from multiple seals (in the case of multiple paladin parties), but the effects of identical seals do not stack. The casting of an identical seal on a target player overwrites the original seal. (e.g. A paladin casts Seal of Reckoning on a player. A second paladin casts Seal of Reckoning on the same player several minutes later, replacing the original Reckoning with the new one and resetting the 30 minute timer.)

In summary, a paladin in a group with four other non-paladins can cast as many as five different seals, one per party member, each lasting thirty minutes. Groups with multiple paladins can stack seals on any given player, provided each seal is unique.

I prefer a flat casting cost to a mana drain because I don't like more complex 'draining' game mechanics. Adjusting mana cost and seal utility ought to be enough to balance the much improved seal duration. Basically, we're looking at simple buffs that are constrained by time (30 minutes or so, chosen because that's a common buff duration and is ideally short enough to help defuse cries that paladins are overpowered, etc) and more importantly constrained by target (one unique seal per group mate). I think that notion is infinitely more fun than what we're stuck with now, and could really make multi-paladin tactics far more interesting than they currently are.

I'm only a 26 paladin, so not that experienced, but I'll admit that I frankly don't use the seals I've bought other than Protection and have stopped picking them up for now. Attempting to use Fury in groups was not particularly enjoyable (as I had to spam it several times a fight in additition to Holy Strike and occasional healing) nor was it particularly effective cast on myself (still only occasionally out-taunting my party mages). Current seals, I generally won't bother with them. The seals discussed in this thread, I'd buy and use them all.
