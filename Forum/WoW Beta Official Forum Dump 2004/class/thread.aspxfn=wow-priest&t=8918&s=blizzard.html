I've played a priest since the alpha, and in previous pushes it was a highly versitile class. Never the highest dammage dealer, I could still effectively solo, and enjoy soloing instead of feeling the grind as I wacked away with my staff. I could also be purely support in a group, being the core of a successful instance party. During PvP, I was a force to be reconed with, be it backing up my allies or toe to toe with a mage.

Over the last couple of patches i've seen that versitility weakened to a great extent. Does Blizzard intend for the priest class to be basically a support healer only? and if so, why not go all the way and make the class a full blown healer?

I know the official blizzard line on charicter balance is that "we'd like to make it so that there are a multiple of play styles that are possible for each class." I just don't see this after the recent shadow changes.

These are my suggestions for what changes should be made:

1. Make the Holy line our primary tree and Shadow our secondary. We're priests, we're the holyiest of the holies. Priests have no insta-cast anything now, on either tree. Pain is our primary dammage spell, and it's a dot. Anything dammage based that's really worth using is in the Shadow tree.

2. Switch the timers and dammage on Smite and Mind Blast. You want to decrease the power of a priest, fine. I agree mindblast can be a pretty nice spell. But there's really no reason for me to use Smite because of it. The increased spell time and decreased dammage of smite means I can't kill something as quickly. Short cast time should be low-med dammage, Big cast time should be big-mana-efficient cast. And it would put a little punch into the holy tree that's lacking.

3. Since Priests are the holiest of the holies, we should have the potential to be the best healers as well. So how about some more effective heals, maybe a truely insta-cast heal for low restore, and a highly effective long group heal, neither of which we have currently.

All in all, it just seems to me that the class is backwards a bit. Holy should be our strength, not shadow, and we still need some way to effectively solo at the same sort of pace as a warrior or mage.

3. Will Fade and Taunt ever serve a useful function in PvP? Many people have suggested these should have retargetting effects, forcing players to either not target (in the case of Fade) or not change targets away (in the case of Taunt) for a set period of time. Fade could even allow the player to be targetted if any aggressive actions are taken; it would just be nice to not always die in the first 3 seconds of a PvP encounter.

4. Why was the recast timer on Psychic Scream doubled, and why can't we restore the old timer with the talent to reduce the recast time?

5. Are there any plans to make Warriors less vital in PvE groups? As it stands, Priests have had their necessity completely eliminated by a good Druid or Shaman, but doing an instance without a level-appropriate Warrior is all but impossible due to big pulls and the irreplaceability of Taunt.

6. What new "flavor" is planned for the Priest class?

7. For a good 3-4 patches, Mind Control has been an incredibly weak spell. It breaks if we're struck in combat, it breaks VERY often all on its own (even with the 10% reduction to shadow resistance), it doesn't give the Priest any access to a PC's abilities, and ALL aggro generated by the controlled target is transferred back to the Priest when the spell breaks/wears off. Locking out the Priest from moving or using any other abilities while controlling a target is fine, but only if the spell is really worthwhile -- currently it is absolutely not. Are there any plans to make Mind Control a fun and useful spell again?

8. Are there any plans to change Mind Vision to do what it is supposed to do? Right now it says it has a range of 50000 yards, but I can't cast it on a party member outside of my range of vision, much less in another zone or on another continent. As weak and useless as this spell is, it would be at least SLIGHTLY handy if it worked (if for no other reason than spying on them Night Elven Druid party members when they hit the showers).

9. Why are the only "fun" Priest items trinkets? The ingenuity and creativity of trinkets are outstanding, but beyond those, Priests (particularly non-Engineering Priests) have no access to these fun abilities and items. Rogues get +evade, +crit, +attack; tanks get +defense, +weapon; we get...nothing.

10. Are there plans to increase the laughably small +heal and +dam bonuses on items? Most Priests are not willing to sacrifice 10-20 points of stats to gain "up to 6" shadow damage or healing. Expressed as a percentage, these bonuses would often be in the range of 0.2% -- the costs far, Far, FAR outweigh the benefits, there.
