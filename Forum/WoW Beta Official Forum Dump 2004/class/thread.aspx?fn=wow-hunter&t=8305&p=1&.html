 1. More Hunter PVP Suggestions. | 10/13/2004 12:58:16 PM GMTDT
1)Hunters need a root. Troll axethrowers use nets, we should have a similar ability. As it stands we have no viable tactic to create distance between ourselves and our attackers. Every other class has a method to enable them to use their primary DPS abilities, Hunters need one too.

2)Hunters should be able to drop traps while in combat. Shamans can spam totems at will during combat.

3)Hunters need a 'get out of jail free card'. An escape route. Something, anything viable that will give us a chance to escape combat we don't want to participate in. The net would work for this.

4)Failing any of these, aggressors should be forced to deal with our pets in a more subtantial way than simply ignoring them. Pets should either be more of a threat or growl should function to switch the agressors targeting at regular intervals.

Until some of this is implemented I strongly suggest folks avoid creating Hunters on the PvP server.

 2. Re: More Hunter PVP Suggestions. | 10/13/2004 6:24:37 PM GMTDT
I completely agree with the idea that Hunters need some loving. Nevertheless, I cannot realistically see any of these being implemented with the exception of #2. They're either overpowered, or out of line with the class:

#1 is out of line, unless the root can only be used to initiate combat. Hunters shouldn't be masters of escape, they should be masters of detection and trapping. They're not supposed to be running away from their prey.

#2 is viable, and I think would be a better utilization of an escape plan. However, if you could use traps in combat, then either the traps effectiveness needs to be nerfed, or the traps need a huge cooldown timer (5 minutes is not out of line if it can be used for escapes).

#3 See above. Hunters should only have two no-brainer escape routes at most. If traps could be used in combat to lock-up your chasing enemy, that's one. With a better taunting pet to be sacrificed, that's two.

#4 Pets might need more of a taunt, but having growl switch targets as a distraction is much too powerful. It's more powerful than the warrior's taunt ability in PvE because it bypasses the entire aggro system. In PvP, it renders the rogue useless because it completely voids the rogue's damage ability: combo points, and it would wreak havoc with the casting abilities of other classes.

 4. Re: More Hunter PVP Suggestions. | 10/14/2004 12:10:22 AM GMTDT
Note that I said "should be masters of detection and trapping," not "are masters..."

The traps hunters have now are very nice, but they are awkward to use around combat with all the other abilities (sniping, pets, skills) that must be juggled. I think traps need to be made a bit more attractive, and I'm open to many ideas in that realm. In fantasy, hunters (rangers) are usually the survivalists, the ones who are able to artificially use the environment against their enemies.

As for detection, hunters have the underused Flare ability. In grouped PvP battles, use of Flare can be devastating to druids and rogues, but I've never seen it used. Either most Hunters don't understand the skills well enough, or the skill itself needs to be streamlined to make its use more attractive.

Also, certain tracking skills, especially against enemy PCs, should be implemented. Now, I think that having enemies appear on the minimap would be a bit too powerful, unless only the closest enemy appeared at any given time. Perhaps another method of tracking implementation would be to have an arrow appear pointing in the direction of the nearest enemy PC formation, with the enemy's strength indicated by the intensity of the arrow's glare. I'd add a cooldown timer of 30 to 60 seconds, so that it can't lock on an enemy's movements, but it would still give the hunter a damn good chance at tracking down that enemy rogue that thought he'd escaped.

Edit: This occurred to me, but probably won't happen. What if the UI of tracking was such that the hunter could pull up a list of the enemy races in the zone, and by selecting one he could track it by the method described above? Now, he couldn't see the class or the level of the race. Though he'd certainly be someone to be feared on the PvP field at higher levels.
