    Although I edited my post, I suppose I ought to reply:

    Yes, that's my point. Paladins generally have the full range of functionality all other characters do (including ranged), with the limitation that it's restricted to undead. We all know this coming into the class, that paladins are exceptional against one type of foe and limited against the rest. So, your problem isn't so much the lack of ranged as it is the undead focus on the ranged/it's viable in only "a small, finite portion of the world".

    But that really doesn't matter, we're debating semantics there.

    The question is, what solution can you offer that Blizzard is going to accept? I think ranged weapon skills are entirely off the table. Right or wrong, my impression is that those things are non-negotiable as far as the development team is concerned. A ranged, all-purpose damage spell (DD or DoT) seems unlikely to me, as it's offenssive spellcasting outside the undead limitation. I know there is some precedent via holy bolt (albeit a Diablo precedent), but I just don't see Blizzard caving on that point, either.

    I know people get attached to their own ideas, so I recognize my bias here, but I think the best we can hope for is some sort of ranged taunt spell that deals no damage. That actually addresses two paladin issues, IMO, the lack of pulling and the inability to tank effectively in high DPS groups. Naturally, it has to be a compromise of other classes' abilities, so I expect it would need to have a somewhat limited range (25 yards or so) as well as accumulate less aggro than a warrior's Taunt. That's my solution, and while I'd simply prefer spending 200sp to buy guns or bows, I really think a measure that meets our needs and Blizzard's concept in the middle is the best we can hope for.
