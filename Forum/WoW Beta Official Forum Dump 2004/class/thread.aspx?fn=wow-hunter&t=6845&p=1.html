 19. Re: Hunters can be awesome in groups. | 10/6/2004 11:31:12 PM GMTDT
I also feel that hunters need some more work to make them more group friendly. I think hunters are fine solo in PvE, and that they can have use in groups, but, as has been stated above, currently there are other classes that do the group thing much better, so as a result it can be harder getting an instance group with a hunter than other classes. Yes, some of it is perception of other players, but we do need something more that helps change that perception.

The main group roles are tank, healer, DPS, and maybe CC. Hunter falls primarily under DPS. We can do just a little CC under correct circumstances, but hunter is basically DPS. A group is usually going to want a tank, and some healing. That can either be a priest, or perhaps 2 backup healers. So 2-3 spots can potentially hold DPS. If another healer (primary or backup) wants to join it seems most groups would rather have tank + 2 healer +2 DPS than tank + healer +3 DPS. Mage and rogue both do quite a bit more damage than us (yes hunter talents aren't in and may change this some) and are more likely to get taken.

My thoughts on things that can help a hunter in groups:

Hunter is most natural for role of puller. But in groups I've been in, I usually get pushed aside in favor of a warrior with gun/crossbow, or rogue with daggers, as puller. Yet hunters are supposed to be the "tops" with ranged weapons. So, I think something that sets us aside and makes us the top puller would give us a distinguished group role. I can think of 2 different ways to accomplish this.
---One way, would be to give us a bit more range. If we could pull from further away than other classes who can use ranged weapons but are not "specialists" in it, we would have something to set us apart.
--The other way, would be if we had a lower aggro factor when pulling. Ie, a hunter making a pull from a group of monsters would be much more likely to bring just one monster than other classes using ranged weapons. Note I'm not saying we should have 0% chance to bring adds, just a reduced percent.

Another factor in groups--our pet can die quick, and healing a pet while the group is getting pounded on is low priority. If, in a group, we had a small circle attached to our group portrait, with a health bar for the pet, it would make it easier for the group's healer(s) to see the pet's health and target the pet (this should also go to warlocks). We could keep our pet out, and the healer could get to it.

Traps are another aspect that seem to need some improvement. Currently I've found that groups usually get irritated with me if I take time to lay a trap before doing a pull. Sure that could be "bad group" but I find it is common. We need an ability to set traps in battle. One trap at a time limitation is fine, and traps can have a cooldown period such that once you set one you cannot set one after a bit. Setting a trap can have a channeling-type bar that if you get hit, it has a chance to break (and then you would need to start the trap from the beginning). If we are able to set traps in battle given restrictions, we would be able to use them a lot more.

A small one, but Aspect of Pack is not available until level 40, and by then players start getting mounts, so even this "group" buff is not so viable to a group. If Aspect of Pack were available at lower level, that would be another hunter-distinguishing group ability (group taxi).

Keep in mind again, that when I wrote what I did above, I'm not saying "hunters suck" or even "hunters have no use in groups whatsoever". I am saying that "compared with other classes they can choose, most players will choose someone other than a hunter for their group, as the hunter has little distinguishing role, and other classes can be more beneficial to the group".
