Regarding Perkele's and Ziandra's posts:

I feel a % reduction in damage for pvp is a great solution, if the right balance of reduction is found. This is how the devs went about it in Star Wars: Galaxies, and I felt that, for the most part, it worked nicely. It was a bit hefty, at 75% redux.

What ruined it all was the implementation of armor, the best of which could reduce all damage taken by up to 90%. So a blow that would have dealt 1,000 damage gets reduced off the top to only 250, and uber armor further reduced to only about 25-30 damage. Insanity. I'm worried that this may be the case (especially concerning mages)with resists when they're implemented.

And Ziandra is completely right about people whining when they see their damage output being 1/4 or 1/3 of what it is in PvE. It's silly, because I think it makes the fights more engaging than just a game of one-shotters (that is, with only the straight PvP redux off the top, though 75% is too much, imo), but resists can still muck it all up if not implemented well.

I just hope Blizzard will be a bit more moderate about nerfs/uberness, unlike the fairly extreme power sways most classes have experienced so far. There is a nice thread by Tikk in the mage forums about that matter, if anyone cares.
