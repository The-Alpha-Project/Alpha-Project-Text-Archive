As was already mentioned, it depends on your tastes.

If you want a brute who wades forward into anything, Warlocks are not for you.

Warlocks require finesse and a bit of micro to handle properly. This isn't a bad thing given they have an answer for just about anything. Their DoT and DD spells are decent. Their pets do well as either tanks (VW), or an extra damage source (Succubus) or as a buff in groups (Imp). Who knows how the Felhunter and Infernal will help but they'll no doubt be entertaining.

When all that fails, Fear effects, the VW taunts, the Succubus's Soothing Kiss and (to a lesser extent now) Banish help the VW with crowd control.

Plus, some of their utility spells like drain life and the various conjured stones add to their effectiveness.

Since they got their talents the Warlocks have become very fun. They effectively ARE their pets though which is their primary drawback. If a pet goes down and you have multiple agros and/or can't get away you'll have issues. As it should be though given any given class shouldn't be infallible.

As a whole Warlocks are my favorite. I have other characters now but I always come back to this one.

Why a Warlock is fun to play;
- No downtime when soloing.
- Not dependent on equipment.
- Made for long battles.
- Least panic class. IMO
- Most healing options.
- Several Mana options.

Those 4 things are what I like best about Warlocks. When soloing, if you pull 1 mob at a time and manage your life and mana, you will not have any downtime. It will take longer to drop a mob, but I have had 10 food and drink in my inventory forever. If I really wanted/needed the inventory space, food and water would be the first things to go.

The least panic class and that is related to the no downtime benefit above. I use very little life and mana to engage 1 mob, so when I get an add, I don't really sweat it. That makes for very fun, long and rewarding battles. I have been able to handle as many as 3 adds (melee mobs) of the same level. I view the warlock as a pool management class. Drain Life, then tap the life for mana, slap on your DoT's. Manage your life and mana and let your DoT's tick away. Warlock takes patience and management, perfectly fits my playstyle.

The warlock does not heal as well as a priest but a warlock has up to 5 healing options. Drain Life, Healthstone, Siphon Life, Health Potions and Bandages.

Drain Mana and Life Tap are methods to keep your mana pool healthy along with mana potions. I have played most of the other classes to lower levels and I can't remember what abilities the other classes had to manipulte their mana pool.

For me, Warlock is managing your life and mana pools and using a lot of various abilities and items to do it with. That spells FUN for me.

Haven't played my warlock very much this patch, the channeling bug is just way too annoying. Warlock is definitely going to be my first and primary character come retail.

My warlock is at lvl 30 now. I haven't had the chance to do much PvP yet, but the warlock is very powerful in PvE right now.

I'm using the default warlock PvE technique: VW, and Affliction with the occaisonal SB when nightfall procs. I'm being conservative against even-con mobs, managing the fight so that I end it at almost full health and mana and not going all out unless I get adds.

Even so, I find that not only is combat less tedious and much safer than it was with my lvl 30 warrior, but I kill mobs about as fast, if not faster than I did with my warrior at lvl 30.

The warlock feels very powerful. I can easily dispatch even-con mobs without using the class to it's fullest potential. Adds are no problem whatsoever, and I can solo situations which would have been extremely difficult with my warrior.

This class probably has the most combat options in the game. There are several viable ways to fight with a warlock: Fear kiting, affliction while pet tanks, tanking while pet does the damage, or just flat out nuking like a mage.

I also really like the fact that my warlock isn't gear dependant like my warrior is. The power of the class remains at a constant while you level up, instead of fluctuating wildly depending on the weapon you use like it does with warriors.

Last night, I had a wild fight against an even con elite mob in 1k needles. I had to use every trick I had to bring it down, starting with my VW tanking while I loaded up the dot's, then switching to fear-kiting halfway thru the fight when I had to sacrifice the VW. The fight ended with both me and mob lieing dead on the groud, but I can safely say that with my warrior, it would not have ended in a draw. =D

I imagine that PvP should be really fun with this class, particularily with a succubus. The nice thing about it is that If I get CC'd, I'll still be able to CC the enemy right back with my succubus. Something that wouldn't be possible with any other class.

I still think there's room for improvement tho. For one thing, the top tiers of the talent lines are very underwhelming compared to what other classes get. The succubus needs to have her DPS improved to make her a more viable alternative to the VW. And they need to make it easier to get soulshards IMHO. At the very least, warlocks need to be able to get soulshards from enemy players, otherwise they're going to be rather gimpy in end-game PvP.

Finally, I wouldn't recommend going Alliance if your going to play a warlock on the PvP server. Undead Melee's absolutely tear this class apart. I went UD with my warlock, because if you think about it, everything a palladin can do to me, I can do it to them as well, only better.

First Aid and Alchemy/Herbalism really compliment this class as well.

 35. Re: How FUN are Warlocks? | 10/23/2004 12:40:48 PM GMTDT
I think people have really gotten the right idea with this: The appeal of a Warlock is its variety. When you're fighting as a Warlock you have so many things to watch at once: You have to decide which curse to use, you have to replace you DoTs when they run out, you have to decide which drain to use, you have to manage aggro, you have to control your pet, you have to work out the resource management of life and mana [we're the only class which has abilities to easily refill both while in battle]. And rather than being annoying, all of this is interesting. Paradoxically, we're one of the slowest killing classes and yet we have some of the most intense battles. Add on top of this nifty utility abilities like the Eye of Kilrog and summoning, and we're definitely one of the most interesting classes to play. And in a group the dynamic becomes even more layered.

Compare this to Mage, who can definitely kill mobs quicker than us, but, as other posters have mentioned above, quickly becomes more banal in their combat routines.

How much you will enjoy Warlock will be proportional to how much you care about efficacy compared to variety. If taking forever to kill mobs starts to get to you, I recommend looking into Rogues: They kill much faster, have interesting battles, and with cooking or First Aid will also experience very little downtime.
