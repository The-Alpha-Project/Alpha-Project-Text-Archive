 1. Why I respecced to destruction | 10/18/2004 7:59:06 PM GMTDT
Picking talents for a Warlock is key in utilizing your character
to the fullest. After thinking long and hard on it, I've
respecced to a destruction-based character. It was a hard choice.
Affliction-based Warlocks get Fel Concentration which allows
drain life tanking and Dark Pact which is amazing. It was a tough
choice to give them up, here is why I did it.

When it comes to grouping, I bring two things to the table.
First, I control the enemy. I can Banish elemental/demon adds
(and I really like the change for PVE, its a fantastic spell in
BRD where I am now). I curse to reduce the enemy's overall
effectiveness. I offtank with the Voidwalker. I have fear,
including an AOE fear, which though rarely used can make a few
encounters much easier than they'd otherwise be. I feel fine on
this aspect of my contribution.

The second aspect is damage. Now, against bosses we are damn
good. We can stack all three DOTs (Curse of Agony, Corruption,
and Immolate) and chain-nuke with Shadow bolt while throwing in a
Shadowburn every 15 seconds. Against a boss that doesn't resist
any of those elements, I'd wager a Warlock can do more damage
than anyone else. If a mage manages to beat us, it won't be by
much.

The problem is in shorter encounters, elites lasting 10 seconds
or a little more. I've run the numbers on the DOTs and only
Immolate is worth using. I didn't really need to run numbers, its
obvious in play the DOTs really don't do much. Curse of Agony is
obvious even just reading the spell why it is weak, it ramps up
damage and doesn't do good damage until the 20+ second time
frame. Even though its mana cost is low, it still takes almost
two seconds of cast time to use (spell refresh plus lag plus
reaction time). You need about 14 seconds for it to do more
damage than would a Shadow Bolt. Note that because its cost is so
low, if the group is low on DPS and taking 15ish or more seconds
to kill mobs, I use it. Corruption requires 18 seconds to be
worth using over a Shadow Bolt mana-wise. Because even in long
fights I go with Curse of Agony first, Corruption needs even
longer fights to be worth bring out. For me, Corruption has
become boss-only. I don't even use it soloing. Its not hard to
realize this would be the case just looking at the spell, the
last Corruption is a level 54 spell, spells that are 6 levels old
tend to not be that useful.

The net result is that I basically Immolate and chain nuke with
Shadow Bolt, mana being the only consideration as to how often I
nuke. So in groups to be an adequate damage dealer, I have to act
like a second-rate mage, a pure nuker. That's why I specced
destruction. Still, it isn't all that bad. Immolate really is
fantastic. Fights last long enough that it gets a few ticks off,
and with the destruction talents it is a darn good spell. +10%
fire damage, +5% chance to crit, 100% crit damage, and +25% to
the initial nuke portion of Immolate adds up to a great opening
punch for the mana. Shadowburn really helps to, it is a slightly
less powerful Shadowbolt that is instant cast, has a low cooldown
timer so it can be used every fight if you want, and by 60
Warlocks will have learned how to deal with soul shard costs.
Shadow Bolt, with the added crits and the very helpful half
second casting time reduction, is now good sustained DPS. Our
damage is actually pretty good, our biggest problem compared to
mages (the most comparable class) is that we use a lot more mana
to do this than they do. Immolate certainly helps but the thing
that lets us stay somewhat competitive is that we have Life Tap
and they don't. Life Tap combined with bandages means near
infinite mana. Often you don't need bandages because the party
healer constantly heals your self-inflicted damage. I tell them I
am doing it to myself and not to bother unless I am getting
pounded, but I think they get bored and do it anyway. Or maybe it
is unbreakable habit.

Unfortunately, at level 60, Searing Pain is not worth it, even
with its +10% chance to crit and its +10% fire damage from
speccing destruction. It uses less mana than does Shadow Bolt,
but its DPS is inferior. The combination of a 2.5 second cast due
to talents and the fact that Shadow Bolt is brand new at 60 means
it will be doing more damage than Searing Pain. After running the
numbers, I didn't bother to spec the Searing Pain +10% crit
talent, it isn't worth it at 60. It probably would be worth it on
the way to 60, say during retail, but I'd still skip it over
knowing once I got to 60, its not going to be used much. Don't
get me wrong, I like Searing Pain. The aggro is not an issue if
you have a competent tank. The problem is it needs those 5 talent
points to work well, and you have so much you want to spend them
on. It needs just a little bit more damage.

Instances have certain encounters that have AOEs written all over
them, and Warlocks have good AOEs. Our 'safe' AOE Rain of Fire
isn't as good as mages, but Hellfire is better. Of course
Hellfire is hard to use effectively, but it really rocks when you
can use it. What's not to like about a 230 DPS AOE? Not only does
speccing destruction get you +10% damage to your AOEs, it gets
70% chance to channel through damage and 25% chance to stun
opponents. Maybe AOEs only truly shine in particular encounters,
but those are the encounters people build parties to get past.
With Hellfire, Warlocks definitely can compete with Mages for an
AOE spot in those groups. Speccing to be a sought-after member of
a group is a good thing.

The last talent in the line is Conflagrate. Right now it is
useless. But look at what happened to Shadowburn, it is only a
matter of a patch or two before the spell upgrades to Conflagrate
are in the game. Depending on how mana-efficient it is, it will
be another fantastic addition to our quick-fight DPS. To be at
all useful, it has to be noticeably better than Shadow Bolt since
it eats up your immolate. Still, based on what they did with
Shadowburn I think that will be the case. On 10 second fights, I
can see using a line up of: Immolate, Shadow Bolt, Conflagrate,
Life Tap, move to next mob with a bandage needed every 3 or 4
mobs. If Conflagrate gets enough of a boost, say to the 500ish
damage range, with crits this combo will be doing an average of
about 1500 damage over a 10 second period. Of course, currently
we can just Shadow Bolt twice for this, but I'm really hoping
that Conflagrate costs a good bit less mana than the second
Shadow Bolt for comparable damage. Also, you have the instant
Shadowburn every 15 seconds to throw in to push you to about 2000
damage per mob. There is usually enough time between that you
could use it every mob, though you'll need to go into collect
soul shard mode about half the time.

As for soloing, I am fine. Solo speed as a nuker is comparable to
that of an affliction-specialist. I use the succubus for her
added damage. Voidwalker soloing is safe but it is so slow. Plus
you need one more spell of some kind to make up the damage you
lose from the Succubus. Even using the most efficient one you
have, Corruption, that's 290 more mana per fight, and longer
fights. No, Succubus is the solo pet for me whenever possible.
After respeccing destruction I went out and soloed for a while to
get the technique down. My targets are the undead just north of
the Argent Dawn camp in Eastern Plaguelands. There is a good mix
of mobs there, so it is great for testing. I found my preferred
strategy is to send pet and use Curse of Agony. I let the pet
fight for 5 or so seconds. Then I cast Immolate, Shadow Bolt,
Shadow Bolt, mob dead. The Shadow Bolts take some extra time
because the mob comes over and beats on me. Letting the Succubus
tank the beginning is solely so that the mob dies on the second
Shadow Bolt or shortly after. In other words, there is a few
seconds of 'waiting' time needed with this combination to kill
the mob, so let the pet take the beating instead of me. Against
caster mobs, it is easier. Send pet, Immolate, Shadow Bolt,
Shadow Bolt, dead mob. I have more downtime than I did when I was
affliction specced due to no Dark Pact, but I kill faster. I tank
for less time so durability isn't going down as fast. Overall,
destruction-specced soloing is comparable to affliction-specced
soloing, something I didn't really expect.

But even soloing well too, the real payoff is that I think a
destruction-specced Warlock can be a good damage dealer in a
group. It does require acting much like a mage, and not being as
good at it as a mage. Still we bring other advantages, its not a
case of being nothing more than a second rate mage. In a really
good group, Hellfire is better than mage AOE. With both Banish
and a tanking pet, CC is better than a mage's. (Don't get me
wrong, sheep kicks butt too.) Plus we have Curse of Weakness,
Curse of Tongues, and Curse of Recklessness. They give out free
water, but that can be bought so it is just money. We have
Soulstones and Healthstones. I've been using Soulstones a lot and
even with the nerf, more often than not they allow your healer to
stay and rez everyone provided the healer realizes when a wipe is
coming and moves to the correct spot to die in. Plus we can
Summon people to us. Overall I think the group contribution is
really pretty even, and Warlocks might even be better.

Unfortunately, perception-wise, I believe most players think
mage>>warlock. Perception is hard to change. But specced the way
I am, I am no longer nearly as pessimistic about Warlocks as I
once was. We have the tools to change the perception. The biggest
problem is that the Warlock power is spread out over so many
areas, it takes a lot of skill to make the most of it. You really
need to be on the top of your game to do it all. A tough pull, you
need to think about Banishing the adds, getting the other adds on
your pet, Curse of Weakenssing them to keep your pet from being
beat up too fast, assisting in the kill with damage,
recklessnessing the mobs before they run, health transferring to
your pet when the adds start to get the better of him. You need
to think of mana conservation, prioritizing what you spend it on,
dealing as much damage you can and which options you can afford,
life tapping to replenish mana, drain lifeing to keep life up to
life tap more and transfer to your pet. In other words, you have
to play damn well to really get the most from a Warlock. But as
long as you do, when there is only 5 people in a group the
Warlock can bring so much using up just one slot that I think we
will be in demand for informed groups, and those are really the
only ones I care about anyway.

Unfortunately, non destruction-specced Warlocks aren't so well
off. Affliction is hurt by speccing in DOTs that are worthless in
groups. Immolate, the good DOT, is destruction so you are left
with enhancing two DOTs that you rarely use. You do get the +10%
shadow damage that helps Shadow Bolt, but you really need to dip
into destruction enough to at least get the reduced casting time
for it. Dark Pact would let you have more mana and nuke more
often, but it doesn't work well with the Voidwalker, and using a
different pet greatly reduces your contribution to a group (extra
tankage is highly valueable.) As for Demonology, I hate the tree.
I get my Stamina boost and my extra Voidwalker taunt both at the
top of the tree, and I wouldn't think of putting more points into
it for the rest of the stuff. Most importantly, I can't see
anything in it being a valueable contribution to the group each
fight like the stuff in destruction is.

So overall, I think destruction is the way to go. At least it is
for those who play their Warlock like I do.
