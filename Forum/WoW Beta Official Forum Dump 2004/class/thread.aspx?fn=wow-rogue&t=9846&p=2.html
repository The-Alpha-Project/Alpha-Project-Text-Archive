I think armor should factor in when applied to the damage a weapon does. The problem with rogues exclusively is we are tied to one handed weapons, and dual wield. Hard hitting two handed weapons are impacted less by armor.

Rogues should have a much greater chance to hit with the off hand, and our off handed damage should be inately higher then warriors, and hunters.

Rougues should be at 10% chance to miss while dual wielding instead of 24%, and weapon damage should start at 50% in the off hand. (The dual wield skill states that off hand weapon damage is 50% of the weapons DPS, however this has been consistantly parsed at 25%.)

Rogues should also receive an inate bonus for using daggers. I understand Blizzard is staying away from class unique weapons, basically if you have the skill you can use the weapon. This puts daggers in a precarious position, because of their speed it would be extremely overpowering for a warrior or hunter to use a very hard hitting high speed 60 DPS dagger. The work around to this problem is to put high speed hard hitting daggers in rogue only quests and to give rogues an innate advantage to using daggers.

Blizzard could also put in a repeating rogue only quest that increases the DPS of any dagger given to the quest NPC.
