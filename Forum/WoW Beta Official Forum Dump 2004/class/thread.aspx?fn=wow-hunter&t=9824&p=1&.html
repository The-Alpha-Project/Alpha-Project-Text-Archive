 1. The Hunter and Rogue Should be Tied for DPS | 10/20/2004 6:42:27 AM GMTDT
They OBVIOUSLY aren't, but they should be.

Think about it this way, of all the classes there are two which bring no functionality in terms of group play, other than their DPS.

The magic/healing classes are obvious: priest, pally, mage, shaman, druid: we KNOW they offer heals, buffs, sheep (god I love sheep (and I mean the spell)) etc.

The warrior and warlock don't offer the same level of direct player aid (heals and buffs), but the warrior tanks, has excellent DPS and has buffs and debuffs (battle shout, it's in there), and the warlock has a great deal of functionality (soul stones, teleporting, etc.)

That just leaves the rogue and the hunter as the oddballs out.

As a rogue, you have to consider the fact that you've TRADED all of that direct player functionality which the other classes can offer each other for your other abilities: you KNOW that you won't be welcomed into a group except for your ability to KILL things.

Now, what people may or may not have realized is that the hunter is in the SAME boat as the rogue. We don't have buffs, we don't have cool functionality (aspect of the pack becomes useless with the advent of mounts), we don't have massive debuffs, and we sure as HELL don't have the killing power of a rogue, but, for all intents and purposes, we SHOULD.

And thus, we are in our current situation.

Has anyone else thought about it from this perspective: the notion that the hunter and rogue offer the same to groups, only one does it so much better than the other? It wasn't until I went over this in my head that I realized that the only other class which offers zero player-aid functionality is the rogue...
