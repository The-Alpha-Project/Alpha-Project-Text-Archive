 1. Mage's Aggro and possibility of Invisibility | 10/18/2004 2:35:59 AM GMTDT
I've played a Mage up to lvl 50. I really, honestly, don't want to hear about how a Mage works in lvl 60 in BRD or whatnot, because gameplay mainly takes place in lvls 1-60, not after 60.

I know that in BRD and other end game instances, Warriors had better go Defensive Stance, or else they aren't doing their job. However, this is not the way it is seen in the earlier levels.

I remember going to RFK at lvl 30. We had a Shaman, Priest, Rogue, Warrior and myself, a Mage (I believe these were the classes, it was a while ago). Figuring it was my role, I nuked the hell out of the monsters. The monsters then attacked me. Figuring it was the Warriors role to taunt them off, I thought I would be safe. Instead he charged at them (back when Charge was something you could use multiple times in battle), Hamstringed them, etc.

So I ran away. They said don't run. I told them I didn't want to tank. They told me to stop nuking as much as I was. This frankly confuses me. They wanted me to do less damage than a Warrior.

This isn't uncommon, I've found. In many groups that I've been in, the answer to the problem is often "stop nuking as much as you are." (In other words, stop doing the most damage and as a result gaining the most aggro.) If a Mage can't do as much dmg as a Warrior, what role in the group is a Mage supposed to have?


If you answer crowd control, I can refer you to quotes that answer that. There is not supposed to be any specific class which has the role of crowd control.

If you answer Arcane Missiles/Arcane Explosion, I ask you this question: are Mages seriously supposed to specialize in two spells/one talent tree, in order to be good in groups? I could see respecing at end game in order to be good in groups, but not from lvls 1-60, because in those levels, you're mostly going to be soloing.

Does being a soloer mean you shouldn't be good in groups? No. You should be able to solo, and be able to do your job in groups.

Warriors can specialize in Offensive Stance for soloing (and they are best off when they do so). When it comes to grouping, they can effectively use Defensive Stance and do their job of tanking.
However, when it comes to soloing, Mages are best off NOT specializing in Arcane. Yet when it comes to group situations, they cannot effectiely do their job of doing the most damage without Arcane talents.


My point is that the Mage should be able to do the most damage in the group, without specializing in Arcane, and without a Defensive Warrior in the group.

Making them either A) have a defensive Warrior or B) specialize in Arcane is way too specific of a requirement for Mages in levels 1-60 (though I do realize that it isn't too specific for end game, considering everyone is going to be expected to specialize for group situations).

I have two solutions. My favorite is having Invisibility work in combat. In Warcraft 3/TFT, Invisibility was often used in combat in order to save your heroes/units from dying. Just cast up Invisibility when they are low on health, and run them back to base to use for another battle.

I would basically like to see it used the same way in World of Warcraft. Invisibility would mainly be a combat skill. You would use it to save yourself and PERHAPS other players in group situations. You are getting attacked, you use Invisibility, the monster doesn't see you anymore. During the Invisiblity period, your aggro starts dropping (built into the skill, I'm not talking about the same natural aggro-drop you might see if a Mage were to just sit back and stop nuking). As soon as the Mage feels his aggro is low enough, he pops out of Invisiblity and starts blasting away.

Now, people might complain that this is identical to Vanish. Obvously there would be some differences. First, it couldn't be used against other players, because you would still see the player. You would ALWAYS see the Invisible Mage as if you were detecting a stealthed Rogue. Second, it would be better in the way that the Mage would have a bonus 'aggro lowering' aspect to it. Mages also could use this ability once per battle (instead of the long cooldown of Vanish). I think that the difference between reagents would be that the Rogue buys his, and the Mage must summon his before the battle begins (and it might require something hefty, like 300 mana to cast).

There's one final difference in how this would be used. It wouldn't be instant cast. In fact it would be interruptable. It would have a 2-5 second casting time (depending on what's balanced). So the Mage couldn't cast this as if it were Vanish... he wouldn't be able to cast it at the last second to save himself. He would have to plan a little, instead of casting it whenever he has 50 health left.


Perhaps the aggro-lowering ability would be a channeling spell. So he would have to use it before he even gains the most aggro. Here's how it could be used:

Warrior charges in. Mage casts nukes, but just below the amount that would make him gain the most aggro. The Mage then uses the channeling ability to lower his aggro very far down. Then the Mage uses more nukes. After nuking the second time, he now has the most DPS out of anyone in the group. He could keep doing this throughout the battle, and the result would be that the Mage would be doing his role in the group (doing the most damage).

Perhaps he would not be doing his role as well as he would if they had a Defensive Stance Warrior, because the Mage would have to channel this spell (and maybe this channeling spell would cost a lot of mana, so he would have to sacrifice a little casting for having lowered aggro).


Tell me what you guys think. If you have an alternative that is already in the game, I would love to hear it. If you have different opinions of what the Mage's role in the group is, I would also love to hear that. Just giving MY opinion. If my opinion is wrong, I'll gladly sit down and shut up. :)
