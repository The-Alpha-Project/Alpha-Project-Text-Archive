 1. Why we have no place in groups... | 10/22/2004 5:16:10 AM GMTDT
It just occurred to me: this is why hunters aren't being invited to groups. Every other class can offer something which will prevent group wipes if they know how to properly utilize their abilities.

Warrior: tank, most obvious. Keep foes off the casters so that they can heal/sheep and otherwise do their jobs.

Rogue: stun abilities and the ability to dole out piles of damage in an instant to take down a mob attacking a caster. Excellent support for a main tank.

Paladin: Healing/tanking/ressing, again, obvious. Any of these can salvage a party in a tight spot.

Druid: Heals and resses, insanely good buffs. Keeps the party strong and can back up heal.

Priest: duh.

Mage: AoE, ice chain, and SHEEP (gotta love that spell). Food and water don't hurt either.

Warlock: Soulstones, insane debuffs/DoTs, fear. These can be the saving grace of the warlock, as they will seldom draw aggro for DoTs. Soulstones can likewise prevent a party wipe.

Shaman: I'm honestly not that familiar, but with heals and resses, and buffs, the class can certainly make a difference.

But hunters? What single ability do they have which can (and frequently does) make the difference between life or death in a party? What can the hunter do which is akin to a warrior grabbing all aggro, a healing class landing that heal JUST in time, or a mage transforming an add into a sheep? These are the abilities which make us sigh in relief when they happen, and I don't think I can name one amongst the hunter which finds itself on par with these.

Forget about the pet and the fact that it's a liability in high instance dungeons. The hunter just doesn't have the tricks to pull off a party-saving maneuver. Is it POSSIBLE to do this? Yeah, but never with the same effectiveness another class can offer in our place.

If we were instead a...

Warrior, players could let aggro fall to us without concern.

Rogue, we could latch onto a stray add attacking a caster and ensure it a short stay on this mortal coil. We could also vanish in the event of a potential wipe or transfer aggro to the tank this way.

Paladin, we could be a secondary tank and backup healer and resser (and those auras ain't bad either).

Druid, we could deliver powerful and often essential buffs to the party, as well as be a backup healer and tank (in bear form).

Priest, heal, buff, res, etc.

Mage, turn adds into sheep, ice chain them in place, generate food and water and offer awesome DPS.

Warlock, debuff the bejeezus out of targets, fear those which are too much to bear and offer soulstone resses, as well as portaling in an additional player if someone needs to leave.

Shaman, heal, buff and res (sorry, I know the shaman can do much more than this, but I don't know the class very well).

The bottom line here is, we need SOMETHING to make people sigh in relief when we do it, and right now, we don't have that.

A hunter could save the group from a wipe by just being a good player, but the problem is that ANYONE can do that by being a good player and they can do it better than we can due to more apt tools for the situations.

And yes, I know some people will insist that talents will fix this. If that is your opinion, I draw your attention to exhibit A: the paladin class. This class is, likewise, unfinished. They, like us, have no talents, but they are still a viable class in groups and PvP alike, despite the fact that their talents are still on the way.

If you believe for a second that talents will fix a class with no group role, then you ask far, far too much of said talents.

Until the hunter can offer something which will at least put us on even odds with the other classes of this game in group play (I don't ask for better, I just want a decision between a rogue and a hunter to be a coin toss instead of a laugh), then we will forever be the bastard child of WoW.

I'm sorry if I came off harsh in this, but it's the truth and I won't sugar-coat it.
