Q u o t e:
At my level, I have a 51 dps 2 handed axe, a 41 dps one handed axe, and a 36 dps offhand axe.

If all hit 100%, the dual wield would do more damage (41 + 18)=59 dps.

However, there's the inherent miss chance.

2hander: 51 dps * .95 (5% miss chance)=48.45 dps.
Dual wield: 59 dps * .76 (24% miss chance)=44.84 dps. To equal 48.45 dps (that the 2 hander gives with the 95% chance to hit), with that inherent dual wield miss chance, you'd need dual wield weapons of 63.75 dps (backsolving is great). There are 40+ dps offhanders available, but you'll have to do some serious instance delving or hope for an epic world drop to get them. At the same time, there is a 76 dps 2 handed sword out there (epic from BRS) and some 60 dps instance dropped 2 handers.

 26. Re: Why isn't dual wield as good as two-hande | 10/19/2004 5:09:44 PM GMTDT
Another strike against dual wield is that the weapon specialization talents in Arms only apply to main hand, not to off hand. (Blizzard has said this is a bug but it has not been fixed yet.) So with a 2H weapon you have put 5 points into that weapon type and another 5 points into 2H weapon and all 10 apply. With dual wield you can have 5 points in the main hand but nothing applies to the off hand.

A further problem comes when the target blocks you, as any monster seems to be able to do whether or not they have a shield. Block carries a fixed damage reduction, as seen on your shield, which counts heavier against a faster lower-damage 1H weapon than a slower higher-damage 2H weapon.
