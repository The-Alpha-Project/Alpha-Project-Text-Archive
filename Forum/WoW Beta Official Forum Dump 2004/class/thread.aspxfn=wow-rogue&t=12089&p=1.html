More and more as I play my rogue, i'm starting to notice a few glaring useless abilitys..or..rather..abilitys that are extreamly situational.

Solutions?- I really dont know..if it was only ment as a way to do damage over time to long boss fights, I guess its working as intended.

2. Slice and Dice- I guess this is useful if you dont want to pull aggro, or used in conjunction with certain talents, but in a normal fight solo or group...evis will be better, mostly because it will do more damage. Obviously if you have an amazing crit on your wep, this will be a good thing.

Solutions?- Well in groups, i'd like to see unused combo points still able to be used for like the next 3 seconds after a target dies, so you could use those unused combo points on pulling off a S and D.

Expose Armor- Also, looks good, but by the time you build up combo points for it to be worth it, the mob is almost dead, solo or group, so that doesent help much. So just like rupture, its useage is cut 90% right there, and is only good on boss mobs, and good it is. When its vital that your tank keep aggro, instead of an evis, you can cut that bosses armor by a good chunk, not bringing any aggro to you, but still increasing the damage taken

Solutions?- I dont really see any. Another boss only/stoneguy ability

I'd love to hear what any other rogues have to say, i'm only level 26, these are just my observations thus far

Not sure how on topic I'll be so bear with me...

SnD: Definately not useless. However the people suggesting it's some boon for poisons are fooling themselves. SnD is only really useful in a group setting *generally*. And in any group you should be fighting enough to blow through all poison procs with time to spare. For me SnD really shines when a mob is almost dead and you have combo points to spend. It happens. Fire off a SnD and do the extra damage to the next mob.

The one problem I have with SnD is efficiency. 1pt SnD > any other SnD efficiency-wise. For every point after 1 you get before using SnD it gets less efficient. It needs to be a flat 5-6 seconds per combo point.

I really don't know how efficient SnD is relative to other finishers. All I know is the only real reason *I* see to use it at more than 1 point is if I have points to waste right before something dies. Generally...
