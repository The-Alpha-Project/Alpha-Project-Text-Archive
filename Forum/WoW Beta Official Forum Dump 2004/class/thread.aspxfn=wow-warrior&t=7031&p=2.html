General moves

Pummel and Shield Bash are pointless now. You interrupt the mob, it starts casting again IMMEDIATELY. I was fighting various elite Undead casters in Andorhal, and they'd happily start casting a_nuke_01 again right after being interrupted. It was useful last patch, now its useless. Don't understand this change. If it was a problem in PvP, change it for PvP only, don't nerf PvE effectiveness.

Charge was nerfed too hard. Either put a timed debuff on a charged player (Affected by Charge) that prevents them from being charged again, or revamp its behavior solely for PvE. Whatever the case, I dislike having a move that can rarely be used in instance situations. The number of times I'm not 'in combat' where I need to use Charge far, far outweigh the times I can use it to start an engagement. Outdoors fighting 1v1 against mobs, you can still use it (more or less), but that's really, really neutering the effectiveness of the move.

Thunder Clap takes too much rage for too small of an effect with too low of a duration. Yes, it is possible to do a rage build and spam TClap in some AoE situations for aggro if nothing else, but this is a pretty pointless use of a move. Since it is also indiscriminate AoE, you can't always use this in teams either, due to messing with several forms of crowd control.

Heroic Strike does not scale damage based on delay (something you added for the Shaman Flametongue this patch), meaning that a faster weapon is always superior to use with it. Not sure if that is deliberate or not.

A small issue, but figured I'd bring it up. Taunt should be tweaked. Spamming taunt is just irritating. I don't have any problems with maintaining aggro (nor have I ever, regardless of the changes you've made over the months), but the constant mashing of the Taunt button is an annoyance. This is dangerous to play with by increasing the timer or the rage cost too much, as you could leave a group member quite dead if you can't pull aggro again (though there are other tools besides Taunt to regain aggro). A flat increase in hate generation would be nice though. Pushing ONE Taunt now and then instead of mashing it to regain aggro would be nice.

Overpower is pretty gimpy. Against certain mobs you'll get to use it fairly often, but against most, it rarely comes up. Nerfing this to only dodges was a bit harsh imo.

Revenge is pointless without the Talents to back it up. Not sure if it was your intent to force Warriors to take talents in the 'matching' talent tree while using a stance to make the moves effective, but this seems like a bad choice. Without Improved Revenge, your Rage is better spent on Sundering Strike.

Berserk stance needs a total rework. Cleave and Slam only? Come on. 20% boosted damage from ALL types of damage in exchange for 10% haste? Why? Sweeping Strikes and Mortal Strike in Arms Talents? What? I fear that as this stance has recieved no love since Talents were introduced, it may never, and I think that's a terrible mistake :( There's a lot of potential here to give the Warrior a really useful toolkit for AoE situations (some of the more entertaining fights in the game imo), but currently, its just poor.

Talents

Protection

Iron Will is poor. Too many precious talent points required for a minimal and situational effect. Talent points are too precious to be used on Talents that are only (marginally) useful in SOME situations.

Improved Taunt and Improved Revenge are bad design decisions imo. Taunt because it should be useful for all Warriors regardless of talent specialization, and Revenge because it is useless without this Talent.

Improved Disarm, Improved Shield Wall, and Improved Shield Bash - all terrible. Extremely minor enhancements for extremely valuable talent points.

Arms

Improved Thunderclap - Um yeah. Crappy damage + % of crappy damage = crappy damage.

Tactical Mastery should be the way the Warrior operates period imo. Restricting this to Warriors who dump 5 (or 6 for Anger Management) Talent points seems silly. Reward the Warriors who switch stances effectively in battle, don't force them to take Talents to do so.

Improved Hamstring - don't get this one. Add an attack slow or something, basic Hamstring already slows a mob plenty.

Mortal Strike and Sweeping Strikes totally baffle me. They give Arms/Battle warriors versions of Slam and Cleave, dilluting an already weak Fury/Berserk stance even further. I dislike both of these moves for that reason, but if they are staying, consider tweaking Sweeping Strikes to allow the Warrior to choose a second target for the strikes - this fits with the 'controlled combat' of the Arms warrior, and allows avoiding crowd controlled mobs with indiscriminate strikes (ala Cleave).

Fury

Poor, poor and bad. Fury and Berserk need a rework.

Improved Battle Shout should actually boost the effect - all the duration does is give you a tiny amount of extra Rage over every X minutes combat time. Not a good use of points.

Booming Voice - staggeringly poor. Too focused on a limited set of moves, amazingly weak bonus. If this boosted the effectiveness of EVERY shout by some amount (more attack power, more attack decrease, longer paralyzation, longer stun) then it might be worth taking.

Unbridled Wrath is strange - unless I'm missing something about the mechanics, the rage boost should be scaled to the speed of the weapon, otherwise this is more useful for fast dual wielders than it is for 2H users, which Fury (and Berserk) both focus on.

Improved Intimidating Shout - Using Intim Shout in instances is already dangerous (unless you prep by Hamstringing multiple mobs, not an easy thing to do), and having this boost the chances of paralysis but giving it a 75% chance of doing so is asking you to a) burn Talent points and b) roll the dice on your party in an instance. Not good.

Improved Slam/Cleave - as with Imp Taunt and Revenge this is just not good. Berserk Stance is already weak, now you have to dig down into the Fury tree to make Slam worth using, and to enhance Cleave (in some situations, not all - it is difficult to get full effectiveness out of Cleave, and remember, if you've got 4 mobs beating on you, you're taking 20% more from every one of those mobs)

Death Wish is one of the few moves in the whole Fury/Berserk mess that I like. Giving the player the option to go nuts and take more damage is good, forcing him to take 20% more is not.

Bloodthirst is not party friendly. While it is a hilarious talent solo (particularly against lower level enemies), it is not useful in an instance situation where the odds of you getting a killing blow are quite slim (and really, wth are you doing in an instance with a Fury tree specced this far down anyway, eh?). This needs to be reworked so it triggers if any mob you damaged dies.

Them's my opinions ;) I love the Warrior in World of Warcraft. A lot. Best Warrior in any MMO ever, and one of the most fun professions to play. My biggest disappointment is Berserk stance (and by extension Fury talents), the rest is just stuff that would be nice to see tweaked.
