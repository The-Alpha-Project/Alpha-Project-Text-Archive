 1. Hunters uncreative/badly designed:Suggestions | 9/24/2004 10:24:43 PM GMTDT
Hunters currently are a barebones missile class with pets that are pale shadows of warlock ones. This isn’t intended as a rant about how a class not fully implemented is currently underpowered (Though they are…) but the fact that there are signs that nothing creative is being done with the class as a whole. Some examples:

1)Raptor Strike/ Mongoose strike/Wing clip/Rapid Fire: The first two are generic abilities you wouldn’t be surprised seeing a rogue use in combat instead of a hunter. Wing clip = Hamstring named differently. Let me stop a second with Rapid fire. This flat out rushed or outright lazy design. It’s named exactly what it is, does nothing special and it doesn’t even greatly improve damage output.

2)Bite/Growl/cower/Mend Pet/Scare beasts: These are just versions of warlock and warlock pet abilities.

Hunters should have nothing in common with warlocks. A warlock bribes a demon with a soul to show up and do his dirty work. A hunter and his pet are friends and companions who should work together to achieve a common goal.

Why not better integrate the hunter and the pet in combat? For instance:

Mongoose strike currently works by acting as a free attack triggered by the hunter successfully dodging an attack...why not have it act as a chance for the pet to get a free hit in when the hunter dodges? Or even the reverse…the pet dodges an attack throwing the opponent off balance and letting the hunter get in a free hit. That would at least be something different than an ability that feels stolen from a rogue.

Raptor strike is a free melee attack and completely generic, something you’d expect to see warriors doing. Why not spice it up by making it a combination attack? For instance having it work as a focused attack by you and your pet on the same part of the mob, dealing increased damage.

Mention wing clip to a warrior and they’ll go “Oh yeah...we just call ours hamstring”. Perhaps remove this from hunters and give them something more class related. Instead give hunters a knock-back shotgun type ability, a powder only blast from the gun or a blunt arrow that knocks the opponent backwards, doing no damage but allowing you to fire off a concussion shot. This would leave hunters with a ability to get stuff off of them while not copying a warrior ability.

Rapid Fire: This ability was clearly simple to design and implement but it lacks any effort n a designers part....range attack haste….couldn’t this be something like “Emerald Visions: Hunters pet share it’s vision of the emerald dream with it’s master, allowing 40% haste, improved accuracy and adds random nature damage to the hunters range attack.”

Scare Beast: Please change this abilities name. You can think of something better than this……name it after the “Flight or Fight” response and change it such that it works by “Taunts beast increasing agro, increases run speed and fears it .” The argument being is the hunter first triggers a flight response but as soon as it wears off the animal returns to fight what scared it.

Pet abilities:
They currently operate exactly the way the warlock abilities do. Will all pet specials work in similar fashion? Why are they clickable abilities instead of something unique to the class?

Suggested Pet changes:
Change all pet abilities to proc’s instead of triggered and cap them to be able to use only three abilities at one time. Consider the pet as working with the hunter as opposed to a summoned demon doing what the warlock tells them to do. This would allow for slightly more powerful abilities (Since they would be offset by being outside of the owner’s direct control) and more novel setups.

For instance instead of taunt/bite/detaunt, you could remove the taunt from the pet bar and add in a “Chance to proc a backstab” “chance to proc detaunt” “random bite ability”.

Or even better “Nerve Bite: Chance to proc 5 second sleep” “Growl: proc taunt” “Tendon Slice:Procs Snare randomly” ability….and boom you have an offtanking pet that’s not a succubus or a void pet in disguise but would rival both for utility.

The point being it would allow players to mix and match pet abilities to get a specific task done, but there would be trade offs…..decision making the source of fun in all games. Also the loss of control would increase the sense that the pets are there fighting with you instead of being some mindless demon there doing exactly as you tell it. As an added benefit if any ability appears too powerful you could throw in negative modifiers, such as something in the nature of:

Focused Rage: Pet’s armor decreased 50%, Attack boosted 50% and can use it in combination with say a backstab proc ability and cower.

Talents could also be used to increase the hunter/pet combination, for instance abilities such as:
Faithful Companion: If the hunter drops below 20% health the pet enrages in an attempt to save you, it’s armor drops 50%, attack boosted 50%, loyalty increases each second and taunts at double normal effectiveness.

Empathic Bond: Pet and hunter work at a higher level of cooperation, each critical hit scored by the pet triggers a chance for the hunter to score an extra range attack.


To wrap this up, and get to my main concern, a copy of existing class abilities is a poor way to create a foundation for something new. What is intended to make hunter pets something more than warlock pets with different models? Will hunter melee abilities remain as they currently stand or will they be changed to something less generic?

At risk of appearing to respond to my own post, I have just a few more comments I think are worth mentioning:

Feed Pet:
What exactly is the point of this? Why do we have to feed the pet every half hour'ish?

A counter example: What if warrior were changed such that weapons grew less effective over time due to being worn dull from combat, thus causing warriors to have to sharpen their weapons every half hour?

The warrior class board would burst into flames and melt to the center of the earth, echo'ing up from the hole would be screams of why you added a design change that adds nothing to the game but an irritant.

Why then do we have to constantly feed the pet?
Is this supposed to somehow build a bond between me and the pet via the fact I have to give it food every hour or so? Is it intended to balance the pet's damage output on the theory we won't bother to feed it all the time and keep it at max? I can't imagine any hunter currently going around with a pet that's not maxed.

Traps:

This is honestly the only creative thing the hunters have at present and they are very cumbersome to use. Most of my groups travel very quickly through zones, if a warrior can run out and shoot a mob and instantly begin combat and keep the ball rolling they aren't going to be willing to watch a hunter setup a trap and then pull the mob over it to initiate combat. Would making them castable in combat throw things out of whack?

I am aware that if a hunter can just cast a trap on top of a mob in combat you might as well just be casting a standard dot or debuff on them, could you create a radious around a mob in which traps couldn't be set but can be triggered? To restate that better: Traps would be castable in combat but not within say a ten foot radious of creatures in combat. You could justify it by the fact you can't set a trap in plain view of your prey....


Pulling:

One of the key problems of hunters pulling (assuming we're intended as pullers) is the comparison to warriors with guns pulling. A hunter who pulls standard operating method is to find a mob, hunters mark, shoot it then get down on his or her knees and pray the warrior gets it off of them quickly while frantically clicking disengage.

It's very much more straight forward to have the warrior just pull the mob themselves and tank the mob from the get go.

Cheap fix: If your looking for a quick way to make the hunters better you could just make the warriors worse. Say by removing guns/bows from warriors leaving them thown weapons they could still pull with, only from a shorter range.

Warriors would crucify you but that'd go a long way to making the hunters range abilities something special and a consideration for pulling.

I think I just mentioned Disengage, that's an entire posts worth of topic right there, but let's look at that can of worms.

Disengage:
This ability is obviously designed to get the mob off of us after the initial pull but it seems insane. Why are we spending the first ten seconds of our fights trying to get out of combat?Why is this another melee ability on a range focused class?

Couldn't this be better done by removing this melee ability and adding an ability called, for example:

Prowlers Stealth: Mob unable to determine where and who fired the bullet/arrow, thus first range attack generates no aggro."

That would get the mob to the group since the hunter would have done say 1 point worth of aggro to get the mob running to him, it would then run over the trap, and the warrior could whack it one hit as it ran past and suddenly be tanking. This has got to be better than the current ridiculous method of franticly clicking disengage and weeping for a warriors help.

I hope some of that is worth reading, and gets passed along to a developer somewhere. I'm just a hardcore gamer throwing out ideas on a Friday afternoon. I think Blizzard could do much, much better.

 6. Re: Hunters uncreative/badly designed:Suggestions | 9/25/2004 6:38:23 AM GMTDT
This is an idea for pets I've had for a really, really long time... and something I hoped would eventually be in an MMORPG.

Advanced Pet AI, which I'll further bring up in a moment. Furion is totally barking up the -right- tree when he mentioned pets having procs and acting a bit more independantly. If you command a pet, it shouldn't be some natural ability.. it should be a trick! Something you trained it to do, not a natural ability that you unlocked. Growl, Cower, Swoop, all that junk, should be natural, randomly occuring things.

Here's a trick I'd love to see... if anyone remembers Final Fantasy 6, there was a Ninja named Shadow. He had a pet dog named Interceptor. And while any character could be Missed, or Evade/Block against damage... Interceptor would occasionally leap out infront of Shadow and take one for his owner! Now wouldn't that be a great proc? You could get real creative with the execution of it, too. The pet would take damage in place of the hunter, and temporarily taunt and grab aggro - even if the hunter had so much aggro the pet would have no chance of pulling the mob off, it could be a 'temporary' taunt that grabs aggro undisputedly for several seconds or so. Powerful? Sure. As a proc? Its classy.. as a command ability? Way too powerful, and a refresh would make it worthless.

Imagine a higher level, advanced version of this - which would trigger 'every' time a critical hit was scored on the hunter? If your pet was in range, the Hunter would receive a 'knockback' effect and take no damage from the critical. Effectively getting 'knocked' out of the way by his own pet, who suddenly leaps/charges/etc. into the Hunter's previous space, and grabs aggro. That sounds way too powerful... every time a critical hit is scored? Make it a one minute refresh proc, and cause the pet to take extra damage for recklessly throwing itself into the face of danger. Better yet, have it take no extra damage, or have no refresh, and cause it to 'delevel' one rank of loyalty.

Loyalty ranks and food add SO much scope to random pet abilities... its a whole new level of penalties. Having these 'major' procs and abilities that lower loyalty levels. Foods that buff the pet, or cause it to grow in different manners. Making it a deadlier killer, or a sturdier tank.

Imagine some pet based stuns, that only certain beasts could get. Beasts could 'clamp' onto a target, or something... the technology is out there, it can be animated and performed. As your wolf BITES down on the arm of a humanoid, and it shows them struggling to get the wolf off their arm for a few seconds, only to get a free attack in on the poor wolf, with some knockback effect.

On larger mobs, the wolf would start frantically running around the target's feet, barking and growling madly, darting in and out. Causing the beast or large mob to trip up, get knocked down, be stunned by 'panicking', have attack rate slowed from confusion. The ideas just go on and on... like I said! The hunter is a utility class, and him and his pets should represent that!

Now, the idea of learning abilities from one beast, and training them to another, is very neato. Unfortunately, that's just asking for trouble. Instead... a Hunter should be able to observe a pet and learn 'tactics' from the pet. Tactics he could then train to other pets... not abilities. Tactics would be a set form of AI, that included specific procs.

You could have tactics where the pet would automatically detect extra mobs being pulled during combat. And instead of sticking with the same mob, would immediatly run over to intercept the next mob, and the next, pulling all aggro onto itself. But -only- on mobs who have already had their threat range compromised and are in the middle of charging the hunter and his party members. These tactics might provide the pet with a buff to his HP and armor factor. And might even give him a proc that would Fear and Amnesia a target. Scaring the target away 'and' causing them to think twice about coming back. Then again, that could be an ability of that Tactic Set - which would drop the loyalty level of the pet by one level.

Of course, to avoid misuse of such a powerful ability, you would have to have a 'required loyalty level' just to use it. And certain Tactics all together would suddenly fail if that requirement was lost. The pet reverting to the basic commands that they would forever have: Attack, Follow, Stay, etc. I'd even say pets would always be in passive mode, and would 'learn' Defensive Mode and Aggressive mode at certain Loyalty Levels.

You could have Crowd Control Tactics, where the pet would taunt and evade, but never attack. Possibly wildly running about in circles, barking and making lots of noise, and generally being a nuisance. And causing all enemies in a certain AoE to have their attack rate reduced, and defense lowered. With a proc chance that might Knockdown a target.

I'm sure I could think of more and more ideas... Sentry and Skirmish Tactics sound like nice names for those... But the concept of having a 'fire and forget' pet, with advanced AI and limited 'commands' would be awesome. And, of course, the tactics could be more varied as well. After all, you could just have an old fashioned crocolisk that beats the junk out of things, with no tactics at all. Just following Attack/Follow/Stay and doing what it was told. Tactics would be something found on either specific creatures, and of higher level. And those could be learned and 'trained' to other pets.

Certain Tactical Sets would give certain buffs and procs. And certain creatures would have their own special abilities and procs, regardless of tactics. Nothing the Hunter can control, but things that play a part. Making certain beasts much more effective with certain tactic sets, than other ones.

Okay, enough ideas... ^.^
