 1. brief weapon buff analysis | 9/22/2004 5:13:32 AM GMTDT
Very simply the changes to our weapon buffs now mean the following for the highest level spell available

Rockbiter = +36 dps

Flametongue = +22.7 dps

and Windfury is dependent on the average base weapon damage. For it to be roughly equivalent to Rockbiter the weapon must average 100 points per swing.

The talented upgraded versions are of course

RB = +39.5 dps
FT = +24.75 dps

and the breakeven for Windfury for RB (enhanced) is 50 points per swing

Thus if you weren't going to go with the enhancement tree, then stick with Rockbiter. However the upgraded Windfury is better with higher damaging weapons.

On a related note, the ultimate spell in the enhancement line, Stormstrike is a mana waste. It takes 387 per cast and gives you 1 extra swing plus +20% nature damage for your next 2 casts. Well the most you could gain from the nature damage would be to cast earthshock twice, which would give you an additional 153.2 points of damage. Add in your weapon damage (most likely not more than 100) and you have a very expensive spell. At 253.2 is .65 damage per mana, a waste. It seems like the enhancement tree, as others have noted, is not maybe so good past Flurry

 5. Re: brief weapon buff analysis | 9/22/2004 6:58:14 PM GMTDT
I think the mana cost on Stormstrike is way too high. I believe it's only 240 mana, though, not 389.

In order to maximize efficiency you'd have to hit the mob with two high-damage spells. Unfortunately, with Lightning Shield so incredibly good, the mob is always going to take at least one LS hit and you only deal about 35 extra damage from that.

Basically stormstrike's main damage component is always going to be the extra swing, so your weapon has to hit for about 200 points of damage for it to be at all efficient.

This pretty much rules out anything but awesome two-handed weapons, which may be why that skill is a prerequisite.

At a minimum the "next two sources of nature damage" part needs to be removed and made something like "for the next 5/10 seconds the target takes 20% increased damage from nature spells and attacks."

But it should probably just become a "windfury on demand" and give two free attacks (sans added attack power). This would make it tolerable for one handed weapons doing at least 90-120 per hit, and pretty decent for two handers doing 150-180 per hit.

I think you're leaving out some important considerations...

1. Flametongue damage is unaffected by armor. Rockbiter damage isn't. This has a very substantial effect on the actual DPS. Mobs with meaningful armor values are extremely pervasive, mobs with fire resist are not. This is often the case in PvP, although it's far more difficult to predict.

2. Stormstrike doesn't share the same cooldown as the shocks. Therefore, Stormstrike gives you additional burst DPS that simply isn't even possible if you don't have Stormstrike. There are definitely times when it's extremely worthwhile to sacrifice mana efficiency for burst DPS (handling adds or just about all the time in PvP).
