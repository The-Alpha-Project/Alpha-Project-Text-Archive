 33. Re: How I think MS should be *nerfed* | 10/17/2004 8:46:43 PM GMTDT
The problem is *not* that MS works best with a slower, harder hitting weapon the problem is that there aren't enough talents that perform better with a faster weapon and that the slower hitting weapons have sufficient dps to make it worth using them over clearly better slow weapons (that is better in everything other than MS).

I can think of 2 abilities off the top of my head that can be used to more impact with a faster weapon. One is the mace stun ability. The more swings you get the more stuns you get. The other is HS where it adds a set damage amount to your weapon damage. The issue of course is that HS costs too much rage to be spammed on a very fast weapon. Still I like the idea of getting a very fast mace type weapon when tanking for the extra stuns.

Rather than Blizzard adding talents that improve all weapons equally, I would prefer for Blizzard to add more talents that improve each weapon type.

Example:
Slow weapons - MS
Fast weapons - stun proc, HS
DPS weapons - recklessness (it adds the most damage to the highest dps weapon), % to crit, extra swing
All weapons equal - rend, hamstring

In addition straight damage adds like sharpening stones give a bigger advantage to fast weapons than slow weapons.

Let us build characters around the type of weapons we want to use based on our preference not based on having one choice for what is best under all circumstances. Make slow MS weapons great for jousting or 7 second burst damage, but make sure that DPS weapons outperform MS weapons over say 30 seconds of solid fighting.

Etc, so on, so forth.
