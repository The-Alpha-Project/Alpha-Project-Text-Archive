 6. Re: Hunter professions? | 10/21/2004 11:05:20 AM GMTDT
Ive done both. I have 20 something hunter with Leather working and Skinning. I also Have a 33 hunter with engineering and minning.

I agree with the above posters in that the hunter is a huge money sink. I have developed a few strategies to counter this.

1. Everyone should do the three free professions first aid, fishing, cooking. And, start them asap in their career.

2. Find pets that eat fish and keep them. At higher levels, the meat you have to buy adds up and becomes costly. If your pet eats fish, this cost will be eliminated.

3. Make a mule to do Engineering and Leatherworking. Have your main do skinning and Mining. Your main will level up and be able to gather the resources your mule needs. This way you will have both profession sets, with a little hastle of course. You'll have to mail the resources to your mule and the products back to your main of course.

4. Make a map of mining resources for yourself and do a run through at the begging and end of each play session. This will more then be enough to get your engineering skill up and will also keep you in ammo.

5. Sell practically everything on the Auction House. After a few times using the auction house, you'll be able to figure out what sells and what doesnt. The deposit you will lose in the process will pay for itself 100 fold once you get good at using the AH. Many things sell for much much more to players via the AH then to NPC vendors. Some things that I was suprised to see sell well are: Cloth Stacks (all types), Food Stacks, Ammo, Low Level Gear, Smelted Ore, Herbs,Leather Stacks, etc etc.

6. Farm MOBS a few levels below you. Farming, in my view, is the best way to make loot and gain experience. However, it is extremely boring. 30 minutes of farming can seem like an eternity sometimes. So I made a rule, I farm the beginning of every level until my pet levels up. Then I will go questing or whatever. Try it, you'll see that it works nicely.

Thats about it on my ways to keep gold in your pocket and gear on your back as a hunter. Hope that helps.
