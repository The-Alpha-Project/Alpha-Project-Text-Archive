As far as the devs' conclusion on the role of the druid, I'm fine with that. I had hoped that we would be more versatile, but that is another arguement I won't delve into here. First off, the druid class is bar-none one of the most EXPENSIVE (aside from hunter) classes, due to the sheer number of spells/abilities we get for each of our forms. Now, combine this with the fact that each of our forms requires a different set of armor/stats to be considered useful (yes.. without said stats we would NOT be useful.. imagine a lvl 60 dire bear with all int/spirit gear trying to tank BRS -> which is highly unlikely anyway in the current setup but assuming one "could".. it would be impossible without the proper gear); Our bear form needs stamina/str, our cat str/agil and our caster form needs int/spirit.

So, like most of us have already posted (and will continue to ask for) is that the druid gets a more set-in-stone stat (1 or 2) that we should strive for. Having int/spirit translate into str/stam for bear or str/agil for cat would be a perfect solution imo. This would make it so that we do not need to have to worry about getting 2 extra sets of equipment depending on our role in the group for a given day, thus decreasing the already incredibly high cost of maintaining a druid, and also improving our role in groups (this last one has two meanings behind it which I shall explain): (1) We no longer would need extra equipment for the given situation, so if necessary, we can quickly shift between forms depending on the situation (which with the new change coming up where we will not be able to switch equipment in battle would be devestating to this). (2) We would no longer be battling over every single piece of leather/cloth that drops (thats right.. as it is right now, we need the same items that rogues, shamans, other druids, hunters and sometimes mages/priests need... this is another reason why ppl don't want us in a group).

So that is my suggestion. If nothing more, I would hope that u would at least take from this post that most druids feel that this is a fix that is ESSENTIAL for the druid class. I already mentioned in a different post that this class is pretty close to being done imo, but this (along with making us more unique so that we are actually *desired* for groups) is something I feel is ESSENTIAL for making the druid class balanced (and essential to making one WANT to play a druid).

I agree completely with Naruto about the stats idea. After reading through this entire thread, here is my evaulation of what improvements seem feasible and practical, including my own additions:

1) Stats need to be consolidated. Make +SPI gear/buffs add to AGI when in cat from, and add to STA in bear form. Likewise, +INT gear/buffs should translate into STR in both cases. This would make spirit the primary focus of a druid, unlike most other casters who focus on INT. SPI would actually make sense to some degree, for the more spirit we have, the more powerful a form of nature we should become.

2) Talent revamp. Our end talents are inadequate.

2A) Make the final restoration talent a Rez buff; 30 minute duration, 5 second cast, moderate mana cost, revives the target to 1 health and 1 mana with 3 minutes of rez penalty 5 seconds after they die.

2B) Make 2 final feral talents, sort of branched out from alike abilities between cat and bear form. Final abilities should be a passive +DPS(25%ish) for cat form, where as bear form should gain block/dodge(15%ish, or something close to that of a warrior). These abilities would require 25 pts in the tree, with prereqs for each. Obtaining both would require near 60 talent points, not allowing for specialization in ballance.

3B) Turn hurricane into a cyclone - the ability centaurs use on you. Have it only work on one target at a time, makes the target invulnerable, and disables them for 20 seconds (rank one, mind you this would be around level 40, perhaps a duration of 35 seconds at level 60). This would add a CC dimention to a druid who specialized in Balance. An alternative to this (as much as I hate CoH) could be hurricane as it is in City of Heroes; this skill has a 50% chance to knock down targets within 10 yards of the caster for 3 seconds, pulsing at 5 second intervals while draining mana. Adjust the mana cost to compensate for underpower/overpoweredness, or the % chance of success.

3) Adding some unique abilities to the feral forms. Something desireable, yet not needed, and not a freakin' clone of warriors/rogues ( I understand that this might have been done simply for times sake). This might sound sort of cheezy, but perhaps a CC ability in bear form "Wrestle". Basically it would occupy the monster and the bear, dealing no damage. Give cat form some more group-oriented abilities, like a +AGI/STR growl, 60 energy, lasts 10 seconds for all groupmates.

To sum it up, give the druid more options to specialize, making them on par with the rogue, healer, warrior, or nuker - but in a unique way.

 55. Re: The Class Role | 10/29/2004 12:14:45 AM GMTDT
When Blizzard says that Druids have "the most powerful buff of all the classes," I was expecting Druids to have a wide array of beneficial spells to confer to other players. As it stands, there are only two spells, and apparently only one buff that people care about in the higher levels.

If Blizzard professes to avoid "carbon-copy" solutions to class balances, then giving Druids other kind of buffing spells would be a logical way to distinguish Priests from Druids. At present, the effectiveness of Thorns scales poorly at the higher levels, and Mark of the Wild is the only other buff that Druids have. Maybe Druids could be given buffs that improve accuracy, attack power, or speed. We've seen how Druids of the Claw from Warcraft 3 have shouts that buff all nearby friendlies, and such a spell would certainly remove the tedium that results from having to cast on an entire group.

Similarly, if Druids are to have a unique support role that can be distinguished from a Priest, more effective debuffs should be added to the Druid's repertoire. In its current form, Faire Fire is relegated to being used in specialized situations; primarily to prevent someone from cloaking. Its armor decrease only confers an extra point or two of damage per hit on a target. It might make more sense to either increase the armor reduction or scale it according to a percentage of the target's armor, with a cap to restrain its maximum effect.

Without Flash Heal or Resurrection, and wielding a smaller mana capacity, Druids are an indisputably weaker healer than Priests. However, instead of focusing on making Druids equivalent to a Priest's healer powers, I think that Druids could find their own niche if Blizzard focused on improving Druid buffs.

While people will debate as to the cause of the Druid's weaknesses, empirical results reveal the overbearing problem: If every class was available for a five person group, the Druid is usually the last choice. When a class' ability to join a group is dependent on the rarity of another class, it demonstrates the fact that the class lacks something appealing to the players. Eventually, the rarity will be gone, and either two things will happen: 1.) You'll be a clone of another class, or 2.) You won't be wanted.

I'm not saying that what I have mentioned above is the answer to the Druidic Question. It is just my perspective on how the situation can be alleviated without injuring other classes. However, something needs to be done before Druids become a bunch of Emerald Dreaming lore-pieces again.

--Garfield
