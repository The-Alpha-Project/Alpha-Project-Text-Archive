 1. More druid ideas for the dev team | 7/30/2004 6:50:29 PM GMTDT
I know that a lot of this has been pointed out before so I’m going to simply list some suggestions that I have had in mind after playing my druid more.

After reaching lv30 I’ve found the most useful forms are Aquatic and Travel. The only thing I use cat for was dash before I got travel and then sneak. I wonder if forms as alternate combat types should simply be dropped all together and just have forms be more of utilities like travel and aquatic. Perhaps ambient that draws no agro, thus allowing the druid to “really” sneak up to something, transform back into druid form and launch a sneak attack.


I have seen many druid and druid like NPC’s that seem to have a range of very effective spells those us, as players do not. Such as:

The geomancers in Razor Fen Kraul have an entangling roots that is AOE and does not brake. This would be a great addition. At least fix roots so it always holds and perhaps add the AOE ability at a higher level.

Other NPC’s have cyclone. This was a druid ability from Warcraft 3, would make sense. Also would give the druid the ability to isolate 1 opponent when mobbed. All the other classes have some way of doing this, sap, ice chains, fear etc.

I really really really think the druid should have resurrection.

Some buff/aurora ideas

Shield of Leaves: Absorbs damage for x seconds

Maybe some ability that transfers damage to the owner that replenishes health.

Summon treeant. The keeper of the grove in Ashenvale have this. I think this would be a great spell. The treeants would not be pets, they would just be temporarily summoned, and they would function off their own AI and would basically just be creeps. Perhaps their main purpose would be to draw agro, which would be effective as then the druid would be free to regen mana and heal.

Something needs to be done about mana, either regen or amount or something. My temporary solution has been to always find a high level mage before I start my play session and get 3 stacks of 20 drinks. With these I’m able to minimize downtime and actually be effective in a group as a healer. In large battles as the sole healer I am always casting everything and then sitting down to drink and then casting everything again and mostly have been successful.

The other idea is to just drop bear and cat and ad one combative form, treeform. It would use mana and health just like the druid so there would be no weirdness with that. Some ability ideas are as follows:

Sticky Sap: Slows movement and attack and spell casting rate

Draining vines: ensnares the target transferring x amount of health

Bark skin: increases armor by a significant amount

Healing rain: similar to tranquility, not as powerful but do not have to channel

Branch bash: stuns all nearby opponents for X seconds

Branch swipe: knocks down all nearby opponents disrupting spells

Holding Roots: Ensnares 1 opponent in roots, disabling all actions until released. Either has a long effect time, or when the druid is holding someone, they are then also rooted in place; the druid would still be able to attack and use spells though.
