I have seen plenty of math posts, but I haven't seen any that were thorough enough to convince me. In your calculations, there are a few things you've ignored. The first is that you've used weapon damage instead of actual (pre-armor) damage. My dagger has a damage range of 23 - 43, but the damage on my character sheet is shown as 62 - 83, so the average damage is actually 72.5, not 33. Thus, for weapon damage, you should consider that damage.

To redo your calculations:

Sinister strike: 72.5 + 33
Gouge: 32 damage
Backstab: 90 damage + 1.5x 72.5

SS = 105.5/40 energy = 2.6375 dmg/energy
gouge/bs = 230.75/65 energy = 3.55 dmg/energy

Even without energy regen during gouge:
gouge/bs = 230.75/105 energy = 2.1976 dmg/energy

Ok, so gouge/bs WITH energy regen is still more effective, BUT there are a few more things to consider. First off, when you take the 65 energy figure for gouge + backstab, you're not really considering time. You still need 105 energy to do gouge/bs, even if you get 1/3 of it while the enemy is stunned. With 2.6375 dmg/energy vs. 2.1976 dmg/energy, Sinister Strike really does do more damage over time. With Gouge/BS you're trading damage over time for 3 or 4 seconds of not being hit.

Try it. Attack an enemy your level with nothing but SS and see how long it takes to drop. Then time yourself with G + BS. I have. It always takes me longer to G + BS, but I usually save a bit on health.


Another thing is that with the gouge/bs combo, you can't have Deadly Poison on your weapon, which is the best DPS poison in our arsenal (at my level, anyway). Now, you can use instant poison, which is still good, but you'll definitely lose DPS. At my level, instant poison has a 20% chance to do an average of 50 damage. Deadly Poison has a 30% chance to do 52 damage over 12 seconds, stacking up to 5 times. The specific DPS difference between these of course depends on weapon speed combined with what specials you use, so I'm not going to bother with an exact calculation. However, it should be obvious you lose DPS with Instant Poison.

Additionally, you accumulate combo points faster with the SSs than the G + BS combo, which provides some other benefit (depending on how you use them, but usually DPS).


Now, please don't think I'm saying the G + BS combo is useless. It's not. It's very good for fighting casters because Kick is usually not enough to disrupt spells, and you don't always want to use your combo points on Kidney Shot, so as long as you have to Gouge, you might as well Backstab too. If you actually take the Backstab talents, then G + BS becomes a better strategy. In groups, when someone else maintains enemy attention, you can Backstab without the Gouge, which will provide higher DPS than SS. Of course, you will gain combo points more slowly, but probably not enough for it to make SS better.


ARMOR: As far as I've seen, the formula for armor soak is not publicly available, but it is not fully percentage based. Therefore, the SS strike approach will lose more damage to armor than G + BS. I don't know the exact amount, but from my experience, I would guess that it's not enough to make SS less efficient from a damage over time perspecitve.


As for the macro, it does work. the /unequip commands will unequip any item by name (assuming it's on you to begin with), and the /equip command will put the weapon into your main hand first, and your off hand if the main hand is full. As long as you /equip in the right order, it will work. I used this macro for a while, but I switch manually now because that can't be messed up, unlike the macro which will mess up when you lag.
