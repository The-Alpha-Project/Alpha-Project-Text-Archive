I made this post on a general forums thread, and coping it to hear:

Lets compare Priest, and Shaman Healing..

FAST CAST HEALS

Shaman Lesser Healing Wave - 357 Mana
1.5 sec cast
40 yd range
Heals a friendly target for 915 to 1021.

Priest Flash Heal - 380 Mana
1.5 sec cast
40 yd range
Heals a friendly target for 812 to 958.

Druid Regrowth - 880 Mana
2 sec cast
40 yd range
Heals a friendly target for 1003 to 1119 and another 1064 over 21 sec.

Paladin Holy Light - 550 Mana
2.5 sec cast
40 yd range
Heals a friendly target for 1207 to 1349.


Seeing the Quick Heals, its obvious Shaman > Priest, Druids actually have the best heal, but extreme mana cost. Paladins heal just sucks.


SLOW HEALS:

Shaman Healing Wave - 578 Mana
3 sec cast
40 yd range
Heals a friendly target for 1504 to 1717.

Druid Healing Touch - 790 Mana
3.5 sec cast
40 yd range
Heals a friendly target for 1890 to 2230.

Priest Greater Heal - 960 Mana
4 sec cast
40 yd range
A slow casting spell that heals a single target for 2396 to 2674.

Yet Again the shaman greater heal > priest. Lets ignore the 1 second cast difference, and look at Hit points healed per mana.
Taking the average amount of hit points, and dividing it by the amount of mana used we get:

Shaman - 2.8HPs per mana
Priest - 2.7HPs per mana
Druid - 2.6Hps per mana

So the shamans greater heal is more mana efficient, and 1 second faster to cast, PLUS they can add 5 talent points, and make it a 2.5s cast.

So its quite clear a Shaman is better at pure healing then a Priest is.
People will say priests have more mana? Well Krog has pretty much put that argument to rest, at level 58 he claims to have 8500 mana... my priest at 60 (thought i admit I dont have the best gear) has 5200

They both have alot of other utility spells, and that is harder to compared, but I believe they are about equal with all the utility spell.

Lets add on to that, a shaman also has much better healing talents then a Priest. All one has to do is look at the tree and compare.

You're forgetting a couple things as well though Raitha. Shamans have totems that mimic a couple of those spells you just mentioned. A smart Shaman will also be able to protect those totems from being killed too.

Healing Stream at lv 60 will heal each party member in a 20 yard radius for 35 health every 5 seconds for 1 minute, this means that each member of the party will be healed for 420 health over the course of that minute without putting any points into Talents to improve the totem. If the Shaman puts 5 points towards improving the healing stream totem, they now heal 504 health over that minute. This is also at the cost of 125 Mana compared to 1030 the Priest spends to cast a PoH for 939 to 991 health in 3 seconds. Even with the 20% reduction in mana cost to PoH, that is still 824 Mana for twice the total Health healed.

So, a Improved HS Totem has an efficiency of 4.03 Health/Mana compared to the Improved PoH which has an efficiency of 1.17 Health/Mana (965 average heal) [note, this is on a per character in party basis, with 5 people in both parties, all within the AoEs of both spells, HS Totem wins.]

Then there is the Stoneskin Totem which reduces damage by 27 per hit for 1 minute and cost 260 mana to cast. Say that a person gets hit 20 times over the course of one minute, that means they take 540 less damage. Now, add in to the factor that everyone in the AoE of the totem gets this reduction. HW:S 10 absorbs 942 damage and costs over twice as much mana to cast. Again, the Shaman wins the contest without even accounting for talents. With talents thrown in, the Stoneskin Totem goes up to 32.4 damage lessened per blow while the HW:S can be cast again in 15 seconds versus 30 wihtout talent improvement.

Simply put, the mathematics show that the Priest is not where they should be in comparison to the other classes that specialize in healing. As such, the Priest needs to be buffed up.

With the original values at left and talents on the far right, we first notice a staggering difference--the healers without talents do not stand a chance.

The first thing to look at is the ratio of the heals. After talents, this stacks up as follows: Druids, 3.42; Priests, 3.23; Shaman, 2.79. The actual value of the shaman number would actually be higher, since they will always have 5% more mana from talents. Nor have we included any shaman totems, which can return more mana than they take to cast, or reduce damage at a greater ratio than it can be healed.

Druids win this category. They will be able to heal the most hitpoints with the least amount of mana.

Over a long period of time, a priest's spiritual healing talent would even out the ratios so that druids and priests are nearly equal. However, let us demonstrate how foolish this randomness is. If a priest manages to squeeze out 10 of his biggest heals (and indeed, he could only manage this by some priestly miracle) he would need an average of 10% crit rate in order for the odds to be in favor of one of them being a crit.

Shaman's big heal is smaller and faster and so it procs the crit abilities more often. This finishes closing the apparent gap between the shaman's ratio and the other classes'.

The druid's advantage grows again when we consider omen of clarity. This is their version of spiritual healing's randomness... only it's incredibly more powerful, proccing frequently and offering them multiple free heals per fight. In the final analysis, the druid wins out because he can simply heal many more hitpoints with the same amount of mana. Druids can clear all the neccessary abilities--that is, all of the ones equivalent to priest talents and anything else that's good--from the healing tree and still get omen of clarity.

Since players can downgrade equipment, and since all classes gain the same effect from stats, a paladin will always have more mana than a shaman, who will always have more mana than a druid, who will always have more mana than a priest. This is because they can all downgrade all the way down to cloth if it means upgraded int or spirit. The higher teir armor offers more equipment options. That a class would have to sacrifice some AC if they take a lower teir of armor is of no consequence since it's the option that matters.

Why do shamans have such efficient heals? The answer is probably that without them the typical shaman would not be able to act as an effective secondary healer. Efficient heals and healing talents enable the typical shaman to fill this role (where a typical shaman has fairly balanced stats).

As noted in this thread when a shaman builds an int/spirit focussed character, he then becomes a more effective healer than a priest (in theory, shamans get less mana per point of int so I'm still not 100% convinced).

Assuming shamans can be better healers than priests, it is definitely the case that the healer shaman will become a popular build, and push priests away from their intended role of best healer.

What can blizzard do then? The obvious thing to do is a general nerf to shaman healing abilities BUT let shamans have one super efficient healing spell, with a significant recast timer (i.e. 10s).

This would enable the low-mana shaman to do spot healing without destroying his meagre mana pool. However, it would mean the int/spirit focussed shaman would be an inferior healer to a priest, since in the primary healer role he would need to depend on low-efficiency spells while waiting for his high-efficiency spell to refresh.

Really paladins should be structured the same way, with a highly efficient heal that can be used from time to time, and less efficient heals otherwise.
