 5. Re: Rogue stuns... | 9/13/2004 11:52:25 PM GMTDT
There's two different possible situations here--one where you are surprised by the rogue (from stealth or otherwise) and the other where you are not surprised. In the event the rogue has succeeded at sneaking up on you (and assuming you're a cloth wearer at even level), your odds of surviving are pretty low. In the event you are not surprised, then you should have a decent shot at defeating the rogue (imho). The thing that's going to give you the best shot at that is a net-o-matic. It is the bane of melee classes in general. If you surprise the rogue, I'd expect they'd lose the vast majority of the time. Most of my encounters work out that way as such:

If I surprise even level:
Mage -> I win 80%
Priest -> I win 90%
Rogue -> I win 80%
Warrior -> I win 15%
The other classes I don't honestly run into enough to say.

If I'm surprised by an even level:
Mage -> I win 10%
Priest -> I win 15%
Rogue -> I win 5%
Warrior -> I win 0%

Now, If there's no surprise to either side:
Mage -> I win 30%
Priest -> I win 60%
Rogue -> I win 50%
Warrior -> I win 5%

The main reason I lose to mages is net-o-matic or good use of nova. Priests don't seem to be using net-o-matic as much. Warriors are very difficult to deal with, even when I get the jump on 'em because of their high armor and HP. Even when I open strong, they have plenty left to fight with and can easily fear me so they can charge+hammy+ms, rinse and repeat. Even when not surprised, the second I'm in charge range it's usually over. Honestly, this situation works fine for me. I think that rogues shouldn't be that hot vs high AC/high HP targets and they should be better vs low AC/low HP targets.

As far as the stuns going through priest shield, my presumption was that the shield blocks damage, not effects. I believe you can be nova'd despite, be hit with the net-o-matic, charge stunned or feared throught the shield because they are all "effects". I'd expect you can be sapped through the shield too, although I've never tried it myself.

Regarding, "where's the fun in getting killed in such a way that you have no chance to even fight back." Well, I know all about that from mages and warriors. I figure that it's a great tactic to use and when I have the chance to do it I do. Of course, when I do it, it's more about high burst DPS openers and some stun effects followed by a quick finish. Rogues aren't built for drawn out fights. If you get much of a chance to fight back, we're gonna lose most of the time. :D
