 41. Re: Upgrading Daggers, and Gripe about BOP (W | 10/15/2004 4:39:47 PM GMTDT
Silverblade and Stonevault are indeed drops from those instances, but considering the chance is something less than 0.1% for one to drop, you would have to kill approximately one thousand enemies in the instance to be sure to get one. Estimating that 100-150 mobs are killed that are of the level to drop those in the respective instances during a run, thats 7-10 runs to guarantee that it drops, then take into account that at least one other person will most likely want to roll on it.

Hypnotic is about as close as you can get to a caster-intended dagger, +8 Int, +3 Spt, meaning nearly every caster (warlocks, mages, priests, shamans) feel they have to roll on it, not to mention the stats are all but useless to a rogue.

My point is, if you are trying to get a blue dagger, there aren't enough that you can see a path toward that doesn't rely almost entirely upon chance. The only ones that I've known I would be able to get if I just worked hard enough on getting it were the quested ones like Blackmenace and Lifeforce.

BoE blue daggers now all have a random drop chance off of either all of the mobs of a certain level in a certain instance, or all of the mobs of a certain level excluding instances, which means that getting one is very, very unlikely and can only be relied upon if you enjoy disappointment. Examples:

Widowmaker lvl 42 req, drops randomly with a less than 0.1% chance from mid-high 40's mobs in zones like tanaris, feralas, swamp of sorrows, stranglethorn, and the hinterlands. I, personally, have completed nearly all of the quests in all of those zones (at least one thousand kills) and have never seen this drop for me or my group, never seen anyone celebrate the drop of one, and I've also never seen anyone in my guild have one drop.

Stealthblade lvl 44 req, drops randomly with a less than 0.1% chance from most mobs in the Temple of Atal'Hakkar: I'm just now getting into this instance and have only done 2 runs through, but I have yet to see this drop for me, my group, my raid, my guild, or my friends.

BoP blue daggers are almost always 10-20% drop chance off of a specific boss in a specific instance, example:

Coldrage lvl 38 req, one of the few choices to accompany a Blackmenace in the late 30's or early 40's, is a 15% drop chance from Amnennar the ColdBringer in Razorfen Downs, a rare drop from the last boss of an instance that it is rare to see anyone ever go to.

So many items go to waste because of BoP, perhaps there should be a looting cap level on instance items and they should be changed back to BoE. Clarification: Looting cap level to prevent players well above the requirements of the item from soloing the mob and looting solely for the purposes of selling.

Maybe certain BoP items should be changed to BoE and tagged as "priceless" ;) becoming unable to be sold in the AH, limiting their purpose (somewhat since they could still be manually traded) to helping others and equipping alts.

I miss the days of being able to pass down my old weapons and armor to my friends and guildmates, BoP and BoE have such a stranglehold on the flow of items that are actually worth passing down that I have literally had no opportunities to help out anyone beyond the occasional lending or gifting of gold.

 44. Re: Upgrading Daggers, and Gripe about BOP (W | 10/15/2004 10:23:15 PM GMTDT
Chalon, you are correct that Widowmaker, or most any other BoE blue dagger for that matter has an equal drop chance to almost any other blue BoE weapon of the same respective level. However, there are many more swords, maces, axes, etcetera available to drop, meaning that the overall chance for a BoE blue sword to drop is much greater than for a BoE blue dagger to drop.

I have heard many different solutions to the necessity of item decay. One of the most interesting I've heard is a combination of Soulbinding, Use-based item decay, and Trade Decay.

Essentially, most items would be BoE and BoP as they are now, however, BoE would still be tradeable when Soulbound, but at a penalty to the potential item durability, example:

Player A trades BoE Dagger with 100/100 durability to Player B, the Dagger would be traded but would lose some of its potential durability from the trade making it 80/80.

Each time it is traded the item loses 20% of its original durability meaning that after 5 trades it will be destroyed.

The system could involve tradeskills as well, for instance instead of simply being tradeable at a penalty a BoE dagger that is soulbound could be modified by a smith to become BoE again at a cost of durability and perhaps with some reagent(s).

It could also be made independant of durability by placing a trade limit stat on the weapon, listing how many more times it can be traded before being destroyed.
