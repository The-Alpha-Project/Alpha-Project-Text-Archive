 1. Bliz read. Warrior problem, long. | 10/14/2004 8:43:37 PM GMTDT
There are several very serious problems with Warriors currently and since I haven’t done a write up since alpha I thought this would be a good time to address the things that have been worrying me. I will be using terms that may not be familiar to everyone here. I will use RtD to refer to Rage to Damage, this is the amount of damage that is caused per point of rage spent for an ability. For example Rend Rank 7 causes 196 dmg over 21 seconds for a cost of 10 rage, so the RtD of Rend 7 is 19.6.

Mortal Strike’s design is horribly flawed. As it stands MS obsoletes about 90% of the weapon selection available to warriors. Since MS is set to a flat rage cost and reuse timer it becomes impractical to use anything except whichever weapon has the highest high end dmg. I currently have 3 different weapons in my bags with dps within a .1 spread of each other. I will link my weapon selection.

http://www.thottbot.com/?i=18877
http://www.thottbot.com/?i=18320
http://www.thottbot.com/?i=18956

Unfortunately because of the way MS functions I would be castrating my effective dps by using anything other then the polearm. I will explain the problem in more detail for those who don’t understand the problem. MS is 20 rage with a 6 second reuse and does 220% weapon dmg. Lets take my fastest and slowest weapon and do a break down.

Polearm 151-227 dmg 3.5 dly. 54.0 dps
Sword 99-149 dmg 2.3 dly. 53.9 dps

These two weapons are equal in all regards, we will leave the procs out of the equation and just look at the actual design flaw here. Since rage build is directly correlated to your dps both weapons will generate the same amount of rage within any given period of time. In a 12 second period both weapons will cause 646.8 dmg. However when MS is factored in the numbers become very skewed.

Each MS with the polearm will yield 415.8 dmg, 2 MS’s in a 12 second period will net 831.6 dmg. RtD 20.79. This will also make the effective dps on the polearm 123.3

Each MS with the sword will yield 272.8 dmg, 2 MS’s in a 12 second period will net 545.6 dmg. RtD 13.64. This will also make the effective dps of the sword 99.3

So over the course of 1 minute here is what we have.
The polearm will net 7398 dmg, 4158 from MS.
The sword will net 5958 dmg, 2728 from MS.
Considering these weapons are supposed to be “equivlant” this is a very very serious discrepancy.

Now that the raw numbers are laid out, here is my suggestion on how to fix the problem. Change the reuse on MS to be twice the weapon dly and the rage cost to be 6 times weapon dly. Lets look at what the numbers would look like over the course of 1 minute if the following changes were implemented.

The polearm will net 6859 dmg, 3619 from MS. This makes the effective dps 114.31 with a RtD of 20.1

The sword will net 6791 dmg, 3557 from MS. This makes the effective dps 113.18 with a RtD of 19.76


The next problem is the way that Strike functions. The problem here is that since Strike takes the place of your next attack you are paying MUCH more then the 15 rage listed. Rank 8 Strike does an additional 128 dmg, RtD of the top Strike is 8.53. Now I don’t know the exact formula for rage generation so I will use hypothetical numbers to demonstrate the problem.

Weapon A) dmg 200-300, 3sec dly. 83.3 dps
Weapon B) dmg 100-150, 1.5sec dly. 83.3 dps

Let us assume that both weapons will generate 60 rage every 12 seconds so 5 rage per second.

Weapon A will generate 15 rage per attack
Weapon B will generate 7.5 rage per attack

Next lets assume that Strike is used every time I have 15 rage. Over the course of 1 minute here is how the numbers would play out.

Weapon A will Strike on every other attack. Average dmg of Weap A is 250 per hit and 378 per strike. Every 1 attack generates enough rage to Strike on the next attack. Since Strike replaces the attack there is no rage generation for that attack, effectively making each Strike cost 25 rage. 25 rage for 128 dmg leaves us with a RtD of 5.12. Weapon A will cause 6280, 3780 from Strikes. We are left with an effective dps of 104.6

Weapon B will Strike on every 3rd attack. Average dmg of Weapon B is 125 and 253 per Strike. 13 Strikes and 27 regular attacks. The effective rage cost of Strike for Weap B is 15+7.5 = 22.5. 22.5 rage for 128 dmg gives us RtD of 5.68. Weapon B will cause 6664 dmg, 3289 from Strikes. We are left with an effective dps of 110 and have 7.5 rage left over.

My suggestion to fix this is simple. The bonus dmg from Strike is effected by armor anyways so it doesn’t make a damn bit of difference if the dmg is attached to the Strike. So simply make Strike an instant ability. Flat 15 rage for 128 dmg. It would still be used for the same thing its used for now (when you have too much rage and need something to spend it on ) but you could use it without crippling your rage generation. An alternate suggestion, which would make it worth replacing your next attack, is if Strike attacks ignored armor. Then you could justify that 22.5 and 25 rage cost for the ability if it allowed an entire attack to bypass armor.
