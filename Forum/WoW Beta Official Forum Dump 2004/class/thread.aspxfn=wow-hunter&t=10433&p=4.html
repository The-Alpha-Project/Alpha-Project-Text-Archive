Q u o t e:
the role isn't best left to the MT for many reasons


In Everquest, yes. An effective puller in Everquest has the ability to prune mobs before the pull, and has the ability to mitigate/completely avoid the damage given to him.

But as it stands, the MT is the best candidate for the job. No class in WoW currently can accomplish both these things on a consistent basis. So, if you have the warrior at the front, you get him rage-building from the beginning, an group agro so that AEs go through.

Although, I take that back slightly. The best person for the job will always be the most competent one. In cases of equal competency, the MT wins.

With the current content, pulling in the everquest sense is not a necessity. The combat mechanics are built around having a heavy concentration of mobs per "pull" (aka, multiple classes with varying degrees of crowd control), and the mobs are balanced (read, don't hit for 500-1000 a pop) in such a way that these fights are feasible.

Q u o t e:
It's not eq, however there will be raid content and there is raid-able content.


If you elevate the role of the puller in a raid, you will almost certainly destroy the rest of the fun of the raid. This is because pulling in the everquest sense is a slippery slope: once you give a single class the ability to mitigate content to the degree that a monk can in Everquest, then the raid content becomes the monk's and not the raid's. This is 5 years of pulling experience as an everquest monk speaking.

The natural alternative is to give the "pulling" class in wow lesser control over their pulls. That way, more classes are involved with the dungeon content. Wow's currently on the other end of the spectrum; the concept of pulling is split evenly among so many classes that there is no "perfect" puller.

I'm extremely skeptical that a balance between the two sides will be found such that hunters aren't both controllers of the end-game content and are deemed "necessary" in groups because of their pulling abilities. So I'd rather see our pulling ability turn into "good crowd control ala rouges and mages" and our DPS or utility be the focus.

I'll start from the beginning.

The warrior can use bows/guns to pull mobs. These mobs come in either chains, or one at a time. Sometimes, you can pull only one of a group depending on how well you time the shot so that the mob is struck when standing AWAY from the group.

The pulled mob(s) will then come running over to the offender and begin attacking him/her. At this point, it is absolutely ideal if the offender is the group tank because the tank was made to handle this damage while the other classes either heal or pick off these mobs one at a time.

The reason why this role doesn't fall to the hunter is:

1. Having the main tank also be the puller saves time by bringing aggroed mobs to the appropriate player right away.

2. Having the MT be the puller frees up a spot for an additional healer, mage, etc.

3. There's nothing particularly special the hunter can do to pull mobs. The hunter, warrior and rogue all fire the same shot at the mob with the same distance limitations. The ability to prune mobs one at a time from a group comes from timing, not from any special ability the hunter has.

Thus, I don't see why groups will need/want a hunter, and as it stands, they don't.

This is all I'm saying: that the hunter doesn't have a group role because the role designated for it is in fact better handled by a class which has some armor. Maybe, in EQ, this is different due to different ways you can make feign death behave to draw over specific mobs, but in WoW, they all go back to spawn at the same time.
