As a 58 warrior, i am now expirmenting with dual weild and the fury line. I am loving dual weild, however there are problems with it that i don't think have been adressed before because it is rare to see a dual weilding warrior.

Dual Weild
1) Miss rate on the offhand seems alot higher than mainhand.

2)Damage is reduced by half on offhands?

Ok so you miss more and if you do hit you hit for less. What is the realy incentive to make you want to dual weild then you could just plop on a 2hander and slam someone for 1k? Not to mention there are NO talents for dual weildling specifically. There is a 1hander talent (defense tree +dmg 1hands) There is 2hand specialization, (+dmg). If there is going to be trees that allow you to specialize in 1hand/sheild combo and 2hands, it would be nice to see a +chance to hit and +dmg to offhand talent in the fury line for dual weilders.

Sword/sheild
As we all know the sword sheild combo seems like an awaesome idea to deflect tons of damage. Well...ok but whats the point in taking damage if you have nothing to allow you to dish any? Sure revenge is alright...and sunder armor is kewl. But this combo is useless in pvp because you will get snared and kited, and you wont be able to do anything. How to fix? Not sure...just know people are having trouble with this combo.

Two Hands
It seems to me that all the high end warriors are nice pre-set cookie cutter warriors. Everyone goes 2hands and mortal strike or slam talents. Why? Because it does ridiculous amounts of damage with instant hits. But whats dumb is that the other trees such as defensive and dual weild in pvp seems to be ignored and so there in order to be a sucessful pvp warrior you have to go with a 2hander. I don't know about you other wars but i see a big lack of individuality in our class.

Charge
The biggest thing we are all having trouble with is this nerf. Some are learning to adapt, but should it have been so brutal? I mean yes, i now chain charging is annoying, dumb, and "cheap". But allowed us to close distances we could have never been able to before. IMO the stun was the biggest problem. And i know people were also complaining about our huge rage buildling.

Suggested fix
I think that when you are out of combat, the charge is normal, with a nice 3 second stun (yes 3seconds) because as of now the stun is stupid they run away by the time i reach them. When you are in combnat, you should be able to charge, but there is no stun but rather a daze because i mean...if your running straight at someone you should be able to knock them up a bit. Your rage from that charge should also be reduced. IMO that fixes all the problems, and allows us to close distances and have some chance at hitting our range foes.

Why charge in combat?
What other way do we have to close a distance? Hamstring...ok that is only really effective if you get the +% talent...which some of us cant afford. And we have to be able to reach them before we can hamstring them...and let alone build up some rage first. If we are in combat we lose.
Let me put this in perspective, when i fight a mage this is what happens. If i am lucky enough to get off of a charge, i hamstring him. Unfortunatly this is useless because he has ICE sheild...so he snares me without even casting anything. So he just blinks away, and sheeps me, then he will frost bolt me, I wakeup, try to run to him...but im snared and he fireballs me for 800-1000 damage. Then fireblast for what 500+ dmg? Then if i happen to finally reach him, what does he do? He frost novas! And runs back and finishes me or kites me more with frostbolt. This is how it is with EVERY caster...

Druids cant root you for what 30+ seconds?

Mages have frost armor, frost nova, frost bolt, cone of cold, polymorph, and blink.

Warlocks arent so much a problem because they only have fear which is NOT instant cast so we can actually pummel them for a chance of killing them.

Priest this gets pretty ridiculous if you are fighitng a smart priest. Whats stupid is they end up chain fearing warriors even if you charge in on them. Sure you can hamstring but what help is it when they chain fear you whenever it is up for almost no mana? I think their recycle on this spell needs a big increase. Hell our warrior fear is 5mins. Not to mention they can instant cast sheild, instant cast renew...fear you...instant dot you and nuke u some more. Only chance you really have is to use recklessness to become immune to fear but recycle is 30mins.

I know pvp isn't supposed to be purely balance for 1v1s, but are we meant to take no casters, and get 2shotted by rogues? Maybe nerf some 2hand damage skills, and allow us more chances to close distances. Other classes get tons of other ways to keep you away, why do we get almost no way to close gaps?

New Skills
Im hoping sometime soon you guys will start implimenting the new +50 spells and such :). Personally im hoping to see the berserker tree have more skills. Im also hoping to see whirlwind added (as it was planned). It would be nice to see this as a berserker tree since it just seems to fit there so well. And maybe more gap closing/dmg skills would be nice to see :)

I would like to see fury be put on the map as a viable way to spec. I am hoping that the upper lvl skills, as well as some tweaking to the higher end talents (i.e. change bloodthirst to something useful) could make this happen, because how it is currently... fury is nothing more than a few skills to supplement your arms spec.

 5. Re: In tune for next patch? Suggestions | 10/6/2004 3:44:47 AM GMTDT
On dual wield:
I'm not sure how this works out after damage is modified for strength, attack power, mob armor, etc, but for raw weapon damage, 2H seems to be roughly 150% of 1H damage, maybe a bit less since this patch. If the only penalty for dual wield is the 50% damage reduction, that puts the total dps on both weapons right at 150% of 1H damage, maybe a bit less because it's much harder to find a good offhand weapon. Seems to me that the ridiculous miss rate is an unneeded penalty... maybe double miss rate would be OK, but 5x is insane.

2H: The problem is the attacks that aren't tied to attack speed... i.e. Slam and MS, the two skills that warriors have that are actually capable of dealing decent damage. You get an MS every 6 seconds no matter what, so why not get as much damage as possible on it? And with a point in imp slam and a 3.7 speed weapon you can attack 3 times as fast and barely even lose rage if you alternate 2 slams 1 attack (up until you crit a slam at least). I don't think Slam needs a nerfing, and MS isn't anywhere near as overpowered as everyone claims it is (though it could use a tweaking), but there needs to be some similarly viable skill that's usable on fast weapons. I hate turning down really awesome weapons with like 5 more DPS than my Rockpounder just because they're faster... there's no logic to making fast weapons so gimped.

Possible fixes:
Add more or improve procs on critical hits or other flat % on hit procs like mace mastery. These are the only reason to dual wield or use a fast weapon of any kind now aside from slightly smoother rage gain. Deep Wounds depends on damage per hit and so is equally useful with all weapon speeds, and I might be wrong on this, but isn't Flurry a set number of swings that go faster, and not a period of time over which you swing faster? Either way it increases DPS by the same factor, but lasts longer for slow weapons and thus is actually better for them... (I think, this logic seems a bit odd to me, can someone tell me if I'm just insane?)

Removing or greatly reducing the hit penalty for dual wield would probably move it up in usefulness to equal Slamming. Right now even with the extra hits you get in, you're missing so much that the actual gain in number of crits per time is much smaller than it would seem at first.

Adding an ability (or modifying MS) so that it scales based on attack speed would help. MS's cooldown could be changed to, say, 3x weapon swing time. Or its damage could be changed to scale based on weapon speed. Making it not an instant attack would help fix this problem but it takes a lot away from the uniqueness of the skill. Same goes with making it add a flat number of damage.

Faster attack speed gives a slight advantage with Heroic Strike due to being able to use it more often for the same damage increase. That doesn't apply to Cleave, because Cleave effectively adds cleave bonus x 2 + weapon damage again, not just a flat number, so it's much more effective still with a slow weapon. Using either of these abilities repeatedly, however, will totally burn through your rage in no time at all, while Slammers and MSers keep whacking away spending like 3 rage per super fast, super damaging swing. I'd suggest toning down the amount of rage generated by instant attacks, and add rage generation for next swing attacks. Even if the whole rage for instant swing thing is a bug, I still think it should stay... but it needs to be consistent and work on crits and on next-swing attacks too, and it probably needs to be less effective.

Sword/shield... I have no idea how to make this viable outside tanking, other than massively buffing shields or adding some really good shield-required defense talent that, say, blocks 25% of projectile spells. Making Revenge actually do decent damage might work... maybe add 25-50% of weapon damage on revenge talent instead of the broken stun, so that protection warriors can actually deal enough damage to solo without mind-numbing boredom, but still significantly less damage than arms or fury.

Charge:
As many have said, it makes completely no sense that Blizz buffed charge up in one patch, and then nerfed it back down WAY BELOW how it was before they buffed it. Charge as it is now is essentially broken, though still useful. The main problem is the complete inability to use it if aggroed on or tagged once in PvP. The other major problem is the fact that a resisted charge doesn't move the warrior. How exactly does a warrior try to rush really fast at his opponent, but because of the opponent being able to defend against it, somehow is magically back where he started? Certainly no stun on a resisted charge, I'll take no rage, I'll take putting me a short distance out of melee range instead of right there (only if it's resisted), but not moving at all is just silly.

Charge should be usable anytime before the warrior uses a combat ability. Inner Rage, Bloodrage, and changing stances shouldn't count as combat abilities unless necessary for balance.
Resisting Charge should still move the warrior.

Berserker Stance:
Definitely could use a few skills to fill up that bar. A full Fury spec berserker is surprisingly viable as it is now, but there's a bit missing. There's no interrupt ability; I suggest a shout, zerk only, that silences (probably AoE) for like 5 seconds, has a 10 second cooldown, and must be used BEFORE the target starts casting. Adds a bit of flavor over the essentially identical pummel/bash. Another possibility is a mocking blow-like taunt that acts as a reaction skill for, say, a party member being crit. It would be an actual taunt and quite powerful, due to situational use and the user's lower defense. Berserk also could use a rage generation skill; I suggest a skill that increases (doubles?) all rage generated by attacks and being attacked for a short time (10 sec?) with a cooldown of 2-3 minutes... this and Inner Rage would be able to be alternated between fights.
