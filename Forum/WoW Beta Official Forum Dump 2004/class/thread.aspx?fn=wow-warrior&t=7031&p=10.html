Thanks very much for offering to listen to our grievances with the shiny Blue Text.

I shall address my thoughts on Charge, since I'm not yet familiar with Mortal Strike or Taunt's previous incarnations.

While I understand that Charge may not have been originally intended to be used while in combat at all, I feel that design and mindset is fundamentally flawed. I feel the fix that was offered was not proper, and harms Warriors in their soloing, grouping, and PvP in a grievous imbalance.

I shall address the details with the following examples.

1) I am wandering around solo. I see two mobs, A & B, standing near each other, with mob C in proximity. I know I can pull A & B and avoid C by using my gun. But I also need Rage. I have two choices: a) Shoot my gun at A, get Instant Rage, and fight or b) Charge A, and risk aggro'ing C. Not a huge situation, because the instant rage can help out.

But assuming I have a battle afterwards where I face a similar pulling situation, do I need to sit around waiting for the cooldown on the rage to disappear so I can use it again? Or should I save Rage during the fight and hope that by saving the rage I can still win that fight and have enough left over to help out in place of Charge?

I am now hampered in my ability to solo.


2) I am in a group in Gnomeregan. There is another Warrior tanking, I am supporting dps and tank in Battle Stance. We are fighting three Troggs, and the Tank loses aggro due to lack of Rage for a Taunt. A Trogg runs after our Healer and proceeds to beat him down. I can now no longer use Charge to quickly intercept that Trogg and save our healer.

I can hope my weapon is enough to gain aggro (instead of the brief stun and weapon) to get him off our healer, or I can use a Mocking Blow, which assumes I have the Rage for it and assuming the cooldown is expired.

I am now hampered in my ability to group.


3) I'm wandering the Wetlands, when I spy a Warlock from the Horde. I move to get a Charge in, but feel the whammy of Fear and/or Seduction. I am running away from the Warlock now with no control for X seconds, being blasted at from afar. I am in combat, so when Fear expires I cannot close the distance soon enough and can be taking heavy damage and risk further control loss.

Or, I spy a Mage. I get a Charge off, lucky me, but after the brief stun, the Mage Frost Novas me. I am no frozen and suffering Chilled. The Mage tosses a Frostbolt, and runs away, kiting me the entire battle with Nova, Frostbolt, and the Chilled effect, using Blink to evade if a crucial moment occurs.

Or I spy a Rogue. Rogue Gouges me, Backstab, SS, and Evis, doing heavy damage. Rogue also slow poisons me, and runs off to build Energy. I have no way to get in close because of the slowness, and if I'm lucky I had enough Rage to Hamstring him.

Or perhaps I'm chasing after someone I wish to engage in PvP with. Suddenly a Level 10 Wolf bites me because I got too close. Suddenly I have no chance to Charge the enemy player because I am now in Combat.

Repeat ad infinitum. I am now hampered in my ability to PvP.


I feel the change to Charge failed to take into account these situations and focused perhaps on the original and somewhat faulty basis of Charge.

A better solution, that is fair in all occasions, for both Warrior and opponent, is to have Charge act like First Aid. When I Charge someone (in or out of combat), the opponent now has been "Recently Charged... Cannot be Charged again" for X seconds. The stun timer on Charge is also 1-2 seconds max with a 20-30 second cooldown.

Now, I can pull and charge an enemy solo, I can save my Healer, I have a chance against my PvP foes without being overpowered and cheesy with stun/hamstring juggling, and I am no longer rendered helpless at a distance because I aggro'd a nearby mob.

It is a simple solution that I feel is fair in most regards and leaves Warriors viable without making them overly strong.
