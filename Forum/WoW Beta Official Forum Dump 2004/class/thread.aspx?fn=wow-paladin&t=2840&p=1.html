 1. Turn charger into that missing fun part | 7/30/2004 7:13:11 AM GMTDT
Alright, I can't wait for the charger, as mostly I just can't save up enough money for a normal horse, but there's a few other things that I'm thinking.

1. the word charger when dealing with a horse is sort of a way of saying warhorse.

2. Look at the "jouster's" equipment set, it's plate and it has quite a few bonuses for int and spr, this means that only Paladins really want to be "jousters"

3. Essentially everyone is complaining that the Paladin is a boring class to play and that it's missing something to make it fun.

4. Warcraft 2 (Which I consider the pinnacle of the Warcraft franchise)

I know they've said that mounted combat isn't planned at this time, and at least not for release. However I think this is the absolutely perfect place to give to the Paladin that thing that everyone says is missing. Give us a tab kind of like the warrior stances or druid animal forms, for mounted combat, and to go with that a new set of skills that can only be used while on horseback (some kind of charge like move, a knockdown attack) while making other skills unusable for balance purposes (I'm thinking maybe even holy and crusader strike [don't have the manuverability to get the right swing]) with just a couple others that can be used in both forms (holy light? [the image of the noble paladin casting the healing spell while sitting on the back of his horse just seems to cool]).

Lastly, the paladin is the thing that seems closest to the chivalrous, and noble horseman, and most likely to do this sort of combat (also remember Warcraft 2 as precedence). If this is done, could I even possibly suggest that the paladins get it at level 30 or something (even if it runs slower because it is armoured) because I think that if a character is a member of the order of paladins, the organization may almost be willing to supply them before other classes can resonably get them (although shamans and druids still get their perks earlier).

I'll admit that I like the paladin class, and would like to see them get a lot cooler, but I think that this is something that could fix this problem that most people acknowledge is there (the boring one), while still having the ability to be balanced if done right (like everything else)
