 101. Re: The Class Role | 10/26/2004 9:38:21 PM GMTDT
The problem with a number of the hunter's abilities is that they're incredibly useful in short spans of time or specific situations, but the rest of the time are garbage.

In contrast, most of the other classes have skills which are slightly to incredibly useful most of the time and only won't work in specific situations.

The hunter seems like a "sacrifice" class, and I'm not sure why this is. Gain 20% movement speed but be stunned on hit, feign death but come up with res sickness, slows the target for 10 seconds but needs to be delivered at melee range, etc.

Many of our abilities seem to come with a big "but" thrown in there. I don't understand the foundation of this. The rogue's eviscerate delivers a whollup of damage with combo points, yet the rogue does not become stunned after delivering it. The mage doesn't lose life when casting pyroblast, and a pally's aura doesn't cause those aided by it to be stunned when hit.

If the hunter was capable of dealing insane amounts of damage or could root a target for long periods of time, the many drawbacks of the abilities would make sense as they would balance the hunter's otherwise incredible arsenal. However, the former isn't the case and thus we end up with a semi-masochistic class.
