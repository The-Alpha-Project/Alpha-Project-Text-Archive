 2. Re: Stealth for hunters? | 10/17/2004 5:38:55 AM GMTDT
Stalking: Hunter activates stalking on his target, the target shows up on the hunter's minimap and the hunter gains stealth versus the target in a similar manner to rogue's lvl 1 stealth (60% move speed, easily detected by higher level enemies, and more easily detected the closer the hunter gets to his target, any attacks made by the hunter or on the hunter remove the effect as well as any casting done by the hunter). However, the stealth is only versus that target, meaning that you are not stealth from any other players or mobs, also, if the hunter is within the line of sight of the target an icon is placed on the target's buff bar called "stalked" so that players being stalked know that they are being stalked if the hunter is not careful to stay out of the player's LoS through terrain usage or maintaining a position behind the target, even if they do not actually detect the hunter's exact location.

 6. Re: Stealth for hunters? | 10/17/2004 5:34:08 PM GMTDT
If anything I think that camoflage should be the opposite of stealth. While rogues that stealth get up close to you to attack you without being detected, Hunters should have the ability to set traps and set up a shot at a target without being detected. This can happen by decreasing the draw distance for hunters that are camoflaged...

Camoflage:

60% normal movement speed.
Draw distance of hunter is reduced, so that target cannot see Hunter until the hunter is within a range of 35 - 30 yards, or until a hunter performs a hostile action. This allows the hunter to take advantage of his surroundings, set up traps for CC, Damage, etc. and get a first shot in. Once that happens, you lose the element of surprise, and combat proceeds as normal. There should be a flag that lasts for a short amount of time in which the target is immune to further camoflage as they are actively aware of your presence. This would prevent frosttrap/camo kiting.

This allows you the chance to set your traps outside of combat without some seeing you do it. It allows you to get the first shot off, provides you with time to set up a plan of attack using the environment that you are in to your advantage.(Finding higher ground or a area that is hard to get to to allow you to shoot freely without getting immediately charged.)

Of course its my opinion that camoflage is primarily needed for PVP more so that PVE. At this point, mobs agro radius is extremely small in comparison to our shooting range with is 35 yards. Thus we have plenty of time and space to set up traps, get off a first shot, use the terrain to our advantage. PVE doesn't seem to be a problem for a hunter. It's definitely not for me. But PvP is a different story. Everytime I have come in contact with the Horde, I have been ganked. With the exception of the one time I ran into a Horde Hunter and we both just went our separate ways. Having this camoflage skill would help me to stay out of sight to avoid PvP combat or the set up for PvP combat. That I think would be very useful to the class.

Please feel free to add your thoughts on the matter.
