 1. Warlocks, Pets and Balance. | 10/20/2004 7:55:27 AM GMTDT
Of our three current Pets, only one of them (the Voidwalker) is truly a strong fit for a Warlock. In groups Warlocks tend to deploy an Imp or Succubus; this is possible because other group members fill the Voidwalker's solo role..

More than any other Pet, the Voidwalker complements the Warlock offensively and defensively; both holding aggro and doing a small amount of damage in his own right. Our spells are Damage over Time, and his design gives us the time needed for those spells to land.

There is a flaw in this approach, however. The Voidwalker, in fact, is designed around the assumption that the Warlock is a weak mage that needs a tank in order to live long enough to kill things.

The Succubus does reasonable damage, but has significantly fewer hitpoints then the Voidwalker and less armor as well. Her "Lash of Pain" attack isn't very impressive; the "Torment" of VW has far more utility.

The Imp has a great group buff in Blood Pact, but his very low HP (still under 1000 at Lvl 54) and complete reliance on mana for attacking make him a weak option, even before his lousy AI is factored in.

The Solution: Increase Interplay Between Pet and Warlock.:

We've seen baby steps toward this solution with spells like Dark Pact, but the Imp (ironically) has the right idea.

When you summon an Imp, he provides a large group stamina buff. This cycle of providing buffs and unique character capabilities THROUGH the Pet should be essential to the Warlock's character.

Instead, we have ONE Pet that essentially does this, and he's the Pet no one uses solo (and sometimes not in groups) thanks to bone-headed AI.

Succubus has no equivalent ability; Voidwalker is an enabler for our mage-side.

The solution to this situation is to concentrate power in a Pet and less in a Warlock. There are multiple advantages to this situation, including:

Pets can be tuned for particular abilities.: Instead of having to rely on the Voidwalker (or assign a VW task to a non-VW), Warlocks can gain a unique set of capabilities that boost them in an area complimentary to that of the new pet.

Warlocks do not become overpowered.: Because each Pet unique abilities (and no more than one Pet can be active at one time), there's no fear of Warlocks becoming overpowered by synergistic reactions between Pets.

The problem here is not that the Warlock's class defining features are not-in-place, but that our current system of talents and damage really works best with the VW as opposed to any other Pet. The solution, as I see it, is not to make Pets more like the VW--its to adapt the Warlock + Pet combination to fit a wider variety of playing styles.

 3. Re: Warlocks, Pets and Balance. | 10/20/2004 9:10:19 PM GMTDT
I have not played EQ, so I cannot speak to this.

I've seen a lot of posts about how "Warlock balance isn't done because we need new Pets."

I think this (at the moment) is a substantial misnomer, because it assumes we use the Pets we have. I'd say the vast majority of Warlocks use the VW the vast majority of the time. Succubus does have a following and / or specific uses. But the Imp gets pulled out in groups or for specific situations.

The appearance of a Felhunter or an Infernal is not, in and of itself, going to fix this situation. At best, it might replace the Voidwalker as the "uber" Pet, but this only transfers the problem, it doesn't fix it.

I'd like to see each Warlock+Pet combination focus on a different area or skill. Right now, our Pet skills basically focus on increasing damage, HP, and mana.

If you think about it, that basically makes each Pet "more" like the Voidwalker.

The Succubus' current Lash of Pain has a 12 second cooldown base, can only be used when the Succ does NOT have aggro, and does 99 base damage. This is at Lvl 60.

That's not an attractive capability, and the prospect of spending 5-7 talent points to turn it into something useful isn't real nice either. When mobs have over 3000 HP at Lvl 60, why is the Succubus still hitting for 99 every 12 seconds as a "special" attack?

If the Succubus is going to be so fragile, than she should bring more abilities to the table that directly enhance Warlock offensive damage and potentially life drain for herself. "Vampiric Lash" that returns damage she deals in the form of life drain sounds a lot more effective than the current Lash of Pain.

If going Succubus over Voidwalker meant trading defensive capability for a *real* increase to offensive capability, more Warlocks might use her.
