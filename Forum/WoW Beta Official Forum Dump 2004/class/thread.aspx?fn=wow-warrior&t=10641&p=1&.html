 6. Re: Fury warrior build, is there any point? | 10/17/2004 12:09:11 AM GMTDT
I'd say a split Fury/Protection build is by far the best choice for someone who's going to be tanking alot, but wants to be pretty good solo too. Most people seem to just spec Arms/Fury for solo/PvP and respec full Protection or Prot/something for instances, but if you're looking for a more retail-viable build or just don't like running to Thrall's chamber or wherever every time you want to tank, a Fury/Prot build is great.

Fury skills, in general, work much better for tanking than Arms skills. Improved Battle Shout, Improved Demoralizing Shout, Piercing Howl, Improved Inner Rage, Blood Craze, and Improved Challenging Shout are all useful tanking, and Enrage is a nice little bonus too. I skip Blood Craze in order to afford Death Wish and Cleave for non-tanking time. At least one point in Slam is pretty much necessary if you want to solo in Berserk effectively.

The useful Arms tanking talents, BTW, are: Tactical Mastery (maybe), Deflection, Anger Management, and weapon spec. Much less added utility there, except for Tactical which isn't really going to be useful that often if you're main tank.

The main flaw with this build is that it is absolutely, totally horrible in small PvP fights. It's not that bad in mass raids, though. Mainly this is because Slam requires you to hold still and has problems hitting moving targets sometimes, and your only other damage skill is Cleave, which is AoE. All the fear immunity is nice, though.
