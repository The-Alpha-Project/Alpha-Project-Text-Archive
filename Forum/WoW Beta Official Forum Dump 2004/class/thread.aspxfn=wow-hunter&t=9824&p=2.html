The key areas that should be focused on to scale properly:

Abilities: Arcane/Raptor/Multi-shot/etc. are not scaled properly. For their mana cost past 40 they are effectively useless. Concussive Shot is useless past lvl 1.
Talents: Still need 'em
Equipment: Guns/Bows/Crossbows. There either need to be more of them, more of them that are hunter-only, or less people that can use them. Competition for bows between hunters is bad enough, having to compete with other classes is horrible.
Associated Costs: I'm one of those people that is sick in the head when it comes to grinding. I will sit there and fish for an hour, just to get the skill points. I'll hunt skinnable mobs just to craft armor that I can't sell to anyone, again for the skill points. I cook everything I can find so that I can learn the next new recipe. At this point I can use everything I come across to craft items that I would normally be buying from a vendor. With all of that I have never had more than 4 gold to my name, and that was last night after Gnomeragon. You know what I did? I finished buying all of my lvl 32 skills, picked up Growl 4, and bought a new scope for my crossbow. I had to hearthstone back to SS because I couldn't afford the griffon ride. Maintenance costs for Hunters is ridiculous, and definitely needs to be tweaked. When there are pets I won't tame because I can't gather the type of food they eat, meaning I can't afford to feed them... That is a shining example of a class crippling issue.

I will say that you were right, the group I was in last night could have picked any class and still made it through the dungeon. What I was trying to convey is that after three failed attempts with widely varied groups it became obvious that the classes you pick are inconsequential. Even when my only job was to jump on the bombs to keep them from hitting the warrior.
