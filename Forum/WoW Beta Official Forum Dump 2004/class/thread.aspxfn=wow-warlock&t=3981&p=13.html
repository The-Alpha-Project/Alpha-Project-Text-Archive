The warlock does not have a good way with dealing with most of the 5 magic classes. It also has no dispel, so that if someone has shadow resistance, there is no way to get rid of it. Since shadow magic is the warlock's mainstay, you are pretty screwed, since there are not many fire spells to draw on.

By contrast, look at my priest; I get holy and shadow protection (+50, +75, +100 etc. resistance), that makes these very hard to land on me, and do little damage when they do.

Suppose my opponent has a holy or shadow resistance buff up, or potion? No problem at all; Dispel Magic gets rid of those.

For frost magic (which does not do much damage) I simply Dispel Magic on the effect when I am frost nova'd.

Nature damage is generally the weakest of all 5 types (in PvP anyway). That leaves a priest's vulnerability to fire magic, which is the most damaging in PvP. However, priests can counter mages pretty well.

Shamans, meanwhile, get Fire, Nature, and Frost resistance totems that they can whip out, depending on who they are fighting. They can also Purge buffs (or use Lightning Shock).

Mages get a fire and frost ward, and a dispel curse.

Warlocks use shadow + to a small extent fire, and should therefore get some sort of fire protection, since it seems most classes in the game get some sort of protection against the type of attacks they themselves use.
