 107. Re: Paladin Questions for the Developers | 10/15/2004 4:27:44 AM GMTDT
Q u o t e:
I in no way advocate the addition of ranged attacks to the Paladin class.


Why not? I used to agree with posts such as this. Now, however, I think that the lack of ranged pulling is a source of many problems.

It slows down exp for the paladin. Because if they are solo you have to pull the mob to a safe spot so that runners won't cause adds. So, you have 3-4 seconds extra of running in toward a mob to pull it and then backing out as well. Or, if you prefer not to waste that time you are left in a position where you charge mobs and run a very high risk of adds. FInally, if we are to be tanks it is fairly important to get pull aggro. Sure, you can pull without ranged but it slows down groups a bit if you do. Therefore, a group with a paladin as the tank will nearly always end up in the mage/roge/hunter pulling. Then the paladin has to play catchup.

We don't NEED ranged attack. Should we have it? Yes. It's not imbalancing whatsoever. If you want to stick with role play type paladins make it so we still can't use ranged weapons but we pull with some sort of debuff or dispell.

I really see absolutely no reason whatsoever for paladins to not have ranged. If you want to argue that we don't need a ranged attack you can say the same thing for rogues and warriors. So, need is not a valid argument.

I will still play a paladin (assuming talents are good) whether or not we get some form of ranged pulling. However, I think about 50% of the complaints about the monotony of a paladin is mainly because we can not pull well. Pulling in a mmorpg is a big part of the game. Being able to range pull would add a whole new dimension to how one could play a paladin (not to mention increased aggro..yay).

I swear, decisions like this that are stated emphatically at the beginning of development tend to limit a game. I don't think anyone really remembers why they decided paladins shouldn't get ranged (unless you want to pull the role playing card). All they remember is that they said, "No."

Sometimes you need to take a look at the aspect of the game and determine whether or not it would detract from or add to the gaming experience.

Blizzard has a great game here and I'll be playing it no matter what. However, I think they are being pretty shortsighted where issues such as this are concerned.

I'll give up my seal of freedom for a ranged. Give up self seal of protection for a ranged. I'll even give up my mana costing horse for a ranged. Heck, I'll give up all 3 of those. I'll give up the stacking effect on crusader strike too (since it doesn't stack).

That being said, I can see why being able to damage from afar would be slightly overpowered for a plate class that can heal, IF it was a big nuke like exorcism. However, if you made it some minor debuff or just one of your run of the mill rifles, bows, crossbows, etc. it would not be overpowered. It would just be good gameplay (I'm in favor of a ranged debuff or a very minor/high mana cost nuke say 100DD at lvl 60, costs 250 mana).

So, we're not the only class that doesn't need ranged abilities. When you come here with something less generic than "need" or "role playing" issue then I might be convinced. Until then, tweak the paladin abilities a bit and give it ranged.

 116. Re: Paladin Questions for the Developers | 10/16/2004 9:13:28 AM GMTDT
1) Will be seeing any auras being moved to seals?
In groups or solo, I primarily use only Devotion or Resistance.
Wisdom's benefits are really insignificant to classes with high mana yield. It's something I only use during downtimes.
Retribution is very conditional. I've only used it a couple times against duel wield mobs. For group use, it's not something that can be used as a primary aura.
Personally, I feel Might is useless. The Trade Off between DMG Reduction from Devo and the Increased Dmg from Might heavily favors Devo every time.
Of course, this is assuming that you only have 1 paladin in the group.
Retribution, Might and Wisdom. I feel are better suited as seals versus auras.

2) Will Paladins get Higher DPS?
In all my runs of BRD, the combination that has the most problem at 7 ghosts is 2 paladins. I'm sure this might be surprising to some especially considering that we are supposed to be anti-undead. Nevertheless, our DPS is surprisingly low and because 7 ghosts is a timed event, every 5 man group that had 2 paladins has failed 7 ghosts. Exorcism, Holy Strike + Crusader Strike isn't enough. Considering that our DPS is tied to our mana. The moment we run out of mana, our dps is insignificant. In a 5 man group for any of the high instances, you rarely see more than 1 paladin. One rarely sees the benefits of multiple paladins (i.e. 2 auras 2 seals. etc etc). Most of our damage comes from Holy Strike and thanks to changes to our hit ratio since Beta 2, we do significantly less damage from missing. I'd be willing to sacrifice Divine Shield and Seal of Protection durations for higher dps

3) Will there be Seal of Salvation changes?
Because there are many high level instances that include AE areas, it's not uncommon to see multiple mages in groups or raid groups. Currently, paladins can only cast Salvation on one target at a time. Even though there is some overlap from Salvation duration v. cooldown, it's troublesome to be unable to cast Salvation on two targets at once.

4) Will Seal Duration Times Be Increased?
A paladin requires a lot of micromanagement. Far more than some of other classes I've played: Hunter, Mage, Priest. This is mainly due to the extremely short cast times on seals. In fact, it has become so bothersome that I've ended making macros for every seal. Nevertheless, I would seal duration to be increased so that I dont have to recast every fight or multiple times during a boss fight.

5) Will we be seeing more attacks?
Unlike our melee counterparts, we are extremely limited in offensive combat. Most of our spells are seals or heals. While these may be good for groups, they are not necessarily good for soloing. The biggest complaint that I've seen from Paladins and yet the complaint that is never addressed is the lackluster paladin combat. I think I had more attacks as a paladin in EQ than I do in WoW. It's simply boring to solo. The other issue is since HOly Strike can be dodged/parried/missed/resisted, Paladins miss out a huge part of their dps if they are missed.
