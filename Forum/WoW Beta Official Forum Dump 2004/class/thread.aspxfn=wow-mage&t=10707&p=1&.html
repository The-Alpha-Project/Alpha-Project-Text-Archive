I have soloed almost the entire way to 56. Probably 6 of my levels have come from grouping. Here is my level 60 spec:

Arcane Talents (15 points)

Improved Arcane Missiles - 5/5 points.

Arcane Concentration - 5/5 points

Improved Arcane Explosion - 5/5 points


Frost Talents (36 points)


Improved Frostbolt - 5/5 points

Winter's Chill - 3/3 points

Ice Shards - 5/5 points

Improved Frost Nova - 3/3 points

Cold Snap - 1/1 point

Shatter - 5/5 points

Arctic Reach - 2/2 points

Frost Channeling - 3/3 points

Ice Block - 1/1 point

Improved Cone of Cold - 2/3 points

Frostbite - 5/5 points

Ice Barrier - 1/1 point
-------------------------------
I solo everything the same. I cast frostbolt over and over until I get a clear cast then I use arcane missle. You have a great chance to freeze stuff and then a 50% chance to crit for double damage. If something gets in my face I still use Arcane Missle to finish it off even if I don't have a clear cast buff.

I took instant Arcane Explosion for when I am in group or if I want to farm things low level. Ice block and Ice barrier will save your butt in a pinch.

Note that I didn't take perma-frost because if I'm casting frostbolt over and over then who cares if it lasts longer. I refresh it every time I hit them. I also didn't take improved Blizzard because it's a channel spell. No matter how slow the MOBs run if one of them resists or isn't hit and gets on me then the entire spell is waisted. However, if I get a clear cast buff and I'm in a group I won't hisitate to use Blizzard even on single MOB.

May not be the "uber" way to spec but I'm really happy with it.

 11. Re: Current best talent spec for PvE soloing? | 10/21/2004 3:09:28 AM GMTDT
I find that the frost line gives me the least amount of down time. The reduced mana cost of frost, slowed mob speed, and protection spells allow me to fight maybe 3-4 mobs before I need to rest. (...If those mobs are within ~ +3 of my level)

I was doing fire only for a long time, but didn't like the mana usage on that line. I then tested arcane only for a few levels and still didn't like the mana usage there.

Next I tried arcane/fire with an emphasis in arcane. I liked this build a lot. Clear casting, instant AE, PoM, and evocation made it quite fun to play. However, I still didn't like the down time of this build. I think the regen rate for mana drinks are a bit too slow.

So next I went full frost. I had tried full frost previously a couple pushes ago and didn't like the killling power then (probably didn't have enough talent points for it to work well). With talents in frost, I can get off 3-4 frostbolts before a mob even gets to me. The reduced mana talent in the frost line also helps a lot. Depending on what level spells you have, frostbolt is about 1/2 the mana cost as fireball while doing 3/4 the damage. Although combat lasts longer, I prefer the ability to fight more mobs before resting. Frost does have the drawback of being weaker against ranged and caster mobs, but you can switch to other spells when attacking those mobs. I mainly use frostbolt, nova, fireblast and scorch in this line.

I also like ice barrier (shield against xxx damage...including DoT unlike mana shield).
Casting ice barrier, then cold snap when ice barrier absorbs all it can, then ice barrier again, pretty much doubles the amount of damage you can take at that time. Plus you can move on to mana shield if you still need shielding.

On the other hand, I have not found much use for Ice Block.

I'm just starting to put points in frostbite now, and that seems like a nice bonus...almost like clear casting.

One other thing I noticed is that I seem to crit more often with frost (I do have points in Shatter, but I crit more when mobs are not frozen).

So frost is good against melee, but weak against casters. In PvE, it's mostly melee, so for this reason I'd go with frost.

Once I get more talent points I will try frost+arcane. I really like clear casting, and evocation. I'll also try going back to fire/arcane.

1 more benefit of frost in PvE.

In groups, if you pull a melee mob, it will target you and move very slowly toward you while other members of your group attack it without getting harmed. If the mob has too many HP, then this should not be done, but generally this should work against elite mobs around or below your level.

So you end up with a group that hardly gets hurt. They just need to wait for you to drink/eat every few mobs.
