 1. Why PvP sucks for druids. | 7/27/2004 3:17:19 AM GMTDT
Druids really need some work on PvP because they really stink at right right now. Right now our only role in PvP right now is crowd control and healer... If you choose to moonfire every single enemy you see when in PvP it leaves you with ZERO MANA pretty quickly and since thats the most efficient pk spell you got, well thats all you got...

If you choose to go healer status, you pretty much only have enough for 3-5 good heals before your out of mana since healing spells take so dang much of it. Rejuv is a good way to keep your allies alive, but other than that, healing touch casting time is unreasonable for PvP, and Regrowth has a 2 second cast time for all ranks, but only heals them for a small faction of their max health and the rest is eventually regained. Both healing touch and regrowth have such high mana costs, a druid can barly keep his party alive in a PvP situation, and trying to keep people out of the party is pretty much impossible since a druid could not afford all that mana.

If you choose to go shapeshift status, you lose your main buffs, most of your mana, and the ability to escape in travel form assuming you are level 30. And since any rogue or warrior could easily own any of your forms, it would be pointless anyway.

If you choose to fight in regular druid status, your left with the option of either moonfire or wrath. If your honestly going to moonfire every enemy player you see, you would be out of mana only damaging each of them only by a very small faction of their health. Wrath is a decent DD spell but is nothing compared to any other DD spell by other caster classes. As for melee damage, you won't be efficiently contributing enough melee damage for the damage you would probably be taking. Since our damage in regular form has been nerfed, and our disadvantage of only the use of leather, we would be taking more damage than giving if we were to go melee.

The only good thing about druids is the use of entangle. It has a fast cast time, and far casting radius. The only problem with entangle is that its random, and there is no way of knowing how long it will last on your target.

Basically, druids are just as bad for PvP as they are for PvE. They cannot fill the role of the other more efficient classes, and they contribute very very little to a PvP battle.
Mages cast more damage than druids with a bigger mana pool
Priests heal more health than druids with a bigger mana pool
Warriors and Rogues are obviously better than animal forms
Druids suck for PvP...

 7. Re: Why PvP sucks for druids. | 7/29/2004 8:50:47 PM GMTDT
You know... no doubt, druids are one of the classes that need the most tweaking for pvp. I'm going off Blizzard quotes here...

First, Blizzard said every class is balanced on group combat as far as pvp goes. With that said, druids are nice to have in your group for pvp. Their buff is killer. (I know, I know, no one wants to roll a class for a 30 minute buff) Their heals are always good, obviously. Root is insanely nice. Root is easily the best offense against fleeing opponents in group combat. Charge is a close second, but I find a lot of times warriors are already engaged in combat or wasting precious seconds waiting for to come out of combat to charge. I guess their root being so great is a result of the druids downfalls though. Seeing as they don't have much else to do, they're jonny on the spot with roots and it's definately nice. Cat form is decent dps as well.

However, Blizzard has also said that they don't want any classes to be support classes in WoW. That's definately what a druid is, through and through. Druids can do nothing, really, when alone in pvp. Let's be honest here, I mean I know every druid has killed someone. Maybe you've even killed a lot of people. But when pitted against a decent pvper on even ground, how did you do? I bet you got smoked more often than not against every class. I finally gave up on my druid for this reason. Druids are easily a support class.

We'll see what talents do for druids. I know lots of people say talents shouldn't balance a class, but hey, if talents can give druids some more abilities, why not?

I think the answer to the problem is to make shapeshifting free first and foremost. Let druids jump between forms. That would be a huge boost, and make the class insanely difficult and FUN to play. Difficult is good. Difficult is challenging.

After that, if they were still very lacking, I'd suggest some pvp abilities for their forms. Druid form is fine, but for pvp all you get is a 1 minute cool down stun in bear form, and pounce to stun out of stealth. While these are handy situational tools, they're not consistant. Give one form a hamstring style snare and one form a spell interruption move and I think they'd be fine.
