Q u o t e:
Make specialization in a full talent more attractive and distinct…

Make two levels for some of the abilities in each of the talent trees. One with basic “needed” talents and a second tier with the most improved versions of that ability only accessible at higher levels.

Examples:

Sap – Near the beginning of the tree place the “improved sap” talent. Give it 3 possible points and a 90% return to stealth rate at max, pretty much what it is now. Then higher up the tree, maybe even with a 25pt category requirement, have “Knockout” with 1 point that brings it up to 100% but you would pretty much have to be a 100% subtlety build rogue to get it before level 60.

Master of Deception – Entry level 3-5 points will give you a slight edge over players/mobs but a player around your level will still be able to find you (again, pretty much what it is now).
**Second level MOD will be 1 point, 30-point requirement and double (?) what you have put into MOD so far. Maybe even add a cool blurred character or particle effect to let others know you are a MOD master. Again you will probably be only able to get this at 60 or at 55 if you have ONLY put points in subtlety.


Obviously these two talents get talent-expensive to become masters of but since you have sacrificed a lot on the damage end (no cold blood, adrenaline, weapon spec) this shouldn’t be overpowered. It would be more for players that want to run around and be sneaky and don’t care about the mega dmg hits. For players that do care about the crazy hits they can go assassin build. And the group minded players can go combat. The same principles can be applied to other talents and I think will help with players who feel “cookie cutter”.

By doing this you still get some of the dire “needed” abilities in the front load-low level talents but someone who becomes a “master” of a talent tree will have a superior version of it. Since the “pro” versions are high up the tree it will really reward someone who stuck through the tree the whole time and who is level 55+. Also it provides more options to players who want samplings of all the talents and some of the “cool/fun” factor without really getting to use them to their most potent. That person would also feel different from others of their class but will never know the true power of a specialization. In retail that person would have to spend several levels without cool abilities they could be getting to dedicate themselves to this one line and thus should be rewarded for sticking with it.


Last thing:
Give rogues some sort of defined role. I.e. we are not the dmg dealers by a large margin, do not area effect, summon, heal, crowd control, basically just feeling without a niche


This i completely agree with, rarely have i met other rogues that prefer specializing in subtelty over assassination or combat (this isn't my first rogue, so before anyone flames me, i am not exactly new at the class,:P )

Also, Why does aggresion give bonus to ambush and SS, when Ambush is mostly focused in subtelty and assassination tree?

Maybe it should be SS and backstab instead of ambush and SS, and maybe that same talent but for ambush should be in subtlety tree and cost 20 points. Just my 2-cents for the day...
