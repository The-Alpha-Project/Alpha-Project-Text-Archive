With as many complaints about miss ratios being too high, I got to wondering what the basic percentages were to begin with, so over the last week or so, I've done quite a bit of testing, and here are the results. Note, I'm not claiming any of this is a bug. In fact, while the numbers were slightly higher than I was expecting, they weren't enormously so.

Here's the methodology I used for testing. It's a little-known fact that when you're fighting with a fishing pole that the game uses your fishing skill as your attack rating. And it has always seemed that it was your attack rating versus the level of the mob that determined hit/miss ratio.

So I started with a fishing skill of 1, and picked a particular mob, and fought it repeatedly in increments of 5 skill in fishing, to simulate capped attack for a variety of level ranges. The mob I picked was a level 23 Elite Defias Captive in the stockades. It was a good choice because it doesn't disarm, and the only debuff it casts is an increased damage one. So a 1 skill is equivalent to Level -23 (well, 22.8), 5 is Level -22, etc.

Here were the results. Note that this is just normal combat, no specials, because a fishing pole is not a melee weapon so you can't use overpower, mortal strike, rend, hamstring, etc.

Tyren or other Blizzard dudes, if any of these numbers are not within the expected ranges, please let me know, and I'll be happy to either send the logfiles I generated to someone there, or do some more specific testing.
