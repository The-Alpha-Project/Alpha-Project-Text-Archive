 2. Re: Item stat formula's. Whats best for a war | 10/18/2004 8:42:36 PM GMTDT
You're not taking items with +defense into account. You get 5 defense every level, so an item with +10 defense means that anything swinging at you needs to make its to hit check as if you were 2 levels higher. Now, to temper that, you don't start to have noticeable gains from that until you get about +100 defense on items - in PvE. This may be different in PvP.

You also are not taking agility into account. A point of agility is worth 2 or 3 armour, and increases your dodge chance.

And finally, armour. 1000 points of armour make a huge difference in how much damage you take when you get hit. As an example, there are mobs in blackrock spire that can sunder my AC from 7000 to zero. At 7000AC, that mob is hitting me for about 200-300. At 0 it's more like 800-1000. Mitigate 100 points per 1000 armour or thereabouts, so 100 armour mitigates about 10 points (this is from memory, won't be perfect).

Personally, I go for AC/stamina (using 20AC = 1 stamina in general), and if I can get agility with it too, that's great. Strength is a distant fourth on the stat list. But then again, I'm PvE. But, bear in mind that you'll eventually want to tank stuff in an instance someday. Your job there isn't to do damage.
