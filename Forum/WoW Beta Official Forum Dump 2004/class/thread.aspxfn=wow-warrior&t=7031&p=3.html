Overall, I'm fairly happy with the state of warriors with just a few exceptions.

CHARGE
We all knew a nerf was coming to this, so the changes aren't terribly suprising, but I think a better job could have been done to maintain a little more of it's usefulness.
This patch there were 3 changes to charge that I've noticed so far.
1. Can no longer charge in combat (this includes the 'mob sees you first' case when you can't charge)
2. Stun reduced to ~1 second
3. Charge is resistable
On the plus side, combat now ends instantly when a mob dies, so you don't have that 5 second wait anymore to use charge again.
What I'd like to see is either
A) Charge is usable in combat again, other nerfs (2 & 3) remain. The stun is really trivial on this now, so I don't think it would be the huge pvp issue that it used to be.
B) If you're absolutely set on it not working in combat, at least get rid of the 'mob sees you first' cases where charge will not work. This is super annoying and serves no purpose that I can determine.

TAUNT
People really like to complain about this but I don't think it's the huge problem that people make it out to be. I really have had no trouble maintaining aggro for either xp camping or high level instances.
That being said, I never seem to have enough rage to do anything else while tanking. Either a small increase to rage generation while in defensive stance OR a decrease to the cost of taunt would make me relatively happy with this ability.

BERSERKER STANCE
At this point, you could totally remove this ability and nobody would miss it. I'm perfectly happy with the other two stances. I don't think anybody ever uses this stance. I'm sure a lot will disagree, but I'd be perfectly happy if you were to remove it and incorporate some of it's admittedly sparce abilities into the other stances.
Of course, maybe you have plans for Berserker that will make it great, but I just don't see a 3rd stance being all that useful when Battle and Defensive stance seem to offer all of the flexibility I'll ever need.

Anyway, overall I'm faily content. People are always going to complain when you change things around, but overall, our class still seems fairly strong to me. Get rid of the few annoyance factors and I'm a happy warrior.

Adding a few more "fun" abilities to Defensive stance and reducing the rage cost of taunt might be enough to fix this problem, as it seems quite obvious that most people are having trouble doing anything but taunting with rage limitation in defensive stance. I don't have any good suggestions for this, but I have seen a few good ones in this thread.

Defensive stance and Protection talents need to be reworked. Right now these are tuned exlusively for PvE group combat. In any other role (PvP or soloing), an Offensive/Arms build is significantly more effective.

In PvP, a defensive warrior is almost a non-factor. Because we do so little damage, we're very low on the opposistion's kill list, certainly lower than priests and mages. In PvP combat we mostly just chase after players who are beating the stuffing out of our poor casters, then helplessly watch them die because we have no ability to pull off the attackers. An offensive warrior is actually BETTER at protecting his groupmates in PvP, because of hamstring.

Defensive warriors need abilities that are effective in PvP. The ability to "protect" another player and shield them from damage would be great. While protecting, the warrior would take all damage for the protected player, and would have his attack rate lowered for balance. Of course, the warrior has to be next to the protected player for this to work.

This would be an incredibly useful and FUN addition, even if it came with stiff penalities. Instead of mashing on taunt, losing rage, and gaining frustration, the warrior would protect his groupmate and thereby gain rage, because he's taking more hits.

Making taunt work in PvP would be nice, but it's tricky. Even if taunt simply caused the taunted player to target the warrior, it would be somewhat effective. Sure, the taunted player could just re-target, but this would at least slow him down and let the warrior soak up one or two hits.
