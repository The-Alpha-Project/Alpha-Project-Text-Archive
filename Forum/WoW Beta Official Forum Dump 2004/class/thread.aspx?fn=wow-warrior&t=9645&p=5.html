Q u o t e:
From the QA team:

The miss ratio against an even level NPC with maximum weapon proficiency for the level has not been changed. What can be misleading is that if an NPC rolls a successful block but has no shield, it will be reported as a miss. The same holds true for NPCs that parry but are not holding a weapon. The combined total can be perceived to be double or even triple the normal miss rate, when in actuality up to 2/3 of that percentage is the NPC’s inherent avoidance skill and not reliant upon the player’s ability to hit.

We hope this clears up players’ concerns regarding this issue.

Right, so what you are saying is that mob avoidance has been improved, unless I'm misreading that. This is the only explaination for why warriors are reporting more misses- is that mobs "block" more.

I put block in quotes there because as has been pointed out, a mob without a shield should not be allowed to block. Incidentally, I have parsed this using Cosmos and my dps has dropped, very significantly. I will re-run the parse after servers come up and post my data in its entirety if that is what is required to convince the right people that there IS a problem. Whatever the cause, such a substantial reduction of my dps cannot be justified, especcially when the previous system seemed not only to work well, but make a lot of sense.

Putting it another way- if pre-patch as a warrior I was fighting a mob which obviously had high armor (a turtle, a mob with plate/mail armor graphics) I expected a harder fight. Same if the mob I was fighting carried a shield, was obviously marked/coded as a warrior (eg, quillboars who dual-wielded and bore the title "Warfrenzy", used battle/demo' shouts).

That is all FINE. However, fighting a raptor which is now magically able to block without a shield is just wrong. In fact, I have a very good example of this sort of annoying trait from another game, Everquest.

Newbie casters used to complain un-endingly about how a SNAKE could use the ability "kick" and interrupt a cast. How, it was justifiably argued, does a snake not possessed of lower appendages kick a caster in the face and interrupt casting?

It changed game balance at the lower levels. This change for mob difficulty hurts ALL melees at ALL levels. It makes melee PvP longer and more convoluted as has already been pointed out by Quaintwar. It makes PvE a throw of the dice, even when the mob you are fighting is an unsophisticated animal who somehow blocks without a shield. That is NOT what we want to see as players in retail.

One of the *incredibly* frustrating things about Everquest was the sheer amount of time it took for melees to kill each other in PvP. The reason is simple- the longer it takes for a warrior to kill a rogue or another warrior, the more chance something will turn up to influence the outcome of the fight decisively. Also, it's tedious and annoying if avoidance has been upped to the point at which I swing and miss 6 times when fighting a player 5 levels below me who then beats me.

If the issue lies with mob avoidance, I strongly suggest a return to the previous avoidance system. In the previous system, mobs with shields or higher armor were still a harder fight, and elites were not worth the trouble unless you had a group. The old system of avoidance was fine I feel, nothing needed changing PvE.

Even in PvP, the old system was fine, because as I have pointed out, melees want a certain amount of certainty when they fight each other. Making it more of a russian roulette discourages skill and hurts players who work on their PvP abilities. Under the previous system, if I fought another warrior and lost, I would be inclined to say "good duel" (if it was one) or silently respect the opposing team player who bested me with skill and/or preparation.

Since the patch I curse the game, not the lack of my own skill. That is surely an unhealthy result from whatever change was implemented. Again I suggest a return to the pre-patch model, it worked and I liked to play in it.
