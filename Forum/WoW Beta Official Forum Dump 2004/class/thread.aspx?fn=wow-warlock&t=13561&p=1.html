 1. Firestones: Part of the key to Warlock melee. | 10/21/2004 9:37:01 PM GMTDT
I've seen a fair number of posts on how Firestones are "useless" and not worth having. This is actually VERY much not the case, but it may reflect a confusion about the proper way to use the weapon.

You'll need...

1. A one-handed weapon of a certain type.
2. A Firestone.
3. Lots of cash for enchantment.

Choosing a sword or dagger.
The first thing you want is a particular one-handed weapon with a chance to proc for additional damage.

I use a Sword of Hanzo, which has a chance to "Wound" for another 75 damage. Shadowblade, Blazing Rapier, Blood-Etched Blade, and the Shortsword of Vengeance are all examples of these types of weaopns. There's actually quite a few of them, so getting one should be little trouble.

Equip a Firestone.:

Now that you have a weapon with a base chance to proc, you equip a Firestone. At my level, that's about 75 additional fire damage.

Enchant the weapon with "Fiery Weapon."

This adds an additional 40 damage when it procs.

Result?

Instead of hitting for an average of 36.3 DPS, I hit for 36.3DPS with three chances to proc for additional damage. Even if my base melee damage is quite low, I still have a good shot at firing at least one of the three.

This strategy wont' turn you into a warrior, but it'll make a difference.

 14. Re: Firestones: Part of the key to Warlock me | 10/22/2004 12:10:10 AM GMTDT
It would be interesting to get the exact probabilities for each of those 3 procs. If you knew that probablity you could find how many times you would expect to hit the mob before you would proc. Since there are only two outcomes for a single proc, procing and not procing it is a bernoulli random variable. Since you would want to know the number of times you would have to hit before your first success (proc) you could describe this as a geometric random variable. So, on average if the probability to proc, p, is equal to 30% then the average number of times you would need to hit before experiencing a success (proc) would be 1/p, which is 3.333... times. This is assuming that each attack is independent of the last attack. Too bad it is very difficult to get some exact numbers for many things in the game because of modifiers and other unknowns.
