 12. Re: New Hunter here, Best too keep the first | 10/1/2004 3:38:47 PM GMTDT
From what I have seen in the stress test and so far in the beta it definately seems like training higher level pets does increase the stats of the pet (armor, hitpoints, and damage especially). There will be some variation based on pet type since some pets are higher in one area than another type.

My level 14 raptor trained to lvl 15 is doing 16-23 damage, has 683 armor, and 312 life. My lvl 16 white lion is doing 17-24 damage, 727 armor, and 349 life.

I know the lion I tamed at 12 and had leveled up was still capped in the teens for damage. I do not recall its hitpoints and armor. From what I have seen it appears that there are level ranges where you will see differences on the damage, armor, and hit point caps.

It does appear from what I saw in the stress test where I had more range of pet levels that the ones picked up at lower level stopped gaining damage after a while and then the only way to improve the pet's dps would be to tame a new one of that species at a higher starting level.

When doing beast lore on beasts of the same level I could see variations on damage, armor, and hitpoints between the pet types. I suspect the slightly lower damage on cats may be offset by their attack speed and the possibility that they are dual weilding.
