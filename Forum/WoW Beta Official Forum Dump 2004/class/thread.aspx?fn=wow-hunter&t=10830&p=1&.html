The Hunter is a vicious damage dealer. Their pets add to the damage count and can aid SOMEWHAT in managing monster aggro. The variety of traps allows the Hunter to control crowds TO AN EXTENT, as well.

I just wanted to make a few comments based on this post since obvious it is hypocritical to try to help the hunter class when it is barely finished.

First of all, I wanted to address the whole "pulling" issue. Pulling is something that is done by tanks in 95 percent of the instances that are done. Sure it is cute that hunters can mark the enemy, but they shouldn't be pulling in the first place! If they were a tank class I suppose this would workd out ok, but they aren't :(. Anywyas, I suggest you get rid of the whole "hunters pull" thing.

Second of all, I am getting the feeling that blizzard really doesn't know what a hunter is supposed to do. With the words "somewhat" on aggro and "to an extent" on traps/cc I doubt that the devs honestly don't know what the role of a hunter is in the first place. I just want to clarify my position by saying THIS IS OK!! :) No games that bring in this hunter/archer/ whatever class really know what the role is. The truth is it is a fun class and people will play it regardless of how good or bad they are in groups/pvp/whatever.

In addition I want to say that the idea of a hunter being a "vicious damage dealer" is a great one! Personally I believe this is what will make the class acceptable for pvp /pve /soloing /grouping "the whole 9 yards". Make us a great ranged dps class that does obscene amounts of dmge. Other players in the group arent gonna want us for our CC (bring a mage/warlock) and they arent gonna want us for our aggro management (bring a warrior/tank). However, if we can do a good amount of dps, that gives us a use in group play.

After reading "the class role" for all of the classes, I can tell that you are trying to avoid making classes too one sided. You obviously do not want one class that is "the best healer" or "the best tank" or "the best cc". Once again, this is ok! Sure you can keep the trap/pet idea to fit the classes persona (thats not a problem at all). Just (imho) make our dps outweigh our traps/pets! That way you can keep the whole "Look hunters can do this and that! Wow!!" without losing our ability to contribute to groups!

I strongly suggest getting rid of mail armor for the hunter class. They really don't need it! In both solo and groups, we always have either a pet or a main tank taking the aggro anyways. Do you know how hard it is to find mail/agi equipment for a hunter? Leather seems to work better for agility anyways. I understand that you gave mail armor to the hunters because " you just needed a class to give it to on alliance since you gave it to shamans". But lets get rid of mail armor for the hunter.

I just want to stress that I'm not playing a hunter in retail so what is done to the class does not effect me in any way. This isn't a "omg buff my class/ we suck / i want to not suck for the rest of beta" post but rather an attempt to provide some constructive criticism. It is a fun class to play regardless of how it is currently and I think the devs did a great job on it!

Anyways, in summary, I believe you should make hunters the strongest single target dps class in the game. Again, take away our mail armor since we really don't need it. If I had my way, I'd have pets and traps completely taken out, but I understand that just isnt going to happen. Let's make it so we do not get the "well hunters are good at soloing" "well hunters have nothing to contribute to groups" threads in the forums! Afterall, hunters may be good at soloing, but I'm yet to meet any hunter that can solo instances at high levels for decent equipment.

Anyways, Good job on the class! Can't wait to see how the talents do when implemented! :)

archer style char vs nuking mage style char:

archer pros- outstanding ranged dps vs single target. archer should have a high probability of winning any 1v1 fight that begins at range (all other things being equal). archer is a combatant - wears almost as much armor as a fighter and has almost as many hp's as a fighter.

nuking mage pros- a full suite of area of effect spells. mages should be able to freeze, burn, poison, or whatever, a full screen's worth of targets both at range (preferred) or up close (ruh roh). mages should have a selection of utility spells subject to balance and one's particular tastes in fantasy (teleport, invis, levitate, polymorph, etc)

archer cons- limited multi-target abilities, limited melee capabilities, limited selection of utilities (hmmm thankfully ALL these limitations are in place for wow hunters)

mage cons- low hps, low ac, mana dependent. requires full time baby sitter (especially in pvp).
