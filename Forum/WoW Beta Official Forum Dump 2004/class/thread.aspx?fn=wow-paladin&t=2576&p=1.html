 16. Re: Seals - Anyone else not even buy them? | 7/27/2004 4:24:50 PM GMTDT
Here's some things to consider:

I've been in several groups where I have had one or two people comment that they were surprised that as a Paladin I actually use seals effectively. That comment is vanity or anything, its just meant to demonstrate seals ARE useful.

Honestly, I think a good portion of it comes to me understanding Aggro/Threat/Hate system.

Here's some things you guys not using / experimenting with seals should learn to help your game.

1.) When to tank: If there is a warrior in the group, you should not be tanking period. This means either lay off the high DPS weapons and favor low damage, fast speed weapons; lay off the Holy Strike; --OR-- do both. If you do too much damage, you're going to move up the hate list too fast and the groups healer will then have to use heavier healing spells to keep you AND the other tank alive instead of chain casting lower, more mana consious spells.

2.) Seal of Salvation: It is extremely important to note that SoS mitigates threat, it doesn't remove. If you cast Seal of Salvation on an ally while they aren't doing anything threatening it doesn't help nor does it help alot if you cast it on them after they have done something very threatening. SoS helps best if they have the seal on when they are doing something threatening. You can sit there and recast it everytime it wears off but all that is going to do is create a mana sink for you. What you need to do is be concious when a Priest or a Mage is likely going to do something highly threatening.

In the case of a mage, a smart mage will never start off with power nukes. What they will/should do is let the Tank take a few good swings at the creep, then the mage will usually throw a few weaker attacks in then after that, throw their power nukes. When you recoginize they are using lesser attacks and getting ready to move to more powerful attacks, that is the time you should be using SoS.

On the priest side, the most effective way to heal someone is to chain heal using lesser heals. Its more mana concious and usually does the job especially in long drawn out battles. If you notice the main tank is taking too much damage, throw SoS to the main healer because he is likely getting ready to throw a big heal that will cause alot of threat.

3.) Seal of Protection: One thing many players (Paladins and other players alike) don't seem to realize is SoP comes really in handy for three reasons. First off, there is the obvious, it prevents any additional harm comming from other creeps. The second is also fairly obvious, casters can still cast both defensive and offensive spells while SoP is in play on them. The last is the real trick to the spell. SoP, like Divine Favor, actually removes players from the creeps hate list while its in play. That means SoP is an almost instant "stop creeps from attacking me" seal. It does take 1 - 2 seconds for the hate list to clear and the seal only does last 6 - 12 seconds (depending on level) but its the best way to get something off an ally immediately. Be forewarned though, when SoP DOES wear off, your ally enters back into the area at the exact place on the hate list he/she was before. You should almost always follow up with Seal of Salviation.

4.) Seal of Fury: You should start off almost every battle by throwing this onto the main tank. This will allow him to build up a high position on the hate list very quickly so that it will be tougher for other party members to move ahead of him on the hate list. During long battles it is sometimes (rarely) a good idea to cast Seal of Fury on your groups nuker *IF* the tank is about to die and your healer is too low on mana. This allows you to temporarily force the nuker to tank. If you do this, you absolutely need to follow up with SoP and immeidately there after SoS as soon as possible to replace him apprioately on the hate list.

5.) Seal of Reckoning: This is a great mid battle seal for tanks and a great seal to keep on rogues whenever they need healing. SoR allievates a great deal of presure on the primary healer to keep the tank alive. As far as rogues go, SoR can practically keep rogues completely alive without anything else needed.

6.) Seal of Sacrafice: One fundamental you shouldn't forget about this seal is it works both ways. You split all damage you and your ally takes weither YOU are taking it or HE/SHE is taking it. It also goes the same way with healing too. This seal is great for saving your life or saving the life of your allies. Beyond the fact that you want to conserve mana throughout battles, the other reason to retain a low level of threat is so that you have a great deal of HP you can use to levy the damage taken by your allies with this seal. If you are both low on HP and you use this seal to try to save him, you could litterally kill him and you.

I hope this helps you guys not using seals understand them better =).
