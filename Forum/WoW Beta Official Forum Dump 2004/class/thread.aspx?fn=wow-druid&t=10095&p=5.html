 97. Re: If Druids stay the same, will you be a druid? | 10/27/2004 7:56:49 PM GMTDT
Most definately NOT playing a druid in retail because of the following reasons :

1) The lame change of our speed enhancements only working outdoors (and then on top of that the term "outdoor" itself being bugged as well). It's a massive slap in the face, the thing I enjoyed mostly about my druid was using my travel speed at any time, especially in big towns to hop in & out of all houses to check for NPCs with quests.

2) The fact that you need primary strength in bear form, agility in cat form (and stepping on the toes of rogues big time for item rolls) and intellect in druid form is a major drawback. The point is simple, if druid keep remaining all 3 stats as primary stats depending on what form they are in then screw it, I'm not playing this class. The devs should make intellect our primary stat INDEPENDANT of what form we are in, period.

3) Bear form is extremely ugly. It's head isn't even animated (unlike catform), it's just one big ugly head with a wide open mouth that swings up & down like a jojo when you run. The bear model should be redone completely. It's also way too big and has a fat ass. Please model a prettier bear.

4) A supplemental cat form needs to be added at level 50 (similar as how dire bear is an upgrade for bear form). The damage we do compared to rogues even with full feral spec is a joke.

5) Druid bear & cat form need to be improved in general. It seems it's OK for a full restoration druid to be equal (or even better) than a priest. But at the same time a feral druid isn't even in the same ballpark as a warrior (when in bear form) or a rogue (when in cat form). Feral form damage definately needs to be upped.

6) A result of the above point, people you want to group with FORCE you to be a healer. Players KNOW that a restoration druid is a good healer (even primary healer replacing a priest) and all other templates just make you a weak version of a warrior, rogue or tank. The ONLY viable druid for grouping & instances is a restoration druid. There is no other way. At this moment I'm L45 and I always spec to feral to solo and respec to restoration for grouping. Fact is, when I want to solo and I'm restoration I simply suck, I can barely kill even con mobs. And when I'm feral specced (better solo play) I'm a healer that runs out of mana very very fast.

7) Entangling roots needs to work indoor as well so a druid can play another role apart from being an indoor healbot with moonfire spam. It isn't even funny how utterly boring a druid is in instance groups. Druids (and hunters) are WAY too dependant of outdoor zones.

8) Not having any resurrection is a BIG downfall. You will always need to group with a paladin or shaman. Resurrection should either just be added to the druid line or else be a talent available at low/medium restoration spec.



What class would I play then? I am now mostly leaning towards a shadow spec priest with secondary in holy. That way I am a good nuker and healer for groups and the only gear I need to worry about is gear with intellect, spirit & stamina.

Sorry Blizzard but your current version of a druid definately needs some work. At the moment I see the class useful for one thing, being a MotW buffbot and a speedy resource gatherer.
