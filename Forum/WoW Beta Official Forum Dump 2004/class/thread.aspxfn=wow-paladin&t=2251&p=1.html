 1. Holy Vail - Remove the Art of Running | 7/21/2004 3:41:10 PM GMTDT
I have been giving this issue considerable thought recently.

We've heard a couple well known Paladins speak out recently that running is NOT a Paladin thing to do. A good quote that I think embodies what Paladins should be comes right out of Babylon 5.

Q u o t e:

Babylon 5, J. Michael Straczynski

We go to the dark places where no one else dares venture! We stand on the bridge and NO ONE shall pass.



A Paladin just shouldn't be running from battle. He should give of his life first before running. The irony is WoW has made Paladins the great survivor who runs from danager.

A cool idea I had was to give Paladins an ultimate spell called "Holy Vail". This spell would have a 30-60 minute cool down but have one hell of effect. Instead of running from a battle, the Paladin would throw up Holy Vail and it would AOE pacify EVERYTHING in the immediate area and give all everyone a 2 - 5 minute pacification debuff.

This spell would prevent the Paladin from running and not vastly curb the downtime that would occur if a Paladin ran. The effect could/should be a stationary AOE Effect so it wouldn't allow the party to exploit it by moving to other portions of the dungeon. This would force the recovery where they are and allow them to continue just as if the reser DID run.

In addition to this suggestion, here's some other thoughts on how Paladins 'able to run' might get nerfed.

Divine Favor ~ Modifications
  • Slightly increase the length of time the spell lasts
  • Add a +15 Spirit modifier at higher levels of the spell
  • Decrease movement speed by 25%
  • Keep caster pacifying effect
  • Temporarily remove caster from Hate List while spell is in use and for a 2-5 seconds after the spell wears off (already in to some degree).

    These modifications to this spell would allow the Paladin to become a slightly more effective healer / sealer in a crunch during battles. It would allow him to more favorably stay and heal instead of run.

    Divine Shield ~ Modifications
  • Add a modifer that slightly increases the time the shield lasts based on how much you are being hit. Invision like a rage bar in defense mode where you gain rage from being hit but loose rage when you aren't attacking. This would make it more benefical to stay and fight with this shield instead of run
  • Keep attack speed reduction
  • Give a partial seal of fury increasing threat by 15% - 25%
  • Increase recast timmer to 8 - 10 minutes

    These changes would make the Paladin a pure tank in this situation. Throw Seal of Fury on top of Divine Shield in this case, and you could have no better tanker.

    Holy Light ~ Modifications
  • Modify spell so the cast is instant but has a cooldown.
  • Slightly reduce healing effectiveness

    The change here basically evens out the normal time it would take to cast Holy Light while channeling it uninterupted. The difference beyond established lore in this modification is to make it so it is viable for a Paladin to actually TANK, FIGHT AND HEAL. Novel idea huh? Thats why most people play the class!

    Divine Intervention ~ Added Spell
  • Allows the Paladin to res himself every 60 minutes.

    Divine Intervention ~ Added Spell
  • Scarafice the Paladins life to pacify all active battling factions for several minutes (2 min - 5 min)
  • 60 minute cooldown

    This, again allows the Paladin to do best what I think everyone feels he should he should do; save his party at the cost of his life.
