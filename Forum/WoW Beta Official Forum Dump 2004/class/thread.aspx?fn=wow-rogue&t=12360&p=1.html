 2. Re: Tips for a low level? | 10/11/2004 9:05:17 PM GMTDT
Q u o t e:
It would be easier if you can just put a dagger in your offhand and find a mainhand sword.


Are you asking why you can't backstab from your offhand dagger? The answer is because then you wouldn't have to make a choice between combat and assasination. You would have the best of both worlds, your DPS would be much higher, and you wouldn't be as balanced.

Finding an offhand sword isn't especially easy, but it doesn't matter that much because there's no reason you _need_ an offhand sword. Your primary weapon determines your specials, so your offhand is generally just going to be the best DPS item you can find -- be it sword, mace, dagger, fists, etc.

 4. Re: Tips for a low level? | 10/12/2004 12:21:44 AM GMTDT
Not being able to Backstab isn't a big loss. The increased energy cost offsets its increased damage. I'd rather have more Sinister Strikes (SS) and the resultant combo points.

Right now I dual-wield two swords because of their quality, and I am more effective than ever. If I'm up against a caster, lead with Cheap Shot and lock it down. If I'm up against a melee character, lead with Garrote and make 'em bleed.

The only real ability that I lose is being able to Ambush. It is possible to Cold Blood -> Ambush (crit), SS however many times you want, Preparation to reset timer on Cold Blood, and then go for a 5-point Eviscerate + crit. That combo is used to blow out another player in PvP, so I carry a dagger just in case.

It's worth noting that your ability to apply poisons to your target is reduced when you aren't using a dagger.

Now, with all that said, my advice to a low-level Rogue is this: re-roll a warrior instead. Rogues on the PvP server enjoyed a four-hour stint of being overpowered, and have been viciously nerfed ever since. Rogues should be a lot of fun on the blue (PvE) server.
