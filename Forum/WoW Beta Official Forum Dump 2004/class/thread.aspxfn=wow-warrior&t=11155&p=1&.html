Interesting topic.

As a non-cosmos user (nor any other UI mod), I make use of the "Shift-1" "Shift-2" etc... for alternate hotkey setups.

Those hotkey bars are usually nearly identical with slight variations. For instance, one bar will have disarm in the place where shield bash usually sits, but is otherwise identical. I use one while fighting melee oponents, and the other while fighting casters.

Some other possible variants:
- Solo bar: no taunt / aggro grabbing skills
- End boss bar - making room for shield wall/last stand and other skills on such long timers that one doesn't normally bother using them.
- Downtime bar - include bloodrage and battle shout, as well as food/bandages/hearthstone (this usually has real estate to spare)

Basically, I try to think of the times when I have time to do the "Shift-X" step, and try to keep things set up so I'm not changing hotkey bars in the middle of combat.

 7. Re: A question of hotkeys | 10/21/2004 5:15:25 PM GMTDT
Two points on attack hotkeyed - one, I wouldn't use up a slot on the hotbar for it (bind a key to it), hotkey spots are too precious to waste with something that can be explicitly used.

Two, *absolutely* hotkey it. You could get yourself (and more importantly, your party) into trouble by accidentally rightclicking the wrong mob. Left click to pick the target, then hit the attack key to attack it.

On a related note, you won't stop attacking if you shift targets in battle, *unless* the target dies. For this reason, the nearest npc key is extremely useful. I think part of the reason I get frustrated by other group members who need to be, say, taking out Shaman totems is that they're targeting setup is sub-optimal. Mixing nearest npc + attack key allows you to rapidly shift and attack targets. Not only that, the attack key functions as "Target nearest NPC AND attack" in one push. Drop one target, position yourself so that your desired target is closer than any other, hit Attack, you'll start attacking it immediately.

Also, there are cases where you need to turn OFF autoattack, and having it hotkeyed lets you do this in the fastest manner possible. I've managed to abort an attack on a mob when I saw another party member hitting it with a CC ability between swings.

Rapid reactions are handy, and hotkeys facilitate that. Attack is your most basic function in combat, makes sense to me to use every tool available to control it well ;)

So. Small point, very important :)
