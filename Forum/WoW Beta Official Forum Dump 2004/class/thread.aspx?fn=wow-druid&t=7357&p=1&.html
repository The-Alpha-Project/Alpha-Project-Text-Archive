 1. What are the class 'Prime' stats? | 10/9/2004 6:02:36 AM GMTDT
Basically it seems to me that druids have every stat as a 'Prime' stat, as opposed to 1 or 2 for other classes. This is my take on those... I would appreciate clarification if anyone has had experience with the other classes.

Prime Stats (?)
Warrior - Str/Sta (Agi?)
Rogue - Agility
Paladin - Str/Int ?
Priest - Spirit/Int
Mage - Int (Spirit)
Warlock - Spirit/Int
Shaman - Str/Int ?
Druid - Str/Sta (Bear), Agi (Cat), Spirit/Int (Druid)
Hunter - Agi

As I see it (I could be wrong), every class except druid has 1-2 primary stats that they want to pump into to be most effective in a group. Warriors tank, so they need Stamina (and probably Agility). They need enough DPS to keep aggro. Rogue's are plain DPS and want to crit like madmen, plus they get extra bonuses from Agility, so the choice is clear. Warlocks, Mages, and Priests all rely a lot on spell casting, so they are Spirit/Int, with mages probably just being Int, with a little Spirit. I am less clear on the hybrid classes, namely Hunter, Paladin, Shaman, and Druid, but you get the idea.

Druids are in tune with spirits of nature, right? What do you think of Spirit being our primary stat? Toss in Int if we need 2 primaries. Make bonus hp in bear form based on Int instead of Stamina (stamina is still what grants you basic hp in druid and cat form... the extra from bear would be from Int, so it definitely benifits you to have stamina for your other forms). DPS in cat form increased by Spirit, maybe by making our crit/dodge rate in cat form based on it instead of agility, or even in ADDITION to agility (Rogues get extra dodge/crit per agility point... this would bridge the gap a bit. Obviously we wouldn't get a full bonus).

Basically I find it annoying that I have to spread my stats out so much in gear, especially since I don't have room in my bags for spare gear, nor could I afford extra gear for my various forms either.

It is very difficult to specialize in every stat, since one way or another, we need every stat to fully utilize our class gimmick - Shapechanging and versatility. I need Int and Spirit just to have mana to change regularly, since mana cost scales up with level, in addition to needing it for druid form.

Well the way the talent trees are set up, you almost have to specialize in one of them to get any real benefit and to be honest I am fine with that. The beauty of the Druid is that if can fit numerous playstyles yet retain alot of its utility.

So ideally if your going the mage/healer route you would want Int/Spr/Sta in that order.

If you were going the Feral route you would want Str/Agi/Sta all equally valuable.

This actually makes the class alot cooler because you will have a ton of diversity in the high end druids based on the individual druids playstyle. Also even if you specialize you never complete lose the utility of the other two aspects of the druid. The other aspects are just not quite as effective and that is to be expected.

Now on the the major problem with this. All three specializations are not created equal.

Currently only the Healer specialization is balanced and good and I hear that Druids builds based on Restoration can possibily out-heal a Priest.

Feral specialization will not even get the Druid anywhere near the necessary capability to be useful for anything besides soloing. A full spec Feral build (at least 31 point in Feral) needs to get Druids up to approaching Warrior/Rogue effectiveness. Not quite equal but close enough to be the main Tank or DPSer in the end game.

The Balance Speced (or Mage Speced) Druid I haven't heard too much about. It get the impression it is ok but not great. Might be somewhere between Restoration and Feral as far as effectiveness.

The point here is that there really is no issue with a Druids primary stat if they balance druid talent specializations correctly.
