 1. Howto: Druid Shapeshifting Class Defining | 10/14/2004 2:04:56 AM GMTDT
I have been reading the boards since I entered the beta and while I still think that alot of people are just whiners, I also think there is a common theme of whats wrong with this class, and what people think it should be, in reguards to the shape changing. In various threads you will find the following points:

1) Shapeshifting is supposed to be our class defining skill but there is nothing special about it to make it unique.

2) Druids need 3 sets of gear to be effective, because they need different stats in each form.

3) Druids are excluded from looting items that are clearly better for a pure rogue or pure warrior.

Here is my idea, to make the shapechanging skill more useful and give it some definition as the defining skill of druids.

One thing to note:

Each character has 14 slots in which a character can wear gear, other then the tabards and shirts. Two of these are not used by Druids because we can't use shields or the ranged slot. Another two are used for trinkets and of course our weapon makes number five, since we don't use it for dps when in animal form.

That gives us 5 slots that really are useless when in Animal form.

What I am proposing is that Druids are given 10 additional slots. Five for Cat and Five for Bear, these slots are used to hold special druid only drops that enhance the druid. I was sorta going with the idea of Special stones enchanted with earth magic that give us additional boosts in skills and statistics.

These items only work when shape changed, they override the item that is in the same slot in caster form. So we lose the stats on our weapon, and can't access the trinkets without switching back out of animal form.

We would have five different "items" for catform, that only affect us in catform and five different items in bearform that only help us in bearform.

The items would be druid only, and only useable by druids.

I don't know if anyone else is going to like this idea, but hey, i'm just trying to solve the problem, not whine about it. And it may not even be possible to do what i've suggested without major changes in the code and thus a really bad idea, but who knows.

This idea would address all 3 issues.

You wouldn't need 3 sets of gear to be effective in animal form, but im' sure some people would still go for 3 sets of gear to get that edge, but they wouldn't have the right to complain that someone said an item should go to a rogue first, since they have their own unique equipment to improve with.
