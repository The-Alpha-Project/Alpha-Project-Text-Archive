 21. Re: How do you solo? | 10/22/2004 5:00:13 PM GMTDT
Ok, I will say I haven't read all the posts here, so if this has already been said, please forgive me.

For me it depends on the talents on how hard solo is. I got into my 30's before talents were introduced and we were still working off of the old combat system. I focused mostly on melee skills and used Rejuv and Moonfire as my main spells. I always used MOTW and Thorns, and usually FF. For my gear I focused on high armor, spirit, and agility. The idea was to keep the mob hitting me for less and less often. That combined with using good weapons and my Dots and regens I was able to take on mobs +4 my level. Sure the fight might take a while, but I would always come out at about 75% of health and mana.

Then the patch came that changed spirit, combat system, and gave us talents. I tried a couple combinations, but the one i liked the best was working Balance till Omen of Clarity and then pushed the restoration line to focus on crits for regrowth. This worked great! Omen would proc on average of once a fight, sometimes more. I was able to completely heal myself and usually end fights with 90% mana/health or 100%. I was mostly fighting +2 lvl mobs at this time and I was in my mid 30's. Life was great... then another patch came and they moved and changed talents again...

So with this current patch at 40 I ran several test with all sorts of talent combos. I tried focusing on all bear talents, all cat talents, all healing talents, all balance talents, and some combos of bear/healing, cat/healing, healing/balance. Out of all my testing I have had the most fun going all balance, which I am currently using. Bear and cat for specs proved to be very nice and powerful, but neither can hold a candle to Omen of Clarity. That ability has saved my butt more times then I can remember! At 40 (Clvl = char level) I was able to get hurricane, which btw rocks! I am able to solo 2 mobs = to Clvl, 3 mobs -1 to -2 to Clvl, 4 mobs -3 ro -4 to Clvl, and 5 mobs -5 to Clvl. I will say that this has more to do with my play style then the abilities I have available, but it is possible. I have had to use some potions in some of these cases... in fact in each of the larger number I was forced to use a potion or my Gem-Encrusted Belt. Also I would end these fights at 0 mana but usually 30-50% health. I'm not saying this is super effective killing, but it sure is fun!

Anyway, with play style as a consideration, I was able to solo effectively using ANY combination of talents. I could solo = Clvl mobs wither either feral form, specializing in healing, specializing in balance, or a combination of any of them. I don't know what your style of play is, but I know it's possible to solo just fine. Also, you might want to try killing mobs that are -2 to -5 of your Clvl. You can kill them faster, you'll hit harder and more frequently, they miss more and hit for less, you resist more and crit on your spells more. IMHO it's easier to kill 2 -2 Clvl mobs then 1 +1 Clvl mob. You get the same xp for each, but one is less risk and more loot =)

You might want to take a look at your playstyle.

Well, I may be only level 25, but I have found a very effective way to solo.

I can take a mob 2 levels above me or 2-3 even levels and be at full HP by the end


Start in bear form, pull attack once, Demoralizing roar. Stun, Maul, shift to druid form.

Cast Rejuv, Restoration on myself, leaving the longer one for the second cast. Shift to cat form.

Attack until hes dead, or if there is an add, repeat the cycle when 1 is gone. Generally I can have the mob (if its single) dead by the time my HoTs run out. WIth them stacked, and the lower HP of the cat form, I stay right at full hp, or a little below.
