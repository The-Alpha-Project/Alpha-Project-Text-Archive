 4. Re: Any thoughts on Druid Talents? | 7/10/2004 11:13:29 PM GMTDT
When I saw the change in spell type classification ("Balance" etc), the first thing that popped into my mind was preparation for Talents. If we're able to read effectively into the combination of previous classifications and the 3 fairly well balanced factions of Druid usage that are now grouped as such (Healing, Shapeshifting and Detrimental type spells), we could possibly assume Druids will be pushed to specialize in one of these with their Talents in the same way that we are seeing Frost Mage and Fire Mage builds start to pop up. On paper this is great; obviously rhetoric and speculation isn't worth much in reality, but this seems like a wise foundation to build Talents upon, branching out from there. I would absolutely like to see specializations based on more specific abilities/shifts within these foundations to promote even more diversity and character identification, but if the Talents are built like the current Mage system would imply, all of this would be intuitive and expected. That said, it is only* the foundation; all the previous accomplishes is simply diversification, Talents should bring creative interesting elements to the table. On the plus side, what we see from the Mage is indicative it will; I've made a concentrated effort to group with as many different Mages as possible in the last few days and let me tell you... no two Mages play alike anymore =p They truly have diversity; I have been surprised to see each Mage in my group will not follow the same spell sequence himself! This is obviously a far cry from flipping Rejuv and Moonfire back and forth, so I take it as a positive sign of both creativity and priorities from Blizzard. The Druid class has the potential to be far more diverse in the Talent set due simply to the wide range of things we can be doing at any given time, let's hope these opportunities are capitalized upon.

 8. Re: Any thoughts on Druid Talents? | 7/11/2004 5:14:10 PM GMTDT
While I hope there are optoins to specialize in each specific form (bear, dire bear, cat), I would also like to see more well-rounded Shapeshifting Talents for those who won't be specializing hardcore into Shapeshifting; e.g. (very non-creative ideas here, it's early still, just for the sake of communicating my sentiment) increased damage that would apply to all shifts, or better *bonus* mana regen that would apply to all shifts (again, this cannot be "the fix"!). I expect some Druids to have a Shape in mind they want to commit many Talents to and they should be accomdated to but at the same time those who want to just dabble in Shapeshifting Talents should also have an option to invest points that will never become obsolete if they invest in that particular manner. Most of us are quite used to not really being in Shapeshifted forms besides Travel for the majority of our playtime, these blanket type Talents would be a nice segway into adjusting towards dynamic shifting and in fact encouraging Druids to specialize from there, having been exposed more to the combat shifts from these blanket Talents.

Also, this may be a foregone conclusion, but all Bear Talents should absolutely* carry over into Dire Bear form, even if Dire Bear has it's own Dire-specific goodies down the road. A new Druid might easily invest in Bear Talents with no knowledge of Dire Bear coming down the road, to find them to be wasted points when he hits 40, the equivalent of being blind-sided with a baseball bat. Additionally, there really is no harm done doing this, Bear is Bear is Dire Bear is Bear, no potential imbalances or exploitive Talent usage come to mind when thinking about this, the one form is being replaced by nearly the exact same form, take the abilities with it. This may be an entirely worthless point on my behalf, as I wouldn't doubt this has been the intention all along, but just in case. ;)

 9. Re: Any thoughts on Druid Talents? | 7/25/2004 4:37:34 PM GMTDT
ohhh boy, does my druid need talents.

for the shape shifting tree -
you can expect something like diablo 2 druids with better hp and defense bonuses for all forms, probably some bonus damage and skills for the different forms - but overall the talents in this tree i am hoping for are a bird form and a kind of ultimate form. bird is easily explained - used for travel to pass over hostile npcs and rough terrain with the speed of a 100g mount.
the ultimate form would be a little more complex, it would have to have high travel speed (preferably that of a mount, but travel form speed would suffice) it would need excellent tanking abilities and high hp like a good warrior, however this form would not have the same skills or item bonus of a warrior. This form would need to be designed well for pvp and instance tanking alike, as well as assaulting and ambushing multiple enemies at once, he would need close range aoe stuns and heavy aoe dps. this would include a couple skills including a charge ability like the warriors. while this form would be highly effective as its well worth the 40 or so skill points it takes to achieve, he would still need to run away to heal before defeating powerful warriors. he is perfect for ambushing and stunning groups of casters and leather wearers before his group can move in to finish them off.

for the balance tree -
first off arcane magic doesnt belong on druids. when i think of arcane magic i think of a mage high in his tower figuring out how to open up locked chests etc. druids should have both of the arcane spell's graphics and names be redone to nature or cold based spells.
the balance tree should have bonus damage, lower mana cost, and decreased casting speed for all the different spells druids have already, but included should be a few new spells that would seem usable by a druid. i would think they should be weather based - and thus nature damage. a cyclone spell that increases in radius and damage could serve as a good crowd control/dot spell, snaring and dotting everything in its path until they escape. if this were a channeling spell it should be long ranged, and movable by the druid, to keep enemies trapped for the entire duration.
other talent spells could be rain or hail storms like weaker versions of the mage's blizzard, but not channeling based. i would also like to see a violent tornado spell like the cyclones from warcraft 3, lifting up 1,2, or 3 people depending on rank.

restoration tree can get very interesting with group heals and buffs, the druid is already a powerful healer over time. with talents to increase all healing spells mana efficiency and healing power, restoration druids would easily rival priests - regenerating so fast that it would take multiple aggro from mobs to kill you with a druid heal on. increased range and duration could also help. i would suggest adding a mana heal or mana heal over time, as well as a group mana heal - much like tranquility. and for the ultimate, restoration talent --> a PASSIVE mana and/or hit point heal over time. everywhere the druid steps, friendly players and npcs will slowly gain back hit points and mana within a 10 foot radius, this of course will increase with ranks. and on the third rank, the druid would be so loved by all that the little critters would come to follow the druid wherever he went, no beasts would attack the druid, and hostile npcs would have a farther agro range. may sound too powerful, but its probably worth the sacrfices made in the balance and shape shifting trees to be an elite restoration druid!
