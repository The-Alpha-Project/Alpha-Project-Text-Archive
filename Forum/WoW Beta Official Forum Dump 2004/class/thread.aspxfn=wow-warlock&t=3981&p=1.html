There has been alot of discussion in this forum about possible improvements to the Warlock class, but I have a few ideas:

Spells

1. Curses/DoT stacking!
This has been debated alot on the boards. However this is crucial to our class due to partying issues (You will HARDLY see a 5 warlock group...) A 5 warlock party should OWN. Even if 2 warlocks party, the only benefit is having 2 separate curses and an extra pet. The Blizz team should definitely consider letting at least our DoTs to stack. I can deal with curses not stacking.

2. Increase DoT Damage/More DoT spells
When in a full party, by the time we cast ONE DoT or curse the monster is dead. The only time our DoTs are effective are on a Boss monster where DoT and debuffs help. Alot of my fellow warlocks have stated that we are the "masters of DoT", but currently we are not. Also, we only have 2 DoT spells, Immolate and Corruption. How about another DoT that snares the mob? Or a DoT that causes ailments?

3. Channeling spells that deplete when hit, rather than cancelling.
Again, fellow warlocks have stated that channeling spells should not be cancelled when hit, but treated like spells. So, when casting a channel spell, the length of the spell is shortened per hit, not cancelled. This becomes a little aggravating when, for example, trying to get a soul shard off of a mob but he is attacking you. Impossible.

4. Shadow Bolt Casting Time Shortened
I haven't seen this on the boards, but I feel our only direct damage spell shouldnt take forever to cast. I am not suggesting it be 2 seconds, since our DoTs should be doing some major hp draining (hint hint =)) but I hardly use this spell because it just takes too long to cast.

Now on to pets. Fellow warlocks feel free to add what I missed :)

Pets

1. Tweak Voidwalkers Torment
Once aggro leaves our lovely Voidwalker, there is minimal chance of aggro going back to him. And if he does get aggro back, we are dead!

2. Tweak Succubus attack power
Blizz excellent job on her design IMO. But thats all she has...LOOKS! How about being able to upgrade her whip? Or maybe giving us a buff to up her attack power? Every lash of that whip should cause gruesome pain! Im not saying make her hit for 5000 hp, but bump her up a little ;)

3. Revamp Imp
If we run out of soul shards, our only option is our Imp. Its really difficult to get a shard with him because...well..he's weak and cant take more than three hits. Maybe give him a spell that makes him morph into a HUGE imp for 10 seconds or something? It wouldn't increase is damage output, but maybe his HP so he could tank while we Drain Soul?

4. "Tweak Voidwalkers appearance"
I have read around the forums of getting rid of his blue appearance and make him more "demonic" I agree he needs to look like he is from the pits of hell, dont u think? :)

5. Ability to Equip Pet
As I mentioned earier, uprgrading Succubus' whip, upgrading Voidwalker's bracers, etc.

Team Spells

1. Ritual of Summoning
Ritual of Summoning IMO should not use a soul shard. My reason behind this is due to the delicacy required to even use it. If a party member even sneezes, the portal disappears. Or, since it requires a soul shard, we shouldn't need two other people to cast it.

2. Soulstones/Healthstones
I hate trading it to a party member! There should be another way to give it to a party member. If a priest is dying in the middle of a battle, and I want to save her life, she has to accept my trade? If there is another way around this let me know guys. How about an AOE spell to give it to team members during down time?

Thats all I have for now at LVL 28. I may have some more later :-)

I rerolled my level 45 Warlock this push to begin playing anew so I can further critique the changes to Warlock class.

In my opinion, the 1-10 tier of Warlock is perfect right now. Instant cast curses eliminated ALL the problems Warlock had in the first stages of the game. During the first 10 levels, the Warlock is FORCED to tank. There is no other option. Thus, instant cast curses play a big roll in this. A Warlock can get off two dots and then use instant cast Curse of Weakness and he'll tank just as good as a warrior for the early levels.

At lower levels, the imp damage is fairly decent and the nerfs to him this push do not effect him. However, his AI NEEDS to be changed back to how it originally was. Even at lower levels when I am the one tanking, the imp will run around like a two-year-old trying to play WoW. As an illustration, I noticed if a mob just CROSSES the imps path (meaning runs over him) the imp has the probability of becoming "feared." This issue needs to be addressed and I am hoping it will be modified to some extent. The imp should not run when he is not aggro.

Another issue I found with the imp is the inability to stop him once he has begun casting a spell. Once I hit CTRL-1 the imp will begin casting his spell; however, if I push CTRL-2 he will not interrupt his casting. This is very annoying and I suggest others /suggest this as well.

Finally, I noticed the imp is not critical hitting this push. I heard he did last push with a VERY low probability. His critical hitting needs to be increased. The Succubus and the Voidwalker (which is a non-damage pet) both have critical hit. The imp's critical hit rate should be increased to approximately the same as the other two pets. This would be a nice compensation to his damage nerf this push. (Which nearly halved his DPS)

At lower levels, the Voidwalker is fine. I am able to nail a mob with 3 dots and then use shadow bolt and I will not gain aggro. However, when I was 45, one immolate would be enough for the Voidwalker to lose aggro and the mob would stick to me like glue. Additionally, I noticed when I obtained my Voidwalker this push the Voidwalker I fought in the summoning circle used a "rage" type of spell. It increased his attack speed and damage temporarily. I am going to assume this was a spell originally planned for the Voidwalker but later removed. (Similar to how NPC mages still cast Chains of Ice).

The Voidwalker at lower levels is how it should also tank at higher levels. However, the problem is his damage does not scale to match the Warlock's DPS at higher levels and Torment at higher levels is not adequate enough. My suggestion would be to give the Voidwalker the Rage spell that the NPC Voidwalker casts.

The Rage spell should be slightly tweaked to help fulfill the Voidwalker's role as a tank. It should hasten his attack speed but also give him an aggro buff that will cause him to generate even more aggro. The spell should have a 1-2 minute recast timer and only last as long as 5-10 seconds.

You will ask, how will this help the Voidwalker at higher levels? The problem with the Voidwalker at higher levels is for it to be an adequate tank the Warlock must allow the Voidwalker time to generate aggro on a mob. This means the Warlock will basically lower his DPS by a substantial amount. Giving this spell to the Voidwalker will allow the Voidwalker to generate enough hate to obtain initial aggro on a mob. This will allow the Warlock to be able to freely dot the mob with immolate, corruption, and the curse of your choice. Additionally, the Warlock should be able to throw a bolt following those attacks and not over aggro the Voidwalker. From this point on, the mob should be at least half health or less so the Warlock can sit back and bolt the final ounces of the mob's life away.

The most obvious benefit of implementing this rage spell for the Voidwalker is the fact it is already there. The spell itself would just need some minor tweaking for the aggro suggestion I have proposed.

Curse Combinations

I love the aspect of curses for the Warlock class. I feel this should be the main reason for a group to add a Warlock to the party. For example, groups add priests for their healing, warriors for tankage, and mages for damage.

My idea is a combination system for the Warlock and his curses. Curses will now create a combo effect when stacked with dots and other curses.

NOTE: Every combination requires Corruption and Immolation and one curse or more.

Examples of combinations:

Corruption + Immolation + Agony = Added effect of all dots now tick much faster then before.

Corruption + Immolation + Weakness = Mobs now take damage based on the damage they lost from weakness and acts as a damage shield. For example, if the weakness decreased the mob's attack damage by 10 everytime the mob attacked a person it would take 10 additional damage.

Corruption + Immolation + Tongues = An added dot is placed on the mob that depletes his or her mana. This dot will deal damage to the mob's mana every tick as long as tongues is in effect.

These combinations could be very fun and give us even more strategy when planning out battles. Additionally, I want combinations to solve the problem of multiple Warlocks in a group. Personally, I feel that there is no reason to add more then one Warlock to a group outside of acquiring an extra curse and a new pet. (Some other classes suffer from this as well) My idea is with combinations Warlocks could create "super combos" with other Warlocks in the same group.

As an illustration:

Corruption + Immolation + Agony + Weakness = Added effect of dots tick faster, mob takes damage equal to that subtracted from weakness, AND a third effect of any person attacking the mob with a melee weapon will gain 10 health on each successful blow.

Corruption + Immolation + Weakness + Tongues = Added effect of a mana dot, damage shield, AND a third new effect of all spells casted by the mob has its damage reduced by a percentage equal to that of Weakness. For instance, if weakness lowered the mobs attack by 16 his spells would be lowered by 16%.

These super combos could get more powerful as more Warlocks are added to the group.

For instance:

Corruption + Immolation + Weakness + Tongues + Agony = Mana dot, damage shield, dots tick faster, lifesteal, spells reduced by percentage equal to weakness, AND a new effect of all damage depletes both the opponent's MANA and HP. For example, a 100 damage blow would lower the opponent's hitpoints by 100 and his mana by 100.

These combinations would make Warlocks very class defined. They would also encourage adopting multiple Warlocks into a group. Moreover, it would allow us to plan strategical attacks with other Warlocks and or ourselves. The ideas listed above are just drawing board ideas to try and illustrate my point. Some of them might seem overpowered in this stage but I needed to create something to use as an example.

Edit: More combination examples

Corruption + Immolation + Curse of Elements = Added effect of reducing target's spell damage by the amount the Element increasing damage by. For instance, if elements increased damage by 20 the opponent's spell damage would be decreased by 20. NOTE: Does not apply to Shadow damage
