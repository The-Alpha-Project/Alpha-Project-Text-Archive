 1. The Demonology Dilemma Dissected. | 10/21/2004 11:37:11 PM GMTDT
The Demonology tree isn't weak--its mostly misguided.

First Tier Spells:

Improved Healthstone: Handy enough and a good 'early' talent, but the small nature of the buff means it won't have much impact on Warlock HP. Suggest +15% / +30% or even +20% / +40%.

Allow Distribution: If I can give someone a Healthstone, I can give them an improved healthstone.[/b]

Imp: Nice idea, but doesn't address the Imp's fundamental problem--at best, he's packing about one thousand HP in areas where mobs hit him for 150 per shot. Great buff to Blood pact.

Demonic Embrace: One of the few "solid" talents in this tree.

Improved Health Funnel: Doesn't matter much; possibly helpful for keeping Imp alive.

Improved Voidwalker: This would be most useful at high level, 1-2 levels before getting "new" torment spell.

Fel Intellect: Addresses a problem that only one Pet really has--the Imp. My Succ (when I use her) and my VW almost *never* run out of mana. More to the point, if they DO run out of mana, the extra 15% this spell provides probably doesn't help much.

Improved Succubus: Seduction improvement is welcome, but improving pathetic damage on Lash of Pain by 24% max means its now pathetic and I wasted 3 talent points.

Fel Domination: Marginally useful for downtime reduction after soulstone or corpse run. Useless otherwise, as mana regen and lifetap take care of this plenty fast as-is.

Fel Stamina: Useless. The Imp takes 100-150 damage per shot, +10% HP gets him 80 HP. that's one hit.

The Succubus has a similar though less severe problem.

The Voidwalker benefits at about 4 hits worth of stamina, but he doesn't NEED the HP much anyway.

Master Summoner.: See Fel Domination.

Master Conjuror: Utter waste of points. This spell eliminates combat downtime, but downtime is not an issue for Warlocks. IN-combat this kind of talent is more useful, but I typically dont' stop in a fight to summon a healthstone. If I do, its because I screwed up and forgot to make it beforehand.

Improved Enslave Demon: Worst. Talent. Ever. What's next--Improved Detect Invisibility when there are no invisible mobs in the game?

Demonic Sacrifice: This is a great idea in theory, but the percentages to buffs needs adjustment; currently you give up too much to gain too little.

Improved Firestone: Requires FAR too much investment into the tree to gain a minor melee boost to a class that doesn't depend on melee. This is nearly equivalent to buffing how quickly warrior's regenerate mana. At rank 1 or 2 I'd invest in this, because I like melee, but its just not worth it this far in.

Unholy Power: Yawn-worthy. A 15% boost to Pet attack would be more interesting in the primary Pet I used did more than 35-45 damage per attack. Now he does 52 damage? Gee, thanks.

Succubus gets maybe an extra 8-10 points per shot. Again, far too much invested, far too little back.

Soul Link: This spell actually makes YOU take damage when your Pet is hit.

This is dumb. Really dumb, and it fits into one of those "Does anyone play warlocks" questions?

I have a Blueberry. He gets hit so I don't have to. I have much less HP than him, so anything smashing him is going to CRUSH me. Taking damage FOR him is not the goal.

I'd have to be absorbing 50-75% of the Imp's damage to make this useful; and then I'd be in perpetual danger to dying, especially if the little schmuck lost aggro.

A boost to the absorbtion rate *MIGHT* make this useful for a Succubus. I'm not sure.

Improved Spellstones: Does anyone use Spellstones now? Well why not?

Mostly because they absorb far too little and are difficult to use. This "talent" changes none of those problems.

Key issues.:

The general Pet buffs are too weak; the specific Pet buffs are badly focused.

You can't give a percentile buff to stamina and expect both Imp and Voidwalker to benefit; Voidwalker needs the LEAST talent help of any Pet.

If you want to design a tree that works, at minimum, give us talents that address the core issues with our Pets that we've been identifying for six months. Its not as if this is the first time anyone ever went "The Imp is way too weak."

Dump the Spellstone buff entirely, or change spellstones. Move Firestone to the top, or drop it too. The "reduce casting downtime" talents are scarcely worth our time; mainly because they address problems no one was having...

And that's why this tree doesn't work. My Imp needs more mana, my Voidwalker barely does, my Succubus doesn't at all. My VW holds aggro with Torment, his melee is very secondary.

For my Succubus, melee is huge, she has no torment. Give HER the melee damage buff, and make it bigger.

Either design the talent tree to fit the problems and situations that players are having, or don't bother having one. At the moment, Demonology is 1-2 good talents, a couple "ok" ones, and mostly useless junk.
