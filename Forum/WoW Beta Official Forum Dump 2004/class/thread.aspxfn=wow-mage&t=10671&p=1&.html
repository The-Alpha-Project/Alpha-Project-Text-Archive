 1. My own personal fire/frost/arcane damage test | 10/20/2004 7:18:47 AM GMTDT
I have seen what people say here on the DPS and whatnot. I decided to do my own test.

Here is how I tested it:
I went to mobs exactly my level or thereabouts. I used the Naga Explorers in STV as the test subjects. They are level 43 and 44, not casters.

I wanted to test just how much damage I took, how much mana I used, and how long it took to kill the target. I was not testing AE spells. No sheeping or kiting, no special tactics.

Started each and every fight with full HP, full mana, Ice Armor on. No change in equipment during the test. This test was over the course of two days.

35 talent points into Fire (all of them) -
Ended each fight with around 76 to 80% of my HP. Ended with about 60% of my mana, generally.

35 talent points into Frost -
Ended each fight with around 76 to 80% of my HP. Ended with about 60% of my mana, generally.

35 Talent points into Arcane -
Ended each fight with around 76 to 80% of my HP. Ended with about 60% of my mana, generally.

I fought maybe 30 Nagas with each talent tree. The results were pretty much the same every single time. I could nearly exactly predict where my HP and mana would be at the end of the fight. All fights took basically the same time (I didnt use a stopwatch, just feel).

I tried some side experimenting after my tests were done. On the side experiment, I did not heal to full HP/Mana after every single fight. Instead, I turned and fought a second Naga. I did this maybe 20 times.

Again the result were around a 20 to 24% loss of HP from when the fight started, and around a 40% loss of Mana from when the fight started, generally. This was in line with my testing.

Conclusion: The biggest differences in the talents really comes from mana regen, which doesnt seem to be all that much but was noticeable over extended time.

There are surely also PvP differences, and AE differences. Other situations will result in seeing differences, I'm sure (such as getting beat on by multiple mobs). But if you are talking just straight out beating a mob down, the end results seemed the same to me. I saw very little end difference.

Here is some more info.

For each of the three main tests (one per talent tree), I used:

1) The main range damage spell for that particular talent type I was testing (frost bolt, or fireball, or arcane missles).

2) I also used fire blast with all tests.

3) Ice armor and arcane intellect with all tests. No other buffs or protection spells were used at all.

That's it. Really we are a DPS/CC class. I wasnt testing AE's and I wasnt testing CC, so doing the spells above seemed fine to me.

Why use fire blast with each? It is short range, and is there for the casting. Since it is available to all of us whether we improve it with talents or not, I used it.

Strangely enough, I thought fire would do better. Why? Because during the fire test, the Fire Blast spell had improved talents into it. Also fire is supposedly the highest damage, so I hear.

So what talents did I pick?

In each case, I went for talents that improved:

1) range improvement (if possible)

2) Damage improvement (for the ranged main spell. Fireball, frostbolt, arcane missile). Fire blast was improved during the Fire talent tree test.

3) improved cooldown (if at all possible)

4) improved slow or stun, if possible

5) Criticals

6) interruption/cast delay prevention (if possible).

7) reduced mana when casting, if possible

Basically anything that might improve my main ranged damage spell in some way, or fireblast (improved only during the fire test), I did it.

I didnt have to spend all my 35 talent points in one tree, because I didnt use all of them for this test. But I did anyway, if for no other reason then to show they werent spent elsewhere. Each tree tested purely.

At the end of all this, they seemed pretty much the same. After the fighting, Arcane regened mana better.

I really think it's all in what type of mage you want to be. Each talent tree is viable.
