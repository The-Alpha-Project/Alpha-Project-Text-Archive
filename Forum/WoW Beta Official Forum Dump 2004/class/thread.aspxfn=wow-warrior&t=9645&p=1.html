 6. Re: Hit/Miss ratios | 10/13/2004 8:30:10 PM GMTDT
From the warriors in my guild, myself included, there definitely seems to be a consensus that the warrior's ability to solo has taken a hit.

Part of the problems seem to be:

a) Rage decay (bug?)- At the start of combat, it seems we generate the correct amount of rage, only to lose about 6 of it almost instantaneously.

b) Hit/Miss- None of the warriors I've spoken to have actually *parsed* this (watch this space) but there is certainly something there. Ususally there is a certain amount of paranoia (*I'm sure I run slower since the patch*, there's a giant green spinach monster following me around that wasn't before, that kind of nonsense)

However, many of the warriors I've talked to about this in game have played MMORPGs (EQ/DAoC/AC) for a very long time. One develops a "feel" for how such a game is played after a few of them.

c) Weapon delay before battle. This has already been extensively addressed.

d) Overpower no longer being available on "strikes/specials". I think that it is fair to reward players who are skilled or pay attention to the text over a mob's head. Make the skill threshold higher if you like (eg, use it or lose it in 3 seconds)- but having it useable on all hits/misses of any kind is important to soloing.

f) Rage generation is far more of a pain now than before.

These plus what I raised in an earlier thread I think summarise what people are feeling. On charge- again, to stop people getting free rage or being able to close the distance between self & target ad infinitum, all that needs to be done is to limit *all players and mobs* to being charged twice per two minutes.

Warrior A charges mob M, then warrior B charges mob M- warrior C has to wait 2 minutes after B's charge lands. Regardless of how many warriors try to charge, each mob could be set to be chargeable only twice in that time-frame (possibly the same solution for players, or leave the current system for PvP)

With that safeguard in mind, re-instate charging in combat. It will entirely remove the incredible annoyance of a mob breathing on you and then being unable to charge it.

It's certainly not all doom and gloom though.

GOOD changes/nerfs in my opinion:

1) HAMSTRING: It can't be dispelled, cured, healed or gotten rid of in any easy repeatable fashion (excluding death). Not only that, but it can be spammed when you miss.

Reducing the snare capability (as was done with Earthbind and Mage quasi-snares) still leaves it arguably as the warrior's BEST pvp skill.

2) Mortal Strike no longer gives you rage:

Yes yes it was fun while it lasted, but when I complain about it being hard to solo, I want it to be a challenge, NOT trivial. Currently it's a bit too much of a challenge, so much so that I find myself farming food, cloth for bandages and money loots (the first two to heal with, the latter to pay for durability costs).

However, having MS generate rage? Overpowered, fair and justified nerf.

3) Animations

I can dance, swing my weapon and charge without looking like a lobotomized orang-utan. As cosmetic as it may seem, I know it's a lot of hard work, and believe me it makes a huge difference to how the game plays.


A final point to anyone who's read down this far. Why do you think there are less priests than warriors? Priests are obviously more *necessary* to a group trying to do anything difficult. The reason is no one wants to play a class that isn't either fun or efficient to solo with.

The warrior was clearly both pre-patch. Post-patch it is just about fun enough for me to log in for 10-15 mins, smack a few mobs, pay for repairs and then log onto my shaman.

I know this sounds like diatribe, but it demonstrates how much I enjoyed playing my warrior. Yes, some balancing needed to be done, yet I humbly submit it is possible to balance a class without making it less fun to play.
