The original writeup for priests included crowd control. Crowd control would add a lot of fun back to a high level priest. Is the priest going to get back more useful crowd control utilities? Mind control and shackle undead are very situational and rarely used.

Priests have crowd controlling as part of their power set, but it is not necessarily the focus. The development staff feels the spells Psychic Scream, Shackle, and Mind Control are enough to support this side of the Priest's ability.

(Yes, more questions will be answered in the near future. Please be patient with us.)


I don't know whether to laugh or cry at this response in all honesty Caydiem. Many of the Priest players have pointed out the reason why these three spells are lacking, but I'll go ahead and add my reasons as well why I think these are not enough and/or need to be reworked.

Psychic Scream - The problem with this spell is that when used, the likelihood of other mobs joining in is high. The spell is mainly used in the following three ways: when dealing with a single target that you know has no friendlies around and you can keep them from attacking you while using spells to kill them, to escape from a bad situation while soloing (cast and run the opposite direction the mobs go), or in PvP to keep hand to hand classes away so you can whittle them down and eventually kill them.

This spell when used in an instance like the Temple, Zul Farrak, BRD, the Monastery, Gnomeregan, or BRS spells almost instant doom for the party and a wipe it the general result (it's rare that a party can survive the use of Psychic Scream in one of these instances). When used in these instances, you usually get twice to three times as many monsters returning as those that ran.

To fix this spell, this needs to act as a AoE stun instead as this will make it far more viable or make it so that the runners are slowed considerably.

Another problem with this spell is mobs will also break out of the fear effect early with no action taken by the Priest or members of their party. I have seen mobs that are grey to me break from this spell right after being affected while I was soloing (not sure if talent fired and forced them to return or not, but I don't recall anything firing that broke the fear, I've also seen something like this with my Warlock and her fear).

Overall, Psychic Scream needs to be relooked at if the Devs believe that that there is nothing else that needs to be added to the Priest in the way of crowd control.

Mind Control - This is also a very situational spell. For the most part, it isn't useful while soloing nor while PvP. In rare instances, Mind Control does come in useful in PvE, but those times it is mostly where you are looking at two mobs and having them weaken each other or the mob you take over helps the party out immensly with healing and the like (see Dark Iron Medics in BRD for this). Other than these two instances, Mind Control is rarely used by Priests.

Reasons why this is the case is as follows.

First, and foremost, while mind controlling your target, you cannot use any of your skills or it automatically breaks the mind control, this means that someone will have to take primary healing while the Priest has controlled something unless the controllie has healing powers. (Dark Iron Medics are a perfect example of when this spell is useful as the Dark Iron Medic has a very high level Greater Heal [healing somewhere on the order of 3k to 4k health], a very high level Prayer of Healing [healing all in range equal to the highest level Greater Heal a Priest can learn], and a very high level Power Word: Fortitude [giving something on the order to +70 to 80 stamina].) So using Mind Control leaves the Priest not really being able to do much of anything. They take one mob out of the fight, but they also take themselves out of the fight and more often than not, the lose of the player is more deterimental to the party than the loss of the mob to the engine.

Secondly, while the spell is in effect, the Priest is not regaining mana as the spell itself is a channelling spell. So, if the Priest does take over a target, when they finally lose control, the mana they had before they took control is what they have when they lose control. So, not only have to detrimented your party by not being able to heal, you detriment them also by the fact that you mana is still the same after, at maximum control time, a minute.

Thirdly, the Priest that controls the target in question will be attacked until the target dies or the Priest dies when considering the Players vs. the Engine. No amount of taunting from a Warrior or Fading from the Priest will get the target to leave the Priest alone until one or the other dies.

Fourth, if the Priest takes any damage while controlling a target, the target automatically breaks. This means that if a mob in the target's group uses an AoE attack and the Priest takes damage, the target is automatically losed. Also, if there are more in the opposition group than in the group of the Priest and people are not able to pull all the opposition group against them, one of the opposition group will make a beeline for the Priest and attack to break the control.

As you can see from the above four reason, Mind Control has very little use to the Priest. In order to make it a more useful spell, the spell should actually make the target a temporary pet so that the Priest can continue doing their duties (healing, shielding, etc) while they can micromanage the controlled into doing useful things for the group.

Shackle Undead - This spell is very nice, under the right circumstances, more often than not, the Priest does not encounter those circumstances till very high level (Zul Farrak graveyard, Temple, Stralthome, and Schoolmance) or against the Horde faction, specifically against Undead players. Until the Priest reaches those levels, the only places they will see a use is in Razorfen Downs, Dead Mines (dead miners area), Duskwood, Shadowfang Keep, Ruins of Amalath in Darkshore, and Azshara. Outside of the above, the spell has no use.

How to make it more useful, I don't really know other than adding more undead to the game.



I can only hope that you can take this information to the Devs and then have them answer, given the above information, why they think these spells are sufficient as they stand and why the Priest shouldn't gain more CC abilities?

Orisella, lv 60 Priest
