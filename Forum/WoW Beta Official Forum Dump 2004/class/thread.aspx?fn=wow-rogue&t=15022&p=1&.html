 2. Re: Stun lock rogue? | 10/24/2004 3:03:33 PM GMTDT
Here's my mace stun build:

Assassination Talents (23 points)

Malice - 5/5 points
Improved Eviscerate - 3/3 points
Ruthlessness - 3/3 points
Relentless Strikes - 1/1 point
Murder - 1/2
Imp. Slice and Dice - 2/3
Lethality - 5/5
Cold Blood - 1/1
Improved Kidney Shot - 2/3

Combat Talents (26 points)
Imp. Sinister Strike - 2/2 points
Lightning Reflexes - 5/5 points
Precision - 5/5 points
Deflection - 3/5 points
Dual Wield Specialization - 5/5 points
Improved Evasion 2/2
Mace Specialization - 5/5 points
Blade Flurry - 1/1 point

Subtlety Talents (2 points)
Camoflage - 2/5 points

What you should know about hammers is that the hammer speeds have an impact on how often you stun, but not as much as you'd expect. I wield a Hammer of Grace and a Mug O' Hurt, and when I switch the main-hand to a swiftstrike cudgel, I don't gain that many more stuns. So don't sacrifice too much SS damage. Stunning works great, usually goes off once or twice a fight with SnD on. In PvP, you cand do very well by alternating KS with mace stuns, gouge, and cheapshot for ALOT of stun time. In PvE, stuns lower your damage, which ultimately save your casters mana and keep you alive longer. I'd say mace stunning has helped me tank a fair amount by constantly reducing damage... a good way to delay a tough mob til your team is done with the others.

Keep in mind that this build is highly dependent on hitting your enemies with your maces or KS, so you'll have a hard time against rogues. At it's best, it's a total lockdown, and downright unfair. I favor crippling poison with this build as maces do good damage so long as you're making contact. Keep them stunned!

 3. Re: Stun lock rogue? | 10/24/2004 5:43:47 PM GMTDT
I agree; a stun build is perhaps the most powerful and group friendly build, with perhaps the exception of subtleteyw ith improved sap and hemmorage, but only in certain situations.

A Stun Rogue and Shadow Priest is perhaps the best duo combination, though mobs that are immune to stun are obviously much more difficult.

Between Disarm, Mace Stun, and Kidney Punch, I can keep mid fifties elites at a very low threat level for a fair portion of the fight, without using any of my five minute timers.

If one or the other of the duo has herbalism and alchemy, then things are simply that much better.

Six percent is not somethign overly reliable however; you can chain stun one mob the entire battle. and then onyl get one stun the entire next fight. But overall three seconds of stun is far more valuable than one extra sword attack. Dagger specialization is a bit harder to gauge, since the critical chance stacks with backstab and ambush bonuses, but stun is far more useful when in a solo or duo situation.
