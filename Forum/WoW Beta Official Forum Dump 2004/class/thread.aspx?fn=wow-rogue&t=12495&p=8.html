Blocking/parrying mobs are warrior or rogue-class mobs. I don't know enough about game designing to fully explain, but in order to prevent each mob being its own unique class (which would take way too much time and memory), monsters are divided into classes similar to players. This is why some mobs use totems, some charge you, some backstab you, and some frost nova you.

It worked like this in Everquest too; the big AoE-casting dragons were "bard" class mobs since their AoE worked essentially the same as a bard song.

Cross-posted in the general forum thread of the same topic.

I've been paying a lot more attention to my combat log since this issue first came up, and I've noticed a few patterns.

1st: Tyren is correct in that our position relative to the target is causing some of the misses. By moving from the target a little the miss-strings seem to be broken. I tend to think of this as a temporary solution. In general, I need to make a very small (ie tapping a movement key once) adjustment, and not always closer to the target. Sometimes a left or right movement, our a step backward fixes the problem. Since I don't use backstab that much, my position relative to the target shouldn't make a difference.

2nd: The strings of misses we rogues have been seeing applies mostly to our specials. My miss rate for all my attacks is right on the money if I look at all my attacks in a given session, normals or specials. If I take a look at just my specials hit/miss ratio in that session, my miss rate is a bit higher than it should be. When I look at the individual fights however, I noticed a disturbing pattern.

When I miss with a special, I almost never miss just once. Often I'll miss 3-4 specials in a row, regardless of the number of normal auto-attacks my character gets in between each. During these miss-strings, my normal attacks are uneffected (ie the normal auto-attacks have the appropriate hit/miss ratio). The frequency of these miss-strings is about what I'd expect if my specials hit/miss ratio was accurate (ie instead of missing 1-2 specials per fight, I'll have 1-2 miss-strings of 3-4 misses each per fight). It's almost as if the game gets stuck for a short bit after a miss occurs with a special. I'm not sure what's causing it, but moving my position doesn't seem to effect a miss-string on my specials (although it will effect a miss-string with my normal auto attacks).
