 16. Re: agility versus level, why? | 10/15/2004 12:59:11 AM GMTDT
Q u o t e:
i gain a level, and my crit and dodge% chance go down, why?


2 parts answer:

Sapphy is right. The crit% in the tool tip is comparing against mob of your level. So when you gain a level, the displayed crit% is now comparing against a mob 1 level higher, who is gonna be a bit harder for you to successifully land a crit. Now we do gain a point or two of agil when we level, but usually it takes more then 2 agil per level to maintain the same crit%.

Dodge% is same in that the displayed value is comparing against mob of your level, with one additional factor. Your own defense skill also affects dodge%, in that Every point of defense skill below cap is -0.03% dodge, if I recall correctly. When you level, your def skill cap is raised by 5 while the skill itself is not, so you lose an extra 0.15% of dodge due to that.

This whole system is to make motivate us to constantly seek out better +agil gears to keep our crit/dodge up to date. Otherwise a rogue can just load up on stam gear while still getting the high crit/dodge benefits.
