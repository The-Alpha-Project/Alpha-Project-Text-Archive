There is one thing that stands out to me in the statistics posted here. The fact that CoW provides more reduction than it has listed, combined with the now generally accepted notion that the damage subtraction happens before damage reduction from armor, directly implies that the base damage of the attack is actually less than what he was getting hit for without CoW. Which would mean that there is a penalty for having AC below a certain amount for any given mob. Assuming that the damage reduction for AC is in fact linear, the "actual" base damage of the level 55 grub would be in the neighborhood of 72-96. After the penalty for AC, the range would be boosted up to the 86-114 that was recorded.

This finding could be wiped off the board though if the post-CoW numbers never hit the high end of the mob's range of damage. The same applies if the pre-CoW numbers never hit the low end of the damage. I guess that's why this is called speculating...

Aside from that, Death's numbers indicate the ratio of damage reduced by CoW will be consistent for any player (or pet) tanking the mob, regardless of AC. If it provides 40% reduced damage for a warrior with 6000 AC, it'll provide 40% reduced damage for a warlock with 1500 AC. The exact numbers differ, but the rate at which CoW slows your health drop from melee damage is the same regardless.

Q u o t e:
He is not saying that it doesn't reduce more for you than for the VW, he's saying that the percent of damage reduced is constant.

Um.... Kinda.

I'm saying that the percentage is constant on a given mob regardless of who gets hit, but the percentage could vary from mob to mob. Let's take a case with a mob that does 100 damage per hit (no variance for the sake of simplicity), with a 'lock that has 10% damage reduction from armor and a VW that has 50%.

Base damage: 100
Warlock damage taken per hit: 100 * (1-0.10) = 90
Voidwalker damage taken per hit: 100 * (1-0.50) = 50

Curse of Weakness base damage: 100 - 31 = 69
Warlock CoW damage taken per hit: 69 * (1-0.10) = 62.1
Voidwalker damage taken per hit: 69 * (1-0.50) = 34.5

Damage mitigated for warlock: 90 - 62.1 = 27.9
Damage mitigated for Voidwalker: 50 - 34.5 = 15.5

Percentage mitigated for warlock: 27.9 / 90 = 31%
Percentage mitigated for Voidwalker: 15.5 / 50 = 31%

The percentages are the same. Now let's look at a mob with a base damage of 80, with the same damage mitigation.

Base damage: 80
Warlock damage taken per hit: 80 * (1-0.10) = 72
Voidwalker damage taken per hit: 80 * (1-0.50) = 40

Curse of Weakness base damage: 80 - 31 = 49
Warlock CoW damage taken per hit: 49 * (1-0.10) = 44.1
Voidwalker damage taken per hit: 49 * (1-0.50) = 24.5

Damage mitigated for warlock: 72 - 44.1 = 27.9
Damage mitigated for Voidwalker: 40 - 24.5 = 15.5

Percentage mitigated for warlock: 27.9 / 72 = 38.75%
Percentage mitigated for Voidwalker: 15.5 / 40 = 38.75%

The percentages are still the same, but higher, because the base damage of the mob is lower. Note also that the damage mitigated stays constant regardless of the base damage of the mob. I agree that the description of CoW is lacking right now, but it may be tough to reword it so that it's not confusing to the player base.

Well, I hope this explains my thinking better, and didn't make things worse for you guys. :)
