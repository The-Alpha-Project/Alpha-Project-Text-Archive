 22. Re: Priest Questions for the Developers | 10/12/2004 11:14:00 PM GMTDT
The main issue right now for me in the Holy Talent line:

1) The offensive talents in the Holy line are completely unusable. .1second/rank? +6% damage? Why would anyone want these? First tier shadow and discipline are much better.

2) The healing talents give very low bonuses. Worse than other calsses talent lines. Worse than our own Discipline and Shadow line equivalents (ie. mana regen and damage). For example our best healing boost is "Master Healer" which is the exact same as a first tier Shaman healing talent. We would need to be around level 40 to get full master healer. A Shaman would need to be level 14. This seems very unfair to a class that is supposedly the best healing class in the game.

3) Crit heals are a bizzarre and mostly wasted game mechanic. They are not like damage based heals where you want to cast or attack as much as possible to kill a mob. Healing is designed to be conserved and to be maximally efficient. If you do this well, you will waste any crits most of the time. Suggest that overflow from crit heals become a HoT or HW:Sheild. Or that they are removed. Or minimum the chance to get them is increased.

My belief is that Blizzard wants to limit Priest healing effectiveness because of all the complaints that a group must have a priest to survive difficult instances. In my mind, these complaints stem more from the shortage of priests, because psycologically people don't want to play a healing role and because the class has lost a lot its fun with the 5 second rule et. al. However, there are some people that do love to play the healer and they should not be punished for this. Druids and Shaman that take their healing talent lines are now very good healers and provide other benifits. People need to learn to use them. But when they want a pure healer the Holy Priest should be the best at it. As is, it is marginal. Maybe the 40-60 spells have something to offset this?

There are other concerns with the class that other folks have brought up. For the most part I have enjoyed the Priest greatly, but it has certainly been a class that has degraded in fun factor consistently since the alpha.

I cannot stand critical heals being such a focal point for the holy tree.

A critical heal is something that is a nice bonus (even though on greater heal it tends to be completely pointless, going way over the target's HP max). It is not something that can be counted on, especially when planning on what heal to use.

I never ever say to myself "I could greater heal this target, I have the time and the mana, but I'd rather flash heal in case I get a crit!". I really feel that a talent which raises my crit heal amount is a waste... I only use it as a prereq and because a crit heal is required for other talents. And having spiritual healing (such a vital talent!) tied to something so infrequent and unpredictable is only frustrating and disappointing.

Tying talents to crits also leads to silly gameplay mechanics, like overuse of prayer of healing just because of the greater crit chance, or spamming holy nova and praying for a crit.

I guess bottom line: critical heals are nice, but having holy talents count on them so very heavily makes the talent line maddeningly frustrating and borderline useless.
