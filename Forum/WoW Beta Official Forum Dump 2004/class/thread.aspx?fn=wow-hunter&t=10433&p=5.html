Q u o t e:
The Hunter is a vicious damage dealer. With their ability to pull extremely well and hit monsters hard from far away, they certainly help take down monsters more quickly. Their pets add to the damage count and can aid somewhat in managing monster aggro. The variety of traps allows the Hunter to control crowds to an extent, as well. The combination of their extreme range, decent damage, and trap capability, Hunters are the perfect pullers for any group.


Having the standards set for us is a great start. While both we and Blizzard know they haven't driven Hunters to this point yet, I feel we all have a responsibility to help reach this goal, rather than complain about how underdeveloped our class is at this point. That being said, I'll just list my suggestions, and pray that someone actually reads them.

Disclaimer: these are just suggestions. They're not how I feel the game needs to be, and they can easily be worked with or scrapped... I feel it's much better to have too many suggestions than too little. Also, some of these may have already been suggested. If so, take it as an agreement.

- Give Hunters some sort of benefit to the party that hasn't been implemented to another class yet. If the intent is to make them the "perfect pullers", this is probably the best outlet. Allow them to pull one mob from a group of three.

- Addition to being the "ultimate pullers", allow them to hit from a farther range. Possibly increase the amount of damage you deal to a mob the closer you get to counter the advantage this would give.

- Give pets stronger diversities. Certain pets need to be better at creating threat, tanking, and damaging. All this could be done through unique pet skills, or plainly through stats alone.

- Make traps more useful. If their purpose is to give hunters some form of crowd control, allow them to use it when crowd control is needed most... which, in my opinion, is NOT at the beginning of a battle. Crowd control is needed when the "crowd" is out of "control", which tends to happen mid-fight with adds and whatnot. So the most simple solution would be: allow hunters to place traps once per fight, but allow them to do it at any time during the fight. Reset when hunters are no longer in combat. At the very least, do this to the ice traps. Or, if traps absolutely must be placed pre-battle, make them more potent.

- A long distance CC would work wonders... possibly a net toss of some kind. Maybe even an AE net that lasted for less and less time for the more targets it hit.

- An ankle biting trap. You know, one of the old fashioned kinds. THIS is the kind of trap that should be dropped pre-battle. Make the trap absorb some of the damage that's dealt to the trapped mob. The more damage you deal to the trap, the faster it gets to breaking and setting the trapped mob free.

- Different modes of shooting. Shotgun mode would allow a spray that could hit multiple mobs. Sniper rifle would require you to be farther away to deal more damage. Pistol mode would allow you to shoot while running, but would deal far less damage. Machine gun would use up tons of ammo, but drain tons of mana as well.

I'll probably think of more as days pass, but this is all I got for now.
