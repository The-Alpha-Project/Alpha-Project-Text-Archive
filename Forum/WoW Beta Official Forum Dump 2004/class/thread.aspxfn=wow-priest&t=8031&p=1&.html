Read the other forums, most of the classes have a blue post from Caydiem letting them know what upcoming changes are being contemplated for their class.

Our class board is one of the few that didn't get such a post, presumably because we don't have any changes coming...

I'm with everyone else, I'd really appreciate some changes made to the Holy tree, it doesn't need a lot, but it needs something to make it attractive. I had three ideas, none of which were unique to me, but in isolation I think they're enough to make going Holy more credible:

1) A Talent for reducing cast time on heals. The standard here from other classes seems to be cutting 0.5s off the time of a spell, which is probably enough. I admit I'd prefer a 3s Greater Heal vs a 3.5s one tho. This would get us closer to parity in terms of cast time with Druids and would be a concrete benefit you can count on every time.

2) The effect of Holy Specialization should be 2% per rank, not 1%. Right now I don't crit enough to make this talent worthwhile, and it's kind of the stepping stone and basis for other talents that are also made unworthwhile because I can't get crits to a reasonable point. I say this as a level 35 Priest that has around 150 int, so it's possible that at higher int levels the crit rate is enough, but I'm starting to doubt that. Honestly after putting 25 levels of Talent points into Holy, including every crit talent, it should be doing well for me and it isn't.

3) Spiritual Healing needs to be looked at in light of the 5s mana regen rule. It should either give full mana regen for 10s or be 15s like the Shadow talent.

Basically the way I see it today, there's no reason not to go full Shadow until 60, and then pay the price to respec to Holy.

The continued discussion of this issue with no response at all is kind of demoralizing, since it makes me feel like the devs aren't listening to our issues. Look at the posts and you obviously get a sense that the Holy line is the red-headed step child of the tree, and yet there's no blue text letting us know that *at least* they're considering what to do about it.

One thing I loved about DAoC was that there was a class representative for every class in the game. That person was who was blessed with access to a board with a higher signal to noise ratio and as a result had interaction with devs. They then were able to come back to the community at large and give a status of the class post on a fairly frequent basis.

The problem with the DAoC system was that there were sooo many classes, it was fairly uneven in how good the representatives were. Some classes had AWOL reps, others had frickin game designer geniuses. ;)

Blizzard, with a much smaller number of classes, could get by with fewer people tasked with this responsibility, and as a result classes that aren't feeling the love will get some attention.

The big huge risk Blizzard is running at this point is that when they DO post, it's gonna be in even a bigger fishbowl than exists now. That problem is going to get worse, and incents people to not post. It's a vicious circle. The only way to turn the vicious circle into a virtuous cycle is to change the dynamic. Doing that will either require a consistent behavior change by devs (difficult) or a new party.
