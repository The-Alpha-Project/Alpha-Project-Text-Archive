I may play a Horde druid in retail. I really love the class, but that doesn't mean the class doesn't need a lot of work.

Druids don't seem to have a defined role in a group. Druid buffs aren't strong enough to make people want us in a group just for buffs. The damage dealing in cat form isn't enough for people to want a druid in a group for dps. And the bear form cannot tank enough to be the main tank in a group. While druids can heal decently, they cannot rez in case things get ugly. So people will almost never rely on a druid for a main healer in a group.

In order for people to want a certain class in the group the class has to bring a certain quality to the table, something which it excells at. Warriors? Tanking. Priests? Healing and rez. Mages and rogues? Dps and crowd control. Druids? A druid has no niche.

Sorry for the long-winded post, but a big part in determining my character come retail is how much my class will be needed or wanted in groups. I am not in a guild, so I don't want to be level 40 or so stuck in Ironforge all the time spamming, "LFG," with no responses. Which kind of makes me not want to pick a druid.
