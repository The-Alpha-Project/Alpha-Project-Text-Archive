 1. Mage feedback, problems and suggestions. | 10/5/2004 5:31:36 AM GMTDT
Let me start this with a whine to point out problems.
My mage’s gear is mediocre. Some is bad, some is good but it’s average on a whole. I’m a bit concerned with Stamina more than Intellect or Spirit, yet somehow my main stat is Spirit. I’m sorry, but this stat is near WORTHLESS with the five second rule. I can’t avoid accumulating Spirit, and it’s getting annoying.

So I’m a mage, the #1 BURST damage class. Yeah right, only if I am doing AE. Warriors are hitting for 2,000, Rogues CONSISTENTLY hitting for 1600. Shamans and Priests are nearly un-killable in PvP by a mage. Yet the worst hurt to my mage pride are Warlocks. I realize they are the anti-caster caster, but come on this class is supposed to be sustained damage. With only two talent lines, Warlocks are out damaging mages in both the short and long term.

Hunters are still new and receiving ‘tweaking.’ Paladins however are drooling with the knowledge that they are next in line for talents. I can hear paladin guild-mates drooling in Teamspeak as they discuss possible talents. I have good faith they will go from a solid class to strongest FAST with their talents.

Casters in general NEED work, especially mages. What people need to know is, will you finish talents and game content and then balance; or will you continue to work on balance as you go along.

Current fixes seem to be to extreme; giving huge swings of power, or removing it via a nerf. Honestly, the worst thing Blizzard ever did to mages was give them talents first. Now they are a crippled class from consistent down-tweaking. People used to complain until it turned to whining about mages. One critical with a 6 second casting time that hits for 1600! Melee now wouldn’t think twice about that damage, they would just return the favor.

The scariest thing now is resists. To keep up with damage a mage MUST specialize fire, forfeiting usefulness of the other two lines. Problem is the grand percentage of fire immune creatures, and fire resist gear. A warrior could run around in complete thorium gear. Loosing hit points and about 15-20% damage. However, now the warrior is nearly impervious to mages, or any caster for that matter. Yet, they can still hit for 1600 after their damage reduction.

I honestly only see balance in druids now, but I don’t play one and could easily be wrong.

1600 seems to be the magic number; first mages, then rogues and warriors (and for a while shadow priests). Next will be paladins if Blizzard isn’t cautious.

~~~~~

These are the major problems I see every day that are annoying and a slap in the face to mages. This game is very melee heavy currently and needs some changes. Luckily, I have complete faith in Blizzard to take the time to balance correctly. Here are what suggestions to mages and casters I can make.

~~~~~

Plus damage, it’s overpowered on low level spells and scorch. Determine a maximum percentage +dmg can add to a spell. For example: 50%, now +dmg can only give a total 50% damage boost of a spells damage including talents. Next is the decision if +dmg applies to critical hits. If not then you can hit for 2.5x spell’s normal damage, and if so, it’s three times. The ‘up to X damage’ is really annoying, have the item say +X damage period. The players will know that it will not exceed the pre-determined maximum percent added.

Giving up 10sta 13int 13spi for +9 damage as I switch from green lens to spell power goggles is a joke. The choice is obvious here, +dmg is a tradeoff of bigger stats versus a bigger boom. The question is where does a player prefer numbers? Personally, I love +dmg and the idea behind it, I just wish to achieve a reasonable amount of +dmg.

Items should be a tradeoff. With stats giving a large result per stat, there becomes a grand dependence on stats. Therefore with mages stats, resists and +dmg are the tradeoffs to consider. I hope that as the game progresses and expands this doesn’t change too much. Everquest hit that point, and eventually gear just becomes ridiculous. I never want to have 3 +dmg items to hit a cap, 3 items for max resists and the rest to boost stats. I never want to have it all. I just want to be specialized in one area.

Fix Arcane Missiles; it’s a bit to long to cast or to little dps. The risk of channeling for five seconds doesn’t payoff. Maybe the spell should be a 3 second channel; half a second of initial channel, and then 1 missile for each additional .5 seconds. Plus critical hits on this spell just do not work right. 191 per missile and critical missiles hit for 245 or 291, that’s just plain weird.

How about an arcane talent to reduce casting time on all spells? 5 points, drop half a second off all non-channeled spells. Casting times seem a bit long sometime. An NPC can hit me 2-3 times on any given spell (non-instant), out damaging my spell with ease.

I once read a post recommending a certain percent of a spell line’s damage being non resistible depending on talent points in the line. This idea was pure genius and should be re-examined. It was something like this:
% Unresisted Talents spent in that line
5 10
12 20
20 30
33 complete line bought
Something like this should be examined, maybe switch the effect to effectiveness. So heals or nukes were more effective if you don’t like un-resistible

Another good idea is one made by Myko. Lower spell damage across the board, but then make int or spirit a spell damage modifier. This allows casters to do about what they do now, and more as gear progresses. Thus keeping up with the boosts melee gain with ‘mudflation’.

Or maybe stats should be different for each class, such as Ethren said. Agility reducing casting time or spirit affecting criticals while int is mana pool. Something to those lines would be fantastic.

One of the last great ideas I heard was by Viserion. He suggested items help spells, such as blizzard lasts longer. He also suggested that items like these have requirements, must have 3 ranks in this talent etc. How about items add to talent points? Like a helm that helps frost nova cool down, or gloves that help the talent causing chill effects to last longer. This would be very similar to items that had effects like +2 fireball in Diablo. That was a great idea for improving items. Doing that here would give another way around, and allow just a bit more strength to casters.

Overall, there’s some need to tune up mages, and down some of the other classes. I think dmg/ delay on weapons should be examined. As well, the stat bonuses seem larger on chain and plate items (being that those classes are gear dependant). I’ve been having a great time playing so far and am thankful for the experience. Hopefully some of these suggestions are useful.
