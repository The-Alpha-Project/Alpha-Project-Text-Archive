 1. Pet Ability Restrictions: Bad Idea | 9/30/2004 8:57:30 PM GMTDT
I recently learned that my crab couldn't learn the bite ability. I found this odd, as he has a maw which no doubt functions. Furthermore, I found this distressing as my crab was now evidently not as useful as another potential pet.

This is and will be a problem in the future for two reasons:

1. There will eventually be a "best pet", that being the one capable of learning the most useful abilities. This would completely defeat the purpose of so many unique and interesting animals being present in the game. For example, a pet which can learn bite, claw and growl is more useful than a pet which can learn claw, growl and cower, as cower only presents its usefulness in very certain situations.

2. This will force people to abandon long-time pets to capture new ones or continue onward with a potentially inferior pet. I already hated this fact at level 10. I can't fathom what it would have been like at level 50, had I brought my crab to that level before realizing it didn't have the same potential as my raptor.

Thus, I recommend allowing all pets to learn all abilities, even if they don't feasibly make sense.

However, I offer a solution to this which should not only make things fair but also add an additional element of strategy into the game.

Upon its inception, your pet bar has four slots free to add additional abilities. Right now, I believe abilities which aren't on that bar are still auto casting. I say set them as inactive unless they are on that bar. This solves two problems:

1. Abilities like cower which I don't want to auto use will still be auto cast unless I have it on the bar and turned off. This would solve that problem immediately.

2. You can stick with whichever pet you like based on personal preference, but you will be forced to choose your pet's four abilities for the next battle based on the situation at hand. This is exactly like reequiping for specific situations, only you're doing so for your pet.

I love the current setup which requires that you catch animals to learn their abilities, then teach those abilities to your current pet. This is like collecting weapons for your pet to use, making it as though you're outfitting your personal assistant in the best gear you can find. This makes the pet system vastly better.

But having to give up on your long time pet because it can't learn the abilities you want it to just plain sucks. Furthermore, you have to choose between utility and personal preference. I do that all the time in real life. I don't think we should have to do so in WoW.

So in short, place no restrictions on pet abilities, but only allow four active/usable abilities at once. It'll allow hunters to have the pet of their choice but it will force them to devise a strategy based on the abilities of their pet and reconfigure said strategy for the situation at hand.

Pets already differ on stats, but this won't severely change how they perform in battle (once everything is balanced, I hope). However, special abilities will more than likely be the deciding factor in whether or not you win or lose. It should be your choice in those abilities which determines the outcome, not your pet's inability to use those abilities.

I disagree with the original poster.

There cannot be a "best pet" because the definition of "best" would vary for each player. Certainly, power gamers would tend towards finding and using a particular pet that they can get the most out of, but they also tend towards wearing the same equipment, fight the same way, go to the same places to hunt and quest, etc.

Various hunter players-- those who aren't particularly interested in power leveling, at least-- likely will choose a pet for aesthetic reasons, for roleplaying reasons or because a pet's ability pool best complements their playing style.

Finally, I highly doubt any one pet will have the ability pool to learn all that ever will be available. Right now, cats are the only pets that can use growl, bite, claw AND cower; but if/when abilities like charge, poison, stun, or knockdown come around they certainly won't be able to learn all of those.

A "best pet" is the pet with which you are most likely to succeed in whichever battles you fight, period.

There can and will be a best pet, unless absolutely extensive studies are done as to the value of special abilities, and even that is unlikely to work.

Look at it this way: right now, there are useless spells in the game. Aspect of the monkey, for one, adds 8% to your ability to dodge and is vastly outdone by hawk which adds 20% to your ranged attacks.

Pet abilities right now are rather straight forward, but what happens when there are 50 abilities to choose from? There's no way in a frozen hell that Blizzard will be able to account for the potential balance and usage for all of these. A lot of the ideas for spells and abilities sound good in theory, but in practice are nothing shy of worthless or they already have a better alternative out there. It won't be long before someone figures out a combo which allows their pet to infinitely stun an opponent or something along those lines.

Then, everyone will be out shopping for that particular pet. Will it get fixed? At some point, but the next "best" pet in line will just pop up to take its place.

There are too many variables to ensure there will be no "best" pet if abilities are restricted. Best case scenario, there will be 2-3 best pets, but I guarantee you that, when that happens, every hunter past level X will be dragging around one of those 3 pets. Guilds will demand that their hunters acquire one of three said pets because they are the best for combat and PvP. Just like the already ask that you acquire specific armor and weaponry, so too will they require you to use the most effective pet in the game.

I don't mean to sound like a voodoo futurist here, but the fact is, certain spells/special abilities are already more useful than others. When the pet type which can learn the most useful spells/abilities is found, it will be the dominant pet.

I would love, LOVE, to be wrong on this, but thus far, they can't even get PvP right. In fact, it's arguable that you can't ever get PvP right in a MMO because players will always find loopholes and techniques which are essentially exploits.

This being the case, how do we expect them to perfectly balance X number of pet abilities, allocate these abilities to each and every pet so that no pet has a leg-up over another, and ensure that no 2-3 pets are the most prized for the special abilities they can learn?

It won't be due to lack of trying, but I think it will be impossible.
