 2. Re: PVP Scenarios | 10/21/2004 7:21:46 PM GMTDT
Your lack of PvP fighting really shows... You are partly right on most of the secnarios.

First of all, Hunters are great for group PvP. With our DoT, Debuff, Mana-drain, Stun, multi-hit, AoE attack, etc, we can lay down a lot of anoyance on the other players :) I love chasing down enemies and getting the last shot on them while the real PvP players keep tanking and attacking the main group... great stuff!

As for your scenarios, let me address them one at a time...

Warriors: Yeah... fighting warriors sucks... If they get in range, game over 98% of the time. So... just don't let them get in range! This is where the skill of playing a hunter comes in. You can run faster so long as you avoid mob's. Yeah, we'll lose 98% of the time because of this, but we do have a chance. Wait for the talents to come in (*crossing fingers*).

Mages: Biggest problem with mages is if you let them get in range in the first place. First off, they can only blink once typically for the whole fight. Even though our viper sting sucks, if you do some skirting and just tag 'em with it a few times they will be good as useless. Again, we lose a lot (sheep == death for us). Hopefully they will increase the mana viper sting does.

Warlocks: yeah, warlocks suck :) stay out of fear range! I have yet to really test the distances on this, but it should be somewhat possible. Try using viper sting on them a few times before going in for the kill...

Paladins: Paladins are the one class that hunters should never lose to. Seriously. If you lose to a paladin (near equal level) you need to not be playing PvP as a hunter. Just run around them. They have no ranged attacks. They have only one stunn (which means they get 2 or 3 hits on you, before you wing-clip them and get out of range again). Yeah, so what, they got tons of armor and shields and etc. Just keep running around them and they'll die quick. They can't run away from you (you run faster). Paladins are easy... I love them :)

Druid: If you fight a smart druid right now, yeah, yer owned. However, most druids arn't smart enough to get in our "can't attack" range. And, to let you in on a little secret... at range, we totally outdamage druids. Add on top of that viper stings... they can't keep you rooted forever. Unfortuantely druids are starting to realize they can't keep distance on us and win... :(

Shawman: Never fought 'em being horde and all, maybe an alliance char can pipe in on this one.

Priest: Priests suck. I typically don't die against them, but I can never kill them either. With their shield and our DPS while running, they can always run to a town before we do enough damage to take 'em out. Also, we can typically run and stay out of their range. If you are lucky you can viper sting away their mana early enough before they are safe... just don't count on it. Just make sure you dismess your pet, cuz Priest will mind-control you and have you attack your pet. By the time it wears off, both you AND your pet are near death...

And the two classes you forgot:

Rogue: Rogues are probably a 50/50 for us. If you can survive the first round they dish out (which we usually can't) you have a chance of wing-clipping them and escaping. Remember, if they sprint to catch up, tag 'em with a concussion shot... slows 'em down :) They are easier than warriors in the fact that if you keep them at range, they have a harder time of hitting you (almost like a paladin).

Hunters: Good luck... hope your equipment is better than theirs :)

Yeah, we need a lot of love for PvP, but we are definately not worthless in PvP. If you get the jump on someone, you should be able to give them a lot of pain.

Omi's hunter portrayal is far more accurate.

A lot of it comes down to how smart the players are. A lot of pvpers pick certain classes, so the others appear to suck. Big difference between a smart druid and a newb druid, for example, like Omi said. Same goes for hunter in most situations.

The whole paladin outlasting you bit... well that's what paladins are supposed to do. If there's ever a counter class to paladins though, I would definately say the hunter. With mail and decent hp, you should never be in trouble. With snares, paladins fairly low mana pools and your mana drain, you should be able to kite them until they're out of mana. A paladin is dead without mana.

Hunters definately need a lot of love though. That blue post mentioned "changes" for hunters next patch as well as talents. I hope it's for the best. Primarily, due to the hunters attack range, they need to be on the move constantly. And their abilities really suck on the move. They need to be played just like a mage really, staying out of combat and relying heavily on insta cast nukes and snares. They don't really have either of these.

As for the warlock bit, fear increases your movement speed so one fear should easily put you out of range for the second fear unless you get a bad run of luck and run around in circles instead of a straight line. The range on Fear is really low though. You should be able to stay out of fear range pretty easily.

Also, whenever you find yourself sitting at range scoping out some prey, use rapid fire first. Keep firing until they come at you, and use con shot. I know it's only 3 seconds, but you can get off a few more shots, buy yourself more time. The rapid fire really makes a difference. You should be able to, with a good ranged weapon, mow down just about any equal level caster, priest included, with rapid fire.

Lastly, Bliz and everyone else has said it a thousand times... this pvp system is built for group pvp. I know some classes do better solo, so it makes other classes like the druid or hunter look worse than they are. In group pvp, however, as Bliz intended, both classes are really effective. All of your complaints about the hunter seem to revolve around any enemy getting into melee range, because that's the hunters weakness. That's not so easily done in group pvp. Go to Hillsbrad and participate in some of the constant warring and battles going on there. I bet you'll be much more pleased with your hunter.

Like Omi said, she has much more fun mowing people down while the other pvpers engage in battle. That's what the hunter does. They stand back and offer support fire. Pick up First Aid, head out to Hillsbrad, and I bet you'll be having a lot more fun. If you've got the levels to use Flare, then you'll even be apprecaited.

(Haven't ever seen Flare work but haven't seen it properly tested either. I know in battles there are constantly rogues lurking in your ranks and they'll effectively assassinate one of your guys and escape scott free. A good hunter with a working Flare could prevent this. Mark as well would help against rogues.)
