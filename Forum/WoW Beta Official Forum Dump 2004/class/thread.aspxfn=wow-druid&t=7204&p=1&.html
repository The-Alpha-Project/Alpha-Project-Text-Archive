 1. Druids are a solo class | 10/8/2004 10:55:34 AM GMTDT
Recently I made a trip to the Alterac Mountains. The first thing I see is a level 37 ogre. I was 38 at the time. Seeing as the ogre was a level under, I engage in battle with the normal Starfire/moonfire etc. This didn't seem like a normal ogre, however. Only midway did I actually find out he was an elite mob (Harder to tell without the +). Yet even after getting an add (36 elite) midway through, I still took down the 37 ogre with health and mana to spare. No potions were used. No items. No help. No forms. I came back a little later and dispatched a few more elites, this time with 50% health and mana.

Now, it's not right for any class to be able to take down same level elites solo, and it should be changed. Herein lies the problem: Blizzard has been fixing the wrong things. After druids all over made outcries of underpowered druids, blizzard spent a little time to fix certain aspects - forms, roots, added talents, and while it's debatable how much has been fixed, there has undoubtedly been some progress. Blizzard however, ignored a crucial part of a class: Groups.

First of all, I should not have won that battle with the 37 elite ogre, especially when I used little strategy, no items or extra buffs, got an add, weak equipment, and completely ignored forms. But let's take a look at why I won. I by no means killed the ogre quickly. It was a long, drawn out battle in which his health dropped slowly.

It was a battle of attrition. I dealt sustained DPS to the ogre while healing when my health got low. I had sustained melee damage and cheap moonfire damage to keep the damage going. A mob or anyone would have to first burn through my mana pool before touching me, and then would have to outlast me. Non healers cannot do this - They are limited to their single bar of health. Priests cannot do this - their damage is limited to their mana pools. Paladins and Shamans most likely could. However, Shamans are already overpowered and Paladins suffer from the exact same thing as druids - great in terms of soloing but weak in groups. Go figure, the three hybrids are the best at outlasting opponents. This is in no way a coincidence. Hybrids are able to use every aspect to their advantage, dealing damage and healing at the same time while using a minimal amount of mana. The counterbalance is that because they can do everything, they cannot be the best at any one thing which makes them weaker in groups with no defined role.

I am aware that this is nothing new. Most people know this already. But the crux of this problem is that people see a class's soloability and claim the class is balanced or even overpowered. Even worse, another set of people look at the inability in groups as underpowered. Simply put, every time druids have been improved, their solo abilities have been improved while the group abilities haven't been changed as much. Blizzard has been fixing the wrong aspects.

Doesn't convince you? What about this:

We are given an okay cc/snare in Entangling Roots and Nature's Grasp. It's best used for soloing as it doesn't work in most instances.

We are given a self res ability. It is useless in groups and only usable soloing (Though limited).

Our big group heal, Tranquility, is useless.

Our only AE requires 30 talents in balance (At eariest, level 41) and isn't that great. It is most useful in instances.

We have no res, yet we are supposed to be a main healer.

Forms are geared for soloing with the mana regen and that they aren't too effective in groups. Not to mention that they are dwarfed by warriors and rogues.

Travel forms don't provide any buffs or any help to group members.

Okay, so there most likely are still doubters. I tanked all of so-and-so instance, you say. I've contributed a lot to my groups, you say. People always say I'm effective in forms, you say.

My answer is, so what? Being underpowered doesn't mean not viable. It means we are weaker. It means there is less of a reason to choose a druid over a warrior, or a priest, or whatever other class. All you have to do is take a look at the disproportionality of our group ability and our solo ability to see the huge disparity. And you might also notice that our abilities diminish by about half when we enter a group.

Druids were made a solo class by being a hybrid. Blizzard enforced this by giving druids mainly solo abilities, and with skills that can be done better with another class. Blizzard then made it even more clear with the changes to shapeshifting. Blizzard then stamped "Solo Class" in big red letters onto druids by giving us self res.

So what’s the future for druids? Balance between soloing and grouping, I hope. Original abilities, I hope. Useful talents, I hope. I fixed class I hope. Or they will make our buffs and heals self cast only. And why not? Druids are an overpowered solo class.
