 1. So what's to be done with the mage? | 10/21/2004 6:24:54 PM GMTDT
Let me get this out of the way -- I love playing my mage... at least I did.

Since I have friends that play on the PvE servers, and unfortunately, they're mostly alliance, I started a new character -- a rogue.

My rogue is now 43, where my Mage has languished at 36 since. Why? The rogue is more fun to play, has more utility, and has more combat options.

Why isn't the mage this fun?

The rogue can stealth, either sneak up and launch one of 4 different openers from behind, or if I'm detected, quickly cheap-shot and then go into a number of different combos. Then there's multiple finishing moves that are all situational to the group or what I'm fighting. Sap, Blinding and Kick to CC, disorient and silence... great damage, fast fights, and skills that just make all my attacks more effective, as trees have bounuses that reinforce each other. Oh, AND poisons, which just make me do more damage or cause other addl. effects, again situational to the creature I'm fighting.

The mage I... shoot my primary damage spell, then my other damage spells, then repeat. No strategy to which spells I use (as you really have to focus on mostly one tree or another), no combat utility except for Sheep, no situational spells that are used depending on fighting ranged / casters / melee / running mobs, etc. Yes, CoC can slow, and nova can freeze, but you always use these -- there's never any hard choice of "this or this" during combat for the mage, and that's disappointing.

I don't get it. The mage used to be pretty darn sweet about 4 patches ago. Now, I sit a lot after a few battles. I can't invis, so travel is a pain in PvP, Pyro is no longer good for much, I can't open locks, poly is my only CC option, I have no damage shields (although all the enemy mages seem to), I can't do any of the cool "twiddle" stuff that mages should do, either. Another post in the forum mentioned a "heat weapon" abil to disarm opponents temporarily. Where's the fun utility stuff like this that's useful in combat (bread / water / teleport is all non-combat utility) Why can't I use my dampen magic and amplify on enemy targets? And just what is detect magic good for?

As much as I love some of the fire and arcane talent trees, there's no cross-enhancing like there is for other classes. Arc / Fire or Fire / Arc (sorry, I can't bring myself to use frost any longer) and that's about it. The only question is how far you go down each tree.

Sigh. I didn't mean to get on a rant, but I want to have a hard choice between playing my mage and playing my rogue, but at the moment, there's really no choice, and that's disappointing.

Am I off base here? I mean I love the concept of the mage, but the implementation just seems so narrow and one-directional that it's hard to rationalize playing them atm.

Blizz, please tell me that you're aware of the mage's narrow role and are going to do something about it. I don't want to do everything, I just want to be more than the one-trick-pony I feel like.
