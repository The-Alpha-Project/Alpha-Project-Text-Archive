Dagger Spec does NOT belong out of the combat tree. Those that DO want it out of the combat tree are Assassination rogues who want the extra crit chance.

I'm OK with what they tried to do by moving dagger spec up the tree. They won't come out and say it, but people were building Lethiality, Opportunity, IMproved BS, Improved Ambush, Hemmorage, Dagger Spec, Malace, Cold Blood builds and blowing the competition away with crits hitting Anywhere between 400 - 600 damage per backstab, 1200 on the opener, and 1K on the closer. That was HUGE damage, and WAY to much to do with just talents.

Some rogues had a good 1/4 chance of hitting a crit, which is a bit much with the talent build as you had it last time around.

They moved dagger spec down so the assassination people woudln't have it quite so easy, without taking it out of reach of the combat people. If you're combat speced you get great crit percentage (malace, dagger spec) and good backstabs, but you suffer the opening ambush and have a DAMN hard time even pushing out lethiality for the rest of it. Assassination tree specialists get all the multipliers on their specials (SS, Evisc, Ambush, BS) but their crit percentage is a bit lower.

That is why dagger spec is moved in the tree.

IMHO (and it's only an opinion, untested this time and strictly out of the air) I think talents are setup wrong. Personally I think things would be easier to balance if certain talents or taking a certain level in a tree locked out other abilities. Instead of moving dagger spec to 20 point cost to stop the major Assassins from taking it, it should simply have been locked out from them.

I don't know how they'd do it and don't want to give it any more thought, but it feels to me like moving the dagger spec up to 20 was a really bad idea.
