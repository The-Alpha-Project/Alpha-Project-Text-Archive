 18. Re: Why do druids have so little mana? | 8/4/2004 1:46:39 AM GMTDT
Druids have so little mana because we are stretched so thin on stat distribution; so many are appealing to us that I have often found on my Druid moreso than every other class I will take a piece of armor that has the most "bulk" stats in lieu of my premier stat choice (Spirit) should that bulk be chunky enough (e.g. a +lot of STR/STA/INT piece might very well take the place of a singular Spirit piece if the total is hefty). We end up with more rounded solo/quest group stats with a penchant for our favorite, and a bag full of Int gear for instances. It's not necessarily a system we cannot function within currently; if a Druid chooses to go all out Int he can, at the cost of efficiency, our Int:mana ratio isn't a whole lot worse than Priests or Mages, its 9 vs. their 10 last I checked. Combine this with the lack of stellar stat pieces in comparison to Cloth (not to mention Mail or Plate) and as a class we don't tend to have a very large manapool on average, often wearing Cloth in instances.

I've attributed a very large part of this to the breakdown of Leatherworking at the beginning of the push, wherein nearly all sets were taken over by AGI friendly gear and/or Mail Shaman converts to begin with, most evident in the Artisan lines, but as I see the current highest end instances it has additionally become apparant the lack of Leather caster friendly gear. In some respects we should be seeing proportionately less due to half of the Leather casting contigent picking up Mail (Shamen) but it is disproportionately low in every respect. A capped "high end" is always a funny thing to examine, in that everything can turn around in one level, but in all honesty Leatherworking went totally useless in the 180s this build, and I couldn't bring myself to pick up a worthless Artisan line despite the desire to thoroughly test; theres just nothing there, and instance drops reflect the same problem. A little more caster friendly Leather saturation should be examined from both the 180+ (especially Artisan, what a let down!) area of Leatherworking and the level 30-50 instance drop itemization.
