 1. Far Sight | 10/4/2004 6:28:44 AM GMTDT
Ok, I have a suggestion, thought I'd get feedback from my fellow shamans.

Far sight, as it is right now, is pretty darn useless. Who here has had trouble casting it any more than 50 yds away? I have. Unless you're on a mountain, you'll never get a good range on it because every single time you cast it it'll fail because the target isn't in line of sight.

What I suggest is this:
The code is already in place to allow you to cast it on the minimap. Why not get rid of the line of sight factor and simply confine the spell to what you can see on the minimap? Here's my reasoning:
1.) That's how the Far Seer worked in WC3
2.) We would be confined to the max range of our minimap, which isn't all that far anyway.
3.) It would not be unbalanced in PvP. Consider a rogue. They can turn invis at will. The only way they can be seen is if you get close to them. Well, the rogue doesn't have to get up close and personal in order to get information about a group of enemies, so they won't necessarily be seen at all. Also, Far Sight has a sustained spell effect where you cast the spell. It looks like a yellow swirly thing, like in WC3, so the enemy can see that a shaman is nearby and spying on them and they can react accordingly.
4.) It is a channeling spell, so we can't do anything else while we're using that spell.
5.) If in PvP, what are the odds you'll catch a group of enemies by randomly casting the spell? The way to do it would be a rogue scouting and the shaman casting far sight at the spot the rogue finds the enemy, and that's only if you have a rogue.

The far sight spell as it is is useless and anything it sees, you'll be able to see without casting it, so what's the point of it? However, being able to cast it on the minimap WITHOUT the necessity of line of sight would make it useful. It would still leave rogues as the best scouts but would just give shamans the ability to keep an eye on them as well. Kinda like having 2 people there instead of just one.

What do you guys think?
