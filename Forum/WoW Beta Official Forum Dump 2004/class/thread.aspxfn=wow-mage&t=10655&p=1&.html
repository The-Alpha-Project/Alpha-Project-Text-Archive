 1. Fire Spec vs Arcane Spec - my numbers | 10/20/2004 4:15:45 AM GMTDT
Alright, having crunched the numbers over here, I came up with some interesting restults. Maybe I'm wrong, maybe I'm forgetting something, maybe I just don't get it - but I really think that fire spec is roughly equal to arcane spec on raw burst damage.

Arcane Instablility didn't get figured in - since I figured it was a rough trade off for not figuring in the average dmg potential of the fire +crit talents. Ignite and Fire Power were counted. So - here we go:

Fire spec:
Pop combust - 1 sec cooldown
Pyroblast - giant 6 sec wind up - cranks for 2208 dmg total, once the dots are all done.
Fireball - 3 seconds of casting - 722 dmg + 79 over the rest of the fight.
Fireblast - 1 sec cooldown - hits for 517.
Blastwave - 1 sec cooldown - hits for 603.
Scorch - 1.5 seconds to cast - hits for 279.

Total Damage (13.5 seconds): 4239
Total Mana spent: 1785

Arcane Spec:
Pop Arcane Power - 1 sec cooldown
Purity of Mind - 1 second cooldown
Insta-fling Pyroblast - 1 sec cooldown, 1483 damage total.
Fireball - 3 seconds, 887 dmg + 96 over the rest of the fight.
Fireblast - 1 second cooldown, 634 dmg
Scorch - 1.5 sec cast, 343 dmg
Arcane Missiles - 5 seconds, 1350 dmg.

Total Damage (13.5 seconds): 4793
Total Mana spent: 2591

So, at the end of the day, for that flurry of spells Arcane gets 10% more damage in exchange for 1/3 more mana spent. That pyroblast in the fire-spec attack has a windup that I know most people will not want to choke down. you can get 2 fireballs off in exactly the same time - but (assuming you combust one of them) it works out to almost exactly the same damage, in the same time, for twice the mana.

I, personaly, happen to like the front-load of *nothing* in the fire-spec line - simply because if the bad guy doesn't know you're there, it doesn't clue him in.

I also recognize the versatility of the Arcane line, and it seems much more suited to PvP servers with lots of 1on1 fighting. I'm thinking more of the Battlegrounds (and, in doing so, assuming they will work roughly similiar to Daoc battlegrounds) with large battles of people where a mage can try to keep back from the "front lines."
