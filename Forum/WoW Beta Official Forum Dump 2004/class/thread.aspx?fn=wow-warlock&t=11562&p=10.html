Regarding soulstones:

I understand the reason behind the recent change to soulstones, but as they currently stand, they seem useless. Perhaps some degree of increased utility could be gleaned by adjusting the way they're used slightly. My suggestion:

Keep the current implementation, but change the ressurrection effect to include an aggro-lowering feature. So long as the newly ressurrected person does not affect mobs or directly buff/heal other party members (I'm thinking casting an imp who puts out a blood pact wouldn't count towards that) the ressurrected person will be attacked last by all monsters in the current melee.

This would not allow bypassing content, because the monsters will still attack you, but only when there's no other targets. It would allow the priest or caster who suddenly falls to get back up, dust him/herself off, and heal up to continue fighting. Note that the person would still be at a severe disadvantage for the next 2 minutes due to rez effects. At that point, soulstones become a sort of gimped combat rez, which can be handed out. (Especially important for keeping the only ressurrection casting healer around) Basically it's an insurance policy against a wipe caused by overaggro from the more fragile party members.

Of course, in solo play, they'd still be utterly useless.

Just my 2 cents, keep the ideas flowing.
