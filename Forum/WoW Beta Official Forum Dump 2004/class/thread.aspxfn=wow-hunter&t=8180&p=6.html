 117. Re: Hunter Questions for the Developers | 10/15/2004 4:35:21 AM GMTDT
The problem with pets, abilities and uniqueness is a HUGE friggin' quagmire because, no matter which way they go, it's a catch-22.

Here's why...

Scenario 1
Every pet is unique, can learn 4 abilities out of a pool of 50 and behaves differently from other pets.

End result:
Players figure out what the BEST pet is and every hunter worth his/her salt will have one. Which is the BEST pet? The one with the best damage output to damage taken ratio and the special abilities which are most useful for the majority of situations.

I've seen the beta population ALONE produce accurate statistics of gameplay data (the miss/hit ratios being a prime example). If this were the retail population, people would do tests within a week of release and have an answer for which is the best pet for any situation.

We might have pets which are better for certain situations than others. For example, say a pet has the ability to TURN UNDEAD (one shot kill on undead creatures in most games, FYI). That's a pretty impressive ability. However, the fact that it's not useful ALL the time makes it nearly not worth mentioning.

Best case scenario, we have 2-3 "best" pets which have comparable damage output to tanking ability, factoring in usefulness of special abilities. Every hunter who intends to be the best possible in groups, have the best chances in PvP and overall be on par with other hunters will have one of these three pets, period. Anything else is turning down the chance to survive situations which you otherwise could not survive.

Scenario 2 (how it is now)
All pets are the same in stats (or about the same) can learn the same special abilities and don't need to worry about taming the best pet.

End Result
People pick the pets they want, but we know that, in reality, they're all the same. What could have been a neat and varied part of the hunter class is instead reduced to being a sea of commonality which does not serve to differentiate hunters one iota.

Thus, either way Bliz decides to go with this, I think the results will be less than desired. I'd like to see scenario 1 happen, but I know that, unless the system is balanced over a period of months, there will be a best pet.
