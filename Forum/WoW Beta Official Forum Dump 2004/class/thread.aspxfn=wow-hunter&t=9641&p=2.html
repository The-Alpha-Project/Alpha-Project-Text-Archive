in instances, your pet can become a liability, especially if you are of the "low lvl" range to be in an instance, your agggro range is relativley large, and this extends to your pet, i generally keep my pet on passive, and if there's a risk of messing up the group aggro or aggroing additional mobs due to pet pathing, i don't send the pet in. its more a matter of judgement than a requirement to get the pet in the fight.

most instances i find myself standing in a corner, sniping the mobs that the tanks run around to engage, since me running around just makes for more mobs aggroing.

concussion shot is an excellent pve tool as it tends to make the mob running away hit a 180degree turn and run towards you instead.

i went with a high STA with AGi/STR build in contrast to more spirit, i do run out of mana, but only if i use all the hunter skills, i typically try to stay just below the war/etc in aggro by using sting dot, and regular shots, i crit often enough to keep my dps up, in PvP i tend to favor longer fights by kiting and using sting dot and concussion to keep the opponnent at range.

i rarely engage in Melee combat because our lack of "special" attacks and relatively weak melee damage along with lousy armor makes melee suicidal in most cases.

and i am using a relatively decent axe for my level, corpsemaker, 28.9 dps base, 48+ dps after stat/skill mods(according to the char sheet). this means if i get lucky i can land some 250-350 dmg crits in melee in an emergency, but more often than not, i'm dodged/parried/missed/blocked/evade so melee output is piss poor.

I actually have only a few small suggestions to the stated tactics.

You can lay a trap up until the moment you are 'in combat'. To utilize this effectively I will do one of the following.

Solo: Get as far away as possible. Send the pet (pet attack is bound to the 'r' key). While the pet is en route, set the immo (or frost if I'm expecting adds) and throw hunters mark. By this time the pet has used the first growl. As soon as it refreshes manually hit it again and fire a mob appropriate sting. Arcane, multi, etc. Lather rinse repeat. For the duration of the fight I am manually hitting growl/claw/bite as soon as they refresh on the pet. The only time your pet will ever lose aggro is vs. an elite

Group: Traps can be set while running. With your pet on passive, let the tank initiate combat. Use your 'auto-run' button (mine is mapped to Mouse button 5) you can run over to the mob and drop an immo right on top of him. Using appropriate key bindings you can drop the trap, set your pet to attack, and kite out to 8+ yards and begin firing all within ~3 seconds.

I typically save concussive shot for when I need to pull a mob off of a caster, or for those times that the pet loses aggro (or dies) and I need time to switch to AoMonkey.
I'm not addicted to the game, I can quit any time.
