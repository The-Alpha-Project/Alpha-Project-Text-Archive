http://www.worldofwarcraft.com/thread.aspx?fn=wow-general&t=481749&p=1&#post481749

A solution to make the Defensive Warrior viable in PvP.

Implement a "Bodyguard" ability for warriors. Possibly only make this apply to PvP attackers.

The warrior selects someone to bodyguard, and turns on the ability. While bodyguarding, the warrior's attack abilities are diminished, but all his defensive abilities (armor, block, parry, etc) are added to the person he is defending. The warrior has to remain very close to the person he is protecting, or the ability drops.

Some specifics...

  • defensive stance ability

  • requires the use of a shield

  • an icon appears over the head of the person being protected, and over the bodyguard.

  • costs rage (an amount analogous to taunting)

  • a person can have only one bodyguard at a time, and 2 warriors cannot bodyguard each other


    • This solves many of the "PvP Taunt" problems brought up in other threads. Notably...

  • It does not remove player control by forcing people to change targets

  • It makes defensive spec'd warriors viable in PvP

  • It is an obvious PvP replacement for taunt

    Problem since day 1 of MMORPGs (or MUDs, or whatever) - humans think better than AI. The concept of a "tank" class is pointless - who in their right mind is going to spend time killing a walking HP factory that isn't a threat, when there are dozens of squishy mages, warlocks, rogues, hunters, etc behind him?

    The ONLY recourse you have to be a tank in PvP is to BE A THREAT. As it stands now, looking over a battlefield, you're probably going to skip defensive warriors on your kill list, and go right for the juice - the mages, which present a massive tactical threat with firepower AND crowd control. Fighting that warrior hacking away at you isn't even an afterthought. You spend the whole battle running around chasing people like an idiot. It's like that scene in Monty Python's Holy Grail when Lancelot charges the castle... "Hey..." not much you can do when the enemy swarms over your position, ignores you, and kills the people you're supposedly defending.

    There are two answers as I see it. As presented above, a form of "bodyguarding" where they HAVE to deal with you before dealing with your buddy/buddies OR a system of crowd control (taunts and fears). Blizzard seems to be against more crowd control (as stated in the original post), so that leaves your "bodyguard" role, which opens up whole new realms of tactics and possibilities. Zerker and a defensive warriors together could make quite a deadly team.

    Frankly, I would like to see both systems. Rogues have that "distract" ability that distracts you for a split second - we should have a taunt ability that changes them to aggroing us for some short period of time. We should also have a system to protect our allies, which we currently do not have (and are thus rendered useless).

    A tank in modern warfare is deadly because of its potent firepower and strong defense. You can't ignore it. A defensive warrior in WoW is NOT a tank, at least in PvP. They are a gimpy Paladin without any heals or special abilities.

    I'll continue this rant once I get to class. :-)
