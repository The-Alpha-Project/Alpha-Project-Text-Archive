The Journal of Durrgan Stormbreath

I dinna know why I be writing this down. Tis not vanity that’s for certain. Perhaps I want me bairn to know what their Da did in his day. Perhaps I want MY Da to know so he might understand his lad a wee bit better. Who really knows, aye? But writing it down I am and ye can take it as ye like.

I started me journeys sooner than most Dwarves might. Ye see I’m not like other Dwarves. I canna stand to be cooped up under the earth. Och, I see the beauty in the grand caverns me kindred has delved in the mountains, but that is not all I see beauty in. I crave the feel of wind through me beard and the crunch of snow under me boots. I suppose I had something to prove to me Da so I left me home when I was still nearly a lad meself. Had to sneak out I did. Sure’n me Da is still upset about that. Maybe he’s disowned me as I’ve not gone back yet so I truly dinna know.

Now dinna get me wrong. Me Da is a great Dwarf. He rules our small kingdom of Khaz Deln fairly and our people respect him, as do I. But because of what I said before, and because of me friend who I won’t name here, me Da and me we dinna see eye to eye. Now Durbold being King it dinna look too good for his eldest son and heir to be so ‘different’ so I took me leave of him and me home. Most likely I’ve forfeit me claim to the throne but I have younger brothers more fittin’ to that sort of thing than I be. But that’s enough about that.

The first place I traveled to and found me a bit of adventure was Anvil Marr. This was a good thing because being new to the ‘real’ world I was a wee bit nervous about what I might be findin’ out there. I found a few friendly Dwarves what helped me out and set me a few simple tasks meant ta hone me skills before I got too far. There were a might lot of Crag Boars and Timber Wolves in that area who’s hides fetched a keen price with the local vendors. Figured I get the lay of the land first before I ventured too far afield. But twasn’t too long afore I felt ready to take on more challenges so I set off.

Next I found meself in the wondrous land of Dun Morogh. Now that’s beauty for ye! It’s forever under a veil of snow and there be beasties everywhere ye turn. They all have their own beauty but not all be friendly if ye take me meaning.

Take the small town of Kharanos for example. The hills around that place be nearly swarming with Ice Claw Bears and Winter Wolves and the like. I reckon in one day I battled a near dozen of each! Fortunately, the local traders are in need of the meat and even the teeth from these beasties. And I’ve earned a fair price more than once for scouting out some hidey-holes for the locals.

Ye may not know, but the lands of Dun Morogh also have a problem with Frostmane Trolls. Seems they claim these lands as theirs and dinna care for the Dwarves to be there. Not much for sharing those ones. But then, I reckon we Dwarves are not big on sharing either. A little village just west of a frozen lake the name of Ice Flow had some problems with these nasties. A chap in Brewnall Village asked me to scout out a nearby hold and see about some Trolls. It was well’n I did for the place was crawlin’ with Headhunter Trolls. I was lucky to get out with me hide. But now the villagers know where the hold is and what kind of threat it may be. There was even another camp of these fiends t’other side of the lake. They’re all but surrounded in that little village. But we Dwarves are hardy and brave folk and I reckon there’ll be Dwarves there long after the Trolls be gone.

There be another beastie in the hills surrounding Kharanos. Have ye ever laid eyes on a Wendigo? HUGE brutes they be. I bested some what be more’n double me size! Now mind ye I not be bragging here. More often than not some kind lad or lass would be around and would help me out a bit and I did the same for them. The Gnomes have taken refuge in Dun Morogh so there be lots of them running around. Funny little creatures, look a wee bit fragile but they sure can surprise ye. Most of ‘em know some sort o’ magic the likes o’ which you don’t normally see around Dwarves. Impressive little buggers and good help in a scrape. Recently there have even been Humans wandering around Dun Morogh. Seems there’s been a travel system set up betwixt their lands and ours, but I’ll tell ye more about that later.

As I was saying, lots o’ these folk are quick to help ye when ye need it. Oftentimes we’d get us a good group and go tackle some o’ the worst o’ the beasties. But sure’n I prefer to be wanderin’ by meself. Most o’ them lads – no fault to them mind ye – are only concerned with the next fight. Me, I like to take the time and look around. Ye run around too fast lookin’ for trouble and not only are ye bound ta find it, but yuir gonna miss out on a lot that be around ye. Why I saw trees what must’ve been 2 meters thick and 4 times as tall! The mountains are a wonder all their own. And I must say, I love meetin’ new folk. Always good ta hear ‘bout other people and other places. I think that most folk what bury themselves ‘neath mountains in their great cities are burying their heads as well. Canna see the cavern for the rock as a wise old Dwarf used to say.

On the road to Loch Modan I found the Gol’Bolar Quarry. Seems they be havin’ a problem with Rockjaw Troggs disrupting their mining and damagin’ their equipment. A sturdy old Dwarf enlisted me aid in clearing some of the cretins out and I was more’n happy to oblige. Seems he enlisted a few stout folk which was just as well for there were more’n enough of the Troggs fer us all to have a stab at them. A good thing it was too for they was disgusting and nasty creatures I must say. Look a bit mad and demented and they have no love of Dwarves.

I musta spent a good couple o’ months traveling around Dun Morogh and enjoying nearly every moment of it. I found me a fair number o’ small copper mines and set meself to some mining. No reason to throw away all I’d been taught aye? Now here I be with a good amount o’ copper ore in me bag and no way to be smelting it! I needed me a forge and an anvil if’n I wanted to make something worthy o’ selling and padding me purse if ye take me meaning. Then one day I come across this rather strange looking Gnome where’n a green robe and sportin’ a strange comb-over hairdo. Not sure how he be staying warm without a good head o’ hair and a beard but that’s not the point. His name be Gollum and he pointed me in the direction of a Dwarf city called IronForge. Sure’n he even led me there and gave me a tour o’ the place.

The Dwarves that built IronForge are some skilled craftsmen let me tell ye. Nah, that dinna do them justice. This place be massive and impressive. They’ve tapped into a lava flow to provide heat for the immense halls carved outta the mountain itself. There be shops everywhere of every type you can imagine. In the Military Hall I even found me a stout Dwarf willin’ ta teach me a thing or two about being a warrior so’s any future scrapes I might find meself in I have a fair chance o’ comin’ out with me hide intact. Old Gollum even showed me to the High Seat. King Magni himself be there! Och! Now that’s a magnificent Dwarf. Now to be certain, I dinna try to talk to him, for sure’n he had better things ta do than ta talk to a scruffy traveler. Inside the chamber of the High Seat there be these tremendous crystals that glittered in the firelight that lit the room. I’d never seen the like and don’t reckon I will again.

Finally, old Gollum he took me to tha Great Forge. It is the most impressive, gigantic, fantastic forge I ever did see! Even tho I have said that I’m not yuir normal Dwarf and that I crave the fresh air and such, I coulnda help feeling like I had come home when I saw that grand forge. Me eyes were watering from the heat and because they were ‘bout outside their sockets with awe at the sight. I’m guessing it was powered from the lava flow the way it glowed red with the heat. A number of small personal sized forges were set around the main forge along with some anvils. A finer place to smelt and hammer I’ve surely not seen before or since. And there were blacksmithing experts here too. They were more’n happy to show me a trick or two ta extend me skills.

I smelted all me copper ore and pounded meself out some bracers and other such stuff to sell to the local vendors and was about to set back out to the surrounding lands when old Gollum showed up and directed me to the Hall of Explorers. There I see yet another wonder that I sure’n never expected to see let alone RIDE. There be GRYPHONS there!

To be continued…
