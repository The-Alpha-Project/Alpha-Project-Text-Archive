Brief info here...


Name: Lothurin
Gender: Male
Race: Night elf.
Occupation: Herb gatherer, owl breeder/trainer/tamer, hunter, so to say, and a little more since he's NE.
Pet: An enormous white owl named Qionus, highly intelligent (even for elven standards)...one of the ancient creatures from sunken Kalimdor...ya know...the priestesses used to ride them into war before they swapped it for the white tiger.
Weapon: A spear carved with runes, his owl, and any other bird form in the area.

IC:

Lothurin was regarded by the night elves as 'queer'. Not that type of 'queer' mind you, but the strange, unnatural queerness. He was a night elf, a pure night elf whose fathers date back to the times of the highborn. Lothurin himself was a only a boy (from only a handful of boys) that survived that time. It was said that the waters from the Well of Eternity addedhis brain during the war, or he wouldn't have selected the occupation as an owl tamer. A beastmaster even, a beastmaster of anything that flies. It was known that the night elves have a certain control over nature, but Lothurin's control seem to surpass the standard control. Nobody question him when he found an egg from the almost-legendary race of the giant white owl, nor did the village had anything to say when he somehow accquired a flock of ravens on top of his house, and no questions were raised either when two large hawks suddenly paid him a visit. He was a bird tamer, that was all.

But normal bird tamers don't talk to birds as though they are other night elves, do they?

"What is that you say? The harpies have fallen?" Lothurin answered the constant clickings from his mount - Qionus the owl. The two have been flying around the desert for a while, looking for a few rare herbs. Lothurin was hoping by putting a few impressive herbs in his gardens he would actually attract a female. All of the male night elves he knew (and there were not many of them) have already at least eight females under their care. He, on the other hand, had none.

Qionus hooted in disapproval at his owner's sudden fall of self-confidence. The two ravens flying behind Qionus cawed in agreement. Their master was not lacking in anyway. He was tall, shapely, handsome, clean, for a change, and caring (well at least to them). It was only a matter of time, yes, only a matter of time.

"Circle here," Lothurin ordered as the night elves shape caught his eye. Bodies of dead harpies laid strewned about and Lothurin could make out the shape of a troll and a tauren about the shadows. A troll and a tauren? That was interesting. Qionus followed the orders and began circling above the little group below. Lothurin recognized the group that left his village earlier.

"I thought they were hunting in the forest..."
