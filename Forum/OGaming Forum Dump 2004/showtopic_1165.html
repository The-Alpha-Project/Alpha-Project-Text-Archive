I bid you dark tidings, for we are:

Shimmergloom: A city of the Night Elves. And the Jewel of the Dark.

Introduction
Deep beneath the southern wastelands, hidden away from the sun's burning kiss, lies the Night Elven city of Shimmergloom. Sister of Kebra Nagast and Daughter to The Dark One's Towers of Silence, Shimmergloom lies like a slumbering beast within her cavern lair.

From the surface down through the Screaming Maw, beyond the Bride's Wound and finally across the Nethervein lies the gates of Shimmergloom, each aglow with evident power. Blackened metal gates, strengthened by her children's magic and the blood of a thousand dwarves, guard the way to the Market Ward.

Guards walk the avenues with hungry steel in hand, eyes alert for signs of trouble. The Mother will tolerate no disruption to order, no break in routine. Merchants and slaves, nobles and travelers go about their business here in the most open of Shimmergloom's sections.

Beyond the Market Ward, reached by the massive tunnel known as Dragon's Crawl, lie the gates and towers of the Black Borough. Here dwell the commoners of Shimmergloom, the tradesmen and crafters who keep the Mother alive. Herds of livestock and farms of fungi populate a large section of the Borough, separated from the residents' homes by the warm river waters of Harrowvein. Minor temples of Melek dot the cavern's walls and alters in His honor are common. No foreigner may pass the Borough's gates without fear of reprisal for this is the heart of the Mother.

While the Black Borough may be Shimmergloom's heart, it is the final district known as Glitterdoom which is her soul. The four great Houses rise like ebon fangs from the cavern's floor, each dedicated to the training of the Mother's children. The largest of these, House Absolute, serves as both the school and main temple to Melek's priesthood within Shimmergloom. Neophyte and Master live side by side here, immersed in their duties to both God and Mother. In addition to the great Houses are the homes of the city's nobility. While many of the most affluent children dwell within the great Houses, many maintain private residences within Glitterdoom as well.

This then is the city of Shimmergloom. Teeming with life yet silent as the Dead's breath, she is a wondrous Mother of contradictions. Caring of her children yet hateful and jealous of outsiders. Kind and beautiful yet cold and deadly. This is Shimmergloom, Jewel of the Dark.

Fenix continued his lengthy journey down through the pits and firey tides of the Earth. He was searching within the depths of these mysterious, magical realms that lie beneath the barren wastelands of the south. As he traveled, he sensed an immense power, whether it be dark or light, he had no knowledge, but nevertheless it was an essence of energy. Energy wells such as this were rare, and powerful... Fenix always held his desire for power as his number one priority.

His muscles ached and triggered electric pulses to his brain, filled with messages of exhaustion and pain. He ignored these cries for rest within his body, knowing his reward would be great if he reached this mass of energy that lie deep beneath the Earth. Whilst in his ethereal form, Fenix could travel through nearly any means of shielding, at the cost of quantum amounts of life force, however. He sensed he was nearing whatever this energy source was, his skin tingled with excitement as he approached the source.

Fenix was not prepared for the surprise that awaited him as he exited his interdimensional rift into the physical world once more. As he stepped out of his portal, he felt the force of gravity upon him, pulling him down to the bottom of whatever awaited him. Thinking upon his feet, with his great intellectual power, he shoved his Magus Staff deep into the wall of which stood adjacent to him. This suspended him in midair to view his new surroundings from an aerial haven.

Fenix could only allow his jaw to drop as his mind absorbed all of this infromation before him. It was a Night Elven city, but it differed from the ones he had seen before. Great spires and walls were erected as defensive barriers. Massive black gates and towers of old stood with their mighty age and wisdom of centuries. Within the confines of the city, he sensed danger, evil, malevolence... and power, immense, indescribable power...

The obstacles that lay before him, would challenge his intelligent mind to its limits. Guards were everywhere, patrols always on watch, great barriers that prevented freedom of movement, and an inner sanctum which must have held the power. And here was Fenix, with his potent magics of old, and little energy to spare, he only needed to rest before his true wrath would be unleashed. But would he have time...

This was a beautiful rose of a city, but its poisonous thorns were clearly visible, if not meant to emphasized completely by the city's architects. It would be difficult to rest peacefully here, so Fenix scanned the city while stealthily inching down the side of the stone wall of which he hung. He touched the ground of this blackened city and began to search for a place to gain sufficient rest, staying out of sight as often as he could...
