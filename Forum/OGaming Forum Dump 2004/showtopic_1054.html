Away from the small undead camp, in the tall trees above, a large snowy owl took off into the sky and set westwards, towards the single one tower sitting alone at the base of the cliff, hidden from civilization. On top of the loney tower, created from gray stones, topped with a dull red roof, sat two creatures. Humanoids, gray in skin colour, slightly pointed ears, with a pair of large, dark, dragon-like wings. The two demonic constructs gazed across the top of the forest with their piercing, moon-yellow eyes.

"I see dead people," one of the creatures, a girl, said finally.

"Very funny, sis," the two second, identical to the first aside for gender, retorted. The two stretched their leathery wings and laid out on the large, cone-shaped roof of the tower.

"You don't suppose they're looking for him?" the boy asked as the watched the clouds hovered by overhead.

"I doubt anybody would," the girl replied with equal dullness. The boy rolled over and let his wings droop like withered flowers onto the roof.

"And I was hoping for some action."

"Not a chance, bro, not a chance. He won't let us anyway. Remember last time? He spent a week patching up your wings. He said he's going to give you pixie wings if you lose them again."

The boy cringed at the memory. At the same time, the owl came flapping back.

"Morn, Qionus," the twins chimed. The owl hooted back in reply as he landed on the roof (which was now rathar cramped beside of the two demonic contructs, their wings, and the very large owl). Qionus continued to mutter and series of clicks and hoots which the two listened with fascination.

"Thinking undead? That's awfully strange; aren't they all zombies?"

The owl hooted once.

"Apparently not...I say we investigate," the girl said as she got up.

"But sis, you said-" the brother started.

"As long as we don't get hurt seriously we'll be fine. Besides, he'll out for a walk, won't hurt if we take one ourselves. We'll explain later, or Qionus can explain, can't you?"

The owl hooted indignantly.

"Dandy, it's settled. Who knows? Maybe he'll get interested too. He's been looking for testing subjects. If he gets all riled up about us we can always snatch a zombie back or something."

"I suppose so," the brother replied.

And the two took off into the air, towards the commotion of the thinking zombies.
