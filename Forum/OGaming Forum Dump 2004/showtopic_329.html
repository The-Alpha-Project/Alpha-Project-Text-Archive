The citizens of Azeroth grew complacent in the years after the Great War. Defences and outpost became towns of drunken and disgraced soldiers, slumping around like the undead. But when there is knowledge, there is vigilance. The tree city Quez' Embwal' was the nerve centre of the Kalimdor night elf druid clan Ghost Dragon. The entire tree town was covered in libraries, churches and forum centres. The town remained quiet for years and years until the day that Q Lin, a resident druid, found a secret chamber in the trees restricted area. Inside was a number of undead skeletons preserved in clear sap and an assortment of old documents. But one thing stood out, a purple piece of parchment, and on that parchment was a map and an encryption, Q lin could just make it out as "The Underworld Portal to Dreanor". In his mind he knew what he should do. Act immediately, his father had told him that the only way to find a weakness in evil was to go where it was strongest. Q always wanted to be an adventurer and be responsible for critical information for the resistance. He knew who he needed. Rougehunter received a owl post letter the next day requesting that he meet Q in a abandoned outpost 57mlz from there. So immediately he picked up his demon hunter blades given to him by Nightsoldier and left. He wondered what quest they had for him next.

And rightly so were they extra suspicious of a goblin in their town. Overall he had benefitted from the goblins become independent from the horde- now he could go into any city to con people- but he had never really succeeded at conning night-elves. What was wrong with selling anti drunk elixers that didn't always work! The worst part was, he couldnt even steal from them either because many of their items were somehow spiritually linked to their owner, making them impossible to steal.

Thats exactly how Zein found himself in the forest in night elf lands. One night elf had been less than pleased with the thieving actions of Zein, and kicked him out. It was then when he heard the light rustling of leaves and saw a night elf walk by who hadn't noticed him.
