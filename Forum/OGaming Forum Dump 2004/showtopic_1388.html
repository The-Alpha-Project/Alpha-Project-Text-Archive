As the Warchief of HammerFall, I led my company outside of Orgrimmar. We brought our tents, accomadated by our Tauren Warrior Kilik Stormeye. We made a fire and brought plenty of fresh meat. It had been a good raid party the week before.

The Raid

We had plundered hundreds of pounds of meat, plenty of weaponry and gold. As well, our blacksmith had great fun taking hundreds of iron belts that he would use to forge weapons and armor.

I, of course, was not wholely interested in gold and riches. I raided the Night Elven caches and found what I was looking for. Rare herbs. I knew they had to have had some of the world's finest ingredients to have had such powerful potions to keep them alive that long. Such a weak village, and weak opponents, not one of our warriors sustained any injuries.

Finally we rode home on our well-fed hounds. The Kodo beast followed closely behind, carrying tons of our plundered loot. We returned back to our hall in Orgrimmar. We named our home Ogrim Hall, in honour of my father.

We left the gold in our safe bank, the weapons, armor and iron belts (not literal belts, these were sheets and bars of iron used to create armor) went to the blacksmith.

The herbs went into my personal locker, for I knew the rest of the guild would find no use to them. That night we fired up the furnace and cooked great red meat. We ordered ale and drank and sang.

In the morning, we headed out into the wood, on an adventuring expedition. We left our trusty Warlock Kazil Thal'Sin to look over our Hall. We knew least of all people, he would be greedy enough to steal anything.

The Expedition

We were in the woods. After spending one night just outside of the reaches of Orgrimmar, we encountered some Razormanes. We fought them with ease, for they were no opponents for the HammerFall. We went back to their camp, ready for an easy slaughter when almost three hundred of their kind surrounded us.

I remembered the looks on the other warriors. They looked, fearful. As they began reading a teleportation scroll, I took out my Axe. "Shadow Axe" as I called it would hew many Razormanes this day.

"You may trust only the valor in your heart and the lust in your veins" I yelled as the other warriors began taking out their weapons. Commander Thalin, our Elder Shaman released his Magiks as I began taking out these vile creatures.

Finally, the battle had ended. Much pain had been given, as well as taken. Many grevious wounds cursed us, but we had shamanistic healing to aid us. Valarin Khuzdul had been poisoned by one of the Pyromancers of the camp.

We could do little to aid him. I sent the fastest of us on my steed, my wolf named BloodThirst. Normally, a Warchief never lends his faithful beast to another, but in this case formalities would not be the cause of a death.

As we awaited the rider to bring the herbs from my personal storage, we comforted our brave warrior. Suddenly, a noise crept near. The bushes made a shuffling noise. Cautiously, I took out my Axe. I would take no chances.

My staff warmed in my hands as the powerful energies, taken from the world around me and twisted, entered it. I opened the necessary channels to release them- electricity, in this setting. It would do the most damage and not harm the wildlife. We are not men. We do not believe in "collateral damage".

As the rustling slowed, a shape began to crawl out from the underbrush, and as an Initiate lifted their spear to hurl, I shouted to calm them. "No mistakes! Hold!"

A small orcish child, battered and bruised to the point of agony, unable to stand, pulling herself through the underbrush, exited. "No attack! It's a child!" I shouted, as I ran toward her, shoving my way through the Mages, Warlocks, and Shaman I lead.

I reached her at the same time as my Warchief, Mogrin Shadowaxe, and without being asked, began to release the power I had built up in my staff in a more subtle, refined way- tendrils tiny beyond recognition, where I had been prepared to use an axehead. Her wounds sewed themselves shut, and the swelling went down. The pain she felt visibly lessened. I felt my muscles go slightly limper as I expended some of my inner energies, but it was not as much as it would have been when I arrived at the hall of the Hammerfall clan, a young rebellious student. I have wisened since then, and through my wisdom, my abilities have grown rapidly.

Now, in much less pain than before, the young girl can, between sobs, tell us what has happened. "Men!" she shouts to us. "In our village, to the east by three miles! It's not far off, maybe you can avenge us before they move on!"

She tells us how the men attacked without warning, razing everything to the ground, barely stopping the attack before they began quarrelling over the bounty.

I spit on the ground, and look to my warchief. "As usual, the are distracted by the glimmer of gems. Now is the perfect time. Their discipline is low, and they are angry with each other."

Warchief Mogrin nods, and begins to order the hordes to move. We march as brothers, disciplined, but free. The men will not stand a chance.

As we were leaving the near-dead child, my rider came striding in. He stepped off BloodThirst with the herbs.

"I am sorry, Warchief. I did not know which herb was needed, so I took them all. Excuse my ineducation"

"You have done well my warrior. We have found this child, a group of men razed their village. Stay here, we can revive the child and come back to her after we finish off the humans"

I turned around to face the dead glare of a helpless orc child. I kneeled down and wiped my tear. I took out a cloth, and put it over her.

"It is time, my brethren. The vile humans have destroyed a helpless village of our people. It is time to avenge their deaths, and make the humans pay."

I trudged forward, looking back only once to the dead child. I quickened my pace. BloodThirst ran behind me. I jumped on and ran in front of my fellow clan warriors.

"Lok Tar-Ogar! Let us give the humans hell!" I yelled as I travelled off into the distance.
