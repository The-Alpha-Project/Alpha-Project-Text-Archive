Alchemy is a good choice as Xmet said, but if you want to be rich, choose two gathering skills. This will get you piles of cash, and you can just buy the potions of the AH.
Infact, you will come out richer, as the materials for the potions are more expensive than the potions themselves.
Eg. you sell the materials for a major healing potion for 8 gold, but you buy the potion for 4 gold.
Its the same thing with blacksmithing, I believe that for the higher end items, the mats can cost upto 200gold, yet the item will only sell for 50 gold.

When all is said and done you will not be making much of a profit, from production professions.

Some potions are needed in blacksmithing and tailoring and leatherworking. So you might want to consider that as well. Not a lot of players spend time of roaming the countryside for herbs (which I do), and not a lot of them have high herb skill to gather rare herbs. It's a matter of rarity. I choose alchemy because I don't like going to the auction house every time when I need a potion restock. It's easier brewing them (and gaining high skill to brew higher potions for higher profits), especially when I main place of activity is ashenvale. It a simle matter of time: I can brew potions on the spot, giving up a bit of money, but on the other hand I don't have to spend 30+ minutes running back up to the docks, over the continent, taking flight paths and running into the auction house to place a bet in hopes that I MIGHT get the potion, and taking the trip back to pick up where I left. I could've used the time to go more quests and get an even higher profit from killing stuff.

And I really doubt the fact that materials needed to make the thing is more expensive than the product - there would be no high end stuff that I see in the houses auction. I believe Blizzard would work on that if it is true, because it would be a major, major problem to the economy.
