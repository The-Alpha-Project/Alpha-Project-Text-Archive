Here's the profile for Loth'eal, in case I want to throw him in somewhere again. This profile should explain something about him. Note: Loth'eal is strictly an RPG character.

Name: Loth’eal
Age: Ancient, no questions asked. He lost count after 1,200
Race: Once night elf, once high elf, now something else
Rank: a priest, a simple priest, yes, only a simple elven priest
Appearance: An elf with the face of thirty human years. Long pale blond hair that’s loose and uncut, not pulled back like a proper priest, tied in a loose tail behind his back while strands of it dangled in the front. Has pale blue eyes hidden behind a pair of dwarven-made glasses.
History: Loth’eal was a born highborn, or chosen one. His talent in magic earned him the favour of the Queen in his early years the jealousy of even his highborn peers. Unfortunately his disinterest in the magic and his somewhat dry humour and even dryer attitude towards his peers left him alone in the cold wing and earned him the name of ‘the queer highborn’. He paid little attention to his peers and their thirst for experiments in magic. He spent most of his time in the dark confines of his study reading, or doing what his peers would call ‘wasting his talent’. In truth, Loth’eal had little love for magic, and nature even less. He spent his days and nights looming around the schools and eavesdropping on the night elven philosophers as they preached their knowledge.
As the first war came around Loth’eal’s study was destroyed and he fell into the well of eternity where he almost drowned. He should have been blasted away like upper Kalimdor, however he survived miraculously and was swept ashore and rescued by Furion Stormrage and Tyrande Whisperwind. The waters of the well had soaked him thoroughly and the elf soon found that his blood’s magic concentration was much like the well itself, if not more potent. The sudden boost in magic drove him wild. Under Furion’s guidance Loth’eal managed to retain his sanity and adopted a milder personality than his previous one. The two got along well. As the highborn was exiled Loth’eal insisted that he went along because ‘a being cursed with so much magic as me living in a society where magic is banned is impossible. Besides, I don’t like to sleep.’
After witnessing the building of Quel’thalas he exiled himself (or rather, the high council exiled him because he made harsh jokes about them) and lived alone in some hidden part of the forest that escaped the Scourge’s attack. Rumours sprouted about an ancient demon in the forest soon after.
Strengths: His blood’s concentration of magic was lethal to any other being except for himself. Through the magic he managed to stay young and well without relying on any other source of magic. He has the ability to control the magic around him and has through command of chaos blood magic. The blood itself heals the body automatically after any injuries. His eyes turn colour according to the type of magic used (the glasses hides the colour change). He also has command of twenty different dialects and has a knack for driving young elves crazy.
Weaknesses: Very susceptible to physical attacks such as headlocks, strangles, being pinned to a wall…And has some suicidal tendencies, as in, he is rather found of slashing himself and watching the blood flow (it's needed if he needs to do any blood magic). Also, he gets tired easily under too much sun.
Personality: Despite of Furion’s guidance Loth’eal still has a very dry personality. He smiles most of the time so it made it impossible for others to guess his thoughts. Half of the time what he says are not for real or has a second meaning, he walks the fine line of being amusing and being downright obnoxious and arrogant. Almost nothing can anger him (because he doesn’t care about anything anymore). He loves sending anyone brave enough to approach him into frustration, or pushing them until they almost reach the brink of insanity before pulling them back into sanity. One can almost call him a sadist. He rarely uses his magic. It is only in times he sees fit should he do something about it, so on one side you would see him conjure up a firestorm to protect himself after being almost pummelled to death, and on the other hand you’ll see him flicking his finger and sending some poor chap’s horse running down the forest and out of sight.
Goal/Mission: nothing exactly, he views himself outside of the world. Few approached him for help. Of course, if they could stand him for more than a day they would be helped, but that rarely happens…

Here's another Loth...Lothurin. From the Pirates RPG.

Name: Lothurin
Age: 10,000+++
Race: Night Elven Highbourne
Occupation: Merchant...underground merchant, captain of the Nightinggale

History: Just Loth'eal altered slightly to fit the sea-faring situation of the RPG. This is would Loth'eal would have been had he not set his foot on Quel'thalas during the exile of the highbourne. He proceeded to sail around the seas with his crew, doing small trades, any trades, including underground ones between rival factions. Known throughout the sea-faring world as somewhat peculiar, strange, and downright annoying when bothered, not a lot of sailors would approach him unless it was needed. The crew of the Nightingale tends to stick together, and seldom would they be seen apart. The crew lives on the ship, but it is rumoured that the crew has an Island somewhere in the Maelstrom where they resided as a base during low trading season, and on that island lay quite a few articfacts which they have picked up on their trade journeys from the different factions they deal with as 'gift' for special 'services'.

Notes: Compared to his high-elven counterpart Lothurin lacked the power that went to the sanity. The magic concentration in his blood was not as high, nor as potent, as Loth'eal. He also seems to be relatively more sane than the high elf, and has more of a social life with his crew, and actually picking a romantic interest in one of the members of his crew.

Appearance: He is rather short for a night elf, only standing to a height of six feet flat. The markings on his face are clean, two slashes on either side of his cheek and one down the middle on the top of his forehead, faint to a point where it is almost unnoticable unless under strong light. He does not wear glasses, and tends to have a more roguish look from the years of sea-traveling. Unfortunately that did little to change his built, which is still slim, but cannot be easily mistaken for a woman...somehow.

Personality: as mentioned, sane and thoughtful, or at least more sane than his high elven counterpart. To be blunt, he actually gives a damn to what happens to his crew, especially his 'interest', of which he considers his already (of which the crew has no objections to). Other than that he is quite wishy-washy, he would much prefer to do things the easy way, and tends to say the unwanted, much easier unsolution before giving in and plotting up an actual workable one.

"One good suggestion would be to sacrifice the hostage, and the case would wrap itself up without any hassle," would be his most common quote and best representation of his attitude towards complex situations.
