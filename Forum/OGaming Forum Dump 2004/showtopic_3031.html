*******************************************************************

Both factions can take part. This RP is bascially an outline of the attack on Stormwind Keep, the highest beacon of Human Power (their main city and castle). The Horde is attacking, the Alliance is defending. Any race/class allowed, but know your disposition.

*******************************************************************

'Every type of battle that is described to a child, where the armies sit and face each other from 100m apart and then begin inflicting an attack is such an intruth to real battle that in situations like these, I feel like going to these children and showing them the reality of war, and death.

But the battlefield is not a place for a child, it is a place for a soldier. With flaming bolts flying over my head and pieces of flame dancing around me, it seems the reality of war is blocked out, and only the tatooed firing of flaming rock seems to entertain my consciousness' thought the brave but horrified footman.

Legions upon legions of soldiers begin to raid a small hole in the gate, while the Elves of the West fire their arrows, piercing the thick armour of the horde's frontlines and taking them down. Blood becomes a river, slowly pouring down through the streets of the Trade Quarter. The women, children and merchants take refuge in the hollow basements of their steads, while the Stormwind Gaurd runs frantically, trying to cover and maintain the seal of the city.

Helios Nightfoot stares shockingly at the entire scene, almost unable to comprehend. A rough hand touches his shoulder.

"Grab your sword, and get to the gate!" one of the Guard Generals yells and continues on toward the gate. Helios runs, as fast as his legs would take him, and as fast as his heavy armour would permit, and got to the gate. He finally shook off his own shock, and his fear and began to fight. But the crowd gaurding the inner gate was so large he had not 3 foes before he was forced back by his general.

"We are doing a good job maintaining our foothold for the gate, and if we can continue to weaken their numbers by picking them off one by one, we can win this battle with half the casualites they will suffer" General Marcus said, as he rode away on his horse to tend to other matters on the battlefield. As the fighting continued, a spree of warriors, at least a hundred rushed forwards. Their onslaught continued up the Valley of Heroes, which was now defiled and vandalize almost beyond repair, but the artists and architects of Stormwind were no rock chippers, they were true artisans.

The horde pushed back, and the rest of the Alliance forces pushed upward with this group. The horde took a stand near the outer gate. They fought until a hundred more joined the group, and nearly 200 foot soldiers pushed even farther, driving the horde temporarily out of the gate's radius. The General returned on his horse, ordered evertyone else to hold their positions within the gate. A red flare fired, and suddenly everything started to move.

"Close the gate! Before the horde can get to us!" yelled the General as the hundreds rushed toward the gate to push it closed. A long metal spike drove into the doors, and at least a dozen gaurds grabbed and pushed, helping to close the gate. Finally as there was not 2 meteres between the two gate doors, where the soldiers finally saw the sacrifice, Helios did too.

"You left them out there to die!" Helios yelled.

"Boy, they made their sacrifice for Stormwind. Perhaps you should try and make yours" barked one of the Paladins. Things got real quiet, and finally the General spoke.

"For now the Horde is at bay. Our ranged teams are no match for theirs, and as long as the gate is sealed they have not a thing they can do to enter the city. As the General began to talk, a screeching sound hit the air. Overhead the soldiers looked, and dozens upon dozens of Wyvern-Riders and Gargoyles enterd the air and began to swoop down and wreak caranage on the ranks of alliance soldiers.

The Gate doors began to rumble... the skies furied and doom was upon Stormwind Keep.

Helios ran toward the Dwarven District of Stormwind Keep. He passed the flaming buildings and the petrified towns people and finally reached the smokey and dead quiet place.

He came forward and entered the fort.

"Milord, we are in need of The Explorer's League to send its riflemen to fight!" yelled Helios.

A grand dwarf stood up, raised his sword and threw it against the wall.

"Too many of my friends died helping the humans survive their trials. I won't take part in this massacre" he said as he sat back down, chewing on some bread.

"Milord, if you do not answer this call, you shall find yourself dead. And at no avail either. What hope do you have of finding more clues to your heritage if the Horde breaks down your headquarters here?" reasoned Helios.

Suddenly a big crash bellowed in the streets. The roof began to cave-in, and the room quickly emptied, everyone evacuating into the cellar underground.

"If you know what's good for ya lad, you will come hide out with us" advised the Prospector.

"If what is good for me is sitting here listening to the painful deaths of thousands, than I guess I won't be feeling so good after all!" yelled Helios, throwing down his shield, picking up the sword wedged in the wall, and then unsheathing his own blade.

The fliers of the Horde ravaged the town from the inside, while the main battle was progressing no where. Although the gate was now open, the frontlines just pillaged upon each other, making mountains of carcasses on the stone-paved entrance of Stormwind.

Helios fought for many hours, and finally tired of watching his ranks get decimated by the air-riders, he climbed to the Aviary.

"Sir, we need to call upon the Royal Gryphons of the Alliance. They are our only chance to survive" said Helios.

"Boy, these are being used to transport the royalty and wealthy men of Stormwind. We cannot just send them out to die.

"Helios then took out his sword, wiped his brow of sweat, his cheek of tears, and said "Set loose the gryphons or I will murder you and your Royalty, GryphonMaster' he threatened.

Distant cries under could be made out faintly "We are going to die", "There is no hope" and "Run for your lives". Then everything became silent, and out of the dark sky came a beacon of light. Helios rode on top of a Gryphon, and began to take out the Wyverns and Gargoyles from the sky.

None were a match for a gryphon, unless of course 3 of them teamed up on the poor animal. Blood splattered all over as the brave gryphon fought off 3 gargoyles but could not survive. It died in the air, and sent Helios crashing into the ranks of the Tauren and Orcs.

He was slowly dieing, as his enemies didn't bother finishing him off. He heard some orcish yelling and in the bluryness of his final seconds of life, he spotted the Riflemen of Dun Morogh charging up the hill, being assisted by the Gnomes. Hope existed.

Snarling Maedris leaves the fighting and rides towards the line of footman trying to fence them in. Another Dark Priest followed him seeing the threat at their rear. The steaming corpse falls, his own power having burned out of his body and immolating him. The two Undead continue on through the line sowing confusion before breaking out to rejoin there charges.

The Druid, Kunye, ran out of the large wooden doors of the Stormwind Keep. Passing through the streets of the City, he was struck with horror. He was an emissary from Kalidar, and was frantic of what was happening. "Surely, these cannot be the Orcs under the command of Thrall," muttered Kunye in shock. The Alliance and The Horde had signed treaties of peace.

Kunye did not wish to fight, but seeing the blood pour along the pure white stone, he sighed and threw aside his cloak. "Give me strength, Cenarius," he prayed quickly and transformed into the Druid's form of a raven. Flying quickly up apon a tower, he perched himself to see the battle from above. Quickly, he swerved down and moved to land near the borders of the Elywnn Forest where he became a Night Elf once more.

Raising his hands above him, he called upon the trees. Two of the trees near him uprooted and moved toward the attacking Horde. The trees swung Orcs out of their paths, but soon Orcs swarmed over them and began chopping madly. Kunye felt blood form from his lips as he felt the pain of nature. Dropping to his knees, he breathed heavily as the trees fell. He had not the power to take on the Orcs.

Slipping into the shadows of the Forest, he sadly watched, until he changed back into a Raven and fled into the City.
