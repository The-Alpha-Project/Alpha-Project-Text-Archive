There is no auto attack for your character. You must first target a monsters, then click to either attack it or use a spell. If you click attack, which is your melee attack, you only have to click it once, and your character will continue to attack with your melee attack. If you want to use a spell or skill, you will have to click it each time.

For the warlock (and I assume the Hunter) your pet can be set on aggressive, which means it will automatically seek out and attack monsters near by. It also has autocast spells that you can set, too.

If you do go AFK, its best to do it in a place you know for sure is safe. A couple times I was sitting on the road in Westfall and gone AFK, and a wolf has come by and slowly killed me because I wasn't paying attention.

As for the quests, there are some very unique and cool quest. In the beginning though, I will admit some of the quests can get very tedious. In the Loch Modan you have kill these troggs over and over and over again. There are some really cool quests though. One of them I did recently was I had to travel around and collect various types of alcohol from various towns to give to a bartender. There is also a quest in Loch Modan were someone has planted a bomb on the Dam, and you have to find it before it goes off. If you do find it and report to the Dam engineer, he will let out a yell alerting everyone in the Loch Modan area that you are a hero and that you have saved the Dam. Not to mention there are class specific quest too. To get my voidwalker, I had to go kill a women who stole a precious amulet from this master warlock. Once I returned it to him, he then summoned a voidwalker that I had to defeat to break it, so I could take control of it.

There are going to be tedious quests in every game, but so far I think they have done a pretty good job on the quests. The only real problem I possibly see are that alot of the beginning quests are very similiar. Like if you start a dwarf then work on a gnome later. You quest progression will be very similar(since they start in the same area). I had a dwarven priest during alpha, so when I went to start my gnome warlock recently I got kind of bored with the dwarf area quests. What was nice is that I could head down to the human land, and work on those for a nice change. I hope that gives you some perspective on what quests are like.

A warrior is one of the easiest classes to play, however that doesn't mean he is a walk in the part. There is some definate strategy to play one. Warriors can not use guns/bows till level 20 though. The character your wife would most likely want to play is a Hunter. No one has had a chance to play hunter yet, but basically its a ranged fighter with a pet. I am not sure if they start with ranged weapons or not, but if not they probably get it early like at level 10. Hunters are also suppose to have skills that enhance ranged weapons, too. Hunters seem like to me that they will be like a warlock that fights with a ranged weapon instead of spells. Right now I am working on a warlock and learning to control a pet can be somewhat difficult. More than a few time I have not been paying attention and my Voidwalker will run off and aggro a whole group of mobs then run back to me. In fact I single handly wiped out my entire group while hunting murlocs because of this. I have not yet had a chance to try hunter, but I plan to as soon as they are released.

Right now no one uses bows or guns very seriously. Their damage so far hasn't been very good, and most warriors use them to pull mobs because they don't have a ranged attack. Recently though better ranged weapons have been showing up, so there is a possibly that they could actually be worth. Since warriors have abilities that enhance their melee weapons, it would be kind of pointless as a main weapon to use a bow that wouldn't benefit from any of your skills. Rogues have throwing daggers, but i see these mainly used to pull like the warrior.

Also one more thing to take into consideration. Currently ranged weapons seem to be race specific. What I mean is that some races can only use certain ranged weapons. Orcs for example can only use bows and can not use guns. Dwarves on the hand can use guns but not bows. I know Undead can use guns, but besides that I don't know about any of the other races. Make sure if she decided to go hunter that she picks the right race.
