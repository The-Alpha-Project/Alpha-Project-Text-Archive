Sorry, Tucks, you are a bit mistaken. I play a Level 18 Dwarven Warrior. At level 10, visiting a warrior trainer in one of the main cities triggers a special warrior quest. I remember doing it in Stormwind, and other times when I had to go out and slay beasts in the wild, usually sub-bosses of a type of mob.

I am told by a low-skilled Warrior trainer in a smaller city to go visit "Joe Shmo" in the Joe-Shmo Tavern in Stormwind. I went to see him, and he gave me the task of fighting a bar drunk to see if i was worthy or not. Anyways, its been quite improved. Now completing that quest automatically gives you the stance (Not some book like it used to), as well as gives you 3-4 skills to start off with (big money saver, instead of paying the trainer).

I forget which stance Berserker is, but basically it goes:

Level 1-9 you get Offensive stance.

At level 10, visit an NPC for your defensive stance.

At level 20 or 30, you visit another NPC for the Berserker stance.
