After passing the Gatemaster, the mercenary warrior Drimlor journeys to the market in the main hub of Stormwind, searching for a tailor to repair his slashed and stained tunic. Tall for his race, he is a dark-haired, strongly-built soldier of the line of Danath of Stormwind. His ragged but noble appearance defines an honourable, seasoned warrior of many battles. He is clothed in breeches, leather boots, tunic and hooded cloak, all once a subdued shade of green now muddied. Underneath the tunic lies a now-dulled byrnie. He appears bereft of all wealth save for a gleaming long-sword wrought by dwarven kings many millenia ago - a gift from a thankful king for valour in times long gone - and a purse of ten gold peices as payment for his last mission.

On guard on his way through the back streets for those who would wish to relieve him of his hard-won gains he comes across a small girl who is weeping and vainly trying to rouse her dead father lying in the doorway of a dishevelled hovel. Even though he is not skilled in the arts of magic and mana Drimlor still senses a deep and limitless power stirring within her. 'What is your name?' he tentatively asks the girl who would be all of ten years. 'Azure, my sir. Please can you help my father? He was attacked by a dark man who came from the shadows.' She began crying again. 'Your father is gone poor lass, but allow me to take you to a place where you may be safer. It is to a small chapel we will go, where I shall call upon the priests to look after you and then I shall return here and tend to your father.' 'Ok' murmured Azure defeatedly and after embracing her father one last time the unlikely pair set off for the nearby sanctuary where Drimlor would be sure that the young girl and her brooding, untapped power would be safe before any evil recognized it for what it was - the gift to manipulate mana for the purposes of greater good, or malevolent evil.

Drimlor's attention is attracted by the ever-increasing commotion near the Town Hall, as he continues to argue abstractly with the petulant young mage Rine. 'You know I'm not going to help you desecrate the temple's library just for your own perverse purposes, now be off with you.' declared Drimlor. At this, Drimlor was about to return with Azure back to the chapel to attempt to reconcile with the riled priest when he noticed a shadowy figure clad in a hooded blue cloak and wolfskin, armed to the teeth, watching the drama unfolding near the Town Hall. He grew increasingly suspicious when he saw the man say something to the passing Rine, at which Rine had stopped and taken notice to what he was saying. After a brief conversation Rine looked around furtively and then the unlikely pair took off towards the far side of the crowd. Suspicious of the pair's intentions he would have liked to have followed them, but this he could not do whilst he was still custodian of Azure. 'Talking to a strangely cloaked man was he?' the priest inquired curiously, 'No good will come of that boy, I fear'.

'His interests are simply elsewhere at his age' Drimlor stated abstractly.

Azure wandered, lost deep in thought and imagination through the hall of the chapel in which the tumulous with Rine had just occurred. Drimlor attempted to keep his voice low so that she would not hear and become upset while he was discussing the morbid business about her father with the priest, who he now knew as Culann. 'Now, if you may help me good priest, I found this young girl crying over the body of her murdered father. She has nowhere else to go and I fear for her safety and, if I may be so foresighted in reading her correctly, for the safety of a good many other people. I sense a great power within her. I do not know much of the ways of magic but in your misguided pupil, the young mage-in-training, I could sense but little power, yet in this small girl who has not even come of the age where her powers can be tapped I sense an almost limitless well of energy!'

The priest listened intently and replied 'This business with her father was unfortunate, I will send a few of this house of Light to retrieve his body and give it a proper burial. As for the child, I will get one of the other younger girls that we have here to tend to her and look after her, and we will train her in the ways of the Light, so that she is not strayed and tempted by the abundance of evil passing daily through the gates and back streets of this city.' The priest leaned closer to the ear of Drimlor 'It may not be too forward to presume that some in this city have already recognized the power within the girl, for that may be why her father now lies dead in the gutter. What it does not explain is why the girl remained...perhaps their deeds were stumbled upon by others in the street.' Culann's thought strayed for a moment and then, realizing that the young paladin was waiting for him to continue. 'If indeed her power has been recognized these hallowed halls may not be able to protect her entirely. May I ask you to return occasionally to check on things and keep your ears open for any information or dark dealings concerning her, for you would pass unnoticed more readily in a dangerous slum than I would, no offence. Also, I would like you to keep an eye on the young Rine and find out all you can about the warrior he was speaking with.'

'All of this I had already intended to do, good priest.' Drimlor replied.

'Very good, very good. May the Light guided your path and protect you in these ever evil times' The priest replied in conclusion, and with that went to tend to Azure. Drimlor followed him to say goodbye.

'Azure, I must be leaving now for I have other matters to deal with. Culann, the priest here, will look after you and take care of your father for you. You must stay with them now, I will return soon to see how you are going.' And with that Drimlor gave Azure a small charm of a forest nymph that sparkled in the sunlight from a nearby window, took his leave of the priest, nodded a short prayer to a statue of a deity of the Light and then left the hallowed halls of the small chapel behind him as he entered once again the streets of Stormwind to reacquire the trail of the unruly mage and his suspicious acquaintance.

In the sanctuary library a young boy looked down from the balconey of the library to the scene below.

"Master, it seems like the head priest adopted a new girl," the young lad, barely over 15, reported to the librarian.

"Well now how very interesting..." the keeper of scrolls said as she filed the piles of paper. Reth'orai has been, and always will be, the centuries-old keeper of magic of the sanctuary. Having witness the several great battles, the high elf no longer has a taste for blood but dwelled in knowledge. Books of old, books of magic, any source of knowledge were open to her. There was also this little fact that the elven maid was blind, and no longer deemed to be useful in the fallen city of Quel'thalas, that she went away and lived with the humans.

It was this blind-curse that saved her from the undead invasion.

How she was blinded she didn't say, but it seem not ot affect her ability to move about and most curiously - read. The eyes were normally kept closed, and only opened when she wants to intimdate someone. When opened the eyes were two orbs of blank silver - something that chills anyone foolish enough to look into them. The elf sighed as she piled the last bundles of scrolls on to the shelves before turning to her helper - a young priest in training named Tain. Tain was half elven - of which elf she couldn't say. Tain tends to call himself half-night elven, since he could see better in the dark than most, but Reth'orai had her money on high elven - she could smell the ancient magic in him. When not training Tain would go and help the lone librarian out. Reth'orai would land Tain with facts and figures and knowledge around him, like now.

"She is gifted," the librarian said.

"Gifted? Like me?" the boy asked. Tain knew he was of a different heritage, that was what made him a priest at such a young age. He natural affinity for holy magic made him quite a reputation in the sanctuary.

"Not like you," the librarian answered, "she is different. Very different. Now fetch me those books."

"Where?"

"Beside the table, under the shelves."
