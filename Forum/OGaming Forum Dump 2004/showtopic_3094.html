Well I have to say, not since I first got WoW alpha have I played this much WoW in one time. This weekend was completely dedicated to playing WoW. I got up to level 17(so close to 18) and have a play time of around 25 hours. As usual, I have taken some nice screenshots of this weekend's events, and I also have a few PvP stories to tell =). So sit back and enjoy the ride. =)

First I thought I would explain how the auction system works more indepth. I wasn't really sure how it worked, so I decided that the best thing to do was just jump in and try it. I started by setting the filter to cloth shoulders, and looking for any shoulders around my level. One popped up and I made a bid on it.

http://web.ics.purdue.edu/~aswaney/wow/auctionbid.jpg

When you place a bid, it takes the money you have for it and stores it in the auction house. This prevents people from bidding on something they can't afford. Well sure enough while I was out questing, and someone outbid me. A message popped up saying I was outbid, which was something I thought was cool. I got back to town and checked my mail, and sure enough there was my money that I put up for the auction. If you get outbid, your money is sent back to you in the mail from the auction house. I could of tried to rebid on the auction, but I really didn't feel it was worth it, so I let it go.

Next I decided to place a few auctions of myself to see if I could sell anything.

http://web.ics.purdue.edu/~aswaney/wow/myauctionarmor.jpg

Now to put something up for auction, you have to place a desposit. Its almost like you have to pay the auction house to advertise the item. The longer the auction goes for, the more money it costs. Now there is a bug right with this desposit money. If someone buys your item, you are suppose to get the money you put up to auction the item back, however right now it does not do that. I came back to check my mailbox and I noticed I had some mail.

http://web.ics.purdue.edu/~aswaney/wow/auc...ailshortbow.jpg

A short bow I put up for auction did sell. I am not sure if I got a message or not, becasue I was afk for a little bit while playing. Now when you put the item up for auction, you put it in the auction house, so when the player who bid and wins, the item is sent to him through the mail and his money that he bid is sent to you. This makes it really easy to collect your items without any hassle to either player. Unfortunately my armor auction and malachite auction got no bids. I got a message that my auction had expired, and I found both items in my mailbox, returned to me from the auction house.

Some last notes, you can cancel any of your auctions at anytime, however you may not retract a bid you make on an item. If your item does no sell, or you cancel your auction, you do not get your money back that you desposited to post the auction. There is also a "Buy it Now" price that you can set so people don't have to wait for the auction to end.

I think the auction system is really great so far. It makes finding things you need much easier, and the whole process is easy for both parties. Some of the problems are that the auction house for each major cities are not tied together. So the Ironforge Auction House does not have the same items as the Stormwind Auction House. I think this was a cool idea for them to implement, however in practice it doesn't really work. So far everyone has been going to Stormwind to sell their items, so Ironforge and the NE City auction house are pretty much empty. Some people suggest linking them, which will probably eventually happen. Thats the only real problem I see now.

Thats all for the screenshots, now lets move on to the stories.

First let me say that up to this point I hadn't encounter any PvP at all, nor have I even seen a member from the opposing faction. My first encounter with an enemy faction player was in Thelsamar in Loch Modan. The orc warrior ran into town and basically went from house to house. I think he was trying to kill the npcs, but wasn't very successful. The guards were chasing him back and forth all over town. The other players in town were also running around trying to cause him some damage. I decided that I would to try and frost nova him, so when he ran into the herbalism house, I decide to stand in the doorway and do it. Only now do I realize how stupid I was. I got the frost nova off, and he of course resisted. I am sure you can guess what happens next......he killed me, bad! One hit and I was down. Besides dieing, it was pretty fun. The orc eventually ran out of town and then used something to teleport away or go invisible. He left the area and everything returned to normal.

Now the next story is with my second encounter with another Horse player and it went much better. =)

It started out that I was heading to the Dam to get the quest where you have to save the Dam. On my way there, I noticed something odd behind a tree. I approach to see a level 29 Orc warrior. I waved to him, but got no response. I mentioned this in my guild chat, and they all told me to kill him since he appeared to be afk. I shot a couple fireballs and frostbolts, but he only resisted them. I started to go in closer, when all of a sudden he came to life! As soon as he did, I bolted to the Dam as fast as I could. He did not pursue, so I felt a little safer. I alerted the zone that an orc was spotted by the tunnels north to Wetlands, and continued my quest.

I got to the next part of the quest where I had to go to Wetlands to talk to the explosives expert, and I asked in General Chat if the Orc was still around. Sadly a couple players said he was, and that he was hanging out by the tunnels picking off low level players that wander through. This upset me and when I got to the enterance of the tunnel I noticed another player standing there. He suggested that we party and try to take him, but I told him there is no chance because we are just to low level.

I mentioned in my guild chat that someone was guarding the tunnel, and right away one my guildmate pops up and asks where. I told him in the tunnel north of Loch Modan, and he responded curtly that he would be there as soon as possible. I told the other guy with me in the tunnel that I had a guildmate coming to help escort us. I didn't think of it at the time, but I assumed the guy coming could handle the level 29 guy because most of the people in my guild are in the late 20's up to upper 40's(they have been playing from phase 2, so I am playing catch up). I noticed a horse coming down the road to the tunnel, and sure enough that was my guildmate, a level 49 Human Paladin. I was really amazed and grateful that he offered to come all the way here just to help me out. We headed through the first tunnel but did not find him. We continued on through the next and found the corpse of the orc who was killing the low levels. We assumed the guards must of got him or something. We start to continue on, when all of a sudden he comes to life. This was either really cocky or really stupid. Not only was my level 49 guildmate still there on his horse, but also a couple of low level 20 players were there too. My guildmate got three hits on him and the orc went down like a sack of potatoes. He spit on the orc and said, "All in a days work". I told him I was really greateful and appreciated it. He said it was no problem, and he rode back to Loch Modan. I continued on and completed my quest with no more problems.


Well thats all for now. I hope you enjoyed it and I will try to have something more for you next week. Later =).
