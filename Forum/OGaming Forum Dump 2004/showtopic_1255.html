True was the fact that the young night elf donned traditional night elven robes, and had long blue hair flowly loosely behind his back, and the face of a human woman, the air he carried was like any other night elf, flowy and elegant, and to put it lightly, if he was anymore feminine, he would pass as a wench than anything else.

Other than that his crew was totally normal...a group of strong elves -night elves (most of which are somehow, men, rather than woman). Only two women was in the crowd, and their personalities couldn't be more different. One was in nothing with three pieces of triangular leather and a small cape and armoured pads and a long sword. She fills her outfit VERY nicely, and attracted the eyes of many men (of which she ignored). The other one, a quiet one, was donned in a lot more fabric and sat beside the captain as she sipped at her honey tea.

The group was talking merrily, with the out going female leading the conversation and Lothurin only nodding his head and agreeing. From the eyes of the others, it was evident who the 'captain' was. But Lothurin was leading for a very good reason.

The the whole group of elves, he was the one who could control the ship by sheer magic.

Yes, these are no normal Kaldor'ei, they are the exiled, or at least, the part of the exiled that never really landed. And they seemed happy with it.

"There now captain, you should be more manly! You're not going to attract any girls looking like a woman!" one of the night elves said as he gave the captain a pat on the back, making the captain spill his water.

"It's quite alright Nathos, I'm fine as I am," Lothurin replied with a gentle smile.

"I find him attractive..." the quiet night elf woman sitting beside the captain said in a very small voice.

"Thank you Litania, I appreciated that," Lothurin replied with a smile, which only made the girl blush a brilliant scarlet.

"Here now Loth, you're making her blush!" the out-going, scantily clad night elf said as she crossed her arms and gave the night elf a 'look'. She was the second in command, and she made a point to show that...both with her voice and body, "if you really appreciated that you should show it!"

"I did," Lothurin replied with a frown, "I said thank you."

"That's not enough!"

"Would you rather I give her a hug in thank you? Or something more intimate?" the captain replied gently. It only made Litania blush even more.

"Here here! Back to the topic! I've heard a few more ships are robbed! Just yesterday out at see! We should be more careful," another crew member said while waving his mug of beer about.

"I agree," another member said, "they still haven't caught the culprit ship yet."

"That's quite alright," Lothurin said with a smile, "I'm rather sure that nobody would like to rob our ship. Right, Liccie?"

"Correct," the second in command replied with a smirk, "the Nightingale has enough anti theft curses in there to ward off the whole rogue population in Stormwind!"

"Even without the curses we're fine," Nathos said with a grin, "all we have to do is put Liccie at the door and ANYONE would think twice before trying to rob it."

"Or they would just stand and gawk at her beauty, no? She can knock a man out just by her looks," Lothurin said with a smile.

The group laughed merrily at the second in command's expense.

Marcus looked over the prow of the Axis and saw nothing but fog, stretching out for miles in every direction.
This was not an unusual occurance, as ever since the incident over fifty years ago, a thick grey fog hung continuously over the once proud ship.
No matter where the ship sailed, they could not rid themselves of the fog.
It was a curse and a blessing for anyone not on the ship.
A curse for those who missed it coming, and were unlucky enough to try and sail through, and a blessing for those on land, and on the sea in general. if the crew of this ship were able to see outside the fog, then there would be a nightmare unleashed.

Marcus walked from the prow and into his cabin, amidships. He stopped briefly to look in the mirror.
Staring back at him was a strange image. it was a man, dressed in full regalia of a first officer aboard a proud battleship of the Lorderon Navy, but a man whos skin was pure white, with locks of curly jet black hair.
He was not alone in his appearence, as the entire crew had undergone a similar transformation. He still remembered the day vividly...
Marcus shuddered slightly and walked over to his bunk.
As he lay down, the voices started again, as they always did... dark, screaming, tortured....

Kill...you can save us!...kill them!.....help us all....KILL...Save our Souls....

Marcus gritted his teeth and shut his eyes tightly, trying to block them out... but they would not go away... he only knew one thing that would make the voices stop....
Marcus left his bunk, all hope of rest forgotten, and went up to the captain, standing next to the wheelman, hands clasped behind his back. The elderly man, now something else like Marcus, did not speak. he never did.

"Captain..." marcus whispered.
The captain turned his head ever so slightly and looked expectantly at Marcus.
"Captain, the voices. you know what they want."
The captain turned back, and nodded lightly. He tapped the wheelman on the shoulder, and the Axis shuddered as the course was altered.
Marcus turned away, and leaned on the ledge once more. And, as if in a trance, he said a strange verse that seemed to spring from the recess of his not quite human mind.


Whenl the Sea is red
with the blood of the dead
When the fog at last doth part
Then, oh Axis, shall your voyage be done
And only then, may tortured souls depart.
