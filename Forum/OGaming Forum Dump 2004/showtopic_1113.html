Here's a few tidbits found in 'The Art of WarCraft' book that Blizzard released some time back. It does contain quite of bit of information as well as art. IE, explanations of various details that you would otherwise not see.

Braids: For every kill in battle, the Tauren braids a section of his coat. Legend has it that Warg Stonehorne had 87 braids on his hide upon his death.

Tauren Shields: Tauren shields are marked by icons. Each symbol represents an act of bravery or courage in the Tauren's life. This also includes any deeds or accomplishments that they have done. This may include being a priest, being on the council of elders, participating in a special battle or war, seeing a rare or symbolic sight, etc.

Pouches: Each Tauren carries with them a pouch that contains a sacred wrap and bundle. Various other articles within it such as totems, herbs, and wards are kept to protect against "The Fever". (I'm sorry, but it doesn't explain what the fever might be. But I might guess a bloodlust similar to the orcs.)

Tauren Weapons: Weapons created by Tauren and that are handled by great figures earn a history. Attached to the weapon ia piece of horn or hair from the original owner, to show proof of the weapon. Also, a cloth/paper like wrap is secured by bindings and wrapped around the weapon where upon the entire history of the weapon since it was created. This includes what battles it has been in, how many it has slain, the number of owners it has had, and their names in some cases.

Just my few cents. Thought some might be interested in the info since many have mentioned the lack of Tauren information around.

So, enjoy!
