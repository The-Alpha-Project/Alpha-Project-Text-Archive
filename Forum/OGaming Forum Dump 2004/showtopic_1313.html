Spairy Halfcast, a nice looking Gnome girl, with ice blue hair up in pig tails. Her body is lean for a gnome, and her eyes are wide and orange, like a full harvest moon. She hops merrily down the path in the woods to the inventor's house carrying an armful of magic books. The inventor, a greasy Gnome with a mad mullet had asked her to stop by because he needed a few spells to be cast on one of his new creations. Not knowing exactly what it was that she would need to cast, Spairy brought her eyeable collection of eight magic books. Finally she gets to the inventor's workshop, and outside she sees a gigantic construct.

It was made in a very slick aerodynamic shape, and had two large propellors on the back, the thing resembled a ship. Spairy walks up to it, and knocks a few times on the hull. A greasy looking Gnome, with a large mullet pops his head out of what looks like a port hole on the side of the ship. "Ah, Spairy my darling, you've arrived! Isn't she a beauty? Well how are you doing deary?" The gnome motions to the ship.

"I'm doing fine Mr. Smith, what is it that you want me to cast on this...wonderful machine?" Spairy looks at the odd Gnomish riveting style, and the half done paint job. "It's truly large.."

"Yes! It is large! I've been working on this for such a long time, in secret of course, can't have anyone stealing my ideas.. But it's a flying machine. The best damn one this world has ever seen. I've just now put the final touches onto it, and I need to start the engine. That's where I need you my dear."

"How so, I'm no mechanic!" Spairy replies with a quizzical look. "How does this engine run off of magic...?"

"Well, this is obviously too large for a simple mechanical engine that I could build. What we need to do is cast a permenant spell on a water elemental, and a permenant spell on a fire elemental, then seal them in this chamber. They'll create an infinite supply of steam that will fuel the ship. I have the contraption ready, all you have to do is cast the spells ma'am." Mr. Smith grins at Spairy, which makes her feel rather uncomfortable. "Ok ok, no problem. But you have to come by this week and fix up that hole in my roof for payment." Spairy scratches her head wondering if the gnome had gone mad.

"Ah yes, of course, no problem. You can come up to the house later too if you want, Helga is making apple dumplings tonight, I can smell them from here!" Mr. Smith sniffs the air in delight of his wife's cooking. "Ah yes, I suppose I could stay for dinner as well. Well let me get to work then, show me where this engine contraption is."

And after a few spells, the task is finished, and the airship blows out a vast amount of steam from vents on the side, the propellors begin to rotate. Spairy begins to gather her books, and get off the now loud ship deck, and leave the gnome inventor to his own creation, when she trips over a jutting out pipe. Her largest book (Cooking Charms For Unmarried Gnomes) hits the ground, and flops right down on a pedal, forcing it down all the way. The ship comes alive, and shoots like a rocket into the air, and away in an instant. The force of it throws Spairy to the opposite side of the ship, and Mr. Smith completely off of it, landing on the ground with a loud 'thud'. The book remains on the pedal as Spairy and the machine go flying off into the distance as she attempts to get to the pedal, and get the book off of it. After a long struggle of crawling across the deck, she finally gets to the book and removes it. The pedal doesn't come up with the book. "Oh shit!" Spairy tries many times to pull the pedal up, and finally gets it up after at least a minute of trying. The propellors stop, and the ship begins to fall dramatically. Spairy looks down and sees a forest bordered by a mountain, she quickly compiles a feather fall spell in her mind, and casts it on the ship numerous times before it begins to slow down enough for a survivable fall into the mountain side. The ship hits the mountain, and Spairy is flailed about as it comes to a harsh halt. There's snow around, and it's cold. Spairy notices a path not far from the crash, and she walks over to it, hoping to find some form of civilization in this alien area.

If not for Anteni’s minuscule stature, the brilliant white snow, without a trace of doubt, would have been smothering him. It had fallen but yesterday, and the ground he tread upon had yet to be trekked. He could guess at its depth, but to do so would only make the prospect of sinking in it even more troublesome. Each time his booted feet touched the dubious ground, a scant whisper in his mind assured him that this would be the moment in which the snow would claim his lower torso. Eventually, however, these whispers became even less than that, soon to fade in and out of his mind periodically until they were of no importance at all. Such is the method of traveling long distances in uncertain, inclement terrain, he supposed.

A mist of breath came from the heavily protected Gnome (he was, of course, covered in numerous insulated coats and other various winter equipment), as he halted to grope beneath his myriad layers. For a moment, an onlooker would swear that the entirety of both arms went completely limp, and a strange crawling of sorts could partially be seen around his chest, beneath the garments. A pipe appeared from beneath his clothing, entered his mouth, and was promptly lit by an unknown source, yet again from within the layers of protection. In an instant, his arms awoke and his gloves were once again filled with the small digits of a Gnome, and a satisfied grin stretched across his bowl-harboring lips.

In this unmoving state, there was complete silence all around him, but Anteni had failed to note it until the serenity was shattered by a strange, distant cacophony. After a few moments of listening, he discerned that it was unmistakably getting nearer, and the origin of the noise brought the traveler’s face skyward. Gradually, the sound became harshly loud, to the point that he thought whatever was causing it would be right on top of him. But it continually grew stronger in intensity, and the unearthly sound was beginning to rattle the Gnome’s brain, until finally, a giant mechanical thing of sorts soared by, scraping the treetops. The exaggerated whirring sound faded for a few scant seconds, and ensuing was a thunderous crash detailed with cracking wood. Intrigued, Anteni took a quick puff from the pipe and began to run.

Following the indisputable path of destruction with great unchecked haste, Anteni nearly ran into a similarly small woman walking his way. Utterly surprised, he threw his weight back to prevent from hitting her, and fell upon his buttocks, landing softly in the snow. A wisp of smoke mingled with a cloud of exhalation, drifting upwards. Less than a hundred yards behind the equally astounded Gnomish woman was a machine of extraordinary design, and Anteni’s eyes burned brightly with radiance, in awe of what he could see. He quickly stripped off his outer coat and tossed it to the girl once he was upon his feet, but the first words he was able to mumble were, “What is that?”

“A flying machine,” he repeated, to no one, with a hint of sarcasm as if to mock himself for not realizing such a fact after seeing that exact object soar overhead. His awe, however, worked its way into his mind like fleetly growing ivy that tangled any thoughts of rational capacity and proceeded to strangle them mercilessly, allowing only garbled, half-thought phrasing to filter through to his pipe-filled mouth. Most, if not all Gnomes had encountered the mass produced, traditional flying machines that even the remotest amateur engineer could partially recall the designs of from basic studies of the trade. But this piece of machinery... indeed a true prodigy of airship technology advancement.

Perhaps a minute or a half of an hour had passed, Anteni could not determine, but a slight shifting in his rapt vision towards the aircraft alerted him to the presence of another living creature. Calmly, he looked towards the other gnome, faintly recalling what she had imparted to him. “My name is Anteni. First of all, we both need help getting this back to its owner... we’ll have to inspect it soon enough,” he said, a trace of eagerness ringing in his last syllables, though it quickly disappeared as he continued, “but we most likely don’t have the needed items to fix what went wrong. Second, you’re in the Dun Morogh mountain range, and I’m on my way to a mine. In a matter of hours, we could reach it.”

Admiring her magical capabilities for survival, he almost missed the food she held out to him. "Save it, I ate less than an hour ago," replied Anteni to the offering. Perhaps she wanted to munch on something later, for at the moment he was unsure where they would end up.

Instead, he pulled from his persona a leather pouch, and in turn from that a strip of beef jerky. He ripped a stringy chunk from the meat and chewed thoughtfully, looking off into snow-white trees. The Gnome had wanted to board the ship when the woman had done so, though to do so would have been disrespectful, nevermind awkward. It was a revolutionary piece of artwork to him, and he wanted to critic its every detail. Whatever it was, it far surpassed what he knew of engineering, so he contented himself with the thought that even if he had gotten onto its deck, the craft would have been so foriegn that the whole inspection would result in one simply baffled Gnome.

When Spairy seemed satisfied with the portion of food that she consumed, Anteni stood up, despite the fact that this action truly did nothing to add to his miniscule height, stretched with a groan, and let out a heavy, relieving sigh. His bag of jerky concealed once more, he again placed a packed pipe in his mouth and lit it with a small fire-creating device. It took a few frustrating movements of the thickly-gloved fingers to finally get a flame in the frigid weather, but ultimately a poorly executed smoke ring was exiting Anteni's mouth. He looked at Spairy inquisitively for a brief moment, and then he glanced down at her leg.

"Are you able to travel?"
