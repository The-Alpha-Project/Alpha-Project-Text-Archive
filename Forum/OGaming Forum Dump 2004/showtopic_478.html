our draft for organization thus far:
3 Ministries:
Ministry of Knowledge - Headed by 1 Lord, assisted by 2 Officers
Ministry of State - Headed by 1 Lord, assisted by 2 Officers
Ministry of Roles - Headed by 1 Lord, assisted by 2 Officers


Division of Responsibility:
MoK: Website updates and designs, distribution of information on guild events through all appropriate means, distribution of members works (ie, member created fiction, fanart, and other creative pursuits), also distribute information in regards to current and update knowledge of the game itself or it's community. Discusses information policy at Conference.

MoS: The responsibility of recruiting and discharging members, rendering assistance either directly or indirectly to members requesting so, the enforcement of guild protocols, oversees guild merchant force and it's markets - rendering assistance if needed or setting guild discount policies, Discuss internal affairs of the guild and it's policy at Conference.

MoR: Coordinates and plans Role Playing events within the guild or with allied and enemy guilds. Assists those not familiar with Role Playing or unsure of their characters or the guilds Role Playing, in charge of executing RP events, coordinating with other ministries on scheduling and providing information, Discuss Role Playing Policy and anything relating RP of the guild as a whole.

The Ministries' Lords have those powers and also able to name adjutant officers to assist the officers subordinate to them. The reason for Officers to assist the Lord is that sometime the Lords do not have that much free time to handle all their duties in a timely manner, and also that sometimes players in leadership position sometime are absent from the game for a long time or quit leaving the guild with no leader and unable to progress. This way if a Lord is gone for a long time or short time, the whole function of the guild isn't crippled as the other Lords are still there. In the mean time, the senior Officer of that Ministry can step up and take command of the Ministry. If the Lord is gone for intolerable amount of time without notice, the senior officer will be named as the new Lord of that Ministry. The disposed Lord will be notified via email or other communications that he has lost his rank and has the option of reverting to an Officer of that ministry, become a regular member, or be discharged. The same policy goes for the Officers.

Laws, Foreign Affairs, and Policies are set by the Conference of Ministries. The Conference is called by the senior Lord or the two other Lords and Officers from the senior Lord's Ministry. Naturally, the other Lords can request the senior Lord to call a Conference.

The most senior officer of a ministry has the power and responsibility to represent his or her ministry in a conference should his or her commanding Lord be unable to attend.

Naturally, the Lord of each Ministry is expected to communicate with his subordinate officers so that everyone understands each other. It helps when the officers know what the Lord's intentions are when he or she is acting as a representative of his or her minsitry.

-Alpha Caelum-

My MMORPG days startet back in AC, but i never really got into that game.

Then since im a norwegian myself, Anarchy Online caught my eye.
I played AO since the beta stages, and Formed (together with Patk9) the guild Lost Chapter, which is now one of the largest guilds in AO.

but i slowly stopped playing AO regularerly, for RL reasons. and i never managed to get back.

Since then i have been looking for a new game to really make me get that good feeling again. Personaly i think that the reason that AO was such a great game for me, was my guild. Creating and running a large guild with such a great "spirit" and attitude between the members made me log on to Rubi-ka every day.

And i must say that WoW is giving me that great feeling once again, this game has the potential to become a great MMORPG. And with a great guild the experience would be complete for me.

That is why i have decided to start a WoW guild with my Rl friend Hedge, who also played AO.

We will focus on creating a guild with the same "spirit" as LC had, great people, who enjoy playing the game, and hanging out with each other. Not people who are in the guild to get "phat loot" or to "powerlvl"

We will recruit for the guild purely on personality. and kick people for the same reasons.

Some guild info:

Name: Alpha Caelum (Caelum is latin for sky, so in english: First Sky)
Conflict Side: The Alliance
Realm: not yet decided
Homepage: http://alpha.web-art.no (coming soon)
Founders and Current President's: Fesan and Hedge
IRC: Alpha-Caelum@quakenet


The guild structure is based on the good old army principal (see picture for overview):

we have 6 ranks:

-President
-General
-Squad Commander
-Unit Leader
-Unit Member
-Applicant


The president is the Leader of the guild,

The generals form (with the President) the inner circle, only people who are generals or president can vote in a inner circle-vote. Each General has responsibility for 1-2 Squad commanders. If all the Generals vote to owerthrow the President, a guild-vote will be held, where everyone can vote for a new president

The Squad Commanders have responsibility for 1-3 Unit Leaders,

Each Unit Leader commands one unit of members, number of people in each unit can vary from 1-10. Each unit has one leader, and 1-9 members.

Unit Members is the main bulk of the guild, each member is attached to a unit.

When you join the guild you will be a Applicant for 2 weeks, if noone in the guild has a reason why you should NOT be a member after those 2 weeks, you will be assigned to a Unit.

to get a overview of this system take a look at the attached picture, the people with a red flag, can vote in inner circle-votes. during guild-votes everyone exept applicants can vote

http://www.web-art.no/leadership.bmp <Follow this link to see the picture, image too big for the post //WoWLands Staff

The reason for this structure is two-folded,

the main reason is to be able to controll a large ammount of players the fastest way possible during a large PvP Battle, the experience i have drawn from AO (and countless battles between clans and Omni tek) is that organisation is EVERYTHING during such a battle, when you have 200+ players in a fight on each side of the conflict, there is total chaos.
with a good infra-structure we will hopefully have a advantage over the other guilds.

the second reason is that it makes it easyer to "find your place" in the guild, not just in pvp but socialy. when you enter the guild you will at once have 10 buddies in your unit, and if there is any problems or questions you will always have a unit leader to ask, and the unit leader knows exactly who he should go to if he feels the problem needs to go higher up in the system, and so on.

we will have alot of "team-building", competitions inside the guild between the different units, pvp tournament between the units for instance, or competitions to see who can clear a dungeon the fastest, or who can collect the most pvp kills during a week etc.

With our previous experience from MMORPGs and Leading guilds i think we can create a guild with a nice atmosphere.

Since both WoW and the guild is still in the process of being created, the rank system and 2week applicant period is not yet implemented. all of this is currently just thoughts, ideas and wishes. Alpha Caelum is currently a vision.


if you think this is something you want to be a part of, contact me or Hedge at #alpha-caelum @ quakenet or wow.no@quakenet
