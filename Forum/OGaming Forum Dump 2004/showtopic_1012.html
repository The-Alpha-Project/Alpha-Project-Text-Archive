Not far beside Sevitius stood a looming figure that was giving the dreadlord the chills of dread. It was an annoying presence, something that one couldn't shake off. It was eating at the dreadlord like some annoying fly that he should swat...or bicker with for that matter.

Of course Loth'eal was totally aware of that, but he was too busy concentrating on the food table in front of him to do that. Beside him were two other elves, a priestess, and a night elf.

"Loth'eal..." a night elf said in a warning tone.

"I'm not thinking about it! I promise!" the priest protested.

"Yeah, right," a feminine voice piped up behind the elf. There was a flurry of wings, and two forms appeared behind Loth'eal. Vampires, or demonic constructs, twins, a girl and a boy looking in their early twenties. A pair of large slender leathery wings pretruded from their backs, and both dressed in very proper vampiric dress. The girl had her hands on her hips as she looked up at the elf with piercing eyes. Loth'eal gave a weak smile.

"He spiked the drinks," the brother concluded.

"No," Loth'eal replied.

"Not yet anyway, huh?" the sister said. She gave her wings a stretch and gazed down at the group of milling people.

"You are dressed in a most presentable fashion," Loth'eal commented, raising an eyebrow at the somewhat curvy suit the girl was one.

"Tis traditional vampire wear, I see you're in your white robes," the girl shot back, "are you not thinking of brawling tonight?"

"I don't drink."

"You don't need to," the boy countered. Already he could notice the tension between the elf and the dreadlord.

"Go for it I say," Licia said and she prodded Loth'eal from the back.

"Go have fun with Tathar I say," the priest retorted, only to be rammed even harder at the side.
