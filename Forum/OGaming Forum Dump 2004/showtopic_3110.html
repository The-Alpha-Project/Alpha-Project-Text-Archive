I did an analysis of a lvl 60 druid, shaman, and priest, and found that priests are marginally better healers, by about 5% over the course over 20 seconds. (full healing talents(restoration, holy, and the shaman one))

Priest healed for ~13000 for ~4200 mana
Shaman healed for ~11500 for ~3500 mana
Druid healed for ~12000 for ~4300 mana

Its all a matter of (quickly) getting the feel of when to cast your big heals, as flash heal and the other short cast heals are terribly inefficient time wise, and even worse aman wise.
Flash heal ~500 HP per second of cast time
Greater Heal ~700 HP per second of cast time


Besides you'll probably find spamming flash heal boring, if you don't mix it up by adding a renew or greater heal here and there

the point of the post was to dispel some myths about priests not being the best healers,(which you will find if you look through the official priest forums for long enough) and to provide a bit of interesting information.

that said regrowth owns (druid, 50% crit rate with talents, heals ~1100 HP upfront, +~1100 over 21 seconds for a 2 second cast) not very mana efficient though.

/edit this is a bit out of date given the new patch. I don't know what the current stats are for the heals. (i play warlock)
