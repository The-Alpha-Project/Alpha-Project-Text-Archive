Munin Steelhammer swayed back and forth with a mug of foaming Ale in his hands, singing a tune about fried chicken. The fire in front of him crackled as it burned, reflecting against his flaming red beard and coal-black eyes. He hiccupped and laughed wholly and he slid down his log seat and onto the ground, narrowly eluding a slide into the fire. After a long belch, he smiled happily and patted his full stomach, loosing his belt slightly.


“It’s good to be a Dwarf,” he said and raised a mug into the air, as if crashing mugs with a fellow Dwarf. “Cheers,” he mumbled drunkenly and gulped down the last of the Ale, some of the beer missing his mouth and dribbling down the side of his face.


The area around him was frosty and cold, with ice hanging to every scarce tree. He was just leaving Ironforge and traveling to the city of Stormwind to trade some of his Ale that was famous throughout Azeroth (He found a delicious ingredient while passing through the Elwynn Forest).


Munin fingered the tip of his axe that was hanging on his belt. The smooth wood surface was cold and icy. Tied to a tree was Munin’s pony, Beergulper. Beergulper tossed his black hair over the side of his neck and neighed annoyingly at the rest that was lasting three days.


Munin’s nose twitched and he rose to his feet, mesmerized by some delicious scent; the sausages on fire were finished and he could smell their warm and rich meat in his mouth already. Rubbing his hands together and licking his lips, he tossed them onto a plate and gobbled two sausages in one bite and then swallowed the next three easily. After belching, he licked his fingers.


After filling himself to the fullest, Munin settled down to sleep, but keeping a hand on the handle of his axe.

The Wilderness had been brimming with movement for days now, you could tell by the way the animals were acting: the smaller ones were nervous and on edge, most likely frightened for their lives. The bigger ones were getting more adventurous, exploring more open areas of the forest at night time in hopes of finding a camp of adventurers fast asleep and the food that they carry with them. But it was the patterns of the trolls and the other large carnivorous creatures that Grim Pawstalker was interested in. The trolls had once again begun to patrol the woods, they were wearing their hunting masks. The winter solstice must be on it's way, Grim thought to himself, for they are preparing to hunt the Great Yetis, whose mating season would begin on the new moon. the Yetis will soon becoming out of their fortress like caves in search of a mate, which is when the trolls will strike. But tonight the trolls patrolled for a different reason...

Grim strokes his chestnut brown, unkempt, beard. He has been living in these woods for nearly a decade now and his appearance has never been an issue for his only companion is his pet Mountain Lion named O'lapp, which he has raised from a pup. As O'lapp purrs he and Grim stare down from their tree top hide out onto the trap that they had laid. You see, down in the clearing on which this tree is on the edge of, there sits a very beaten, very bruised, child troll, tied down to stake. The child was unconscious and through all of his bruises you could see the markings of the chieftains. Grim had captured the Chieftain’s son the previous evening when the majority of the clan was out hunting. Grim had always loved hunting the Trolls, and what better bait could their possibly be to lure them into his trap

Suddenly there is a crack of a branch and from his branch he sees two Winter trolls trudging out into the clearing, their hot breath hangs in the air as they rush to their fallen prince. Before they know what’s happening O’lapp has leapt from the tree and left a deep bleeding gash in the throat of one of the trolls, who is now screaming on the ground, bleeding away the last few seconds of his life. The second troll lets loose a terrible howl and lungs for O’lapp, but mid leap the back of his head explodes with a deafening crack as smoke lingers out of Grim’s hand crafter Blunderbuss, a precision Boomstick. As birds fly from their trees in fear, grim descends form his tree top perch. O’lapp has buried his fangs into one of the trolls, who has now turned the once pearl white snow a dark crimson red. Grim Stands over the body of the Troll that he took down, he removes a long, thin blade and begins to skin his victim.. Troll Skin is tough and sturdy, and while blue when it is attached to its owner, it tans a dark black. It is the same Dark black leather that Grimm wears as gloves, boots and long jacket. The young Troll awakes to this gruesome scene and yells in his Trollish tounge “WHY HAVE YOU DONE THIS!?” Grim, with out even looking up from his work replies in his quiet, crakling voice, “Because your people would of done worse.”

He gets up after he has skinned the Troll entirely, walks up to the Troll-boy and with his callus covered hand knocks the Troll-boy unconscious again. He takes his Blunderbuss and the now almost empty ammunition pack and slings it, the troll skin, and the young troll over his shoulder. “Come on O’lap” he says in his gruff but quiet voce, “There are outsiders in the Forrest again, lets go find them before our friends the Trolls do, and if they weren't agry before, once they find these two bodies they will be for certain."
