Firstly I should say that I’ve only played one MMORPG in my life – Ultima Online. I played UO for several years (I first bought it in 1999), and I considered myself at the time to be fairly well-versed when it came to online games.

But after reading the official World of Warcraft beta forums, I feel pretty stupid. Raiding. Grinding. Ninja-looting. Pulling. Those are just some of the MMORPG terms that I have never heard used before ever in-game, and while I understand the meaning of those specific ones now, I’m still fairly clueless when it comes to other words in this new lingo for me.

A lot of the players on the WoW beta forums seem to be veterans of EverQuest, a game which I’ve never played and know little about, I admit. At the risk of sounding insulting to some, and I'm sorry if I sound like I am, but the players of EQ seem much more focused on owning the best items available, reaching the highest level as quickly as possible, having the best skills, etc. than their UO counterparts, at least from my own experiences.

Like my time spent in UO, I solely want to play WoW for one reason: to have fun. I don’t want to stress myself by trying every class and every race and every possible combination for a character in WoW. I don’t want to have the most powerful character possible. I don’t want to gain online fame or notoriety. I don’t want to spend countless hours merely for the purpose of obtaining a single item. I don’t want to concentrate on completing every quest. I don’t want to have any purpose in-game other than for my aforementioned reason.

Obviously a lot of the more hardcore gamers find the above things enjoyable, and that’s perfectly fine, of course. They pay the same amount to play the game as everyone else, and have every right to do what pleases them. But, for lack of a better word, I’m finding myself almost overwhelmed. Even the roleplayers I find seem more aligned with the hardcore gamers than the casual ones.

So if anyone else out there considers themselves to be a casual player, please raise your hands, and alleviate my ‘concern’ that casual players in WoW are few in number. :)

Well... I HAD to start out casual since I was using my bro's computer and I couldn't always play...

Soon as I got my own computer I got a little hooked and then mellowed out, as Tucks said. WoW is my first MMO and all the terms will come very easily to you. Raiding is just a big attack from the opposite faction, grinding is killing monsters over and over again to level up as quickly as possible, Ninja Looting is when someone loots a really good item without rolling for it (WoW generates a random number from 1-100, made to determine who gets item drops fairly) and pulling is just an in-game thing where you hit the monster from a good range so that he comes to you, and that you don't aggro other monsters (meaning you dont get their attention and they start attacking you).

Soon as you play beta for a good 2 weeks, those terms will come easily.
