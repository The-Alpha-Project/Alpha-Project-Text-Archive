There's becoming a startlingly large number of newbie warriors in the WOW world. The problem is that people are chosing warrior for the wrong reasons - warrior is the *tank* class. Tanking is the warrior's sole role in the game, yet everyone seems to be gearing their talents to fury and arms. In a group today with another warrior he asked me what talent tree I'd gone into although he was 4 levels higher than me. Being a higher level should make him the tank of the group, but he asked because he was hoping I'd have defensive abilities so that he wouldn't have to tank. If a warrior doesn't want to tank, there's a problem and not with the class. The defensive tree of the talent system is amazing and makes the warrior a tanking machine that can take on just about anything. Although I tend to be the tank in my groups, when watching other groups I always see warriors using battle stance and DW which, even with superior armor capabilities, leaves the warrior on par with any other class for tanking ability(disregarding the fact that their DPS sucks so they can't hold aggro for shit). The abilities in fury and arms are fun and cute as pie and stuff, but defensive should always be the warrior's primary concern. As for my talents: [Defensive]: Shield Specialization(5), Anticipation(5), Improved Shield Block(3), Defiance(5), Improved Revenge(3), Improved Bloodrage(3), Combat Endurance(1), Concussion Blow(1); [Fury]: Enrage(5), Flurry(3). That's at level 35. Now, I can honestly say that I know everything, but who's to believe me, so do what you want, but as a suggestion: If you want to be a DPS class, don't be a warrior.

Any thoughts?
