For the longest time I have been hearing of how people are "weak roleplayers" and are crap at this. I have written this Guide in light of people and their whining of how bad they are. Hopefully this will make people confident enough to roleplay and can follow this guide to be successful. I want more Roleplayers on the boards, and more importantly I want them to have fun.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Area One: Spelling, Grammar and Vocabulary

This area seems the nastiest for people. I will adress the easier stuff first: Roleplaying is supposed to be fun, and supposed to keep you in the mood that you are somebody, and you are PLAYING a ROLE. When you do a roleplay post, make sure that your spelling and grammar is good. This is important because bad spelling and grammar destroys your post and makes you look idiotic. If typing "u" as "You" is a habit, type it in Word and then paste it into the thread. Vocabulary is probably THE most important thing (in my opinion) in an RP.

Why? Because it is your vocabulary that defines what kind of writer you are. I am talking about the way you use verbs, ADJECTIVES, and any other parts of speech. When you are posting, describe EVERYTHING you do. The more descriptive, the better. Which sounds better?

A) He walked up the street and knocked on the door.

B) As his feet tatooed against the dusty cobblestones, he reached the large wooden door. Taking a deep breath, he made a single, but loud knock against the oak, hearing the echo on the other-side.

Even knocking on a door and walking on a street can sound good - the above example is great. Always try to use good words like those. Always make sure your adjective matches how you use it, and where it is placed. Making Roleplays and actively participating and learning from others is the best way to gain experience, as well as learn new words and begin to use them. Also I am not saying go out and read, but fantasy books (Wheel of Time, Lord of the Rings) are perfect things to read to expand your vocabulary for writing.


Starting a Roleplay

Everyone is tired of the tavern! Everyone! Why is it everyone feels it is necessary to start a roleplay in a tavern? There are other places to start people, and if you MUST begin in a tavern, try to make it a unique experience. I think the cloaked mystery man in the corner is a bit cliched and overused, and the barfight is a classic but usually doesn't lead up to any good RP stories.

Try to find a unique conflict, a quest to do, and then find a creative way to incorporate others into the roleplay. Also when roleplaying, leave a NICE opening for other players to enter/continue the roleplay. When you leaveoff just saying "There is a treasure in the mountains", you aren't leaving an opening for someone to join you on the treasure adventure. Saying something like:

"Mogrin (or your character's name) went into town searching for someone to accompany him into the dangerous mountain pass. He left a poster regarding the adventure, telling whoever wanted to join him could find him at his home during the day tomorrow"

That leaves an opening for someone to join Mogrin on his adventure. Make sure you leave these openings everytime you start or continue an RP. This makes it easier for the next person to continue on, and if everoyne does it, it makes the entire cycle smoother and better.


Characters

So many people insist on super-godly characters that own any creep in sight, making RP's quests that are easily won with amazing rewards with characters that are invincible. An important factor with character development is to have flaws... it is what add depth to a character. How interesting would someone be if they were 100% plain boring? Make your characters have weaknesses, and let those weaknesses be taken advantage of during the Roleplay - it shows you are vunerable and gives someone else a chance to help you out.

Off-Topic Messages and Other Character Associations

This section was added since I forgot it. Roleplayers should always use the tags when out of character, talking off topic and when they want to get back in character.

OT: Stands for off-topic, for off topic messages not related to the Roleplay.
OOC: Stands for Out Of Character, for messages pertaining to the Roleplay but while not in-character.
IC: Stands for In-Character, for when you are ready to get back in character and to show when it starts.

Also remember not to do major functions over other characters, like killing them or doing major actions with them. Small things like common speech or gestures are fine, but do not control other players' character - this may not be the players plan for his character and it may or may not distrub the RP.


Also remember your character is a person like any other, so you can turn evil if you like, be greedy. You don't always have to be the goody-good adventurer, feel free to change your alignment or add a twist
:twisted:.


Finally remember to have fun! Having fun is the most important part of Roleplaying, this guide is simply to help new people to get accustomed to knowing the basic information, as well as some tips to improve. Remember - have fun and see ya around,

-Mogrin.

PS: I hope this does not intervene with anybody else's view on RPing and what guidelines should be like. I think I posted a general outline on the basic topics, if someone wants to get together and write an advanced and more elaborate one, contact me.

PSS: Sorry if I missed something, I am happy to add it, and sorry if you don't like it, I am just trying to help :)
