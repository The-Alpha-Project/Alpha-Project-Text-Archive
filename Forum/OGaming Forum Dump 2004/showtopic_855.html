Name: Dunaryll de Priact

Race: Human

Class: Paladin

Gender: Male

Age: 31

History: Son of Dunathall de Priact, Knight-Commander of the Order of the Golden Shield, who was slain near the end of the Second War. For three years before his death Dunathall, training as a paladin himself, schooled Ryll in the art of the Holy Light. When left without a father, Ryll lost heart in the Light and entered the ranks of the forces of Lordaeron as a youth where he became a career soldier, reaching the rank of Captain before the Scourge came to claim the land. With the realisation that Lordaeron was lost, his commanding officer ordered a detachment of the remaining forces to Stormwind to aid in the defence of the final bastion of mankind. Dejected at being ordered away from the defence of his homeland but knowing that he must fulfil his orders in honour of his slain brethren, he arrived upon the city of Stormwind and attempted to rally reinforcements for the Alliance forces that remained in Lordaeron. After receiving no aid he assumed command of the City Guard of Stormwind in the hope that he may save the last human civilisation from devastation.

Once word reached Stormwind that the Burning Legion was defeated and the advances of the Scourge were halted, Ryll called for an expeditionary force to be assembled to search for survivors in Lordaeron. The councillors of Azeroth reasoned that the standing army of Stormwind could not afford such a loss in manpower and thus refused the request. Angered by the second refusal, Ryll resigned command as Captain of the City Guard of Stormwind and, with a small band of loyal soldiers, took up his father’s mighty sword ‘Glimroth’, resumed his training in the Light and set off to explore the northern reaches of Azeroth and the remnants of Lordaeron.
