Following Mogrin's good example in the Warrior forum I created this post to answer quickies about the Mage class. This thread is not for discussing the class, only for questions. I myself played Mage the entire second push and a while now in the third and will do my best to answer questions.

Questions will be added to this post and anyone that answers first will have their answer added here.



Q: I intend to play a gnome mage, but when in beta am not sure if i want to group. Now can mages play a good solo, or do they excel in a group?

A: As with all classes Mages can solo well in most cases, actually in most cases more than any other class (As it is now, it's still only beta). Mages are considered the most powerful class at the moment (and that WILL change).

Mages also have very good crowd control spells like Frost Nova and Polymorph to name a few. Also with the new talent system many fire spells can be upgraded with stun effects.

---------------

Q:Is the mage class able to extend his specialities to all of the magic tree branches (frost, fire, arcane) and still solo reasonably well? And in doing this "Tri-Threat" method, will the mage still be able to spend his talent points to such an extent that he is still a force to be reckoned with?

All in all, I am asking if a mage has to sacrifice 2 of his skill branches so he can specialize his talent points in that area in order to become powerful?

A: This question does not have an easy answer because the third push started not long ago and that's when the talent system was introduced. The mage I have experienced with got his talent points placed in almost only fire talents by Blizzard, only a few points was put in frost and arcane talents. That character works well but of course fire spells have to be focused on. I am pretty sure however that Blizzard wants to allow different mixes of talents and still have the character playable, I would never fully sacrifice frost spells for example.

---------------

Q: People are always talking about the three sorts of magic: fire, frost and arcane, but what about summoning food etc. Do you always have those or do you also need to specialize in it?

A: The entire Conjuration tree has been combined with the Arcane tree.

---------------

Q: Kerry, do you prefer to go with all +SPIRIT gear or all +INTELLECT gear? I hear that INT is far superior for a mage in PvP because they may easily continually cast spells in succession without rest, and SPIRIT is far superior for PvE players because 1: You get more kills per rest. 2: You rest time is very short.

Can you clarify upon this or give me some just plain facts and figures (ie: mana points per INT, +regen per SPIRIT). Although I would prefer you give details on the gear you use to play your mage. Also, please do tell which server your mage is on.

A: As stated already, PvP Mages likes intellect better because there aren't any long PvP fights (You will have enough mana to last the fight anyways so there's no need in having a high regeneration bonus) and PvE Mages likes spirit better (at least I do).

I have not played a PvP Mage yet (Maybe someone else can answer that?), my mage is on the PvE server. Since I'm still pretty poor after the item wipe from the last push I use the best items I've found so far. My staff is a +10 SPI +3 INT, amulet +5 SPI +5 INT +5 STA, my chest is +6 INT +5 SPI, my wrists is +3 SPI and my legs are +7 SPI +4 INT.

In my opinion spirit is much more fun to have @ PvE and thanks to the Arcane Intellect buff spell Mages gets a huge intellect bonus anyways.

That makes my character have 110 Sprit (Aprox. 37 mana per second regen) and 134 Intellect (with Arcane Intellect buff, +32 INT, 2534 mana).

---------------

Q: Please elaborate on what a "Counterspell" is, or if it is an event or something of the like, how it works or when you can use it. I have heard of it, but I am not sure what it is.

A: "Counterspell" is an instant cast spell that is used against enemies casting spells with casting times. If you're fast to use counterspell when an enemy casts a spell there is a chance that the spell will be interrupted and won't be able to be casted again for some time.

---------------

Q: I have read that some sort of "threat" is generated while casting a spell. (Calling for the need of the talent Arcane Subtley) What is this "threat"? How does it occur? And what does it do?

A: Threat, also called "Hate" is what is generated by all attacks, all offensive and all healing spells. It is generated against the mobs that have aggroed on you (IE attacked you) and the more threat you generate against them the more angry they become on you. In a group threat management is very important, if a Mage keeps dishing out his highest damage spells he will generate lots of threat and the mobs will attack the mage instead of the tank, a warrior for example. However the warrior can then use his "Taunt" ablility which generates threat and the mob will attack the tank again. In short, the one that has most hate (The one that has generated the most threat) is the one that the mobs attack.

---------------

Q: In reading more about talents, and planning on how I will use them. I have come across an issue. Which do you think would be more useful in the long run: Rank 5 Fire Power [Adds 10% damage to all fire power] or Rank 5 Improved Scorch [Gains an additional 40% damage, which is dealt over 5 seconds.?

A: Fire Power. The Improved Scorch talent only affects the Scorch spell which is mostly used for finishing off mobs rather than middle fight so there's no need for it to have a high damage over time.
