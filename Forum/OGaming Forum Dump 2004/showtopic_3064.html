"Ranged" is too general and a bit.... inappropriate term for WoW. Certain classes can LEARN to be ranged, its not an actual group role.

Mainly, people think: "Ok, Tank-Healer-Nuker" or "Tank-Healer-Support" whereas "Tank=Warrior/Paladin, Healer=Priest, Support=everyone else"

WoW is much diifferent. Though healer and tank ARE needed, the type of support is up to you. Druids are the best buffers, though Warlocks can provide an extra body and some good spells/de-buffs.

Rogues are a damaging support class. They can take SOME hits, not many, but can dish damage when played properly. On subject of groups, my favourite group is:

Rogues are sort of a mix of 'character types'.

When needed, rogues can tank, if supported by a good healer (My rogue at level 26 has 700 life, a warrior of equal level would have a bit higher, but its still pretty average life.)

But it can only wear leather armor which isnt as strong as mail or plate armor which would reduce the damage received, so it cant aggro too many mobs at once like a good warrior can.

Rogues are also a good damage dealing class. With good daggers I can do up to 300-400 per Ambush, and then backstab for around 150 each time. But this all requires me being behind a target.

So in an effective group, with the warrior taunting the mob so the aggro stays on him, the rogue can be the main damage dealer while staying behind the target.

But solo, Rogues are sort of 'paper tigers', dealing a lot of damage, but not being able to take it back. (Stealth is awesome!)
