Drisiel stood at the meeting of events, the focal point around which all of what had been, was, and will be, revolved. Now was the time Drisiel would sift through the threads of fate and determine the best course that they must take to preserve the balance. The Order Aspect of Drisiel gazed at the Nexus of Time, the threads of past and future where unwound about him, in a majestic tapestry the was the slate upon which the lives of mortals and immortals alike where recorded. There! a thread there just stopped it's ever spiralling growth and faded to a pale grey color, a Nathrezim whose machinations had finally led to his own death, there! a thread of fate that had barely begun to grow halted and greyed a child barely even moving yet within the warm womb of an expectant mother miscarried. Everything was laid out before their eyes, and they must protect this, the most important artifact to exist, created by the ancient Titans themselves to record and manipulate time itself. They a mere quasi-deity in comparison to them o most mighty of creators. They must protect this from the Dark Raven, the Black Winged Angel, and The Fate Ender, who would plunge the entire multiverse into a chaos of his chosing, his forming, where his will alone would be done. Would that darkest of angels then be able to challenge even the Titans themselves?

The Nexus contracted back in on itself till only a brilliant white orb floated in place of the mighty web that it had once formed. Hiding it within the folds of it's very being Drisiel morphed back and forth rapidly between it't two aspects, Chaos and Order, Maedris and Samiel, the mortal beings that made up the dual existance that was Drisiel the Guardian of Ultimate Balance. The Nexus safe within them now, Drisiel fades away on whatever event his required his presensce, at whatever key point he was required where he might tip the scales in the favor of neither good nor evil but in the favor of the ultimate law, the one law that must be upheld or perhaps everything mortals and divine alike believed in would be turned upside down.
