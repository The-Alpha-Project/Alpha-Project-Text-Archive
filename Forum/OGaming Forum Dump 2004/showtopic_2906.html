Rogues are the best straight up damage dealers. The rogue can do massive damage. I have seen screen shots of rogues that have done nearly 1000 points (yes, the three zeroes are right) with eviscerate without even having a critical hit. I have seen about 1800 point hits with a critical hit. Let's face it folks. Most players or monsters are going to be down for the count if they get hit with that.

The problem of the rogue is that it takes a skilled player to pull it off. They use charge-up skills and finishing skills similar to the assassin in Diablo II. In order to do the big point eviscerate you must have hit enough with charge-up attacks to have accumulated five charges. During that time you will have some trouble staying alive because of low hit points and light armor.

The rogue does have a skills to help with staying alive. The sap attack allows a rogue to stun an opponent for several seconds which allows them to get behind for a backstab if they are quick. There are also a couple of escape skills that allow you to run faster and avoid hits on you. Add stealthing in to the mix and a skilled rogue can be darned hard to fight against.

The warrior is a straightforward class that allows you to tank. If you like to just go toe to toe with your opponent then it is the best. Good damage, heavy armor, and big hit points make it the best dueling class for the newer player and those that do not want to learn how to pull off the timing and technique required to play a rogue.

Nevertheless, an excellent rogue player can beat a warrior in most duels (unless they get nerfed before release). It is just a question of how much effort you wish to put into learning to play effectively. The warrior is simpler while the rogue requires more effort but gives more reward if you can learn to play them well.
