In the Beginning

A screaming child has been brought into this world, a little baby tauren. This calve was pretty good size for a new born and he had dark, red fiery eyes. This baby was born to the great war shaman Merloc Warfist and his lovely wife Mena. They decided to name this little bundle of joy, Grem. After his Great grandfather Grem Warfist, who served Durotan as the leader of the Shaman council and as his personal adviser. Grem was born in the small village of Staghorn with-in the great lands of Kalimdor. The lands have been greatly scarred by the epic battle of Mt. Hyjal, where the alliance of the human’s, Orc’s, and Night Elf’s defeated the powerful armies of the burning legion. Merloc was the Orc’s ambassador to the great alliance and right hand man too the Orc Warchief, Thrall. Merloc warned that the Human’s and Night Elf’s would betray the Horde forces, but Thrall would have none of it. So Merloc employed the help of Cairne and the Horde elders to try and convince Thrall to end the alliance with “the weak Humans,” as the late Grom Hellscream liked to say. Thrall stayed loyal to his honorable alliance with the Sorceress Jaina Proudmoore and the Arch Druid Mal’Furion Stormage. Thrall had Cairne and his Tauren clan was banned and told to never return to the lands of Inner-Kalimdor. However, Merloc and his family were aloud to stay, cause of the baby. With the Tauren’s done, the horde was week. The humans took full advantage of this, allying with the Night Elf’s to bring down the horde forces. The Night Elf’s, with there shadow melt ability, slain the great Shaman, Merloc and took the few remaining Tauren’s into slavery. As for the Human’s, try ravaged the mighty Orc guilt and murdered the entire high Orc council. However Thall, and a few others escaped. The lucky ones…

New Friends

The few that escaped, escaped slavery and the horrendous torches of the Alliance forces. These few were Warchief Thrall, the Elder Drek’Thar, Captain Nazgrel of the Security Council, Chief Engineer Guzlowe, Grem, and his mother. These six weary souls had been on the run from the alliance for weeks now. It seemed like months since they had eaten, drunken, or even rested. Months since they had seen any forum of civil life, they didn’t know if they could do on. Slowly the seven collapsed, they could go on no longer. The next day, as the sun was rising, a Troll hunting party came across the six strewed bodies. Their was no sign of life left in the six travelers and the trolls decided to bring their carcasses back to their villa. As the Troll’s started to drag the bodies across the ground, Thrall began to stir. The Troll’s quickly gave Thrall and the other companions water, this revived all but Mena. At that moment, saddened my is mothers death, Grem decided to join Thrall in his quest to bring the Alliance to their knees. After Thral, Nazgrel, and the Troll’s berried Mena’s body, they decieded to travel to the Troll villa and see the Warlord Rokhan. So the Six followed the Troll’s back to their villa and brought Thrall to Rokhan’s tent. Inside the tent, Thrall told Rokhan the tail of “the fall of the Horde”. Rokhan hearing this sad tail, decided to help Thrall in his quest to bring the Horde back to its glory.

The Great Divide

With the absence of Cairne and his tribe the Horde had been undone, but what became of the Warchief Cairne and his tribe. Cairne, angered that his tribe was banned from Inner-Kalimdor, they decided to forget their alliance to the Orc’s, once and for all. His clan scoured the lands of Outer-Kalimdor for weeks and week. Finally coming to a small Satyr village, the tribe decided to claim the land for them self’s. After a quick battle, where the Tauren slaughtered the weak satyr’s, the tribe moved in and set up camp. The area was a great provider of needed minerals and food, the tribe flourished their. One day as a hunting party was out searching for food, they came across a very tired and scared young Tauren. The Tauren’s were brought to the Warcheif’s borrow, for questioning. Grom found out that the Tauren’s names was Grem’Warfist, and family of the now late Shaman Merloc’Warfist and Mena. Grem explained what had happen to Merloc and the once powerful and glorious Horde. He also told the story of Thrall and the new found friends, Rokhan and the Darkspear tribe. Hearing this news, Cairne decided to seek out Thrall and forge an alliance with him and the cunning and agile Trolls. This alliance would be known as the Horde, and the Horde would be vowed to destroy the Human alliance. Grem, however, vowed to revenge Merloc’s death, by hunting down and slaying the Arch Druid Mal’Furion.

A New Beginning

Cairne and his tribe set out to find Thrall and the Troll’s. They searched for about a week, or so, tell they came to the Troll villa where Thrall and the others where staying. Thrall was happy, but confused to see Cairne. “I’ve come to aid my brethren in undoing the Human Aliance,” explained Cairne. Thrall exclaimed, “Great, to the power of the Horde!” The nexted day, Thrall, Rokhan, and the group, followed Cairne back so his new found village. The new Horde was building its forces and becoming a strong force once again. With the Taurens, Orc’s, and Trolls and Horde forces was strong enough to crush and Alliance. Meanwhile, the town needed to be set-up and the Alliance needed to be watched and scouted. Thrall sent his top wolf rider, Mogrin, to seek out the return with news of the Alliance forces. In the mean time, Thrall and Cairne would get the base and troops ready for war. One day, a weird looking creature came to the front gate of the new Horde city. “What do you what,” Said the guards. Roxxar explained, “I seek an audience with your Warchief.” “What’s your business with him,” replayed a guard. Roxxar explained, “I come to deliver a message to your great Warchief”. “You my pass”, the guard replayed and pointed the way to Thrall’s borrow. “Warchief a moment,” said Roxxar. “Who are you warrior,” said Thrall. “My name is Roxxar and ive come to deliver a message,” Roxxar said while handing the report to Thrall. “How did you get this report, where is Mogrin.” He fell in the wild and his dying wish was for me to give the message to you,” said Roxxar. “Thank you,” answered Thrall. “Now the Horde has another strong partner to aid in the war verses the Alliance.”

An Un-likely Alliance

Having broken free from the tyrannical rule of the Lich King, a renegade group of undead seek to retain their own free will while destroying all those who oppose them. Known as the Forsaken, this group is dedicated to serving their leader, the Banshee Queen Sylvanas Windrunner. These dark warriors have established a secret stronghold beneath the ruins of Lordaeron's former capital city. Situated deep beneath the cursed Tirisfal Glades, the labyrinthine Undercity is a sprawling bastion of evil. Within its shadowy confines, Sylvanas' royal apothecaries scramble to develop a devastating new plague - one which will not only eradicate their hated Scourge rivals, but the rest of humanity as well. Knowing that they could’nt ally with the Human race, that the Undead had destroyed. So, the Forsaken entered into an alliance of convenience with the primitive, brutish races of the Horde. Thrall and Cairne were worried that this Alliance would end up just like the Human alliance, and bring the Horde down once again. Thrall knew, however, that with the help of the Forsaken, they could crush the Alliance forces. Holding no real loyalty for their newfound comrades, only aligning with the Horde because of their common enemy, the Human’s. Only time will tell how these disciples of doom will fare in their mission of vengeance

The creation of BurningSoul

The battle’s between the Horde and Alliance have Been raging on for about 10 years now. The Horde lead my Warchief Thrall and the 110 year old Taruen Chief Cairne’Bloodhoof, and the Alliance lead by the High Sorceress Jaina Proudmoore and the noble Arch Druid Mal’Furion. This war claimed many lives the destroyed many homes, from both the Horde and the Alliance. The great Tauren village of Doom Wood was the first Horde village to fall to the Alliance, this village was the home of Cairne and his tribe. When Thrall got to the village it was to late, it was burned to the ground and all the Tauren’s slain. As Thrall’s generals searched for any form of life in the desecrated village, they found Cairne and young Grem barely alive, under a pile of brocken up wood. Thrall brought young Grem to his main fortress and offered to train him in the ways and arts of a Shaman. Young Grim, filled with rage, decided this would be a good way to avenge both his mother and father. Grem started on his path of the Shaman at the age of 18, studying under the great Cairne Bloodhoof. “The road to becoming a Shaman is a long hard one,” exclaimed Cairne. ”I will become a great the powerful Shaman, to avenge my parents,” exclaimed Grem. Cairne took Grem through the Shaman trails, as well as fighting the Alliance together. Four years of fighting, four years of learning and walking the path of the Shaman. Finally, finally it came to an end; Grem was now an official Shaman of the Warsong. Now Grem needed a name that would be remembered and strike fear into the hearts of the Alliance. Since Grem had red burning eyes, he decided to change his name to “BurningSoul”. Its been 22 years since the death of his father and 15 since the death of his mother. The time has come for the Refenge of BurningSoul…..

Warriors of Stranglethorn

The Horde is divided into many guilds witch fight for the Hords. A guild is a group of different Horde races that have banned together to fight the Alliances forces. A group of Orc’s, Tauren’s, Troll’s, and Undead worries fight together to bring down the Alliance. The reason for these guilds, as the Warchief Thrall says, is to have different Strength so the alliance will have to worry about more than one thing. Since, BurningSoul had been one of the original member of Thralls party, Thrall gave him the option to pick any guild he wanted. BurningSoul looked around the world of the Horde, for a guild. Their was The Ragging Volcano Clan, The Blooded, The BloodMoon, and Warrior of Stranglethorn. He had to make a decision fast, so he started looking into each group very thoroughly. HE desided he would join the Warriors of Stranglethorn and follow the Warchief’s Dagrin Stromgard, Milo Stormcaller, and Dravkroth. BurningSoul saw them as great leaders and organized warriors, and knew that they would be the faction that brings down the Alliance.
