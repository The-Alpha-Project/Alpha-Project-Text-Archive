“You shall feel my blade upon your backside pitiful pink skins!" screamed a battle starved Orc. Saliva ran down his chin as he charged towards two well-armored humans in a battle stance waiting to counter the angered Orcs deadly charge. Both newly recruited this would be one of their first real live battle experiences, in a panic one human quickly runs out of the Orcs way and dives to the ground and rolls behind some brushes. The second human was a bit wiser, he waited until the Orcs battle-axe was meters away from him, then jumped to the unguarded side of the Orc and swung madly at the air. It his something, sure enough a black colored blood dripped from his sword. Howling the Orc turned around and then shouted, "You will pay for that!" Ripping a large hatchet from his belt he outstretches his arm and throws as hard as an Orc his size could throw. In a blur of gray and brown it flies through the air and embeds itself into the humans chest, cutting right through the armor and chain mails below. Blood pours from underneath the helmet as the Human coughs up blood and then falls to the ground and twitches for a moment and then slowly slips into death. The one Human is curled up against a rock whimpering softly. The Orc slowly walks towards him and then shouts “Now you to will end up like your friend!” The Orc raises the axe above his head and howls loudly and begins to swing the blade downward when a loud bang overpowers his voice and the Orcs face grows grim as steaming blood sprays from his stomach on the frost covered ground. Dropping the axe to the ground, the Orc grasps the wound with one green hand. He turns around mouth agape panting. A lone dwarf on a hill stands with a blunderbuss in his two hands. The Dwarven rifleman spits out a huge goober of chewing tobacco and then presses the gun against his shoulder and aims down the scope. Squeezing the trigger he fires again, killing the Orc before he hits the ground. Slowly walking towards the huddled man against the rock, he stares at him with his cold eyes. As he does this, the man notices one of the eyes is dead. The Dwarf spits on the mans shinning armor and leave a huge black stain. “Pride of the Azeroth army your are.”

Hours pass and the human male is unable to move from his spot, he is transfixed staring at the dead Orc, crows land on his body and begin to eat his skin. It’s a disturbing site indeed. Finally he climbs to his feet and walks away from the two bodies silently. Not even stopping for his dropped sword he walks directly back towards the encampment two hours walk away. During this time he still has the image of the dead Orc in his mind. After the two hours pass he walks into the darkened encampment, night has fallen over there are and campfires light the place. A man runs up to him and grabs hold of his shoulders “Wear have you been Garret! Where is David? Where are your supplies?” The man known as Garret stares him straight in the eyes and says, “I have seen hell.”

I walked around Orgrimmar, enjoying the sound of the bustling trade sector. I entered a few shops that I knew were the best in the whole city.

I got some provisions, food, water, potions and ale. I went to the clan hall around the corner, I knew their warriors, they would let me use their forge. I quickly fixed up my equipment, gave them my regards and was off.

I made my trek down the dirt paths of Durotar, enjoying the uneasy peace that settled over the harsh land. I entered a small forest, near the northern areas.

I encountered my camp, where a few friends of mine were stationed. We were hunting beasts, collecting treasures and selling them. We would camp out for a few weeks. We had sent one of our warriors farther north, to try and locate some beast encampments.

"Where is he?" I asked as I met eyes with a few other of the warriors. They sat around the fire, eating the roast pork we had caught a few weeks ago.

"He hasn't returned yet" replied one of them with a mouthful, barely able to say anyhting audable.

"Hasnt returned?! He has been gone over a week! Why have you only sat here!" I asked angrily.

"You know him, Mogrin. He is one to hunt on his own, we cannot judge where he is just by his length gone" one said.

"I am going to search for him" I said, as I dropped some group provisions I piicked up, divided some to make a lighter pack, and continued on. I stumbled upon bloodstains.

I followed up the hill, and there I found our old friend, lying dead. I punched the tree next to him as I got out my blanket. I wrapped him up, and took him back with me.

I returned to the camp, grim faced and spirits saddened.

"Friends, look." I said as I slumped his dead body on the ground. The faces went dim immediately.

I hid like a coward beside some rocks watching in horror. When a loud bang split through the air and startled me. Then another and the Orc dropped to the ground dead. It was a Dwarf." Garret then spoke louder over top of the other men. "It was not just any Dwarf. It was Eliv Bronzefist." The men the literally gasped aloud with no regards to whom heard it. "How could Eliv still be alive? He was said to be dead many years ago?" said one man aloud. "Believe me my friends, he is alive, and he is well. What his purpose in Kalimdor is unknown to me. But he saved my life, and I plan to find him and give him my gratitude. Perhaps found out why is still alive. That Goblin Sapper team destroyed his body. We all saw it." Garret spit into the grass staring out into the woods. "Something compels me to find him, not just for the sake of thanking him."

The men looked around silently when one then broke the silence. "I say we best be on the lookout. Those Orcs wont be to happy when they find that Orc body next to a human one. Tomorrow we will pack up and begin the search for Eliv Br-" Garret then shouted "No! I will not endanger your lives by taking you into deeper Orc territory. I will do this alone; everyone is born with a purpose. I am beginning to think that this is mine."
Garret proved to be cowardly on the field of war, but extremely brave with his words. After an hour or so of hushed talking the other men finally crawled into their blankets and sheets.

Garret took of his armor and helmet and placed it softly on the grass. His long blonde hair flopped out of the helmet and his golden brown eyes came into view. But before he turned in for the night he went into the bushes and fetched two sticks and made a cross in memory of David. Grabbing a bucket of water he threw it onto the fire and doused it out.
