RE: Mage spells, instant?
Ok, I'll break it down by spell type for you.

Fire based damage spells: You have four types of fire based damage spells: Fireball, your most basic, medium length casting time direct damage spell. Scorch, a short casting time, low mana cost, low damage direct damage spell. I tend to mix up the names of the next two, so I'll just say you also have a medium damage, instant cast direct damage fire spell and a long casting time, direct damage plus dot area effect fire spell.

Cold based damage spells: Your primary cold based damage spell is frost bolt, which is a medium casting time, low damage spell with the added effect of slowing the enemy's movement for a short duration (10 seconds, I think) Next you have Frost Nova, which is an instant casting time, VERY low damage, area effect spell that roots enemies for 6 seconds. Dealing the target damage also has a chance to break the root. The third frost-based damage spell is Blizzard, which is a medium cast time channeling spell with a large area of effect radius. Let me explain channeling spells here... you start to cast them, and as soon as you start, all the mana cost is taken and they start doing damage. If a channeling spell does 100 damage over 10 seconds, it will deal 10 damage every second for 10 seconds. If, at any time during the casting of this spell, you are hit, the spell ends. So if 5 seconds into the spell, you get hit, you will have dealt 50 damage and the spell will be over. This makes Blizzard very difficult to use effectively, since chances are one mob will not have sufficient agro on someone else to keep it off of you, and so it will walk over, hit you, and end Blizzard rather early in the spell, costing you a buttload of mana for very little actual damage dealt.

The third type of damage spells is arcane magic. There are two offensive spells of this type, arcane missile and arcane explosion. Arcane missile is a single target channeling spell that fires off magic missiles at a target. Arcane explosion is a medium casting time area effect spell that deals moderate damage.

As for non-damaging spells, buffs like arcane intellect and frost armor are generally pretty short casting times. Flame and Frost ward are instant, I believe. Mana shield and blink are definately instant. Summoning a phantasm is a very short casting time, summoning food and drink are somewhat long casting times. The spell to unlock chests and the like is definately a long casting time, you can't really cast it under attack and expect to survive. (Trust me on this.) Teleporting is also a pretty long casting time.

Well, I think I've pretty much covered casting times and basic effects of most mage spells. Hope this helps answer your question.. mages have all sorts of casting times, depending on the situation and the effect we want.

RE: Mage spells, instant?
Not all spells are instacast. Some are, some have a short casting time, some have a longer. And then something like a portal spell will have a VERY long teleport time. When you start casting a spell, a bar pops up, and starts filling with blue as you cast your spell. When it's full (say 3 seconds later if its a 3 second casting time spell) then you cast the spell. If you get hit while casting a spell (obviously this won't happen with instacasts) then your spellcasting time bar gets set back a certain amount, delaying the spell somewhat. And then there are things like bashes that cancel the spell completely so the bar dissapears. Does that answer your question?
