RE: Question about Mage spells
Barring human, at level 30 you have 30 skill points. You start with 1. Human have 36 at that point, I believe.

The skill/spell system... ok, spells are broken down into four skill classifications. These are fire magic, frost magic, arcane magic, and summoning magic. The maximum possible skill for each of these (as well as most other non-tradeskill skills such as staff, defense, etc) go up 5 per level. So at level 1, your maximum possible is 5, whereas at level 25, its 125. So assuming you use all 4 types of spells, at level 30 all of your skills should be reasonably close to or at 150.

When you reach an even numbered level, you gain the ability to buy new spells. Generally, these are bought with cash, though I've very rarely seen a quest to get a book which will teach you one. I know warriors get one at level 10, I think mages may as well. In later levels, in addition to getting new spells, you'll get upgrades to existing spells such as Fireball rank 2, rank 3, etc.

Phantasm is in the game, though I'm not sure its working properly. Rumor has it the damage it deals via melee isn't really dealt, but its damage shield works. Either way, I haven't seen any screenshots of it. Its a low mana cost, 30 second duration, uncontrollable pet that has low hp, deals low damage, and has a damage shield. Kind of useful though, due to its fast casting time and low mana cost.

As for trade skills and talents, they are entirely seperate. Talents you start out getting 10 points a level, at level 10 that increases to 15 points, then I think at 20 its 20 points, and at 30 its 25 points. This enables you to purchase talents such as stat increasing, fire or frost specialization, staff and wand mastery, that sort of thing. Tradeskills you get 1 point per level (except humans get one extra point every 5 levels) and you spend those to get tradeskills and additional weapon abilities (for mages, the use of daggers. For warriors, stuff like guns and bows and 2h weapons)

I think that answers all your questions. Feel free to ask any more.

You dont max individual spells, you max their 'lines'. You can have every single mage spell for your level, providing you have the money to buy them at their current ranks. Each spell has more than one rank, each rank doing more damage than the last, but taking more mana. By using fire spells, you level the fire 'line' of your skill list. By using ice, you level the ice 'line', etc.
