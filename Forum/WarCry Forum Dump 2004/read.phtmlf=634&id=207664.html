 Ruler's "Holy" Priest Guide V.3
My holy priest is focused on healing, shielding, and smiting. It's an excelent group priest, and more then an average soloer. here is how it's built.

 

Talents: Ordered from most important to least important.

Holy Specialization ( always max when you can  - increases effectiveness of your holy spells

Indomitable Spirit ( second of importance ) - increases spirit ( 7 mana 3 health regeneration per minute per point 

Meditation ( 6 mana per minute per point ) - increased mana regeneration

Mental Acuity  - increases mana by 20

Mighty Intellect - increase of intellect ( mana )

Magic Resists ( least important ) - increases all magic resists by 1 ( this can be substituted by any other physical damage resist talent )

Another popular approach on talents is putting Mighty Intellect as number one of importance, and loading up on water/milk/juice to recover your superior mana pool.

 

Spells :

Get all the spells available for your level from your trainer. Mana usage is important, so try to use lower rank spells, because they might be more mana efficient.

Gear:

Get all the spirit and intellect loaded gear you can. Stamina also doesn't hurt.  What armor value it has is not as important, because Holy Word: Shield will provide most of your defense wether you are in a group or solo. Armor value is important, but not AS important. 15 points of armor decreases physical damage done to you by 1, so it will increase the potential for your shield.

Keep in mind that you can use inner fire to up your defense and offense with little mana usage. As far as weapon choice, I personally use a mace and a torch which gives you +2 to spirit in the left hand. The best part about the torch is that its effect is AOE, which means that it effects everyone around me. However, If a staff comes along which is better statistically, I switch. Especially when soloing.

Group Advice :

When in group, i use only holy spells, no shadow at all, except "Sleep" sometimes if there is too many mobs on one person, or i'm needed to pull with Sleep ( Sleep is similar to Mesmerize from other games ). I look at my groupmate's bars alot. if their health bar goes down at all, i cast a Shield. If they are below half life, i try to cast a heal. Try not to get groupmates below half life, because heals take much more mana then you would spend trying to shield groupmates from damage. If mobs try to run away, and you don't have groupmates with ranged attacks, it can be appropriate to go on the offense and cast a holy smite or Mind Blast ( shadow specialization spell ). Try to stay away from known spawn locations, and if everyone is shielded and at full health, consider throwing wands or engaging an enemy in combat to help out with damage dealing. You can cast while in combat, so it is not a minus if you are always in combat mode with a mob, as long as you don't attract too much "aggro" or "hate". If you choose to be always engaged in combat, choose a target which is not at full health, and the person who is hitting it, can outdamage it, and keep the mob on her/him. Also, try to shield the puller before he/she pulls. If you shield them right after, you might end up with the pulled mob swinging away at you.

Advanced Tip : If you see a groupmate's health drop down faster then usual, look around. He/she might be getting nuked ( damaged by spells ) from caster mobs. If that is the case, go straight to heal, no shield, no HoT. Always try to watch whats going on the battlefield as well as the group health bars ;o).

Another advanced tip : When you have a good tank, who takes little damage, you can cast "Renew" ( HoT ) on him/her instead of heals in a combination with a shield. this regenerates the tank's health while being shielded, which is an awesome mana saver, because Renew takes significantly less mana then heals. The more you use Renew instead of heals, the more mana efficient you are, and the faster your and better your group becomes.

Third Advanced tip : Ask your groupmates how many HPs they have, so you can adjust your heals accordingly. Even if a mage has 2/3rds of his/her life down, your heal might be too powerful, and only half of its potential might be used to heal the caster all the way. in a heat of a battle, every mana point counts.

Fourth Advanced tip: When traveling with Paladins, the damage of your Holy Smite will increase in power per cast after they have landed a few crusader strikes. If planning on smiting, then do it towards the middle of the fight up until the end. 

Wands : Try to use a Holy Wand, as your holy specialization will have an effect on it, and increase its damage. Crusader strikes will also improve Holy wand damage.

Solo Advice :

Holy Priests make excelent soloers. Here is a typical approach I do when trying to kill a single mob :

 Shield > Holy Smite > Holy Smite > engage > Mind Blast ( insta DD ) > Pain ( DoT ) > engage again>  reshield > engage > reshield > engage  rinse and repeat until mob is dead.  Cast Mind Blast whenever the timer runs out, and cast Pain whenever its effect wears off. Always keep yourself shielded!

Another approach pointed out to me by others is the following:

Shield > Pain > Mind Blast > Holy smite > engage > then rinse and repeat Shield, Mind Blast, and Pain in the manner described in the previous approach.

If you get more then one mob, shield yourself, sleep one of them, and proceed to do the above on the first. when the first dies, do the same for the second. if the fight takes a long time, you might want to try to resleep the sleeping mob. sometimes however, if mana allows, you could try to tank both of them with your holy shield while DoTing both of them with Pain, and concentrating on one with melee and Mind Blast until it dies. whichever strategy you prefer.

That should get you started on your Holy Priest! GL!

- Ruler X
