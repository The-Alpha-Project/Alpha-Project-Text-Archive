Taken from www.worldofwarcraft.com



Soul Shards
The Warlock requires Soul Shards for certain spells and pets. You receive a Soul Shard by using "Drain Soul" on a monster as it is dying. A catch is that you cannot get Soul Shards from monsters that don't give experience. So you can't go into a newbie region and kill easy monsters for Soul Shards. Soul Shards take up one slot in your bags which means you don't want to carry a whole lot of them. But you do always want to have some in your bags so you can summon your pets. When using Drain Soul you cannot do anything else such as move, attack, or cast another spell, or be interrupted. If you do not stand still the Drain Soul will not work. You will need to learn how to do this properly which can be challenging.

Soul Shards do not stack. This is for balance reasons and so you won't horde huge numbers of them.

If you find yourself without Soul Shards you will have to get them without the use of most pets which can sometimes be a challenge. The Imp can be summoned without the use of a Soul Shard so you can use them to get your Soul Shard along with DOTs and then switch to a more powerful pet.



Edit to add:  Sorry I misread the topic title.  however, On the same site (http://www.worldofwarcraft.com/info/classes/warlock.shtml) I did find this. 

Soulstone (requires Soul Shard) - This is the Warlock's version of Resurrection. The Warlock creates a Soulstone. Any player who dies while carrying a Soulstone can return to life by using the stone. Do not use a Soulstone while you are alive, or you will instantly kill yourself.

Soul shards are what warlocks use to create things like blood stones and mana stones, as well as summon their pets. They can also make soul stones with these, which are a one-time (each) revival tool to instantly revive you when fallen. No cooldown has been mentioned, but if you're dying fast enough to implement a cool-down, then it wouldn't really matter how many soul stones you have, you'd be dying too fast to make any real impact on whatever it is you're dying for.
