There a couple different kinds of "bosses". We have the "named" mobs, which are stronger versions of monsters with their own names roaming the planet. Like Ol' Sooty, the bear, for example. Then, there are the bosses in Instanced Dungeons, which are remnicest of bosses in single player RPGs. Like in the Scarlet Monastary (hardest dungeon in the game right now), there is the boss (forget his name), and when you enter the chamber where he resides, you can see him standing at the end of the chamber which is filled with tons of other monsters all just bowing to him. Instanced Dungeons are really cool because they allow for a single-player RPG-like experience and add possiblities that would not be possible in a persistant zone.

RE: bosses
Really the only "group" quests are those flagged Elite. They actually have (Elite) after the name. That means the monsters are going to be elite (they have a + after their name) which means more hp, hit harder, and give more experience than normal mobs their level since they are meant as group content. So elite quests can pretty much only be done by a group, or someone so much higher level than the quest is meant for that the reward is trivial. Normal quests, however, can be done either solo or grouped. Some quests, such as collection quests, work better solo because you're not trying to get 9 crude flints x 5 people = 45 crude flints. Slaughter quests, on the other hand, work better with groups since killing 10 mottled raptors and 10 mottled scytheclaws means you kill 20 mobs, whether you're alone or with 4 other people. But you can do both types whether you're solo or grouped, or join a group halfway through, whatever you want.
