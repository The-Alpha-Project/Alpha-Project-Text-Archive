Starting Out Advice
Another post stolen from the good Finnien, guildmaster of Sunder and moderator at Penny Arcade.

Coming into World of Warcraft release, I thought I’d take a shot at writing up a little beginning guide, which may or may not help new players.

Everybody starts in a little newbie area, standing next to a few quest mobs. First thing to do is get those quests. If you’re a dwarf, one will be to deliver some hot coffee, then return the mug – that one alone gets you most of a level. Others have you report to a commander a few feet away, or kill the nearby monsters. Grab your quests, and start killing the little stuff.

Loot everything, vendor often. Spells come at level 2, 4, 6, etc. The starting area trainer only has spells up to level 6, but if you’re level 8 and still in the starting area, you’re doing something wrong.

6-slot bags are 5 silver. If you get extra cash, look at how much your next spell set will cost you, and if you have 5 silver more than that, buy an extra bag. Inventory space is at a premium early on.

Around level 5, you’ll have completed the quests in your starting area. Start traveling to your next destination. For humans, this is Goldshire. For dwarves or gnomes, it’s Kharanos. You’ll probably have a quest or two from your starting area to speak with somebody there. Head on over, and get all the quests you can.

At this point, Thottbot (www.thottbot.com) becomes your best friend and ally. It is the ultimate searchable database and reference tool. You can search for quests by zones, and every quest NPC it lists will have its location on a map you can click on. Do a quick search for your area, and you’ll find who has quests around you. In Elwynn Forest, there are quests in Goldshire, the two farms to the south, the guards at the bridge a ways to the east, at the Eastvale Logging Camp, and at a wanted poster at a guard tower southwest.

Efficiency is key. Once again I’ll use Goldshire as an example. In Goldshire, you’ll get a quest to explore a nearby mine infested by kobolds, just to the south. You’ll also get a quest to collect gold dust from the mine, and a quest to collect candles from the kobolds. Additionally, if you go to one of the farms and speak to “Auntie” Berniece Stonefield, she’ll give you the Lost Necklace quest. After talking to Billy, collecting some boar meat, and making him a pie, you get the final step of the quest – to retrieve a necklace from a large gold-toothed kobold (cleverly named Goldtooth) inside the mines.

Another way to increase efficiency is grouping for certain quests. Anything where you need to "slay x mobs" or collect 1 of something from some mob gives credit or the quest drop to every group member, so 5 people killing 10 mobs goes much faster than 1 person doing it, and 5 people completing a named mob kill at once is much more efficient than each person waiting around in a line to kill the named mob.

So, venture into the mine, kill Goldtooth… and complete four quests at the same time. That’s a perfect example of how quest efficiency works.

The best quests to do are ones that are green. You can do them quickly, easily, and you still receive xp from the mobs and the full quest reward. After awhile, you’ll find yourself running out of quests, or only have a few harder quests remaining. At that point, it’s time to move on. My advice is, instead of running over to Westfall, for example… take the Deeprun Tram over to Ironforge, and run to Kharanos. If you’re in Kharanos, run over to Goldshire. You’ll find a whole new set of quests that’s easily within your level range, that you can do quickly and easily. Then do the quests along the road to Loch Modan, do the lower Loch Modan quests, and pop back over to Goldshire. Finish everything there that remained, run to Westfall, do the easier stuff over there, then head back.

Of course, if you somehow run out of quests in both the Dwarven and Human areas, you can always run over to the Night Elf lands. I’ve heard Darkshore has been thoroughly reworked and is much easier and more enjoyable than when I did it four or five months ago. However, since my experience with it is out of date, I can’t really say too much.

Sometime before release, I’ll try to write up an actual leveling guide, starting from Elwynn and going through to around level 25-30ish, detailing the route that I’ll most likely take at release, what quests I’ll do, what hidden NPC’s there might be, etc. I'll also post information on training and guild locations, but I have to go do a bit of research myself - I haven't done the 1-20 game in awhile. If there’s any specific questions I can answer, please, let me know. I’m always happy to help.

And yes, this probably would have been a lot more useful a few weeks ago, before everybody got into the open beta. I've been busy lately!

P.S. Everyone should get First Aid. No reason not to. And if you don't know what tradeskills to get, get herbalism/alch. Good luck, see ya in retail. =
