To Party or Not To Party
I'm going to share with you all an experience. An experience that I had whilst my lv 25 paladin, Taeura, foolishly enlisted in what would soon become (unbeknownst to her of course) a juvenile crusade nightmare into the Westfall's Deadmines. For those who aren't familiar with the Azerothian territories or Westfall for specifically, Deadmines is home to the popular instance that has been reduced to the title VC, short for Edward Vancleef, the final installment in like an eight part quest called the Defias Brotherhood. Now, because there are 6 sub bosses and 1 final boss, most people party up with five, although I have once done it with four. As you might know, it's often difficult to get a good party together, and as this adventure will prove, sometimes you can't tell just how bad your team is until you're in the thick of it. Well, here goes...

*Note, the names have been changed to protect (against merciless flaming and mockery) the identities of the poor souls involved in this fiasco.

The Players: Taeura (myself), Lv 25 Human Paladin
                    Borgodd, Lv 20 Dwarf Hunter
                    Iamking, Lv 21 Human Paladin
                    Celestia, Lv 19 Night Elf Priest
                    Gavan, Lv 22 Human Warrior

The Enemy: The Foreman, Ogre, Entourage (0-2)
                    Shredda's Machine, Mech, Entourage (0)
                    Shredda, Goblin Engineer, Entourage (0)
                    Gilnid, Goblin Forgemaster, Entourage (2-3)
                    Mr. Smite, First Mate, Entourage (2)
                    Goblin Captain, Cook, Entourage (2-3)
                    Edward Vancleef, Defias Ringleader, Entourage (4)

 

So we all meet at the brink of the instance, a shimmering blue portal deep in the murky depths of the Deadmines, the most forbidding and perilous sector in the whole of Westfall's godforsaken and overrun territory. After fighting our way to the instance, we're waiting for our mana to regenerate. The leader of the group is surprisingly the least physically able and most likely to perish first, Celestia, a Night Elf priest, whose skills in healing are respectable, but whose ability to choose who in the party needs the heal most and dish it out are sorely lacking. Meanwhile, while Celestia's mana is practically in the negative, overly friendly human warrior Gavan is jumping up and down tirelessly shouting, "Priest! Buff plz! Priest! Buff plz!"

"Calm down," I say. "She's (although Celestia is clearly played by a male for reasons you'll find out later) regaining mana."

This sensible comment was clearly enough to send the Ritalin-deprived warrior hopping through the instance.

Borgodd and Iamking grow tired of waiting very quickly. And charge a few lingering miners. 2 Defias enchanters repop behind them.

"Forget them," I type quickly. "Just run through the instance." (Why Celestia had told us to wait outside the instance is beyond me). Borgodd takes off with his bear cub in the opposite direction with three miners hot at his heels. The paladin, Iamking, heroically battles the enchanters to his dying breath.

A moment later, Borgodd ghosts, and Celestia and I are left to prudently pick our way through the mines and resurrect their bodies.

"Why didn't you heal yourself in combat?" I casually asked Iamking on the way back to the instance.

"How do I do that?" asks Iamking as if the concept of a paladin healing is a novelty.

I stop walking. "Do what?"

"Heal."

"Are you serious?" I ask. "You're level 21. It's a spell."

"Oh, you mean heal," remarks Iamking. "I got confused."

It must have been my fault, I think to myself. I never had a nack for explaining things well anyway. We head through the instance.

Gavan's on the other side of the instance: naked. And still jumping. "Buff plz!" Is the first thing out of his mouth.

"Priests aren't generally buffers," I tell him. "Especially at level 19. They have 1 real buff. But I'll buff you."

"Thnx!" he says. Then he tells a joke.

We head into the mines, killing miners and Defias scum as we slaughter our way into the darkness. Finally, we get to the Foreman, an obese ogre with a bad temper and a propensity to run players over like Florida roadkill on a Sunday morning.

"Lemme pull!" Gavan says jumping up and down.

"No," I reply calmly. "That's Borgodd's job. He's the hunter and he's got artillery. Don't use your pet," I tell Borgodd. He's mining and I can only hope he's listening. "Just set a snare then shoot and pull the ogre. We dont want to draw his entourage. Wait till he walks out a bit."

Fortunately, Borgodd succeeds in this endeavor. The foreman rushes out. Somehow, even though I hit the hardest, Gavan draws aggro. Everything goes smoothly at first. The priest does her job healing. The tanks do their job slaying. The foreman falls to our onslaught.

We continue to deal with the Defias threat from all directions (they send patrols from the rear occasionally to mix it up) and work our way down to a chamber full of goblins and a gobbo boss named Shredda  (or something like that), a goblin with a massive inferiority complex backed up by an equally massive war machine.

"Priest! Buff plz!" Gavan bellows as he breaks into a dance. Iamking and I disregard this idiocy and work out our aura distribution. I'll take Devotion Aura, he'll take Retribution. Borgodd succeeds at what he does best--drawing goblins one by one. This works. At first. Somehow we manage to accumulate 3 goblins and a rear Defias patrol of two overseers and an enchanter. Great. Gavan's dancing while the two overseers go after our feeble priest. I try to draw them away, leaving Borgodd and Iamking to deal soley with the goblins. If I keep the priest alive, at least we'll have healings and resurrections in case the mission gets fubar. So I stun one of them to buy some time and work down the other one. So they're both on me. Borgodd panicks and splits back for the instance (which rarely works, because as most of you probably know, aggros will follow you ALL the way back to the portal in an instance). Iamking is quickly destroyed by three goblins (naturally he's not healing himself in combat). I manage to cut the Defias overseers to pieces and make for the enchanter. The priest is alive at this point, but she also makes a break for it instead of stepping back and healing me. Three goblins rush by me and head after the priest. I stun the enchanter and take her out, noting on my screen as the lifeblood of Celestia is quickly reduced to nothing, then I rush back up the chasm and join Borgodd (whose courage is magically restored) in combat. Miraculously (one major heal potion and a seal of protection later), we survive. Barely. Then I read the group message log.

Gavan: Where'd you guys go?

This sort of nonsensical melee and mayhem ensues as we continue to work our way down (for the next two hours of gameplay) though the Shredda, the goblin forge boss Gilnid, and Mr. Smite, Vancleef's ebon-furred Tauren first mate. By now, I'm at my wits end, but we're almost to the finish. So I decide to hang in there. Maybe VC will finally drop that barbed sword I've been looking for since I first visited those dreadful mines.

While Gavan's obliviously fishing off the docks in the cavernous atrium from which Vancleef's pirate ship makes birth, I trade Celestia some mana potions and milk to help her replenish her mana much faster. She takes them and asks me if I want a staff she picked up. I casually inform her that paladins need staves like Pamela Anderson needs a breast augmentation. Celestia proceeds to remove most of her armor then begins to dance in front of fisherman Gavan and says "Don't I look friggin' sexy?" We continue up the ships planks and work our way through the decks dappled with Defias pirates, goblin shipwrights and squalshapers.

"Careful," I remark as we near the top. "Don't go up this ramp too fast or else you'll draw all of the pirates on the last deck and the goblin captain." Yet again, my words fall upon deaf ears.

The chaos begins as Borgodd's bearcub (creatively named Smokey) charges past me up the ramp. The rest of the gang takes this as a sign that we've changed our plan of attack to an all out rush. I'm swept along with the patter of eager little feet and we charge into the fray. We're instant engirded by 2 squallshapers, 2 pirates, 2 parrot minions, the goblin captain, and his entourage of two pirates. Our life meters drop faster than the jaws of a human exploration team stumbling upon an elven hot spring.

"Step back and heal us," I cry to the priest in vain who is quickly cut to shreads by the pirates and squallshapers. Iamking's inability to heal adequately and sensibly leaves me in the lurch, so instead of dealing the powerful damage that makes me valuable to the team, I stuck healing everyone and keeping the lesser players alive. As usual. "Focus on one enemy at a time!" I add.

At least they listen to me on that account. Borgodd and Smoky, Iamking, and Gavan all surround the goblin captain (probably because he's the most impressive looking) and manage to take him out. It surprises me that Iamking uses his stun on the captain, because it's the first time he's done it during the entire quest. Borgodd plays an in-and-out game decently, but his bear is killed quickly, leaving him with a smoking blunderbuss and not much to show for it. With Borgodd out of the game and Gavan soon to follow, Iamking looses his nerve and takes a dive overboard. I try to heal Gavan so he can hack into the rest of our foes, but I'm slain before I can even get one spell off. Gavan follows me to the spirit world faster than I could type "$&#@!"

"Resurrect us," I say over the party channel to Iamking as I'm sure he's swimming around idly several decks below my fallen paladin's crumpled frame.

"What?" he says. "How do I do that?"

 

I'm not even going to describe the Vancleef fight, although I can assure you all that it was a delectable misadventure of poor fighting, lack of communication, and utter misuse of skills, talent, and efficiency. Please, everyone, when choosing a group, select your members with the utmost prudence, jurisdiction, and care, else you might actually end up with a bunch of fourteen year olds who can't type, strip their clothes off, do the macerana, and have no idea what team effort is all about.

Safe travels to all mature, skilled, and dependable World of Warcraft players out there in the world. Hopefully, one day, I'll find you.

~Tae
