RE: When is the best time to...
I'd get to around level 5, grab leatherworking and skinning, and head on over to Kharnassas (I think that's the name of the second dwarf village). Like you, I wanted a NE hunter or rogue, my friend wanted a dwarf hunter. Having a bit more play-time than him (he only played OB, while I did both ST's as well), it was decided I'd do the traveling.

It isn't really that difficult...frustrating at times, but can easily be done. Simply do a few quests in NE town to get to around levels 5-7 (couple of hours work), then head to Ddarnassus, portal to the village, hop a gryff to Darkshore, then take the boat from Darkshore to Menethil harbor. Make sure to talk to all the griffon masters (in Menethil, Thelasma (sp?), and Ironforge), else you'll have to make the same run again and again.

Once in Menethil, you're stuck with your feet for travel and this is the frustrating part. You have to run through the Wetlands, where the lowest mob I personally saw was level 22. Some of them are within agro range of the road, and at level 7, two hits killed me. Just stay on the road, rezzing as and when necessary, and eventually you'll go through several worked tunnels and eventually make your way to Thelasma. At a certain point, you stop spawning at the graveyard in Wetlands and respawn in Thelasma; at this point you may want to take the durability hit. Once in Thelasma, you're golden; though the mobs will be a few levels higher, you have a really good chance of outrunning them before you die...most of the mobs are below level 10. Continue down the road to Valley of Kings and take the first right...run some more, past the Quarry, the Depot, and then you'll be in Kharnassas.

I did the run with my NE rogue at lvl 7, simply because the dwarves don't often tend to get skinning and leatherworking. This was an opportunity for my NE Rogue to get skinning *and* leatherworking maxed to 150 in a single day. A dwarf with mining/blacksmith and a NE with skinning/leatherworking kept our rogue/hunter team with better gear than we saw dropped at those levels, with no additional expense beyond the investment in crafting tools. We made it to level 15 in a day and a half with few deaths...then I dropped my rogue and started a NE hunter, did the same run, and when OB ended, we were in our low 20s.

Although he can easily do the same run in reverse, I'd advise the NE to do the traveling. For one, there aren't as many dwarfs being played, so less competition for spawns, mines, etc...for another, Ironforge is a short tram ride away from Stormwind and the human lands. Centrally located...and as long as you've talked to all the griff masters, you can pop back to NE lands any time you want.

Also a word of advice...do all the `youngster' quests you can get in NE, dwarf, and human lands. This raises your faction quickly and easily, which is always a good thing. Our hunters were Honored (with commensurate lower vendor prices) in all 3...and almost there with the Exiled Gnomes.

That's about it. Luck to you!

RE: When is the best time to...
Blah, I was set with the same kind of problem because I know my husband is going to start a NE hunter and I will probably be a human. And so, on the last day of beta just to see if it was possible, I made the trek with a brand-spankin new level 1 human. I ran her into SW and took the tram to IF. Then ran to Loch Modan to the east and north from there to the Wetlands. This was the dangerous part, but I only died twice and it didn't slow me down at all. Actually it was quicker because it popped me up in a graveyard half way to the harbor, lol. And since I was still wearing total newb gear, any end penalty didn't matter from taking the rez. When I got to the harbor, I jumped on the ship to Auberdine, then shipped from Auberdine to Darnassus. Ran east till I got to the newb area and started to train. It all wasn't as much trouble as I thought it might be and it was kinda fun having every other elf run by me and scratch his head or say, "Hey, how'd you get here human?"

With your friend being a dwarf you could do the same trip, just backwards. And not have to take the tram to SW of course. Oh yeah, and don't forget to stop and tag the hippo/griffon trainers along the way while you're nearby them.
