Looting with Roll Dice
A guildie said in guild-chat last night that while he was partied up in a dungeon, someone had berated him for choosing to participate in a dice roll for an item he couldn't use. He thought that was a bit odd and I agreed. Another guildie opined that to not participate in a roll for something you couldn't use was "standard" and that we were both wrong. I asked him to explain how it was standard, and according to whom. He replied, "All the beta players".fficeffice" />>>

Now, I played in 2 betas and have been on many instanced dungeon runs and never did I hear from anyone that it was "standard" to pass on an item you couldn't use on the character you were currently logged in on. I'm not a greedy player, I actually did pass far more often than not on items that neither my main or my (one) alt could use, but I found myself bridling at the suggestion that there was some tacit understanding among "all the beta players" that this was the way to do things. I reasoned that there was no "standard" and that the best way to set up some framework of understanding for loot etiquette was to do just that, set it up at the start of the run each time. Agree at the beginning of the run what to do about the uber loot. It only seemed logical to me. Even if there was to be a set of guidelines created for the guild, they could be altered upon agreement at the start of an individual run, or not... either way it would be done according to a clearly communicated dictate and not left to the whimsical perception of a single player with no real objectivity.>>

Later, I thought about the fact that I did choose to self-police previously and that my decisions to not participate in a roll in the past were always based on my being able to use the item and/or the obvious need of another party member for the item. Maybe I just don't like being told what to do and would rather find my own path to the opinions of the majority? I was having second thoughts.>>

At any rate... what do YOU think? Is it wrong to participate in a roll for something you have no immediate need for? Or is it wrong to not agree ahead of time either by Guild fiat or party agreement before each run?

Well before I go in an instance with a group, I usually make sure that everyone is clear about how looting will be done.

I always tell everyone, "Don't roll on it unless you can use it on this character, agreed?"

People usually have no problem with it.  This can be good and bad however...

My first run through Wailing Caverns, it seemed that everything that dropped was geared for a Warrior, which was great for me.  My second time, it was rather mixed and everyone got something it seemed, so it was no big deal.  However, the third time I ran through WC, there was only 1 cloth wearer and it seemed like 80% of the drops were Cloth.  It got to the point where the Priest had 2 or 3 vests.  I walked out of there with some boots that were barely better than what I had, but it bothered me that the Priest kept rolling on weapons even though he had just looted a  Blue 1h Mace that was almost as good as my weapon... while other team members still hadn't looted anything good.

While, playing with some guilds, they usually say everyone gets 1 item they can use, and then everyone gets 1 item to sell.

I try not to let loot really get in the way of playing too much... but I'll be honest.. if you're in my group, and you're being greedy and taking things you can't use even though other members, who work just as hard, could use them... I'll just let you die.  No one is indespensable... not even a Priest.

There are really a number of reasons why people will say things along the "standard" line. The main one is, well, lets face it, a lot of players are greedy. They'll take what they don't need and completely disregard other players' needs. Think about it this way: if you're in an instance and people are helping you kill monsters you couldn't kill by yourself, its the least you could do (as a warrior for example) to pass on a staff of medicine (for a magicker). It's just... well...logical. Or... standard you might say.

Blizzard has implemented a need before greed looting system, but I've NEVER seen it used yet. However, it's a good idea to choose your hunting party wisely. Most players will agree to let other players have items if things aren't going their way or they really need it. Or, they might suggest a trade. For example, (for those who have been to Westfall on the Alliance side) I was doing Edward Vancleef runs. Now, as you may or may not know, there is TONS of quest stuff dropped in deadmines and the instance within it. My groups usually conduct a sort of manual round robin pickup for items that could be used by anyone and a need before greed pickup for items not everyone could use.

The short of it is, you don't need to pick up EVERYTHING. And while there isn't a codified "loot etiquette" so to speak, it's sort of an unspoken phenomonon that players (the good ones) sort of agree on. So when a player says, "don't loot man. it's standard." Reply back "it's not standard, dude. but what the heck am I (a druid) gonna do with a two handed battle axe? here ya go."
