Here's my take on it. Hunter's will be the third best in melee (I'm not counting druids in shapeshifting form) while yes, they may lose to a warrior toe to toe, as well as a rogue. Making them "ok" in melee.

I cant say I know much about how the Hunter is built but I do want to play one. I think the best way to make the Hunter versatile would be to make the pet need skill points in order to advance it's power. Pet Training it could be called or something similar. As I understand it now, the pet simply gains in power right along with its master as he gains levels, so does it. But if my method was used it would force the Hunter to continually use skill points on his pet in order to fight as a tag team type of Hunter. Of course you would have enough skills left over to also make your bow (or gun) or whatever quite effective as well as this seems to be the main idea of the Hunter.

However, here's the kicker, for those who dont want to play a cookie cutter Hunter exactly like the next guy fighting while his pet tanks, you could choose NOT to put points into pet training. This would allow Hunters to expand their style to be entirely all ranged, or maybe half ranged/half melee. Hunters forgoing the pet route would actually be quite dangerous melee fighers, if not as powerful as warriors head to head, certainly powerful enough to greatly weaken foes from a distance and then finish them off in melee. Duel Wield becomes much more desirable in this type of setup.
