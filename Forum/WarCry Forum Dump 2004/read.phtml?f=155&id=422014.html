Oblien ua l'lúva - Roleplaying Guild


http://s7.invisionfree.com/Oblien/index.php?act=idx

Name:

Common: Adherants of the Moon
Taidune: Oblien ua l'Lúva

Requirements and General:

Races:  Night Elf primarly; exceptions can be made if Dwarf and Human priests are roleplayed as Elune followers.
Classes: Priest only; no exceptions (One Rogue, Warrior or Hunter is allowed in Stalker rank)
Roleplaying Skill:  We want to become a hardcore roleplaying guild, so we are seeking for exceptional roleplayers and higher.
Planned Member Cap:  We plan on having a member cap of about 20-30 serious members

Ranks:

(1) High Priest/Priestess - the highest rank, and the most respected.  A very influentual leader, who uses his or her wisdom to create the final decisions.  A High Priest can only be removed of his/her rank when he or she has refused to use the decision made by the majority members of the High Order during the final meeting, or if he or she resigns.                       
(6) Disciple/s - Six well respected members who have been chosen by the High Priest/Priestess as advisors to the court.  Their votes are important to the High Priest/Priestess when he makes the final decision.
(1) Stalker - A spy, tracker, assasin and huntress, she uses her arts in trickery and deciet to aid in more cloaked missions.  She is a natural spy, a talented tracker, and an assasin who praises Elune.  A honourable agent of High Priest/Priestess him or herself, the Stalker only takes orders given to her by the High Priest.  (Rogue, Warrior or Hunter only)
(X) Partisan/s - Loyal captains of the cause, they are officers who keep the lower ranked adherants in control during battles, raids and other events.  Typically military commanders, who are Acolytes with the power of partial control.  There must be a 3:1 ratio of Acolytes to Patrisans.
(X) Acolyte/s - Loyal priests and priestess of the moon goddess, these are fool blooded members of the guild.  Although they are full members of the guild, they cannot create decisions, yet can give opinions to the Disciples to give them their ideas on how the guild should be held.
(X) Novice/s - They are apprentices to the High Order; although not true members as of yet, they have an one week to a full month trial before they can be promoted to Acolyte.  In this time they cannot have a tabard, and cannot make consider themselves in the guild in regular chat.  They must be assigned to a sponsor who they must refer to as "Master".  These "Masters" are in full charge of the Novices.

Process of Decisions:

Disciples will often consult Acolytes about their opinions and meet.  The High Priest cannot listen into these discussions for they are for private only.  These meetings should happen once every week or even more if necessary.  When the Disciples believe that they have come to an issue that requires the immediate eyes of the High Priest, then they can arrange a meeting where all the Disciples and the High Priest will meet.  The High Priest cannot speak his opinions in this meeting, for the Disciples reveal their issue and then the meeting is ended.  The High Priest then must retire into thought and look over the issue and the opinions of the Disciples.  A second meeting is called and the Disciples and the High Priest meet once again.  The High Priest gives his opinions and the meeting is over until the third meeting where the High Priest and Disciples debate.  In the end the High Priest makes his decisions and must follow what the majority of the six Disciples have come up.  If not, he will have the right to be removed from the rank of High Priest and replaced with another.  If it is a minor decision the second and the third meetings will be mixed together.  When the High Priest has been replaced he/she will become a Partisan.

Guild Aims:

The Adherants of the Moon desire to stretch the aid of Elune across Azeroth to help against the war amongst Alliance.  With the Horde, Burning Legion remnants, and Scourge as core enemies, the Adherants hope to fight back them if necessary but rather use passive healing, support and patronage to aid all allies.  As Elune would rather, the Adherants are kind to all races, from High Elves to Taurens.  We only fight the Horde because they are allied with the Forsaken, whom we believe have tricked the Orcs, Tauren and Trolls.  We will not seek out to fight Horde members, unless they are indeed Forsaken.  We hope to fight the Horde only to save them from the mistake of affiliating with damnations.  We are to be honourable even if the enemy wouldn't even think twice about sparing our lives, for Elune would desire it as so.  Exceptions to these rules are demons and undeath.  The Priests and Priestesses of Elune hope to achieve exactly what the Moon Goddess would rather, attempting to peace keep and purge the world from demonic and dangerous substances upon the world.  Although they lack in fire power of other guilds, the Adherants hope to team up with allies in many cases to help fight back the evil and spread their faith.  By grouping with affiliated orders, the Oblien ua l'lúva hope to accomplish great achievements.  Oblien ua l'lúva plans on reaching no higher than a member cap of twenty to thirty serious loyalists.

Applying:

When applying to the Adherants of the Moon, you must fill out an application sheet right away.  This must be filled out with great detail to help impress the Disciples that you are indeed worthy of joining the guild.  Much description, feeling, and originality is always looked happily upon, and is requested to make the decisions of the High Order that much easier.  An application must be personal messaged, emailed to oblienualluva@hotmail.com or sent via AIM File Tranfer to the High Priest/Priestess who will then pass the application sheet down to the Disciples to overlook.  The application looks like the following...

Roleplaying Information
Character Name)
Age)
Race)
Class)
Appearance)
Equipment)
Personality)
Flaws and Positives)

Personal Information
Age)
Email)
AIM)

When filling out this application please remember that it is a Night Elf only guild (exceptions can be made for human and dwarf priests) for roleplaying reasons, and you must be of the Priest class.  All the information must be filled out, and please leave out any other information to help keep the application easy to read.  Other information can be given out later.  After sending this application in, and the Disciples have deemed you worthy of the Novice name, then you will be assigned to an Acolyte who you must take personal orders from at all times.  They are your unofficial leader, and in roleplaying conditions you must refer to them as "Master".  If the Acolyte finds that you are stepping out of line they will contact you and immediatly will tell you how you can fix this.  The Acolyte will monitor your steps and movements and will report to the Disciples with a personal report over your accomplishments.  This report will be put into consideration when the next decision will be made.  When this has been made you will have to attend a personal quest; this quest is where you will show your final roleplaying abilities as a Novice.  Then the final decision comes to pass where the Disciples decide.  The High Priest/Priestess will then start the ritual will promote your rank to Acolyte, a true member of the guild.  At this stage you will be allowed to purchase or be given a tabard which you may wear.

Alliances:

Oblien ua l'lúva thrives on alliances and strong relationships with other orders and tribes.  Without affiliating with others the Adherants would fail in their cause due to lack of fire power that can be supplied.  The Adherants of the Moon are support fighters using their divine arts given to them by Elune and Malorne to help aid.  Alliances are always required for the Oblien ua l'lúva, and you will often find either Disciples or Partisans approaching others for core alliances.  Below lists our current alliances that our guild holds.

Current Alliances:
Dae ua l'Undomai

Roleplaying Guild History:

Once an old secretive order during the first invasion of the Burning Legion, the Oblien ua l'Lúva used the flames of Elune to help fight back the oncoming swarms of endless demon waves. Using their priestly magic’s of divine arts, the Oblien ua l'Lúva sent their finest agents into battle to aid with Kaldorei at the front with their supporting spells of healing and enchantments. Stars fell from the sky at the call and the waving gesture of one priest, and with the combined powers of all of the adherents, a reckless storm of flaming stars erupted, plunging their heated forms to help dissipate the hungry minions of the invading Burning Legion. The Oblien ua l'Lúva were fearless as they rode into battles, mounted upon tamed frostsabers, combining their powers to help fend off the oncoming hordes summoned by the Quel'dorei.

Azshara was foolish to ignore their might, whether their allies and enemies recognized their order's exhistance or not. Secretive to even their closest of allies, the order kept their members shrouded from the knowledge of their friends and foes to help keep them safe. Without their order existing to the public they wouldn't be considered an organized threat, and this secret had to be kept from their allies to help keep gossip from slipping through their ranks. A few of the tribes that did take part in the Oblien ua l'Lúva were the Starstriders, Blackspears, Silverleaves, and Moonwalkers. Using an elven language unknown to many, the order gave their orders and released their fury given to them by Elune's teachings to help vanquish Azshara and the rest of the demon spawn.

Lead by the fearless Ariessa Starstrider, the order aided Malfurion with the final plan, where the Well of Eternity was destroyed, Azshara's plans were halted, and where the world which the Kaldorei knew so well would be ripped from beneath them, reforming its contents to a new shell. The sudden explosion scattered the Oblien ua l'Lúva, yet when they all settled in Kalimdor, they were re-founded. Yet the desire to rebuild their nation aided in the disbanding of the order, and soon the Oblien ua l'Lúva remained only as a secretive fairy tale that flowed within the back of their minds. The Starstriders, Moonwalkers and the other tribes that made up the Oblien ua l'Lúva made one last order before their disbandment, making a pact to never reveal their order's existence in fear that the remaining Quel'dorei would desire to take vengeance for their aid during the War of Ancients.

All the tribes lived on, aiding the re-creation of the Kaldorei Empire, more feral, primitive, and connected with nature than their last. All the Oblien ua l'Lúva former tribes settled in Nighthaven, in the secluded land of Moonglade, where they kept in close contact, keeping their past a secret which they would not allow themselves to dwell upon. Yet it was difficult, and the tribes created a lullaby which would help remind them of their heroic acts. Many outsiders of their blood-line thought it as only a fairy-tale, yet the former Oblien ua l'Lúva tribes knew the true meaning, at least only the elderly ones.


Abusing the arts of the well,
the others played in the night,
as the servants of the moon,
kept away from the spells in fright,
for when the arts had back lashed,
an order of secrets arose,
one of power, and dignity,
who marched for Elune.



Although the lullaby spoke of truth to some, a lot of the verses seemed like false tales made from crazed scholars. The former Oblien ua l'Lúva kept this poem within their hearts, as they recited its magic to their children at their birth night, hoping that this song would stick with them forever and a part of their once mighty order would live on.

The lullaby passed on, as the males of the former order became druids and moved on to the Emerald Realm to live their lives in dreams in return for their gift of immortality. The women would only live on in the real realm of Azeroth, and instead of reforming the order with what few remained, they decided to move on and continue their studies in the faith of Elune. Their paths were separate, fighting for the Sentinels, and becoming high in rank in the temples that worshipped Elune. Their faith and power aided in their climbing in the ladders of ranks in the society of the Kaldorei, yet their past slowly slipped from existence to them, only that one poem lived on.

One priestess who once was the sister of the former leader of the Oblien ua l'Lúva, gave birth to a new born child nine months after the druids left for the 'green dream', one which she named Callehen. A son, a rarity to see in the new society of the Night Elves. Yet Callehen's mother died soon after her process of giving birth, and the child was kept safe by the temple which his mother belonged to. His part in the world was small, serving only as an eye catcher for his fellow scholars in the Elune faith, yet as his life began to grow in age, and his maturity began to increase, Callehen became one of the most respected priests of the Temple of Elune in Nighthaven. Upon his five hundred and seventy first birthday, Callehen was approached by his master, Ariessa, and most of his blood-line's past was revealed to him in their first sitting on the subject.

The lullaby was song to him, and some hints about the ancient order also was given, yet it was up to Callehen to regain inner strength after the ill news of his mother and then discover what all these hints and the lullaby meant. It took some years for Callehen to feel comfortable once again, and soon afterwards he puzzled himself on the next words that were revealed to him. Taking the lullaby and his notes on Ariessa's hints, Callehen left Nighthaven and he began to research ancient artifacts, and libraries in other cities, hoping to find information to solve this riddle. His journey only lasted two months before he returned with an answer. He had found a small sentence in a forgotten journal from a long dead Kaldorei captain during the War of Ancients, who questioned whether there was an affiliation between a group of priestesses and priests in the Kaldorei army.

With this new found information Callehen approached Ariessa, and she began to fill him with more and more knowledge of this order. She revealed that she was his mother's sister, that she was once the leader of this tribe, and that she required the order to rejoin each other at arms. Callehen rushed to the idea of leaving and finding the scattered remaining priestesses who once belonged to this order, but Ariessa calmed his excitement. He was to continue his studies, and discover one last riddle.


Black as coal, a soul of death.  Walking between the line between insanity and wise he strides.



The riddle was so simple, yet so broad. So many different creatures could be this man, so many fall under this type, yet to find the exact one would be difficult. As he continued his studies in the faith of Elune, he kept on his research with his side quest, hoping that he would solve it soon so he could re-unite the fallen Oblien ua l'Lúva. Finally looking back into the journal which he had found earlier, he discovered a paragraph which had told about a former veteran war general of the Quel'dorei who had been twisted into a creature of devil-spawn during the explosion of the Well of Eternity.

 
A darkness seemed to sweep over my old friend when the news reached my ears of the Quel'dorei's betrayal and trickery, and when I sided with the Kaldorei armies he finally went insane.  His berserk rage only caused him to die in heart, and be reborn as a mad man who walked the line between insanity and wise.  Although his cunning wisdom aided him in many ways with his foul objectives, he still seemed to play a roll in the insane part of his mind, seeming to become more and more madden with lust for power.  He indeed had become a monster since when I once knew him, but now as I march my party towards him to end his devilish schemes, I hope that he will look into my eyes and leave his insanity behind so I can bring him back to Elune.

~Devarius



Callehen brought his discovery to his master right away and she gave him a smile of approval. She told him that he had taken the last step and he was indeed wise enough to re-unite the secretive order. Sending word to the scattered former members, Callehen met them all upon a wildly grown grove upon the top of plateau in the mountains to the north of the Moonglade. With the nod from Ariessa, Callehen re-united the order under his control, with Ariessa aiding as one of the Disciples of the High Order. The next mission was to march into the tunnels below to defeat the dark satyr war-mage, Katrys.

The newly created Oblien ua l'Lúva marched into the tunnels below and fought a secretive battle for centuries. The battle seemed like it would never end, as the thousand year anniversary of this war would soon arrive. Callehen was growing irritated by Katrys' cunning blows. Indeed the satyrs held much more power in numbers, yet Callehen needed to find a tactic that would work successfully. Dwelling in the hidden texts of the Oblien ua l'Lúva, Callehen discovered a tactic used by his mother in the ages of the Ancients. The core healer would be shielded by a five man "u" in front of him, and as one of his soldiers fell he would resurrect them with the arts of Elune, giving life to them once again. Using this tactic, the Oblien ua l'Lúva invaded Katrys' throne room and he was slain, as the near invincible priests ransacked and pillaged the devilish spawn of the Burning Legion.

After the battle beneath the mountains, Callehen and the rest of the order decided to create a secretive home upon the plateau where they met. They named the place, Lúva ua l'Dae, which is Moon of the Shadow in common. Here the secretive order kept silent in their studies and prayers to Elune, as they kept away from the city to the south until the Burning Legion returned for their third invasion. This time the Oblien ua l'Lúva joined up again with the Kaldorei empire, and with the re-awakened druids, the Oblien ua l'Lúva knew they would have a better chance against the foe, yet the hopes of victory was slim as the age of doom approached more steadily than the ages in the past. The Burning Legion was indeed an old foe to the Oblien ua l'Lúva, yet as they stared upon their monstrosity, they felt a smudge of fear. This would be their final battle against this exact army, yet the empire behind their operations was still a threat, no matter if they defeated the foes or not. With the faith of Elune once again, the Oblien ua l'Lúva aided in the destruction of Archimonde, and the victory against the darkest army they had yet faced, yet the order paid a price as well.

With all of their members killed except for Callehen, and his dying aunt, the order seemed to be on the verge of extinction, yet by abiding the hopes expressed by Callehen's aunt when her death consumed her, Callehen took heavy control of the order, in hope that he would succeed in recruitment. Knowing the weakness of the order in recruiting proper priests, Callehen allowed Oblien ua l'Lúva to become more known to the public, hoping that priests would flock or become more visible to his eyes. Yet recruitment would not be his only goal to keep the ancient order from plunging; alliances to help give him more fire power, trade and protection could help the Oblien ua l'Lúva to remain alive. Callehen retreated to the Lúva ua l'Dae, and began on his new mission of recruitment and diplomatic dealings with other orders. Yet as he occupied himself with recruitment, his true goal behind his order would be to spread the faith of Elune, protect those that were in need, and fight all remaining undead and demonic influences.
