Warlocks on the Horde actually do fairly well since they don't have to worry about the Forsaken rogues who deliberately target rogues ("ganking" to use the colloquialism). Some strategies for warlocks involve repeated use of single target fear to isolate specific members of a group for surgical strikes then use overwhelming force on said individual (priorty ranges from Priests, Druids, Warlocks and Mages; the armoured classes are best kept for last). Dwarves could prove to be a problem since they could neutralize your corruption.

For large scale player vs. player raids, I can only speculate. I would be worried about teams of alliance rogues taking out cloth classes, in which case I can only say, be alert, strafe and come prepared with potions or if possible have some engineers booby-trap any access points in hopes of catching them off guard. I would keep one or two druids grouped with warlock squads for Faerie Fire and Snare/Roots.

For anyone to be successful in PvP, good communication is essential. Protect those wearing cloth; use support in lieu of damage as a few seconds is always vital, and it is good to assume your opponent has fire resist potions and anti-demon potions so don't rely on the typical warlock strategies. If you can use the terrain to your advantage, even better (fear off of cliff or drowning is quite satisfying, the latter is quite effective since feared players go straight to the bottom).

As a high lvl lck in CB I can saw if you learn to play locks well they can be the most powerful in pvp. Dont base your PvP opinion of them until lvl 30 because thats when the options for them really open up (26+)

I can take down anyone up to 6 lvls ahead of me except for hunters (2 lvls) shamen (1 lvl) ahead of me, and rogues Equal if we see each other at range, im down if he gets the first blow (equal lvl)

Use mana drain on palys and priests, priests and palys dont realize the mana is gone until they try to heal at last second. and then they are very weak. I lose to fight these guys

Dont bother with mana steal on mage, they have too much, dot him, fear him and get close and wack him. fight can go either way. Mages will love to poly you and then kill the demon while its alone, So i use the imp for these guys, keep it passive until after it polys me, so its in phase shift and cannot be attacked, and then turn it on for the ranged nuke when I break the poly.

Hunters are problems because of the pets disrupting your casting. Dot the pet dot the hunter and get close to get, cast within the 10 yard minimum bow range of his. these guys are iffy

Rogues freak me out, use the rain of fire where you think they are when stealthed. sick pet when seen, dot and run in circles so they cant hit you. Wait for dots to kill them, Dont expose your back to them, these guys are hard to survive.

Shamen oh Shamen- dont try mana drain, and dont get in their melee range, dot them and nuke, but you cant run around in circles while they tick because they have a ranged stun. If they use a totem just hit it once. Drain life. hard to beat

Warriors Dot and whenever they hit you run though them, turn about and try to drain life what you just lost.

Druids not so much experience with these, but they are good in the right hands, a bad player with a druid will suck horribily (like warlock) but a good player will be amazing (like warlock)
both of these classes reciure knowing what you are doing. Or you stink.
They will go bear to deal with a caster (good idea) or stay as they are to deal with a melee as in their caster form (good idea)

A druid wont out melee a warrior or out cast a mage but they can switch to fit the situation making them stronger than both, if you have the know how. (hard to beat or easy, depends on player)


Demon tips, if you have the void out use his sacrafice if you are about to die or getting rogue backstabs up the wazoo.

Use the imps phase shift to keep him safe when needed

* IMPORTANT Use the succubus's invisibily to sneak up and get the first blow, or turn her on passive and then press charm so she keeps that player out of the fight, the player cannot be damaged and it might break, but it gives you time to heral with food or first aid or forulate a plan... or run away.

Use Felhunters awesome all around abilities to accent any fighting style you may have, plus sniff out the warlocks bane rogues.


All of the above wins will require a pet stuck on them while they run around and possible use of fear to keep them out of melee distance.

Use Mana Drian on priests, its the obly real way to win, every Warlock can out dmg a priest, but if they can heal themselves you slowly lose, so take away their mana and they are just kids with sticks.

A mages poly is always a problem, but as soon as he hits you you beak out, try to stay out of the Aoe effect ice, if he frezzes you get a wand bolt off, redo Dots drain life, Drain life on the mage and you will be able to heal yourself and hurt him. The wind thing they do is annoying, but you can tell when a mage wants to use an AoE on you. Most of the time a mage will stay max distance he can, a problem since he has a bit more range, but when they want an AoE they run in, so stay right and the AoE max distance, then run backwards, or attack him with melee to stun his casting.

Always have a demon on the mage the hits of the succubus and void will stun his spells making him take longer, plus the dmg. If he polys you use the Succubus's charm to keep him octupiled (wont work if you have a dot on) she will die if he gets her one on one. The void might outlast him, but his dmg is so much weaker than the Succubus that I dont use him much in pvp anymore, Sacrifice has proven everusful, but the Succubus has more redemming quailities.

If a rogue pops up behind you, attack with the succ, try to charm with her, sac the void use a healthstone and a pot, and run. If the Rogue just pops up behind you and gets the combo its a win for you if you get away alive.

How to deal with a rogue that just went stealth? Use an Aoe, put the pets on aggressive, and dont just stand there run around, in stealth he moves at 50% speed, so just move faster than him.

Use curse of agony and corruption on the rogue, then fear, do not let him touch you or he will win, while hes feared use wand blasts or recast immolation or life drain. when he breaks free fear again. They have high dps , but low health. If we just run around while the dots tick they will die, but its real iffy. Rogues are the only class that truely frighten me as pvpers, gankers, and duelers. They are the warlocks bane.


Yes we can beat mages. In fact I like fighting mages because I womp them so easily ) dmg to me at end of battle demon 1/2 health or dead, and mage dead or defeated yelling WTF.

Start the fight off, sick pet, amplify curse, curse of agony, corruption, possible immolation, then drain life as he hits you, around this time he might poly you and eat/drink, use the suc to charm or phase the imp.

A lock with know how can beat a mage easy, but a mage with know how will try to get the demon- and you cant do it without the demon.

The only trouble you might have is against chain polyiers (7 times in a row) Then its hard to keep the demon alive and you will fall.

I like Palys and Priests the most, I dot (fear if paly) mana drain till the manas gone then start fighting from there. If you are hurting fear and life drain, make sure to see the mana down to 0 on them or they will heal. Dot as needed.
