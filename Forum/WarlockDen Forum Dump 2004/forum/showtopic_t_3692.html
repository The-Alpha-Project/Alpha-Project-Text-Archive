Drinking and Eating in combat is being all but eliminated. You will still be able to eat and drink outside combat to reduce downtime. Note that "in combat" means that a monster nearby is engaged with you, so once you have started a fight with a monster, no more eating/drinking until the monster is dead or is disengaged. This will, of course, make dungeons much more challenging than they currently are.

Very interesting, luckily Warlocks don't rely so heavily on food and drink as much as other classes because of our tapping ability, and in a way this may make Mages not so harrassed / needed as players won't churn through it as much, thus not needing it summoned as much.

*edit* Add On :

Warlocks will be much better under this change as some people have already figured out, since they are less reliant than other casters on their mana pools. We will also be working on their level 30+ pets very soon and evaluating all his spells. The Warlock will be a high DPS class (sustained DPS, not burst) with powerful debuffs.

-Eno

Here's some more information on the topic... seems it's got a lot of tongues wagging...

I'll go ahead and confirm what I already said in the other thread.

No more drinking and eating in combat. When you do an action that causes you to be engaged with a monster (attack it, heal someone attacking it, debuff it, etc) then you will be "flagged" as in-combat. When in-combat, you will not be allowed to drink/eat, you will get an error message when you try.

Why did we do this?
As many people have pointed out, eating and drinking were ALWAYS intended as a way to reduce downtime, not a way to introduce a meditation mechanic into the game.

What about encounters that are too powerful?
Any encounters that are too powerful with this change will, of course, be changed, but this will probably happen slowly since I am willing to bet that players being the resourceful people they are, will still find plenty of ways to defeat the encounters in the game.

As a sidenote - we have been doing all internal play testing on the new dungeons with the new functionality and with tougher elite monsters, so I don't anticipate too many problems.

What are some of the side effects of this change?
- Potions will become more valuable, since they can still be used while in combat.
- Wands should become much more useful since they do not require mana to use.

Lastly, this is not a change that will go away based on beta tester complaints. Drinking and Eating in combat creates enormous balance problems now and in the future. Once it is in the beta test, we will resolve any other problems individually, NOT undo the change. This change is not being put in the make the game "less fun", but to enable to us to properly balance the game. Here are some areas we will keep an eye on:
- are there any encounters that really are too difficult on one mana bar?
- are mana pools large enough?
- is Spirit effective enough?
- the balance between melee and caster classes will need to be evaluated
- etc, etc

-Eno
