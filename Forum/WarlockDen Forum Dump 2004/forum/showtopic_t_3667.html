Been playing for awhile now, here's what I've come up with regarding classes and PvP. Please note that this is NOT directed towards Player-Computer battles. I'll start with everyone's favorite (or most hated) class.

MAGES: Currently the strongest class in the game, for a few reasons. First and foremost, invisibility. There are so many flaws with this move as it stands right now. I understand the necessity of invisibility, to help mages get around those tight spots, since their armor can't take the beating. However, I don't believe it is being used properly. This move has a very low cast time, and since it does not have a long cooldown as many have suggested, it can be used constantly. The mana cost also barely scratches a mages' mana pool. Some solutions I propose are a longer cooldown, increased casting time, and//or a higher mana cost, obviously, to fit all three things above. I highly doubt all three will be necessary.

Secondly, mages are a bit too strong at close quarters. Well, perhaps that's not phrasing it right... Close quarters against mages cannot happen. Frost nova locks you in place usually for a good 6-8 seconds. Chilled will slow me the moment I hit a mage, basically providing a hamstring effect without any in-battle cost whatsoever. Not only does this seem unfair, it seems far too crippling.

The third reason that mages seem to be so strong right now is, quite simply, because they have talents when many other classes don't. Thus, it is hard to balance these, so I won't even try here.

Well, pyroblast criticaling people for 1500 damage is a problem, because it's not much of a critical hit with the amount that it happens. There is also a bug that occasionally lets prybolast casting time be masked by invisibility.

Also, talents allowing increased ranged casting are causing problems for melee classes when fighting ice mages. Today I had a mage who I could not even hit once. I was frostbolted from an insane range, and slowed to an embarrassing speed. It only got worse when random frost novas started appearing on me when frostbolts hit. Not sure if this is a talent or not. Regardless, the point is that mages have it a bit too strong in their ranged capacities. A melee class should be able to get a decent chance to hit them at least once.

I'll talk about polymorph later.

WARRIORS: This class is relatively balanced, needs more tweaking than anything else. Again, it is hard to balance talents, so I'd rather not delve into that, except to say that Mortal Strike is obviously overpowered and needs a cooldown. Blizzard has already identified this as an issue, however, so no real reason to stress it.

As for the differing types of warriors, and roles in parties, aside from Mortal Strike, things seem to be fine. However, the idea of using two-handed swords isn't working out too great. Once Mortal Strike is changed, I don't see two-handed warriors being all that useful, because they won't be able to tank as well as defensive warriors, but won't have a very high damage output, either. This is probably the primary area where abilities might have to be tweaked. Abilities//Talents designed specifically to benefit two-handed warriors (A Dramatic Increase In Parry Rate, Something of That Nature) might be needed.

WARLOCKS: Warlocks are hurting pretty badly right now in PvP. the uselessness of summons in such combat is blatantly obvious, taking away all of a warlock's strength. If it weren't for the move fear, which might need to be tweaked anyways, should summons become more useful, warlocks would have no use in PvP. They just don't have much damage output or staying power.

ROGUES: It is a bit hard to test rogues in PvP right now due to the way stealth is malfunctioning, since many rogue abilities rely on stealth. Even so, some obvious issues must be addressed. Namely, mages are doing far more damage than rogues, in a shorter period of time, and mages don't even have to be close to their target. With things such as garrote and cheap shot missing now, the class is only growing weaker and less fun to play. My rogue personally cannot stand up to any class but warlocks for the most part.

Another big issue with rogues right now is the way stats are working. I am so torn as to what kind of gear to get anymore. I have tried setups of agility, strength, and stamina, and in PvP, stamina seems to be working the best. This concerns me, because rogues could lose their identity and become watered-down warriors. Once Blizzard does figure out what stat is supposed to be primary, many items will have to be reconfigured, mainly quest items. Also, not enough quest rewards are daggers.
Poisons are incredibly expensive, as well. To put deadly poison III on both my blades constantly, it will cost me 22s every thirty minutes. Poisons are the only way a rogue can even remotely stand a chance in PvP, and this cost seems to be a bit much perhaps. Not sure how it will play out once talents are implemented, however.

PRIESTS: Priests are a tough one to analyze, because I am unaware of how Blizzard WANTS them to work in PvP. RIght now, they are very fierce opponents. In groups, they increase the strength of a group exponentially, but it is hard to see how this will play out. The reason I say this is because Holy Word: Shield seems to be the only priest move that might need tweaking. I am hoping talents will separate priests into different branches, such as attack priests and PvP group priests. Again, hard to comment when I'm in the dark on this. Other than Holy Word: Shield, I see no real problems.

DRUIDS: Druids are in need of desperate help, as far as I can see. In PvP, shapeshifting is nearly useless, due to the loss of use of druid spells, which seem to be all that holds a druid together. (plus the bug where you lose your buffs...) Without these spells, specifically healing, druids fall down fast in PvP, but even with them, because of the physical weakness of humanoid form, they still get torn up. Making druids able to wear mail at 40 might be an option. Talents focused towards allowing the use of certain spells when shapeshifted or making these forms stronger could change all of this.

PALADINS: No problems that I've seen with Paladins in PvP, except perhaps the DivineShield, Heal, Heal combo. Everything else seems balanced, though I'm sure some undead would disagree with me.

SHAMANS:I see this the same way as I see the paladin. An all around good strong class, with no overpowering, or underpowering abilities. Lightning shield MIGHT be a bit too strong, and their healing abilities might be a bit too superior to that of a druids, but even that is debatable.

Long read, but one more thing I'd like to discuss before I'm done.

In my opinion, these total disabling moves are really ruining PvP. I cannot name one person who ENJOYS fighting Polymorph, Fear, Intimidating Shout, Psychic Scream, Shackle Undead ...if I've missed any, let me know, I'm tired. These moves destroy alot of the fun of PvP by ruining tactics, because losing complete control of your character really changes how a battle was playing out and messes up any strategies that may have been implemented. I suggest removing these moves from PvP combat. I know, other balance changes will probably have to be made to compensate, but on a fun aspect, this isn't working out.

As for the PvP system itself, all I see right now are mages going around invisible ganking lower levels while doing questing. I trust that this isn't even close to Blizzard's final PvP server design, so I won't elaborate

Sorry for the long read. Feel free to post your own thoughts here.
