Thinking about talents and pets I came up with something a more generic idea that could be implemented alongside talents: The ability to cast a spell 'through' your pet, which would give it a boost.
Just as an example: Imp could boost immolate.

You would cast immolate without a pet up, and hit for 50-60 direct and 70 dot damage.
Now, summon an imp and he gives you a 10% boost to immolate damage, so the same immolate will hit for 55-66 direct and 77 dot damage for as long as the imp is around. At a higher level you would be able to teach your pets improved versions of the spell boosting.

As long as theyd each boost relevant and often used spells, it would be a cool feature and not result in everyone using the same pet all the time.
Expanding on that, there could even be pet specific spells, spells you can only use if you have that pet in action. You could for example gain access to an instant cast health funnel type spell if you have voidwalker up, or gain a lifedrain proc on your weapon with succubus up.

Good idea? bad? lets hear it.

here is a well thought out response

Blueberry (VW) could give a boost to Curse of Weakness (make the attack power boost it gives to the enemy smaller) and Recklessness, perhaps even allow you to cast both on the same target? As a different ability he might have a magic dampening effect, which would reduce the amount of 'threat' or aggro your spells cause.

Suc could increase your shadowbolt damage, or allow you to invis a limited period through use of her spell.

Theres endless possiblities for the abilities we could come up with, the question is if the idea as a whole is a good one and wheter Blizzard would be likely to implement something like this at all. Well, I'll keep on suggesting and hope some of it goes through, would be cool if it did.

topic posted on BNet by Darakym.

this would be a great thing to add in lue of pet talents. rather than figuring out a way to balance pet talents with our power, pets could simply gain the ability to amplify our own spells.
