The server is unavailable so I cannot comment on anything specifically. Though from what was quoted, such a system would be prone to corruption. Still, one would have to be quite naive or foolish to trust in the benevolence of a few immature players. The level requirements for PvP in other MMO games were quite easy to exploit. In Everquest, players would level up, then deliberately delevel so they would have an advantage over other lower level players within the permitted range. Then there was the problem with players playing on multiple accounts. The argument about "players learning skillz" or some such is bunk. In computer games where asymptotic limits are pre-established, those who are higher on the curve will always have the advantage.

Besides, Blizzard is probably wise enough from their experience with Diablo to know that anything of that nature would hurt their bottom line. If they were implement some sort of unique rule set, I propose they make their legal department create a judicial process by which players who break the rules are appropriately punished (i.e. Players who are psychopathic killers, have their characters executed. Game over. They will learn quickly, right?)

Failing that, just create a seperate PVP server, so the immature brats can go get their jollies and leave the mature players to themselves.
