This is a post for those similarly addicted souls.

My name is Jubal, and Im an AH-aholic. I hate to shop in RL and I do so as a typical guy...I know what I want when I enter a store. I find it, buy it, and leave.

However in MMORPG...Im a market freak. I dont know what it is but the ability to play the market, is an irresistable draw for me.

Helpful hints:

1. Dont overprice your inventory! The intent is to make money...volume over individual price inflation. Browse a little bit and see what similar items go for, then price accordingly.

2. Once you get a feel for the AH prices, buy low when you see them, and sell a bit higher for the profit. This especially goes for TS supplies. If you see someone setting a "must-sell" price for items such as cloth, hide, ore...buy it and set it at a more equitable market value. They get their cash, you stabalize a market while profiting and you increase your inventory so another crafter can buy from you.

3. Dont use the 24 hour option except for rarer items. You're basically over-paying for storage. Use the 8hr, unless for Blue+ items. Try to time your auction period for the 5pm - Midnight rush of folks. In general this will reap the most long term benefits.

4. Set a Buyout! (Except Recipes/Blue or better items) Most folks want to Tradeskill now, not later. For TS items set a reasonable buyout and rake in your cash. For Blue + items and patterns/schematics/recipes/plans/etc, I reccomend setting a reasonble start price and let it ride. Highest bidder wins. You'd be surpprised what you can rake in here.

5. For TS products, set a fair price. Establish a buyout. If you do these things regularly, the crafters on your server may start /w you personally when youre on, to set up some personal service. I have done this on several occasions. Works out well for both parties.

Remember when playing merchant...yes, you are trying to turn a profit. But, a fair reputation will go a long way to making this a reality than the temporary benefit of "gouging". Repeat customers are a good thing.

My $.02, but with inflation probably worth much less.
