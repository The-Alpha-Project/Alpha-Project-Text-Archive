Well, it's usually done in components. Like the graphics engine obviously handles the rendering, and your other components handle things like physics, the "main" or game itself, the AI, etc...

The "main" thread is what tells everything else what to do. It's kinda the traffic cop. Then all the other parts do their respective jobs to create the whole game.

It's a very simplified explaination, but I think it gets the point across.

as for the Graphics engine think of it like this.

the Graphics engine is responsible for things like loading and maintaining the materials(textures, Piexl/vertex shaders, render/testure states) , Models (Vertex buffers, index buffers, possibly more vertex shaders) and then figuring out which of those "objects" are potentionally in the view frustrum of the virtual camera and making sure they draw to the screen. if you are interested in engine theory, Eberly has writen a pretty good book on the topic, while Heavy in math it goes through most elements of a game engine. here is a like to it on Amazon...
