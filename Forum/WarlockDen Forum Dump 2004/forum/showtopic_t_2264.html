by OneMadOgre

It was a bright day in Goldshire when I found the diary of Brann. It was the tale of a brave dwarf who had adventured to the wilds of Stranglethorn jungle and was never heard from again. How a Bronzebeard, one of the royal family of dwarves could go missing and no one would seek him out was beyond my comprehension. So I decided to set out at once to journey to the Stranglethorn jungle and discover what had become of this long lost dwarf.

Luckily Brann had left a detailed description of his journey so I set off across the great swinging bridges and past the great jade idols of the Stranglethorn jungle. I was progressing steadily down the path until I was set upon by two huge beasts of the jungle. I’m not sure what happened exactly after this. When I came to, there was a nice gnome fellow standing by a huge glowing rock who I can only assume had dragged me to safety. While he wasn’t much of a talker, this gnome, I could only assume that he would surely help out a dwarf as freely as a human given the recent events and the generosity of the dwarvish nation in sharing their home with the poor gnomish people.

I briefly took a look around the town of Booty Bay. I could only assume that a great explorer like Brann Bronzebread would jump at a chance to take a ship across the Great Sea to the far off continent of Kalimdor. I had heard tales of the continent told by the veterans of the last great war. With the wanderlust that seems to affect all dwarves in this day, I knew that Brann would have set sail at the first opportunity. A goblin Captain Morgan seemed eager to help me cross the ocean. After a swig of his remarkable flask, I awoke the town of Ratchet.

Given the reputation of the mightly town of Ratchet, I was surprised to see it so rundown. Another goblin fellow asked me to help him with recovering a device to the north, so I set out immediately, the gizmo he wanted retrieved almost as important to me as getting away from a Ogre who seemed to eye me hungrily.Everyone Loves Ogres!! People say that Ogres can exist in society with some of the more barbarous races, but I choose not to take my chances.

The Barrens were aptly named. The landscape was somewhat familiar to my own homelands of Westfall, but the stalking raptor beasts where something I had not beheld before. They were incredibly fast, but fortunately, my fire skills were up to the task of dispatching them, and I found that their skin made for a good strong leather.

As I journeyed north, I came across a fearful sight. A troll of considerable power stood in my way to my objective north. Brann had mentioned such creatures in his journal, so it seemed likely that these foul beasts may have taken my mentor prisoner. This troll was well beyond my ability to fight my way past, and trolls are not know for their social skills. Not willing to give up on the brave Brann, I took a deep breath and leapt from the cliff wall to a ledge below, hoping and praying that I would be able to survive the fall.

On the valley floor, I came across a severely wounded troll.
Not Much of a Threat. Taking this opportunity, I pressed him for information which he was hard pressed to give. He spoke in some foul language, but I was able to divine that the trolls had taken my journaling friend Brann. I would have to seek out the mighty warchief Thrall if I was to set this to right. Fighting the entire troll nation was certainly something that I would not be able to accomplish.

I thought that it was a stroke of luck then to find a Orc guard not far on this valley floor. I tried to explain my business here, but he was not interested in any business other than attempting to take my head from my shoulders with an axe. It was only with the slimmest of margins that I emerged the victor of that fight. I had not fought an orc before, but the tales of their speed and skill in battle seemed greatly underestimated. This fellow was quick and vicious. I decided it would behoove me not to attempt to talk to any more of these orc guards, they obviously could not see past their primitive bloodlust.

While I ventured north avoiding the raptors, crocs, and certainly the orc guards, I came across a welcome sight! A human fort out in the middle of the Barrens? How could I be so lucky. Amazingly, their commander would not accept my audience. While they would not attack me, they would not speak to me either. They seemed set on the ways of the poor admiral who had lead them here. I took leave of their company after leaving them some food and continued north.

I had vowed to try to avoid the orc presence as I headed north, but I came upon a small cave where cultists were performing dark magic rituals. I knew this was dangerous magic and as one of the wizard order, I had sworn an oath to contain these sorts of cultists where I could. Two orc warlocks fell to my fire and frost, and I continued on to the gates of Ogrimmar to see the mighty war chief Thrall.

Nervously, I approached the forbidding walls of Ogrimmar. For the warlike demenear that is hinted at in the legends of my people, the Orcs outside of the gates seemed distant and perhaps a little hostile, but certainly did not attack. But nor would they speak on my behalf to the two Orc grunts that guarded the gate.

So I took a deep breath and approached the grunts who keep a silent watch on the road leading into Ogrimmar. Although I approached them with open arms, they seemed less than eager to receive me and my pleas to meet with the mighty Thrall fell on deaf ears. Prehaps I would have to develop a relationship with some Orc in Ratchet in order to approach Ogrimmar again to plea for the release of my friend. I have dedicated myself to that cause.
