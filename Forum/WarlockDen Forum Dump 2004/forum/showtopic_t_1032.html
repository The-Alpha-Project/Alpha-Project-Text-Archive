This is the background story for my character. It's very long and purly descriptive, as I can't write vived stories like Kalazor or Akasha. So be warned if you want to read it. I'm sorry for crappy grammar, misspelling and such.

Description

Name: Celadyr Felfire

Age: 112

Race: Blood Elf (human in game)

Height: 6’2’’

Weight: about 150 lbs.

Hair color: Blond

Eye color: Blue

Class: Warlock


Backround

Celadyr Ar’than was born as the third of three children into a family of mages. Having shown a talent for the arcane in his youth, it was only natural that he followed his older brother and sister into the magic academy in Dalaran to study under the high elven archmage Keryth Dyr’thronden.

Celadyr was gifted with a high inborn talent for arcane magic, but wasn’t a dedicated student, seeing his study more of a prolong of his childhood, which made him being criticised regularly by his master and his more dedicated kin. Though Celadyr showed some interest in making potions and magical theory, having fun with his friends was at least as important to him. In Dalaran he spent most of the time with high elven friends who shared his “fun-approach” to studying, but had also a very good human friend, Barin Duringster.

Spending most of the time with other high elves in a human kingdom, Celadyr also came in contact with Kael’thas Sunstrider – son of the late first high elf king of Quel’thalas, Darth’ Remar Sunstrider – a member of the Kirin Tor and later the leader of the blood elves. Of course there was no close contact, due to the differences in age and rank (and dedication…).

Celadyr spend most of his holidays in Silvermoon, roaming the surrounding sees and forests there with his friends. He had one close high lf friend named Tallador Dragontooth, a Ranger who just lived outside Silvermoon. They had become friends because the sister of Celadyr's mother had married a brother of Tallador's father and they therefore spend a lot of time togeather when they were young. Because Celadyr went to Dalaran to study the friendship became a bit less close, but whenever he was in Silvermoon, he made sure to meet his friend Talladar. After the destruction of Quel'Thalas, Celadyr has never heard anything from Talladar, and believes him dead.

During one of these holidays in Silvermoon Celadyr's life should change: The magical wards of Quel’Thalas, believed to be impossible to breach, fell before the might of the Scourge. In the following battle Celadyr lost almost everything he loved and held dear, his family and friends, and his home. All that remained of proud Silvermoon were scorched towers, burned trees and a corrupted Sunwell. Celadyr took part in the defensive of Quel’thalas, as did his whole family, and saw his brother and parents perish before his eyes. What happened to his sister he doesn’t know and doesn’t want to imagine. Celadyr would have fallen on that day along with his kin, if he wouldn’t have been saved by a mighty fire strike vaporizing the storming ghouls who tried to tear him apart. “You’re not to die on this day, we have to avenge our fallen kin”, that were the words of prince Kael who saved him on that day and on many occasions in the following years. Celadyr joined prince Kael and his followers, who renamed themselves to “blood elves”, to always remember their fallen kin. In the following months the blood elves waged a guerrilla war on the Scourge hoping to hold out until reinforcements from the Alliance arrive to help them. But alas, they waited in vain and saw more and more of their people to fall before the Scourge. After heavy battles with the undead and becoming more and more exhausted and desperate, because of the loss of the powers of the Sunwell, they finally managed to reach a group of human Alliance troops under the leadership of one Garithos. But that didn’t lead to their rescue, only to humiliation and imprisonment in the dungeons of former Dalaran. Thinking to die in his cell, Celadyr had lost all hope when finally help arrived in the form of Illidan and his naga. With their combined strength the blood elves could escape Dalaran, forge a new home in Outland and even a way to satisfy their magic addiction, by siphon on the arcane energies of demons. Celadyr will never forget the power throbbing through his veins, after laying his hands on his first killed demon, a mere Felguard. Forgotten were the pain and exhaust he endured for almost a year, even if only for a short period.

All the things that happened in the last few years; the deaths of friends and family, the destruction of his home, the feeling of betrayal by the Alliance, the many battles and exhaustion due to the lack of the Sunwell, all that changed Celadyr: gone is the fun loving student of magic, now there is a taciturn Blood Mage committed wholly to the revenge on the Scourge and the furthering of his own magical powers. A glimpse of his former self can be seen on the rare occasions when Celadyr is within a small group of friends, when he can relax away from battle.

His own magical studies and experiments centre on Felmagic. On the one hand he tries to find ways to convert fire based spells like Flame Strike or Fireball into Fel based ones. That would give the blood elf an advantage when fighting demons and of course other mages. On the other hand he tries to make the use of Fel spells and energies less corruptive and more save for the magic user, seeing his own physical change due to the heavy use of Felmagic (see below). To further his studies he has begun to learn Eredun (the language of demons). But the mission for prince Kael and the many battles with the Scourge don’t leave him much time for his studies and he is far from any breakthrough. On a more practical level he continues to brew magical potions and he always carries at least two or three of them with him.

Celadyr sees other races mostly from a typical blood elven point of view: He thinks the Alliance is at least partly responsible for the utter destruction of his home, and therefore generally doesn’t like humans and night elves. But due to his friendship with many humans during his days in Dalaran, he can judge them more by their actions than the usual blood elf. An exception are Paladins who he can’t stand. During his childhood, and while studying in Dalaran, he has heard only bad things about the night elves, and he things their hate and fear of arcane magic to be a great folly. Also he only once has had a real contact with night elves, and that was during the fight between Illidan and the Warden Maiev in Outland, which confirmed his negative views of them. He feels a close kinship with the high elves and would like to see the blood and high elves to be reunited, to fight the Scourge and to rebuild their civilisation. He isn’t interested in the other Alliances races, or the Horde or most of the neutral races. An exception are the naga; Celadyr is thankful for their help and would always try to help naga in need, but he can’t relax in their mid. Also he is usually friendly to goblins, because he depends on them for magical resources for his studies and experiments. He hates forest trolls, but above all he utterly hates and despises the Scourge and the Burning Legion, and fights them wherever and whenever he can.

His appearance is that of a typical Quel’dorei, although he’s a bit slim, even for a blood elf. He usually wears the typical clothes of a blood elf, donning mostly red and black. He doesn’t have tattoos or body paint and his hairstyle is more typical for a high elf, than for a blood elf. Since becoming a blood elf and using Felmagic his eyes started to glow in the eerie yellow-green colour of the Fel, whenever he casts strong magic. This glow gave him his nickname Felfire, the last part stemming from his love of fire based spells. This physical change does disturb Celadyr a bit, but not as much as to question the use of Felmagic as such, or the siphon of demon magic. Quite the opposite, he can’t see any means to build a new Sunwell at the moment, and sees the siphon of demon energies as the only way for the survival of the Quel’dorei at the moment. He’s also quite awed by the power, this gives to the blood elves and believes that the negative effects can be controlled. He is absolutely convinced that the blood elves won’t walk the same path the orcs did. But he is no “preacher type” who tries to sticks his views in the face of everyone he meets.

When on a mission in Azeroth, Celadyr almost always dons his black dragonhide armor, a gift from his late older brother, and a magical ring of protection. Another possession he always carries with him, is his back of holding, where he keeps his clothes and food, as well as his spellbook, brewing kit, potions, spell scrolls and other things he needs for travel.

His most priced possession (as in “my precious”) is his spellbook. This is covered in the hide of a Felbeast, he slew in battle that almost cost him his life. On the front is his personal mark, a yellow-green “C” in Thalassian on a stylized red flame. Below are written the words “beware fool” in the always writhing letters of Eredun, to give any wannabe thieves (and himself from time to time…) a realty check. He also has casted a powerful “wizard lock” on his spellbook. Inside most of the spells are written in Thalassian, but everything concerning Felmagic is written in Eredun.

Knowing of his vulnerability, Celadyr likes to plan fights in the advance, and tries to have a healing potion or scroll of teleport with him at all times. He fights almost exclusively with his spells, and therefore carries a Felmagic imbued dagger as his only weapon for melee, but he can’t use it very well. Rather relying on this, he likes to cast protective spells like demon armor and summon a demon for his defence. When fighting the pawns of the Scourge, he becomes reckless, casting away most of his sense of protection, wading into battle with offensive fire and Fel based spells, trying to kill as much of the hated creatures as he can.

At the moment Celadyr is with a small group blood elves in Azeroth on a mission for prince Kael, ordered to convince high elves to join them (not necessarily becoming blood elves thereby), search in “magical” lands like former Quel’Thalas or Ashenwood for magical sources strong enough to rebuild the Sunwell, get magical artefacts into their hands, and to gather information about the situation and developments in Azeroth.

Recently during one of these missions, a report back to Outland, Celadyr got ambushed by some Fel Orcs and a Felhunter. Overconfident in his arcane powers, he underestimated the Felhunter which nearly killed him, only the live draining power of his Feldagger saved him from certain death. Celadyr survived, but his mana was so throughly drained by the Felhunter that he couldn't regenerate it for some time. When at last he could made it back to his brethrens base in the Hinterlands, he secluded himself from his friends and brooded about how to regain his powers.

As even his arcane might has failed him in his encounter with the Felhunter, he convinced himself that only further study of demonic magic would help him to regain his powers and become stronger. Therefore he learned how to summon demons to Azeroth to learn from them directly, and then despose of them when he had learned enough. Beginning with imps, because at that time he had to rely complete on mana potions for his mana supply, he studied them and then siphoned on their energies until only a dry shrivilled shell remained, therby slowly regaining his mana. When finially he had regained his powers, he began to focuse on stronger demons and proceds always in the same way. Summon them, get all their knowledge and then kill them. He got more and more accustumed to controlling demons and enjois nothing more than crushing their will to use them to kill other demons and then despose of the summoned ones.

As he learned more and more demon spells, he lost his power to cast mage spells, and over time turned into a warlock - a transformation he has never regretted.
