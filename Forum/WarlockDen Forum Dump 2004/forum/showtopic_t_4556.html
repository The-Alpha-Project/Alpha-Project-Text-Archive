Well I finally got to actually play with my Felhunter tonight, and right off the bat can I just say, "WOW! I totally love this pet!!!!" Yes even multiple exclamation marks to prove just how excited I am about this pet!

Tonight Thor and I decided to head back to Duskwood to finish off two quests we had lingering, perfect setting to test out the new pet, mobs not to high so if it didn't do a good job it wouldn't be a problem, but not too easy that it wouldn't show his true fighting ability. On average he hit for mid 30's and the highest crit I saw was 57. Over all, extremely close to the damage my Succubus does. The Felhunter also has a spell called Devour Magic, and at first when I read the description it made zero sense... admittedly it still doesn't make a whole lot of sense lol.

Devour Magic
Purges 1 harmful magic effect from a friend or 1 beneficial magic effect from an enemy. If an effect is devoured, the Felhunter will be healed for 250 damage.

I did notice that this spell can't be put onto auto-attack (unless I'm missing something) and has an 8 second cool down period. Given the damage he was doing it wasn't too bad and we killed rather quickly. I wanted to test the tanking ability, but either because of the mobs lower level or Thor's super uber taunting ability (stifles a snicker) we didn't manage to do anything about that. My Felhunter, at level 34 had 919 HP's, so not overly many.

Later on, after completing those quests, we headed on down to Stranglethorn Vale to do some easy and fast quests so I could ding. Now the mobs around here are more to our level and we start killing away. I had played with the Felhunter's spell a little in Duskwood, but this was the real test. Basically every time I hit the spell, he was healed for 250 points. I tried it with mobs that had zero spells on them, curses on them, dots on them, nothing seemed to stand out in anyway and all the different tries, the Felhunter healed himself for 250. It appeared as if he had the ability to heal every 8 seconds.. hey not bad!

We came into a camp with lots and lots of mobs to kill (yipee) and in we set. We had used the 'Thor pull, I assist' tactic for a while now so it was not surprising that was the tactic we led in with. When we received a couple of adds however I chucked my pet onto them and was surprised at how well he tanked, and when you factor in the heal spell of his, he tanks almost better than the Voidwalker. If you then factor in the nice damage he does, he's basically a Voidwalker and a Succubus smooshed together... I knew if I left them together for long enough it would bound to happen O.o

The more times my pet tanked, the more I saw how well he coped and the more I tested his limits. It went from Thor pulling and me assisting, to me running around like a mad woman and leaving Thor trailing behind with barely enough time to loot what I had killed. This pet rocks, he was tanking two, three and even four mobs with ease, of course I had to make sure I hit that spell every 8 seconds and occasionally even transfer some health, but hey, I had to do that with the Voidwalker and he didn't do this much damage!

At one point I got a bit too cocky and ended up with close on nine mobs hitting my pet, that was fun to try and keep him alive through that. A quick shout to the boyfriend that a little help could be well used at this time and the "hehe, I see" that followed proved I had managed to pull an Akasha Train. (those are when I get a train and act all blonde "how did that happen? Oops" only it's not an act) The thing that impressed me the most is we came out of it alive, and during all the fighting my pet was constantly tanking between 2 and 3 mobs while Thor smacked on the others and I concentrated on mashing the Felhunter's heal, transferring my health, draining life to top mine back up, chucking a bandage on Thor and ensuring no mobs ran at low health. I just LOVE being that busy!

All night it was a reoccurring theme to see me hugging my pet and Thor saying, "I love your pet, have I mentioned that yet?" The only downside I could see was the fact that he doesn't seem to run as fast as the other pets, and on a couple of occasions we had to stop to let him catch up, I even think he poofed at one point from distance.
