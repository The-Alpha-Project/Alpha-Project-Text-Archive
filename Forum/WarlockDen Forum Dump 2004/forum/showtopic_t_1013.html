Wooo, might get a little ire for this but I'm one of these warlocks

Well it depends on the situation. As a solo player you obviously always have a pet out (I actually see the gameplay consequences of this as a class flaw, but that's a topic for another day). In pairs, never with a rogue in this patch, but last one voidwalker worked well, sometimes a voidwalker with a mage but just for multiple pulls or congested areas and always summoned on the fly, imp with a defense warrior, no pet with an offensive warrior, and voidwalker with a beserk warrior, imp or succubus with a priest if any though usually none, imp with a paladin almost always, voidwalker with a druid because you might as well be soloing, and suppose shaman is the only class I would always have a pet with.

In groups bigger than 2, no pet unless we have 2 tanks (imp then) or summon voidwalker at and only at the most difficult encounters.

In an instance of course I always use a succubus (Unless I'm walking someone through a lower level instance under-manned) but always set on passive.

I think that any Warlock would be quick to unfairly judge one that does not use a pet. Some people just don't realize how much their pets do. The imp is not impressive enough to make any sort of difference in any situation, I just summon his to give static buffs and be courteous to everyone. I would go with a Succubus more often, but I'm mainly a pvp warlock and thus getting ganked and losing the pet and the shard used to make it is commonplace. Plus it's pretty useless too except for CC and hunting monsters smaller in level than you are. I almost always have my voidwalker with me for pvp reasons and because I'm a solo player for a good sliver of my existence. I don't consider myself to be a poor player as a warlock and everytime I do not use a pet I'm either convinced it is the better choice or not willing to bother with the hassle. Pets are rarely worth it in groups. You summon your Nightma... erm Felsteed and ride up to the people that just let you in their party and you begin. There is no time to summon a pet, or if you do it during downtime it eats up enough mana to undo its effectiveness pretty quick. So you'd better hope you don't have a revolving door party or one that will break when you've accomplished the task at hand. Nobody wants to wait for a pet, or even a warlock for that matter. Also the pathing, aggro, and trains are something to think about. Some pets are just terrific though for specific uses and pairings.

Excellent uses for pets:

Warrior with Berserk. A berserker warrior will definately love you if you know how to work your blueberry right. Not sure what the spell or talent is called but there is a particular skill that does massive damage if you attack an enemy within a certain time after killing another. This, to be effective, requires chain pulls. Luckily for your warrior friend you are the best chain-puller in the game (Must have come from all that pulling on chains in the torture rooms no? ). One of the warriors I did this with was familiar with a warlock and wondered why I didn't use the imp for the chain pulls. After all it was ranged and quick and also gave him a static buff. He argued so much he almost left the party without checking to see if maybe I was right. Well I talked him into staying and used combination voidwalker torment + weakness to pull 6 Dustbelcher ogres at a time. I was pulling them faster than he could kill them too, and having a grand old time doing it. All he had to do was kill and ogre, and move right onto the next. It was the fastest leveling I'd ever been through, like having a pyroblast mage with unlimited mana. All in all I think I saved myself from the danger of making his /ignore list and I've now been on his friends list for some weeks.

Rogues as of last patch:

This was the only other class the voidwalker could forcefully draw aggro from without any cooperation, much to their shame . There really needs to be a class like the rogue was last patch, but in a way I'm glad they've become the powerdamagers they were meant to be.

Summoning as needed:

If we ran out of straggler mobs and needed to rush a camp, if we were going after a named who was elite or who summoned minions, or if we were just getting in over our heads, that's usually when I'd summon the pet. I get so sick of hearing my mage friend say "No pet today?" and having to reply "If we need it, sure". If you've never played a warlock (and she never has) and don't know how it works then stay out of my buisness. I barely have time to get a single curse and immolate off before she kills anything within 3 levels of her, and if there's an add _I_ can CC or tank it as well as a pet can because I know it's going to die very soon when she's finished with whatever she's killing at present. Nothing wrong with a little immolate + weakness + stab stab stab.

See'Es:

The whole rebellious thing is for the spell "Enslave demon" not a summon (yet). Enslave Demon has about the use of a priest's Mind control with one small difference. The difference is that there is not an abundant amount of demons. So I've never used that spell sadly enough.

Onto chain pulling- I apparently explained this badly. Maybe I was just excited because it was the most fun I ever had playing a warlock and the most I ever felt pets were worth their salt.

I was with a friend who happens to be a warrior in the badlands at the time this occured. Because of the density and nature of the beasts we were fighting I was going with no pet. He was in defense mode so I didn't need a tank, and since beast mobs have overlapping roam distances I didn't want to chance pulling many down on us all at once with the imp or succubus.

By and by our questing takes us to a "Dustbelcher Ogre" encampment. Now usually a warrior in defense mode and a warlock are a good pair, because the damage is heavier on the warlock side and the warrior is all tank. Partying with warriors in offensive or defensive mode also means that your DoT spells will go to completion, and that just gives me an overall feeling of being more useful. The problem with these Ogres was that the warrior was outdamaging me and we couldn't figure out why. Turns out these Ogres had very low armour quotients, and while all my spells do uniform damage regardless of armour these Ogres were beefy in health points and low in armour. The result was the individual warrior hits did much more damage and he was killing faster than I was.

To maximize our advantage of this, he switches from defense mode into berserker mode. In berserker mode he does much more damage per second, especially on mobs with low armour. I didn't even need him to tank for me (meaning use a threat generating skill like taunt) because I couldn't pull aggro from him anyway. So as a warrior in berserker mode you have a 5 second window after landing a killing blow on one opponent to land a starting blow on the next opponent for a massive amount of damage. In the case of these ogres which were our level and 1-2 above, it could take as much as 60% of their HP.
The problem was that you cannot feasibly run to your next opponent in 5 seconds, so he had to be right there waiting for you. That's where chain pulling with the pet came in.

The warrior wanted me to use the imp, but the timing that would have taken on pulls was really not worth the stress. I decided against it and decided to use the voidwalker. I could send him (with the attack command) as far away as I could visibly see enemies to engage them, then I would use the follow command to bring them back to where the warrior and I were fighting. This way by the time the warrior had landed the final blow on the ogre he was occupied with, the voidwalker would be back at my side waiting with a mob attacking him and waiting to be killed by the warrior. (think 'Scorpion' from the original Mortal combat)

As expected, I got a little fancy with it. By sending the voidwalker out to get many enemies and casting weakness on them, I could have an entire row of ogres ready and waiting to be killed by the time the warrior had finished one. This made for a very fast gain in experience and money, as well as some interesting items.
