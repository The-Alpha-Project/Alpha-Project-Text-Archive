My wife got into the Beta of World of Warcraft, something that is just not right in my eyes. Now I have no problems with a woman getting into beta… I have no problems that the woman was my wife… I have a problem with the fact I was not accepted! Hello Blizzard, as if she doesn’t have enough to hold over my head with the, “You never help clean!” “You never spend time with me!” and the favorite of the month, “They are your kids so stop pretending you can’t hear them and take them to the Movies!” Another joyous side effect of my wife being in beta is I get to hear these comments a lot more often because... well... she’s playing.

When I met my wife she thought I was a dork… she was right. She couldn’t understand why I would rather stay at home and play “those stupid computer games” than “hang out” at the local shopping mall. It must have been my superb charm and whit that kept her coming over even when her friends picked on her because before I knew it, we were spending a lot of time together and my guild were filling out missing person reports. Not long after that I was watching their beautiful specimen of nature walk around in my old T-shirts and painting her nails on my kitchen bench and I just knew that this was the sign I needed to pop the question… 12 months later it is the morning of my wedding and I am on my guild forum posting a message that I will be un-contactable for a week or so and can anyone bring me on if something drops I might want. Fellas let me warn you... do not let your wife catch you talking to your guild on the morning of the wedding… just trust me on that.

On return from our honeymoon I waited till my new wife was asleep and jumped on line to let everyone know I was back… busted! Who knew she would be craving a glass of water 3 seconds after getting into bed. She sat down, looked at me and said those magical words… “Why don’t you show me this game thing, I might like it.”

It must have been my stunned mullet expression because I remember her laughing and repeating it. I think I misspelled my password four times before getting it to work, I was so excited! This beautiful creature that lives with me, cooks with me, laughs with me is going to play games with me?! Wahooo!! I was almost ready to call my brother at 2am to inform him off the good news and rub it in his face as he sleeps on his lunch breaks at work because the only time he can play online is around 3am in the morning, when he knows the troll, er, lovely wife of his is asleep.

That was the start of a very wonderful life. My wife and I played side by side from that day forward and she even offered to pay me to breastfeed so she could attend a raid when we had our first child. That has all changed now… WoW is on her computer and she won’t let me play! She takes great enjoyment from tormenting me like this and has even pass worded her computer to block me out. It’s been a couple of weeks since she got in and I think my pathetic whining and fake crying has finally broken through… because today my friends, I share you the story of when “I was a “I was a Newb Gnome Warlock”

I was so excited, it was a Saturday morning and I was sitting at my wife’s computer watching World of Warcraft load. I already knew what class I wanted to be from watching all sorts of WoW sites, and I thought for a change I would give a Gnome a try. I chose my look and hit create character, holding my breath I waited… my name was accepted, rock on! I took a deep breath and hit enter world. I came in on a patch of dirt in the middle of Snowey Mountains, wearing a red robe and looking rather… short. I must have been so taken with being in game I was missing the chatter right beside my ear, “Look for the guys with yellow exclamation marks above their head, they will give you a quest, press 1 to get into attack mode, food heals and drink gives you mana back, you get the most xp from quests…what are you doing? Why are you running around there? You need to get a quest first, why are you still running? Turn around!”

“Hunny,” I said “can I please just have a look around first?”

Sighing she replied, “Fine”

I ran around for a while taking in the scenery, which was obviously too much for my beloved as she went and started to fold that washing in the way only women can do while letting you know at the same time they aren’t impressed. You really can’t enjoy scenery that much when you have someone glaring at you waiting to hurry up, which is odd because she has already done exactly what I am doing now… but I guess it’s just not the same. “Ok hunny, all done, can you come show me what I need to do next?” Course I knew what to do, but wouldn’t you rather enjoy somewhat enjoy your play time than have iron marks on all your clothes for the rest of the day.

“Ok find a quest, ooh you can get one from him, no him, him… that one… geez right click already!” By this stage my other half is practically sitting on my lap and her breath is fogging up the screen, “ok, ok I got it... I gotta kill some of these mo...” I am cut off with directions where to go in order to find these said mobs for the quest, which I follow precisely and I get so excited I shoot off my shadowbolt apparently before she was ready as the same time it hit the mob I heard a sigh from beside me. “Odd, I didn’t think a nuke made that sound, let alone sounding like it’s in stereo” I say looking sarcastically surprised. “I wanted to tell you something first that’s all” came the pouted response.

One thing my wife hasn’t learned about me after all these years is when I ask, “What?” right after she says she wanted to tell me something she needs to tell me what, and not “nothing” because when I hear “nothing” I shrug and keep going with what ever I was doing. Luckily I was saved from the normal “Why don’t you care enough to want to know” because I finished my quest and was promptly given directions to run back and see “the guy” again, and no stop killing the mobs randomly, that is just stupid (imaging me putting on a girly voice there… ok no I don’t sound like that).

I ran back and finished my quest to get a nice chunk of xp in return, which when added to the xp I had already received killing dinged me to level 2. How exciting! For the next couple of hours I got listen to my wife tell me where to get quests, how to get to the next area and watched her get aggravated at my repeated dying when trying to get my Imp, yes dear, those Frostmain Trolls really don’t play fair, I will write a letter to their leader and mention that it’s not nice to gang up on me because I didn’t realize a ledge was there /sarcasm off.

I was taught on the minor points a lot of other gamers miss, in that when you play a gnome and you start off in the snow, the most important piece of armor by far is a pair of boots so your feet don’t get cold, followed by gloves so your fingers don’t go all numb and you can’t cast spells. I laughed at her ‘Roll-playing” and was met with a scaringly serious face and being told, “Roll-playing is what geeks do!” Yes ma’am.

Being the male I am I played all day ignoring the natural need for food and only stopped long enough to relieve my bladder of unnecessary bursting, to which I blocked my keyboard by sitting my two-year old on the desk, back to the screen with chocolate all over his face. Why did I do this you wonder? Because women can’t stand dirty and this is doubled when it involved normally gorgeous child who now resemble a swamp monster. If I rushed I could always get back to my desk before said child was finished being cleaned meaning I could continue my travels with out the risk of being logged off so she could get back on.

I listed to live commentary from all over the house while the bat cleaned the house all day. I tried my hardest to enjoy every minute and ignore the continual flow of instructions which I learnt not paying enough attention to will earn you a slap across the back of your head. I played all day and managed to see the entire area of Dun Moragh plus seeing the magnificent Ironforge and falling into lava when I leaned a little too far over the edge. I got lost 3 times but thank the stars I had my ball and chain beside me to rescue my poor self.

All in all I had a great time; I managed to get to level 8 and am currently trying to think of ways I can get on long enough to get the next pet... the almighty Voidwalker /rawr! I have even gone as far as to bribe my wife to let me back on waving my Credit Card in the air and promise not to report it stolen until 6pm that night, sadly however my plan foils as the look of excitement that crosses my face when her rear end raises from the seat gives it away.

I’ve learnt that my wife is a complete nerd and I love her more and more for it. I’ve learned you can not separate a woman and WoW for any great length of time and that next time I better the be the one to get in beta as the way it is now means I have to cook my own dinners, and trust me that is not a good thing – however the lost weight from baked beans and toast is doing wonders for my image. I love my Warlock and can’t wait until I have one of my very own I can play full time with out side kick Susan telling me what to do. And I’ve learned my kids are going to boarding school as soon as WoW goes live so we don’t risk loosing them to child services for neglect… that’s a joke… hunny I was joking… oops time to go!
