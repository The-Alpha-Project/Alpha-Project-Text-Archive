I tend to agree with Trem's ideas on the hellfire combination but I have a few qualifications to add. If you are fighting a warrior they can attack both of you simultaneously with cleave so try to maintain a moderate distance between you and the pet. Also remember that warriors have fear-like abilities with battle cries and a frenzied specialized warrior can make your character stand in one spot instead of fleeing normally. Rogues who come prepared with poison can slow your casting as though you were afflicted with Curse of Tongues not to mention the added damage.

That said, if your pet is incapaciated either by root or some such, dismiss it with the sacrifice if you have a voidwalker and be ready for the hellfire technique. Then do what Trem suggested and cast damage over time spells then nuke with shadowbolt.

Be mindful of abilities like Shield Wall and the like (and other defensive techniques with visual components).

Otherwise be prepared to use Curse of Exhaustion and make your escape. (If you have an invisibility potion even better)
