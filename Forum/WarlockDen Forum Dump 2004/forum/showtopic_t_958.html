Much has been written about the World of Warcraft skill system, combat, quests, and races. But as the game's title implies, it is the world that's the true star of the game. Journey with us as we show you the sights and sounds of Azeroth.

In this edition, we'll be exploring the icy mountains of Dun Morogh, as the evening turns into night.

Dun Morogh's outward appearance is one of great beauty, but also great danger. Amid the snow-covered trees and frozen lakes rove giant bears, fearsome wolves, even ruthless trolls. Despite these dangers, it's easy to see why the races of both Dwarves and Gnomes call the land their home.

The region seems to be caught in a perpetual state of immaculate beauty. A perfect layer of silver-white snow can always be found covering the trees, mountaintops, and dwarven encampments. When dwarves aren't fishing in one of the rare areas of open water (or fighting off a troll), they're most likely enjoying a bit of ale from one of the land's several brewmasters.

Most dwarven dwellings are nothing more than a tent and a campfire, or perhaps a makeshift wagon. Perhaps this was in an effort to not draw attention away from the nearly-unbelievable city of Ironforge. Housed entirely underneath a massive mountain, its passageways and halls stretch literally as far as the eye can see, and then some.

The sheer beauty of the landscape has caused many young adventurers to underestimate the danger of the region. There's something about the serene calm of the snow that calls to the adventurer within us all.
