I've about exhausted myself on skimming for facts and speculation on gameplay mechanics in WoW, so until some more up to date info comes out let's take a bit of a you-turn...

There's a good beginner grouping guide up, so I thought I'd make a quick little RP'ing in a MMO guide. It should be noted this guide will not map exactly to WoW. I admitedly don't know the storyline well (but I'm learning it :P ), so I'll refrain from adding elements about it since I would likely get them wrong. This guide, instead, could be used as a foundation when trying to understand the theory/practice behind RP'ing in a MMO.

I'm not a hardcore MMORPG RP'er, but for a while I used to be a big time live role player in chat rooms. A lot of the same ideas apply, only you probably shouldn't take them to such extremes as you would in an RP chat room.

And of course this guide is opinion based, namely mine. If you've got anything to add or criticism about it, feel free to put in your two cents.


Definitions
Let's first define a few concepts in RP'ing, and then apply them for a good understanding.

IC- In character
OOC- Out of character

IC and OOC? What the heck? It simplistically boils down to this. IC is your character, when you're IC you're basically being the character. When you're OOC you're being yourself, and you need to recognize a SOLID line between the two. I don't care if the character is based on you, you need to realize that you're "acting" if you will as a character when you're IC, but you're never really that person.

This solid line between the two needs to be taken in consideration when RP'ing. If a character insults your character, they're not insulting you, they're insulting your CHARACTER. I know it sounds simple to most, but a few people initially get caught up on this.

When you're OOC, you're yourself. People typically (and should) seperate the fact that they are talking OOC in some widely used (by the rest of the community) or recognizable fashion. Like so:

Tjien: All your faith and so-called virtue has brought you nothing... you might want to consider changing affiliation.
Tjien: (OOC: Urg, BRB... dog wants out)

What this would show is me speaking IC as Tjien, and then realizing I need to hop away from the keyboard for a minute to let the dog out. My character doesn't have to let my dog out, I do. This also boils down to a frame of reference... more on that in a bit.

Also, to avoid a headache, understand the difference between the types of people who play MMOs. I'll break them into two categories, but know full some people fit into both. We'll just keep it simple.

Role Player- RP'er, a person who plays the game on more of a storyline sense. They'll take care to flesh out details of their character (personality, history, so on) and they're probably more concerned with doing quests and exploring than with leveling.

Gamer- A person who sees themself as playing a game, not playing out a story. They're typically (as in not always) more concerned with how the game is played, rather than why.

Be aware that neither type of person is better or worse. Some people just like to play a MMORPG without all the story and drama, so be it and let them. It'll save you the headache. Actually I think a lot of people fit into category that is a mix of both as well. For instance you might run into an avid RP'er, but this person is also probably very concerned with leveling and making the most out of their toon.



Frame of Reference
To be a good RP'er you have to understand your character has a frame of reference, much like in a book. Characters in a book don't stand around and talk about the author, they don't know anything about the author in fact. To them the author does not exist, only their world and other characters around them exist.

By this idea, your character has a "Frame of Reference," the in-game world. There is not a chat screen to your characters (do characrers in a book ever tell one another they mispelled something they said?). Having trouble clicking an object? You might be, but your character is NOT. Play your character as to being totally oblivious to the fact that they are in a game. Play them in their "Frame of Reference."

Storyline
Having a history, or storyline for a character isn't typically required to make yourself a good RP'er in a MMO. But if you want to take the extra step, go right ahead. Here's some pointers from my experience:

Research the game world, and then research it again. After that, do it one last time for good luck and flesh out your story. Nothing is more aggrivating than creating a long history for a character only to have someone say, "Well actually according to page XXX of (insert fanboy bible here), you are wrong." A believe me, there are people out there who will know EVERY gritty detail about something, to a point of annoyance. Try to get it to flow along with the game's natural story and history.

Mesh it with the personality. A history should provide a means to a why your character is who he/she is. Beaten severly as a child? It may stand to reason your character is somewhat shy and introverted then.

Draw the line between simplicity and complexity. Try to write an "average Joe" history as opposed to creating one where your toon is the "On High Exhalted Ruler of So On and So Forth." I think it's usually best to make your character's history (and personality) a sum of what he/she DOES as opposed to who they were.


Personality
This is the most important line between the Gamer and RP'er. An ability to come across as a certain kind of person through your character. Again, you're "acting" so to speak. Here's some tips for this:

Think methodically, especially if your character is not like you very much. How would your character react? Try to establish a STYLE to your character's dialogue. There are words and phrases you may use a lot, so why not have your character have their own little verbal "quirks"? If your character is a very quiet person, learn to use the emote commands to display actions of your character to get them across.

Realize the risks. This is especially important if your character acts brashly, or in any way most people might find offensive. Realize that some people won't make the solid distinction between you (the person pretending to be the character) and the character itself. You will come across people who take personal offense to an offensive character. So if you want to RP an offensive character, be prepared to accept this.

Try to make a flexible personality. Even if people are reacting to your character as their character, there is going to be tension if your character is offensive. Try to make a character that will get along with a wide variety of people, as you'll probably need teamwork later on in this game. Don't get yourself deadset into playing a characrer that everyone (either IC or OOC) will hate. Maybe just a few people. :P


Safety In Numbers
Try to group and RP with people who RP on the same level as you. Not ability-wise, but in just how technical they want to get. You'll actually become a better RP'er if you RP with people who do a really good job of playing believable characters. You, however, will do nothing but give yourself a stroke through trying to get a group of people to RP to the same technical standards as you.

Also, if you want to do some HEAVY RP'ing with your own stories and plots in game, it's best to RP this out around people who are interested in the same type of gameplay. It's no good to just suddenly say to your group, "We must slay the evil Trogdor, the Burninator! To Rivendale my chums, with haste!" A group probably doesn't care about your grand ideas for a good story. Instead, look to a guild, and group of people who share your same intensity for RP.



Ettiquite
This becomes fairly important (so important I think I spelled it wrong?). Anyways, try to apply these ideas when trying to RP in a MMORPG.

RP if you want, but even if it's an RP server, don't insist your group MUST RP to the same standards you do too. Again, it's a one way ticket to stroke city folks. If you're doing your best to RP, and some guy in your group says stuff like, "OMG you guyz we kan pwn those trollz! lets move camp!" just ignore it. JUST IGNORE IT in case you didn't read it the first time. If you try to force people to RP, more likely than not your group is just going to grow tired of your anal-retentiveness.

Don't be a drama queen. The word is queen, but I mean it in a gender neutral way. Don't try to make your character the center of the universe. Try to instead just "play along" and have fun. Worry about having your character be the main person to your own plots only if you've got a group of people into it. Don't drag people who just don't care along.

Don't be super-technical. Example, you don't know a person's name until they or someone tells you it, but you don't need to get this picky about RP in a MMORPG. It's just... prohibitive. Chances are you'll get so stuck on technicalities, and trying to correct people OOC, that you will only do a good job of ruining your day. Maybe someone elses.

Don't RP your character to a point that interfers with grouping (ESPECIALLY a leveling group). It's a MMORPG, we might like story, but we all like leveling. Have your character be how you want them to be, but not to the point where it hinders a group. I've touched on this in a reply thread somewhere. As an example I'll get just as much pleasure out of my Warlock by having him get into arguments with a Paladin, than if I were to have him just plain not help the Paladin in my group.

Tjien: That book of yours wouldn't even make good kindling. Let me show you some TRUE power through MY faith...

If someone takes your character to personal offense, try to apologize quietly. Send them a private message, explaining your character is insulting theirs, not that you are insulting them. If they don't get it its probably best to just tone it down then. You'll save yourself the explaining to a GM (who will probably take your side of the story if you're right) anyways. No personal conflict is better than personal conflict even if you're right.

As a rule of thumb, just RP how you want to RP, but don't make it a point to force others to RP as you do. It's a point I've touched on numerous times here, and I can't stress it enough.



Well there you have it kiddies and kiddos. That rambling jumbled mess is my take on how to RP well. I might edit it later, I feel I might've forgot some things.

All in all just remember to HAVE FUN. That's the reason most of us RP in an MMO anyways; because it's MORE FUN for us to do so. And just because someone doesn't find it fun doesn't mean they're ignorant, and it will only make you have less fun to convince them otherwise.
