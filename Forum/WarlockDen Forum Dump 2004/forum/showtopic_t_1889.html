Falesh hit it closely to the mark. Term PK started out in UO where there were people just out to "Player Kill". UO in the early day's didn't have any boundries on PvP or any restrictions. So many people just went on killing binges Player Killing everyone in sight. PK'ers also tended to be the "Bully" type, only attacking a sure kill and usually running away from an even or sure loss. Now if you think about that, that's good tactics in a warfare sense. But in a game, it's shouts out foul play.

In EQ, I think a more appropriate term really came about, griefers or "griefing" (again a UO term originally) and the term PK was a description of any player involved in an act of PvP and actively hunted players, but not necessarily a griefer.

Griefers are the ones who at level 60, go into newbie zones and hunt and kill level 5-10 players just for the fun of it. In EQ and Shadowbane, those guys were usually the ones who shouted out how good they were after those kills but ran away verses someone of equal skill level. And if they lost the lost to a "cheater".

So I think the best definition of PK is a person who actively hunts or goes out of his way for PvP. A PvP'er, and this is my definition is a player who will get involved in PvP, but won't necessarily engage in it depending on the situation. And the two roles are interchangable.
