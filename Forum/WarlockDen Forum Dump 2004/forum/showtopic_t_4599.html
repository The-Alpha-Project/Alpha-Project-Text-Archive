A person I highly respect once told me, “Words are like tools; they can tear down and build up.” Over the internet facial expressions, vocal tones and body language is lost, this heightens the need to ensure what you are posting comes across the way you want it to. With so many members from so many different backgrounds, countries, languages and more, I ask all members to abide by the following rules in order to keep the forums as pleasant and accessible as possible to everyone.

Please note the following are in no particular order of importance and all should be followed. A members age has no impact on if they should or shouldn't take note of these rules, maturity can exist, and be missing from people of all ages.

No flaming or personal attacks on the boards.
There is a zero tolerance policy on this forum in regards to members attacking one another in any form. I am sure everyone has come across a time when someone didn't agree with something they said, or visa versa. I ask that all members try to open their mind to other people's points of view, even if they don't agree 100%, disagreeing does not make the other person wrong. In the event you have a problem with a den member or an issue that needs resolved, please get in touch with a moderator and refrain from doing it on the boards, show you are the bigger person and walk away.

No derogatory or racist remarks.
We are fortunate enough here to have people all over the world, because of this, there is a zero tolerance policy towards any racial slurs both clear and masked. This also includes where some lives. While I am aware of running jokes about countries, for example New Zealand and the "sheep" and while this may be ok to say in your own home or around friends, most people on this site can be classified as strangers and you never know who you might offend. To avoid any upsets please refrain from any comments on race, place of residence or ethnic background, even when meant in jest. Remember over the internet the humor side can be lost and someone may take offence.

It also should be noted this goes for making mass assumptions about a country, for example "Asian bashing" because some gaming members farmed cash etc.

No spamming!
This includes, but is not confined, posting threads on topics where a thread already exists, posting the same topic in multiple forums (if you have a post that crosses two categories, make a separate post for each in the proper areas) and making non-constructive posts, especially unnecessary sarcastic comments (note there is a difference between humorous and sarcastic). Use your judgment. Spamming is definitely off limits, I don't wish to read the same thing from the same person multiple times - posts like this will be edited / deleted.

No harassment.
No member is to ever feel harassed by another. Things that could be construed as harassment include constantly arguing with views of one member unless you have valid, constructive posts to back up your feelings. If you don't like a person then ignore their posts, there is no need to constantly try to antagonize them by replying to everything they say unless you have a valid point to make. Do not harass via PM's thinking that it won't be noticed, all members of this board have the right and are encouraged to contact a moderator if they feel they are being harassed and the situation will be dealt with.

Keep profanity and vulgar posts to a minimum.
While profanity and swearing is becoming a second nature to most people, it really isn't needed to make a point. It is always more pleasant to read a well constructed post free from constant bad language. If you find yourself using a lot of unnecessary words to make your point, take a break, calm down and post again later, in most cases alternatives can be found. There is also a language filter in place, any means to try and get around it by using ! in place of i or any other means is against the rules.

No "lewt" speak!
I think I can safely say everybody is sick of it, it's really not necessary and in most cases makes your post harder to read while everyone tries to decipher the "lewt". The only exception to this rule, is if your describing something someone said to you

Please do not excessively abbreviate.
Abbreviations always occur, and in some cases they make a lot of sense. No one would type out "Laugh out Loud" and nine times our of ten everyone knows that is what "lol" means. Well used abbreviations are fine but do not abbreviate every word with vowels as sometimes other members will loose what you are trying to convey. There are some abbreviations built into the forum that will type out the alternative text for you, however this is not an excuse to get lazy when posting.

Show respect to your fellow posters.
Probably one of the major rules of this forum, everybody MUST show respect to their fellow members. Each and every member of this board is important even if you or I don't agree with everything they say, this does not mean they deserve disrespect from other posters. If what they are saying is a problem, the situation will be handled by the moderators.

Unlike other forums, this site is highly moderated to maintain the high level of maturity we strive to achieve. Posts will be edited if they are against the rules. Should you notice one of your posts edited, DO NOT re-edit to comment or alter it, simply make another post following the rules or contact a moderator if you feel you were unfairly treated. Sometimes miscommunications happen, especially with so many nationalities and ways of speaking.


Violations of these rules may result in temporary or permanent banning.
