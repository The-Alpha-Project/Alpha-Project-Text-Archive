If ever I've cursed myself for not screenshotting something, this would be a prime example. Ugh.

Questing around with my lvl 29 Troll Rogue on the PvE server. Just turned in a quest reward to an NPC near Stonetalon Peak in the Stonetalon Mountains. Needed to head back to Crossroads for a bit, and the fastest way for me to do that is to run back to Sun Rock Retreat and fly from there.

So on the way there, probably about halfway between Stonetalon Peak and Sun Rock Retreat, I come across a pair of Alliance NPCs along the side of the road. Having never attempted to kill an NPC before, I got curious and decided to give it a shot just to see if I could do it, and also what happens when/if I succeed. The NPCs were a 26 Elf and a 30 gnome. To effectively solo them, I sapped the gnome and started attacking the weaker elf. She went down easily with little resistance. Then I turn my attention to the gnome. Things are going well, he's hurt me a bit but nothing serious, and I've got him at about 25% health remaining.

Then, as I'm still fighting the gnome, a 26 NE warrior and 25 NE druid show up to defend their NPCs. Of course, by attacking the NPCs I've flagged myself for PvP, even though I had no intention of fighting another actual player character. As I deal the killing blow to the gnome, the druid does a /cry on my shoulder, and the warrior makes a /rude gesture at me. They haven't started attacking me yet, which clues me in that they must be waiting on a nearby reinforcement. Thinking I'm probably fucked now, I decide to go out in style.

I /bow before the warrior and /lick the druid's cheek. Then I turn around and lunge myself over the cliff into windshear crag and start hauling butt towards Sun Rock Retreat, which is still a considerable distance away. I peek behind me and see that indeed they are now hunting me, hot on my heels, and to make matters worse they seem to be faster than I am. I go to use my sprint skill, but notice that there's still a 48 second cooldown from when I used it previously. So now I'm just crossing my fingers in a mad bid to get to my destination. They've started to catch up to me now, and are taking swings at me as we're running. Then, much to my disfortune, the warrior got smart and let me lead for a bit, then charged me, which stuns me for a few seconds. Now I'm deadlocked in melee with the warrior while the druid is rooting me and nuking me with spells. I'm at about 50% health at this point when I look at my sprint cooldown and notice there's only 8 seconds left on it. This made me excited, until I noticed off in the distance the other reinforcement they were waiting on, a 34 druid, who was rapidly approaching.

When entangling roots finally wears off (which thankfully was about the same time thunderclap wore off as well), I turn towards the warrior and gouge him, stunning him for 4 seconds. I then turn around and finally activate sprint, enhancing my speed and giving me some breathing room. One last druid nuke hits me before I can get out of range, taking me down to 7 hp! I'm getting really close to Sun Rock Retreat now. Sprint wears off and they're still hot on my heels. I start to seriously doubt my survival at this point.

I hop a cliff and round a corner, and can SEE the front gates of Sun Rock, when the unthinkable happens: the warrior had caught up to me SOMEHOW and charged me again. Now I KNOW I'm screwed. But then I notice that even though I'd been charged, the warrior was still just out of melee range. The druids had caught up just enough to start a nuke spell, but by some miracle I was able to get out of range before they completed it. I MADE IT! I run through the front gates of Sun Rock, kiss the ground, then turn to survey my attackers. There the three of them stood, looking at me in all my 30% health glory.

Before they turned tail and ran, I waved at the warrior and did a /cheer. Haha, sucker!
