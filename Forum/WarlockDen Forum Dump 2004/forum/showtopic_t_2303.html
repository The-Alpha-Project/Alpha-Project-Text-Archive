Pets
If you are a class that uses pets you should learn everything you can about them and learn how to best control them.

Summon Pet
Once you are allowed to have a pet you need to learn how to summon them. Typically you need to first get training to receive your pet, then you will find a new summon pet button in your spells menu. Hunters will have to convert a pet with their special ability. Drag the summon pet button on to one of your action bar pages and summon your pet. You will now see a new mini action bar that is used to control your pet. This is your pet action bar.

Pet Action Bar
Pet users get a special little action bar they can use to control their pet. You can drag abilities onto this action bar. Your pet bar disappears when your pet dies.

Pet Training
You can gain new abilities for your pet by visiting a pet trainer for that specific pet. If you don't know where to find one, ask more experienced players for that class. The trainers are around but they may be difficult to find. Try visiting a new town.

Pet Location
Your pet shows up on the mini-map. This will come in very handing for keeping track of how far your pet is away from you as you travel around the world.

Pet Death
If your pet dies you may have to wait for a cooldown period before summoning it again.

If you get too far away from your pet or if they get stuck they will eventually unsummon. You will have to summon your pet again. You will have to learn how to best lead your pet around. Another option is to dismiss your pet then summon it again once you've reached your destination.

Pet Healing
Your pet will heal after combat is over. You can heal it yourself if you want too.

Pet Buffs
You can buff your pet up! Give it a shot.

Dismissing Pet
Right-click on your pet's name bar to see additional pet options. You can dismiss a pet to make it disappear if you no longer need it or wish to switch pets.

Pet Modes
While your pet is active you have several common buttons to control them:

Attack: select a target and hit the attack button. There's a hot key to do it too. Look it up in the options/controls menu.

Follow: This tells your pet to ignore everything else and just follow you.
Stay: This is useful when you want your pet to stay in place. You can leave them outside of town or a building, go do your business, then come back to them. That way they won't be traveling around trying to reach you.

Aggressive: If a monster comes within range of the pet they will go out and attack them.

Defensive: the pet will respond if attacked but otherwise won't care

Passive: the pet will just sit there and not attack. This is useful if you want to stop a pet from attacking to play around with hate lists or for other reasons. It comes in handy.

Pet Control
You need to manage the hate of the monster between you and your pet. If they are overly attacking your pet and about to kill it then you need to heal your pet or get the monster to attack you instead. You can transfer the monster over to you by casting spells on the monster that really make it angry. You will have to figure out which spells do that.
If the monster is too focused on you, you need to stop attacking it and use whatever pet abilities your pet is to make the monster angry enough to fight them instead. This can take some time to learn how to properly do this. The goal is to keep both you and your pet alive at the end of combat.

If you're in a party and your pet is attracting too much attention, have it stop attacking until the monster focuses on someone else in the party. Then wait a bit longer then have your pet join in the fight again. That way your pet can continue to attack and live while the monster is attacking someone else.

Rookie Mistakes
-Don't put your pet on Aggressive mode when traveling through dungeons. They will typically go crazy and wake up lots of monsters. Change it to Passive or Defensive mode. Don't put your pet on aggressive mode when traveling pretty much anywhere. Change it to passive mode.

-At the start of a battle make sure your pet is attacking. If it is standing around, it's not helping. You can use hot keys to make your pet attack faster such as Control then 1 to use the pet's attack button.

Pet Fun
-Go into a newbie region and use your pet to destroy the monsters. That's funny!
-Kill someone with your pet in player vs. player combat!
-Use emotes on your pet! /hug pet =]
-Use powerful pets to scare newbies. They will think it's a monster if they are not familiar with the pets that players can use.

Common Pet Questions

Can I give items to my pet?
No.

Can I name my pet?
Hunters can name their pets, Warlocks cannot.

What classes get pets?
Warlocks, Hunters, Mages, and Priests can control humanoid monsters as a pet for a limited amount of time. There are other ways to get pets in the game such as Engineers can construct pets.

Imp: Various ranks of Firebolt (Rank 3 at level 28 which hits for 55ish)...Various ranks of Blood Pact which add stamina to the group.
