Xeverus wrote:
Warlock class is nowhere near as bleak as everyone is making it out to be. One of the main problems I see with warlocks is that no one seems to understand our abilities and so in a group setting they don't play off of our strengths but instead hinder them b/c they don't understand what we can/can't do.

Some Tips:

Healthstones: Warriors please use a healthstone during every single fight possible. I have 0 problem summoning you one after every battle and if i know my business I will be getting a shard every fight and have a limitless supply. Healthstones are great to use early in a fight b/c they generate more aggro on the warrior and allow the warrior to get considerable aggro on all mobs before the Priest has to heal. If you use a healthstone when able it saves the priest from gaining that first heal aggro that is a common wipe in instances.

Succubus: Yes, my succubus can mez targets and remove them from combat for 15 seconds pending that no one does damage to them and breaks the mez. Thankfully this patch blizzard added a new graphic for the mez and it is a lot more noticeable. Please don't break my mezzes and understand that Mages aren't the only ones with CC.

Banish: This is a guaranteed 30 second mez on a target that cannot be broken. Awesome for use against tough boss pulls or the like. Factor this spell into your game plans before the tough pulls b/c i can neutralize a mob for a good while using banish. Coupled with succubus mez I can keep 2 mobs for a pretty hefty length of time.

Runners: Do not worry about runners when a warlock is in the group. Save that mana your using on nuking at the end, icing, frost novaing or rage for hamstring/major combo. I have an instant cast spell that keeps runners from even thinking of getting away. Warlocks are the snare kings and you can leave that duty to us and concentrate on other things.

Healers: I am self sufficient at keeping myself alive 90% of the time. I will constantly be sacrificing health for mana and then health draining mobs. This is how I never have to drink. So do NOT fret if i burn my health down to 50% I do not need a heal UNLESS in the rare case I somehow have gotten major aggro and have a mob wailing on me. Then yes I probably need a heal...or perhaps not if I use that ever useful healthstone. You can pretty much forget about me for the entire grouping experience b/c if I play my cards right I will never need a heal.

AE's: Yes, Warlocks get Area Effects just like mages. Ours are not of the same caliber but we can definitely help when it comes to AE pulls. Just want to get the word out that if you are going into a dungeon where AE's are a large benefit warlocks are the second best class for Area Effect damage.

I was thinking of posting this on the general forum but right now it's far too full of comments regarding the patch so I figure it would get lost in that sea of worthlessness. Also i wanted to run it by you guys b/c I'm sure you all have more good tips on top of the few I could think of.


This is something that I think all warlocks as well as other classes should read. Sort of condences everything to a coherent set of ideas with which you can tell people things about our class.
Thanx man for the orginal post.

stay safe everyone,
-Sala
