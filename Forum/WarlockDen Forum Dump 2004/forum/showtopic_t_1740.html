Finn the Warlock IC:

I am not sure why I tell you this…. I am not sure why I should tell anyone. But I fear if I do not, then the story of how I came to be at this…. this…. crossroad, will never be known. I can feel them now… watching me… waiting until I make a mistake, waiting to devour me. Why you ask… Why would they bother with me, a weak young man, naive in the ways of the world? It is because I know their secrets! I have learned the truth about The Burning Legion! They think to control me, think I will become their pawn. They believe that because I am only a human, because I am mortal, I am weak. They think I will fall prey to the lust and greed that so many seeking power often do. They are wrong. Oh, I am human and mortal to be sure, and though my body may be weak, my mind is strong, and I have found the will to live…. My soul already gone, they can not corrupt me! The beginning, I should start at the beginning.

My father, Fionn Aran was a founding member of the Kirin Tor in Dalaran. Both he and my mother Kiera Valin and their parents before them were Magocrats who helped to rule over the city-state of Dalaran.

I see your smile, you think me daft now. You are thinking to yourself, how could that be, the Kirin Tor was founded over 1200 years ago. Well, be patient my friend. There is much more to the tale, by its end you will think me completely mad, and yet I swear to you it is true.

As I was saying, my family has lived in Dalaran for generations. Every member of the Aran family entered into the study of wizardry. Our line produced some of the greatest mages that Azeroth has ever seen. Many had even served on the Council of Tirisfal. But these facts are unimportant. It is the Kirin Tor that drives the story forward, one of the many tasks my father undertook for the Kirin Tor was the cataloguing and researching of newly discovered artifacts. It was an artifact that started the trouble. A Warrior coming back from the Alterac Mountains northeast of Dalaran brought a large white stone set with a single jet black gem with him that the Kirin Tor would name the “Ciaretern” meaning Forever-Dark. It radiated an incredibly strong magic, yet none of the mages could discover its purpose. My father studied it for 10 years before he uncovered its hideous purpose.

The Ciaretern was created by the Demon Lord Raxzanth’Kal to help spread despair within the Highborne elves civilization. The White stone feels slightly warm to the touch and gets warmer and warmer the closer the holder is to his True love. The Black Gem is really the heart of a Succubus cut to resemble a highly polished gem. When two lovers are together holding the Ciaretern its magic comes alive. The Stone will grant them one wish, but the price is high, higher than either will realize until it is too late. A child born of their love will loose its soul; the shell of its body will become an imp, forever wandering the void. A truly evil design.

My father knew that no good could ever come of such an artifact, so he searched for a way to destroy it. This is where I come into the picture. At this time I was only a young boy of 13 seasons, but already on my way to becoming a Magician like my father and mother. I was visiting my uncle, learning from him the secrets of Mana, when a demon ripped loose from the confines of its prison and began ravaging the city. As my uncle and I ran from the area of destruction we witnessed a blinding flash of light and there standing in the center of the chaos was an old man radiant with power. He lashed out with power at the demon and I watched as the magic ripped the demon to pieces, scattering its body like cannon balls into the surrounding buildings. It was the first time I had seen a Guardian before, and my awe caused me to hesitate when the building next to us imploded. I was thrown to the ground and the light drifted from my eyes. My uncle picked me up and brought me to my parents. My time in the world was short, I was dieing, and nothing could stop it. Even the Guardian came before me and tried to bring me back, but it was beyond his powers.

As my mother wept, my father searched his mind for an answer, and he found it in the Ciaretern. As I lay dead before my parents, I can feel my soul seeking out its new place in the world, I am floating up above my lifeless body. I feel calm, free, and weightless. My father holds the Ciaretern in his hands; it looks to be glowing with a life of its own except for the black gem in its center. My father whispers into my mother’s ear and she grasps the stone, I can feel their thoughts, carefully they think, word it right or it will all be for naught. “We sacrifice our lives for the continued life of our son, take him where he will live again and find love.” I feel the air around us tingle, the white stone on the Ciaretern brightens and my parents drop it to the ground. The white stone fades, a spirit… no, a soul unwraps itself from the Black Gem. The Soul of a young child, I hear its ghostly moan, “I am sorry.” Then my own soul is pulled to my broken body. I open my eyes. Mother is crying.. Father is holding me. Then a tug,… again… and the light all space and time courses through me. The world goes black.

I am on the ground. A battle is being fought around me. It is Dalaran to be sure, but not the one I remember. I stand groggily, swaying on my feet. Mother and Father are using magic, Strange Ghoul like creatures are charging at us. I see the Black Gem on the ground, it rises up and around it the form of a scantily clad woman slowly takes shape. She holds a small wood handle with many leather straps on it. She turns to look at me, “Now for my payment, come to me young one and keep me warm.” She lashes out with the weapon in her hand, I feel it hit, and a burning pain. I feel myself torn in half, my soul leaves my body again, but this time it’s not a floating sensation, more like a sinking one. I feel my soul wrap itself around the Black Gem, and then we drop to the ground.

My body, oh my poor body, its withering on the ground, shrinking, changing, I don’t recognize it. But I can feel it, trapped. I hop around, I must hurt something…. I must burn things…. I can feel him.. it… my lord, my master. I understand what he wants of me… I will do your bidding master…. I see the two humans, the man, the woman. They turn and look at me, shock on their faces. I cackle with glee. I will show them fire. I will show them pain. They don’t see the huge shambling creature that comes up behind them. They don’t feel the pain as it crushes them to the ground, they are looking at me, and they have water seeping from their eyes. Weak Humans. Then I see the Child Soul, it is looking at the stone on the ground, pretty stone, all white with a black gem in the center. He comes towards me, so I run away. I must get away from it. I run, run towards more fighting. I see them now, humans on horses, they are shiny, and their light hurts. I am trapped. Caught between the Child Soul and the Shiny men. One of the men on a horse points at me; he charges the horse swinging a mighty weapon in his hand. Maybe I can burn him, I throw fire at him, but he comes anyway. I am thrown to the ground. I feel broken. The Child Soul reaches me and bends down to put his hand on my head. “I am sorry” he says. It all goes black.

“Gather to me Paladins of the Holy Light” shouts Gabriel Brightshield. The remaining Paladins quickly gather around Captain Brightshield. “I fear this day is lost, gather the remaining forces and retreat back, the scourge has taken Dalaran.” As the Paladins quickly gather the remaining forces together, one of them finds a young boy, lying close by. “Sir, this boy is still alive, but he is barely breathing.” “Bring him along” shouts Captain Brightshield “but quickly.” The Paladin scoops him up and lays him across the saddle of his horse. They very quickly ride south towards the Alliance Camps.
At the Alliance Camp, Captain Brightshield reports to the General of the Alliance forces about the complete destruction of Dalaran. General Aberdine is not happy with the news. There are also reports from the camp Priest about a strange boy who they can’t seem to heal.
