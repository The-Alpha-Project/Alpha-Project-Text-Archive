To be a good party member you should learn about the basics of good party play.

Pick a leader and stay with your group!
Pick someone to lead the party around. Make sure it's clear to the party that they should be following that specific player. Otherwise people wander all around and it's a mess. They also wake up a bunch of monsters and the party often dies. Things are much simpler if the party just picks someone to lead the group.

People who are not leading should do what the leader says and make sure they are always keeping up. You can type "/f name" to follow a character in your group. This makes it easier to keep up. If you need to leave the keyboard, make sure you inform the group so you're not left behind. While you are behind monsters could spawn or patrol in your path. When you try to return to the group, you could "train" the monsters to the group and kill them. Also the group might not notice that you haven't been keeping up and could die thinking they could win a battle with your help, but you're not there! Remember that you can also use "/afk."

Quests
Make sure everyone has the same quest. You can do this by asking them to confirm the name of the quest in their quest log. Otherwise you might be halfway into a quest before you discover that one of your party members hasn't got the quest.

Stick around until everyone has completed the quest.

Select your quests based on party size. If you can do the quest by yourself, don't waste time doing it in a party. Instead, spend time doing quests that you can't do without a party. You also might want to avoid quests that require each person in the party to collect a lot of parts. For example if the party has 5 members, and you need 20 items for each person, that's 100 items! You can complete that much faster by yourself or with a smaller group.

Some quest items not marked as "Quest Items" can be traded with other members in the party. If you get extra, pass them around.

If you're doing quests that require you to each collect x items, report to the party your status as you go. A good way to do it is to say "4/20". That means you have 4 out of 20 items. That lets people know how far you are and how far you have to go. Also after a party member has completed their collection, it gets easier for the rest of the party. Any items dropped for the person with the complete set will now be available to the rest of the party members that still need it.

If you are doing a multiple part quest and some players are behind, you could help them catch up by doing the earlier steps of the quest. That will allow everyone to be on the same step.

Casters
It's the job of the caster to cast any helpful buffs on the tanks. The caster should also heal (if they have it) the tanks and keep them alive. They should also remove any enemy debuffs casted on the tanks.

Buffs
Ask for Buffs again when they wear off. If you're the person casting the Buffs, ask your party members to let you know when they need/want them again.

Health Bars
Watch the health bars of players and heal anyone who starts to get low. If you have some other ability to save the person being attacked such as tank taunting the enemy, do that instead. It is especially important to save the character who is responsible for healing if they are near death.

Tanks
It is the job of the "Tank" to soak up damage for the party. If the monsters start attacking weaker classes such as Mages, Shamans, or Warlocks, it's the job of the Tank to attack the monster and pull them off the weak casters.

Don't Run!
It is important not to run, even if you're about to die unless the whole party is running. If you run you will likely wake up more monsters or travel to some place where it will be more difficult to resurrect you. Ask for help "get this guy off me" and stand still so that the tanks can come and taunt them off you or kill the monster. Don't run in circles either because the melee players won't be able to reach the monster. Stop attacking the monster so that you won't rack up points in their hate memory/list.

Communication
-Let your party know if you see a monster patrolling near that might attack unexpectedly
-Let your party know if you have to leave your computer
-Let your party know if you are low on mana if you are required to have mana for a battle.
-Let your party know if you spot anything they are looking for such as specific monsters, land marks, or even trade skill nodes (Mining/Herbalism).
-Let your party know if you're about to die and you need healing (if you're unsure if they will heal)
-Let your party know if monsters are attacking you, if you're a caster, if they are not drawing them off you.
-Be reasonable in your communication. You don't need to spam or repeat yourselves or otherwise annoy the players you're asking to help you.

Pay Attention/Keep Up
Don't spend time watching TV, reading, or some other distraction. The game usually requires your full attention.

Don't Wake Up Slept/Disabled Monsters
Players put the monsters out of the battle to make it easier. Don't wake them up. Make sure you say sorry if you do and try to correct this in the future. Casters should also make sure they don't try to sleep monsters that are already being attacked.

Pool Resources
If you gather money together you can buy something good for a party member that will help the party. But this should only be done if you are playing with good friends or guildmates.
