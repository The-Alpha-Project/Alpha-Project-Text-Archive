I only played a mage briefly in EQ, so Akasha correct me if I am wrong here.

Mage Elemental pets are exactlly what they imply... Summoned creatures made of pure elemental energy. Typically there are 4 elements to contend with: Fire, Water, Earth, and Air - though I am not positive which are going to be used in WoW. Each elemental form has its advantages and disadvantages. For example, in EQ, Fire pets had a dmage shield, earth pets had a root spell, water pets I think regened HP more quickly, etc... But these pets also varied in their base stats as well. Some would have more HP than others and some could melee better than others. Each pet was balanced against the others in the Mage arsenal. Clearly the art in playing a mage comes in knowing when to use which pet.

Not knowing the true nature of the mage pets in WoW, I can only speculate, but yeah the Water Elemental is made of water and will have abilities of its own which could make it more, or less, usefull againts particular opponents.

How do mage pets stack against a Warlock's? Unknown... Lots of speculation leads many to believe Warlocks will have very powerful pets. I have not seen any similar speculation about Mage pets. It will depend largely on the different abilities of each pet and even the character class itself.

I expect they will be largely balanced against each other. The designers will balance the Mage class vs. the Warlock class, or vs. any other class for that matter. So perhaps their pet will be weaker, but their magic will be stronger for example.

That's correct on the Mage Pets... as we don't quiet know how mage pets will work in wow I can only tell you what I knew from EQ. I had 4 types of pets... In EQ you got pets from level 4 and every 4 levels after that got upgrades. So from level 4 till 65 you looked at either 1 of 4 : Fire, Water, Earth, Air. Each had their pro's and con's and depending on the situation you would use a different one.

General Run Down

Earth : Earth is the workhorse of the elementals. It sports the most HP and the highest ATK, but the lowest AGI and DEX. From levels 4 to 49 it casts Earth Elemental Attack, a root. At 51 and 57 it casts Earth Elemental Strike, and Rathe's Son casts Earth Elemental Strike 2, both which are root with a DD component. Earth also sports a near-immunity to Disease-based spells.

Water : Below 50, Water is the most well-rounded of the elementals, containing average stats across the board. Above 50 it takes on the characteristics of a rogue, and gains the ability to Backstab from behind, but as a side effect it loses much of its tanking capabilities. Below 50 it casts Water Elemental Attack, and above 50 it casts Water Elemental Strike. Water is nearly immune to poison attacks, making it an excellent pet to use at low levels for this reason.

Air : Below 50, Air is a defensive pet, sporting high AGI but low HP. Below level 50 it casts the stun Air Elemental Attack. Above level 50 its stun Air Elemental Strike gains a DD component. Air also features a near-immunity to cold based attacks.

Fire : Below 50, Fire is a pure offense pet, sporting the DS Fire Elemental Aura and the attack Fire Elemental Attack. Above 50 it becomes a Wizard and casts various spells. The main disadvantage to Fire is its low HP, far below that of other pets. Fire also has a near-immunity to fire-based attacks.


Again, won't really relate to WoW because we don't know how they work, but elemental pets tend to be similar across games. From what I heard (and going by memory here) Mage's wont actually get their pets till a later level.
