A while back, 6 months at least I was working really long nights with only commercial TV... blech! I used to take the laptop along to play Starcraft, but occasionally used it to write stuff... this is one of those stuffs I wrote. Reading Kalazor's story inspired me to put it up... it's only really part of what I have in mind for me Warlock, and at the time I had plans to make an alt rogue.

It's more just a story to read now as although the idea is the same I don't plan on having a rogue until a long time into the game, but the nature of Akasha in this story is pretty much what I'm going to be like anyway....

Ok, shutting up now, just read


Stretching her arms high into the air, Akasha breaths in the early morning mist. Surprised she was managed to sleep at all, she longs for the feeling of clean sheets and a comfortable bed. It had been 10 long days since the last time she was around anything resembling creature comforts, but soon, it would be over. She was within 2 days walk of the camp to rescue Piper, her rogue trainee. 5 years ago she met Piper’s parents, in a fierce battle when the town she was visiting was over run by blood thirsty Horde. Would have been quiet happy to have kept moving on her way, but a woman’s scream close by made her instincts kick in and she ran headlong into the battle. It was a young woman holding a small child, “Save her, please!” Akasha outstretched her arm and took the small hand of the child. The woman smiled, thankfulness in her eyes, then turned with a roar and ran into the mist of swords and spells, never to be seen again.

Akasha had made it out to the woods safely and decided that any hope for the small child’s parents were gone with the loud explosions she could hear. She set the small child on the ground and kneeled beside her. She was a pale girl, about the age of 5 or 6, torn leather outfit with a small chain linked vest. Akasha looked at her puzzled, curious at how chain links could be comfortable. Akasha was used to flowing velvet and silk robes, heavy purple velvet with golden silk trim, a large hood to pull up against the weather….

Hearing a twig snap behind her, Akasha was snapped back to the reality of day, a long walk on tired legs, and she needed to find breakfast first. Listening carefully she could hear water, a river perhaps, “Rivers have fish” she thought. Heading south, she headed towards the sound of trickling water, stopping just before the shore, she set up camp.

Looking up at the sky, the heat of the day beat down on her face; the only thing keeping her cool was the darkly covered heavy robes as they worked like an insulator keeping the coolness in and the heat out. “Once I reach the planes I can summon Claticus, my steed.” Walking along the shore of the lake would make it too hard to; Akasha touching her face, felt the scrapes of the last trip she made along shore on horse back with low hanging branches.

Coming to a clearing, she looked out on the open planes and rested, leaning on her staff for support. Summoning Claticus, Akasha mounted her faithful steed. The never ending planes were made short work with a gentle trot and before she knew it Akasha had reached the edges of the small village, the memories came flooding back. The death, the destruction, the loss.

Feeling a gentle touch against her wrist she looked down, seeing the Dwarf Paladin, Akasha smiled, “Bringron, it has been a long time”, she beat down to embrace her long time friend.

“Was it a long journey my dear?”

“12 Days, but I wouldn’t want her traveling any further alone.”

Bringron smiled, this way my dear, she is tired but safe. She’s had a long trip, and for a child of her age, I am sure she was frightened, be gentle on her. Akasha forced a smile and grunted an answer. Walking thru the large wooden door, there was Piper, she looked well, but the look on her face showed a maturity Akasha had not seen before. Walking across the wooden floor Akasha sat down opposite the young rogue, “Are you ok?”

“Yes, I am sorry, I never meant to cause all this trouble, I just had to know where I came from, I have all these…”

Piper was cut short, as Akasha stood abruptly and started yelling, “How could you do this to me?! You had me worried sick, do you know what I have been through trying to get here, it is not a safe path to get here you know. God what where you thinking?! You could have been kidnapped or worse KILLED trying to get here! Tell me what you where thinking!!” There was a few moments silence before Akasha screamed, “TELL ME!”

“I wanted to see my parents,” Piper said quietly looking down at her feet.

Akasha sat down slowly, looking at the child that grew up without her realizing. Taking Piper’s hands in hers, she looked at the tears streaming down. Sighing gently she asked, “Have you seen them yet?

Piper looked up; looking into her eyes she shook her head. “Bringron told me it would be best to wait till you got here. He took me in, looked after me, fed me, but he felt it would be best if you showed me where they lay.” Akasha looked over at her long time Paladin friend and gave a thankful smile. “Well then, are you ready?” Piper said yes through watery eyes.

Rising to her feet, she put her arm around piper; only know noticing that she was almost as tall as her. Heading out onto the street, the made their way through the main part of town. As they walked, Akasha relived all that she could remember about that night 5 years ago, stopping at the side street where she met Piper’s mother. Heading up the grass hill, both stopped at the entrance to the cemetery, “Are you sure you want to do this now?” Akasha asked.

“I have waited a long time for this, but I want to do it alone.” Akasha felt a stab of pain as she heard those words, but nodded with a gentle smile knowing this was something Piper had to do alone, “I’ll be right here”, she said as she sat down on a large stone.

Later that night, after dinner and drinks, Akasha was sitting opposite Bringron, looking at her odd little friend. “You know Akasha”, he said “It is very rare these days you meet a Warlock of your caliber, you are an extremely powerful warlock, how to you remain to keep your head straight and free from evil?”

Shaking her head slowly Akasha looked lost for words, “Honestly? I do not know, maybe it is the positive influence from knowing you for so long. You took me in as a child and raised me; it was your influence that even made me stop 5 years ago when I heard a woman yell for help. But that night changed my life, I know have another to look after, and a Night Elf of all things. I couldn’t save her, and then turn my back on her in the middle of the woods.”

“Why not? Most other Warlocks would have.” Akasha looked puzzled, “Where is this coming from?” she asked. Bringron looked at Akasha as if he was seeking something inside her, “Have you talked to Piper about why she risked everything to come out here?”

“Yes”, Akasha said sternly, “We talked this afternoon, she wanted to see her parents, and she needed closure.” Bringron shook his head slowly, “Listen to her Akasha, listen to what she seeks from you. The reason she came this far is to ask her parents to tell you she is strong enough to fight along side you. She told me that you leave her behind when ever you go out, leaving her with the Inn Keepers.”

“Well yes, but what do you expect me to do? She is barely over childhood, she does not know her skills, her abilities, she could get killed out there, we both could.” Looking at Bringron, she could tell there was more, “Tell me, we have not been friends for this long and been through this much for you to hold out on me now.”

“She is more ready than you know Akasha, she has been training with other Rogues in your area, she is getting quite good at her talents. If you don’t take her out with you to fight, she will venture out on her own, and she will continue to do this until she proves her worth to you. She desperately seeks your approval Akasha; to her you are the only mother figure she knows.” Bringron paused before continuing, “She knows all about you, between the Inn Keepers you trust to baby-sit her, and the drunks that drink there, she knows you are the most powerful Warlock known to these lands. She knows everything, and she wants you to teach her.”

Akasha cut the Paladin off, “What do I know about a Rogue? How can I teach her?” Bringron smiled, “learn from her, she knows her abilities, she just needs your help to enhance them and turn her into the powerful rogue you and I both know she can be. You know”, he paused, “it is rumored that Piper is going to be the stealthiest rogue these times have seen, even better than…” he was cut off. “Don’t you dare say his name! You know that when I put that part of my past behind me, his name went with it.” Bringron nodded.

“Get some sleep my child; a rest will do you good.” Akasha nodded, grabbing her cloak, she made her way to the back room she spent her childhood in.

After spending two days resting and getting to know the rogue that grew up before her eyes, she decided it was time to go. Sitting on her bed, elbows resting on knees, head hanging, she prayed for the first time in many years. Asking for the strength to remember that Piper is no longer a child, and she is coming into her own, a strong, stealthy rogue she prayed for the power to train her correctly in a field she was just learning about.

Rising early in the morning, Piper was already dressed and packed before dawn, “She’s ready,” Bringron said. Nodding Akasha motioned outside. Exiting the small cottage, she instantly noticed the two horses tied to the pole out-front, looking back she gave the Paladin a puzzling look, “you…?” Bringron nodded, “I bought them this morning, they will take you as far as you need to go and leave them with another stable hand.”

Thanking her friend, she gave him a small kiss on the cheek, “You are too kind”, mounting the large black stallion, Akasha resisted the urge to grab Piper’s arm as she didn’t quiet make it up on the first try. Second attempt and they were both mounted. Smiling one last time, she waved goodbye and started her long journey home.

Bringron smiled thinking to himself, “This is where the real adventures will begin, for side by side they will become the most forceful source to wreakon with. I feel for any foe that crosses their path.”

And with that, the adventure begins….
