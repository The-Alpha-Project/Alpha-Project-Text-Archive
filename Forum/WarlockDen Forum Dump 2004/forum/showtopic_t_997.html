A long time ago, way back when I first starting playing Everquest (EQ), I was completely oblivious to what a raiding guild was. I started playing back before the Kunark expansion came out, back when Black Burrow was group central and only the bravest of groups ventured too far out in Everfrost. Sitting with your back to the wall, the great white beyond staring at you, the group would send the brave puller out to snatch up a mob and pray for his safe return. The first time you saw someone actually running across the middle of the zone you could here gasps from across the zone. This was normal, everyday life for me, this naïve-ness continued on through my level’s, and when Kunark came out the spiders in Firiona Vai (FV) were the scariest thing I had ever seen. The first time I ran past those, got rooted and smacked down to my birthday suit was the day that forever implemented my fear of those buggers. To this day, 65 level’s later I still run through there with vivid memories of that day… and the fear of those spiders.

I was playing a Ranger at the time, fighting in ‘Frontier Mountains’, when I decided that I wanted to give a Druid a go, oh yeah, and the boyfriend finally got me my own account and booted me off of his. It wasn’t a huge problem restarting, after all I was only level 18 at the time, and had around 800 plat in the bank; I was rich! Ok, stop laughing, I realize now that it’s not a lot of money, but back then I could make people envious, we’ll just ignore the fact that now my boyfriend has around half a million plat… moving on shall we.

So I restart as a hot little wood elf called Tiigger, and off I went back into the world again. Only this time I’m not starting in Qeynos, the area I know, oh no, I’m starting in Kelethin, home of the let’s kill a newbie starting area. For those unfamiliar, Kelethin is a city in the tree’s, platforms and bridges connect shops, bank and your trainers. I learnt quickly that the easiest way was straight down… not the safest, and I never usually lived mind you, but still, the quickest.

By now I’m feeling fairly sure of myself, those spiders in FV still scare me, but I manage to get back to Lake of Ill Omen, level up, and then, the moment of greatness happened! Ding level 20 – give me a surname! I had never thought of my surname before, and it came to me rather quickly, but from that day on I was known as Tiigger Bouncealot. Oh what a glorious day!

It’s amazing now, when I look back on that, to remember the excitement and accomplishment of just getting to level 20 and getting a surname. I never knew that would be the start of many exciting things to come. My first time porting someone else, my first group buff, the first time I ran across Dreadlands, managed to get to the middle of the zone before I got so scared I had to sit down, mem gate and get the hell out of there. I was in a nice friendly little guild that constantly chatted, and even promoted me to Officer. I know... what were they thinking?! That was quickly becoming not enough for me though, I had become the highest level in the guild, 51 at the time, and as there were only 3 of us at the top, it was a very lonely place. I started looking for more.

Leveling in Dreadlands one day, yes I overcame my fear of that zone… not as scarey when the fog clears, I see in /shout and /ooc a word I didn’t understand. Talisman! It was being said over and over again by lots of people. Curious I did a /who to see who was in the zone, only to realize these people shouting it had the same thing at the end of their name, their guild tag. First thing I noticed was that there was a lot of them, more than the simple 20 or so in my guild, there must have been at least 50! Second thing I noticed was their level’s, if I thought I was high level before, I was quickly corrected.

After some very noob enquiries I soon learnt that Talisman is what they called a ‘Raiding Guild’ and they were capable of killing that annoying dragon Gorenaire who I had grown to loath because of the scars left on me. What happened between the level’s of 51 and 58 was a bit of a blur to me, but I remember meeting Ggak and Jeana who were both in Talisman, and of all things, lived in Australia! I learnt what it was to be in a Raiding guild from Ggak, and I wanted in! I pushed to make max level, including spending an entire weekend on hardly any sleep pushing myself to 60 so I could join. I remember the first time I got to raid with them, and when the “Gendal invites you to join Talisman” box popped up on my screen I nearly squealed with delight.

I had met quiet a few Talisman people while I was trying to get approved to join, some of which had become good friends. Sadly though I couldn’t be permanent until I received a certain amount of votes, but to me it just meant I got to see the invite box more often as I had to be guilded to raid… how else would I see what was going on lol? The first time I applied I didn’t make the cut, second time around and fail again, by this time I was ready to give up. Kaitlynn, a Paladin in the guild was my strongest supporter, she listened to me complain, and say “whyyy? Doesn’t anybody love me?” just like Sid the Sloth from Ice Age. Try number three however, boom baby I was in! My life was about to change….

Monday to Saturday I got up at 7am every morning to start raiding, being in an American guild while living in Australia was tough, and trust me, there were some mornings I am glad they couldn’t hear the things coming out of my mouth. Winter was the hardest time, no one wants to get out of their warm bed when it’s cold outside, let alone to get up for a game. The plus side however, I got to see more parts of the game than I could have ever imagined. I got to wear armor that I could only dream of, and it was then I learnt what it felt like to be ‘Uber!’ At the time, Talisman was #1 on the server and the first time I had people coming up to say hello purely because of my guild tag, I felt like a famous person in normal-ville.

I left Talisman due to personal reasons, a guild member that needed to have her head shoved in a vice, and I got a day time job, gone were the days of raiding between 8am - 2pm. I spent almost two years with that guild and loved every minute of it, that’s why, after a break I felt the need to find another raiding guild, one close to my time zone. Because of the sudden stop in raiding I found myself bored and making lots of alts, so this time around I was a cleric and got to see raiding from a different point of view. That guild broke up, moved servers and I got bored again. I started up my mage and was actually having fun not being part of a guild, then I started to get friendly with the boyfriends guild members and that itch to raid had come back…. time for guild number three.

All in all I raided over half of my EQ life, and I would have to say that most, if not all of my best EQ memories revolved around a raid somehow. I learnt a lot about myself, the game and how to play my character. I have made game long friends, some of which I still talk to today. In EQ I went through a total of 4 guilds, but I came out with the knowledge of knowing exactly what I want in my next one, which at this stage will be in World of Warcraft (WoW).
