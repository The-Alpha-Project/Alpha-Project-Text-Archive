As you all know (or do if you check these forums much), I've spent a lot of time the last ten days testing Warlocks for bugs and for data instead of just "playing" the game.

I've finally got a way, I think, to discuss and explain (as well as help answer) some of the basic Warlock questions people ask like "What's the best Pet?"

It all comes down to this:

Are you a Fastlock or a Slowlock?

Fastlock: A Fastlock plays his Warlock and picks his talents with an eye towards front-loading damage and loading it fast. A Fastlock, however, is *not* a Mage or a Mage-wannabe--our skills and talents themselves allow him to significantly differentiate himself from this class.

Fastlocks will tend to prefer a Succubus or Imp for a Pet. Long fights generally are thought to equal inefficient fights. Fastlocks that use the Succubus primarily are more likely to find investment into Dark Pact (and using her as a mana battery to be a good idea). Fastlocks who go Imp may want to emphasize intellect more for this reason. All Fastlocks hit stamina-gathering heavily--with such lousy armor, HP is our first, last, and best line of protection.

The disadvantage to being a Fastlock is that many of our "faster" spells aren't very efficient in terms of mana. In PvP however, Fastlock is the *ONLY* way to go.

Slowlock: Slowlocks (of which I am one) prefer the Felhunter or the Voidwalker for a Pet. Slowlocks will use longer DoT's more than Fastlocks, and will deal damage more slowly (hence the term). Because the Voidwalker is required to hold aggro, Slowlocks tend to have casting patterns--you develop a "feel" for how much damage the Voidwalker can take.

Skills like "Improved Health Funnel" might actually be quite useful to a Slowlock; a Fastlock isn't interested.

These terms are only conceptual, there are only a handful of "true" Slowlocks or Fastlocks. The question to ask yourself when wondering what skills to take or what talents to test is whether or not you want to tank for your Pets and use them to add damage / mez...or do you want your Pets to take, while you hang back and judiciously apply DoT's.

The reason that many Warlocks in CB are Slowlocks is because for a long time (pre-talents) this was our only viable option. We were repeatedly shredded if we didn't carefully manage aggro. Warlocks on th PvP server were either gimped or Gods, depending on whether or not they landed Fear.

So keep this in mind as you evaluate what you want to do with your Warlock. Are you a Slowlock or a Fastlock? It should help.

I came up with "Fastlock" vs. "Slowlock" mainly to help warlocks decide what Pets would be best for them. The Imp and Succubus are Fastlock Pets, the Voidwalker and Felhunter are more Slowlock oriented (the Voidwalker *is* the quintissential Slowlock Pet).

This categorization is NOT meant to divide Warlocks into two camps so much as it is to provide two theoretical "end points" from which to make decisions. Obviously the majority of folks will change their playing style depending on what is going on.
