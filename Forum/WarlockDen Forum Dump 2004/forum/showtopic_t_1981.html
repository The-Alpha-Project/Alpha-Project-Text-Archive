By Zyphanta on the beta boards.... wondering your thoughts?

Right now, they are probably the worst.

Here is my logic. There are 4 factors that determine how good a class is-

1- survivability, this is armor and hp for the most part

2- healing capability

3- extra abilities that help but don't directly help combat

4- damage

As far as survivability, warlocks are the worst of any class. Mages might have slightly lower hp, but frost armor and polymorph are much stronger defensive abilities than anything a warlock has available.

Healing- basicly none. We can make healthstones, which is nice, but it's not really a heal because with the cooldown and casting time involved it's just a oneshot deal. Any class can carry potions and have about the same ability.

Extra abilities- soulstones, ritual of summoning. Soulstones are very nice, but nothing compares to just having res. Also, the way they work they donm't actually encourage a group to bring along a warlock, they just encourage the group to buy a few soulstones before entering the instance. Ritual of summoning is a nice unique abaility but again something that doesn't get a warlock into a group.

Damage- it's all that is left, isn't it? Rogues, mages, and IMO Warlocks are basicly just damage classes. Rogues should have the lowest average DPS, because they don't rely on mana, and they have better survivability compared to mages and warlocks. That much should be obvious. That just leaves mages and warlocks.

Why should warlocks have more damage than mages?

Micro managment.

Dealing max damage as a mage is simple. Target and fire. Nothing to it. Maximixing your damage as a warlock is not simple at all. You need to stack multiple DOTs, make sure the pet is attacking, micro manage the pet abilities, hit with a wand, cast DOTs on other mobs, cast soul drain at just the right moment- all in all warlocks require constant clicking to deal effective damage- given all that work they SHOULD have the highest potential damage of any class.

I see Mages as burst damage as opposed to Warlocks being mana efficient.

At a quick computation using lvl 60 abilities a Warlock using Immolate, Cure of Agony, Lifetap x2, Drain Life x2, then recasting Immolate can do 3889 dmg in 33 seconds, at the cost of 913 mana and 60 health, approximately 4.25 dmg per mana.

In those same 33 seconds using Pyroblast and then chaining Frostbolt, a mage can do 6251 dmg, but at the cost of 3300 mana equating 1.89 damage per mana.

A fight lasting 60 seconds or more, the warlock can add Curse of Doom to his lineup doing a total of 9574 dmg at the cost of 2126 mana reaching 4.5 dmg per mana.

In those same 60 seconds, with Pyroblast having a 60 second cooldown, a mages dmg would total 11,344 at the cost of 6160 mana dropping to 1.84 dmg per mana. This is, of course if the mage has a 6160 mana pool.

Any encounters lasting longer than a few minutes, both classes dps and mana efficiency would lower, but a mages more significantly as a warlock would have drain mana, soul pact, lifetap, healthstones, health and mana potions at their disposal opposed to a mage only having mana potions. This does give warlocks a higher dmg potential than mages.

In summary, a mage will be able to do more dmg in a shorter period of time, while a warlock will be be able to more efficiently sustain a higher maximum dmg in longer fights.

If future high end content includes varying encounters with AEs, marathon fights vs high hp mobs, and high dps mobs with low hps, both classes will have their day to shine.

Just my 2 cps worth.
