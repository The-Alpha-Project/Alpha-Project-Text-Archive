The tradeskills in the game pretty much follow the class. They do not have to, but the advantage that you get in making your own armor is huge, I believe.

In this way, limiting to two primary tradeskills...and really one, considering that you would not be a very successful smith without mining or leatherworker without skinning...does kind of pidgeonhole characters.

I would like to see them come up with a dozen more secondary tradeskills like the cooking, first aid and fishing. They could be really minor and insignificant...and limit us to 5 or 7 of them...something like that. But it could be interesting anyway, and give characters a chance to be a bit more diverse. (Like having a level 40 Dwarf Pally, who is a master miner, smith and basketweaver...lol)

But really, the tradeskills that are present are cool because they directly support your character. In so many other games, skipping tradeskills altogether was ok and actually quite common...you did not need to make stuff for yourself...all could be bought for less than it cost to get a tradeskill started.

I plan to do most if not all professions. That’s just the way I am. I’m still fine tuning which characters will do which crafts.

My dwarven cleric had tailoring and skinning. I chose tailoring for armor and bags and skinning or some quick money. Later on I may drop skinning for enchanting.

My human paladin had mining and smithing. But I may give that to my warrior and take mining and engineering for my paladin so that he’ll have a ranged attack.

My human rogue had mining and skinning. I figured he would have the best chance to sneak into dangerous areas with nodes and with the speed that he can mow through animals he can quickly gather skins. These items can either be sold or given to my other characters for their professions.

Herbalism and alchemy just seemed natural for my dwarven mage. Of course now that Blizzard has murdered all dwarven mages, I’ll have to decide whether to go human or gnome.

My night elf hunter will be my leather worker and he’ll most likely also have skinning.

I think those are the only characters I’ll be playing on the alliance side. At some point in time I’ll create hoard characters and get to do all the trade skills over again.

Here’s something to consider for your first character if you want to make a bit of money starting out. You may want to take up Herbalism and skinning even if you don’t plan to use those professions later on. I’ve found that selling hides and herbs, even to NPC vendors, is a good way to make some quick money. These skills take no money to train nor do you have to buy any recipes. The only cost you’ll be out is the cost of the skinning knife. So dropping the skills later won’t break you. The only drawback is that you’ll be starting your chose professions a bit later.

The problem with instanced housing is that it doesn't support the economy.

Why spend money decorating it if no one else can see it.

That was the problem with FFXI's housing. No one could see how you decorate your house, so why bother buying furniture to decorate with? So FFXI had to add in a furniture enhancement feature to get people to even buy furniture.


If people can see it and view it and walk thru it, you're always going to be striving for the bigger and better house. Which helps the economy.

Sierra's "The Realm" mmorpg had the best example of instanced housing I have seen yet.

You went to a booth at the corner of town and clicked an NPC who asked who's home you wanted to visit and it let you type in a name. It then teleported you to the front yard of that instanced house and you could knock and wait to be let in, or if you knew the password you could walk on in. Inside was a chest that was secured with a second password so you could allow friends into your house and not have to worry about them stealing your stored loot.

They could drop items on the floor for you to pick up later or for other people who knew the password could pick up. If I recall correctly, you could leave the front door unlocked so anyone who knew your name could wander into your home to check it out or say Hi.
