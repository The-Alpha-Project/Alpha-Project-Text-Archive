Personally I have a hard time with this question. I've done encounters that others can't do and I've seen some go at encounters I wouldn't really like to try.

It's true pet classes seem to have a major advantage but that is a surface appearance dependant upon who's playing them.

I played a Druid in Open Beta. I saw a 22nd level Hunter get run off by a couple spiders in a path I was running by. I stoped... Looked the situation over, buffed up and went for it and beat them both.

I was 19th level and took out 2 spiders that chewed up that "duo" of 22nd level hunter and pet...

I've taken out mobs even level with pets of their same level - hard yes but not impossible nor even a major event besides recovering afterwards.

I don't know... I guess it can be that 2 vs 1 should work better but I still don't see a hunter nuking, doting, going bear form to melee for a bit using swipe and roar, popping back to human form, clicking a healing potion, tossing regen and regrowth on themselves, flipping back to bear -- 2 times through this cycle and finishing off 2 mobs 2 levels higher than them the way I did it.

I've taken down up to 5 mobs 3 levels lower than me in 1 fight as a druid and trust me, druids are NOT rated the master soloists in the game -- Warlocks and Hunters are rated better at this.

I've seen a priest take out 4 ghosts at 1 time that I wouldn't like to try and a warrior my level solo out a mob I wouldn't have really wanted to try at all (and I'm a bit on the "hell, it's only a CR away" type of person).

Your skill with your char and your style of play will make or break you no matter what class you chose to play and what you go after, monster wise, will also determine how you face the encounter.

I serously doubt that you'll find many hunters rushing into situations where melee debuffs and dots are heavily being used but you'll find casters doing these with fair frequency and ease -- something about 10 to 20 min of reduced armor, slowed attacks, etc... tends to take a lot of the "I can do it!" out of 'em for some odd reason.

Best bet, pick a class you like that'll match your style of play and go for it. There really is no "best class" out there. Just some have advantages in certain situations whereas others are better of in different ones.
