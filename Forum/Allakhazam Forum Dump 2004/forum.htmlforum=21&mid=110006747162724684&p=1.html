It is a "each to his own" thing. All three games are very different. Maybe FFXI and EQII are the most alike. You can not argue that WoW is not cassual friendly because jsut about anybody can solo at a respectable rate. EQII soloiung is viable for most professions. Finally FFXI has a slim to none approach on soloing.

FFXI is built as a community game, more so then any other. They want you to get with 12-18 other people to do what is "standard" etc. Remember, FFXI was made by Japanese, their community as a who tends to like huge group activies.

If you like playing with people, and having alot of flexability in what you bring to the party - the you more then likely love FFXI. Even the economy is commnity based. It's not about you getting to level XX on craft XXXX...its about what you are buying and selling to help others...and the side benifit is you advance.

EQII pigeon holes you. There is little flex to each profession. You will excell at one thing and suck at most others. Some people will like this, others won't.

WoW is cassualy great, even better when you got some friends to play it with. But it won't last with the hardcore players in my opinion. When people acn max thier characters out in less then a month or so with "average" MMORPG play time...there simply isn't enough time sinks.

And that's really what WoW lacks (intentional or not) is time sinks. Some people hate them, other like them.

So to each thier own. I will play a bit of WoW cause I like elements of it, it will be good for stepping away from all the million of things I need to do in FFXI. But FFXI will remain my main - WoW just didn't do it for me enough to move over full-time.
