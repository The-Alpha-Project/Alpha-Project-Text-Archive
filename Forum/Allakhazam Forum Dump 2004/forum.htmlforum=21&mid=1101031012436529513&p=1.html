How big do you honestly think this game will be? I've checked out this site in the past and it seems to be fairly reliable with regards to past games:

http://pw1.netcom.com/~sirbruce/Subscriptions.html

I'm curious if people think WoW will hit L1/L2 type numbers world-wide. Hell, there were over 500k NA/AUS players in open beta, which is more people than EQ1 has ever had - and just from NA/AUS. Couple that with the fact that releasing a Blizzard game in Korea is like throwing chum in shark-infested waters, and I'm almost convinced that this will be the largest MMO in history.

The folks over at Blizzard had to know something was in the air, or we never would have seen an open beta this huge. Testing the servers with a half-million worth of capacity would be pointless if they expected a much smaller turn-out. Also, Tyren has mentioned before that there are enough first-run copies of WoW that, if they sell out, it will immediately become the largest MMO in NA.

The numbers for L1/L2 on the chart above are kind of screwy, if I remember what I read correctly. That huge number of players had to do with something involving internet cafes and weird pay scheduling. Like for instance, the cafe pays for all the accounts and people pay hourly inside the cafe to get access, so I'm not sure how that works since I'm not Korean. Basically the gist was not all of those are "honest" subscriptions, meaning they didn't buy a copy of the game and pay the subscription themself.

So let's throw out some numbers here from our asses. I'm going to say about 750,000 world-wide subscribers by the end of this year, crossing the million-mark after further releases into European markets. But depending on how crazy the Koreans get, the million mark may be broken before the end of this year. I really think that it's not so much a question of if it will hit a million, but rather when. Of course I'm making this up as I go along, so I could be totally off-base. Then again we've been WoW-less for a few days now, and I need to kill time somehow.

Let the baseless speculation begin!

One played open beta and has pre-ordered the game. One played open beta and has pre-ordered two copies of the game. One played open beta and isn’t buying the game.
