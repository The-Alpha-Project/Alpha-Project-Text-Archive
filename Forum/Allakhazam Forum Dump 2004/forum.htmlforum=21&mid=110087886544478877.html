I have to disagree with your analysis, lhuffman. You are working under the assumption anyone who gets the game on day 1 will start playing at the earliest possible minute, will play quickly and efficiently, and will bust their butt to get out of the first zone. I see it as more likely that people will have difficulties logging in for the first time and setting up their accounts, etc., after the time delay it takes them to actually go pick up their game and mull over the stuff in the box.

Then they will get started, amid some competition. Supposing even moderate competition, it will take them close about 90 minutes to leave the immediate starting area. The best of players probably takes over 3 hours to get through the entire first zone normally, if they rush and skip content. Most players will gravitate more toward a 5-6 hour time block if they know how to play, 8-10 if they don't. Add in competition that will be increasing as each of your social brackets starts logging in, and you're going to see a huge glut in Teldrassil, Elwynn, Durotar, Mulgore, Dun Morogh, and Tirisfal.

For anyone that DOES manage to get to the second zone, it will be sheer bliss by comparison, but those people will probably have a difficult time of it because they left the zone too early. Others will have run to Ironforge to get their guilds together. Some will have taken time to start all their characters to "lock" the names they want. Any number of things can delay a person who isn't singularly motivated to getting out of that newbie zone against all odds.

And the odds will be against them. Say what you will about the increased spawn rates. It will still be hell. Except for very off-peak hours, it will continue to be throughout the weekend. The only thing that will help this at all is, ironically, the holiday itself will force many people out for a day. College students will likely have travelled home and might not have the means to play from there if they leave their computer at school.

The price tag will not deter most people. A person interested in this game is either going to get it now or not get it until much later. For every person who was in OB and won't be playing, there will be someone else who will be that wasn't. It's just a question of how many get started on day one, wait for the weekend, or wait until Christmas. The serious players, the ones who will care about the spawn competition rather than taking time to fool around idly, will all be day one players.
