Simply put it's Blizzard's game but -- here's a bit of info for you to look at... Things aren't always as simple as they seem and international trade is one hazzy area of law and governance and penalties that gets sticky REAL fast.

First off there are stringent guidelines that must be met to distribute software of any nature internationally so international distribution will take a bit of time to arrange.

An initial release of a product is not the end-all release. They need to get it out to folks and then see about distributing it to others later on "expanding into" other markets. How can they help you with say -- connection issues selling it to you if you are overseas? "you sold it to me, now support it! it's running poorly!!!" Ummm...

Offering services internationally does not remove the tax obligations to a company. They'll need to deal with the governments in the various regions of the world to be able to offer their products in those areas and charge appropirately based upon where you are being billed from. If they don't meet the business tax obligations then they are legally responsible for their actions and any associated penalties.

If, on the other hand, you "steal" from your government by bypassing their import/export and service/product consumption laws, that's your issue and NOT Blizzards -- they acted in good faith, YOU violated any laws your government may have set up and are liable for YOUR actions -- not them.

CYOA means Cover Your Own *** -- that's the first step you deal with. You make sure you have the proper support network setup and proper documents layed out with whatever legal entities are out there before trying to do business in those regions. If you don't have full understanding of the laws and restrictions in an area, block it from your list for distribution until you DO have all the info.

Anti-overseas isn't due to simply "we don't like non-North Americans" but often due to business constraints and an ability to make decent offerings to consumers.

There are only so many resources at Blizzards disposal so there is only so much they can try and cover when they release their product for the first time. Cut them some slack to recoupe this 2+ year investment with 0 profits showing until they do recoupe and then show profits sufficient to expand their offerings.

Sorry for your "delay" bud but if I'm not in a region of the world where something is offered, I'll need to wait usually until it gets to me or... figure out a way around the constraints and then keep my mouth shut about what I did and/or any problems I hit that wouldn't be found by those in that area.
