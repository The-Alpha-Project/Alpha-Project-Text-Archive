Okay... Those of you who are condescending over the strategy guide users:

Get off your high horse. If you prefer to "figure it out for yourself" or to "look it up on the net" that's great. However, quite a few of your are being arrogant about it.

First of all - the two reasons contradict each other. Looking things up on the net is not figuring it out for yourself.

Second of all - even if you only do one of the two (I.e. you don't cotradict yourself) there are plenty of good reasons to use a strategy guide.

I used a strategy guide for FFXI for the entire time I played. The guide had all sorts of misinformation, was missing all sorts of information, but nevertheless, it was a reference.

I could refer to it for spell lists, maps, info on some low-level quests and missions, NPC locations, Skillchain, weaponskills, etc. etc.

Now - I have a trusty laptop that sits next to me while I'm playing - but there is still something to be said for a handy book that is there whenever you need it.

Those of you who choose to be without a guide - that's great. But please don't condescend towards those who do.

Not sure why the bashing of users who opt for guides...

I have 5 days to wait for the game and I want to know as much as I can to pick the class that's best suited for me so I need lots of information.

The user guide is 431 pages, all color, and talks about every element of the game. It is one of the MOST comprehensive guides I have ever seen for a game, period!
