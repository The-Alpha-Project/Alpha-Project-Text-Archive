PvP could go any way with any classes. There is no one end all class in PvP. The one class considered most over the top is Shaman, and from what I hear it does not do well in solo pvp.

Honestly, it is all a matter of playstyle. Anything with range or stealth (Mage, Hunter, Rogue) will be at a bit of an advantage in terms of being able to choose your battles. They are also high damage output classes, meaning if you get the drop on a soft target you can likely take them out before they can react well. However, they are also soft targets themselves, so if you get surprised as one of those classes you might buy it really quick.

So, all in all, pick whatever you want and make it work for pvp.

as a druid i had problems with warriors most, followed by shamans and rogues. that said, a good entangling roots/starfire combo will quickly dissassemble an unskilled warrior or rogue. entangling roots will always keep a hunters pet busy while you finish off the hunter. The roots never work on a good druid or shaman, though, as shapeshifting frees you from all binding effects/spells.

i agree that shaman have it pretty easy when it comes to escaping. the one exception i can think of is when the shaman plops down their earth totem and shifts to wolf form to beat a hasty retreat: the cost of stopping them with roots and forcing them to shift twice (once back to natural form, then again back to wolf) far outweighs the costs in time and mana it takes to catch up on the shammy and cast another root.

if a rogue sapped me, i was toast, but that goes w/o saying. anything that deals a lot of damage could trouce me in a few hits, but only if they could stay close to me and maintain a high dps at range. warlocks, mages and priests could get off a few spells, but i rarely lost to another caster of comparable level.

i find that no strategy is flawless, and a good player was always resourceful enough to figure out how to escape, kill me, or deal enough damage that i'd seriously give pause before chasing them again.
