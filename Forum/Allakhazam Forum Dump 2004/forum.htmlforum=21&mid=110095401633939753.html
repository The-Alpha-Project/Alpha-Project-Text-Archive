Quote:
Keep in mind, it's a playstyle thing. Do you want to get up in someone's face and slice-and-dice, or do you want to hang back and shoot them to death?


I think that sums it up best. But I’ll blather on anyway.

I feel Blizzard has made every class fun to play. I never would have thought it possible, but there it is. I’ve played every class to some extent and I’ve found that I would be happy playing any of them as my main. As incredible as this is, it also ticks me off as I’m having a very hard time deciding which class to play first. But I digress.

The hunter is a fun class to play if you enjoy pet classes. There are an incredible variety of beasts you can choose to be your companion. Plus you can teach your pet attacks from other types of beasts. As a pet class you’ll never truly be alone. You’ll always have a companion at your side and someone to watch your back. What this also means is that your power as a character is split in two. Your pet is meant to take most of the damage and you’re meant to do most of your damage from range. You can use melee weapons and even duel wield at level 20. But you don’t get the support skills to back up your melee attacks. So with the right equipment you should always be doing your best damage from range.

Rogues are great class to pay if you like kicking butt. They are the up close and personal damage dealer. At level ten when hunters get their first pet, rogues get duel wield and a skill called Slice & Dice. If you use your skills correctly you can chop a mob to pieces in short order. Rogues also come with stealth, pick pockets and a kick skill that’s great for interrupting spells or, as I found out a few days ago, interrupting the breath weapon of a dragon whelp. If you enjoy rushing into a fight, or sneaking up into one, I’m sure you’ll enjoy playing a rogue. And with talents you can specialize in many different ways, including either staying in the mob’s face or sneaking behind and backstabbing them.

Both the hunter and the rogue are well balanced characters. And both have advantages over the other. The hunter’s pet can off tank an add and a rogue can stealth through some areas without having to fight to get where he’s going. So in the end it’s really a question of play style. Do you want to be mostly a range combatant and have a loyal companion or would you prefer to fight up close and personal like a living Cuisinart?
