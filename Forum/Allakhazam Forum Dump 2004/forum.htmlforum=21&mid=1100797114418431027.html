There is a long road running across Duskwood, east-to-west. There is a fork to the south about halfway across the zone that leads to Stranglethorn, and that road twists and winds its way all the way to Stranglethorn.

If you decide to swim from Westfall, you should be alright so long as you STAY in the water. There are some crocolisks in the shallows, but if you feel like swimming the entire length of Stranglethorn, you can go all the way down and around without any real difficulty. Just stay close enough to land to not get fatigued. (Warning, this will take a while to do.)

Its easier to run through ashenvale at 18, then to barrens, then down to ratchet, then take the boat to booty bay then it is to run south through SV.

there are also several reasons to go there pre 35:

1) you can get quests there starting around level 32 or so which are level appropriate and done mostly in northern stranglethorn.

2) you can buy a cockatiel or senegal parrot.

3) you can buy the expert fishing book for one gold, if that is the next step.

I went there long before 35. I was starting my stranglethorn vale quests at level 31.

Some quests I got from thottbot quick:
--------------
Supply and Demand

Level 31 Stranglethorn Vale
Obtained at level 26 Horde Alliance

Bring 2 Large River Crocolisk Skins to Drizzlik in Booty Bay.
Large River Crocolisk Skin: 0/2
-------------

Did that one at 31 with no problem, solo as a priest. The crocks for those are level 30 or 31. I did that at the same time as doing the northern quests and the quests from the expedition camp for the first tigers and panthers.

This is a fantastic area and not doing it before 35 is like shooting yourself in the foot, some really great xp and what not here.

------------
Level 32 Stranglethorn Vale
Obtained at level 28 Alliance

Find the hunters' camp and report its location to Krazek in Booty Bay.
------------

That one was very easy. I was already doing the quests there when I was 32 and could get that one.

By the way, if doing these I found it best to get the booty bay flight path and more or less use darkshire as your "inn" still while in the northern part. I'd fly down to booty bay from there to do quest turn ins and such.

I think I went to booty bay and got the flight path around 24, since I could afford a parrot then. Piece of cake going through barrens and ratchet.

I also got a baslisk quest from there, a quest to just investigate the northern haunted island, etc.

---------------

A lot of people are cautious, they say don't go here until this time, don't go over here until this level. Two of the areas I always hear that about are darkshore and SV. On two characters now, I started darkshore at 19 and sv at 31. You are ready then, even solo. When you can start getting quests in an area, you are ready.

The rewards for starting darkshore at 19 are MUCH better than doing the "high" 19 quests in a lower area. I get the spider cooking quest as soon as I can and the kill the spiders quest and soon have the wolf one. then I've soon leveled about twice. Then I get the next quests. Then I'm suddenly nearly level 30.

From here, Last couple notes. Do not attempt to start on SV until you are level 30. Even then, the guide doesn’t start in SV itself. You should make your first trip here at 32.

I will grant you you *can* do quests in SV at 30, but I personally don't recommend it. That's like the proverbial teenager trying to grow up too fast. You CAN do quests in Ashenvale or Duskwood at 18, but you'll only get maybe three done before you hit a roadblock and need a quality group or move on to another zone.



As for getting there through Ashenvale, that's only easier for someone who is already in Ashenvale. I actually don't recommend this route because I *know* you will die at least once doing it. It's almost impossible to get past the Horde blockade at the Stonetalon-Barrens border. There are level 30 creatures up and down the path to the Barrens from Ashenvale, and the point was to make this trip at level 20. Those spiders will web you and kill you.

Even if you do die once running down the big long road, or maybe twice, it's STILL an easier way to go imho. There are only two or three select places where mobs even come near the road, so as long as you keep that in mind, it really isn't that bad.
1. There's a few panthers right before you cross the first river.
2. There's a few apes close to the road after you cross the second river.
3. There are a few panthers along the road right past the arena and some apes shortly after that.
And then you're at Booty Bay.
