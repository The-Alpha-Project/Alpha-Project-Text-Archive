Hi there. Yeah, i'll see tech support stuff faster if it is in the tech support forum

There could be a variaty of things at work here. First, with your router connected to both computers and both computers turned on, unplug the router power cable for 30 seconds. when it is back on, go to your roommates computer and type in 192.168.1.1 if your roommate can bring up the router info screen, then the router is connected to your room mates compter, and being recognized by the router. It should autodetect computers on the net unless you have disabled automatic setup

If you can hit the router page, but can't hit the internet with just that one computer, the issue is likely with that computer. this can happen particularily if you have Norton Internet security installed on that computer. the easiest way i have found to deal with this under windows xp is to remove the netowork device and let it rebuild. you can do so by right clicking on "network neighborhood" and clicking on properties.

if that doesn't do the trick, it may be infected. in that case, grab the latest windows updates manually, transfer them over on a disk, and install them, should bring it back up.
