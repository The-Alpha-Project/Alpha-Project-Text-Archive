It shouldn't take that much food to start off with. Make sure you DO have food though, and it is a type your pet will like.

When you first tame, you'll notice a little red square next to the pet's health bar. That's how happy it is. You need to get it above red by feeding it. Feed your pet and don't start a fight the split second after you do, and you should see messages of your pet's happiness going up (in the combat text window.) Once the box turns yellow, or perhaps even green, your pet will not run away. The pet does slowly become unhappy though, and when you first get the pet, this happens not-so-slowly. Make sure you feed your pet any time it gets into the red. DEFINITELY make sure you feed it after reviving it.

I've never had a pet run away.

As mentioned above, as your pet levels, so must the level of your food or it will take ungodly amounts of chow to affect its level of happiness. I too almost alwasy keep my pet in the green for the bonus, so as I level I need to throw out the old food and get new. The trick to know if you are feeding it Primo food is go to the combat channal tab before you drop the food on him. The optimal happiness level for your pet is 35 happiness points per tic when feeding. If you are only getting 7 points per tic, you need to upgrade your food immediatly. To do so just kill higher level mobs for it, the higher the mob the higher the resulting meat will be (if you have a meat eater) currently I have a cat (meat and fish). Crabs are the best source of food I have found at low 20s. They drop meat very often (for fish eating pets only).
Also, if you are brand new and just got your pet and can not feed it, go back to your trainer and ensure you have the Feed Pet skill. If you do not, you need to train it or you will be on a neverending cycle of chasing down new pets. Hope this helps, good hunting.
