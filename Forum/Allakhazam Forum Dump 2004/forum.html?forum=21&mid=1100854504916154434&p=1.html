I will miss my Hunter....

But most of all, I will miss Snowflake. A more steadfast companion I shall never find. Gonna miss my little snow-white bear (Though she almost killed me, was down to 5hp, when I first tamed her and changed her name from Mangleclaw to my faithful Snowy), trotting along beside me.

Always there was nearby a companion that even when things looked dimmest, never flinched at the danger or fled from my side. Dependable unto death, that was my Snowy.

Me and that little bear had been through alot and somehow always seemed to pull out, often times fighting off three even or higher lvl mobs at once.

I know it sounds sappy, but I got attached to that little fuzzy ball of fury and as I move on to the 23rd, I will probably be playing a paladin, as my guild will need that type of support.

But something tells me...that there is a chance, a good chance that in the future, there will be another Dwarf.... self-crafted tricked-out gun at this hip, shades (flying tiger goggles) firmly in place, small mechanical squirrel scurrying behind...and at his side...his beloved Snow-White bear. :-)

So here is to you Snowy *raises a mug of ale in toast*. Never gonna be another like ya....*sniff*.

Taking aload off with me pets

Aye, first found Snowflake at 12th lvl...for the next 10 lvls, there was always Snowy. She may have spent a little time with the stable master (the Light knows she needed a break), but as soon as I learned new skills to teach her, off we would go.

While mining to get materials for my engineering, she always watched my back. Whenever danger threatened, she was always first to charge forward.

Yes, a Hunter's pet is more than just cannon-fodder, you grow attached to your pets. They become an extension of your personality and style. A Priest I often grouped with would yell "NOOOOOOO!" if Snowy were on the verge of death, and frantically heal her, she would do the /mourn and /cry emote if Snowy died. Would offer to rezz her, but I told her that was my job...I cared for Snowy, she was my as Azuarc put it and as Blizzard so aptly named the Lvl 6 devotion of pets, "Best Friend".

Often the first thing many would ask on seeing me was "Where is Snowy?" The first time I showed up without her, my friend almost had a fit, till I could explain to her that Snowy was taking a bit of time off while I learned new tricks to teach her. OTHER people become attached to your pets too.

So be warned those of you anxiously awaiting the 23rd....If you chose the path of the Hunter, there is a higher cost to your earning a pet than just getting a pixelated 'meat-shield'. Given enough time, and you too will find it difficult to press that "Dismiss" button...
