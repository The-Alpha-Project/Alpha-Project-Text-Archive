Re: PvP question
 Reply Posted by TaggeArdona <Chaotic Rangers> on 2004-10-03 13:46:42
With regards to the question about PvP, it depends on what class your character is, and on what scale the PvP battle is.  As a paladin, I use explosives a lot because they are the only ranged weapon that I can use.  The cooldown time on bombs is long enough that in a one-on-one duel, the best they could do you is a bit of fire damage and a few seconds of stun.  In a large raid group, engineers can wreak havok.  Imagine having a dozen engineers leading an assault on your town, each with an explosive sheep or sapper bomb aimed at the same target.  Because they rarely miss, coordinated bombing runs can do damage on much higher level targets.  Aside from bombs, engineers can make headgear that improves healing spells, gadgets that provide invisibility, shielding, or mind control, and mechanical pets to fight with you.  Plus, engineering is not one of the more common tradeskills, and the stuff they make usually catches the opponent by suprise (especially the harder to make high level toys).  In short, I learned engineering as soon as I could find a trainer, and the stuff I've built because of it has been very useful, both in PvP and PvE.

Engineering Write-up and Answers: Part One
 Reply Posted by piez on 2004-11-22 18:41:43
During the Open Beta, I got to level 31 with my Night Elf Priest, 211 Engineering, 206 Mining.  I chose to go Goblin Engineering for the bombs and fun destructive uses. 

Mining feeds Engineering like nobody's business, but you may want to make a few friends that know Skinning, as a couple of the 170+ recipes have leather components (Compact Harvest Reapers = 4 Heavy Leathers + 1 Medium Leather for the Framework... ick).  Copper, Silver, Tin, the various Stones and Gems, etc, all come from Mining.  Of course, you will have to map out your mining veins and deposits and be quick to farm them, lest the other Engineers and those damn Blacksmiths take them!

As a priest that leaned more towards Discipline Talents rather than the other 2, having bombs and devices to do damage for me really helped me out with soloing more efficiently.  If I was silenced, I threw a grenade.  Need mana for resurections?  Nah, use the Goblin Jumper Cables.  I found a locked chest, and I want it all for myself, but I'm not a Rogue! *Uses a Seaforium Charge to blast it open*.  I liked making guns for my guild and friends, and a couple of them traded Leathers to me for them ;p.  The goggles, although cloth, have an immense Armor value and awesome stats, and are great for anyone (that has Engineering), as head gear is very hard to come by earlier in the game, not to mention, they look wicked-cool.  And yes, any Engineer can make/use bombs.  They're the bread and butter of Engineering!  However, the higher-end bombs made with Goblin Engineering are a closely held secret to the Goblins and their apprentices ;).
Engineering Write-up and Answers: Part Two!
 Reply Posted by piez on 2004-11-22 18:42:39
Engineering is a tad different from the other skills in that... it doesn't really have an actual "Artisan Level", but you must choose between the 2 schools of Engineering, Goblin and Gnome.  They both have their perks.  Goblins have more destructive tendancies, having more bombs, explosives, and weaponry, while Gnomes have more devices that help in general, like Nets,  When you do the quest to pick Goblin or Gnome Engineering, the NPCs CLEARLY state that once you choose a path, there's no changing or turning back.  The head Goblin Engineer in Gadgetzan had me make a few bombs to "show my work" before I got membership into Goblin Engineering.  As Gadgetzan is neutral, I believe both Horde and Alliance members seeking Goblin Engineering are sent here by a Master Engineer from a city of their faction.  I was offered the choice by the master engineer (he's a gnome) in Stormwind, and he sent me to Gadgetzan after I told him i wanted that path.  Seeing as Booty Bay is also Neutral, and there is a Gnome there who seems to be rather knowledgable of Engineering, I would assume he is the NPC that lets you become a Gnomish Engineer.

The main downside to Engineering is that, yes, chaosmonger, it is a giant money-sink.  the vast majority of the items you create will only be usable by you and engineers of equal or greater skill. 
Engineering Write-up and Answers: Final Part! I Promise!
 Reply Posted by piez on 2004-11-22 18:43:20
However, there are a few items that you can make to sell off.  Guns are a big seller.  I sold off at least 5 Silver-Plated Shotguns in one day for between 75 Silver and 2 Gold at the Auction House when I was as low as level 20.  The ammunition for said guns can be sold for decent ammounts, as well.  Once you find the schematics for E Z Thro Dynamite, you can sell that off to non-engineers.  Anyone can use it that is level 10 or higher.  Another thing that sells like hotcakes is Mechanical Squirrels and, when you get higher in skill, Lil Smokey.  People go nuts for the little pets, and the Mechanical Squirrel is horrendously cute, making it a must-have for alot of players.  It doesn't do anything, but DAMN it's cute and fun to look at!

I hope this long-winded post helped answer some questions.  Engineering can be fun, but you have to work at it to make it REALLY fun.  I enjoyed all the things I could do, and even earned a reputation for leading my groups off-course just to mine some minerals and to create wacky and cool things to show off to others!  I'll see you all in retail, folks!
