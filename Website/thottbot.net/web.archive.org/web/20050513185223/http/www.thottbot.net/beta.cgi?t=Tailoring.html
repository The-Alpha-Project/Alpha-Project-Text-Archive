Reply Posted by Shyou on 2004-10-31 13:40:10
The difference in being able to supply yourself or not is how much you group vs. solo. If you are in a group you will regularly be fighting mobs 3-4 levels above you. What I often do is inform the group that I'm a tailor and 9 out 10 times they will give me the cloth that drops (unless their is another tailor in the group and we will split it). Also, there ARE mobs at your level that drop appropriate level cloth so checking thottbot before deciding where to grind can reap big dividends for you when it comes time to craft.

Enchanting does MAKE you money as well as cost you money. Basically I keep any items that I can use and disenchant green items that I cant use as well as my old items. That is where the cost is because selling the green items does provide some cash. However, enchanting items I tailored and selling them in the AH raises my skill in tailoring and enchanting and its fun to make items tailored to a specific class. Sometimes I even make color-coordinated sets with stats that complement each other. It can be alot of fun to go with this combo :D
Reply Posted by psychokitten on 2004-10-31 23:35:39
I'm in a guild as well.  It's not as easy as that.  My guildmates keep me well supplied in cloth, including cloth above my level (felcloth and runecloth), but I still have to buy the leather, potions, dye, and/or thread for anything I want to make.  I usually spend more for the dye and thread than I could make off of any one item (especially fluff items like tuxedoes and wedding dresses).  Unless your guildmates are sending you money as well, you will find very difficult to keep enough money coming in to buy your spells once you get to Artesan level Tailoring.  I expect this to change some what as crafted items are further tweaked, but right now, tailored items are not as good as the drops you get out of instance dungeons, so there is very little demand. 

Tailoring and Enchating really only have an income potential when you no longer need an income; ie. after you reach the level cap.  Even then, Enchanting is extremely expensive. Large Shards often go for 10g or more in the AH, and several are needed for many enchants.  The average player balks at paying anywhere what you spent getting reagents for an enchant.
