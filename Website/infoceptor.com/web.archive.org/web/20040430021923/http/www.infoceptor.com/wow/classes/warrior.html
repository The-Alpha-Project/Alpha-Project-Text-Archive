The Warrior: -Last Updated 3-16-2004

Available to: Human, Dwarf, Night Elf, Orc, Tauren, Undead, Troll.
Best For: Dwarf, Orc, Tauren
Primary Attribute: Strength
Skills Require... Rage

Allowable Armor: All
Allowable Weapons: All axes, maces, swords, thrown, daggers

Warriors are the melee-centered class. These characters are tough as nails and masters of weaponry and tactics. The special abilities of the warrior are naturally combat-oriented.

While in most RPGs, warriors are considered the easiest class to play, with the least amount of options. Blizzard, however, promises to remedy the latter. Like the Barbarian in Diablo II, the warrior will feature numerous combat skills and special abilities that an be activated in battle... playing the warrior will no longer be just a clickfest.

Stances and Rage:

What makes warriors unique in World of Warcraft are battle stances. When engaged in combat, a player chooses a stance. The stance will determine what skills is available to the player, and will have a drastic effect on the play style of the Warrior. In each stance, warriors will gain Battle Rage when they deal of recieve damage (depending on the stance chosen). The rage can then be used to channel certain combat abilities spefic to the stance, much like how mana is used to cast certain spells.

If a Warrior switches from one Battle stance to another, he loses all the Rage points he has gain so far. Thus, it is logical to stay in one stance for each Battle.

Defensive Stance:
Rage Gained When: Recieving Damage
As the name suggests, defensive stance uts the warrior into a more cautious position. In this stance, he gains bonuses to his armor and takes reduced damage from enemy attacks. However, rage is only gained when damage is dealt to the player. This stance is best used when accompanying friendly spellcasters, who need that extra protection.
Battle Stance:
Rage Gained When: Dealing Damage
The exact opposite to defensive stance, adopting an battle stance increases the damage a player deals the enemy at the cost of not gaining rage when taking hits. This stance is best used when enemies when you're adventuring outside a party, and want all the experience for yourself.

Defensive Abilities:
Defensive abilities can only be used while in defensive stance. They're great for keeping the enemy at bay, allowing allied spellcasters and archers to do deal the real damage.

Basic Character Development Strategies:

As in Diablo II, players will want to distribute their skill points that that they can use most of their skills at any one point. That means, it's better decide whether you want to be an offensive or defensive warrior first, and then focus on the one offensive/defensive skill that you wish to use a lot.

This way, spare points can be used in passive abilities that improve your weapon of choice and ability to evade enemy attacks.

When starting out, you may wish to decide whether you want to be a part focused or solo focused character. Since like Diablo II, distributing skills on everything.. while fun, may not be wise in the long run.

If you're a player who likes being in a party, a defensive warrior is best. As it allows you to act as a meat-shield and protect weaker members of the party. However, if you're a loner, then the quickest way to gain experience would be as an offensive warrior. This allows you to kill things faster... and thus journey through the land more quickly.

The Warrior also has no quick means of healing, and should thus consider getting trade skills that make him less dependent on healers. The less time you spend waiting around, the more EXP you'll get. Thus, Alchemy, First Aid and Fishing are great supplementary choices.
