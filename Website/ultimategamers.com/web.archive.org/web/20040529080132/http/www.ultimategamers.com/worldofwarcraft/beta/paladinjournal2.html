Paladin Journal

(part 2)

To part 1

First part of this journal was a presentation of the different elements that every player who creates his or her first character will face, as well as some highlights of the new player zone. This time, my Paladin is ready to face the "real" world and interact with its dangers. This article will focus on the Kharanos region, and try to display it.

With a few new levels under his belt (the difference being displayed in the picture on the left, including a brand new guild affiliation), it is time for my Paladin to go back to Anvilmar, and try to get a few new skills through the Paladin Trainer, Bromos Grummner. . For the time being, I pick Divine Favor, a spell that will protect me from any attack or spell for 6 seconds. This seems like a perfect spell for someone who likes to solo.

On the way back to Anvilmar, I also pick a quest that will send me across the mountains to deliver a letter from Grelin, and take the opportunity to sell some of the junk items that I found while I was looting. Adlin Pridedrift, a merchant near the location where I picked the first quest accepts to buy them. It should be noted that, thankfully, you can go to any type of merchant to sell your loot.

Senir's Observations: This quest requires me to go through a Tunnel guarded by Troggs, and deliver the letter I received, to Senir Whitebeard in Kharanos. The cave is filled with Rockjaw Raiders, Humanoids who look like they just escaped the cave age. Although I was warned of a tough fight, there are only a few Rockjaw Raiders in the cave and they are no match for my level. I guess that I will not be able to try Divine Favor yet.


Kharanos

I reach the other side of the cave without any problem, and find myself in another valley still covered with snow. After a short trip, I eventually find Kharanos, where I deliver the letter and take some time to inspect the area. Kharanos is a small village with only two buildings. Most of the creatures in this area are beasts (Wolves, Bears, Boars). Some Wendigos are also present, but they tend to stay in or near their caves.

The first thing that I notice in Kharanosis a Gunsmith Trainer. I still have to find yet what class would be better-suited with this skill. I doubt that it would be a Paladin, though. In Kharanos Hall, I find a few Trainers and Merchants, including an Apprentice Cloth & Leather, an Armorer, a Blacksmithing Supplier, and a Weaponsmith. No sight of any other Paladin trainer though, at least in this building.

The other building offers more choices, including some class Trainers (Warrior, Priest), and a Physician. A Physician will teach you how to to create bandages to heal you. This might be an interesting training skill to learn, but I will pass for the time being, still having to discover more. While continuing my exploration of the building, I eventually find another Paladin Trainer. This one has a lot more skills to offer than the first one I met. Too bad my pockets are empty and I can't really afford anything fancy. I will settle then for Healing Aura, which can be handy for any group I decide to join. The only thing to remember is that a Paladin can only have one aura active at a time, so I will have to select either Healing or Devotion (armor). Of course, should I meet another Paladin in a group, both of us could pick a different aura, which would doubly-benefit the group.

Quests found in Kharanos:

Most quests found in Kharanos are slay quests. Only one requires you to find a NPC in a remote area.

Beer Basted Boar Ribs: This is another slay quest, which are very typical at low level. This time, I need to bring back some Ribs as well as some beer from the Bartender. A total waste of a Paladin's time in my opinion, but with empty pockes, I do not have that much choice. While I'm in Kharanos, I decide to pick another quest, in case it brings me to the same area of my first quest. In World of Warcraft, there are no time limits for quests, and picking up a few at the same time always save you some trips. After all, Dwarf legs are not really set for long walks, so I will spare as much energy as I can.

Tools for Steelgrill: This quest requires me to deliver some tools to a Siege Engine pilot, northeast of town. With a little luck, I will find Boars in that area. Finding Steelgrill proves very easy, and rewarding, as every time you discover a new area, you gain experience points. I deliver the tools and pick up two more quests.

The Grizzled Den: Another slay quest, this one requesting some Wendigo Manes.

Stocking Jetsteam: This quest requires to find some meat from Boars or Bears. While all quests around Kharanos aref not really original in their nature, at least they are soloable and help you discover the area around town. This is an easy way to level up a character fast. My character now has six quests to complete, all in the same area.

Guilds and races: While working on my quests, I am met by Sennyo, another member of the Ultimate Gamers guild. Guilds are very well structured in World of Warcraft. They offer several ranks, with some privileges for the two top ranks, Officer and Leader. The Guild Leader and Guild Offers can promote everyone to levels under them, demote them, ban them from the guild, and of course recruit. Only the Guild Leader and Guild Officers can invite other players to a Guild.

What we learned from Sennyo's visit was that Sennyo (Night Elf) could nt understand other Dwarves, as she has not learned their language. However, she could understand me (Dwarf) through guild chat.

Back to hunting. All the quests found in Kharanos proved as easy as I expected them to be, with the exception of the Wendigos. These tough creatures are well-organized and hang in groups. Beat one and it runs towards its family, and before you know it, you have 3 or 4 on your back. I wish players that you meet in this cave were as organized as these creatures! Unfortunately, a majority of players do not seem to have any MMORPG experience, and people in a group will continue looting while you're fighting, or just disappear out of reach without warning. Hopefully, when the game goes gold, more mature players will be found. The reward for all these quests was somewhat good moneywise, and helped me reach level 7 and a new talent. I picked another level of proficiency in 2-hand maces to inflict more damage.

After completing the above quests, my Paladin receives two new quests found in Kharanos and in Steelgrill. Both point towards a new area, Brenwall Village. These quests, and a presentation of this region, will be covered shortly in the next chapter. Talk to you soon!
