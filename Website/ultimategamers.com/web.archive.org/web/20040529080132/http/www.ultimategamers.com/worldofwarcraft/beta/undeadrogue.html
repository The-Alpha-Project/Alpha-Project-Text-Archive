Introduction to the Undead Rogue

By popular demand, I am starting a description of the Rogue and the Undead, arguably the most fascinating race in World of Warcraft (with the Night Elves), and one of the most original class in the game.

About the Undead: If you haven't played Warcraft III, now is the right time to tell you a little more about the Undead. Two Undead factions are present in World of Warcraft. The Scourge, led by Prince Arthas, and the Forsaken, led by Sylvanas.

Once an Elf, Sylvanas was transformed into a Banshee by Arthas. After unwillingly serving Arthas for a while, Sylvanas gained her freedom from the Scourge and since that day, seeked revenge against the Scourge, and the Alliance who let her down.

The Forsaken have signed a treaty with the Horde, composed of the Orcs, the Trolls, and the Tauren. This pact is however just a means of survival for the Forsaken, who ultimately want to create a new plague that will destroy whoever lies in their path. Nobody can clearly tell how the pact between the Forsaken and the Horde will last.

The Forsaken reside in Lordaeron, once the proud Human capital. But what was once a prosperous kingdom is now only populated by demons, ghosts, and other creatures who only live to destroy each other. Amongst all enemies, two factions have for goal to obliterate the Forsaken: the Scourge, who cannot tolerate any dissidence within its ranks, and the Scarlet Crusade, a Human faction who wants to regain control of Lordaeron, and purge the world from any Undead presence, be it Scourge or Forsaken.

About the Rogues: Rogues are powerful fighters that can be characterized by their ability to inflict heavy damage and their capacity to hide, using their stealth. However, Rogues are restricted to cloth and leather armors, which means that they do not have a very proper defense, even if they are allowed to equip a basic shield. After proper training, Rogues can become almost invisible, and can also double-wield.

Journey of an Undead Rogue: As any other Forsaken, you awake in a Crypt, just to be told that if your sleep had lasted a little longer, you would have been buried with those who didn't succeed in gaining their Undead status. As every "soul" counts, you are immediately prompted to serve the Dark Lady, and sent to your first mission.

First quests: As every new recruit in the Forsaken army, you are sent to boot camp, which consists of a few missions to cleanse the surrounding area from some mindless spirits that the Forsaken cannot control, getting some needed elements such as animal paws and wings, and eventually showing the Scarlet Crusade that their outpost is not welcome in your neighborhood.

Unlike Warriors who depend on rage, or casters who depend on mana to use their skills, Rogues have an "energy" bar, which allows them to use their own abilities. Rogues start with two skills: Sinister Strike and Eviscerate. For those familiar with Diablo II, Eviscerate is a finishing strike, which means that it can only be casted after another skill. The next available skill is Stealth, which can be acquired at level 2.

Stealth: Stealth is a lot of fun, especially when you crawl near people who don't expect you. As seen on the right picture, it is very difficult to see a Rogue using stealth. However, stealth does not provide total invisibility, especially if you are moving in front of a target. Stealth comes with its own skill bar and a few skills that can only be used under stealth mode. Stealth is the best way to use your Backstab skill.

Skills: You get new skills every "even" level (2, 4, 6, etc...). To get them, you need to find your specific class Trainer. To help new players, Blizzard now puts a letter in your bag that tells you where you can find the first available Trainer for your class. In the case of our Undead Rogue, The Rogue Trainer is located in the Tavern, as specified in the letter.

Initial quests: Running through the initial quests, you will eventually reach level 4, which offers two new skills: Backstab, and Pickpocket. Backstab requires you to be behind your target and causes 150% weapon damage plus 15 damage to the target. Pickpocket requires you to be in stealth mode and allows you to steal from humanoids. It should be noted that you cannot use stealth while in combat. It is always a good thing to approach your target from behind, should you want to empty its pockets.

Most of the quests that you will receive in the first area will help you to get some necessary armor, limited in your case to cloth and leather.

New areas: After leaving the training area, you are dispatched to Brill. What used to be a Human village is now an Undead outpost, where experiments are made on Alliance prisonners and where you will receive further missions. Brill has an Inn where you can rest, and is not too far away from the Undercity (the Forsaken's capital).

When entering a new area, you should always check if there is more than one quest available. Most of the times, the mobs that you will find in an area are a target of a quest. By selecting all possible quests, you will avoid having to run back and forth between the NPC and the quests area.

Another good tip concerning quests is that there is no race limitation on them. So, our Undead Rogue can do the Undead quests, but also the Trolls, Orcs, and Tauren quests. This is important to know when you find out that quests are becoming too difficult in a region. It is then time to move to another one and do easier quests. Transportation to other races' locations is possible through an NPC in front of the Undercity (in our Rogue's case).

Brill: Once a Human little village, Brill is now a Forsaken outpost. There, several NPCs offer quests against the Scarlet Crusade or the Scourge. One even asks you to test a potion on a Human prisoner to transform him into an Undead. Brill offers an Inn, important for the rest system, class trainers (mostly in the Inn), and trade skills trainers. There, you will find a Herbalism trainer, an Alchemy trainer , and an Enchanting trainer. Both Alchemy and Enchanting require herbalism as a prerequisite. Other trade skills are available further away from town, such as tailoring. All trade skills require skill points that you acquire through combat experience (not from quest experience).

Quests: Although most quests are found on NPCs, some are found on some signs. These signs usually offer a reward for killing a named mob. This kind of mobs usually have a "+" sign behind their name, which means that they are much harder to kill that their indicated level. The picture on the right describes one of these quests. Note that, unlike NPCs, signs do not give you any clue that they have a quest ready, either now or for higher levels. You will have to revisit the signs after you level up to see if it is linked to a quest.

Quests around Brill are harder than those you found in the training area. The first reason is that now, most mobs (with red names) do not wait for you to attack them. Get close to them and they will directly attack you. Also, and contrary to what you could find in the training area, most mobs are now at least at the same level you are (you should leave the training area around level 4, after completing all quests there). Finally, while our Rogue can deal some good damage, he is still restricted in his choice of armor, which is his weakness in melee combat

As in other regions, you will need to either kill some mobs, or collect some items on them, then return to the NPC who gave you the quest, unless otherly specified. The easiest quests around Brill involve stealing pumpkins in a farm that is still defended by some Human farmers and Scarlet Warriors, or getting some paws from Undead faithful to the Scourge. After completing a couple of these quests, our Rogue will have enough skill points to select at least one trade skill. I suggest herbalism (10 skill points), which will lead you further on to either enchanting (creation of potions), or alchemy (enhances items). Another useful trade skill combination for a Rogue is skinning and leathercrafting.

By completing these quests, you will also learn about the Undead lore, what brought them here and what are the motivations of their enemies. Eventually, you will reach level 6, which will open new skill choices.

New skills: When you reach level 6, you will have the choice to select a new skill (Gouge), update Sinister Strike, or do both if you have enough money (yes, if you didn't know it already, training requires some financing). In World of Warcraft, most skills have several levels, and it is important to update these levels, as they bring more power to the skill. A level-1 Sinister Strike might have worked fine against level-2 or level-3 Zombies, but will not do too much damage against a level-10 Scarlet Zealot, or not fast enough to keep you alive anyway.

Gouge is a skill that will stun a target for 4 seconds, giving you enough time to either retreat or recover some energy to cast a high-damage skill.

Soloing a Rogue: While certainly not the ultimate class for soloing (Druids are probably the best class for that purpose), Rogues still do fairly well in this chapter. Most early quests around Brill do not present any problem, but later ones (around level 8), such as the one requiring you to get some scales from Murlocs will require a partner. As mentioned earlier, should you find that all the available quests are too hard to solo, you always have the option to visit the Tauren, Trolls, or Orcs to see if they have (and they do) easier jobs for you.

At level 8, our Rogue will gain a level-2 Eviscerate (Finishing Skill), as well as Evasion, a skill that increases the Rogue's ability to dodge attacks.

About Finishing Skills: Understanding the concept of Finishing Skills is very important for the survival of a Rogue. If you have played Diablo II, these skills were introduced with the Assassin and the Druid. Basically, a finishing skill is a skill that requires another skill before it can be used. The more "prerequisite" skills (such as Sinister Strike or Gouge) you "stack", the more powerful your Finishing skill will be. A good combination at level 8 is Sinister Strike, then Gouge, then Eviscerate. As you will have casted two prerequisite skills before Eviscerate, it will generate between 29 and 39 damage points (only 17 to 27 if you had only used one skill before Eviscerate). You can also decide to repeat the Sinister Strike- Gouge combination another time before casting Eviscerate, which would then inflict between 53 and 63 damage points.

Reaching level 10: Level 10 brings two new skills: Slice and Dice, and Sap. Slice and Dice is another finishing move that gives the Rogue a faster attacking speed. Like other finishing moves, Slice and Dice's effectiveness depends on how many prerequesite skills you use before. In this case, the number of skills used before Slice and Dice will determine the duration of the skill. Slice and Dice is limited to Humanoids not in combat.

On the other hand, Sap is a skill that can only be used in Stealth mode, and that will neutralize a target for 25 seconds, unless the target is hit after being "sapped".

Level 10 is also the moment of choice for the Rogue, who can now dual-wield, if he has 30 skill credits available. By selecting this skill, the Rogue will turn into a killing machine, as he can now use two Daggers, but he will obviously lose the advantage of wearing a buckler. Most Rogues dual-wield, which doesn't mean that it is the only alternative. Bucklers still may have some use for a few levels, until their protection level is not high enough to effectively give a defense advantage to the Rogue.

I hope this "Introduction to the Rogue will help you better understand a very good class to play. Although it requires a little more management than other classes, because of finishing moves, it certainly brings a lot of originality with the use of stealth and options such as pick-pocket. If you would like to learn about other classes, please let us know.
