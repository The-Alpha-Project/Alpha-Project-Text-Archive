f, either as a beta player, or just through reading articles, you ever visited Darnassus, the Night Elf capital, you were probably amazed by the beauty and serenity of that place. Stormwind also probably amazed you by its majesty and the strength that emanes from its solid walls, and finally, you probably described Ironforge as organized chaos, deep under a high mountain.

The Undercity cannot be compared to any of these cities. Although spectacular in its nature, the Undercity is the place that you wish you had never visited, especially in your dreams.

Built under the ruins of the ancient Human capital of Lordaeron, the Undercity now hosts the Headquarters of the Forsaken, the Undead faction that separated itself from the Lich King, and now has to fight against the remnants of the Alliance in this part of the world, and Arthas' troops, who want to control them again. What happened to Lordaeron is well-known by the players who followed the single-player missions of Warcraft III (Reign of Chaos and The Frozen Throne). But even those could hardly imagine the devastation brought by the Undead there. Even during day light, the dominant "color" in these lands is a heavy gray, a smog that the sun shies away from.

What used to be the King's palace is now a haunted ruin, inhabited by Undead, Demons, and other malevolent spirits. Nature, or what is left of it is now reclaiming its place amongst what were solid walls of stone, and water has been transformed into a green slime that corrupts everything that touches it, even Undead. To enter the Undercity, one has to go through these walls, where some corpses still lay on the ground, probably waiting to be devoured by haunted beasts or be claimed by the Undead.

After following some corridors that once led to the King's Throne, you will be brought to elevators that are the real entrance to the city itself. Should you be an Alliance player or party thinking that you can easily storm the place, you should know that there is a welcoming committee waiting for you. And not, I'm not talking about the (still) puny warlock on the left picture, but the Abominations that patrol the city.


Taking one of the elevators will bring you directly to the center of the city. This will bring you to a round corridor, leading to the bank, from where you can visit every city quarter. Like Stormwind, quarters are color-coded. Red is the area where Mages and Warlocks gather, purple is for Rogues and Priests, and blue for warriors. Other areas hide some dreadful secrets, including some experiments whose purpose is still unexplained.


The Mage quarter is the part that I visited, and it was probably the creepiest one. Blizzard has managed to render an atmosphere that makes you very uncomfortable staying in the city. By visiting it, you really feel part of an organization that only have one purpose in "life" (pun intended): destroy everything.

 

There, you will find characters that you were familiar with while playing Warcraft III, such as Banshees. You will also be able to join a Warlock classroom where several teachers will show you the different pets that Warlocks can summon, even explaining that against male opponents, you should always try to select a Succubus, just to distract their attention for a few seconds.

 

Either you decide to play the Horde or the Alliance, save one of your characters' slot to create an Undead, and visit the Undercity. If you have nightmares after that, don't put the blame on us though. Our soul is already cursed anyway.
