I set my feet down for the first time on Azeroth. Its green is so very different from that of Teldrassil. Menethil Harbor is in an area known as the Wetlands, and looks like a very fertile swamp. The trip from Darkshore to Menethil was by boat, and although the boat, and boat transport is not implemented for that area yet, the boat is very real in Menethil.


The first thing I explored was the ship itself, testing the gangplank carefully. There are still many areas with bugged objects, such as a tree trunk in Darkshore that crosses a river, but does not actually allow you to walk on it, (which is an object of much frustration, as the river below it is bugged as well). I jump up into the captain's chair.


Note what I say in the text area.

Now that’s not a great view of the waters ahead, so I decide to partake in the other benefits of being a fake captain.

After taking a brief sit in the room, I head out to inspect the rest of the ship.

I take a quick hop inside of the ship, and look down upon those scurvy dogs that run the place.


The ship in all was very detailed; there were barrels all over the place, and storage areas. The crew walks around and make themselves look busy, and there was only one small bug that I found with it, which was that when inside the ship (like the last screenshot) it would take the game a second to realize that it shouldn’t be showing the water level. All in all nothing big.

The next area to look around was the big fort on the land, on my way over however I just had to take a look inside the bar that this fellow fell out of:

Inside was really nice, and of note were the characters upstairs. The first one was as his name implies, quite shady. There are a few people in this world that work out of a bed, and they must be shady dealers.

All joking aside, it was a neat find, but more interesting was the person, or people, next door. After completing a quest (the whole reason I was out here) the Dwarf makes this beauty.

  
(the order in which you see the images matters)

Keep an eye on the text box in the lower left, as it tells the story of the quest.

With the quest out of the way I continue along to the fort, and delve inside. One of the first rooms I came to was quite a find, if only everything was not bolted down.

 

After another bunch of rooms that did not seem very interesting, I came across the big room upstairs, with the Lord of the fort. Of course, by instinct I sat in his chair and pretended to be him.

Of course he did not seem to like it very much, so I had to hoof it out of there, and jumped down to the first floor, looking back up I realized that no one was following me, and that I just needed to justify falling and taking 9 damage for no apparent reason.
