Paladin Journal

After presenting two locations (Darnassus, Darkshore) that my Night Elf ventured through, I'd like to focus on the presentation of a character, from its conception to its early levels. More than building a journal, I will also try to explain all the mechanisms that are necessary to make a better character, through experience, picking up trading crafts, finding a proper equipment, and selecting the right talents. Please note that all pictures can be viewed full-size by clicking on them.

Location: Dwarves start their journey at Coldridge Valley, a remote outpost in Dun Morogh. This starting location is also shared by the Gnomes.

Equipment: As can be expected, a new character starts with very limited equipment (pants, shirt, shoes, and a Hammer in the case of the Paladin).

Skills and spells: A Paladin starts with four skills or spells: Attack, Dodge, Defend, Holy Strike, and Holy Light. Dodge and Defend are both Passive skills, Attack is an active skill, Holy Strike is an offensive skill, and Holy Light is a healing spell (which requires mana).

All skills start at a minimum. For example, Dodge (which regulates your ability to avoid being hit) starts with a 0.00% chance of avoiding being hit. Through combat, each skill will automatically be raised, depending on the actions that you perform.

Acquiring new skills will come through leveling, and finding the appropriate Trainer. Trainers can be found in cities and towns and will require you to pay for their services.

Talents: Another essential element of improving your character is through the acquisition of talents. Talents do not require any Trainer or money, and will be granted to you when you level.

Talents can improve your character either through an enhancement of its attributes (strength, dexterity, stamina, etc.). This will in turn sharpen your character's defensive skills, increase his magical attacks, or build up his proficiency in the use of a weapon. Talents will vary depending on your character class.

Trading skills: A last, but very important element of World of Warcraft is the ability to perform actions to improve your equipment, make potions, create food, etc. Called crafting in other games, these trading skills add a lot to the interactivity of the game. Whether you specialize in blacksmithing (to make better armor or weapons), tailoring (to make better cloths for classes who can't wear armor), fishing (to create food and speed up your recovery), enchantment (to create health or mana potions), or any of the numerous available trading skills, you will be rewarded by having a better character, socializing with other players, and making some money for selling the items you crafted, as they are usually cheaper than when bought through a merchant.

Quests: Quests can be found through NPCs. The number of available quests is impressive (175 for each race). Not only can you do quests that are given by NPCs of your own race, but you can also do those offered by other races of your faction (Alliance or Horde). This is important to know as (if you like to play solo), you may find yourself out of quests in your level range. A travel to other races' locations will then offer you a brand new number of quests that you will accomplish until you feel confident enough to go back to your own race's quests. Unlike other games, quests can only be accomplished once. This said, you can drop a quest at any time, there is no time limit to accomplish them (so far), and even if you lose an item important for the quest, you can always abandon the quest, go back to the NPC who gave it, and reacquire the item. NPCs who deliver quests have an exclamation mark on top of them. A yellow exclamation mark means that your level is high-enough to do the quest (which may mean that you need a full party to do it), and a silver one means that your level is not high enough to do the quest. Finally, clicking on a NPC doesn't automatically give you a quest. You always have the option to accept or decline it.

Looting: The looting system is quite complete. A party leader can decide to either have a first-come first-serve loot, a round-robin loot (each player is allowed to loot in turn), or a master looter (only person allowed to loot). Depending on the quests, each player will have to do his own loot to get the items required to finish the quest, or a boss will drop as many unique items as necessary. Loots will deliver weapons, armor, food, crafting items, money, or a combination of all. Quest-specific items can only be found by players who have the quest.

Back to our journal, and time for a first quest. After looking around for a bit, I decide to go to see Sten Stoutarm, who offers two quests (Dwarven Outfitters, and Coldridge Valley Mail Delivery).

Dwarven Outfitters: This quest requires me to hunt some wolves and return with 6 pieces of meat. Upon completion, I will be granted with some experience, and a nice pair of gloves.

  

The Wolves are in the very near area of where I found the quest. Left-clicking on them reveals a yellow name, which means that they will not attack unless attacked first. To attack them, I just need to get in range, right-click on them, then select one of my skills or spells. It takes a few kills to gather all the meat, as some of the wolves do not give it away. Through the quest, my skills have improved and I also reached level 2, which means that I can now select a new talent. I pick Heroic Strength, which will raise my strength by 1. Time to return to Sten, get my reward and pick up a new quest.

A New Threat: As Sten's other quest seems to be outside the starting area, I search for another NPC who may have a local quest. Balir Frosthammer offers a quest that is very similar to the first one I completed, and asks to kill 5 Rockjaw Troggs, and 5 Burly Rockjaw Troggs. This time though, I only have to kill them and each kill will count into the completion of the quest, as I do not have to pick up any item.

Loot at this level is not very good, but I find at least an AR4 robe, which will help, as my Paladin doesn't carry any shield. As I have to kill two kind of critters, I switch my view mode so that it now displays their name and health bar (but also make it more difficult to find players). The quest completion brings me to level 3, and I am about ready to explore the world. But I must find out if any Trainer is available.

Anvilmar: The first place to go is Anvilmar, a few yards away from the starting location. As expected from Dwarves, this place has been carved within the mountains. There, several Trainers are available, including a Paladin Trainer. With my meager resources, the only spell I can afford from him is Devotion Aura, which will raise the armor of any party I join. Time now to explore the world.

Coldridge Valley Mail Delivery: Back to the starting location, the only quest available is "Coldridge Valley Mail Delivery", an open invitation to explore the world. A look at the quest giver tells me that it will be a one-way trip as no question mark appears above his head. Time to see what the world has reserved for my Paladin. I update my talents with Physical Prowess, which gives me 20 additional life points and I am ready to complete my mission, which is to deliver some letters to Talin Keeneye, west of my location.

The trip is short and uneventful, (I just smack a couple of Boars to get some experience), and upon arrival, I deliver the letters, just to learn that some of them are for another character, Grelin Whitebeard. This is Blizzard's way to help you explore the world progressively. As both Talin and Grelin have new quests for me, I return to Talin to look at what he has to offer.

The Boar Hunter: As expected as this level, this is another slaying quest. I just need to kill 8 small Crag Boars, which shouldn't take long, as I do not have to retrieve any specific item on them. The quest itself doesn't take long, has poor loot results, but the reward (Dwarven leather pants), the experience, and the increase of talents is still worth it. So it is back to Grelin to see what he has to offer.

The Troll Cave: The mission this time is to kill 12 Frostmane Troll Whelps in a cave. From similar quests played either as a Night Elf or a Human (all the first missions of each race are very similar), this should still be a soloable mission. The reward will be an Anvilmar Sledge Hammer, and some Healing Herbs. This is the first encounter with mobs that will not wait for you to hit them before they hit you (red name). This means better loot but also chances to have a few Trolls gang up on you.

The quest brings you to a cave, the first dungeon to explore. Most dungeons at low-level are very small, but this one is an exception. The Trolls found there are tougher than anything my Paladin encountered before, and I welcome my healing skills, although they are quite long to cast. The cave is filled with Troll Whelps, as well as Troll Novices, a useful information, should I need to hunt for them later on. I also find a boss and discover that the Trolls are trying to speak to me (probably to curse me), but I can't understand a word of what they say (in World of Warcraft, you cannot understand what the other side is saying, unless you develop the proper skill).

The Stolen Journal: As expected when I saw a boss in this cave, a follow-up quest was to come. This time, I need to retrieve a journal that he stole from Grelin. So, it is back to the cave again. On the way, I find a Dwarf Rogue who needs the same quest. Rogues are definitely a cool race to play, with their stealthy moves. We team up and follow the path to the bottom of the dungeon where we defeat the boss. As we are in the same party, we only need to kill him once and both retrieve the journal. Back to Grelin again.

Leveling: Through these quests, my Paladin has now reached level 6, and has gained a few talents. I have now added 2H Mace Specialization, which helps my Paladin deliver more damage with two-hand maces, such as the Hammer that he wields. It is now time to take him outside this little enclave, and find him some Trading skills.
