Darkshore

To Darnassus

After venturing a little in Teldrassil, you will eventually find yourself out of quests for your level, which should be around 11 or 12 at that time. From Teldrassil, the easiest region to reach is Darkshore, on the northwestern coast of Kalimdor.

Although you could swim to Darkshore, it is recommended that you use one of the Hippogryphs that are available at a remote area to which you can teleport in Darnassus. The short flight will then eventually bring you to Auberdine, the Alliance outpost in Darkshore.

Most of Auberdine is populated by Night Elves, but the occasional Dwarf or Gnome can be found there. While quite appealing, Auberdine offers a brutal contrast from the serenity that could be felt in Darnassus. Even the sun seems to be shy to cast his rays upon the land that seems to be kept in a dark and mysterious mist.

As every other town, Auberdine has a bound stone, to which your character will be recalled, should he die. In World of Warcraft, a dead character can either decide to walk back to its corpse, at no experience cost, or ask a Spirit Healer (the NPC in front of a Bind Stone) to resuscitate you, at the cost of some experience. A third option is also to have a Priest, Shaman, Paladin, or Warlock resuscitate you. Most players who do not have any of the above in their party decide to walk back to their corpse, which can prove quite lengthy in some instances, even if a bind stone to which you regain life is always at the closest town or city.

Buildings in Auberdine are totally different from what was found in Darnassus. Here, the stone is mostly replaced by wood, and every house has a gloomy look, as if a spell was cast upon the outpost.

 

Several new Trainers can be found in Auberdine. Of interest is the Blacksmith Trainer, who will teach you how to make armor and weapons. To do so, you must obviously find the material (copper, iron, etc.) to craft your equipment. This can be acquired through a Mining Trainer, who will teach you where to find the necessary minerals. You will also need to buy some equipment to mine (unlike other games, you don't mine with your weapon) through the Mining Supplier. Fortunately, all these characters are all located at the same place. A last Trainer is the Bear Trainer, but no information about him is yet available for my Warrior.

The woods outside Auberdine are somewhat more dangerous than in Teldrassil. While most beasts were easily soloable in Teldrassil, encounters of Bears and other Moonstalkers (big panther-like beasts) are always better fought with at least another player. Greater dangers even lurk around in some dark caves, but telling too much about them would be a spoiler.

However, and without revealing too much of the storyline, let's just say that many players' answers to find the Naga in the game have been answered. What brings them here, I won't reveal yet though. Let's just say that the two units that you will encounter (including the dreadful Sirens) are deadly, as shown on the left picture.

See you in the next town!
