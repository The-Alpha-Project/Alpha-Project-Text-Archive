Darnassus

If anyone ever doubted about how beautiful World of Warcraft is, a short trip to Darnassus would calm any fear they ever had. The Night Elves' capital city is a large place that we will help you discover through this article. Note that clicking on each picture will open it full-size.

You start the game in a small village where you have to complete a few quests before being sent in the vast world that surrounds it. When the time is right one of your Preceptors will tell you that the time has come for you to explore your surroundings.

After a short trip through the woods, you finally arrive to this majestic place. The first thing to notice is that its walls are well guarded. Should any Horde raiding party decide to storm it, it would have to deal with the Sentinels, as well as from Ancient Protectors, such as the one depicted here, at the city entrance. If you look at the Priest in front of it, you realize that these guards are huge (and not to mention level 90!). What also impressed me was that they are well aware of their duties. While taking pictures for the site, a Night Saber decided that I would be perfect for its dinner. While I was awaiting for my death to come, the Ancient Protector moved towards us and smashed the Night Saber under its foot. This is the level of detail that you just appreciate in World of Warcraft.

But let's go inside the city now. Once you pass the gate, you realize that exploring the place will take some time. Some merchants stand before the city gate and a second set of walls that hosts the Warriors Terrace. Numerous merchants and trainers are present in Darnassus. You will even sometimes find two different trainers or merchants of a same type, both offering different things.

Trainers and merchants are a very important factor of the game. While you start with a few spells or skills, the only way (so far) to acquire new skills is through trainers, each class having its specific trainer. Training also requires money that you can find by hunting or by selling loot that you found either to fellow players or to merchants.

Darnassus is divided into several areas. The first one is the Warriors Terrace, where you will find Sildanair, a Warrior Trainer. Here, you will learn your first additional skills, such as Rend, Battle Shout, or Thunderclap. Each of these skills have several levels that will become available when you level up. Trainers will obviously only interact with players of their class.

Once you communicate with a Trainer, a box will open that will display which skill is available (in green), which one you may acquire later (red), a short description of the skill and how much it will cost you.

But each class can do other things than fighting. You can also develop side skills, such as fishing, cooking, or making cloths. These new skills are not only important for your survival, but they also allow you to make some money by selling your craft work to fellow players, as items that you craft are way cheaper than those you can buy. Traders and Crafters are located respectively on the north and south sides of the Warriors' Terrace.

In the Tradesmen's Terrace, you will be able to buy weapons, armors, or shields. The Craftmen's Terrace will allow you to develop skills such as tailoring, first aid healing, and also find suppliers for the elements you need to craft (some of these elements can be found while hunting).

 

If you go deeper within the city, you will also find other areas available for other classes. Among them are the Temple Gardens, which hosts the Temple of the Moon, There, several NPCs such as Portal and Priest Trainers are available. A banking area is also available, where you can store items that you don't immediately need.

 

More things are to be found in Darnassus such as a portal to find Hippogryphs, and others that I am sure I missed. But this should already give you a good idea of what to expect when you will finally be able to enter the game yourself.
