Paladin Journal

(part 3)

To part 2

The journal of our Paladin continues with the exploration of the area around Brenwall Village, where quests sent him after visiting Kharanos.

Although he has now reached level 8, our Paladin has still not evolved a lot when it comes to spells acquisition. His healing is still basic, and his damage only improved a little, mainly thanks to the acquisition of a new mace found while looting. The only major improvements came through an encounter with Sennyo (see part 2) who kindly gave our Paladin better armor.

As in most other games, trading with other players is very easy. Both parties just need to be close to each other. A character can accept any item, but can only wear it if it falls under his or her class restrictions.

Brenwall Village

After completion of the quests described in the other parts of the journal (part 1, part 2), my Paladin reached level 8 and accepted the only two quests that were available for him in Kharanos, which both led to Brenwall. As mentioned in other parts of the journal, it is always a good idea to get as many quests as possible at the same time, to avoid running back and forth between places.

Although listed as a village, Brenwall looks more like a camp where Dwarves gather. There are no solid structure, but only tents gathered around a fire. Wolves, Snow Leopards, Bears, and Trolls abound around the camp, and usually hunt in pack, which may mean the end of my solo adventures. The quests found in Brenwall, including the first two that are given outside this area are:

Evershine: This quest requires you to get a cask of Evershine from Rejold Barneybrew in Brenwall Village, located west of Kharanos.

Frostmane Hold: Another quest in a cave, where you need to kill 5 Frostmane Hunters, also near Brenwall Village.

A Favor for Evershine: You receive this quest when you complete the Evershine quest. It is another slay quest, asking you to kill some Bears, Snow Leopards, and Boars.

Completing Evershine was easy. It only required to travel from Kharanos to Brenwall, not a long walk. To go there, follow the road to the north until you find a bridge, then turn left and walk on the iced river until it turns into a lake. The camp is on the other side of the lake.

Although this new area is also mainly inhabited by beasts, the main difference that I notice here is that some species here act and react in groups. Attack a Troll, and as soon as he finds himself in disarray, he retreats and calls for reinforcement. Before you know it, you can have up to four Trolls trying to get a piece of your skin.

After a few trials to complete the Frostmane Hold solo, I decide that I do not have other choices but to form a group, which proves quite frustrating. My Paladin brings armor and secondary healing to the group, and in return gets additional firepower to fight groups of Trolls. After getting my head count, I come back to Brenwall Village and complete "A Favor for Evershine", a slay quest that is completable solo. Frostmane Hold will require me to go back to Kharanos to be completed. Before heading back, I pick up a few additional quests in this area.

Return to Bellowfiz: Requests me to go back to Steelgrill's Depot (where the Siege Engines are) to give some beer to Bellowfiz, one of the pilots.

Bitter Rivals: Asks me to replace a barrel of Kharanos' pub beer by one given to me in Brenwall.

The Perfect Stout: Requires me to get some Shimmerweeds from Troll bodies. This quest can be completed in two other Troll camps, northeast of the village.

The Perfect Stout quests proves too much for my Paladin. Although he doesn't die, thanks to his Divine Intervention skill that allows him to flee danger and stay unharmed for six seconds, it is clear that this quest is not soloable for a level 9 character (I leveled up while reaching the destination). Two solutions are now available: wait for some reinforcements or head back to Kharanos and complete the other quests, and visit the Paladin Trainer.

 

I decide to wait a little and, eventually, a Rogue, two Warriors, and another Paladin arrive. We group and slowly but surely, defeat the Trolls. Most of them are spellcasters that inflict about 50 damage for each spell they cast. Considering that they are never alone, and that a level 9 character only has around 270 HP, it is clear that a little strategy is needed to defeat them. This time, the group works very well, the Warriors tanking while the two Paladins offer additional armor and also act as healers. We notice that some baskets are randomly place within the camps. It turns out that they also deliver the weeds that we are looking for.

Groups: In World of Warcraft, groups are limited to 5 players. As displayed in the above picture, each member of the group you are in has an icon depicting his health status. If you want to apply a spell on any member of the group, you can either click on the icon, or you can just hit one of the functions keys. F1 will apply a spell to you, and F2 to F5 will apply the spell to other players. Note that auras are automatically applied to everybody in your group. Note that you don't need to be close to a group to be invited. A player can just type /invite followed by your name, and even if you are on the other edge of the map, you will receive the invitation.

With the Perfect Stout done, it is time to finish the other quests. Going back to the quest giver, I pick up another quest that requires me to go to deliver another keg of beer to another region. As no other quests seem to be available, I head back to Kharanos to finish both "Return to Bellowitz" and "Bitter Rivals".

Engineering Trainer: While completing Return to Bellowitz, I make a last tour of the buildings there and find an Engineering Trainer in the basement of one of the two buildings in Steelgrill's Depot. This trainer allows you to create some explosive and mechanical devices. It requires another trading skill, mining, which can be acquired through another NPC in the same room.

Bitter Rivals is also easily completed. To swap the barrels of beer, the only thing that you need to do is to buy a beer to the guard. Once he disappears, click on the barrel behind the spot where he stood, and your quest panel will open. I now need to go back to the village with the barrel of beer that I just stole to prove that I completed the transaction. Before that, I complete the quest that initially sent me to the village, and receive another one that will bring me to Ironforge.

You never look well enough: While hunting around Brenwall village, I noticed some Leper Gnomes guarding what seemed to be two of their camps. These Gnomes can be found on your way to the Frostmane Hold quest. I initially thought that I would eventually get a quest in Brenwall village to kill them, but after completing all quests, I still didn't see any NPC with an exclamation mark above his head. Being stubborn, and not willing to leave the area without completing all the quests, I asked for help in general chat (which I usually avoid). I was told that the quest was given in Kharanos. So, as I was in Kharanos, I decided to look around. None of the obvious buildings had any quests for me. I eventually found the quest location with the help of a player , and I have to admit that without him, I would never have found the building. If you want to do the quest, you need to pinpoint a building northwest of Kharanos. This building is almost underground! The best way to locate it is to follow the road to the north (as if you were going to Brenwall Village). Right after you leave town, look for a narrow path to your left. This is where you will find the quest! The bottom line is that if you find a group of mobs in an area, there is certainly a quest associated with them. Don't hesitate to ask about it in general chat.

Operation Recombobulation: This quest requires you to find 8 Restabilization Cogs, and 8 Gyromechanic Gears to repair a machine that will heal the Leper Gnomes and reinstate them within the Gnome community. These items can be found on the Leper Gnomes and should be brought back to the location where you found the quest (see above).

The first Leper Gnome location is located west of Brenwall village (between the village and the Frostmane Hold quest). You can solo this quest if you are careful. Gnomes will gang up on you if you attack one of them who is able to call for rescue. If you are lucky, you will not need to kill too many Leper Gnomes to complete the quest, as some drop two items. After you have all the required elements, go back to the building where you found the quest and stay to see what the Gnomes make of what you brought them back. Waiting will reward you with a good laugh.

That is it for this part of the world. Remaining quests will now bring me to Ironforge, which I can't wait to discover (and report). See you there!
